## Saie Blades
A grass plant found on [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) that produces the cereal grain known as Saiewin. It resembles light green grass where the rigid, starchy, stalks can grow to the height of an adult [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) which can take over a year. When the plant matures, it sprouts red-brown flowers with strands of spiky seeds which are considered poisonous to everyone except [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md). The emergence of the flowers also causes the saie blade stalks to become bitter, so when harvested for saiewin grain, this is done when they are still young and between 2-6 feet in height.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CCV2-3)</sup>

Saiewin has a variety of uses in Solstice cooking, both in the [Corelanyan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) and Argonian traditions.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CCV1-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBFP-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CCV2-3)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Corelanyan_Cuisine,_Vol._2.md)

**Harvested Ingredient:** [Saiewin](Lore_Saiewin.md)

## Saltrice
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-flora-Saltrice.jpg/200px-ON-flora-Saltrice.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Saltrice.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Saltrice.jpg) Saltrice [Saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) is an abundant, nutritious [plant](Lore_Flora.md) and crop that grows in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). The commonality of Saltrice plays importance to the culture of Morrowind, and even the history of the [Great House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), who live in the southeast region around their capital city, [Tear](Lore_Tear.md). They built their expansive fortune from years of harvesting this plant and the slave industry that they currently spearhead. Fermented saltrice is brewed into the popular drink, [Mazte](Lore_Mazte.md), which has a negative effect on one's wit and judgment.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Saltrice), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Saltrice)

**Harvested Ingredient:** [Saltrice](Lore_Saltrice_(alchemy).md)

## [Scuttlebloom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scuttlebloom)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-creature-Scuttle_Bloom.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Scuttle_Bloom.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Scuttle_Bloom.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Scuttle_Bloom.jpg) A scuttle bloom [Scuttlebloom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scuttlebloom), also known as **Scuttle Bloom** is a type of [plant](Lore_Flora.md) native to the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Easily startled, scuttleblooms will retract their leg-like appendages and burrow into the ground when they sense danger, using this behavior as a means of protection. They produce a honey-like substance. When harvested, it can briefly enhance one's mobility. Some wild scuttleblooms, once captured, can gradually become friendly companions and cease to panic when approached.

Some scuttleblooms have been kept in terrariums for display. Specimens in such a state have been encountered in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Scuttlebloom)

## Spidersilk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-node-Spidersilk.jpg/200px-ON-node-Spidersilk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Spidersilk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Spidersilk.jpg) Spidersilk Silk comes from an abundance of sources across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), but it is said only a few varieties can compare with the spidersilk of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It comes in several grades, known to the [Dark Elf](Lore_Dunmer.md) tailors as 'shimmer,' '[Azura](Lore_Azura.md)'s-breath,' and 'contraspun.' Shimmer, the most common, is a fine sort of rippling fabric. Azura's-breath is even lighter, so fine it is often translucent, but it's no less strong and durable than shimmer. Furthermore, it's particularly receptive to enchantment, and is often employed as the magicka-bearing fabric in spellwear. Contraspun, on the other hand, is so dense and impervious it's said it can turn a knife blade—and yet it retains silk's signature soft ductility. A contraspun garment provides a measure of protection without having to compromise for appearance.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LEAYQ-22)</sup>
