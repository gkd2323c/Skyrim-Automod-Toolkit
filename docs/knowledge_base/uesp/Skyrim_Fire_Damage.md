*Enchanting description:* **Burns the target for <mag> points. Targets on fire take extra damage.**

**Fire Damage** is one of the three elemental types of damage available from the School of [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md), the other two being [Frost](Skyrim_Frost_Damage.md) and [Shock Damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shock_Damage). Fire damage has the unique ability to set its target on fire for several seconds, causing additional damage through burning (usually ~30% of the immediate damage). The environment can also be set alight which will also cause damage to enemies that pass through the flames, although the effect only lasts for a very short duration. Fire Damage's primary advantage is that it is the strongest element in terms of damage (both direct and over time), and that there are a fair number of foes in Skyrim that are either non-resistant or outright vulnerable to fire (Nords, draugr, spriggans, just to name a few). On top of that, Fire Damage's Ao E spell, Fireball, has the longest range of any of the element's Ao E spells. Finally, Fire damage is also the only element that can trigger oil traps without the help of a lantern jug, and is the only element that can trigger flammable gas traps. Its primary disadvantage however lies in a questionably useful master spell, and the fact that those enemies that are resistant to fire are unfortunately the most common in their category (Fire Atronachs, Fire Dragons, etc), especially on Solstheim.

There are more variants of the Fire Damage effect than of any other effect in the game, with 70 distinct magical effects that produce fire in some form or another. This allows the game to use different visual effects for various types of fire damage, and also is one way that side-effects of the fire are customized. Other side-effects of fire spells are implemented by including multiple effects into a single spell.

## Spells
The following spells use the Fire Damage group of effects. Following each spell is the specific variant of the effect that it uses, as well as how much it burns the target for, and for how long (fire damage spells employ [tapering](Skyrim_Magic_Overview.md#Tapering_Durations), meaning the burning damage is not constant; the listed magnitude is how large the *initial burn* is):

| Spell | Burning Magnitude | Burning Duration |
| --- | --- | --- |
| [Fire Rune](Skyrim_Fire_Rune.md) - *Fire Rune* (`0005db8f`; `Rune Fire FFLocation`) | 30% | 2 seconds |
| [Fire Storm](Skyrim_Fire_Storm.md) - *Fire Storm 25* (`00082a32`; `Fire Damage FFSelf Area25`) <br> - *Fire Storm 65* (`00082a33`; `Fire Damage FFSelf Area65`) <br> - *Fire Storm 100* (`0007a82a`; `Fire Damage FFSelf Area100`) | 30% of only the *Fire Storm 100* effect | 4 seconds |
| [Fireball](Skyrim_Fireball.md) - *Fireball* (`0001cea1`; `Fire Damage FFAimed Area`) | 30% | 2 seconds |
| [Firebolt](Skyrim_Firebolt.md) - *Firebolt* (`00012f03`; `Fire Damage FFAimed`) | 30% | 1 second |
| [Flame Cloak](Skyrim_Flame_Cloak.md) - *Flame Cloak* (`0003ae9e`; `Fire Cloak FFSelf`) <br> - *J'zargo's Flame Cloak* (`00097ee2`; `MGRJZargo Fire Cloak FFSelf`) | 30% | 2 seconds |
| [Flames](Skyrim_Flames.md) - *Flames* (`00013ca9`; `Fire Damage Conc Aimed`) | 30% | 0.1 seconds |
| [Incinerate](Skyrim_Incinerate.md) - *Incinerate* (`0010f7f1`; `Fire Damage FFAimed75`) | 30% | 1 second |
| [Ignite](Skyrim_Ignite.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> - *Ignite* (`xx02732b`; `Ignite`) | 30% | 1 second |
| [Wall of Flames](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wall_of_Flames) - *Fire Barrier* (`0008f3ed`; `Barrier Fire Conc Aimed`) | 50% | 2 seconds |

Note that, with the sole exception of Ignite, all of the above spells taper quadratically (taper curve 2); Ignite tapers off linearly (taper curve 1). As with all taper spells, tapering does not begin until the spell itself ends, which is primarily an issue with Flame Cloak, Flames, Ignite, and Wall of Flames.

Also, there is a spell that is not acquirable in-game:

- Firebolt Storm - *Firebolt Storm Spell* (`000e9ac7`; `Fire Damage Storm No Damage Aimed`)
- *Firebolt Storm Spell* (`000d4889`; `Fire Damage Storm FFAimed`)

## Notes
- All characters start off with the spell [Flames](Skyrim_Flames.md).
- The perk [Augmented Flames](Skyrim_Augmented_Flames.md) increases the damage done by all Fire Damage spells.
- The perk [Intense Flames](Skyrim_Intense_Flames.md) causes the [Fear](Skyrim_Fear_(effect).md) effect *Intense Flames Fear* to be added to all Fire Damage spells.
- While a weapon with a Fire Damage enchantment or fire spell is drawn, NPCs may comment things like *"Take care with those flames."* and *"Don't set anything on fire, all right?"*
- Running into water will not stop fire damage. At the same time, you can cast fire spells or use fire enchanted weapons in water without diminishing their effect.
- Approaching flammable gas traps while holding a torch or with a fire spell at the ready is enough to ignite the vapors, resulting in a massive explosion.
- Fire damage can also be caused by jumping into [forges](Skyrim_Forge.md). Doing so will slowly improve progress towards your [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) skill. This won't work with the [Skyforge](Skyrim_Skyforge.md), though. - [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) Version 1.2 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) makes it impossible to level up your Destruction skill this way.
- Fire damage can also be caused by touching [lava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lava)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>, doing this will cause 150 pts of fire damage as well as 15 pts of fire damage for four seconds.
- Fire damage enchantments are reinforced by both the Fire Enchanter (from the enchanting skill-tree) and Augmented Flames (from the destruction skill-tree) perks.
