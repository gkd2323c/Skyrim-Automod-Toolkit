Camoran Gelthior was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who was the leader of the Blacksap Movement and a member of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md). Gelthior led the Blacksap Movement in a civil war against his cousin, King [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md), over his perceived leniency toward the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gelthior-1)</sup> Around [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) intervened in the civil war to support Aeradan, which led to the Battle of [Cormount](Lore_Cormount.md), where General [Endare](Lore_Endare.md) and the Jade Dragoons massacred many Blacksap rebels, and Gelthior was captured.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Iirdel-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMa C-4)</sup> After his capture, the [Vinedusk Rangers](Lore_Vinedusk_Rangers.md) imprisoned Gelthior in a remote [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md), ending his involvement in the Blacksap Rebellion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup>

## History
Gelthior was a member of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md), and he was second in line to the Camoran throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gelthior-1)</sup> He had two sons, Gorinir and Lorchon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Iirdel-3)</sup> He was the cousin of King [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gelthior-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HRo S-5)</sup>

In [2E 489](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_489), the Blacksap Movement formed in [Cormount](Lore_Cormount.md) to formalize strict adherence to the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), and Gelthior became its leader.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HRo S-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRo C-6)</sup> Following his accession to the throne, Aeradan sought to renew [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) by moving away from certain [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) traditions; most notably, he permitted a more lenient approach to the Green Pact. Gelthior and the Blacksap Movement believed that the Bosmer needed a ruler who would not discard the ancient compacts, leading them to initiate a civil war known as the Blacksap Rebellion to depose Aeradan and replace him with Gelthior.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gelthior-1)</sup>

By [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Gelthior and the Blacksap had pushed Aeradan's forces to near defeat.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Azareth-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Calunorril-8)</sup> To suppress the Blacksap Rebellion and repel the concurrent [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) invasion of northern Valenwood, Aeradan requested military assistance from [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md), the Queen of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AUAADP1-9)</sup> Ayrenn agreed and sent General [Endare](Lore_Endare.md) and the Jade Dragoons to [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gorinir-10)</sup> One night, the Jade Dragoons set fire to the trees around Cormount and attacked the town from all directions, killing many townspeople.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Iirdel-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMa C-4)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Galriel-11)</sup> Gorinir personally led the Blacksap's response and drove Endare's forces to the ruins outside Cormount.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMa C-4)</sup> At the ruins, the Jade Dragoons defeated and massacred the Blacksap, although Gorinir managed to escape and survive.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Iirdel-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMa C-4)</sup> During the chaos of the Battle of Cormount, Gelthior was captured.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMa C-4)</sup>

Following his capture, the [Vinedusk Rangers](Lore_Vinedusk_Rangers.md) imprisoned Gelthior in a remote [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) that was only accessible via portals.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup> With Gelthior missing and presumed dead, Gorinir and Lorchon became the leaders of the remnants of the Blacksap Rebellion and continued to fight Aeradan's loyalists and the newly formed [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMa C-4)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gorinir-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Lorchon-12)</sup> During Gelthior's imprisonment, the Vinedusk Rangers interrogated him, but the king forbade them from using harsher methods.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maeroth-2)</sup>

Although Gelthior continued to believe in the principles of the Blacksap Movement, he felt that the rebellion ended with the Massacre at Cormount and was a lost cause. He wanted his sons to be captured so that all three could have a public trial, which he hoped to use to demonstrate the righteousness of their cause. He also believed that if the rebellion continued, it would result in the Aldmeri Dominion becoming fully involved in the conflict, leading to his people suffering.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gelthior-1)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Vinedusk Rangers recruited an adventurer to capture Gorinir and Lorchon in order to quell the Blacksap Rebellion. As part of their efforts to capture Gorinir and Lorchon, the adventurer questioned Gelthior in his prison. Ultimately, the adventurer was able to capture Gorinir and Lorchon and imprison them with Gelthior, effectively ending the Blacksap Rebellion.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TBH-13)</sup> It is unknown if Gelthior and his sons ever received a trial.
