Kagouti are large, armored bipedal reptiles, related to [alits](Lore_Alit.md), [guar](Lore_Guar.md), and [wormmouths](Lore_Wormmouth.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EDAYQ-1)</sup> that are native to the regions of Eastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), primarily [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and to a lesser extent [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Their defining features are their large tusks and the thick crests on their heads. Kagouti have a large number of teeth, sometimes growing a second or even third pair of fangs depending on age.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

## Behavior and Diet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg) Kagouti and bull kagouti (ESO) While said to be typically solitary like alits,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KMH-3)</sup> kagouti are known to reguarly form packs,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MFP1-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VE-5)</sup> either as family units,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Daddy-6)</sup> hunting groups,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MFP1-4)</sup> or for the mating season, where they gather in especially large numbers. Despite often being larger and more vibrant in color than the smaller and duller-colored females, males are not the dominant sex during courtship, and are sometimes attacked when they gift food to their partners while wooing them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KMH-3)</sup> Males will, however, turn violent and rush to the defense of the smaller females when they are threatened.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VE-5)</sup> Bull kagouti are also known to be protective of their young.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Daddy-6)</sup>

Among themselves, the males are typically less violent, and normally do not fight with each other for reproductive rights; instead engaging in posturing, reinforced by loud calling, particularly at night.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KMH-3)</sup> This is not always the case though, as violent and fatal clashes can and, rarely, do occur.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> When it comes to other species, however, both sexes experience heightened aggression during mating season, and often attack intruders that get too close.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KMH-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> It is ill-advised to do so, as Kagouti are capable of tossing a grown mortal into the air with ease.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MFP1-4)</sup>

Just as the other members of their family, Kagouti are omnivores, and will eat anything they find palatable; be it plant matter, or unfortunate victims.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-V-9)</sup> Like many wild beasts, Kagouti are known to carry diseases or parasites, such as [ataxia](Lore_Ataxia.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-V-9)</sup> [chanthrax blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chanthrax_Blight), and [yellow tick](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yellow_Tick).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>. Few creatures seem to threaten Kagouti in their native habitat, though they have been noted to fall prey to [Wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

## Variants
There are several variants of kagouti, but the most widespread species, the **Least Kagouti**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-YT-12)</sup> is easily recognizable; pale pinkish-white as calves and subadults, often darkening as they age, with males obtaining vibrant patterns of reds, oranges, and yellows as they reach maturity and become bulls.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The Chalkhide Kagouti, however, native to the Deshaan region, bears a dappled hide of white, black, and gray that aids it in blending into the rocks of its home territory. Its behavior also differs from most other species as well, as it favors ambush hunting over direct and brute-force attacks.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> In contrast, the aggressive Twilight Kagouti, with dark black scales and crimson crest plates, is a pursuit hunter known to charge into brush when catching scent of prey.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

Kagouti have been found as far west as [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md),<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> with some kagouti-related species (sometimes known as "Fanged Gouti")<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-File K-17)</sup> also living in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). These include [Wickeders](Lore_Wickeder.md), [Bounders](Lore_Bounder.md), and [Clawrunners](Lore_Clawrunner.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> They could also be found in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) among the [Ragebound](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ragebound) steeds.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RK-19)</sup>

One variant, known as the Hearthfire Kagouti, was purportedly created by [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) wizards melding [Flame Atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) to Kagouti.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Rarely, Kagouti can be infected with [Vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md); producing a creature similar to a [Death Hound](Lore_Death_Hound.md) which have been seen in the company of Vampires in areas of Morrowind.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/ON-prerelease-Balmora_02.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Balmora_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Balmora_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Balmora_02.jpg) Mounted Kagouti in Balmora (ESO) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Kagouti_Hide.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Kagouti_Hide.png) Kagouti Hide
(Skyrim Creation Club) Kagouti horns and tusks can be used to make a variety of goods.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Powdered kagouti horn is rumored to heighten potency, and the ribs are used as an exotic ingredient for meals.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Dt M-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Their [hide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagouti_Hide) is used to make clothing, armor and household goods, and can also be utilized in alchemy.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MW-26)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

Kagouti meat is edible and used in [Dunmer cuisine](Lore_Dunmer_Cuisine.md), as well as Argonian and occasionally Khajiiti dishes.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Kagouti Steak is a meal can serve about 12 people, the recipe calls for the haunches but the rest of the legs and calves can do if a few more steaks are needed. These have the largest sections of meat and are tender in younger kagouti.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-RFV-29)</sup>

The domestication of kagouti as mounts was considered a recent and experimental development circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
