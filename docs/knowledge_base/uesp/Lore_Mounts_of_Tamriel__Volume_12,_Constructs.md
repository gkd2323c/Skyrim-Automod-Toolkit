Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 12, Constructs **by [Fadus Falto](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Fadus_Falto) of Skingrad** *A look at dwemer constructs, and the modification trade* *
---
The remnants left behind by the long-departed [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) continue to delight and confound researchers to this day. Not a year passes where some aging [construct](Lore_Construct.md) is unearthed, gifting the wider academic community another tantalizing glimpse of their ancient brilliance. Meanwhile the study of constructs that act as mounts, "[vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md)" in the original language, is a tantalizingly active subject to research. While most of Dwarven culture moulders beneath the earth, there's a very active community of enthusiasts unearthing, revitalizing, even modifying constructs to new aesthetic and functional specifications.

Do not mistake me: the core concepts that drive Dwemer mechanisms remain understood only by the likes of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and a rare few. But while the core mechanisms underlying [horse](Lore_Horse.md), [senche](Lore_Senche-Cat.md), or [spider](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md) constructs remain untouchable the artifice surrounding them have seen a robust explosion in creativity in recent years.

To better understand this craft, I spoke by correspondence with the artificer known as "The Spidersmith." This prominent yet enigmatic figure in the secondary vamidium modification trade is well respected in elite arcanomagical research circles. He spoke at length of the joy they derive from seeking an understanding of these ancient mechanisms. Taking them apart, re-assembling them, even restoring long-rusted wrecks to functioning status. Their appreciation for Dwemercraft has earned them some heady commissions from individuals and organizations across the [continent](Lore_Tamriel.md).

He stated to me, exclusively for this text, that he's most proud of the work he's done with the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) of [Sotha Sil's City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). That such a group would allow an outsider to study and confer among their ranks is a rare thing indeed. Together he and several gifted artisans studied an Ebon Horse construct recently discovered in an ancient Dwemer holdfast, a collaboration he considers a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*
