## Activities, Drops, and Locations
The act of fishing has many variables that go into determining what the player catches. The most important of these are the **biome** the player is fishing in, the **time** of day, the **population** of fish at the fishing spot, the **rod** the player is using, and any **quests** that the player may have active.

Artifacts that are not related to quests can be fished out of any fishing location, though only one copy of each artifact can be collected in a single playthrough.

There are a total of **69 fishing spots** along [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)'s many [bodies of water](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bodies_of_Water) (see [Notes](Skyrim_Fishing_(activity).md#Notes)).

For more information, please see the **[main article](Skyrim_Fishing_(activity).md)**.

## Notes
- The first semi-official introduction of usable fishing rods was in Chesko's *Art of the Catch*, one of Valve and Bethesda's short-lived paid mods.
- Fishing was meant to appear in the original game as an NPC-exclusive animation, similar to walking around with firewood or baskets. There are only a handful of markers left over from this concept, with only [Klimmek](Skyrim_Klimmek.md) still having an AI package that actually uses one of these markers. However, since the animation doesn't get used, Klimmek just stands in place doing nothing.
- Speaking with Falk Firebeard at the Blue Palace in Solitude will bring up a new dialogue. You can now purchase a small fish tank and a couple fish plaques for the Proudspire Manor. It appears on the second level in the main room against the load bearing column near the stairs. The new option will ask about additional decorations. Choose it and find the Small Fish Tank for 300 Gold and three Fish Plaques for 100 Gold as a bundle deal. Make the purchase and it should be installed by the time you enter the door. (If it doesn't appear the first time entering just leave and re-enter and it should appear.)
- Slaughterfish and Juvenile Mudcrabs cannot be added to your fish tanks. The fish tank within the Riften Fishery appears to be an exception as it has a juvenile mudcrab inside.
- The "Fishing Supplies" Barrel added to the aquarium adds fishing gear and a bucket full of Salmon Roe.
- Fishing Rods can be used as weapons due to being coded as One-handed Swords.
- The Green Thumb Perk can be used to get doubles of the Juvenile Mudcrab, the Brook Bass and the Tripod Spiderfish.
- The Creation was planned to add a new house, the Emperor's Homestead, which was obtained by hollowing out the corpse of a living Emperor Crab after completing Wave Breaker. This was [cut for time](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Cartogriffi%27s_Posts#2022) and the living Emperor Crab replaced by a ghost, though some location records and an unfinished interior cell for the home remain in the files.
- The model for the Irkngthand War Axe is named Last Rites.nif, referring to the [Last Rites](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Last_Rites) Dwarven battle axe from [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind).

## Bugs
- The Dwarven Fishing Rod uses "bow" sheathe sounds, but somehow got set to use "mace" draw sounds. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.7, addresses this issue. It now uses "bow" draw/sheathe sounds, like all other fishing rods.
- It is only possible to mount fish on the fish plaques in a single house. This is because the plaques in [Heljarchen Hall](Skyrim_Heljarchen_Hall.md), [Proudspire Manor](Skyrim_Proudspire_Manor.md), and [Windstad Manor](Skyrim_Windstad_Manor.md) all use the same quest aliases as those in [Lakeview Manor](Skyrim_Lakeview_Manor.md). As a result, attempting to mount a fish on a vacant plaque in a second location can result in the following message being displayed: *"Only one fish can be mounted to a plaque."* - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.629, fixes this bug.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) This bug is also fixed by the [Fish Plaque Fix](https://www.nexusmods.com/skyrimspecialedition/mods/67227) mod, but in a different way.
