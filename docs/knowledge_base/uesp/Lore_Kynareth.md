# Kynareth

— *[Ten Commands: Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ten_Commands__Nine_Divines)* Kynareth is the goddess of the heavens, the winds, the sea, the elements, and the unseen spirits of the air.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> She is a member of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) and Patron of sailors and travelers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Kynareth is often invoked for auspicious stars at birth and for good fortune in daily life. In some legends, she is the first to agree to [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s divine plan to create the mortal plane, and provides the space in the void for its creation. Furthermore, she is associated with rain, a phenomenon that is said not to have occurred before the removal of Lorkhan's divine spark. Some sources describe [Spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan) as creations of Kynareth.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Avita-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Fury-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOF-7)</sup>

## Mythology
The [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) is described as being Kynareth's sacred mountain by the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), where she breathed life into mankind, and then taught them the thu'um.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The Nords and Nedes say that Kyne was the wife of Shor and that upon his death her tears brought the first rain to the world.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSOP2-9)</sup>

Kynareth is the mother of the [demigod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demigod) [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus), she is also suspected to have communicated with [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) through visions of [Pelinal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelinal)'s imminent arrival.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSOP2-9)</sup>

## Worship

### Nordic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg) A depiction of Kyne According to followers of the Divines, the Sky Goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), worshipped by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as the strongest of the Sky spirits and the widow of Shor, is the Nordic aspect of Kynareth. In Shor's absence following his death, Kyne acts as the head of the Nordic Pantheon, and her role as a warrior Goddess remains the strongest out of the rest of the Nordic Pantheon following Imperial syncretization.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DATN-10)</sup>

Kyne is credited with 'breathing life into the world' at the top of the Throat of the World, exhaling atop the mountain to create mankind.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-COTS-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Froki-13)</sup> Rain is said to be Kyne's tears as she mourns the loss of her beloved [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor), since rain is believed not to have appeared until after his divine spark was forcibly removed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOF-7)</sup> It is said Kyne gifted men with the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), either through her daughters or in tandem with [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), so they could harness the [language of the dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) and use its power to save themselves from the wrath of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) and the dragons.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOF-7)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-15)</sup><sup>:330</sup> Kynareth has a temple in the city of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md); one of the duties of this temple is to tend the [Gildergreen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gildergreen), which is considered sacred to Kynareth's followers.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

### Reachman
After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Kynareth, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Belchimac-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cedran-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Degaine-19)</sup> In the Fourth Era, the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) Reachmen, who strongly opposed their invader's traditions, were observed gathering at the shrine of Kynareth. It remains uncertain whether they were actively worshiping her, however.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> In the Second Era, a Reachfolk group - [Dreadhorn Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreadhorn_Clan) was known to use shamanistic Kyne-magic.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-21)</sup>

### Khajiit
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/ON-furnishing-Sweet_Khenarthi%27s_Song_03.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Sweet_Khenarthi%27s_Song_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Sweet_Khenarthi%27s_Song_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Sweet_Khenarthi%27s_Song_03.jpg) A depiction of Khenarthi [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi), **God of Winds**, the **Gatherer of Waters**, **the Elder Spirit of the Heavens**, and the **Wind God**, is the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) goddess of weather and the sky, the most powerful of the Sky Spirits, who is believed to have given the first sounds to the world, and is the Khajiiti interpretation of Kynareth. When a Khajiit dies, it is Khenarthi who guides their soul either to [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah) for judgment, or to [Llesw'er](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Llesw%27er), the Sands Behind the Stars. And it is her clarion call that will summon the "eternal united spirit of all Khajiit" to defend creation at the end of time. Khajiit credit Khenarthi with leading their ships to safe harbor and their heroes to glory.

Khenarthi, like [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh), is said to be able to see across all the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) of [Fate](Lore_Fate.md).

### Imperial
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/LG-cardart-Plea_to_Kynareth.png/150px-LG-cardart-Plea_to_Kynareth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Plea_to_Kynareth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Plea_to_Kynareth.png) A soldier pleading to Kynareth St. Alessia syncretized elements of the Elven and Nordic Pantheons, forming the Imperial interpretation of Kynareth.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SATD-22)</sup>

Kynareth is often associated with [Zenithar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zenithar). Craftsmen use natural materials to create, and so one cannot revere Zenithar without acknowledging the power of Kynareth.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup> [Canah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Canah) feathers can be offered to Kynareth at her shrines; in return, the one making the offering will temporarily transform into a Canah.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

In contrast to the other Nine Divines, Kynareth has no known Chapel in the great cities of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OBEvents-25)</sup> Some of Kynareth's faithful have claimed that it is not right to build over nature, Kynareth's work, simply so one can worship her, and so have created altars to the goddess in the Cyrodiilic wilderness, instead.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

### Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/ON-misc-Kynareth.png/200px-ON-misc-Kynareth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Kynareth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Kynareth.png) A Bretonic statue of Kynareth (ESO) The island of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony) is considered holy land of Kynareth. This was a factor for [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md)'s involvement in the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md). It was also the reason why the conflict escalated to the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field), which ended the war.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-DFNarrative-27)</sup> Kynareth is the patron deity of [Alcaire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcaire), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-DFKynareth-28)</sup>

Saint [Stental](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stental) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [saint](Lore_Breton.md#Saints) who is best remembered for slaying the demon of Overlook Hill, rescuing the three Sisters of Kynareth from a terrible fate. A memorial, named "The Saint Slays the Leaper", was erected in [Stormhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven) to honor his courage in [2E 388](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_388), which depicts Stental armed with a sword facing the [Leaper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leaper) demon, depicted as a crouched gargoyle.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Stental-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Statue-30)</sup>

Kynareth is an important Divine in [Gonfalon Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gonfalon_Bay). To the locals, she is the patron of sailors and fisherfolk. Every couple of weeks, people celebrate an informal holiday to Kynareth called Angler's Day. Taverns will sell fish-based food at a discount, like Hundred-Year Bisque or Grilled Sweet and Sour [Sea Adder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Adder). Meanwhile, anglers can drink for free in whatever establishment they can find.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-LABHI-31)</sup> The Gonfalon Head Lighthouse is even sacred to the Goddess of Winds and is tended to by an [order of priests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Kynareth).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-VGTHI-32)</sup>

### Redguard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5e/ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg) A depiction of Tava The original [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) spirit of the air and the elements is known as [Tava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tava), the [Bird](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird) God. Though the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) as a whole worship Tava, the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) venerate them under the name Kynareth. In the modern age, Tava has been assimilated into the broader mythology of Kynareth and she is very popular in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), particularly among sailors. Shrines of her can be found across most port cities.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-VOFTF-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-VOFCR-34)</sup> In the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), the minor regions of [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset_(barony).md) and [Myrkwasa](Lore_Myrkwasa_(fiefdom).md) have Kynareth as their patron deity. Their temple, the Temple of Kynareth had many chapters across the regions.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-DFKynareth-28)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Boots of the Crusader
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/OBR-item-Boots_of_the_Crusader.jpg/150px-OBR-item-Boots_of_the_Crusader.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Boots_of_the_Crusader.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Boots_of_the_Crusader.jpg) Boots of the Crusader The Boots of the Crusader (also known as the Boots of Kynareth) were created by Kynareth. The wearer will not be attacked by creatures of the forest. Sir Juncan of the original Knights died while searching for the boots. The pilgrim visited the Shrine of Kynareth, and was told the location of the "Grove of Trials" by the high priestess (later to become Sir Avita Vesnia). Kynareth tested the pilgrim by sending the Forest Guardian, a giant [bear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear), to attack them. When the pilgrim didn't fight back, having reverence for nature, Kynareth allowed them entrance to the grotto where the boots were hidden. The boots were needed to retrieve the Mace of the Crusader; with them, the wearer's faith is strengthened and they can cross the void of doubt.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Fury-6)</sup>

### Lord's Mail
[](File_SR-icon-armor-Lord%27s_Mail(m)_02.png.md) [](File_SR-icon-armor-Lord%27s_Mail(m)_02.png.md) Lord's Mail The [Lord's Mail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord%27s_Mail) (also called the **Armor of Morihaus** or the **Gift of Kynareth**) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) given to mortals by Kynareth, one of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md). It is an ancient plated [adamantium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adamantium) or [mithril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mithril) cuirass of unsurpassable quality, considered to be heavy armor. It grants the wearer the power to absorb or regenerate health, resist the effects of spells, and resist or even cure poison. It is said that whenever Kynareth deigns the wearer unworthy, the Lord's Mail will be taken away and hidden for the next chosen one. The Mail was originally owned during the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) by the bull [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus), a [demigod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demigod) and early cultural hero of the [Cyro-Nordics](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyro-Nord) who was closely associated with Kynareth and [the Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Lord). The cuirass was rumored to have later been taken from Morihaus by Kynareth herself due to his arrogance. The cuirass is considered an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) relic, and is accepted as a uniform in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). Indeed, the armor has been known to bear the red dragon sigil of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) on its breastplate.

### Ring of Kynareth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/SR-item-Ring_of_Kynareth.jpg/150px-SR-item-Ring_of_Kynareth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_Kynareth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_Kynareth.jpg) Ring of Kynareth The [Ring of Kynareth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Kynareth) is an [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) [silver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver) ring with a [sapphire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapphire) gemstone associated with the goddess Kynareth. It possesses the ability to [influence the weather](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weather_Magic), causing rainstorms to arise when worn while fishing in temperate lakes and streams.

### Ring of the Wind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-item-Ring_of_the_Wind.jpg/150px-SR-item-Ring_of_the_Wind.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_the_Wind.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_the_Wind.jpg) Ring of the Wind The [Ring of the Wind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_the_Wind) is an enchanted ring from the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). It is a legendary artifact associated with Kynareth, the Goddess of the Winds, or [Zenithar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zenithar), the God of Work and Commerce. Very little information is known about the ring, other than the fact that it grants the wearer great speed.

## Holy Sites
- [Eldergleam Sanctuary](Lore_Eldergleam.md) - A cave containing the Eldergleam, an ancient tree believed to be older than metal or Men and Elves, which can only be harmed through Old Magic. A pilgrimage site, and guarded by [Spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-39)</sup>
- Temple of Kynareth in [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) - In the adjacent plaza is the Gildergreen, a cutting of the Eldergleam, believed to be sacred to Kynareth and a destination for pilgrims.
- [Kynesgrove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kynesgrove) - The woods above Kynesgrove are considered sacred, and thus are protected against woodcutting and forestry.
- Kynareth's Shrine in the Great Forest - Located in the Great Forest, as it is believed by those in Cyrodiil that a temple of stone would displease Kynareth.
- [Isle of Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony) - The island of Betony in the middle of the Iliac Bay is considered sacred to Kynareth, and her temple on the island also functioned as an insane asylum, where the former Emperor Pelagius the Third was interred. The sacred nature of the island was one of the contributing factors of the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md).

## Planet
The planet [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth_(planet).md), sometimes called **KYNRT**, is one of the Planets in the skies of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), and according to the Warrior-Poet, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), it is one of the eight worlds known to the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). There are no known satellites orbiting around it. As with all astral bodies seen in the skies above [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and their corresponding deities, the planet Kynareth is believed to be the plane of the goddess Kynareth as well as the goddess herself, as seen from the mortal plane. It is said to actually be a different [plane of existence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).
