[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/OB-quest-Vahtacen%27s_Secret.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Vahtacen%27s_Secret.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Vahtacen%27s_Secret.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Vahtacen%27s_Secret.jpg) The ruins of Vahtacen circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Vahtacen was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) settlement located at the swampy headwaters of the [Reed River](Lore_Reed_River.md) in the Mist Morrow Vale region of the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md), south of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md). It was the home of the Vahtache, an Ayleid faction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-So P-1)</sup>

In *[The Hosiric Lays](Lore_Mentioned_Texts.md#The_Hosiric_Lays)*, songs that date back to the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md), the "waving fronds of Vahtacen" are briefly mentioned. This gives credence to the belief that [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) possessed a subtropical climate during the time of the Ayleids.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

## History
During the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) knocked down the doors of Vahtacen to free the prisoners of the Vahtache, while [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) flew overhead. It was after this event that Alessia was called "Al-Esh" ("High Highness") for the first time.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-So P-1)</sup> After the rebellion, the settlement fell into ruin and was gradually lost beneath the earth.

During the time of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), Vahtacen's exterior was still clearly visible, although much of it had been covered by the waters of the Reed. The ruins swarmed with undead, led by a powerful [lich](Lore_Lich.md) known as the Faceless.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> This lich used magic to compel at least one individual to lure others into the ruins, to become blood sacrifices.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the ruins were within the territory controlled by the invading [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md). The nearby [Harlun's Outpost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Harlun%27s_Outpost) overlooking the ruins was a point of contention during the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md), with all three sides struggling for control of the structure. An Ayleid relic was recovered from Vahtacen for Vyctoria Girien of Cheydinhal that year, and the Faceless was destroyed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) began excavating the ruins, breaking through into the top level from a natural cave. The excavation was headed by [Irlav Jarol](Lore_Irlav_Jarol.md), an expert scholar of the Ayleids, although he wasn't involved in the on-site work. The excavation's progress was halted when several mages were injured by an enchanted pillar which reacted to magic. By translating several inscriptions, the mages discovered the correct order in which spells must be cast at the pillar, and in doing so revealed an entrance into the lower undead-infested level of the ruins. An ancient Ayleid helm of some significance was discovered within the ruins.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
