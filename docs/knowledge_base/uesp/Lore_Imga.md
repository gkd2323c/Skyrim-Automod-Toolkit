[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/LO-misc-Monkey Truth-%28cropped%29.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Monkey Truth-%28cropped%29.jpg)](File_LO-misc-Monkey Truth-(cropped).jpg.md) [](File_LO-misc-Monkey Truth-(cropped).jpg.md) A depiction of [Marukh the Prophet](Lore_Marukh.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Image)</sup> The Imga, also called **Great Apes** or **Apemen**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Betmeri-2)</sup> are native [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> These intelligent [apes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ape) are said to have flourished in Valenwood long before the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) ever arrived there.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> They cohabit in the forests with the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) tribes, and also inhabit many of the abandoned human trading posts which had been established during the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup> The apemen are also reportedly found in the mountainous areas in the west and north of Tamriel, and seldom encountered in the East.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Betmeri-2)</sup> The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) kept the apemen as slaves, though not in as great numbers as they keep [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Po M-4)</sup>

The most notable Imga was undoubtedly [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md), a [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) prophet who rose to prominence during the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md). His teachings sparked the massive cultural and spiritual movement known as the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIo D-5)</sup> Other Imga would go on to follow his teachings in the [Marukhati Selective](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSo B-6)</sup>

## History

### Early History
Little is known of the early history of the Imga, though they were known to flourished in Valenwood long before the Aldmer settled there.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> They may have been among the many aboriginal beastpeoples that lived in preliterate communities throughout Tamriel since the early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Betmeri-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup>

### The Monkey Prophet
At some point in the First Era, the Imga Prophet known as Marukh served a "Century of Penance" atop the Stonemeadows for toying with ape-maiden Dulsa. He would allegedly receive a vision of the deceased [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and would be compelled to write the Alessian Doctrines, going on to form the Alessian Order that would rise to power in the early [300s](Lore_First_Era.md#Fourth_Century).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIo D-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

The [Marukhati Selective](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective), the elite sect of the Alessian Order, was said to have Imga sorcerer apes or magiapes among them. Some of these would be among them when they caused the [Middle Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Middle_Dawn).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSo B-6)</sup>

### Brief Disappearance
The majority of Imga disappeared from Valenwood during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) and [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), believed to be part of their time-tested strategy of hiding out during periods of warfare until the strife was safely over.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-9)</sup> The controversial Altmer author [Cirantille](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cirantille) believed that they went to 'visit Falinesti', which had already vanished from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) centuries prior, so that they could wait out the end of the Planemeld and the Three Banners War, though most of her peers disagreed with her opinion on the subject.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Sometime around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), there was an Imgan [Lich](Lore_Lich.md) -Queen who ruled over [Meirvale](Lore_Meirvale.md) in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), but she was later defeated by [Razum-dar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Razum-dar), a well-known agent.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Also around this time, the prophecies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) made references to the Imga, although to a lesser extent than in the past.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> It is unknown exactly when or how the Imga returned, but they are known to have done so by [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup>

### Late Second Era and Third Era
Imga were written of in the first edition of the [*Pocket Guide to the Empire*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition), where they were described sharing the forests of Valenwood with the Bosmer and [Centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md) and idolizing the Altmer. A member of the [Second Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) circa 2E 864 known as [¥R](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:%C2%A5R), would sketch a gentleman Great Ape who was kind enough to pose for him in [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup>

At some point in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), when Simocles Quo was in his younger years, he was invited to join a colony of Imga in harvesting Kolllopi.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRKR-13)</sup> It is claimed some Imga would ally with the Bosmer during the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) after the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) attacked one of their camps, accidentally or otherwise.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MUT4-14)</sup> *[A Dance in Fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Dance_in_Fire)*, a fictionalized account set during the war, described Imga attending a revelry in Falinesti.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ADi F-15)</sup> Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Dunmer of Morrowind were known to keep many Apemen as slaves.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Po M-4)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-misc-Fur.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-misc-Fur.png) Imga Fur Imga are known to practice courting rituals,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIo D-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONEVE-16)</sup> though not much else about their reproduction or how they show affection is well understood.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NORP-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Love Archive-18)</sup> Imga are thought to enjoy [music](Lore_Music.md), using hollow logs as [drums](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Instruments).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-UOLTD-19)</sup> In [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md), colonies of Imga apes annually gather [Kollopi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kollopi), an arboreal rodent, from the ends of the slenderest branches of the graht-oaks. The "ape men" are naturally dexterous, and are renowned for their agility.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBA1-20)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRKR-13)</sup> They climb beneath the Kollopi rodents then jump up to "pick" them from their perches. These Kollopi are then used to make a simple peasant dish of the same name.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRKR-13)</sup> There is a beverage named for the Imga, known as "Sipping Imga Tonic".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

Some Imga see the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) as their superiors and as a portrait of an ideal, civilized society. Imga go to desperate measures to emulate the Altmeri: they wear capes, practice with the dueling sword, and attempt to speak with perfect enunciation and courtly manners despite their gravelly, baritone voices. Some Altmer seem to treat them with a degree of respect.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup> Each individual bears some kind of title, be it Baron, Duke, Earl, or the like, which they use when addressing the members of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) (but despite what the titles suggest, there are supposedly no land-owning Imga). More extreme Imga shave their bodies and powder their skin white to seem more like the Altmeri. The Imga feel that Men are beneath them as lesser beastfolk and pretend to find their smell exceedingly offensive: when Men are around, Imga hold perfumed corners of their capes to their noses.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-UOLTD-19)</sup>

While the Imga share the forests with the Bosmer,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-1)</sup> it has been claimed that they have little love for them.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MUT4-14)</sup> Nonetheless, Imga have been known to attend revelries in Falinesti, alongside Bosmer, [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), and [Centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ADi F-15)</sup> In one account of a [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) sailor attempting to sell carved bone-pipe of "genuine Valenwood make", a Bosmer woman was able to easill recognize it as a fake as the tail was wrong on the Imga carved on the bowl.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

Luxurious hand warmers made from Imga fur are known to exist. It is unknown who makes these but the sewn label inside them claim they were made from "treacherous" Imga.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-23)</sup>
