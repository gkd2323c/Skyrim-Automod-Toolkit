*Daedra Lords are like a child with their mother’s sword: all the threat and none of the discipline.*
— [The Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup> A Daedra Lord (also spelled **Daedric Lord**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTelbaril Oran-2)</sup> or **Daedroth Lord**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWGeneric F-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Daedric Lord)</sup> is a greater [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) being who occupies a rarefied space near the top of Daedric society, just one rung down from the Daedric Princes themselves,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAIA-4)</sup> maintaining great power and hierarchy over [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that is matched only by the [Demiprinces](Lore_Demiprince.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LHDR-5)</sup> and exceeded only by the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive1-6)</sup> The powers of the Daedra Lords are varied in scope, with some embodying their own individual spheres, and others being servants of the Daedric Princes who help maintain sub-spheres.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive1-6)</sup> Many of the more powerful Lords also rule over their own realms and demi-planes within the infinite swirling void of Oblivion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LHDR-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAIA-4)</sup>

Daedra Lords are one of the most powerful Daedra to be commonly seen in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and are to be feared by all but the most experienced adventurers. They are known to be powerful fighters and spell casters, and can only be harmed by [mithril](Lore_Mithril.md) or better [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) has many Daedra Lords in his service.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BS-9)</sup>

While only "a few handfuls" of Daedric Princes are known to mortal scholars, there are dozens of known Daedra Lords whose identities have been recorded, and a potentially infinite amount more within Oblivion, who simply haven't interacted with mortals or been recorded doing so yet.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAIA-4)</sup>

## Known Daedra Lords
- The Bad Man is a minor Daedric Lord of [Bad Man's Hallows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bad_Man%27s_Hallows), a pocket realm centered around his form, a tree that channels dark energy.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EV-10)</sup> He took the form of a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) Lord masquerading as the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) God [Sheor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods_S#Sheor), who is the true Bad Man.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EV-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLBMH-11)</sup>
- The Crow Mother, [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)'s "Nether-Niece" that has taken the form of a [Hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md), who rules over the [Crow's Wood](Lore_Crow%27s_Wood.md) pocket realm.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONCrow Mother-12)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTelbaril Oran-2)</sup>
- [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md) is the leader of the [Shardai Clan](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md) and is associated with Mehrunes Dagon,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BS-9)</sup> who considers her his adoptive daughter.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:156,159</sup>
- [Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md), a [Pumpkin Spectre](Lore_Pumpkin_Spectre.md), is the Daedra Lord of mortal fears who rules over the demi-plane [Detritus](Lore_Detritus.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LHDR-5)</sup>
- [Imago Storm](Lore_Imago_Storm.md) is the leader of the [Dremora Clan](Lore_Dremora_Clan.md) and served under Mehrunes Dagon for a time.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BS-9)</sup>
- Menthyx is a powerful Daedra Lord who was defeated by the Dremora Xynaa and the Ambition Sombren. After his defeat, his essence was bound and he was imprisoned in a dungeon in the Deadlands known as the Spidernest. The [Vestige](Lore_Vestige.md) and Sombren used the skull of Menthyx to recharge his power which undid the curse Xynaa placed upon him, allowing him to reform.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONSombren-14)</sup>
- [Sumeer Jabran](Lore_Sumeer_Jabran.md) is a Daedra Lord who served under both Mehrunes Dagon and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) for a time.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BS-9)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
- [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md) is a Daedra Lord associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-16)</sup> and Tho'at herself resembles the glass-like Shardborn from the realm of [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAIA-4)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-17)</sup> Tho'at has an affinity toward ink, purportedly being made of it herself. She created the [Maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), beings made from ink, during her invasion of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Malkhest-18)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAIA-4)</sup>
- Velark‎ is a minor Daedric Lord linked to the incident at the lands of Edrald family.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KE-19)</sup>
- [Xivilai Moath](Lore_Xivilai_Moath.md) is the leader of the [Xivilai's Clan](Lore_Xivilai%27s_Clan.md) and is associated with Mehrunes Dagon,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BS-9)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-20)</sup><sup>:23,44</sup> who considers him his adoptive son.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:156,159</sup> For reasons unknown, he briefly served [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-21)</sup>
- The Daedric Lord of Fishing is a Daedra Lord of which scarcely anything is known, save that they are a servant of Hircine, and have a distinctive aroma.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive1-6)</sup>
- [The Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md) seems to imply in her writings that she is a Daedra Lord,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup> possibly in reference to claims that she is a [Demi-Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demi-Prince) in mortal disguise.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Daedric Lord) | The term "Daedric Lord" is a phrase with connotations of "nobility", denoting the Daedroth in question as a power of importance. However, the usage of this term may lead some to conflate the Princes with Daedra Lords,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MH-23)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-24)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-25)</sup> which are a separate kind of Daedra.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-26)</sup> All Daedric Princes are Daedric Lords, but not all Daedra Lords are Daedric Princes. |
| --- | --- |

- Demiprinces are also called Daedra Lords,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONTF-27)</sup> but like Daedric Princes, All Demiprinces are Daedric Lords, not all Daedra Lords are Demiprinces.
- [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md) extracted the power of the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md) from [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md) in the aftermath of Dagon's banishment and escaped. As she thereafter possessed a portion of a Greater Daedra's power, she may be considered a Daedra Lord.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONAE-28)</sup>
