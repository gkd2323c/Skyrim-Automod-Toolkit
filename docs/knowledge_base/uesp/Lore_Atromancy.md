# Atromancy

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png/200px-LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png) Supreme Atromancer [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/LG-cardart-Expert_Atromancer_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Expert_Atromancer_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Expert_Atromancer_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Expert_Atromancer_02.jpg) Expert Atromancer Atromancy is a [magical](Lore_Magic.md) art regarded as a branch of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) focused on summoning [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md), known as Atronachs.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Atromancy-1)</sup> It should not be confused with [Atronachy](Lore_Atronachy.md), the practice of creating elemental [golems](Lore_Golem.md), which are sometimes also referred to as Atronachs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHing-2)</sup> Practitioners of Atromancy are known as Atromancers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SA-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EA-4)</sup>

While the magic itself existed prior,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SAB-5)</sup> the modern practice of summoning Atronachs dates back to the early development of the Conjuration school by the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). Atronachs were among the first beings summoned from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) during this period, often acting as protectors and occasionally as servants or [familiars](Lore_Familiar.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The master summoners of the Direnni were also touted as among the first to discover that a conjurer could impose any desired shape on a Atronach, such as that of a [horse](Lore_Horse.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-horse-7)</sup>

Daelnor the Summoner was a conjurer and "friend to atronachs" who specialized in summoning the beings. He had learned to summon almost every type of Elemental Daedra, with the exception of [Cold-Flame Atronachs](Lore_Cold-Flame_Atronach.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>
