[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/SR-creature-Karstaag.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Karstaag.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Karstaag.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Karstaag.jpg) Karstaag **Karstaag** was a [frost giant](Skyrim_Frost_Giant.md) who lived on Solstheim, and has [been dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Karstaag) for over 200 years. The spirit of this ancient creature can be raised as part of the unmarked quest [Summoning Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summoning_Karstaag). After defeating Karstaag, you gain the ability to [summon him](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summon_Karstaag), but only three times, and only outdoors. He is one of the toughest enemies in the game, having nearly double the health of the [Ebony Warrior](Skyrim_Ebony_Warrior.md).

In combat, Karstaag can summon three [ice wraiths](Skyrim_Ice_Wraith.md) at a time to distract you. He is much more powerful than a regular giant, being able to attack with his club, causing you to lose several hundred health points. When summoned, he appears with a [Blizzard](Skyrim_Blizzard.md) around himself, which will ragdoll you if you stand too close. The blizzard will dissipate after a short time, and he will have a [Frost Cloak](Skyrim_Frost_Cloak.md) surrounding him for the rest of the battle. If you leave the courtyard during the battle, Karstaag will have the blizzard surrounding him again when you reenter, but it will become a frost cloak very soon after reentering.

Karstaag has impressive resistances, with 100% resistance to [disease](Skyrim_Resist_Disease.md), [frost](Skyrim_Frost_Damage.md), and [poison](Skyrim_Poisons.md), as well as a 75% resistance to [shock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shock_Damage). He cannot be ragdolled, and is immune to all forms of [paralysis](Skyrim_Paralyze_(effect).md), as well as to [Mehrunes' Razor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mehrunes%27_Razor), [Mora's Grasp](Skyrim_Mora%27s_Grasp.md), [Whirlwind Cloak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Whirlwind_Cloak), and the instant kill effect of [Wabbajack](Skyrim_Wabbajack.md). However, he does have a 25% weakness to [fire](Skyrim_Fire_Damage.md).

He also benefits from a fortified combat healing rate of 0.15% and a fortified healing rate of 0.15%. These two healing rates combined let him regenerate 0.3% of his maximum health every second, or 12 health points per second.

## Notes
- Karstaag is a member of the unique "Ghost Giant" race that gives him his ghostly appearance. It has an ID of `DLC2Ghost Frost Giant Race`.
- Being undead, he is vulnerable to offensive [Restoration](Skyrim_Restoration.md) spells such as [Stendarr's Aura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stendarr%27s_Aura)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>.
- With [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md) on, Karstaag's Blizzard can reduce your health more quickly due to the [Cold](Skyrim_Cold.md) effect, which cannot be protected against by [Resist Frost](Skyrim_Resist_Frost.md) effects or your [Warmth](Skyrim_Warmth.md) rating. Hot food is the only way to remove the Cold effect and allow you to heal yourself.
- Activating Karstaag's throne after his defeat causes your character to sit using a unique animation.
- [Werewolves](Skyrim_Werewolf.md) and [werebears](Skyrim_Werebear.md) can stagger and ragdoll Karstaag, as their attacks ignore his corresponding resistances. This makes the [Beast Form](Skyrim_Beast_Form.md) a useful fallback for weaker characters, if *Dawnguard* is installed.
- An exploit to defeat Karstaag involves climbing a stack of ice chunks at the far end of the room from his throne, to the left of the door. Once you are up there, Karstaag will ignore you and retreat to his throne, allowing you to whittle down his health with ranged attacks. The ice wraiths he summons will also ignore you and dissipate in due time.
