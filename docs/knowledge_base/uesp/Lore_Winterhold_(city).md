—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Winterhold (also spelled out as **Winter Hold**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MCMap-1)</sup> is one of the nine major cities in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRMap-2)</sup> and it serves as the capital city of the eponymous hold, [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md). For the longest time, Winterhold served as the capital of Skyrim, one with a rich and illustrious history,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRKraldar-3)</sup> and even in its prime, it rivaled [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to the west.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup> Found on the once larger [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md), the city had an impressive harbor and many large districts for most of its history.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aren-5)</sup> Most of the city was lost when an event known as the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md) had cast most of it into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) in [4E 122](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_122).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OTGC-6)</sup> The most well-known landmark in the city is the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), Skyrim's premier institute in the [arcane arts](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AMM-7)</sup> The [Fortress of Ice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortress_of_Ice), found somewhere to the south of the city, is also known as the great Ice Fortress of Winterhold.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Shalidor-8)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg) The College of Winterhold [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/SR-place-The_Chill.jpg/200px-SR-place-The_Chill.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Chill.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Chill.jpg) The Chill Winterhold is built on [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRMap-2)</sup> the very tip of the [Broken Cape](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Broken_Cape) and where [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) first landed in Skyrim.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1E-9)</sup> From the south, the city is protected by a [mountain range](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterhold_Mountains) that spans across the northern [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md), and is surrounded by glaciers of the [Ice Fields](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ice_Fields) on virtually all other sides.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:224</sup> In its prime, Winterhold had a port and entire city districts were located on the coast,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aren-5)</sup> and its people commonly worked out of the sea.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-COSWWE-11)</sup> With the loss of much of Hsaarik Head to the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md), in the present day, the remainder of the city is built far above the shore and it is a very steep drop below.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRWinterhold-12)</sup> The island of [Olenveld](Lore_Olenveld.md) was a summer day's sail from the city.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TE-13)</sup>

Winterhold is located at the end of the one road that runs through the region, which wraps around the mountains and runs along the northeast coast. The College of Winterhold is separated from the rest of the settlement by a deep ravine and connected to it via a narrow stone bridge.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRWinterhold-12)</sup> Winterhold is neighbored by several settlements, including [Amol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amol) to the south-southeast, [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) to the west, the iron ore mining settlement of Whistling Mine along the southern road between Winterhold and Windhelm, and [Helarchen Creek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Helarchen_Creek) to the southwest.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ARMap-14)</sup>

The scope of Winterhold and its districts <sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aren-5)</sup> before it was destroyed has never been detailed, but after the Great Collapse, the former capital city was reduced to a set of homes on the main thoroughfare. The only remaining buildings in the town were the Jarl's Longhouse, the local inn across the road, a couple of homes along the mountain, and the College of Winterhold, which remained intact despite the destruction. Homes that are behind the street and closer to the edge of the cape are completely destroyed and encased in snow. A path between the Jarl's Longhouse and the General Goods Store leads uphill into the greater Winterhold wilderness, while a northwestern path leads under the bridge to the college, then wraps around the mountain. The city's prison is called [the Chill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Chill) and is an ice cave on a glacial island<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:799</sup> far north of Winterhold.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRWinterhold-12)</sup>

## History
*For the history of the hold, see the: **[History of Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md#History)***
### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-npc-Arch-Mage_Shalidor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Arch-Mage_Shalidor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arch-Mage_Shalidor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arch-Mage_Shalidor.jpg) Arch-Mage Shalidor [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/SR-quest-The_Jagged_Crown.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Jagged_Crown.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Jagged_Crown.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Jagged_Crown.jpg) King Borgas of Winterhold Even though the Nords have lived in the region since they first embarked onto Tamriel,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1E-9)</sup> the story of Winterhold's origins has never been fully stated. One of the most well-known myths that date back to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and surround the Arch-Mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md), was that he built the city from a whispered spell.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AMM-7)</sup> By the early First Era, Winterhold was already an established city-state.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AHOD-17)</sup>

In [Mid Year](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mid_Year) of [1E 68](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_68), a ship from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) made landfall in Winterhold's quay after barely escaping with their lives. Though its people hesitated to bring them in, Jarl Kjalhund provided them with food and warmth. This was the last record of any ships coming from Atmora.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TSOI-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-19)</sup>

One of the city's earliest known residents was King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md), the last of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s dynasty.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AHOD-17)</sup> When Borgas died in distant [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), the seat of the High King was vacant, and Winterhold's jarl, [Hanse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hanse) was the next one in line.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRKorir-20)</sup> But when the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) passed, Hanse was not given the title and Skyrim delved into a hundred years of conflict called the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1E-9)</sup> In that time, Winterhold and other cities fell to the rise of [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md), which was transcribed in [Svaknir](Lore_Svaknir.md)'s contribution to the [Poetic Edda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poetic_Edda).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-KOV-21)</sup>

In the late years of Emperor [Kastav](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kastav)'s reign, the city of Winterhold openly rebelled against the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and Kastav ordered the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) to come in and quell the uprising. But the master of the Dragonguard refused his order on the grounds that it violated their Oath of Allegiance and they paid the price when their supplies were cut off. In their stead, an [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) warrior named Kalien was sent to personally stop the [rebellion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterhold_Rebellion), which led to the city being sacked in [1E 2805](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2805) and a scar on the public's perception of the Dragonguard.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AOTD-22)</sup> Despite that, the rebellion ended on a high note when the newly-named Emperor, [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) ended it swiftly and made peace with the Nords. His ability to restore peace in the north was noted as his first great achievement throughout his tenure.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RTLOA-23)</sup> The city was restored soon after.

### Winterhold in the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/800px-AR-place-Winterhold.jpg/200px-800px-AR-place-Winterhold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:800px-AR-place-Winterhold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:800px-AR-place-Winterhold.jpg) Winterhold in its prime (Arena) During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Winterhold was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Erarne and had a rivalry with Dawnstar and [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). After they acquired their first piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) from [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) traveled northeast into Skyrim to find the second piece in a place called the [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md). Their quest eventually brought them to the local [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in Winterhold, where they met the leader, Thelen Kaarn. He offered to help the warrior if they retrieved a tablet stolen by knights from the [Fortress of Ice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortress_of_Ice), a tablet that could decipher a part of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) that would reveal the Labyrinthian's location.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ARThelen-24)</sup> After they delved into the dungeon, they returned to Winterhold with the tablet and were able to find their way to the Labyrinthian.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ARLabyrinthian-25)</sup> After the Eternal Champion defeated [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) and rescued Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), loyalists of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) re-traced the champion's steps throughout their quest and occasionally found themselves in Winterhold.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AMM-7)</sup>

In a map that labels provincial capitals circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Winterhold was listed among them as Skyrim's capital.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MCMap-1)</sup>

In the latter years of the Third Era, a large exodus of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) traveled west into Winterhold and brought it into a renaissance of power and influence. The Dark Elves brought their ideals and enriched the city's culture while invigorating its old mercantile spirit. A vast collection of text saved from the east was assembled together by the College of Winterhold and called the [Ysmir Collective](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ysmir_Collective), which had not only become a cornerstone of academia in the college but brought scholars from all over the Empire to the region.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup> However when the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) broke out in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a distrust in magic soon came over the Nordic populace and pitted them against the Dunmer and especially magic users, even though they were at the forefront of defense against the [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OTGC-6)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SRBrelyna-26)</sup> Many Dark Elves were driven out of Winterhold in the wake of the crisis and the people became increasingly uncomfortable with the college.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRKraldar-3)</sup>

### The Fourth Era and the Great Collapse
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/SR-quest-Containment.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Containment.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Containment.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Containment.jpg) Magical anomalies all over Winterhold [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-place-Winterhold_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Winterhold_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Winterhold_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Winterhold_02.jpg) Winterhold after the Great Collapse When the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) took place five years later and many Dark Elves were displaced, the Jarl of Winterhold took in refugees, especially those that could contribute to the College of Winterhold<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OTGC-6)</sup> but some of those that already lived in the city left for Morrowind.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SRBrelyna-26)</sup> In [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16), the High King of Skyrim had relinquished the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) to Morrowind<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SRLoading-27)</sup> and there was an expectation by the Nords that mages in Winterhold would leave for the island, but that never happened and a lot of people were unhappy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OTGC-6)</sup>

This contempt from the Nords came to a climax with an event known as the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md) in [4E 122](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_122). A series of violent storms and massive waves battered Skyrim's coast for a year and ultimately led to Winterhold's destruction, as a majority of the city collapsed and was swallowed into the sea. Many of its people were also lost to sea, even the family of Jarl [Valdimar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valdimar). Because the College of Winterhold was left unscathed, the Jarl's council blamed them as the cause of the disaster. Rumors started to spread across the settlement and the then Arch-Mage, [Deneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deneth) wrote personally to the Jarl to protest the accusation. Deneth claimed that the fact that the college was intact was testament to the protective magick placed around it many years prior, and that they had no involvement in the city's outcome.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OTGC-6)</sup> The city's original seat of power was lost in the Great Collapse and one of the old buildings was repurposed into the Jarl's Longhouse.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:478</sup>

Despite Deneth's claims, rumors persisted throughout the years, and people slowly left Winterhold until it was nigh a ghost town. By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the town was ruled by Jarl [Korir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Korir) and the college's Arch-Mage was [Savos Aren](Lore_Savos_Aren.md). In that same year, the College of Winterhold made an expedition into [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md) where they discovered the [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md), an artifact of immense magical potential. While one of their [initiates](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) left to try to discover the location of the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) agent [Ancano](Lore_Ancano.md) tampered with the Eye and created a powerful barrier inside the college's Hall of Elements. The Arch-Mage, his Master Wizard and the initiate confronted the agent but were blasted out of the college, killing the Arch-Mage, and magical anomalies spawned in Winterhold proper.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SRUnseen-28)</sup> The college took swift action to deal with the anomalies around town and while they mourned the death of the Arch-Mage, the initiate left for Labyrinthian to seek the Staff of Magnus.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SRContainment-29)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/ON-major_adornment-Anthor%27s_Shadow_Crown.jpg/200px-ON-major_adornment-Anthor%27s_Shadow_Crown.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-major_adornment-Anthor%27s_Shadow_Crown.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-major_adornment-Anthor%27s_Shadow_Crown.jpg) Anthor's Shadow Crown [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/SR-symbol-Winterhold.png/200px-SR-symbol-Winterhold.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-symbol-Winterhold.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-symbol-Winterhold.png) Winterhold's symbol *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Nord.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Nord.md#Society)** sections on the Nord page.* The common folk of the Winterhold region are seasoned fishermen that have grown accustomed to the harsh winters and even though the land offers little for grazing, they have managed to make due successfully with food. A staple dish in Winterhold and other Nordic coastal cities is [Coastal Clam Chowder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nord_Cuisine#Coastal_Clam_Chowder), which is made from a combination of [nordic barnacle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nordic_Barnacle), [clams](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clam), oysters, and a variety of [fish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fish), typically the catch of the day.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TESOC-30)</sup><sup>:93</sup>

One of the most lauded pieces of food to come out of the hold is [Ghostflesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nord_Cuisine#Ghostflesh), their regional cheese dish. The cheese is made from the little supply of dairy they have, it is then smoked over a pile of dried seaweed, and then wrapped into a pastry made from [flour](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flour) and [horker](Lore_Horker.md)'s suet. It is then baked to the point that the cheese is molten and steamy and that the crust is light and flaky. Ghostflesh is an ideal snack for Winterhold's fisherfolk while they are out at sea.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-COSWWE-11)</sup>

Many stories and myths from early Nordic history originated from Winterhold. The local [skald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skald) tell the tale of [Ahzidal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ahzidal), the first great Nordic [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md), who spent the rest of his life attaining every bit of arcane knowledge to expand his prowess. He offered his skills to the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) and later became a [priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) in the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). Ever since he sought knowledge in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), he was never seen again.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-AD-31)</sup> Another legend revolves around the last Atmoran "invasion" of Skyrim, two ships of survivors, largely filled with dead passengers. Their descriptions of Atmora have changed drastically over the years.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-19)</sup> One of these ships reached Winterhold claiming that the Frostfall that took the continent was conjured by the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md). People who were not there at the time scoffed at the idea.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TSOI-18)</sup>

The Nords of Winterhold favor six-foot-long axes.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TTD-32)</sup> Winterhold's symbol, a three-pointed crown was recreated as a small [bronze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bronze) adornment called Anthor's Shadow Crown. It was worn by the master of hidden arcane knowledge in the city and some people even say that it never saw use in anything other than secret meetings, but the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) author, [Taleon Mythmaker](Lore_Taleon_Mythmaker.md) doubts it.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONShadow-33)</sup> Other similar adornments are the Dawn's Eye Crown, which features the eye of Winterhold's college<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONDawn-34)</sup> and the Shrouded Crown.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONShroud-35)</sup> The [Wolfhounds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dog#Wolfhound) of Winterhold have lighter and fluffier coats, compared to their cousins in the south. They can even howl a bit like the wolves they chase.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONWolfhound-36)</sup>
