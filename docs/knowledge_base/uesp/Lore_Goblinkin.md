# Goblin-ken

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/ON-creature-Nokhailaedhaz_Even_Bigger.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nokhailaedhaz_Even_Bigger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nokhailaedhaz_Even_Bigger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nokhailaedhaz_Even_Bigger.jpg) A goblin and ogre living alongside one another *Is a Goblin a person? Is an Ogre a person? They're clever enough to put on a pair of trousers, I'll give you that. But if those beasts are people, then the world we live in is full of murderers.*
—An excerpt from *[A Hunter's Journey VI: Fauns](Lore_A_Hunter%27s_Journey_VI__Fauns.md)* Goblin-ken (also called **goblin-kin** or **goblinkin**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMT7-3)</sup> is an unspecific term used to refer to several different [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), including [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), and [Gremlins](Lore_Gremlin.md). Although they supposedly have [elven](Lore_Mer.md) blood, [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) are also widely considered to be goblin-ken due to the common belief that they are related to goblins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-APG-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GF-6)</sup> Goblin-ken have been historically used as slaves by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup>

## Races

### Orcs
[Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), or Orsimer, are a race that have conflicting views on their nature, with some sources considering them elven, and others claiming them to be beastfolk that predate elven arrival to Tamriel. They are the most intelligent of the goblin-ken, and openly trade with and even serve outsiders. Many Orcs are very loyal to Malacath and live a life dedicated to him.

### Goblins
[Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), known as the **Children of Muluk** or derogatorily called **Gobs**, are primitive humanoids that have been found in virtually every part of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and even on islands off the coast of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). Goblins can often be found living in caves, ruins, and sewers in small clan-based societies. Goblins are fiercely territorial, and typically violent towards those not of their tribes or the more societally developed races in general. Many regard them as simply monsters or nuisances. Despite this Goblins are still an intelligent race, and are capable of living in peace with others and showing empathy. They are the namesake of the Goblin-ken family, and as such are considered kin to [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), [Gremlins](Lore_Gremlin.md), and sometimes even [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Goblin.md).

### Rieklings
[Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md) are a race of blue-skinned ice [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), related to the other Goblin-ken. The most common Rieklings are small in stature and found on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), but larger Rieklings were once present on mainland [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They are aggressive, and can be dangerous in groups. Despite being considered little more than beasts by the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), Rieklings are naturally cunning. Rieklings speak the goblin language, and can be conversed with by expert linguists. Some speak [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodilic.md), although they rarely say anything intelligible. Apart from this, they seemingly have little culture of their own. They scavenge what few relics they can find from the other races, and form strange attachments to these treasures.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Riekling.md).

### Riekrs
[Riekrs](Lore_Riekr.md) (or **Riekr Goblins**) are a race of [magically](Lore_Magic.md) gifted ice [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) from the province of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), related to other Goblin-ken. They are sometimes described as [crossbreeds](Lore_Multiraciality.md) between standard Goblins and [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md), or as just a larger variety of Riekling. The Riekr are social creatures; each goblin is expected to be able to pull their own weight within their tribe, and those who can't are not treated kindly.

Their skin color varies between shades of white and blue. Many of them have long white hair, as well as prominent beards or mustaches.

The Riekr have a unique initiation system for those who show magical capabilities where they are exiled and have to buy their way back. The mage must then wait a certain period before they are allowed to buy their way back into the tribe with a food offering such as a freshly slain [deer](Lore_Deer.md), [bear](Lore_Bear.md), or [echatere](Lore_Echatere.md). Once the mage buys their way back into the tribe, they quickly rise in status to become a revered member of the tribe, gaining influence with the chief.

Riekr chiefs own objects that indicate their position of power in the tribe. The Riekr known as King-Chief Edu stole and used an ancient scepter created by the Orgak Orc clan, as a symbol of his power. Another Riekr chief who was notably a spellcaster had a totem as his symbol of power.

Riekr are known to work closely with the [Snow Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) that also inhabit Wrothgar. They are said to provide their Ogre allies with weapons and armor they wouldn't normally have access to. Riekr have a mostly antagonistic relationship with the more "civilized" peoples that reside in Wrothgar, sometimes organizing raiding parties to kidnap hunters. However they have been known to welcome outsiders in their ranks as long as they smell like a Goblin. Riekr have also been known to display attraction towards Orcish women.

Like other Goblins, Riekr tame wild beasts such as feathery [durzogs](Lore_Durzog.md). Also like other Goblins, Riekr are capable of speaking [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), though often require training to do so. Some Riekr are tamed and raised to be servants, doing tasks such as setting tables.

### Ogres
[Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) are a [race](Lore_Race.md) of large humanoid Goblin-ken that are found throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Best known for their great strength and foul smell, they are commonly perceived as primal, dim-witted brutes. Ogres have steely blue or greenish skin, pronounced teeth, and tapered ears. Their large physique often garners comparisons to [Giants](Lore_Giant.md). It has been hypothesized by the scholar [Phrastus](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) that their grey-blue coloration is a method of camouflage against the sky, although this is considered to have been convincingly debunked by his academic rival, [Lady Cinnabar](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md) of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md).

Ogres often have shaggy white or black hair, which can cover the entire body of those found in colder regions. The Ogres of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) are sometimes called **Snow Ogres**, and are differentiated from common Ogres by their thick white fur. They are believed to be more advanced than typical Ogres, and some of them use powerful totemic frost magic.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Ogre.md).

### Gremlins
[Gremlins](Lore_Gremlin.md) (also derogatorily called **Orc-rats** or **Squeakers**) are small, harmless creatures related to [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), whom they can often be found living alongside. Gremlins have a distinct appearance with large feet, and tiny arms incapable of using any [man](Lore_Man.md) -sized tools. While seen as unpleasant by most, they usually aren't met with violence like most Goblin-ken. Instead, they are typically shooed away or otherwise dismissed, though some show them kindness. They are thought to occasionally beg for food.

A number of Gremlins and other Goblin-ken lived in certain iterations of the city of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md). Gremlins were also known to live in the Goblin Caverns of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md). Many believe that the Goblin-ken of Stros M'Kai were banished beneath the earth thanks to [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md). Some [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) who lived there would warn Goblin-ken such as Gremlins to return to their caves, lest Hunding turn them into stone. Gremlins have been used as slaves by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and as [familiars](Lore_Familiar.md) by some wizards in the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), they may sometimes be kept in jars.

Gremlins have their own [language](Lore_Linguistics.md), known as gremlin tongue, which is not usually understood by other races, though some words in it are coincidentally similar to other words in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md). There exists a transmogrification [spell](Lore_Magic.md) capable of transforming someone into a Gremlin. This spell was a favorite trick of [Jaganvir](Lore_Jaganvir.md), the Archmage of the Mages Guild on Stros M'Kai circa [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Gremlin.md).

## Origins and Religion
*What the [scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) I've read say about the Goblin-kin has not been kind, Tim. Yours is a people from a different age. Don't look to prophecy to save you.*
—Sister Chana Nirine answering the question of "Goblin Tim"<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAMoth-7)</sup> The origins of the Goblin-ken races are typically associated in some way with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). The Orcs in particular have many tales of their origins related to the god, though these are [often debated](Lore_Orc.md#Origins). The [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md) tribe of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) believe that the Malacath created Orcs, along with Ogres, and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md), as a means to test his chosen followers and impose hardships.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MATR-8)</sup> Similarly, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) believed that Orcs, along with Goblins and trolls, were among "the miscreated", malicious and brutish creatures who, as [the One](Lore_The_One.md) teaches, must be united against by all sentient folk of good will instead of striving against each other.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Some fanciful historical parchments speak of Goblins as "Fetid Corruptions of Orcs".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GF-6)</sup> Some [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) refer to Goblins as the "spawn of [Malak](Lore_Malak.md)'s putrid feces",<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> while [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) have likewise referred to them as "[Malooc](Lore_Malooc.md)'s squalor".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

Goblin-ken have been described as "a people from a different age",<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAMoth-7)</sup> or as an example of "Old Life".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Some sources from [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) history have suggested that Goblins may be extra-planar in origin, originating from some other [world](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RGH-13)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-15)</sup>

Malacath is known to take patronage over the Goblin-ken races, such as Orcs and Ogres. He considers the latter to be his "little brothers" and is known to protect and guide them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The Orcs worship him as both the Daedra Malacath and the god [Mauloch](Lore_Mauloch.md). The Goblins and Ogres worship a god named [Muluk](Lore_Muluk.md), or "the Blue God",<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Clex-17)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BG-18)</sup> who is believed be an aspect of Malacath or Maluloch.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Clex-17)</sup> The ancient giant Goblins worshipped a deity known as Malooc,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-VOF-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-EGE-22)</sup> who was believed by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) to also be Maluloch.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DWAAPOT-23)</sup>

## Interspecies Relations

### History of Orc and Goblin-ken Relations
*"There are those who say that Orcs and the various races of Goblins are closely related. If so, they must not come from a very happy family, because they never seem happy to see each other."*
— [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) There have been mentions of extinct "[orcoblins](Lore_Multiraciality.md#Orcoblin)",<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-24)</sup> which are presumably some cross between an Orc and a [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md).

At some point during the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), hundreds of Goblins, Orcs, Ogres, Gremlins, and other beastfolk were set free by the rulers of the Summerset Isles, and allowed to settle lands north of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They chose to settle in an uninhabited mountain region close to [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), as the Orcish tribes were dependent on rare, shaggy, giant [centipede](Lore_Centipede.md) herd-beasts that can only survive at high altitudes. Here, they would found the first city of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) (literally translated as Orc-Town).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup> The exact dating of this event is unclear, but various pieces of evidence place the founding of Orsinium in the ninth or tenth century of the First Era.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-WJOTB-25)</sup> Some [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) historians believed the goblin tribes never actually lived in the city itself, instead maintaining a truce with the Orc clans in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), but never mingling.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-AMOB-26)</sup>

In [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874), when the Orcish Warlord [Thulgeg](Lore_Thulgeg.md)'s army of Orcs and goblins was driven from Hammerfell by the Redguards, the Bretons of [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) denied them passage through the Pass and forced them to flee northeast, trudging all the way through the Dragontail Mountains before they finally reached Orsinium. Not a single goblin made its way through the pickets into High Rock.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BSHK-27)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TM-28)</sup>

After the first sacking of Orsinium by the [Order of the Diagna](Lore_Order_of_the_Diagna.md) and the subsequent rebuilding, Orcs would become the sole inhabitants of the city. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Orcs and the other Goblin-ken had little care for each other. The Ironhand ogres of [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) were a significant military threat to the region, and impeded the Orcs efforts to rebuild Orsinium, so they were besieged and slain by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-threat-29)</sup> Priests of Malacath during this time did not consider eating Ogres to be cannibalism, and in-fact claimed Ogre innards were commonly found in ancient Orc dishes.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Orzorga-30)</sup>

Despite past conflicts, as of [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Goblins, Ogres, and Gremlins were once again known to inhabit Orsinium.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup> Though it is unknown if this is true of later iterations of the city.

[Morga the Witch](Lore_Morga_the_Witch.md), an Orc, had many Goblin followers.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Goblin-31)</sup>

### Mingled Tribes
Despite their complicated history with Orcs, the other Goblin-ken species much more commonly mingle with each other. Ogres are commonly known to intermingle with Goblin tribes,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-RGOgre-32)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOgre-33)</sup> often ranking high in them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BG-18)</sup> Goblins, Gremlins, and Ogres were all known to inhabit the Goblin Caverns of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Gremlin Caves-34)</sup> The Riekr and Snow Ogres of Wrothgar are known to work closely with each other. The Riekr are said to provide their Ogre allies with weapons and armor they wouldn't normally have access to.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Live-35)</sup>

- Although not goblin-ken, Goblin tribes have been observed to have [Trolls](Lore_Troll.md),<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Icestalker-36)</sup> [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md),<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-KI-37)</sup> and even [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) in their ranks.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-38)</sup>
- The related term "riekr-kin" has sometimes been used by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) to refer to the deformed remnants of the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), believing them to be some form of ice goblin related to the riekr.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-39)</sup>
- Back when Orcs were still considered unintelligent monsters at the time of *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, it was said that scholars have often speculated that Goblins and Orcs are somehow related, with some stating that Orcs are just larger versions of Goblins.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-APG-5)</sup>
