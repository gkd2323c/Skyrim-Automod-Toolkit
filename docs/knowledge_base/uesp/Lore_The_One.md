# The One


The Alessian Order can trace its origins to the prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md), who preached of a Single God and questioned the legitimacy of [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) rule. This depiction of a Single God became increasingly abstract and unknowable, as the Alessians realized they had to incorporate existing ancient polytheistic elements into their new religion for it to find a wide acceptance. This eventually led to the Order declaring itself the authority on every religion in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), leading to a massive growth in power and influence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>

The Order's concept of the One was rooted in the belief that the God of Time [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) is of unitary essence, as proven by the monolinearity of Time. Furthermore, they believed that his sibling [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr) is "singularly misplaced" and therefore doubly venerated.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TEM-3)</sup> Their hatred of all things Elven was such that they could not accept that their Supreme Spirit Akatosh was also the Elven High God [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TILWWYDB-5)</sup>

The Order therefore believed that their purpose upon [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) was to "reverse the error of Sanctus Primus" and "restore Ak-at-Osh to humanadic purity" by exorcizing the Elven elements of Auriel (the "[Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) Taint") from Akatosh.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vft DB-4)</sup> A group known as the [Maruhkati Selectives](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maruhkati_Selectives) were chosen to perform the ritual dance to carry this out. The ritual resulted in a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) known as the [Middle Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Middle_Dawn), a period of non-linear time said to have spanned one thousand and eight years, which the Selectives intended to use in order to carry out their goals.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vft DB-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ot Dot S-7)</sup>

When the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) broke out in [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321), the Alessian Order's great monastic complex at [Lake Canulus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Canulus) was razed, destroying most of their records.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The Order collapsed within ten years, ending their theocratic rule.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> However, veneration of the One continued. The [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) remained standing, housing the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and acting as a temple to the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) and later the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) revived the ancient Temple of the One cult when he became [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md), and expected his subjects to join it.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), two widespread temples called the [Brotherhood of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brotherhood_of_the_One) and the [Conclave of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conclave_of_the_One) both venerated this deity. The Brotherhood had parishes in city-states such as [Lilmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lilmoth) and [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), and in smaller settlements like [Neugrad Watch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Neugrad_Watch) and [Wasten Coridale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wasten_Coridale). The Conclave on the other hand was seen just as widely in city-states such as [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md) and [Necrom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necrom), and in minor settlements like [Belkarth Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Belkarth_Guard) and [Longhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Longhaven).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ARTemples-13)</sup>

## Notes
- The One is not to be confused with the "Eight and One", the traditional name for the Nine Divines used to describe [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) joining the original Eight Divines after his apotheosis.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
- [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) sometimes refer to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) as "the one".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
