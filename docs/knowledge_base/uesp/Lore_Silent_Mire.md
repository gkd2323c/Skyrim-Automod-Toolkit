[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/ON-place-Silent_Mire.jpg/200px-ON-place-Silent_Mire.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Silent_Mire.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Silent_Mire.jpg) Silent Mire circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Silent Mire is an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) settlement in the [Siltreen](Lore_Siltreen.md) region of [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md), on the eastern coast in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> It is a farming settlement and possesses fertile soil for growing [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vara-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Leel-3)</sup> Silent Mire is noted for its mud huts, in contrast to the stone architecture of the local [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/ON-place-The_Crafty_Monkey.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Crafty_Monkey.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Crafty_Monkey.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Crafty_Monkey.jpg) A Khajiiti slaver ship docked at Silent Mire When the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was founded in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTEP-4)</sup> most [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) Dunmer freed their Argonian slaves and the practice was outlawed throughout most of Morrowind.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> It was during the time of the Ebonheart Pact that Leel-Vata, the village's spirital leader, led her people out of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to settle on the fertile land at Silent Mire. The settlers brought with them six sacred stones and a branch from their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Leel-3)</sup> The villagers followed Leel-Vata's teachings to escape the violence of the world and live uncomplicated, peaceful lives.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vara-2)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the village was betrayed by one of their own: Leel-Vata's brother, Seeks-the-Night, sought vengeance when Vara-Zeen was made the village elder instead of him. When Leel-Vata and Vara-Zeen became mates, Seeks-the-Night made an agreement with the [Renrijra Maor](Lore_Renrijra_Maor.md), a band of [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) pirates and privateers in service to the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). The Renrijra pirates arrived at Silent Mire aboard *The Crafty Monkey* and raided the village, stealing their sacred relics and capturing the villagers to be sold into slavery.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

After learning of her egg-brother's treachery, Leel-Vata enlisted the aid of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in freeing the captured villagers and retrieving the sacred relics. The recovered Hist branch was then used to summon Seeks-the-Night back to Silent Mire; the Hist's power compelled him to return to his hut, where he was slain by the Vestige.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Captain Sa'dir, the leader of the pirates, was also slain aboard his ship,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> and the remaining raiders were driven off by [giant mire wasps](Lore_Giant_Wasp.md) when their nests were agitated by the village [alchemist](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

The [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md) were known to operate a safe house in Silent Mire for escaped slaves during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), located along the route to Black Marsh.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

## Notes
- It appears that the Ebonheart Pact had planned to recruit Seeks-the-Night as an agent against the Dominion prior to his death.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>
