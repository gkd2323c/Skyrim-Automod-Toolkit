## Alchemy
Alchemy is the act of mixing, boiling, and distilling various substances to obtain their chemo-magical properties and create potions and poisons. Potions are usually imbibed orally and usually grant the imbiber with positive effects. Poisons are introduced into the target's bloodstream by pouring it onto a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) and attacking the target with it and usually gives the target negative effects.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AFMA-1)</sup>

Alchemical ingredients include extracts from plants, animals, undead, and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Aa Rt Bo M-2)</sup> Certain ingredients are very valuable to alchemists due to their rarity, many of which are extracts from Daedric creatures.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HGTS-3)</sup> Prospective alchemists often need to experiment with ingredients to gauge what effects can be created, usually by eating samples of ingredients. This practice is referred to as wortcraft.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FOA-4)</sup> Stationary alchemical stations are often used for the preparation of potions.

In the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), they were often utilized by the temple clergy and the elusive [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) as a service to their members.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFPotions-5)</sup> In [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), such stations were not only used by apothecaries but also installed by court wizards and even some taverns.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRAlchemy Lab-6)</sup> Some landowners may also have an alchemy laboratory in their own homes. In contrast, within [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) it was far more common for a smaller travel apparatus to be used by alchemists. These portable laboratories had four components:

- Mortar and Pestle: used for grinding the ingredients together into a paste to be boiled, required for potion brewing
- Retort: used for distilling the brew, increases the magnitude of the potion's positive effects, optional for potion brewing.
- Calcinator: used to increase all effects of the brew, both positive and negative, optional for potion brewing.
- Alembic: also distills the brew, used to decrease its negative effects, optional for potion brewing

Around [4E 8](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_8), tests for discerning an ingredient's four "virtues" (primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary) were developed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TIC-7)</sup><sup>:41</sup>

Some sources claim that the praxes and rituals used by all modern potionmakers, [itemmakers](Lore_Enchanting.md), and [spellmakers](Lore_Spellmaking.md) were originally codified by [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md). Galerion is said to have restructured the schools of magic so they'd be understandable by the masses and to have invented the tools of alchemy and enchanting so everyone could concoct whatever they wanted without fears of magical backfire. It is said that the original tools of enchanting and alchemy that Galerion crafted were more automated than modern ones, robotic master enchanters and alchemists that could create anything the customer asked for as long as they paid for it and could be used for that purpose by any layman, even without any understanding of [magic](Lore_Magic.md).

Even an enchanted sword that could "cleave the world in twain" is said to have been theoretically possible to make through these original tools, obstructed only by the enormous cost in magicka and gold. This level of accessibility is said to have resulted in a number of incidents where laymen created dangerous arcane objects beyond their ken. Galerion is said to have eventually realized the danger his original enchanting and alchemy tools posed, and to have had them destroyed and replaced by less automated versions which required users to have some understanding of what they were doing to use them. Because of these contributions to making the study of magic more accessible and free, Galerion is described in some sources as the originator of the practice of magic in its modern form.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Feyfolken-8)</sup>

### Uses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/OB-quest-Alchemy_Training.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Alchemy_Training.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Alchemy_Training.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Alchemy_Training.jpg) The alchemist [Sinderion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Sinderion), plying his trade [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/OB-ingredient-Refined_Frost_Salts.jpg/200px-OB-ingredient-Refined_Frost_Salts.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-ingredient-Refined_Frost_Salts.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-ingredient-Refined_Frost_Salts.jpg) Unrefined and refined samples of frost salts [Asliel Direnni](Lore_Asliel_Direnni.md) formulated alchemy into an art and science, and even pioneered a way to combat the spread of [vampirism](Lore_Vampire.md) by making a potion which poisoned the victim's blood and killed vampires who attempted to feast on them. The formula to make such potions has since been lost to history.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DRD-9)</sup> It is possible to attain a form of vampirism, a condition normally associated with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), by brewing a form of the virus. Such an application of the art of alchemy was used to treat a [bloodborne disease](Lore_Disease.md#Ostraekeratic_Fever) during the Interregnum.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESONorasea-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HRJ-11)</sup>

[Gyron Vardengroet](Lore_Gyron_Vardengroet.md) managed to create and use a potion of immortality. Unfortunately, his process in creating the potion of immortality required him to test multiple different candidates. The successful potion did not give him any obvious sign that he was no longer aging besides his realization after several years that he had not aged. Thus, he is unsure of which of the many potions he took was the correct one and continued looking for the correct combination.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TS-12)</sup>

Alchemy has been combined with witch magic to form arcane storms.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OH-13)</sup>

Skinmail is an armor of myth and legend created after decades' worth of attentive work in both alchemy and [enchantment](Lore_Enchantment.md). They are the result of vials of experimental liquids, which, upon contact with skin, will harden it and produce plate-like coverings to the wearer's liking. Skinmail was created by an Altmer alchemist, who performed profane experiments on live subjects to reach the results they desired.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

The process of creating Stone Husks, vessels capable of freeing souls from [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), also involves alchemy.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Fennorian TLOB-15)</sup> Transformations can be achieved using potions. The mad alchemist Arkasis created potions that allowed non-lycanthropes to transform into [werewolf behemoths](Lore_Lycanthropy.md). These potions did not infect the subjects with actual lycanthropy.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SG-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MTCA-17)</sup>

Mycologists are alchemists who specialise in mushrooms and fungi. They may be employed to nurture and maintain existing Telvanni mushroom towers created by [mycoturges](Lore_Mycoturges.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

## Alchemy in the Games
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*: There are [potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Potions), but none can be made.
- *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*: [Potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Potions) can be made, but only with the aid of a [Potion Maker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Potion_Maker).
- *[Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)*: There are [potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Items#Potions), but none can be made.
- *[Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)*: [Potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Potions) are sublimated with ingredients, though most combinations have no effect.
- *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*: [Potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Potions) can be made with the proper [apparatus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Alchemy_Apparatus): mortars and pestles, alembics, retorts, and calcinators.
- *[Dawnstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Dawnstar:Dawnstar)*: Among the [Magic Items](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Dawnstar:Magic_Items) are a handful of potions, but none can be made.
- *[Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Shadowkey)*: Among the [Consumables](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Consumables) are some potions, but none can be made.
- *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*: [Potions and poisons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Potions) can be made with the proper [apparatus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Alchemy_Apparatus): mortar & pestles, alembics, retorts, and calcinators.
- *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)*: [Potions and poisons](Skyrim_Potions.md) can be made, but only at an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md).
- *[Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*: [Potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Potions) and [poisons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Poisons) can be made, but only at an alchemist's lab.
- *[Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Blades)*: [Potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Potions) and [poisons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Poisons) can be made, but only with [Lea Jeanne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Lea_Jeanne) or [an alchemist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Alchemist).
