# Rageclaw Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/ON-npc-Lydi_Snowpelt.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Lydi_Snowpelt.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lydi_Snowpelt.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lydi_Snowpelt.jpg) Lydi Snowpelt, leader of the Rageclaws ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/ON-npc-Chieftain_Frostfang.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chieftain_Frostfang.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chieftain_Frostfang.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chieftain_Frostfang.jpg) Chieftain Frostfang The Rageclaw Clan was one of the [clans](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), a region in western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The Reachfolk of the Rageclaw Clan were known to domesticate a breed of stout, battle-ready [bears](Lore_Bear.md). These bears were trained from an early age and bound to a specific clansman or clan family. Clan members were known to imitate their animal companions in many ways.

A matriarchal society in nature, the protection of young clan members was also of the utmost importance. It was not uncommon for entire families within the Rageclaw to go into battle over the slightest threat to their children. They were known for overtaking and absorbing other smaller clans, converting them to the " way of the claw." Women new to the clan enjoyed tremendous control and freedom, and often appreciated the transition to the Rageclaw Clan. Male warriors, on the other hand, were pitted against grown bears in one-on-one combat, needing to earn their place in the clan by forcing an ursine to submit. They were rarely successful, as the trials for those unfamiliar with these massive beasts were often very dangerous.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-COTR-1)</sup>

Under the leadership of chieftain [Lydi Snowpelt](Lore_Lydi_Snowpelt.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WTH-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LS-3)</sup> [Stral Blackthroat](Lore_Stral_Blackthroat.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONVigrod-4)</sup> and chieftain [Frostfang](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostfang)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONVigrod-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CF-5)</sup> they allied with two other Reachfolk clans: the [Boneshapers](Lore_Boneshaper_Clan.md) and the [Stonetalons](Lore_Stonetalon_Clan.md), and signed the treaty on 3rd of the Frost Fall in 2E 582.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TOTTC-6)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> The clans aligned themselves with the [Cult of the Black Worm](Lore_Cult_of_the_Black_Worm.md), which promised power and blessings of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Online:Bermund-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DU-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WN-9)</sup> Rageclaw Clan too part in an invasion of [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) as part of this alliance in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WTH-2)</sup>

While their allies of the Boneshaper Clan surrounded the [Shor's Stone](Lore_Shor%27s_Stone.md), choked off passage between [Stony Basin](Lore_Stony_Basin.md) and the [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) valley,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WTH-2)</sup> kidnapped local miners, and used tunnels as a site for their rituals for the resurrection of [Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md)'s spirit,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BTS-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTM-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Unearthed-12)</sup> they stormed the [Fallowstone Hall](Lore_Fallowstone_Hall.md) and stole the legendary [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), which was necessary for the completion of the rites. This attack was led by Chieftain Frostfang. The Reachfolk killed numerous recruits that stayed to protect the hall as well as its sage, [Tirora](Lore_Tirora.md). Most survivors fled to the shrines while the Reachmen sealed themselves in the inner vault. Harbinger [Vigrod Wraithbane](Lore_Vigrod_Wraithbane.md) and the [King's Arrow](Lore_The_Vestige.md) led the fight to retake Fallowstone Hall, first by rallying any survivors. When the Companions realized that the Reachmen placed a ward on the vault, the King's Arrow made contact with Tirora's spirit, who dispelled the barrier. While the Harbinger fended off the Reachmen, the King's Arrow defeated Chieftain Frostfang but found Wuuthrad gone.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-STH-13)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONVigrod-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTS-14)</sup> They also defeated his relative, Lessa Frostfang.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-STH-13)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LF-15)</sup>

Ultimately, the Reachmen alliance was defeated. Their allied forces, led by [Stral Blackthroat](Lore_Stral_Blackthroat.md) were vanquished in the [Vernim Woods](Lore_Vernim_Woods.md),<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONShroud-16)</sup> and their attack on the Boulderfall Pass was stopped.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SOTH-17)</sup> Rageclaw chieftain Lydi, together with other clan leaders was defeated by the [King's Arrow](Lore_The_Vestige.md) at the encampment west of Boulderfall Pass, as per request of dying [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) soldier Melril.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RTC-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Melril-19)</sup> The future of the clan is unknown, but their invasion of the Rift was thwarted.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONShroud-16)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TN-20)</sup> The Worm Cult persisted after the defeat of the Reachfolk, but they were also defeated eventually.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SS-21)</sup>
