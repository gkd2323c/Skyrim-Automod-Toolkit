[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Dead Men's Respite, occasionally spelled **Dead Man's Respite**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAerolf-1)</sup> and also known as the **Bards Tomb**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRViarmo-2)</sup> is an ancient Nordic barrow with a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) found in the southern plains of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Located near the banks of the [River Hjaal](Lore_River_Hjaal.md), the ruins serve as the burial place of the former High King of Skyrim, [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRViarmo-2)</sup>

## History
Dead Men's Respite contains the body of Olaf One-Eye, who died in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Before his death, he imprisoned the bard and his most vocal critic, [Svaknir](Lore_Svaknir.md) in a hidden chamber at the north end of the ruins and destroyed copies of [King Olaf's Verse](Lore_King_Olaf%27s_Verse.md), a piece of the [Poetic Edda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poetic_Edda) that calls Olaf's success into question.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OATD-3)</sup> Svaknir died in prison with the last remaining copy of the verse in his hands. Olaf passed away in [1E 452](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_452) and he was interred into the biggest part of the barrow. The tomb had been sealed ever since and the key to the inner sanctum, the Ruby Dragon Claw was displayed in the barrow's foyer.

Years later in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), an [associate](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) of the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) was sent to Dead Men's Respite to locate King Olaf's Verse after the Burning of King Olaf Festival was canceled in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). The associate came across the [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of Svaknir, who opened the gate to the ruins and guided them to his grave, where the verse was safely preserved. Deeper into the delve, the two faced the reanimated King Olaf and a horde of [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md). Olaf was defeated and Svaknir's spirit was able to ascend to [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md). The verse was brought to Solitude and the Festival had successfully commenced.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRTending-4)</sup>
