# Stone Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/ON-npc-Xit-Xaht_Stoneshaper_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Xit-Xaht_Stoneshaper_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Xit-Xaht_Stoneshaper_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Xit-Xaht_Stoneshaper_02.jpg) A Barrage of Stone Stone Magic is an arcane art focused on control of the [stone](Lore_The_Stone.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE-1)</sup> It should not be confused with Geomancy,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-G-2)</sup> also known as Earth Magic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Earth_Magic-3)</sup>

## Usage

### Atronachs and Golems
[Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) like [Stone Atronachs](Lore_Stone_Atronach.md) are composed of stone,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Stone_Atronach-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> as are certain artificially created [golems](Lore_Golem.md#Stone). This includes [Direnni](Lore_Clan_Direnni.md) [Stone Golems](Lore_Stone_Golem.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONN-6)</sup> and [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) animated statues known as Ayleid Guardians or Stone Watchers.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AG-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SW-8)</sup> Gargoyles, used by both vampires and wizards from various civilizations such as the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md), [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), Ayleids, and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), are also stone constructs.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gargoyle ESO-9)</sup> Other examples included the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md)'s [Stone Husks](Lore_Stone_Husk.md) and the Argonians' [Voskrona Guardians](Lore_Voskrona_Guardian.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SH-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VG-11)</sup>

### Argonian
*The stones are ours to command!*
—Zatzu<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Zatzu-12)</sup> [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Xit-Xaht](Lore_Xit-Xaht.md) tribe were known for their Stoneshapers and Root-Masons who wielded stone magic. They used this ability in battle, launching stone-based barrages as part of their combat tactics.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-XX-13)</sup> The tribe's [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), [Tsono-Xuhil](Lore_Tsono-Xuhil.md), was revered as the "Mother Root, Father Stone." It was called upon by the Tree-Minder [Na-Kesh](Lore_Na-Kesh.md) to bestow upon her the strength of both the root and the stone. Given Na-Kesh's deep connection to the Hist, it's possible that her invocation of Tsono-Xuhil could also be tied to [Hist-magic](Lore_Hist-magic.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Na-Kesh-14)</sup>

### Mages Guild
The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) possessed some knowledge of stone-shaping. A mage was once asked to stone-shape the hair of the [Mermaid](Lore_Mermaid.md) of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), which she refused to do.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Laurina-15)</sup>

### Orc
The Chief Urzikh of [Mor Khazgur](Lore_Mor_Khazgur.md) was known for her expertise in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CU-16)</sup> One of her creations, a stone-dissolving concoction, was referred to as "stone magic".<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Greymoor-17)</sup>

### Yokudan
The Hiradirge were a faction of rogue [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) and stone magic experts who were theorized to have played a role in the sinking of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), at least according to certain theories regarding the continent's destruction after their defeat in battle in [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-UOL-18)</sup> However, other theories existed as well. [Memory Stones](Lore_Memory_Stone.md) also trace their origin to Yokuda, but the exact link between them and the Hiradirge remains unclear, aside from their mention in the same texts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-19)</sup>
