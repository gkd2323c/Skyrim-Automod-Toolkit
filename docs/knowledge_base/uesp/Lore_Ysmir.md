# Ysmir

— *[36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 9](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_9)* Ysmir **, Dragon of the North** is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) Name of Kings, a title bestowed upon several heroes throughout history, typically by the [voices](Lore_Thu%27um.md) of the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo F-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arngeir-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAH-4)</sup> In his sermons, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) claimed that Ysmir always appears as a great bearded king.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOV-5)</sup>

- [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) High King<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Balgruuf-6)</sup> of ancient Skyrim (and later undead warrior) who was also called "Ysmir Kingmaker", "Ysmir the Grey Wind", and "Ysmir, Dragon of the North."<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAH-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSOKW-7)</sup>
- [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), also known as Talos Stormcrown or "Ysmir, Dragon of the North."<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo F-2)</sup> Dragonborn Founder of the [Third Empire of Man](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and newest member of the [Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines), taking the place of Ninth.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Heimskr-8)</sup> Some heretical stories claim Wulharth was the reason the title "Talos, or Stormcrown" was granted to [Hjalti Early-Beard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hjalti_Early-Beard), because Wulfharth was the literal crown of Storms that shrouded the young Dragonborn at the battle of [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAH-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1C-9)</sup>
- [The Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), a hero who was recognized as "Ysmir, Dragon of the North" by the Greybeards in 4E 201.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arngeir-3)</sup> The Greybeards also referred to them with Talos' title of "Stormcrown".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arngeir-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SREinarth-10)</sup>
- [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), an ancient hero of mankind who fought alongside [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus) as the champion of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md). Known by various other names and titles, one being Ysmir.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-11)</sup> Though some have claimed another bearer of title may have been conflated with Pelinal.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-12)</sup>

## Worship and Legends
*Long has the Stormcrown languished, with no worthy brow to sit upon. By our breath we bestow it now to you in the name of Kyne, in the name of Shor, and in the name of Atmora of Old. You are Ysmir now, the Dragon of the North, hearken to it.*
—The [Greybeards](Skyrim_Arngeir.md) bestowing upon the Last Dragonborn the title of Ysmir and Stormcrown Ysmir is considered part of the Nordic pantheon and has been described as the Nordic aspect of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), who withstood the power of the Greybeards' voices long enough to hear their prophecy. It is also being said that after this event, many Nords could not look upon him without seeing a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo F-2)</sup> The Nords who resided in [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) were known to prefer worshipping Ysmir as their dragon as opposed to the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> It is said that Ysmir, or "Talos", as the newest god of their Pantheon, is now one of the Twilight Gods that shall survive into the next [Kalpa](Lore_Kalpa.md), the other being [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DATN-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-GTNTR-15)</sup> Nords commonly swear by Ysmir in their everyday language.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRGeneric Dialogue-16)</sup>

One story speaks to a king of [men](Lore_Men.md) and dragons alike by the name of **Ysmir the Forefather** who upon death, ascended to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) and took his place in the heavens as [The Warrior Constellation](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Although scholars, such as [Caecilus Bursio](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caecilus_Bursio), were skeptical if the story was truly literal, instead finding it more likely that Ysmir was associated with the Warrior constellation as it represented his own strength as a warrior, and became only further associated with it after his death.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HACBAYQ-17)</sup>
