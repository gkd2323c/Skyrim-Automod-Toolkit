# Animals


The "Animals" category includes a wide variety of creatures, with different degrees of aggressiveness (some have no attack capability at all), and many different habitats. The characteristics common to all animals are that they are modeled upon real animals that exist (or existed formerly) on Earth, and that they rely exclusively upon physical attacks using their bodies (claws, paws, hooves, fangs, horns, etc.) The damage values listed on this page under "Attacks" are all for melee-type damage.

Animals have no true magical capabilities; the only comparable features in animals are:

- Resistance to elements. Several animals have [resistance to frost](Skyrim_Resist_Frost.md).
- [Diseases](Skyrim_Disease.md). Most animals can be infected with (and can transmit) one or more diseases.

Animal behavior is organized into three ranks: **Non-hostile**, in which the animal will never attack unless under the influence of a spell. **Neutral**, in which the animal will only attack when provoked by proximity or direct harm. **Hostile**, in which the animal will attack on sight.

Attacking an NPC's livestock will earn you a bounty, and sometimes cause them to turn hostile. Domestic animals may report crimes and be counted as witnesses.

## Bear
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/56/SR-creature-Bear.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Bear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Bear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Bear.jpg) A Bear **[Bears](Skyrim_Bear.md)** are large, hostile enemies that will attack the player if they come too close. They are found either in the wilderness or in the lairs they make in caves. Bears can transmit [Bone Break Fever](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Break_Fever), which deducts 25 points from the player's stamina. They are very tough, but are vulnerable to fire damage.

- Variations: Snow Bear, Cave Bear

This is a summary of information on bears. See the main [Bear](Skyrim_Bear.md) article for more detailed information.

## Chicken
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/SR-creature-Chicken.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Chicken.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Chicken.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Chicken.jpg) A Chicken **[Chickens](Skyrim_Chicken.md)** are docile, domesticated birds common throughout Skyrim, even within cities and towns. Attacking NPC-owned chickens will earn a bounty, but you can legally harvest [eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chicken%27s_Egg) from their nests for use as alchemy ingredients.

This is a summary of information on chickens. See the main [Chicken](Skyrim_Chicken.md) article for more detailed information.

## Cow
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/SR-creature-Cow.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Cow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Cow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Cow.jpg) A Cow **[Cows](Skyrim_Cow.md)** are large, docile, domesticated animals common throughout Skyrim, especially on farms. Attacking an NPC's livestock will earn you a bounty. Occasionally you may come across a cow with strange symbols on it in red or blue paint. These cows are usually following a farmer, who is bringing the cow to a giant for a sacrificial ceremony.

- Variations: [Soul](Skyrim_Soul_(cow).md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>

This is a summary of information on cows. See the main [Cow](Skyrim_Cow.md) article for more detailed information.

## Deer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/SR-creature-Elk.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Elk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Elk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Elk.jpg) A deer **[Deer](Skyrim_Deer.md)** are passive creatures found throughout Skyrim and will flee when approached or threatened. They can be hunted for [meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Venison), [pelt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Deer_Hide) and [large](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Large_Antlers) or [small](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Small_Antlers) antlers. There are two different species of deer in Skyrim, though the differences between the two are largely aesthetic. 'Deer' resemble caribou or reindeer. 'Elk', in keeping with Skyrim's menagerie of Ice Age creatures, resemble the extinct *Megaloceros*. Occasionally, either species can be heard bugling for mates.

- Variations: Elk, Vale Deer<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>
- Unique Deer: [White Stag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:White_Stag)

This is a summary of information on deer. See the main [Deer](Skyrim_Deer.md) article for more detailed information.

## Dog
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/SR-creature-Dog.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dog.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dog.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dog.jpg) A Dog **[Dogs](Skyrim_Dog.md)** are found throughout Skyrim, usually accompanying NPCs. If a dog is owned by a hostile NPC, such as a bandit, the dog will also be hostile. You can occasionally find stray dogs in the wild that can be provoked into attacking you. Huskies and Death Hounds (both added by the [Dawnguard add-on](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)) are generally found accompanying NPCs, though Death Hounds only ally themselves with [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md). See their respective articles for more detailed information.

- Variations: - Dog, [Stray Dog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stray_Dog)
- [Spectral Warhound](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spectral_Warhound)
- [Death Hound](Skyrim_Death_Hound.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>
- [Husky](Skyrim_Husky.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>

- Unique Dogs: [Barbas](Skyrim_Barbas.md), [Meeko](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Meeko), [Stump](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stump), [Tiber](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Tiber), [Torom](Skyrim_Torom.md), [Vigilance](Skyrim_Vigilance.md), [Ysgramor](Skyrim_Ysgramor_(dog).md)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/SR-creature-Horker.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Horker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horker.jpg) A Horker **[Horkers](Skyrim_Horker.md)** are sea animals, similar in appearance to a walrus, that typically live in packs of three or four. ## Horse
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/SR-creature-Horse.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Horse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horse.jpg) A Horse **[Horses](Skyrim_Horses.md)** are non-hostile animals that you can ride. They can be purchased from a stable, stolen, found abandoned, borrowed for work done, or rewarded in a quest. An owned horse will aid you in combat. If you haven't sided with either faction, a horse can be obtained for free after the player clears [Fort Greymoor](Skyrim_Fort_Greymoor.md). Wait for the Imperial Army to take over, and there will be two horses in the courtyard that the player can take. All horses participate in combat if you cast a Courage spell on them. Horses can traverse steeper inclines than the player can, although a fall that would kill a horse is much shorter than one that would kill the player. The strongest and fastest horse in Skyrim is [Shadowmere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmere), which can be obtained through the [Dark Brotherhood](Skyrim_Dark_Brotherhood.md) questline.

- Variations: [Wild Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wild_Horse)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Wild_Horses.md)</sup>

This is a summary of information on horses. See the main [Horses](Skyrim_Horses.md) article for more detailed information.

## Mammoth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg) A Mammoth **[Mammoths](Skyrim_Mammoth.md)** are huge, lumbering animals that can most often be found in [Giant Camps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Giant_Camps) or being herded across the tundra by [giants](Skyrim_Giant.md). Mammoths are usually not hostile, although they will fight back if attacked and may become aggressive if approached. If you attack a mammoth, the giants herding it will retaliate. Mammoths have high health and a powerful melee attack, so attacking one at a low level is not recommended, although if proper caution is exercised the mammoth can be one of the most essential skill-leveling creatures in Skyrim. Due to their poor ability to cross over certain terrain the mammoth can be easily killed by making use of distanced combat techniques. Mammoth corpses may be looted for [Mammoth Tusks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mammoth_Tusk) and [Mammoth Snouts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mammoth_Snout). Of note, mammoths are the earliest creatures you encounter which can fill Grand Soul Gems.

This is a summary of information on mammoths. See the main [Mammoth](Skyrim_Mammoth.md) article for more detailed information.

Skyrim mudcrabs are hardier than their Cyrodiilic cousins, having nearly triple their health.

## Notes
- Normally-passive animals may attack and/or appear as hostile on your compass due to the [spriggan](Skyrim_Spriggan.md) Call of the Wild ability. This is the only time when passive animal attack values come into play, as they normally flee if attacked.
- In addition to their loot, most creatures also have a 10% chance of carrying minor loot, including leveled rings, gems, and gold. Goats have a 20% chance, while chickens, foxes, and rabbits do not carry any minor loot.
- Every animal, even those that are not hostile, has a red glow when using the spell [Detect Life](Skyrim_Detect_Life_(spell).md).
- With the [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) add-on, some animals can be made into house pets for [adopted children](Skyrim_Adoption.md).
- Some magic effects apply exclusively to animals, such as [Kyne's Peace](Skyrim_Kyne%27s_Peace.md), [Command Animal](Skyrim_Command_Animal.md), or [Huntsman's Prowess](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Huntsman%27s_Prowess). For these purposes, in addition to the above list, [bone wolves](Skyrim_Bone_Wolf_(creature).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Bone_Wolf.md)</sup>, [chauruses](Skyrim_Chaurus.md), [elytra nymphs](Skyrim_Elytra_Nymph.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Saints_%26_Seducers.md)</sup>, [frostbite spiders](Skyrim_Frostbite_Spider.md), [netches](Skyrim_Netch.md), [nix-hounds](Skyrim_Nix-Hound_(creature).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Nix-Hound.md)</sup> and [trolls](Skyrim_Troll.md) are counted as animals.
