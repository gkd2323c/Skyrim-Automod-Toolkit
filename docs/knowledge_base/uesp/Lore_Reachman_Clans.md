# Reachman Clans

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/LG-cardart-Doomfang_Ally.png/200px-LG-cardart-Doomfang_Ally.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Doomfang_Ally.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Doomfang_Ally.png) A member of the Doomfang Clan The **[Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md)** live in scores of fractured **clans**, each with their own traditions and character. There is no uniform tradition among the clans, though many of them worship the same deities and share the same customs. Some clans are nomadic, opting to live in caves, old forts, and ruins, or building temporary shelters. Others choose to settle in dhuns, fortified hilltop villages. Clans can be made up of people who are related by blood or individuals who adopt clan names to mark their allegiance. New clans frequently form as like-minded individuals come together and circumstances change. These enterprising bands may to settle new land and follow herds of prey, or raid neighboring territories to plunder their resources. In this way, the clans are fluid, breaking up and reforming over the course of history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

Many clans try to live in relative peace, though some, such as the [Boneshaper Clan](Lore_Boneshaper_Clan.md), are overtly hostile towards outsiders.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup> The Reachfolk also adorn their bodies with a broad variety of markings that are specific to the clan they belong to.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESORichien_Thelin-2)</sup>

Clanless Reachfolk often face discrimination. Lives of the exiled Reachmen are often seen as worthless.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Leven-3)</sup> The Reach is dangerous for those without clans and career options for clanless Reachfolk in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) are limited.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESONA-4)</sup> Certain factions such as [Wayward Guardians](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md) reject that prejudice and offer their protection and services to those without clans. According to them the status of an exile need not to define people and that stories of clanless Reachfolk can take a different path. They also accept clanless Reachfolk into its ranks.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Leven-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Callan-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ardanir-6)</sup> Certain clans, such as Shunned Ones are composed entirely of the outcasts.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONAchdazan-7)</sup> Clanless Reachfolk can be accepted again into other clans.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gorrof-8)</sup>

Clans and Reachfolk that live outside of Markarth are known as the [Wilders](Lore_Wilders.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONArllonal-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONAnslettar-10)</sup> The Reach Clans have conflicted with the [Glenmoril Wyrds](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md) of the [Ilessan Hills](Lore_Ilessan_Hills.md) and the Viridian Woods for thousands of years.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TGW-11)</sup>

## The Chief-of-Chiefs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg) [Cannear](Lore_Cannear.md), the Chief-of-Chiefs circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Chief-of-Chiefs is an aspect of Reach tradition that transcends clan boundaries. This individual is usually a clan-chief who has earned the respect of allies and enemies alike during their tenure. In a way, the Chief-of-Chiefs is a sort of "chief emeritus"—though they've stepped down from their position as chief of their own clan, they are highly respected and wield a great deal of authority among all but the most headstrong clans. The Chief-of-Chiefs serves as an arbiter and a peacemaker: they settle disputes between clans and help feuding clans repair their relationships with each other. Occasionally, the clans will unite to act against a threat. When this happens, the Chief-of-Chiefs temporarily assumes control of the participating clans' combined army.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-POTR-12)</sup> Overseeing the [handfasting vows](Lore_Marriage.md#Reachmen) is one of their duties.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CCC-13)</sup> The Chief-of-Chiefs maintains their position until they die or choose to step down.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-POTR-12)</sup>

### Known Chiefs-of-Chiefs
- **[Cannear](Lore_Cannear.md)** — Chief-of-Chiefs originating from the River-Elk Clan. She was active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).

## Clans

### Extinct Clans
- The **[Blackdrake Clan](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md)** — Few in number. They rose to prominence under the warlord Durcorach, who rallied the Reach beneath his banner and conquered Cyrodiil, forming the Longhouse Dynasty. Durcorach's closest relatives and friends in the Reach took his appellation, "the Black Drake", as their own clan name. Other clans show the Blackdrakes a degree of respect for Durcorach's legacy, which is more respect than a clan that small would otherwise command.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>
- The **[Horn-Stride Clan](Lore_Horn-Stride_Clan.md)** — An extinct clan that fought against the clan led by the Chief [Rowolan](Lore_Rowolan.md), who claimed descendance from the [Demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Dearola](Lore_Dearola.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTWT-14)</sup>
- The **[Stranglehollow Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stranglehollow_Clan)** — A clan that died out by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The Stranglehollows toasted each other with a drink mixed using the fingerbone of an ancestor.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONBodgrannagh-15)</sup>

### Extant Clans

- The **[Boldclaw Clan](Lore_Boldclaw_Clan.md)** — A clan that fosters a deep respect for Namira.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ND-20)</sup>
- The **[Boneshaper Clan](Lore_Boneshaper_Clan.md)** — A border clan that allied with the Cult of the Black Worm and invaded [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They have a number of unusual rites involving thorny vines and plants. Their name originated from their tradition of lacing thorny vines through the skeletons of their ritual sacrifices. The vines they grow do not appear to be native to the Reach, but they cultivate the plants expertly. When going on a raid or into battle, they create effigies out of vines. Some of their rituals made use of corpses, although the clan forbade necromancy.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-COTRAG-21)</sup>
- The **[Cinder-Heart Clan](Lore_Cinder-Heart_Clan.md)** — A warlike clan found near Markarth. They are known for burning their captives alive. They prepare Briarhearts by filling the victim's empty chest cavity with hot coals. They worship Molag Bal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

- The **[Ragebound](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ragebound)** — A group of elite riders and their steeds known for their fury.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-RH-73)</sup>
- The **[Witch-Knights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witch-Knights)**<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-WKD-74)</sup> — A group of Reachfolk who decided to breed Witch-Knight Charger horses after they were profoundly impressed by the power and splendor of the Breton mounted knights during the invasion of High Rock.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-WKC-75)</sup>

### Unspecified Clans
Several clans have faded into obscurity, with their names either lost to history or left unspecified in various texts.

- ** Unspecified forgotten clan** — A clan that disappeared, leaving only one survivor. This lone survivor emerged after spending a night at one of Mehrunes Dagon's shrines, marked by a thorny crown on her brow and a solemn mission.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-DTA-76)</sup>

- **Unnamed rabbit portent clan** — A clan that believed that their ancestors sent white rabbits to deliver portents or messages.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ESOPC-77)</sup>

- **Unnamed Ragebound horse tribe** — A tribe that, according to legend, earned the first Ragebound Horse from Hircine's favor by relentlessly pursuing a vampire on foot, even in the darkest of nights.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-RS-78)</sup>

- **Warlord [Vund](Lore_Vund.md)'s unnamed clan** — A group that attacked [Dragon Bridge](Lore_Dragon_Bridge.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-DATDB-79)</sup>

- **Chief Kynaer's clan** — A clan led by Kynaer in 2E 582.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-CK-80)</sup>

- **Chief Ulvfae's clan** — A clan led by Ulvfae in 2E 582.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-CU-81)</sup>

- **Chief [Meroch](Lore_Meroch.md)'s clan** — A possibly fictional clan led by Meroch.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-RBT-82)</sup>

- **Unnamed clan of Shadowgreen** — A group that was active in Shadowgreen in 2E 582.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TMB-83)</sup>

- **[Bjora](Lore_Bjora.md)'s clan, descended from [Faolan](Lore_Faolan.md)** — A clan massacred by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of [Karthwatch](Lore_Karthwatch.md), triggering the retribution that devastated the town in 2E 582. Led by Madovan, they were allied with the Icereach Coven.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-TBOOK-84)</sup>

- **Chief [Noldan](Lore_Noldan.md)'s clan** — An ancient clan from the times of Empress Hestra's conquest of the Reach around 1E 1030.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-ESOA-85)</sup>

- **Chief [Fiasof](Lore_Fiasof.md)'s clan** — A legendary clan cursed by Hircine and ultimately killed due to a mistake of their chieftain.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ONVC-86)</sup>

- **Chief [Rowolan](Lore_Rowolan.md)'s clan** — An ancient extinct clan supposedly descended from the [Demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Dearola](Lore_Dearola.md). They were enemies of the Horn-Stride Clan.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTWT-14)</sup>

- **Gorrof's clan** — A clan that was slaughtered before 2E 582, with its sole survivor, Gorof, joining Crow-Eyes.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gorrof-8)</sup>

- **Boyoagh's clan** — A clan that was slaughtered before 2E 582, with one of its survivors, Boyoagh, joining Wayward Guardians.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Boyoagh-87)</sup>

- **Unnamed Briarheart clan** — A clan that captured [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md), and brought him to Markarth to meet the Ard Caddach.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Emperor-88)</sup>

- **[Tancia](Lore_Tancia.md) and [Ulecia](Lore_Ulecia.md)'s clan** — A clan featured in *The Sister Who Turned Her Skin Red* tale.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-STOTDB3-89)</sup>
