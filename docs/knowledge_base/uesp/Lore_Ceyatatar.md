## History
| - *After many hours of difficult travel through tangled hawthorn hells and limberlosts, I was suddenly struck dumb by the aspect of five pure white columns rising from a jade-green mound of vines to perfect V-shaped arches and graceful capitals towering above the verdant forest growth.* —Alexandre Hetrard, *[Glories and Laments](Lore_Glories_and_Laments.md)* |
| --- |

In its prime, Ceyatatar was a remarkable city which prospered through trade with the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) around the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ACOV-3)</sup> According to Cyrodilic legend, "Ceya-Tar" was the seat of the Fire King [Hadhuul](Lore_Hadhuul.md), a particularly cruel Ayleid ruler. In his realms, slaves were drugged with foul concoctions that induced visions of torment, and their children were set aflame for nighttime tiger sport.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> The sorcerer-king was later slain in single combat on the granite steps of his city by [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOP-5)</sup>

King Hadhuul's spirit, along with spirits of his army and some [Flame Atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md), reappeared at Ceyatatar during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), but these invaders were banished by soldiers fighting in the ongoing [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) before they could harm the nearby settlement of [Vlastarus](Lore_Vlastarus.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ON-6)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins of Ceyatatar had been taken over by a group of conjurers and their summoned [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), who made use of the ruin's ancient gas chambers to hold and execute captives.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oblivion-2)</sup>
