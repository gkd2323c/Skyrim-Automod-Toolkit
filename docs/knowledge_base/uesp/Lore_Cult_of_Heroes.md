# Cult of Heroes

The Cult of Heroes is an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) cult devoted to the veneration of heroes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>

It was one of many philosophical cults in [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md) society and was notably among the most famous by [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), alongside other prominent cults such as the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), the [Cult](Lore_Talos_Cult.md) of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), and the [Cult of Emperor Zero](Lore_Cult_of_Emperor_Zero.md). Like some other Nibenese cults, which practiced [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) and held esoteric customs, it was regarded as peculiar from the viewpoint of the [Colovians](Lore_Colovian.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>

Similar practices weren't unheard of outside of Nibenay. [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) were known for their hero-cults. According to *[Reflections on Cult Worship](Lore_Reflections_on_Cult_Worship.md)* by [Cuseius Plecia](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Cuseius_Plecia) Nordic hero-cults offer a significant counterbalance to the prevailing secularism of [the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Empire). The Imperial Cult of Tiber Septim was considered one of these hero-cults, particularly strong and personal among the military, provincial colonists, and newly assimilated foreigners.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ROCW-2)</sup>
