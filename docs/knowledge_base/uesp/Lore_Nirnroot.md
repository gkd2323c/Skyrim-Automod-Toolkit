Nirnroot is a very rare plant that grows near water. It is a broad-leaved plant and possess a turquoise green coloration on its leaves and root bulb. The most notable traits of Nirnroot includes the low hum it produces, its glow, and its unique magical properties. Nirnroot does not require sunlight to grow, so it can be found in locations above or below ground.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Its seeds are spread by birds and other creatures.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Nirnroot is hard to cultivate, and prefers acidic soil to grow.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Avrusa_Sarethi-4)</sup> Despite that it also grows well in the ashen soils of [the Systres](Lore_Systres.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFOV-5)</sup>

Originally yellow-leaved, the plant mutated drastically during the [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md) event of [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668). Due to its magical nature, it sensed as a species its impending extinction and used the [ash salts](Lore_Ash_Salts.md) found falling from the sky as a new source of food. This allowed the plant to live without sunlight. As the ash content of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s soils diminished, so too did the plant's numbers, leading it to the brink of extinction.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TNM-6)</sup>

The [Crimson Nirnroot](Lore_Crimson_Nirnroot.md) subspecies possesses red leaves rather than green. It can be found exclusively in the caverns of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Due to its rarity and the dangers associated with obtaining samples, most consider its existence to be a tall tale.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Sarandil-9)</sup>

The droning noise produced by Nirnroot plants is known to cause headaches in some people. Handling it has also been known to cause rashes.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-10)</sup>

## History
The plant became a subject of passion for the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) master [alchemist](Lore_Alchemy.md) [Sinderion](Lore_Sinderion.md). Worried about its future, he presented his research on it and his concerns of its impending extinction to the [Imperial Alchemy Symposium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Alchemy_Symposium) in the final years of the Third Era.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNMO-11)</sup> However, the Symposium chose not to provide Sinderion with the assistance he requested. After receiving help with collecting samples from the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SYR-12)</sup> Sinderion's renewed research led to a second presentation at the Imperial Alchemy Symposium, where he was once again turned down.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TNM-6)</sup>

Undeterred, Sinderion proceeded with his research alone, and managed to successfully cultivate Nirnroot by [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48). After he learned of the existence of the Crimson Nirnroot subspecies by procuring a sample, he traveled to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) to conduct field research. While in Skyrim he also made contact with the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), and shared his knowledge of Nirnroot cultivation with the alchemist Avrusa Sarethi.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SFJ-13)</sup><sup>[†](#intnote_Relationship)</sup>

Sinderion eventually entered [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md) in pursuit of the Crimson Nirnroot circa [4E 58](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_58), where he would meet his end at the hands of the cavern's inhabitants. His work lived on however, and the knowledge he passed on was used by Avrusa Sarethi to grow Nirnroot as one of the crops on her farm. The fate of Sinderion was ultimately discovered by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who shared his final research notes and plant samples from Blackreach with Avrusa. Building on Sinderion's research, Avrusa succeeded in also cultivating the Crimson Nirnroot.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ARTYR-14)</sup>

## Usage
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/OB-flora-Nirnroot_Plant_02.jpg/200px-OB-flora-Nirnroot_Plant_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-flora-Nirnroot_Plant_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-flora-Nirnroot_Plant_02.jpg) Nirnroot, grown as a houseplant Nirnroot is mostly poisonous when consumed or used in alchemical concoctions.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-NIO-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NIS-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-NIE-17)</sup> However, it can also be used to create potions of invisibility.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NIS-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-NIE-17)</sup> There are also more complex alchemical possibilities, such as the Elixir of Exploration derived from Nirnroot. These elixirs grant a wide variety of beneficial properties useful for adventurers.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EOE-18)</sup> The Crimson Nirnroot's magical potential in alchemy is far greater than that of the common Nirnroot.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ARTYR-14)</sup>

The plant can also be used to make Nirnroot [wine](Lore_Wine.md), an [alcoholic beverage](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md) with strange alchemical properties that cause intoxication without needing to be imbibed. The true flavor of the wine is unknown; although it is often "remembered" as being delicious, no individual has ever succeeded in tasting it.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

The plant is a favorite food of the [Nibenay Bay](Lore_Nibenay_Bay.md) [ram](Lore_Ram.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> [Kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) also enjoy Nirnroot, despite it causing lethargy and addiction when consumed.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

The [singing tuber](Lore_Singing_Tuber.md), a hybrid vegetable created by the [druids](Lore_Druids.md) of [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md), is composed of nirnroot, leek, and carrot. The writer Castus Marius described as a "light, crunchy, bold flavor that left my ears ringing with a chorus of sunset colors", and claimed to need to lay down until the sensation passed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFOV-5)</sup> The [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) chef Gianna described Nirnroot as a "special seasoning" she would use in her cooking.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Consumption of the plant can cause ringing teeth, and glowing excreta.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Some people are born with a curse that makes Nirnroot taste like soap.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-10)</sup>

When magically added to molten [silver](Lore_Silver.md), the ensuing reaction causes permanent blue, silver, and black streaks.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> There is rumored to be a secret Nirnroot concoction that can be used to make cloth that is resistant to staining.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

Nirnroot's unique glow and hum leads to it often being used as a decorative houseplant.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SD-1)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Living samples of Crimson Nirnroot can also be grown indoors and are highly valued, with one being stored in the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md)'s vault in [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Sarandil-9)</sup>

The elusive nature of Nirnroot was harnessed to create Nimblegarb, a set of robes that grants the wearer lightning-fast speed. These robes were once owned by Sinderion.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>
