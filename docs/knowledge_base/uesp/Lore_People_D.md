#### Dahrk Mezalf (fl. before 1E 700)
[Dahrk Mezalf](Lore_Dahrk_Mezalf.md), also known as **Mezalf Bthungthumz**, was a prominent [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [smith](Lore_Smithing.md) and scholar. He worked under the leadership of the renowned Dwemer inventor [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md), alongside other notable figures such as [Bthuand Mzahnch](Lore_Bthuand_Mzahnch.md). He was also known for his proficiency in frost magic.

#### Count Damard Dufort (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Damard Dufort](Lore_Damard_Dufort.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) count of [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md), who resided in Castle Navire in [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md). He was the brother of [Duchess Elea Dufort](Lore_Elea_Dufort.md) and also her court mage. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) traveled to Castle Navire, investigating the [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md), and questioned Dufort, if he had any information to share. Dufort expressed suspicion of the [druids](Lore_Druids.md) of High Isle, believing that the storm that caused ships to go missing was created by environmental magic. After Elea was abducted by her steward, The Vestige rescued Elea and it was revealed that Damard was secretly the **Ascendant Magus**, the second-in-command of the Order. Elea was shocked to learn that her own brother was involved with the Order. Damard would later kidnap [Lady Arabelle Davaux](Lore_Arabelle_Davaux.md), with the Vestige rescuing Lady Arabelle and killing Dufort, halting the Ascendant Order's plans. Elea later expressed regret over the loss of her brother, but she realized there was no other way.

#### Darius Shano (fl. 3E)
According to myth, [Darius Shano](Lore_Darius_Shano.md) was a prolific [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) writer who lived in [Daenia](Lore_Daenia_(duchy).md) sometime in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was a subject of a wager between [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) and [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) on who could make Darius be more hated by the populace. From the age of nineteen, for ten years his dreams were imbued by Vaermina with scenes of abject horror and he went on to write about those dreams, causing disgust and sensation for many of his contemporaries.

For the next ten years, Sheogorath severed his link to Vaermina, putting an end to his source of inspiration. As time went by, he began to think Vaermina was never there to supplement his dreams, which led him to question the existence of [gods](Lore_Gods.md) in general. Darius, in turn, started to write extensively on the foolishness of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s faithful, serfs and nobility alike. Naturally, this produced actual hatred for Darius, unrivaled by that made from his previous works.

This culminated, ten years later, with his final work – "*The Noblest Fool*" – in which he ridiculed [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s status as the [Ninth Divine](Lore_Nine_Divines.md). The king of Daenia, himself a victim of ridicule by Darius' philosophical works, used this as a pretext to have Darius executed, causing Vaermina to lose her wager with Sheogorath. Somehow [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) heard about his tale in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).

#### Darloc Brae, the Golden Beast of Anequina (fl. 1E 5th century)

Anequina's martial traditions began with Darloc Brae's reign. #### Grandmaster Delmene Hlaalu (1E ? - 2E?)
**Grandmaster** [Delmene Hlaalu](Lore_Delmene_Hlaalu.md) was a [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) politician who lived at the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was the Grandmaster of the [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) who led the house in the early years of the Second Era, during the period of history directly after the end of the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920).

Delmene, who was described as an old man, wrested power from the previous house elder in a "bloodless" coup. Even though the coup was generally referred to as bloodless at least six men were killed when Delmene was taking over the leadership of House Hlaalu. During his leadership House Hlaalu amassed great wealth and prestige, exploiting the state of the Northeastern Tamriel after the Four-Score War. This period was known as Hlaalu's age of gilded glory.

#### Denogorath (fl. 2E 582)
[Denogorath](Lore_Denogorath.md) the **Dread Archivist** and **Promulgator of Mandatory Doctrine**, was a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) servant of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Their works included the [guidelines of armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crafting_Motif_17__Xivkyn_Style) for [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) Daedra, and detailed dossiers on the nature of various Daedric species, such as [Cold-Flame Atronachs](Lore_Cold-Flame_Atronach.md) and [Daedric Titans](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md).

Their writings were frequently referenced and studied by scholars and mages with a particular interest in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), including [Arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md) [Martina Abor](Lore_Martina_Abor.md) and Master [Malkhest](Lore_Malkhest.md) of the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md).

#### Dervenin, High Priest of Mania (fl. 3E 433 - 4E 201)
[Dervenin](Lore_Dervenin.md) (or **Dervenin the Mad**) is a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and the former High Priest of [Mania](Lore_Mania.md) in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). He preached the Manic beliefs of [Arden-Sul](Lore_Arden-Sul.md) in the Sacellum of [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md). His rival was [Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arctus), the Priest of [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md). Dervenin came to power within the Sacellum at the whim of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md); he could only preach his Manic teachings if the [Cold Flame of Agnon](Lore_Cold_Flame_of_Agnon.md) was lit for Mania. He was responsible for aiding anyone trying to replace the Duke of Mania, which requires a sacrifice of the current duke's [Greenmote](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenmote) -tainted blood to Arden-Sul.

Eventually, he no longer held his position as High Priest of Mania. He instead became more of a personal servant and plaything for his master, who would play cruel jokes on him.

#### Deslandra (fl. [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580))
[Deslandra](Lore_Deslandra.md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mage of many talents and great charisma who attempted to enact an event known as the Great Nullification, which would forever sever the flow of [magicka](Lore_Magic.md) and its influences on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), as well as the connections that lead to other planes of existence like [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and the [Void](Lore_Void.md). Some speculated the consequences of such an event, with one theory being that it would cut off communication with [deities](Lore_Gods.md), but she felt the benefits outweighed the potential fallout. Her reasoning for attempting to do so was that she believed that many of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s atrocities were caused with magic, with it often being how cults gain power, [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) influence spreads, and the weak are supernaturally coerced. She and her cult, the Soldiers of the Void, infiltrated various factions, gaining intel and pitting potential rivals against each other to further their own goals.

A variety of accounts make the exact outcomes of her plans and her fate uncertain.

#### Divayth Fyr (?b - ?d)
[Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) is a 4,000-year-old powerful [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) sorcerer, whose presence was said to be sensed in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) from as far away as [Dranil Kir](Lore_Dranil_Kir.md), near the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). He is a member of Great [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), though he generally stays out of House politics. Fyr isn't an active member of the Telvanni Council, acting more as a consultant or advisor to them. However, he respects their command and will carry out most requests laid down by them. As a **Master of Secrets** and a neutral overseer of the Old Ways, he was also once a member of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) and seeks balance.

Divayth Fyr is sometimes compared to renowned mages such as [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) and [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md). He reportedly views Galerion as a talented figure who squandered his potential by dedicating it to the goals of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md). In contrast, Fyr shares similar views with Shalidor regarding the role of mages in society; however, he considers Shalidor to have been influenced by what he describes as "short-sighted sentimentality." Shalidor is also said to owe Fyr a substantial debt, which Fyr intends to collect in the future.

In the Second Era he was at least sometimes present at and around the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), where he was associated with Professor [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) and Doctor [Alfidia Lupus](Lore_Alfidia_Lupus.md) and may have intended and arranged the demise of both. He was also known to be a generous patron to the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md). Fyr has a great respect for the Imperial Throne, even though he may not believe in it. His personal connection to the throne gives him access to Imperial facilities which house documents, and as such are of great interest. In all, the Telvanni Council and the Imperial Throne are treated quite the same in Fyr's eyes, giving him the nickname of *Flower Child of Tamriel*. In the Third Era, Fyr lived in his tower of [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md) in the [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) along with his four "daughters" (female clones of himself), [Alfe](Lore_Alfe_Fyr.md), Beyte, Delte and Uupse Fyr.

Divayth Fyr regularly wears a set of [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) armor reminiscent of armor used by other members of House Telvanni. The [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) once speculated that it was a pale imitation of Daedric craftsmanship put together using inferior materials, but his true power, in any case, lies elsewhere.

#### Domihaus the Bloody-Horned (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Domihaus the Bloody-Horned](Lore_Domihaus.md) was a minotaur warlord who forged an alliance with the [Dreadhorn](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) clan, a tribe of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) descended from the [Keptu](Lore_Keptu.md) people. Together, he and the reverent Reachmen planned to wrest control of the clan's ancestral lands in [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) from the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md).

[Gherig Bullblood](Lore_Gherig_Bullblood.md), the patriarch of the Dreadhorn clan, believed Domihaus was a favored champion of the clan's patron deity, [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Although his lineage is unknown, Domihaus supposedly possessed a pedigree of some significance, claiming that his line was "unbroken". Domihaus had a strong connection to the land, and wielded powerful [destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) and [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) magic. He shaped the earth below his hooves and summoned [atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) and intense blasts of fire in his conquest. He also possessed the ability to manifest flaming wings.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) confronted Domihaus at the jarl's manor at [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) after he killed Jarl [Hjurgol Skjoralmor](Lore_Hjurgol_Skjoralmor.md), and ultimately defeated him. His severed head was taken as a trophy.

#### Donolon (fl. 2E 582)
[Chef Donolon](Lore_Donolon.md), also known by monikers such as the **Wizard of Whisking**, the **Prince of Pastry**, and the **Patron of Pies**, was a legendary [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) confectioner that lived during the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). He believed that delicious desserts, cakes in particular, have a way of bringing people together, and that was perhaps the key to ending the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). To that end, he traveled the world by sea and converted a barge into a bakery. His most coveted creation was the Jubilee Cake, which helped propelled him to stardom. His clientele included the likes of nobles and even [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md). Regardless of a person or entity's status, they might have to wait for years to even taste one of Donolon's cakes. To this end, he distributed vouchers which puts the lucky recipient to the front of the list to receive a Jubilee Cake.

#### Donus Falto (fl. 2E 582)
[Donus Falto](Lore_Donus_Falto.md) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) from the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), he was a commoner from [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) who fled after the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) took over and forced its young men into their ranks. His disappearance was regarded as one of the primary catalysts of the rebellion against the legion. After leaving Cheydinhal, Donus went to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and found refuge in an alliance's sewer base.

#### Dorach Gusal (fl. early 1E)
[Dorach Gusal](Lore_Dorach_Gusal.md) was a master [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md), known for his skill in creating powerful and enchanted [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md). Along with the legendary [weaponsmith](Lore_Smithing.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) [Hilbongard Rolamus](Lore_Hilbongard_Rolamus.md), he crafted the amazing warhammer known as the [Skull Crusher](Lore_Skull_Crusher.md). This weapon was created in the forge of [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md) during the Skyrim Captivity, in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).

#### Lady Doryanna Flyte (fl. 3E 405)
[Doryanna Flyte](Lore_Doryanna_Flyte.md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) noble from the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). She was the wife of Lord [Auberon Flyte](Lore_Auberon_Flyte.md), the ruler of [Anticlere](Lore_Anticlere.md) after the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md). When Lord Flyte became bedridden, Doryanna governed in his stead and quickly used her influence to hold complete power over the region.

#### The Dragon
[The Dragon](Lore_The_Dragon.md), also known more specifically as the **Luminary Dragon**, was one of the [Luminaries of Magic](Lore_Luminary.md), mysterious immortal beings of pure [magic](Lore_Magic.md), spirits likened to beams of sunlight from [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) himself, her chosen form was that of a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). She played an important role in the life of [Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md), collaborating with her alongside the other Luminaries to develop the system of Scribing, a "metamagical theoryset" that was the precursor to the modern [Spellcrafting](Lore_Spellcrafting.md) system.

#### Dragonne Papré
[Dragonne Papré](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonne_Papr%C3%A9) (or **Dragonne Papre**) was an enormous [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) who lived in the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was the companion of an [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) Armsmaster named Samar Starlover. Dragonne Papré's lair was located in the bowels of the Battlespire.

When the invading [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) armies of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) began to overwhelm the Battlespire during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Samar told the surviving Battlemages of his plan to mount Papré and fly to the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md) on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) in order to notify Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) (although, at the time, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) was impersonating the Emperor and was perfectly aware of the invasion). However, the daedra had prevented Papré from escaping by sealing the main gate, and presumably killed him. When Starlover fought his way to Papré's lair, all he found was a carcass. Unable to request a rescue force, Starlover gave up hope and took comfort in being "eternally reunited" with his companion in death.

The Battlespire fell to the daedra, who then retreated back into [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Starlover's log was later discovered beside Papré's skeleton by an [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md), who single-handedly followed the retreating armies into Oblivion and banished Dagon in [his own realm](Lore_Dagon%27s_Hunting_Lodge.md). The logs were returned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and came into the possession of [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md). Due to the nature of dragons, Dragonne Papré isn't truly dead and could be raised again; however, it is unclear if his remains survived the destruction of the Battlespire.

#### Drake of Blades (fl. 2E 582)
The [Drake of Blades](Lore_Drake_of_Blades.md) (real name unknown) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) agent and member of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). During the long [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), in the absence of any true emperor to protect, the Dragonguard protected the Ruby Throne in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) from the petty warlords who ran rampant in Cyrodiil. They were driven underground by Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) after captain [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) disappeared but continued to operate in the province under pseudonyms. In circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) she fought against forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) during the invasion of the Imperial City and became the immortal guardian of the [Sublime Brazier](Lore_Sublime_Brazier.md).

#### Mistress Dratha (fl. 2E 582 - 3E 427)
[Dratha](Lore_Dratha.md) was a [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) Councilor who lived in her mushroom tower of [Tel Mora](Lore_Tel_Mora.md) in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). She had a very palpable distaste for men, and by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), had populated her tower solely with women. According to [Master Aryon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Aryon) she was the oldest living Telvanni Councilor by that time and was sustained by necromantic arts.

#### Curate Drelyne Llenim (fl. 3E 427)
[Drelyne Llenim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drelyne_Llenim) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) member of the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) and a Curate of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). #### Dro'Jizad (fl. before late 3E)
[Dro'Jizad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro%27Jizad) was a well-known [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [prophet](Lore_Sight.md) from [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) of some renown, known for a number of prophecies. #### Dro'Zel (fl. 1E 2920)
Dro'Zel was the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) King of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) during [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920). He ruled with his wife, Queen Hasaama. He summoned [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to destroy [Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md), a settlement in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), after disliking a sad story told by a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) bard.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Later that year, he was appointed to [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md)'s court as an advisor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-2920v11-10)</sup> King Dro'Zel was purportedly quite mad.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

#### Dro'Zira (fl. 1E 700)
Famed [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) hero. Their tales say "the Ra'[Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md)", the Ash King, sent out a call for aid in attacking [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) (presumably referring to the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DI1-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FSOKW-13)</sup>). Dro'Zira was the only one of the "Rhojiit" who still remembered that Ra'Wulfharth had once shown them mercy and respect, and so, he was the only one who answered the call. Dro'Zira acted as Ra'Wulfharth's war mount during the battle and saved the Ash King during his battle against [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md). Later, Dro'Zira found himself in the lands of [Sheggorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), but [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) decided to save Dro'Zira due to the great bravery he had exhibited.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTOD-14)</sup>

#### Dukaanfinsot

#### Dumac Dwarfking (?b - c. [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700))
[Dumac Dwarfking](Lore_Dumac.md), also known as **Dumac Dwarf-Orc**, **King of Red Mountain**, and **Dumalacath**, was the last ruler of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) before their disappearance. Along with [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), he united the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and Dwemer clans, and together they pushed the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) out of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) by [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416). #### King Durak gro-Batul (?b - c. [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175))
**Durak gro-Batul** also known as the **first Bloodfall King**, was the founder and ruler of the kingdom of [Bloodfall](Lore_Bloodfall.md), and a very respectable ruler within the [region](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) where the kingdom happened to be located.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DA-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KS-16)</sup> He and his loyal soldiers would fight alongside the Empire during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TBQ-17)</sup> Before he left for the war he selected his son Bazul gro-Batul to be the regent in his stead, after his return from the war and finding out how bad a ruler his son Bazul was during his tenure. #### Emperor Durcorach, the Black Drake (c. [2E 485](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_485) - [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TOAM3-19)</sup> or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542)<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-IEGTB-20)</sup>)
[Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md), known as the "**Black Drake**", was a [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) warlord and later Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). After gaining control of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), he and his army conquered the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533), where he was crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil, becoming the first emperor of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) dynasty. In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541) or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542), he led an invasion of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and captured and sacked many of the province's cities, but he was killed by [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland while attempting to besiege [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md). He was succeeded by his son, [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md).

#### Prior Durdryn (fl. 1E)
[Prior Durdryn](Lore_Durdryn.md) was the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) founder of the [Keepers of the Dead](Lore_Keepers_of_the_Dead.md) and their abbey above the grand Necropolis in [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), whom was active during the early half of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He established the rules and [oaths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oath_of_the_Keepers) that the Keepers abide by to this day. Durdryn was also known for his breadth of knowledge on a variety of subjects, which included [herbs and fungi](Lore_Flora.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) geology and Necrom history. He also had significant esoteric knowledge of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and it was said he was able to command Daedra and resist their temptations due to his piety and strength of character.

#### Durnehviir
[Durnehviir](Lore_Durnehviir.md), is an undead [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Curse-Never-Dying" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), lived in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) when dragons ruled [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Durnehviir took part in many battles against other dragons for control of the skies. Instead of fighting, he delved into "alok-dilon", or [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md). Like many great necromancers, he contacted the [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md) in an attempt to gain their favor. They granted him the power to summon armies of undead from the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md). In return, he was to guard a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) named [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md), who had been trapped in the Soul Cairn by the Ideal Masters. However, the Ideal Masters neglected to inform Durnehviir of Valerica's immortality, tricking him into eternal servitude. In time, his body became accustomed to the Soul Cairn, making it impossible for him to ever permanently return to Tamriel.

#### Lord Warden Dusk
[Low Warden Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Low_Warden_Dusk) (formerly **Lord Warden Dusk**) is a [Grievous Twilight](Lore_Grievous_Twilight.md) and a powerful servant of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) who rose to prominence during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). He was in charge of running the [Imperial Prison](Lore_Imperial_City_Prison.md) during the Daedric occupation of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). Cunning, brutal and cruel, the self-proclaimed "Lord Warden" of the prison was very proud of the atrocities that were committed under his watch. So much so, that he kept a meticulous record of all the indignities that had been committed in the prison; a tome which he coveted dearly. His servants sacrificed the inmates' souls to Molag Bal, and fashioned biological weapons out of their flesh.

Dusk, with the help of a mortal champion, is responsible for trapping the living essence of [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md)'s clanmates. He was defeated by the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) with the help of Lyranth, who trapped Dusk's animus inside a shield upon the destruction of his physical form. She then bestowed the shield upon the Undaunted as a token of her gratitude before taking Dusk's place as Warden of the prison. While trapped in the shield, he would often declare how he would eventually become free again and exact his revenge.

Following his defeat at the Imperial City Prison, Dusk was eventually freed from the shield and demoted to Low Warden. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) led an attack on the Ossein Cage in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) to destroy the [Dolorus Cista](Lore_Dolorous_Cista.md). The cista was protected by Overfiend Kazpian, who used its powers to call upon the greatest servants of Coldharbour to defend it. Low Warden Dusk was among the summoned Daedra, but he and his shades were banished by the Fighters Guild and the cista was destroyed.

#### Dylora (fl. 3E 433)
[Dylora](Lore_Dylora.md) is a [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) who, like others of her kind, has a flimsy loyalty and has been seen in the service of more than one [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). She has two sisters, Vika and Jansa, who have joined her in filling high-ranking positions during their service to the Princes that they have served. Together, they are known as the Seductive Sisters.

#### Dylxexes the Younger (fl. 1E)
A human scholar of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He theorized that the translation of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) as "Deep Elves" might read more accurately as "Smart Elves". The adjective "Dlyxexic" describes his theory.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Dylxexes studied the financial records of the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni.md), an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) merchant family who exploited their human kingdoms. He had this to say on the scarcity of Elven writings:

- *"These [records] may help explain why so much of Aldmeri literature is forbidden, scorned, or untranslated, for I have seen [their] like before. The Direnni were either exceedingly paranoid or their system of economy so inextricably linked with dangerous theosophist numeral-symbolism that much of what is recorded here requires ... sorcerous precautions on the part of the reader. [Hidden magic] is everywhere incorporated in their writings...signs and preternatural runes and [correspondences]... in expenditure columns, even, or margins [that] can be fatal to the uninitiated. Crucial pages were covered in spittle of the previous translator, who had babbled idiotically over the text for days before catching fire."*<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-22)</sup>

He also stated that a true adventure can only begin "by death, by slur, or by letter", meaning someone must die, be insulted, or receive a letter. His statement concludes that if an adventure does not begin from one of these causes, it can be at most an "indiscretion" or "escapade".<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

#### Dyus (fl. 3E 433 - 4E ?)
[Dyus of Mytheria](Lore_Dyus.md) was the chamberlain of [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md) and the keeper of his Great Library. He was originally simply the Prince's librarian, but soon took on many of the tasks Jyggalag found distasteful. His mortality was stripped away when Jyggalag grew tired of him dying. Using the formulae once contained in the Great Library, Dyus can predict all events before they happen, save the Prophecies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).
