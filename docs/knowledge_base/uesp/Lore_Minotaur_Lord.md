*Around Imperial ruins you gather,
Eternal guardians with hooves and horn.
What memories lead you to path there,
Is it Belharza or the Empire that you mourn?*
— *[The Minotaur Song](Lore_The_Minotaur_Song.md)* Minotaurs, also called **man-bulls** or **bull-men**, are a species of [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) native to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) with the body of a [man](Lore_Human.md) and the head of a [bull](Lore_Bull.md). During the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), they were said to have symbolized the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), but in later eras became more commonly perceived as savage monsters by the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM39-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup>

They have thick grey or sometimes brown or red<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLBo PV-3)</sup> hide and fur, and large curved horns. Some Minotaurs clothe themselves in a simple loincloth and shawl or wear nose-rings. Others wear intricate leather armor, and some prefer to be completely naked. They are most commonly found in the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLBo PV-3)</sup> [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md), [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) and the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) regions of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-4)</sup> as well as [The Scar](Lore_The_Scar.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOEL-5)</sup> the [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOM-6)</sup> and in ancient times across [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMOSP-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3V-8)</sup>

## History

### Origins
*We are ada, Mor, and change things through love. We must take care lest we beget more monsters on this earth. If you do not desist, she will take to you, and you will transform all Cyrod if you do this.*
—Pelinal on Morihaus and Alessia. [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/ON-furnishing-Golden_Idol_of_Morihaus_02.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Golden_Idol_of_Morihaus_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Golden_Idol_of_Morihaus_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Golden_Idol_of_Morihaus_02.jpg) Morihaus, progenitor of Minotaurs Although no records survive to confirm their origins, some scholars claim that Minotaurs trace their lineage back to the relationship between Empress [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md). Their union gave birth to [Belharza](Lore_Belharza.md) the Man-Bull, believed to be one of the first Minotaurs, who ruled the Empire after the death of Alessia.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup> This union being responsible for an entire race could be explained by Morihaus' nature as an [Ada](Lore_Ada.md), who could "change things through love."<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP5-10)</sup> Conversely, other scholars dismiss such theories as both lightly heretical and having no basis in historical fact, instead claiming minotaurs to be the result of some magical accident or [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) process, akin to a [mantikora](Lore_Mantikora.md) or [vvardvark](Lore_Vvardvark.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TTo M-11)</sup> Regardless, in the years during and after Empress Alessia's reign, Minotaurs began to appear in greater and greater numbers. During this time, it's believed the early Minotaurs possessed intelligence and culture comparable to other races of beastfolk. Artwork and texts from the First Era suggest they were fiercely loyal to the Empire and were among Empress Alessia's most devoted defenders.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMOSP-7)</sup> Some sources place Minotaurs on Tamriel before the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and therefore before the union of Alessia and Morihaus.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3V-8)</sup>

Despite their devotion to the Empire, Minotaurs were always a target of suspicion and fear because of their size and strength. With the rise of the fanatical [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md), they were re-classified as 'monsters' no better than [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) or [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) and were driven into the forests, destroying whatever culture they previously possessed.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-4)</sup> Much of the evidence to support this claim was also destroyed in the intervening period while the Alessian Order ruled the Empire. One remaining fragment of an ancient tablet depicts Alessian troops revolting against Emperor Belharza, giving some credence to the theory.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup> Minotaurs were not entirely wiped out by the Alessians, and continue to roam the backwoods and hills of Cyrodiil throughout the following eras.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-4)</sup>

## Behavior and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/63/ON-creature-Minotaur_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Minotaur_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Minotaur_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Minotaur_02.jpg) A minotaur sleeping at its camp on the Gold Coast, complete with a bedroll, cooking pot, fire, plates, and hoard of treasure Although thought to be excessively male as a species,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HHH-12)</sup> female minotaurs do exist. Minotaurs are social creatures, and often appear in groups numbering twenty or more. The largest male dominates this clan structure and has breeding rights with all females of reproductive age. Younger males will often challenge this dominant male in a fight to the death.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BAJ-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SVJ-14)</sup> They have been observed to hoard gold and treasures at their camps for reasons unknown.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Camp-15)</sup>

Minotaurs are notoriously strong and difficult opponents in combat, despite their false reputation as clumsy brutes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM39-1)</sup> They are resistant to [magic](Lore_Magic.md), can easily swing heavy [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) with one hand and [their horns](Lore_Minotaur_Horn.md) have the ability to tear through the toughest armor. The most powerful individuals are referred to as "Minotaur Lords".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup> They are believed by some to possess only rudimentary intelligence, but have a heightened sense of smell and can track a man by scent alone.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup> They still seem to be able to produce complex works of metal and leather. Although Minotaurs commonly fight with weapons, some Minotaurs such as their shamans have the ability to use magic.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

One common observation of Minotaurs in the wild is that they tend to congregate around ancient ruins that were of significance to the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). Some believe they are drawn to guard these places on an instinctual level, possibly due to their ancestral connection to Morihaus, Belharza, or the Empire itself.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SVJ-14)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TMS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> There are records of wizards placing hexes on [Akaviri](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) relics, drawing minotaurs in and around the tomb to guard it. "Yowling-powder" made from [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md), [sulfur](Lore_Sulfur.md), [lemon](Lore_Lemon.md) -peel, and [daedra hearts](Lore_Daedra_Heart.md) can be used to break the enchantments on the relics, resulting in the minotaurs gradually leaving their post at the area.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONNisuzi-20)</sup> Minotaurs also served as the protectors of the [unicorn](Lore_Unicorn.md) at Harcane Grove, which was killed in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) for the glory of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup> The Elden Hollow beneath [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) was the final resting place for generations of minotaurs.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONDracien Montue-21)</sup>

### Relationship with Other Races
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg) Domihaus, a winged Minotaur Minotaurs share little civil interaction with humans or elves, but are typically free to settle around the ruins they inhabit. Minotaurs possess their own [language](Lore_Languages.md), but few men understand their speech.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLBo PV-3)</sup> They mostly keep to themselves when unprovoked, but are also very territorial and will react aggressively on intruding adventurers. As a result, most inhabitants of Cyrodiil stick to well traveled roads to avoid contact with such creatures. Hearsay surrounding Minotaurs includes [apples](Lore_Apple.md) being poisonous to them, which results in travelers eating several before a journey.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONIllia-22)</sup> Other examples characterize them as "cruel man-eaters", and they are sometimes used to scare children in tales and song.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-AGCCB-23)</sup> Conversely, some children's toys are made to resemble Minotaurs.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Cont Minotaur-24)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was known to utilize minotaurs as guardians of their guild halls during closing hours.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Minotaur Arena-25)</sup> Minotaurs have sometimes been captured for use in arena matches in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup>

[Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) members of the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) were known allies of the Minotaurs.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FD-26)</sup> At least some Reachfolk were capable of interpreting their language.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-RC-27)</sup> There were fringe cases of Minotaurs living alongside [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Cud-28)</sup> [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-KI-29)</sup> and [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Bloodwater-30)</sup> Some blacksmiths honor the Minotaurs by creating armor and weapons inspired by their characteristics, such as the use of horns in its design or [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) iconography - appropriate for a race so deeply linked to the early history of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM39-1)</sup>

Their severed heads are sometimes exported to other provinces, possibly to create wall-mounted decorations that were quite common in homes throughout Cyrodiil.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONAran-31)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup> Minotaur meat was often used in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) to make slumgullion stew.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Slum-32)</sup> Oxblood fungal spores are known to grow on Minotaur, and are used by some blacksmiths in their work. Their horns are often displayed as prestigious hunting prizes or crafted into ornate warhorns and hunting horns.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-OBPinarus-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Cont Warhorn1-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Cont Warhorn2-35)</sup> Their horns are also valued by alchemists for their various [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) properties.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup>

## Notable Minotaurs
- **[Belharza](Lore_Belharza.md)**: The first minotaur.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup>
- **Bhrum** and **Koska**: Twin minotaurs who ransacked an expedition<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-LMB-36)</sup>
- **[Domihaus](Lore_Domihaus.md)**: A colossal Minotaur warlord who allied with the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and attempted to take [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md).
- **Galrus Grimhorn**: A Minotaur that became a champion of the Hammerdeath Arena in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) some time before 2E 582.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONGH-37)</sup>
- **Hruum the Red**: A massive minotaur who killed Brumiun, Phalevon Vero's cousin<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLBo PV-3)</sup>
- **Khaath the Old**:<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLBo PV-3)</sup> Killed by Phalevon and had his remains fashioned into the Horn of Magnificence<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONRaw R-38)</sup>
- **Limenauruus**: A colossal Minotaur encountered by the Dragonstar Caravan Company in 2E 582. He was discovered underground in the ruins of Fort Faleria, where Lord [Nunex Faleria](Lore_Nunex_Faleria.md) had enslaved and killed a large number of his kin centuries earlier.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONLS-39)</sup>
- **Rahdgarak**: The champion of Hunter's Grotto
