Denizens of Apocrypha **by [Cipher Plautis](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Cipher_Plautis)** *A guide to the Daedra and others found in Apocrypha*
---
[Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) is, of course, a plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Few natural creatures reside in such places. We mortal ciphers are but visitors in a world of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) beings. Some are minor spirits that take forms not dissimilar from the [creatures](Lore_Bestiary.md) of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), while others resemble the [thinking races](Lore_Races.md), and some take on bizarre forms not found in the natural world.

DAEDRIC VERMIN
Spend an hour strolling about [Cipher's Midden](Lore_Cipher%27s_Midden.md), and you'll encounter everyday vermin that are similar to the pests of the mortal world—at first glance, anyway. [Daedrats](Lore_Daedrat.md) and [fiendroths](Lore_Fiendroth.md) appear to exist in all corners of Oblivion, scuttling about and scavenging in much the same way that [rats](Lore_Rat.md) and insects plague Nirn. One type of Daedric vermin unique to Apocrypha is the [tomeshell](Lore_Tomeshell.md). It occupies and animates discarded [books](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Books), much like some [crabs](Lore_Hermit_Crab.md) borrow the shells of other creatures. Tomeshells are mostly harmless, but can lash out if threatened. And don't get me started on [daedmites](Lore_Daedmite.md).

LESSER DAEDRA
Many of the so-called lesser Daedra can be found throughout the Endless Library and Chroma Incognito. Every [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) commands legions of [imps](Lore_Imp.md), [clannfear](Lore_Clannfear.md), and [ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md), and [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) is no exception. However, the Great Eye pays little attention to these creatures. They are simply herded or gathered in places that Mora wants guarded, then left to their own devices. Mortal visitors must take great care with any such Daedra they meet in Apocrypha—these lesser Daedra often mistake loyal worshipers of the Great Eye for intruders, and set upon them.

THE GREAT EYE'S SERVANTS
While Hermaeus Mora generally ignores bestial Daedra, the more intelligent varieties are of much more interest to him. He frequently employs [Watchlings](Lore_Watchling.md) as messengers and recorders, tasking them with obscure clerical duties in the hidden bureaucracy that quietly administers Apocrypha. Their larger and more powerful cousins, the [Watchers](Lore_Watcher.md), are often found as overseers and guardians, tending to the Great Eye's more important concerns.

Two types of Daedra unique to Apocrypha deserve special mention among Hermaeus Mora's servants. [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) are the scholars of Mora's realm. Of all the Daedra in this plane, they seem most attuned to the Great Eye's hidden purposes. They are the keepers of secrets and the scribes of fate, going about their inscrutable duties in eerie silence. The terrifying, fishlike [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), on the other hand, embody the Great Eye's ancient connection to the sea. They are Mora's most powerful guardians, existing only to destroy threats to Apocrypha in whatever form they appear.

INTERLOPERS
Travel far enough in Apocrypha, and you are likely to encounter creatures not native to the plane who have become entrapped here. The best example are the [Hushed](Lore_Hushed.md). Once they were mortal scribes, much like the ciphers of Cipher's Midden. But they have been consumed by knowledge, losing their very identities in their hunger to know more. Most Hushed ignore mortals who encounter them. But sometimes Hermaeus Mora uses these hapless creatures to guard locations he wishes to keep private. Do not interfere with the Hushed as they attend their duties, or they may turn on you with no warning at all.
