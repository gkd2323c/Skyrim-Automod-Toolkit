# Rubedite

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-node-Rubedite_Ore.jpg/200px-ON-node-Rubedite_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Rubedite_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Rubedite_Ore.jpg) An exposed Rubedite deposit Rubedite is a red rare high-quality metal used by blacksmiths to forge of arms and armor exceeding [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) in quality, which is smelted from shiny dark red ore deposits.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESORubedite-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Rubedite)</sup> The origins of who first worked with rubedite is unknown, but by crafting enchanted armor with metalworking tools blessed by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), coating said armor with a rubedite metal enamel, and then tempering it with captured dragonflame, [paladins](Lore_Paladins.md) from [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) were able to create their set of rubedite armor sometime after [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

Rubedite left obscurity around the time the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) spilled into the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), when it along with other equipment created out of high-quality materials were recovered as spoils of war. Vendors from the three alliances exchanged rubedite alongside rubedo leather, ruby ash, and [ancestor silk](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md) for the [Tel Var Stones](Lore_Tel_Var_Stones.md) soldiers looted during their campaign, and master artisans of the various crafting professions broke down the materials to use for their needs.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Rubedite)</sup> Rubedite has also been used to create mortars and pestles used specifically for grinding [scrib](Lore_Scrib.md) leg joints for the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) [funerary practices](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dunmer_Death_Beliefs).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PN-3)</sup> Tendrils of [tomeshells](Lore_Tomeshell.md) leather adorned with rubedite beads at their tips have been used by people as a hair accessories.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Treasures-4)</sup> Masters of [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md) tournaments were rewarded with trophies made of [orichalcum](Lore_Orichalcum.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTO-5)</sup> [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTE-6)</sup> [quicksilver](Lore_Quicksilver.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TTQ-7)</sup> [voidsteel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voidsteel),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTV-8)</sup> and rubedite,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTR-9)</sup> with rubedite being the most prestigious and orichalcum being the least.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOT-10)</sup> Rubedite is rumored to have been used to commemorate 95 years of reign of a ruler from a kingdom bordering [Bloodfall Kingdom](Lore_Bloodfall_Kingdom.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Rubedite) | In a [Loremaster's Archive](Lore_Loremaster%27s_Archive.md), it [was explained](Lore_Master_Assistant_Materials_Acquirer_Pacrooti_Answers_Your_Questions.md) that materials in *[The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)* that are higher in quality than ebony and malachite are the result of the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md) allowing for a greater diffusion of knowledge about exotic materials. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) are credited for learning how to refine such magical ores as a result. This explanation however predates the release of the [Imperial City DLC](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Imperial_City_(DLC)) by two months, which introduced Rubedite as a material. |
| --- | --- |

- Wrothgar and Cyrodiil were chosen to be spots for minable rubedite ore months after the release of Imperial City in [Patch 2.2.4](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Patch/2.2.4#Crafting_Node_Scaling), as the crafting nodes there scaled to the player based on their crafting passives. With the [One Tamriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Patch/2.6.4#Crafting_.26_Harvesting) update in *[The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*, all material harvest nodes were made to scale to both your combat level and crafting passives, meaning exotic ebony is as common as basic [steel](Lore_Steel.md). This includes rubedite, which was [previously harder to get](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Patch/2.1.4#New_Crafting_Tiers.2C_Materials.2C_and_Recipes). These decisions do not reflect in-universe lore and is instead a gameplay convenience.
- The affix word for rubedite and another similar material, Rubedo Leather, is *"[Rubedo](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/rubedo)"*, which is the Latin word for "redness". The term is used in alchemy as a sign of a perfected or finished product.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-UOFThesis-12)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-4Stages-13)</sup>
