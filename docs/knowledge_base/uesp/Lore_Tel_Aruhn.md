[3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) [![](https://images.uesp.net/c/c7/MW-banner-Tel_Aruhn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Tel_Aruhn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Tel_Aruhn.png) Banner of Tel Aruhn Tel Aruhn was an island-town found on the District of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It was northwest of [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md), amidst the rocky islands on [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md), specifically in [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md). For the longest time, it was the home of the Archmagister of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) on Vvardenfell, High Lord Magus [Gothren](Lore_Gothren.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BBo3E-1)</sup> But it was widely-known for the Festival Slave Market, the largest slave market on the island-district.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Gt V-2)</sup> The island of [Vounoura](Lore_Vounoura.md) is roughly less than a month away from Tel Aruhn.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-2920Even Star-3)</sup>

## History
Tel Aruhn's founding dates back to the mid- [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), as one of the many wizard towers built by [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorers that charted the island's coast, but by the late-Merethic Era, these towers were abandoned, alongside the dissolution of the High Velothi culture that later became tribes, early predecessors of the modern-day [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-4)</sup>

Sometime by the early- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the town of Tel Aruhn was founded, populated by the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md). The Great-Mage, [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) was born and raised in Tel Aruhn at the time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Si NN-5)</sup> Following the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md)'s victory in the battle of [Ald Marak](Lore_Ald_Marak.md) during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), many strategic castles across Morrowind's coastline, including Tel Aruhn were to be leased to the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in a truce presented to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md) by Prince [Juilek Cyrodiil](Lore_Juilek.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920Mid Year-6)</sup> However, with the Prince's death later that year, and the renewed aggression following the sacking of [Black Gate](Lore_Black_Gate.md), the original treaty was broken.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-2920SH-7)</sup> It is unknown if the terms of the original truce were upheld in the later [Cervant Truce](Lore_Cervant_Truce.md) that officially ended the war, and thus if Tel Aruhn ever passed into Imperial hands prior to the Third Era.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

As early as [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Tel Aruhn had been administered by Archmagister Gothren of House Telvanni,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOGothren-9)</sup> and from his tower, he governed the eastern [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md) region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BBo3E-1)</sup> That was until in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), when the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) surfaced on Vvardenfell and sought the blessings of the Telvanni lords to be named Hortator. However, Gothren's unwillingness to accept the warrior as the house's hortator led to his death.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWHortator-10)</sup>

At roughly the same time, the Nerevarine traveled to Tel Aruhn's Slave Market, as part of their plan to convince the [Zainab](Lore_Zainab.md) Ashkhan that they were the hero of prophecy. The slavemistress, Savile Imayn played along with their plan; while the Nerevarine looked for exquisite clothing and Telvanni Bug Musk, Imayn coached the slave, Falura Llervu, to perform her role as a high-born Telvanni lady from [Velothis Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velothis_Haven). Their plan managed to work and convinced Ashkhan Kaushad that the warrior was the Nerevarine.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWZainab-11)</sup>
