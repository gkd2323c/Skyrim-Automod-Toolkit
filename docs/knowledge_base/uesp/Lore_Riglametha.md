# Riglametha

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg) Lainlyn ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/DF-map-Lainlyn.png/200px-DF-map-Lainlyn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn.png) The Barony of Lainlyn ca. 3E 405 Riglametha (in the [Banthan](Lore_Bantha.md) dialect of [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md) for *"grateful-offering"*)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GHR-1)</sup> is a holiday held on the 12th of [Hearthfire](Lore_Hearthfire.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GHR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-2)</sup> and observed across the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGT-3)</sup> but has much more significance in the Barony of [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md). It is generally a festival of the graces, which the gods have bestowed upon Lainlyn for centuries. As is tradition, performances are held to depict great moments in Lainlyn's history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GHR-1)</sup>

One of the most popular stories is **Ghraewaj**, which translates into Yoku as either *"The Crows Who Were Punished"* or "*The Crows Who Punish"*. It is about a coven of [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) worshippers led by the temptress, Noctyr-a, who wreaked havoc in Lainlyn by seducing the Baron of Lainlyn. The baroness tricked Noctyr-a into wearing a white robe that transformed her into a giant black bird. No longer under her spell, the Baron had the bird cooked into an enormous roast. When the coven ate the bird, they also transformed, but into half- [bird](Lore_Bird.md) creatures called [harpies](Lore_Harpy.md). This is the purported origin of the harpy, who are common across the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), but especially in Lainlyn. The story has changed over the years, but its prevalence in Lainlyn has remained all the same.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GHR-1)</sup>
