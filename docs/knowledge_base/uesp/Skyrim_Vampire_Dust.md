## Ingredients
30 guaranteed samples can be found in 22 different locations. Locations with multiple samples are:

- 2 around [Autumnwatch Tower](Skyrim_Autumnwatch_Tower.md) ([The Rift](Skyrim_The_Rift.md))
- 2 in the [Dawnstar Sanctuary](Skyrim_Dawnstar_Sanctuary.md) ([The Pale](Skyrim_The_Pale.md))
- 2 in [Drelas' Cottage](Skyrim_Drelas%27_Cottage.md) ([Whiterun Hold](Skyrim_Whiterun_Hold.md))
- 2 in [Hjerim](Skyrim_Hjerim.md) ([Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md))
- 2 in [Honeyside](Skyrim_Honeyside.md) ([Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md))
- 2 in [the Mortar and Pestle](Skyrim_The_Mortar_and_Pestle.md) ([Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md))
- 2 in [Nightcaller Temple](Skyrim_Nightcaller_Temple.md) ([The Pale](Skyrim_The_Pale.md))
- 2 in the [Palace of the Kings Upstairs](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings_Upstairs.md) ([Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md))

It is categorized as a rare ingredient, meaning that most apothecary merchants have a 21% chance of carrying 1-4 samples. In addition, it may be randomly found only in rare-type [apothecary's satchels](Skyrim_Apothecary%27s_Satchel.md).

- Up to 3 samples at 1.7% chance each or 1 sample at 2.3% chance: carried by [Silver Hand](Skyrim_Silver_Hand.md) members.

## Creatures
Vampire dust is dropped by all [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md), including [Ancient Vampires](Skyrim_Ancient_Vampire.md), [Blooded Vampires](Skyrim_Blooded_Vampire.md), [Master Vampires](Skyrim_Master_Vampire.md), [Vampire Fledglings](Skyrim_Vampire_Fledgling.md), [Vampire Mistwalkers](Skyrim_Vampire_Mistwalker.md), [Vampire Nightstalkers](Skyrim_Vampire_Nightstalker.md), [Volkihar Vampires](Skyrim_Volkihar_Vampire.md), [Volkihar Master Vampires](Skyrim_Volkihar_Master_Vampire.md), as well as named vampires such as [Alva](Skyrim_Alva.md) and [Laelette the Vampire](Skyrim_Laelette_the_Vampire.md).

## Notes
- Despite vampires coming about from a disease, their dust *cures* disease.
- Vampire dust is one of the suggestions you can give to [Gianna](Skyrim_Gianna.md) during a [Dark Brotherhood quest](Skyrim_To_Kill_an_Empire.md). She describes it as having an "earthy texture" that it would lend to the broth.
