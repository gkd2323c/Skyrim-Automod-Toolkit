Skyrim, the northernmost province of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), is a cold and mountainous region also known as the **Old Kingdom**, **Mereth**, or the **Fatherland**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POT-3)</sup> or **Keizaal** in the [dragon tongue](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Many past battles have given it a ravaged appearance and many ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3S-5)</sup> Though currently inhabited primarily by [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), the [Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) who they replaced had resided there since time immemorial.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3S-5)</sup> The sovereign, the High King of Skyrim, is chosen by the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md), a convention of [jarls](Skyrim_Jarl.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SR-6)</sup> A jarl is a regional ruler chosen through heredity and, rarely, through right of arms.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SR-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup> The High King typically rules until death, though acts of dishonor, particularly the appearance of cowardice, can lead to the recall and reconsideration of the Moot.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SR-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sybille-8)</sup> Since the [Pact of Chieftains](Lore_Pact_of_Chieftains.md) was signed in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), the Moot does not give serious consideration to anyone but the High King's direct heir unless one is not available.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup>

## Geography
[Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) lies to the east over the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) is south beyond the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) (and the latest incarnation of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md)) is to the south and west, [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) is past the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) to the west, and the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) runs along the province's long northern coastline. Skyrim holds four of the five highest mountains in Tamriel<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup> (the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) being the only one outside Skyrim). Much of the northern half of Skyrim is cold and covered in snow. However, the southern regions of the province are relatively mild.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup>

### Holds
The Nords long ago divided the province into nine geopolitical regions known as "holds". Each hold is a large area of land roughly equivalent to a county in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), each individually governed by a jarl who maintains court in the hold's capital city. The nine traditional holds of Skyrim are: [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md), [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md), and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-THOS-9)</sup> During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), when the Reach became an independent [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) kingdom and lost its status as a Nordic hold, a tenth hold named [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md) was created from the Reach's northern fringe.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gt WSK-10)</sup>

Winterhold, Eastmarch, the Rift, and the Pale, lying to the north and east of the province, are collectively known as the "Old Holds", and remain isolated from the rest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), both geographically and politically. In contrast, the western hold of the Reach has historically been more cosmopolitan and members of all the races of the continent have made their homes there.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup>

#### Eastmarch
[Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) is the eastern [hold](Lore_Hold.md) of Skyrim, and one of the four known collectively as the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md). As its name suggests, it lies against the province's eastern border, close to the [Dunmeth Pass](Lore_Dunmeth_Pass.md), the only passage to and from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the area due to the high and rugged [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md).

[Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) is the only sizable city in the otherwise rural province due to the expansive hot springs which dominate the hold. According to legend, these hot springs were created when the hero [Wuunding](Lore_Wuunding.md) fought a mighty Daedra and defeated it with a flaming hammer. The hammer's blows melted the Daedra, leaving behind the numerous hot springs.

[Darkwater River](Lore_Darkwater_River.md) winds north from Lake Geir into this treacherous hot spot, where it meets the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) which then drains into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) as it passes Windhelm. [River Yorgrim](Lore_River_Yorgrim.md) also joins with the White River before it ends at the Sea of Ghosts. It drains [Lake Yorgrim](Lore_Lake_Yorgrim.md), which lies west in the hold known as [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md).

#### The Pale
[The Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) (also called the **Pale Hold**) is a large region in north-central Skyrim and one of the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md), the original four territories in the province, where ancient traditions continue to thrive. The region acquired its name from both the pale white tundra and the pigmentation of the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). Because of its distinct shape, some people colloquially call it "the old boot". The hold's population is centralized in its capital city of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), a seaside town on the [Frozen Coast](Lore_Frozen_Coast.md) and one of the busiest ports in the province. Smaller settlements include [Bardmont](Lore_Bardmont.md), [Dunstad Grove](Lore_Dunstad_Grove.md), [Heljarchen](Lore_Heljarchen.md), and Porhnak.

#### Winterhold
[Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md) is a historical region located on the northeast coast of Skyrim, known for its inhospitable terrain and snow-blown landscape. Centered around the city of the [same name](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), it is one of the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md) of Skyrim, which are generally isolated by geography and choice. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Winterhold are simple fisherfolk and mostly work on the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) since there is little pasture for grazing and the cold winds prove brutal for livestock. One of the region's most noteworthy landmarks is the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), the province's premier school of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) and center of both academia and arcana.

#### Hjaalmarch
[Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) or **Hjaalmarch Hold** is a lowland coastal [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in Skyrim. Roughly half the hold consists of the [Drajkmyr](Lore_Drajkmyr.md) marsh, south of which is the capital and only major settlement [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) (although [Stonehills](Lore_Stonehills.md) and some farmsteads lie on the tundra of [Hailstone Valley](Lore_Hailstone_Valley.md)). The region is veined with waterways that empty into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), and the [Skyborn Range](Lore_Skyborn_Range.md) further isolates the area from the eastern and southern border.

Hjaalmarch is home to the Karth delta, also called the Mouths of the Karth, where the mighty [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md) and the smaller [River Hjaal](Lore_River_Hjaal.md) (whence the hold's name is derived) empty into the Sea of Ghosts through a variety of intricate channels. The [Deathbell](Lore_Deathbell.md) flower is known to be abundant throughout the swamps. Many ruins dot the hold, including the legendary [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md). Combined with the fog that surrounds the marshes and the creatures that stalk the land ([Wispmothers](Lore_Wispmother.md) purportedly originated here), Hjaalmarch is considered one of the more mysterious holds in Skyrim.

Hjaalmarch has the lowest taxes of the nine holds, but it is also the poorest hold.

#### Haafingar
[Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md) (also called **Haafingar Hold** and the **Kingdom of Solitude**) is a relatively small, mountainous, coastal [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in northwestern Skyrim. Its capital is the famed city of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), and the two are sometimes treated as one and the same. Solitude is perched atop a large rock outcropping known as the [Great Arch](Lore_Great_Arch.md), towering high above the Solitude Inlet, which is found at the end of the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md) and the massive Karth delta. However, Haafingar is far more than just Solitude. The Karth River allows for a great deal of commerce in Solitude and the town of [Dragon Bridge](Lore_Dragon_Bridge.md) in the [Karth River Forest](Lore_Karth_River_Forest.md) (mostly in timber and fish), and the long [Giant's Coastline](Lore_Giant%27s_Coast.md) with the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) is littered with frozen shipwrecks and treasures. [Mount Kilkreath](Lore_Mount_Kilkreath.md) and its associated mountains take a large portion of the hold and it is filled with caves and ruins where bandits and creatures make their homes.

The hold shares borders with two other holds, [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the south and [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) to the southeast, and the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) borders it to the west, the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) found between the hold and [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). The [mountains of Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar_Mountains.md) are only the tapered end of the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md), an enormous range of jagged mountain peaks to the south which rise up among the clouds of the Reach.

#### The Reach
[The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) (also known as **Karth**) is a geographic region surrounded by [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), Skyrim and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Its territory once extended into High Rock, but that portion was split off to create the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) during the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and has remained separate ever since. The region was historically inhabited by the native, [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md). Control of the Reach has changed hands between the Reachmen, [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), and even the [Cyrodilics](Lore_Imperial.md) numerous times over the centuries in various conflicts, and the Reachmen became well-known for resisting foreign rule by using ancient magic and an intimate knowledge of the landscape to their advantage in guerrilla warfare and espionage tactics.

In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the Reach is the southwestern [hold](Lore_Hold.md) of Skyrim. Near the southwestern corner, the capital [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), formerly a [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) stronghold, emerges from the living rock of the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md), which transition into the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) near the hold's southeast corner. The [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md) begins in the southern region and drains the mountains, running like a deep gouge through the middle of the hold called the [Karth River Canyon](Lore_Karth_River.md). Settlements such as [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md) and [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md) can be found along the Karth, with the [Sundered Hills](Lore_Sundered_Hills.md) bordering [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md)'s [Tundra Plateau](Lore_Tundra_Plateau.md) to the east. The peaks of the Druadach range lie to the west of it, and high bluffs typically rise on the east leading to grassland and tundra. The Reachmen constitute the demographic majority of the Reach and its various settlements, and an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) stronghold can be found in the steppes of the mountains in the south of the hold.

#### Karthald
[Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md) is a historical [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in Skyrim that existed during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). It was established as a defensive border against [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) after being carved from its northern territory of the upper Karth Valley named **Reachwater Pass**, along with parts of the [Karth River Forest](Lore_Karth_River_Forest.md). The Reach was at the time an independent [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) kingdom ruled by the Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md). Karthald was established in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577) by the kingdom of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), although [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) clans had inhabited the land for generations alongside the Reachfolk. The fortified village of [Karthwatch](Lore_Karthwatch.md) was established in as the new hold's capital. Karthald's borders also encompassed the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) stronghold of [Mor Khazgur](Lore_Mor_Khazgur.md), though the Orcs were keen to insist that Mor Khazgur was a sovereign outpost of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). While the roads of Karthald were safe enough for travelers, Karthwatch was a spartan settlement with little hospitality to offer.

Karthald ceased to exist as a separate hold after the destruction of Karthwatch at the hands of the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) circa 2E 582. In retaliation for the prior massacre of a local Reachfolk clan by the Nords and the alleged kidnapping of the crucial clan heir, [Bjora](Lore_Bjora.md), they launched an assault. The Reachmen created a [Harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorm.md) above the town in order to drain the life energy of its inhabitants, using their [souls](Lore_Soul.md) to fill ancient reliquaries containing the mortal remains of members of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). Many of the residents of Karthwatch were transformed into [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) monstrosities known as [Harrowfiends](Lore_Harrowfiend.md), including Jarl Olfwenn herself. Others were taken hostage by the witches, although a rescue was mounted by the town's survivors. The sole survivor of the jarl's court was the court wizard, Magister Fridelja, who went on to represent the hold politically in the turmoil that followed. She and the other survivors sought refuge in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). However, the rest of the hold was similarly laid to waste, its people left dead or scattered. With the Nords driven out and the hold's capital destroyed, Karthald was no more, allowing the territory to be reclaimed by the Reach.

In [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), the Reach was conquered by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and annexed by Skyrim as a Nord hold. By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), no traces of Karthald's time as a hold remained, and a ruined fortress called [Broken Tower Redoubt](Lore_Broken_Tower_Redoubt.md) sat on the approximate site of Karthwatch.

#### Whiterun Hold
[Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md) is a [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in Skyrim, located roughly in the middle of the province. Roads are numerous and well maintained, and visibility across the [tundra plains](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) is excellent. The hold is relatively flat, surrounded the by mountains of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the west of the [Tundra Plateau](Lore_Tundra_Plateau.md), the [Skyborn Range](Lore_Skyborn_Range.md) of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) and the [Yorgrim Forest](Lore_Yorgrim_Forest.md) of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) to the north, and the [Brittleshin Hills](Lore_Brittleshin_Hills.md) of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) to the south. However, Whiterun's southeastern corner is dominated by the gigantic and soaring [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) —the highest mountain in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The peak of [Lone Mountain](Lore_Lone_Mountain.md) is found in the center of the hold.

#### Falkreath Hold
[Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) (or simply called **Falkreath**) is one of the main [holds](Lore_Hold.md) of Skyrim, known for its lush pine forest. [Lake Ilinalta](Lore_Lake_Ilinalta.md), the province's largest body of freshwater, constitutes a significant portion of the hold. The people of the hold are generally prosperous farmers and loggers, thanks to the Ilinalta. Falkreath has an ancient association with battle and [death](Lore_Nord_Death_Beliefs.md). Like the hold, the capital city is called [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), but it is also known as the "heroes' graveyard". It contains one of the largest cemeteries in the province. Falkreath's alignment has sometimes shifted between the Kingdom of Skyrim and the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md), the autonomous government of western [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

#### The Rift
[The Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) (sometimes called **Rift Hold**) is a large region in the southeastern portion of Skyrim and one of the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md), the eastern Skyrim territories isolated by geography and choice as the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) have stuck close to ancient traditions. The Rift is distinct for its temperate climate and autumn forest, thanks to the mountains surrounding it blocking extreme temperatures from the east and north. The capital of the hold is [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), formerly the epicenter of a vast trading network between Skyrim and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).

### Solstheim
Although the history of the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) is somewhat complicated, there was a time when it was considered a part of Skyrim.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DBLoading-11)</sup> The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), an offshoot tribe of Nords, inhabit the northeastern corner of the island.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BM-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DB-13)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Nords of Skyrim launched a successful campaign to claim the whole of Solstheim.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> In [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16), the High King of Skyrim formally gave the island to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) as a refuge for the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) after the events of [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DBLoading-11)</sup> The northern half of the island is snowy and mountainous. The southern half was originally covered in coniferous forests, but after the Red Year it was transformed into an [ashland](Lore_Solstheim_Ashlands.md) similar to those seen in Morrowind.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BM-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DB-13)</sup>

### Bleakrock Isle
[Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md), also considered a part of Skyrim, is a small, snowy island in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) between [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md) and [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). A small populace of the Nords inhabited the Bleakrock Village until they were driven from the island by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It is unknown if the island was resettled afterwards.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Online-15)</sup>

### Icereach
[Icereach](Lore_Icereach.md) is a small island in the Sea of Ghosts. Once used by the Nords as an outpost to protect whalers from Orcish corsairs, the island eventually became the home of a coven of Reachfolk witches who adopted the island's name. While the Nords believe that the Icereach Coven seized the island by force, the coven maintain they acquired it through a cursed bargain with its original Nordic inhabitants. Around 2E 582, the Icereach Coven was defeated by an expeditionary force sent by High King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) to investigate strange storms around the island that had been caused by the witches' rituals.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Online-15)</sup>

## History
Skyrim's history is mostly [that of the Nords](Lore_Nord.md#History). The Nords believe the sky, [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), breathed life into them at the summit of the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md), which is now considered the tallest mountain in Tamriel (the Red Mountain was acknowledged as the tallest before its eruption during the Red Year).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-COTS-16)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Loading-17)</sup> While historians tend to portray Ysgramor as the first Nordic settler of Skyrim, ushering in concurrent waves of immigration from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), this is not the case. While their exact origins are unknown, the most widely accepted theory is that the early [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) peoples (a now extinct culture) settled in Skyrim after arriving from Atmora well before the arrival of Ysgramor, and from there spread to the rest of Tamriel.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FC-18)</sup>

In ancient times, Skyrim was ruled by the malevolent [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). This early society revolved around the worship of dragons through intermediaries known as dragon priests, who ruled as gods above men. Sometime in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the mythical [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) saw the overthrow of the Dragon Cult when [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), Akatosh's firstborn, was defeated atop the Throat of the World. The veneration of animals as gods was eventually replaced by the Eight Divines.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-19)</sup>

The many mountain ranges in and around the province resulted from unknown cataclysms in the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3S-5)</sup> The only known relic from this time is the [Skyforge](Lore_Skyforge.md), an ancient, powerful, and still-active forge built into the living rock in modern-day Whiterun. Who built it remains a mystery, but the Elves feared it, and this led the invading [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) to claim it for themselves.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SOTR7-20)</sup>

The [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) and [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) occupied Skyrim until the late Merethic Era, when the final wave of Nordic immigration from Atmora, led by [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md), finally established Nordic supremacy in modern-day Skyrim.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FC-18)</sup> Settlers from Atmora crossed the Sea of Ghosts and made landfall here many times, often clashing with those who had already established themselves.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3S-5)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FC-18)</sup> These early settlers called the land "Mereth", after the Elves that roamed the untamed wilderness. While the Nedic peoples were generally peaceful, the arrival of Ysgramor and the proto-Nords sparked a long, antagonistic chain of conquests that ultimately expelled the Elves from Skyrim.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-19)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup> The instigating spark of conflict was the infamous [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md), where the Elves attacked the human settlement of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md). According to Imperial scholars, the Elves realized that this newer race could outpace their relatively stagnant own. Others posit that the human inhabitants had discovered a powerful artifact, the [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md), that the Elves desired.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup> Nonetheless, Ysgramor escaped the carnage, returning to Skyrim with his now legendary band of [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md), slaughtering the Elves and establishing man as the foremost race in Skyrim.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup> Ysgramor's clan expanded their territory, and it continued to expand and contract by winning and losing territories in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3S-5)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TLST-21)</sup> Elven rule of Skyrim is thought to have ended under the reign of King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) ([1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143) - [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221)), though pockets of [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) hid away in the wilderness.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup>

In the years after [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), however, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) of [Clan Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md) migrated into Skyrim from [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md), following in the footsteps of [Clan Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md)'s exodus. Clan Kragen founded the city of [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md), which flourished despite constant attacks from the Nords. The success of Arkngthamz encouraged other Dwemer clans to settle in Skyrim, and these joined with Clan Kragen to form a loose alliance of four city-states that were considered unassailable. Clan Kragen's presence eventually extended further west into the [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), where they carved out an even larger bastion called [Arkngthamz-Phng](Lore_Fang_Lair.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AP-22)</sup> The prosperity of Skyrim's Dwemer city-states was relatively short-lived, as the discovery of rich deposits of [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md) and the creation of the Aetherium Forge immediately shattered the alliance and launched decades of internal conflict. With the Dwemer distracted and weakened, the Nords under High King [Gellir](Lore_Gellir.md) finally conquered the squabbling city-states in only three years, though the Dwemer would return a century later and reclaim their former holdings.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAW-23)</sup> When the entire Dwemer race disappeared around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), they left behind the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), Snow Elves who they had allowed to take refuge with them and then twisted into rebellious slave monsters.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TFAS-24)</sup> They remained under the ground, occasionally making forays to the surface and creating legends among the Nords of their presence and malevolent intent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TFAS-24)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) Skyrim circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432) After the assassination of High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), a dispute over the succession resulted in Skyrim's division into two independent kingdoms. It began when Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) challenged the legitimacy of Logrolf's heir, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md), and a [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) was convened. Although Freydis was accepted by the Crown of Verity, a magical artifact crafted to test the worthiness of potential rulers of Skyrim, and named High Queen in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), a partial Moot held in Solitude declared Svartr to be High King.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TCOF-25)</sup> From then on, Skyrim was divided into two kingdoms: Eastern Skyrim and Western Skyrim. In the east, Freydis and her successors ruled over [The Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [The Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TCOF-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESSO-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-27)</sup> while in the west, Svartr and his successors ruled over [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Mt C:ES-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-WSSO-29)</sup> [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-WSSO-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Mt C:ES-28)</sup> [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md),<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Gt WSHj-30)</sup> [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md),<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Gt WSH-31)</sup> and later the newly-established hold of Karthald.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gt WSK-10)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TCOF-25)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-27)</sup> This schism persisted at least until the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582);<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SIR-32)</sup> Eastern Skyrim fought in the war as a member of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), while Western Skyrim remained neutral.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TSAI-33)</sup> At that time, [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) did not belong to either kingdom and the Reachmen had banded together under Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the Despot of Markarth, who temporarily allied himself with [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-19)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-27)</sup> The two kingdoms of Skyrim, as well as The Reach, were eventually reunified into a single realm following the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TAH-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SRKibell-35)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the Kingdom of Skyrim waged the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) against [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), temporarily annexing the territories of [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md) from High Rock, and [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and the northern half of [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md) from Hammerfell.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3S-5)</sup>

[Orc](Lore_Orc.md) strongholds dot the wilderness; many Orcs were escorted into Skyrim by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) following the sacking of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) in the early Fourth Era.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TIF-36)</sup> Besides the Orcs, the Elven population has also increased due to the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), when natural disasters in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) sent many [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) fleeing into eastern Skyrim.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TRY-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-DOS-38)</sup> Following the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) officials hunted for and persecuted [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worshippers, thus bringing some small measure of Elven rule back to Skyrim.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-NA-39)</sup> While these smaller creatures vied for dominance over the millennia, the [Giants](Lore_Giant.md) have been largely ignored (and often protected by law).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup>

## Capitals of Skyrim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/ON-item-Crown_of_Freydis.jpg/200px-ON-item-Crown_of_Freydis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Crown_of_Freydis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Crown_of_Freydis.jpg) The Crown of Freydis In practice, the city where the High King resided also served as the capital of Skyrim. This has changed over the course of centuries, and at times of division there was more than one capital city. When the Dragon Cult dominated Skyrim until the late Merethic Era, the capital was at one point Bromjunaar, located at what would later be called [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md). When [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) assumed the mantle of High King in [1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143), he relinquished his holdings in Atmora and established Skyrim as an independent kingdom with [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) as its capital.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FC-18)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup> During the time of [Vrage the Gifted](Lore_Vrage.md) (r. 1E 222 – ?), Windhelm became the capital of not just Skyrim, but the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) that also encompassed [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup> High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md) (died 1E 369) ruled from [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), however.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-AHo D-47)</sup> High King [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) (r. 1E 420 – 1E 452) resided in [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-OATD-49)</sup> In the late First Era, [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) served as the seat of High Queens such as "Head-Rolling" Roleke and High Kings like [Alldimar](Lore_Alldimar.md) the Ghostmaker.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-So SR-55)</sup> During the schism of the Second Era (since 2E 431), [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) and his descendants ruled the Kingdom of Western Skyrim from Solitude, while [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md) and her descendants reigned the Kingdom of Eastern Skyrim from Windhelm.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TCOF-25)</sup> Winterhold was counted among other provincial capitals circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-MCMap-72)</sup> Solitude had been the capital of Skyrim for several years by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Thaer-73)</sup>

## Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-place-Winterhold.jpg/200px-SR-place-Winterhold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Winterhold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Winterhold.jpg) The stone and timber buildings of Winterhold [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/SR-place-Blue_Palace.jpg/200px-SR-place-Blue_Palace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Blue_Palace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Blue_Palace.jpg) The Blue Palace of Solitude
### Nordic
Nord architects are masters of creating structures that last for generations, with designs that can be found from [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) dating over 3,000 years old.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-19)</sup> Many ancient Nordic ruins built into the mountains dating back to the Merethic and First eras remain standing throughout Skyrim.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup> They are towering, foreboding structures of black granite. Some were settlements and temples from when the Nords lived under the rule of the Dragon Cult and after; others are burial tombs for the honored dead. Most modern buildings in Skyrim are built partly underground to conserve heat, and are made with stones, with wood used only for support, and roofs of straw. The Nords are known as masters of lumber construction, and these houses are essential to keep in heat in the freezing climate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup> Some Nord wells are known to have spikes attached to the bottom of buckets to crack through ice.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-19)</sup>

After the reign of Ysgramor, Nord stonesmiths created a method of erecting igneous rock. Initially, these blocks were hewed from porphyry deposits, although by the Second Era, they were somewhat supplanted by hard-wearing granite and volcanic stone gathered from the eastern side of the province. Each block was slotted together without requiring seams or mortar, allowing huge walls, structures, and towers to be constructed with both ease and haste, even with irregularly-shaped stone. Many of these structures proved difficult for invaders to dismantle, such as the Old Fort, a royal bastion on Skyrim's northern frontier, which still stood in the mid-Second Era after being built in the time of the First Empire.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-19)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup>

### Imperial
With inclusion in the Second and Third Empires, Imperial forts are spread across Skyrim's landscape.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-IF-74)</sup> Solitude is seen as the epicenter of Imperial influence.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-SRLoad Solitude-75)</sup>

### Snow Elf
Remaining elements of Snow Elf architecture is largely absent in most of Skyrim, the grandest examples being in the Chantry of Auriel within the Forgotten Vale, completed during the First Era, well into the Nords conquest of Skyrim.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-FV-76)</sup> Another example lays in the underground of Fort Greenwall.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-LLAYQ-77)</sup> Their architecture bears some superficial similarities to the Ayleids or Altmer stonecraft, and they were capable of making wayshrines with magical teleporting gates.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-FV-76)</sup> By the Second Era the Falmer made use of Chaurus chitin to make huts and fencing in their underground dwellings and rarely in their burgeoning surface outposts.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ONGG-78)</sup>

### Dwarven
The Dwemer cities and outposts of clan Kragen dots Skyrim's landscapes, most of which are abandoned.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-SDR-79)</sup> The major exception is the city of Markarth, formerly Nchuand-Zel, which acts as the capital of the Reach.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Markarth-80)</sup>

### Orcish
Several Orc Strongholds are found throughout Skyrim, typically centered around wooden long-houses, many lie directly outside mines. Strongholds are typically surrounded by wooden walls.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-OS-81)</sup>

## Flora
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg/200px-SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg) Skyrim's Pine Forest [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/SR-place-Eldergleam.jpg/200px-SR-place-Eldergleam.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Eldergleam.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Eldergleam.jpg) The [Eldergleam](Lore_Eldergleam.md) Skyrim's frigid environment still supports a great wealth of flora, notably its many pine forests.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Loading-17)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-HGTS-82)</sup> Many of its plants and fungi are useful for alchemy, including [bleeding crown](Lore_Bleeding_Crown.md) mushrooms, the [Deathbell](Lore_Deathbell.md) flower, [Jazbay](Lore_Jazbay.md) grapes, and [Nightshade](Lore_Nightshade.md). [Tundra cotton](Lore_Tundra_Cotton.md) is not only used for making potions, but for making many of the fabrics for clothing and other purposes in Skyrim.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-HGTS-82)</sup>

[Crimson Nirnroot](Lore_Crimson_Nirnroot.md) is a special variant of nirnroot that can be found exclusively in the caverns of Blackreach beneath the province.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-SFJ-83)</sup>

## Notable Places
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg/200px-SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg) Druadach Mountains
[Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md)
- A small island northeast of Skyrim and south of Solstheim.
[College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md)
- A famous school for Magic in the city of Winterhold.
[Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md)
- A busy port on the northern coast and capital of the Pale.
[Dunmeth Pass](Lore_Dunmeth_Pass.md)
- The main pass and one of the few traversable roads leading through the Velothi Mountains between northwestern Morrowind and eastern Skyrim.
[Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md)
- The capital of the eponymous hold is in southwestern Skyrim, close to the border with both Cyrodiil and Hammerfell.
[Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md)
- A lake in the western Rift.
[Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md)
- At the extreme northern tip of Skyrim's Broken Cape, it is the place where tradition holds that Ysgramor's Five Hundred Companions made landfall.
[Lake Ilinalta](Lore_Lake_Ilinalta.md)
- A lake in west-central Falkreath Hold, in the middle of Skyrim's Pine Forest.
[Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md)
- A western river running the length of Skyrim before emptying into the Sea of Ghosts near Solitude.
[Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md)
- An ancient Nordic ruin. Named for the labyrinth built sometime in the First Era by Arch-Mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md), although the ruins themselves are much older, having been the city of Bromjunaar, the capital of the Dragon Cult.
[Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md)
- The capital of the Reach is in the southwest corner of the province.
[Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md)
- The fairly small, humble town of little economic or strategic importance is noteworthy as the capital of Hjaalmarch.
[Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md)
- A secluded pass through the Jerall Mountains on the Cyrodiil-Skyrim border.
[Riften](Lore_Riften.md)
- The capital of the Rift, long a center for crime, lies close to both Cyrodiil and Morrowind.
[Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md)
- An ancient Nordic city that was sacked in the Night of Tears, Saarthal is where the [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md) was found in the Fourth Era.
[Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md)
- Fortress stronghold in the Druadach Mountains built by [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md)'s [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md).
[Snowhawk](Lore_Snowhawk.md)
- A city located in Hjaalmarch, abandoned sometime during the Fourth Era.
[Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md)
- A major port city and capital of Haafingar.
[Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md)
- The highest peak in the province of Skyrim. Though once considered second to Red Mountain, it is now known as the highest mountain in all of Tamriel.
[Treva River](Lore_Treva_River.md)
- A river at the center of the Rift. It flows from Lake Honrich to Lake Geir.
[White River](Lore_White_River.md)
- The longest river in Skyrim, stretching from the south of the province to the northeast corner. Its source is Lake Ilinalta, north of Falkreath. It flows northeast to join the Sea of Ghosts past Windhelm.
[Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md)
- A city in central Skyrim, and the capital of Whiterun Hold. Built at the spring of the White River.
[Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md)
- The capital of Eastmarch lies close to the border with Morrowind. It's the famed location of the Palace of the Kings.
[Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md)
- The capital of the eponymous hold was once quite prosperous, but was devastated in the Great Collapse in [4E 122](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_122).

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-48) During the War of Succession there was no High King for approximately 51 years.
2. [^](#cite_ref-50) [Rislav The Righteous](Lore_Rislav_The_Righteous.md) states that Kjoric the White was killed eight months before certain events in 1E 478, placing his death either in that year or in 1E 477.
3. [^](#cite_ref-51) Hoag Merkiller was [said to have](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md) died at the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md), after which Wulfharth ascended to the throne. However, [some sources](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Wulfharth_Plaque.png) place the date of the battle at 1E 480, and [others](Lore_The_Last_King_of_the_Ayleids.md) at 1E 482.
4. [^](#cite_ref-53) Gellir could not have reigned any earlier than 1E 533. His chief accomplishment is the conquest of Skyrim's Dwemer city-states, and according to *[Arkngthamz-Phng](Lore_Arkngthamz-Phng.md)*, those city-states were not established until the decades after Clan [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) founded [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md) in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420). Since the consecutive reigns of four other High Kings span the years between 1E 420 and 1E 533, Gellir could only have ruled sometime after 1E 533. *[The Aetherium Wars](Lore_The_Aetherium_Wars.md)* notes that the Dwemer reclaimed their lands "a century" after Gellir's conquests, and since the Dwemer had disappeared by [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), it is likely that Gellir's reign occurred before the end of the 6th Century of the First Era. [*The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: Prima Official Game Guide*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim:_Prima_Official_Game_Guide) and Katria's dialogue both state Arkngthamz was abandoned over four thousand years before *Skyrim*, contradicting this, as 4,000 years before [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) is [1E 451](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_451).
5. [^](#cite_ref-56) This unidentified descendant of Temylda was crowned High King forty years after Temylda's death while still a child. He was quickly usurped by Alldimar the Ghostmaker according to *[Scandals of Solitude's Royalty](Lore_Scandals_of_Solitude%27s_Royalty.md)*.
6. [^](#cite_ref-Schism_Rulers_58-0) Skyrim split into independent Eastern and Western kingdoms in 2E 431 amid a dispute over the succession. Freydis was proclaimed High Queen in Windhelm, and her successors (who include Mabjaarn Flame-Hair, Nurnhilde, and Jorunn the Skald King) ruled Eastern Skyrim. Svartr was proclaimed High King in Solitude, and his successors ruled Western Skyrim.
7. [^](#cite_ref-65) The map of PGE1S displays Winterhold as the capital of Skyrim Province.
8. [^](#cite_ref-71) During the Stormcloak Rebellion there was no High King. The kingship was claimed both by the rebel leader [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), Jarl of Windhelm, who killed the previous High King Torygg, and by Torygg's widow [Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md), Jarl of Solitude, who was supported by the Empire.
