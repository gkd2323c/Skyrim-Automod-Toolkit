Umbacano was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) mage and suspected [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [Revivalist](Lore_Ayleid_Revivalists.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), he was working at the Yassu [glass](Lore_Malachite.md) mine in the dangerous [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), along with a fellow Altmer Ayleid enthusiast named Sorcalin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Umbacano had migrated to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), become considerably wealthy, and had begun employing adventurers to seek out Ayleid artifacts across [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for his private collection.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

In 3E 433, Umbacano enlisted the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to search the province's Ayleid ruins for ten ancient statues known as the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBTC-4)</sup> He also tasked the Hero with recovering an ancient carving from the ruins of [Malada](Lore_Malada.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBNYCP-5)</sup> as well as the [Crown](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md) of [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md), the king of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md). It is not known if the Hero recovered the crown or accepted rival collector [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md)'s proposal to trick Umbacano into accepting the crown of the rival city of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBSot A-6)</sup>

What is known is that Umbacano took one of the crowns to the throne room of Nenalata and attempted to use it in order to restore Nenalata to its former glory. There are conflicting accounts of what happened next; some say that Umbacano used the crown of Nenalata in a ritual to have himself recognized as the new King of Nenalata, taking on a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) -like appearance as a consequence and resulting in his death at the hands of the Hero of Kvatch. Others say that he invoked the crown of Lindai and inadvertently killed himself, destroying the crown in the process. Regardless of the specifics, Umbacano is known to have died inside Nenalata.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
