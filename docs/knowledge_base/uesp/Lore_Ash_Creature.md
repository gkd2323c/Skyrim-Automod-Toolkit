# Ash Creature

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7f/MW-concept-Ash_Creatures.png/200px-MW-concept-Ash_Creatures.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Ash_Creatures.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Ash_Creatures.png) Concept art of Sixth House ash creatures *I will continue to draw divine power from the Heart and distribute it to my kin and followers. I will continue to broadcast divine power upon the blight winds, so that it will touch each soul in Vvardenfell, and then more broadly, across the waters to the rest of Morrowind and Tamriel. In time, every mortal in Tamriel shall feel the liberating contact with the divine.*
—Dagoth Ur<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dagoth-1)</sup> Ash creature (or **Red Mountain monster**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> is a term that applies to disparate ashen beings linked to the divine power of the Missing God [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LG-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skin-4)</sup> More specifically, they are typically linked to the ash (or "[Blight](Lore_Ash_Blight.md)")<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOV36-5)</sup> of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), and the power of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) that lay within it.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Neloth-6)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dagoth-1)</sup> The blighted ash of Red Mountain is associated with life-giving powers and immortality. According to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and various other sources, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) once utilized the ash of Red Mountain, the Blight, to make themselves immortal.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOV36-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NARM-7)</sup> Contact with the powers of the Heart of Lorkhan is associated with the [First Walking Way](Lore_Walking_Ways.md#First_Walking_Way_-_The_Numidium), a means of [attaining divinity](Lore_Apotheosis.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LOV15-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup>

## Sixth House
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) The Heart of Lorkhan *Forgive the rude welcome, but until you have declared for us, we must treat you as our enemy. The Sleepers and Dreamers are newly come to Lord Dagoth, and not yet blessed with his power. But the Children of His Flesh, they are deep in the heart of his mysteries. Their bodies swell to contain his glory, and to yield the rich sacraments of our Lord's feasts. And we are the least of his servants, for Ash Poets, Ascended Sleepers, and Ash Vampires stand high above us in the Lord's bountiful grace.*
—Dagoth Gares<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) through rituals of his own devising,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Plan-11)</sup> succeeded in establishing an irreversible connection to the Heart of Lorkhan for himself and his followers. Doing so intrinsically tied his very being into the Heart, allowing him various abilities typically associated with the Blight, Immortality, and more.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Dagoth Ur-12)</sup>

Dagoth Ur would broadcast the divine influence of the Heart via the [Ash Blight](Lore_Ash_Blight.md), reaching the dreams of the people of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) to recruit them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dagoth-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Yakum H-13)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Plan-11)</sup> The Ash Blight was also responsible for infecting them with the "Divine Disease", [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md). Those that received this "gift" and were deemed weaker, became deranged Corprus beasts, such as [Corprus Stalkers](Lore_Corprus_Stalker.md) and [Lame Corprus](Lore_Lame_Corprus.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Plan-11)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-14)</sup> These individuals were known as the "Children of His Flesh".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) who were worthy enough to follow him in the [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md), known as Sleepers and Dreamers, instead began bizarre mutations, evolving through various stages of enlightenment.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Plan-11)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dagoth-1)</sup> His blight and corprus was seen as a gift by him and his followers,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dagoth-1)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dreamer-15)</sup> the mutilation of the bodies of the Dunmer he viewed as them shedding the cursed skin brought to them by the Tribunal.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Vivec referred to the sharing of the Heart of Lorkhan's power by the Sharmat as the crime of benevolence.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LOV15-8)</sup>

Chief among Dagoth Ur's servants were his seven brothers, the [Ash Vampires](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md), a unique type of ash creature described as powerful Heartwights and cunning sorcerers of old.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vivec-17)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> Similar in appearance to Dagoth Ur himself, possessing sharp claws and a third eye, they served as the nobility of House Dagoth.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AVamp-18)</sup> They were known to have slept alongside Dagoth Ur under Red Mountain for thousands of years.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PTDDU-19)</sup> Dagoth Ur was able to give portions of his power and immortality to these few individuals of his original House.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vivec-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PTDDU-19)</sup> As such when they appeared to die, they would eventually be revived at the Heart.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vivec-17)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-20)</sup> While they are referred to as "[vampires](Lore_Vampire.md)", they are not actually a form of [undead](Lore_Undead.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VOV-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LOTD-22)</sup>

The lesser ash creatures are considered the least of Dagoth's servants,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> and [ash salts](Lore_Alchemy_A.md#Ash_Salts) can be collected from their remains.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWGen Ash Salts-23)</sup> The lowest of these ash creatures are the [Ash Slaves](Lore_Ash_Slave.md) and [Ash Zombies](Lore_Ash_Zombie.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-14)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> Ash Slaves have lost their eyes and can cast a variety of minor spells including spell reflection.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Slave-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup> They often had bizzare obsessions with stacking furniture.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Slave-24)</sup> Ash Zombies have hollowed out upper faces and apparently empty skulls,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Zomb-26)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-14)</sup> their heads are said to have burst from enlightenment.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-27)</sup> The Ash Zombies created by Erden Relvel had their faces manually carved and their lips stitched into smiles.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ERN-28)</sup> They are intelligent, aggressive,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-14)</sup> and attack indiscriminately, although they can be calmed using spells. When spoken to, however, they can only repeat a few agonized phrases.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Zomb-26)</sup>

The more advanced mutations are highly intelligent and possess powerful magic, often described in appearance as "half-beast".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-14)</sup> [Ash Ghouls](Lore_Ash_Ghoul.md) are seemingly the next mutation of Ash Zombies, a strange trunk-like tentacle growth emerging from the hole in their head.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Ghoul-29)</sup> They are more coherent than their lesser brethren, and rank higher in the Sixth House.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-14)</sup> However they still consider themselves among the least of Dagoth Ur's servants.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> Ash Ghoul [heart tissue](Lore_Alchemy_G.md#Ghoul_Heart) harvested from their remains is used as an ingredient in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-MWGen Ghoul Heart-30)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-31)</sup> [Ascended Sleepers](Lore_Ascended_Sleeper.md) are the final and most powerful mutation of these ash creatures,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Plan-11)</sup> ranking highly within the Sixth House.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gares-10)</sup> They are highly intelligent beings with faces covered in strange tentacles filled with holes,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Sleeper-32)</sup> these tentacles are seemingly used as [musical](Lore_Music.md) [instruments](Lore_Instruments.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-33)</sup> They can afflict their opponents with a variety of deadly Blight diseases as well as damaging frost and fire attacks.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Sleeper-32)</sup>

After the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) killed Dagoth Ur and his Ash Vampire kin, the remaining ash creatures and Corprus beasts survived and still had to be hunted down and destroyed.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vivec-17)</sup> Dagoth Ur's followers largely fell into despair after his death.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dreamer-15)</sup> Many years later, circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Tribunal cultist Erden Relvel became influenced by the lingering influence of the Sharmat in his mask. From listening to his master's words, Erden became a priest of Dagoth Ur and learned how to spawn Corprus. He used this knowledge to create new Ash Zombies, transforming his fellow Temple priest Arthamis and others into unthinking minions of Dagoth Ur. He then sent these creatures to attack the temple at Ashfall's Tear. Ultimately, he hoped to spread his master's "cure" across the entire world.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ERN-28)</sup>

## Heart Stone
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Heart_Stone.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Heart_Stone.png) A Heart Stone *I feel its heartbeat rattling in my head like a loose rock. Rattle, rattle, rattle. A curious song. Not a heart of blood, but stone.*
—Priest Arthamis describing Dagoth Ur's lingering influence<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-34)</sup> [Heart Stones](Lore_Heart_Stone.md) are molten rocks that are believed by Master [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) to have lain next to the Heart of Lorkhan for millennia, and as a result some vestige of its power has seeped into them. Deposits of them surfaced on (or *landed* on the surface of) [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) during the cataclysms of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md); the eruption of Red Mountain also being responsible for covering much of the island's southern coast in ash.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Neloth-6)</sup>

[Ash Spawn](Lore_Ash_Spawn.md) are seemingly undead creatures that emerge in the [Ashlands](Lore_Solstheim_Ashlands.md) of southern Solstheim. They are created from the heart stones giving life to the bones of Solstheim's dead buried in the wastes and infused with the ash of Red Mountain. They are immune to poison, highly resistant to fire, and have no particular weaknesses.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-35)</sup> The Telvanni studied these creatures and developed a conjuration spell capable of summoning them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Neloth-6)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-36)</sup> A prior apprentice of Neloth, [Ildari Sarothril](Lore_Ildari_Sarothril.md), was killed during an experiment and brought back to life due to a heart stone having been grafted to her.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Neloth-6)</sup> Ildari learned that a stone could be used to prevent [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) spells from wearing off. She used them to bolster her necromantic spells used to revive [Falx Carius](Lore_Falx_Carius.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-37)</sup>

[Ash Guardians](Lore_Ash_Guardian.md) are [golems](Lore_Golem.md) resembling [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md), created by the Telvanni during their study of the Ash Spawn.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-38)</sup> They can be summoned from the ash, but will attack indiscriminately unless controlled with a heart stone.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

## The Ash King
*Shor gathered an army as he did of old, and then he sucked in the long-strewn ashes of King Wulfharth and remade him, for he needed a good general. But the Devil of Dagoth petitioned to be that general, too, and he pointed out his role as the blessed harbinger of this holy war. So Shor had two generals, the Ash King and the Devil of Dagoth, and he marched on the eastern kingdoms with all the sons of Skyrim.*
— *[The Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)* [Ysmir](Lore_Ysmir.md) [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) is an immortal figure from [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) history who was known by the moniker of "Ash-King" after his resurrection as a form of ash creature. His long-strewn ashes were said to have been sucked in and remade by the ghost of [Shor](Lore_Shor.md), the Nordic version of Lorkhan. This was said to have been done in preparation for the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), which the Nords got involved with due to "the Devil of Dagoth" informing them that the Heart of Shor was within Red Mountain,<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-FSKW-40)</sup> as it was being used by the Dwarves to power their [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-41)</sup> Wulfharth's resurrection in ash was said to have resulted in the loss of his cultural identity, being only concerned for getting revenge against the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TAH-42)</sup> Wulfharth was also claimed to have walked the first Walking Way,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup> the same path followed by Dagoth Ur's use of the Heart of Lorkhan.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LOV15-8)</sup>

There have been mentions of other ashen figures in dubious Nordic myth, such as those said to have been part of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s five hundred [companions](Lore_Companions.md).<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-T500MC-43)</sup> A Nordic individual in Vivec's sermons known as [Chemua](Lore_Chemua.md), the Running Hunger, was sometimes known as the "First Blighter". He was said to have the powers of "Heart Roaring and of sky sickening", causing the rains in Morrowind to turn into bile, and was capable of eating the spirits of his enemies and sending them back against their former allies as revenants, like he did with the Chimeri hero Dres Khizumet-e.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-LOV9-44)</sup>
