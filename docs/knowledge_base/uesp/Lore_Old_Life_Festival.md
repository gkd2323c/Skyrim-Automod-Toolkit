# Old Life Festival

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-quest-Old_Life_Observance.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Old_Life_Observance.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Old_Life_Observance.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Old_Life_Observance.jpg) An Old Life pilgrimage site The Old Life Festival, sometimes simply called **Old Life**, is primarily an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) holiday<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOPG-1)</sup> but it is also celebrated throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by other cultures in their own unique ways due to the reach and influence of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFOL-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESTOC-3)</sup><sup>:16</sup> It falls at the last day of the year, and is a time people write messages of remembrance for their dead loved ones, and may occasionally receive an answer from Aetherius.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOPG-1)</sup> Old Life pilgrimage shrines can be found throughout Tamriel, from the west in [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)'s [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), all the way to the east in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOLO-4)</sup> People who are unable to travel to the pilgrimage sites can have a proxy deliver their messages, and if they receive a response, they will know.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOPG-1)</sup>

During the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) in the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), the holiday was celebrated by going to the temple to reflect on their past. Some would go for more than this, for it is rumored that priests will as the last act of the year perform resurrections on beloved friends and family members free of the usual charge. Worshippers know better than to expect this philanthropy, but they arrive in a macabre procession with the recently deceased nevertheless.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFOL-2)</sup>

The Old Life Festival complements the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md), and both are considered two sides of the same coin. The Old Life Festival being a day of rest before the New Life Festival, which is one big party. Summarized neatly as "the day we ask forgiveness for old sins, before committing new ones", even unpopular temples in are packed with people.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> By acknowledging those that have passed, one can begin to move forward, as the pilgrimage shrines serve as a vessel for letting the departed know the things people held to themselves while they were alive. Those who receive a response from a spirit may feel it is a life-changing experience.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOPG-1)</sup>

## Traditions
Witches celebrate Old Life with dark festivities, and to cast aside the old to bring in the new year, the living bring them the husks of those that died. To summon the [sun](Lore_Sun.md), the dead are reanimated to dance a jig along with the living.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NLFI-6)</sup>

Dark tales and unsettling songs are told as part of the Old Life traditions to commemorate the shortening of the days with Old Life. Tales like *The Unknown Passenger* involving an unknown hitchhiker, *The Lost Fawn in the Woods*, where the fawn learns of a wolf wearing its mother's skin and in its panick, flees into the wolf's den, and *The Midnight Lodger*, where the renter vanishes with the innkeeper's child before morning. One of these stories, *The Wolf That Craved the Sky*, involves Storihbeg,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NLFI-6)</sup> [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s Skinchanger aspect.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FOLTN-7)</sup> The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) version states that with the coming of the New Life Festival, two of Hircine's aspects come to conflict with each other. New Life marks the [winter solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md#Winter_Solstice), when the days begin to lengthen due to the return of the sun. Storihbeg the Man-Beast thus pursues the sun in an attempt to eat it, an effort to keep the nights longer. To prevent this, Reachmen summon Hrokkibeg the Mighty Bear to challenge Storihbeg.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NLFI-6)</sup>

In the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) version of the tale, the Skinchanger’s endless hunger once drove it to feast on Morrowind’s volcanoes, creating the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) by draining the Iliac Bay, and to use Valenwood’s trees for oral hygiene. Seeing the moons untroubled, the Skinchanger claimed it could reach them and assume their forms, but the moons warned that the sun would notice. The Skinchanger vowed to devour the sun and stars as well, filling the world with darkness and letting out a triumphant howl. Yet the moons mocked him, saying the sun’s light was too rich a meal. Waiting for the sun’s weakest moment during the Old Life Festival, the Skinchanger struck, only for the sun to flare brightly at the New Life Festival and stop the devourer in pain.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FOLTN-7)</sup>

The Rite of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) is an endeavor performed by Cyrodilic elders in preparation for the New Life Festival, in which their party goes on a pilgrimage to rid the nearby forests of "Old Life": monsters such as [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md). The party then challenges an Avatar of Akatosh, which manifests as a fearsome golden [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). By successfully defeating this avatar, the elders believe that the God of Time will grant them more time, youth, or peace. Some also hope that that the feat will grant them respect.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## Notes
- *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* had the last day of the year and therefore the day the Old Life Festival fell on in its calendar as the 30th of Evening Star.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFOL-2)</sup> However, future Elder Scrolls games has the last day of the year as the 31st of Evening Star.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SR-9)</sup>
