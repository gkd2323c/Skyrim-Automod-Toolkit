# House Redoran

— *[Great Houses of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses_of_Morrowind)* House Redoran (also called **Clan Redoran**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> the **Red Party of Redoran**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Generic Dialogue G-2)</sup> or "Red Hats")<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup> is one of the five remaining [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). Led by the House Archmaster,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GDV-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWSarethi-5)</sup> it governs the Redoran District of northwestern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from the city of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md), close to the border with [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). In the District of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the House's council seat was located in [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Balmora Alignment-6)</sup> as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) as of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup> In the latter, all councilors lived in the hollowed shell of a colossal prehistoric Emperor Crab, in the district known as Ald'ruhn-under-Skar.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GAR-8)</sup> Other Redoran-dominated towns on the mainland included [Cormaris View](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cormaris_View) and [Silgrad Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silgrad_Tower),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-conceptmap-10)</sup> as well as [Khuul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khuul), [Maar Gan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maar_Gan) and [Ald Velothi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ald_Velothi) in Vvardenfell.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWRed Towns-11)</sup>

In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Redoran also controlled the settlements of Balmora, [Suran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Suran), and [Gnisis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnisis). The Redoran also maintained a garrison in [Molag Mar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Mar) and, in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), maintained a strong military presence in the nascent [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Redoran Guards-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CL-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Lt R-14)</sup> In later years, prior to its destruction in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), House Redoran would instead hold an eponymous canton in Vivec City.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Red Canton-15)</sup> House Redoran holdings on the mainland included Tal'Deic Fortress,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TDOwners-16)</sup> the village of [Serkamora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Serkamora),<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Berendas-17)</sup> and the [Darkshade Caverns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darkshade_Caverns) kwama mines.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TIEGTTM-18)</sup> Despite [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) rule, House Redoran Sentinels patrolled the streets of [Old Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Ebonheart) during the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md)'s invasion of [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Redoran_Sentinel-19)</sup>

The main focus of House Redoran is maintaining the traditions of the settled Dunmer and, more specifically, the way of the warrior. As a result, House Redoran has long served as Morrowind's de-facto military. Members of House Redoran often believe themselves to be naturally superior to the other Great Houses.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Inf Vvard Factions-20)</sup> Due to the House's emphasis on tradition and piety, the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) was a natural ally, with the majority of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [Buoyant Armigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Buoyant_Armigers) hailing from the House.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BASV-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-22)</sup> House Redoran maintained strained relationships with the [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) tribes of Vvardenfell, the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong), [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), and [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HRA-23)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Redoran Friends Foes-24)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Balmora ESO-25)</sup> They held respect for [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril), and were willing to fight alongside House Dres.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup> During the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s reign over Morrowind, the Redoran also held mutual respect for the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). They did not hold other [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) institutions (such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), and the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md)) in such high esteem. Like the other Great Houses, House Redoran loathed the cultists of the [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dagoth) and the [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) of Vvardenfell, and disapproved of the [Camonna Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camonna_Tong)'s illegal activities.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Redoran Friends Foes-24)</sup>

Saint [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) the Captain is the patron saint of House Redoran.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MDGH-26)</sup> House Redoran honors the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) as creators and forebears, for without the divine there is no service, no law, and no understanding of their place and purpose.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TNC-27)</sup>

## History

### First Era
*From the original formation of House Redoran, our housekin have held the virtues of duty, gravity, and piety most dear. A frivolous, soft life is not worth living. We focus on maintaining the traditions of the civilized Dunmer and holding fast to the way of the warrior. Our military strength, for example, outclasses the other Great Houses by a wide margin. Our prowess puts us at least one step ahead of them all and in most cases many more.*
— *[Understanding House Redoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Understanding_House_Redoran)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/ON-misc-Banner Image Redoran.png/290px-ON-misc-Banner Image Redoran.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Banner Image Redoran.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Banner Image Redoran.png) The House Redoran House Redoran was founded in ancient times on the 3rd of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md), a date still celebrated by members of the Great House.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Cont Comm Nail Clip-28)</sup> The House has existed since the time of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PSII-29)</sup> By the 107th year of the Golden Peace, they were already considered the most militarily powerful of the Great Houses.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-UHR-30)</sup>

Prior to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), thirty of the most influential and revered Chimer Clans worked together to build the fabled Library of Andule, a grand repository containing the genealogical records related to the earliest [Velothi](Lore_Veloth.md) settlers and other assorted knowledge about the earliest history of the Great Houses.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Bradyn-31)</sup> Five of the thirty Revered Families hailed from House Redoran; the [Heran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heran), Redas, Sarano, Seran, and Venim Clans.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Redoran Tombs-32)</sup> The locations of the tombs were presumed lost in the aftermath of the War of the First Council, along with the Library of Andule and the records contained within.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Bradyn-31)</sup> The five tombs were rediscovered in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), alongside the other twenty-five families and the Library of Andule itself.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Library Of Andule Quests-33)</sup>

According to both Athyn Sarethi and Agrippa Fundilius, warriors of House Redoran fought and died at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) under the leadership of Hortator Indoril Nerevar during the War of the First Council.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TNC-27)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-34)</sup> During the decades in the aftermath of the war, at least one Redoran - General Redoran-Vorilk - had a role in discussing the nascent Tribunal Temple's plans to reorganize the House lands of Morrowind.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PSVII-35)</sup> In the centuries that followed, the Redoran watched the northwestern border of Morrowind along the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) from their ancestral seat in Blacklight, defending against the Nordic raiders from Skyrim who had pillaged the land since time immemorial.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Nord Rumor-36)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Hallfrida-37)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Elone-38)</sup> The constant warring with the Nords likely contributed to the warrior-House's hardened militaristic leanings and position as the 'hereditary defenders of Morrowind.'<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Defenders Rumor-39)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Hallfrida-37)</sup> The House adopted the color red as a designation.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Red Book-40)</sup> The House Symbol is the [Scarab](Lore_Scarab.md), and it is considered sacred by members of the Great House.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-CM52R-41)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-42)</sup> House Redoran did not become heavily involved in the practice of slavery. Indeed, in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) it was said that slavery was uncommon in Redoran lands, and no Redoran-owned slaves were observed on Vvardenfell in the Second or Third eras.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Brallion-43)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-place-Ald%27ruhn.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ald%27ruhn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald%27ruhn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald%27ruhn.jpg) The ancient remains of Skar, slain by Dranoth Hleran. Hleran's bloodline would go on to become part of House Redoran, and the shell would become the city of Ald'ruhn As House Redoran evolved, the standard members were expected to follow suit. Eventually, a set of rules were created and recited to all members of the Great House. The rules stated that a Redoran cannot steal from their kin, strike their kin unprovoked, or break their word. Honoring one's superiors, doing one's duty to the House, and respecting the teachings of the Tribunal was expected of members. Finally, a Redoran must always defend their House, their people, and their honor. If a Redoran failed in any of these things, they were cast out with an opportunity for redemption. If cast out again, the Redoran would lose any hope of regaining their honor or membership within the Great House.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Redoran Rules-44)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/MW-concept-ashlands.png/200px-MW-concept-ashlands.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-ashlands.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-ashlands.png) Redoran in the Ashlands Dranoth Hleran, whose bloodline would one day be of House Redoran, is credited as the one who struck the killing blow on Skar, the ancient Emperor Crab around which Ald'ruhn was built.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-BDH-45)</sup>

In the First Era, an all- [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) gang of thieves from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) infamous for their crimes in Morrowind came to be known as the Blacklight Raiders, after the Redoran capital city. Starting out as just a small band of adventurers, the Raiders eventually traveled all over Morrowind, looting and pillaging as they went. Attributed with stealing some of the Dunmer's most sacred relics, the Blacklight Raiders were buried in the city of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md). Their graves were discovered in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by a Dunmeri woman named Llotha Nelvani, who hailed from a village outside of Blacklight. Nelvani succeeded in unlocking the Raiders' final puzzle, opening a lockbox said to contain their greatest treasure. To her dismay, it contained only a "silly old blade".<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Blacklight Raiders-46)</sup>

The crypts beneath Tal'Deic Fortress in the eastern [Siltreen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Siltreen) region of [the Deshaan plains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deshaan) were originally excavated to serve as burial vaults for the noble Redoran family. They were abandoned after Grandmaster Llerlu inspected them and found the space "small, constricted, and insufficiently imposing for the honored dead of House Redoran."<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TDC-47)</sup>

In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), at the close of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), the dubiously-real Dunmeri woman [Turala](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Turala) was said to have been exiled from Morrowind by her Redoran family after she was impregnated by [Brindisi Dorom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brindisi_Dorom), the Duke of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). Allegedly, she would go on to summon [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) in a bid to kill Dorom and sack Mournhold.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-2920LYFE-48)</sup>

### Second Era
*A Redoran's duty is first to the Tribunal Temple, second to the Great House Redoran, and third to one's family and clan. A Redoran noble must know the virtue of gravity. It is not the Redoran way to laugh at serious matters, for it shows disrespect. It is not the Redoran way to spread rumors, for they fester and breed dissention * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] *.*
— *[The True Noble's Code](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_True_Noble%27s_Code)* At some point prior to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), House Redoran staked claim to the western half of Vvardenfell, including the [Bitter Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bitter_Coast), [Ascadian Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascadian_Isles),<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOSuran-49)</sup> [West Gash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Gash), and a portion of the inner [Ashlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlands).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Hleran-50)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HRA-23)</sup> The Redoran employed the House Hlaalu Construction Syndic to create the modern incarnations of Balmora and Suran for what was deemed a suspiciously low cost. Against the Redoran's wishes, the Hlaalu built the settlements in their own style, rather than the more organic Redoran style. Some Redoran worried that the Hlaalu intended to conquer the settlements through underhanded mercantile means. These concerns, in time, would prove valid.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-HCS-51)</sup> By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), both settlements flew the golden banner of House Hlaalu.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Bal Sur-52)</sup> Sometime prior to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), House Redoran also established the mining village of Gnisis in the northwest of Vvardenfell, and secured a presence in the settlement of Molag Mar. In Vivec City, the House secured a strong military presence after answering Vivec's call for warriors to protect the pilgrims who journeyed to the city.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-LSReddit-53)</sup> By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), House Redoran controlled Vivec City in tandem with the Tribunal Temple, and an entire garrison of the Redoran military under the command of General Vayne Redoran resided within the developing city.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CL-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Lt R-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Inf Vvard Factions-20)</sup> Other Redoran holdings erected around this time included a garrison northeast of Balmora,<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Red Garri-54)</sup> and Arenim Manor in the northern Bitter Coast.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-APWrit-55)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/LG-cardart-Redoran_Oathman.png/200px-LG-cardart-Redoran_Oathman.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Redoran_Oathman.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Redoran_Oathman.png) A Second Era Redoran After the coronation of [High King Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of Western Skyrim in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), a delegation from Lord Vivec presented the instrument 'Jarlsbane' to the High King. After being declared "grossly untunable and overly complicated" by the court musicians, it sat in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md)'s [Blue Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace) as a curiosity for many years. It was rediscovered over a century later by Master Bard Endroni Selvilo of House Redoran shortly before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Nevertheless, the instrument remained in the city, relocated to the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-J-56)</sup>

Some time after the outbreak of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), House Redoran sent a military expedition to the [Ruins of Mazzatun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruins_of_Mazzatun) in the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) region of [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen), intending to wipe out the Xit-Xaht tribe, whom they suspected to be responsible for the disease.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Tfa Iw RDEADV-57)</sup> In Vvardenfell, the House regularly sent exploratory forces out to tame the Ashlands from their stronghold in Balmora, where they came into increasingly violent conflicts with the local Ashlanders as more and more of Vvardenfell was settled. The lands around Ald'ruhn were particularly coveted by the House.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Intro To Vvard-58)</sup> Despite this, official Redoran policy maintained violence against Ashlanders to be a last resort, and preferred peaceful transaction of territory over taking land with force.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-HRO-59)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ONMeriath-60)</sup>

During the mid-Second Era, House Redoran's leader was High Councilor Meriath.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Meriath Role-61)</sup> After the defeat of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal)'s [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) forces at [Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec%27s_Antlers) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), House Redoran was among the four Great Houses that joined the newly-formed [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-PP:C-62)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup> Following the eruption of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), House Redoran's warriors would be among those that fought for control of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), with the House's powerful military playing an active role in the conflict.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Inf Vvard Factions-20)</sup> Rumors persisted that Redoran nobles were upset the Pact was formed in the first place, due to having to share command with Nordic and Argonian allies.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup> During this time, House Redoran also maintained a "Narcotics Oath-Bureau" purportedly charged with the regulation of illegal substances, such as [Skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skooma).<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-JFA-63)</sup> House Hlaalu, whom was officially considered an ally of House Redoran in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HRA-23)</sup> complained of Redoran bailiffs and constables treating Hlaalu House Guests poorly. The Hlaalu urged House Redoran to defer to them on matters of commerce and diplomacy, as the Hlaalu themselves deferred to House Redoran on matters of defense.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-HLC-64)</sup> Redoran-Hlaalu relations were further shaken by Hlaalu practices that undermined Redoran trade routes and business ventures, and their apparent desire to seize Balmora and Suran for themselves.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HRA-23)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-HCS-51)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Redoran-ruled village of Serkamora in Deshaan was infiltrated by the Maulborn Cult, who posed as healers aiding with the rampant [Llodos Plague](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Llodos_Plague). In truth, the Maulborn had created the plague in a bid to undermine the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)'s rule and turn their citizens against them. It is unknown whether Dethisam Berendas, Serkamora's local Redoran representative, survived the events.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Serk ESO-65)</sup> In Tal'Deic Fortress, General Gavryn Redoran was killed by the Maulborn Cult and replaced with a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) simulacrum. With the aid of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the Redoran Captain Valec Doronil exposed the General's death and impersonation, and defeated the Maulborn in the crypts beneath the fortress.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Tal.27Deic ESO-66)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/ON-npc-High_Councilor_Meriath.jpg/200px-ON-npc-High_Councilor_Meriath.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Councilor_Meriath.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Councilor_Meriath.jpg) Meriath, High Councilor of Great House Redoran circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/3/3e/ON-concept-House_Banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-House_Banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-House_Banner.png) A simplified variant of the Redoran banner popular in the Second Era. Simultaneously, House Redoran faced issues with its holdings in the western half of Vvardenfell. With much of House Redoran's soldiers committed to the Ebonheart Pact abroad, the House's resources became dangerously limited when it came to dealing with daedric cultists, ashlanders, and other threats to Redoran prosperity. At the recommendation of Councilor Dolvara, Balmora's Captain Brivan Malrom enlisted the aid of the Khajiiti mercenaries known as Ferhara's Warclaws - ex-Aldmeri Dominion soldiers initially hired by House Hlaalu - to supplement the depleted Redoran military in contested territory. This was done despite protests from the House Council, who believed that Redoran soldiers were more than capable of protecting House interests.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Warclaws_1-67)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Warclaws_2-68)</sup> Tensions ran high with the nomadic ashlanders of Vvardenfell when Redoran land claims clashed with ashlander holdings.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-HRO-59)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ATC-69)</sup> In Balmora, Captain Ulran Releth, son of Councilor Eris Releth, was exiled from House Redoran after an incident orchestrated by his father went awry. Ulran was later killed during a raid on an ashlander encampment by Ferhara's Warclaws on orders from Captain Brivan Malrom.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-HRO-59)</sup> Soon afterwards, Redoran Councilor Dolvara was executed by the Morag Tong. In retaliation for her brother's exile and death, Veya Releth, Councilor Releth's daughter, attempted to assassinate the Redoran Council during a meeting. The wayward Councilor's daughter was stopped by the Morag Tong, but not before claiming the lives of her father and Captain Brivan.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Balmora ESO-25)</sup> In Suran, the local head of security, Marshal Hlaren, was exposed by a vigilante known as the Scarlet Judge for corruption and slave peddling, and promptly executed. The Marshal had been using her station to order the arrests of visitors on minor offenses and then selling them as slaves.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Scarlet-70)</sup> In Gnisis, requests for aid from House Redoran went unanswered while the local kwama mine faced troubles with a Dwemer device and a slain kwama queen, with the troubles eventually resolved locally.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Gnisis ESO-71)</sup> In Ald'ruhn, Dranoth Hleran's descendant Drelyth rediscovered his family's history with Skar and recovered his ancestor's spear, Calderas - the very weapon used to kill Skar millennia before. Hleran's affiliation with House Redoran marked the first known time the Great House's influence extended to Ald'ruhn.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Hleran-50)</sup>

Flags bearing the symbol of House Redoran commemorating the many victories the House claims credit for achieving were in circulation during the sixth century of the Second Era.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Cont HRVF-72)</sup> Elynea Omayn, daughter of a Redoran Councilor, was noted as an exemplary member of Vivec's Buoyant Armigers during the mid-Second Era by Archcanon Tarvus. Despite having had the opportunity to be a high-ranking officer in the Redoran military or a Redoran representative to another Ebonheart Pact nation, Omayn chose the life of an Armiger and became one of the god's favorite servants. It was said that the noble ideals and sense of adventure carried by the Buoyant Armigers matched closely with Redoran virtues, drawing many from Redoran to Vivec's service.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BASV-21)</sup>

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the Imperial Geographical Society reported House Redoran as being "the best warriors among the Dark Elves", noting that the House guarded the western flank of Morrowind.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup>

In the late Second Era, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s legions turned their eyes towards Morrowind. Following House Indoril's lead, House Redoran swore to resist the western invaders to the death, and provided its mercenary companies to the Dunmeri defense amassing in settlements such as Silgrad Tower and Cormaris View. However, House Redoran soon found itself manning the western borders of Morrowind alone, with House Indoril and House Dres preferring to retreat inwards to wage a guerrilla war, House Telvanni remaining neutral, and House Hlaalu advocating accommodation. When Vivec signed the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), House Redoran breathed a sigh of relief, now permitted an honorable way to avoid waging war against the Third Empire's vast legions alone.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OM-9)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/LG-cardart-Bolvyn_Venim.png/200px-LG-cardart-Bolvyn_Venim.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Bolvyn_Venim.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Bolvyn_Venim.png) Archmaster Bolvyn Venim, circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg/200px-MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg) Areas of Vvardenfell controlled by House Redoran in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) (red areas). *A Redoran is a warrior whose duty is first to the Tribunal, second to House Redoran, and third to family and clan.*
— *[Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mottos_of_the_Dunmeri_Great_Houses)* During the Third Era, House Redoran's traditionalist stances would see the House decline in strength.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-PGE3-73)</sup> Further damaging Redoran influence and prestige was the disbandment of the majority of the Redoran military after the Armistice.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DBArano-74)</sup> House Redoran suffered territorial and economic losses at the hands of House Hlaalu and Telvanni during the Third Empire's reign, particularly in Vvardenfell where--under pressure from the Temple--they initially resisted mirroring the territorial expansion of their rivals.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-ASHOM-75)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Bal Sur-52)</sup>

After losing Balmora to Hlaalu rule, House Redoran claimed the former ashlander gathering site of Ald'ruhn as the site of their new district seat, and built a grand city around the shell of Skar at the foot of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). At some point before [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), House Redoran erected the settlement of Maar Gan in the ashlands, and the villages of Khuul and Ald Velothi in the northwest coast of the West Gash.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWRed Towns-11)</sup> Following the erection of the Great [Ghostfence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostfence) after [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), the bones of many deceased Redoran were incorporated into the barrier to sustain it and hold back [the Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight). Such donors had dedicated their spirits to the Temple and Clan as their surrogate families.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Aat D-76)</sup>

At some point in the Third Era, a Redoran noble named Andas Sarethi was believed by many to be the "Hope of the Redoran" after his mother chanted an omen upon his birth. Andas was a prodigy and frequently put down his unambitious cousin, [Athyn Sarethi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Athyn_Sarethi). When he was set to take up his uncle's seat in the Redoran Council, he was challenged by a battle-ready and educated Athyn, who defeated Andas with ease. Afterwards, the omen was re-interpreted to refer to Athyn,<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-THOTR-77)</sup> and he became the true "Hope of the Redoran" instead.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-MWRumors-78)</sup>

As of the late Third Era, [Bolvyn Venim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bolvyn_Venim) ruled as Archmaster of the Great House. Bolvyn was somewhat unscrupulous for a Redoran. Few among the rest of the hierarchy actually liked him, but the Redoran respect for strong leadership (which few Redoran leaders had displayed in recent years at the time) and prowess in arms ensured that he nevertheless retained his position. He was influential in securing Redoran's share of the frontier lands when Vvardenfell was opened for settlement in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), and moved the council to Ald'ruhn as a result.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWSarethi-5)</sup>

In [3E 426](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_426), the Redoran council protested the continuing burdensome tariffs on the native beverages sujamma, greef, and shein. After a costly and bloody tax revolt in Balmora, the council warned that such disturbances might spread to Ald'ruhn if the heavy burden of Imperial taxes was not alleviated. Smuggling and organized crime also became increasingly aggressive and violent in the Redoran House Districts. The councilors blamed local corruption, weakened enforcement, and aggressive competition between the Thieves Guild and the Camonna Tong.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Red Book-40)</sup>

Conflicts often arose with the other Great Houses at this time as well. By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) House Redoran struggled to compete with House Hlaalu's dominance of the [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) trade in Vvardenfell,<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Hlaano-79)</sup> with the most obvious conflict centered on the town of [Caldera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caldera), which was founded by the Imperial Caldera Mining Company with the support of House Hlaalu. House Redoran actively worked to find evidence of corruption in Caldera in an effort to shut the mines down<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Caldera Mines-80)</sup> and to prevent the Hlaalu from building Rethan Manor on the Odai Plateau.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Rethan-81)</sup> There was also bickering with House Telvanni concerning the seizing of large tracts of land,<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Hlaano-79)</sup> the founding of certain new settlements in the Ashlands such as Tel Uvirith,<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Uvirith-82)</sup> and the occupation of old Velothi Towers, such as Shishi.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Shishi-83)</sup> Nartise Arobar, daughter of Redoran councilor Miner Arobar, was kidnapped and used as a political hostage by the Telvanni wizard, [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md).<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Redoran Damsel In Distress-84)</sup> Delyna Mandas, daughter of Redoran noble Arethan Mandas, was held captive in [Tel Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tel_Fyr). This caused Arethan Mandas to go mad, and begin demanding tribute from passing travelers much as any common bandit.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Mad Lordof Milk-85)</sup> Both Houses Hlaalu and Telvanni took issue with House Redoran's attempt to create the settlement of Indarys Manor upon the Bal Isra ridge.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Indarys-86)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg/200px-GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg) The Fall of Ald'ruhn during the Oblivion Crisis [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/MW-place-Redoran_Canton.jpg/200px-MW-place-Redoran_Canton.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Redoran_Canton.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Redoran_Canton.jpg) The Redoran canton of Vivec City House Redoran was further shamed by rumors that claimed that Archmaster Bolvyn Venim was having an extramarital affair with Fathasa Llethri, wife of fellow council member Garisa Llethri.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Hlaano-79)</sup> Infighting further weakened House Redoran, with Councilman Athyn Sarethi narrowly surviving an assassination attempt by the Morag Tong.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-Guard Sarethi-87)</sup> In a ploy by the Archmaster, his son Varvur Sarethi was framed for murder and arrested, to be used as a political hostage.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Sarethi Son-88)</sup>

During the fulfillment of the Nerevarine prophecies in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Archmaster Bolvyn Venim was killed in an honorable duel with the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) after the latter gained the support of the other five Redoran Councilors. It is unknown whether the duel occurred over the matter of the title of Hortator or Archmaster. Regardless of whether or not the Nerevarine succeeded Venim as Archmaster, or whether the vanquished Venim was instead succeeded by another of the Great House, the Redoran subsequently offered the Nerevarine the title of Redoran Hortator. As a whole, House Redoran suffered from attacks at the hands of blighted creatures and [Corprus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus) monsters, with the majority of its holdings closer to the Ghostfence and Red Mountain than that of the other Houses. Some Redoran settlements, such as [Maar Gan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maar_Gan), relied on volunteer warriors to drive back the Sixth House.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-89)</sup> The most notable casualty of these attacks was Councilman Remas Morvayn, who perished after his home was invaded by Corprus monsters. As a result, he left his position on the council to his wife, [Brara Morvayn](Lore_Brara_Morvayn.md).<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Morvayn Manor-90)</sup> The attacks ended along with the Blight when the Nerevarine slew [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) and destroyed the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) inside of Red Mountain.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-MWMQ-91)</sup>

By [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), House Redoran was said to be waning due to its inability to change with the times.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-PGE3-73)</sup> After the coronation of King [Hlaalu Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Drin-92)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Helseth-93)</sup> news spread throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) that the new King had abolished slavery. Houses Redoran and Indoril fought a brief civil war against Houses Hlaalu and Dres on the matter.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-PGE3-73)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-94)</sup> At the same time, it was said that Nords, aided by Orc mercenaries, had besieged the Redoran of Morrowind. It is unknown to what extent these rumors were true or accurate, or what the outcome of these skirmishes were.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-OBRumors-95)</sup>

According to the Redoran Second Councilor Adril Arano, the Empire pulled the legions out of Morrowind to deal with the gates opening in their homeland of Cyrodiil during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433),<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DBArano-74)</sup> with a similar such claim being made regarding the legion garrison on Skyrim's isle of Solstheim due to a lack of Oblivion Gates on the island.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Ho RRI-96)</sup> This is in contrast to the claims made by Imperial authorities during the crisis, stating Cyrodiil could not count on reinforcements due to the legions being fully committed to the provinces,<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Ocato Dialogue-97)</sup> supposedly due to the Elder Council focusing too much of their attention to the other provinces and leaving Cyrodiil to fend for itself.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-Maro Dialogue-98)</sup> The citizenry of Cyrodiil also noted the lack of Legion activity,<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-Gen Dialogue-99)</sup> and none of the counties of Cyrodiil got organized legion aid when Oblivion Gates opened outside their walls in the closing days of the crisis.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-Allies-100)</sup>

With Morrowind itself having no standing army at the time of the crisis, the province was left virtually defenseless save for a few pockets of resistance.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DBArano-74)</sup> During the course of the crisis, the armies of daedra destroyed the Redoran city of Ald'ruhn.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Ho RRI-96)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-OBRumors-95)</sup> The destruction of their ancestral city, combined with the absence of a standing army in Morrowind, prompted the Redoran to take up their historic status as "the hereditary defenders of the Morrowind",<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TNC-27)</sup> and take charge in the defense. The Redoran were slowly able to raise an army, and used it to spare Morrowind from the full wrath of Mehrunes Dagon's forces.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DBArano-74)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/LG-cardart-Redoran_Enforcer.png/200px-LG-cardart-Redoran_Enforcer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Redoran_Enforcer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Redoran_Enforcer.png) A Fourth Era Redoran [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg/200px-SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg) The Redoran town of Raven Rock on Solstheim, circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). *Duty, gravity, and piety: these are our watchwords. We are the wartime defenders of the House Dunmer of Morrowind.*
— *[Crafting Motif 52: Redoran Style](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crafting_Motif_52__Redoran_Style)* After the Oblivion Crisis, the House started to rebuild. A group of House Redoran settlers led by Councilor Brara Morvayn immediately struck out for the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). After successful negotiations with the [East Empire Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:East_Empire_Company), which was rumored to have included a financial settlement, Morvayn's group was allowed to settle in the town of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md). According to Lyrin Telleno, the new Dunmer inhabitants quickly formed bonds with the other Raven Rock residents thanks to their hard work and reliability.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Ho RRI-96)</sup>

Unfortunately, in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5) the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) brought further destruction to Morrowind. The eruption of Red Mountain wiped the settlements in Vvardenfell off the map. A Redoran councilor living in Mournhold coordinated the relief effort, issuing a directive to send soldiers, supplies and able-bodied mer to the settlements which were hit the hardest about a month after the initial disaster, such as Balmora.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-TRY-101)</sup> Shortly after the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), Morrowind came under siege from the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of Black Marsh. While the Argonians were able to quickly advance through the south and east of mainland Morrowind, the army of House Redoran prevented them from sweeping through the whole province.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DBArano-74)</sup>

The political affairs surrounding the Oblivion Crisis and the Red Year culminated in a power vacuum. Animosity between Hlaalu and Redoran increased, as House Redoran seized the opportunity and became the leading power in what remained of Morrowind,<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-102)</sup> taking the position that Hlaalu had held for centuries due to their close ties with the Empire, and House Indoril for millennia before them due to their ties to the Tribunal. House Hlaalu was even removed from the Grand Council altogether, replaced by [House Sadras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Sadras). The Hlaalu believed that the Redoran were directly involved in the reorganization of the council, and have held a grudge against them ever since.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-HORR3-103)</sup> Subsequently, Redoran relocated the capital of Morrowind to Blacklight, as Mournhold had been sacked by the Argonians.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DBArano-74)</sup> With Blacklight as the capital of Morrowind and their army the dominant force in the land, House Redoran's rise was complete.

In the aftermath of the Red Year, Solstheim saw the destruction of the only Imperial fort on the island. Councilor Brara Morvayn, with the permission of the East Empire trading company, brought in some of House Redoran's elite "Redoran Guard" to fill the void left behind by the deceased Imperial legionnaires. The Redoran Guard have maintained control of Raven Rock ever since.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Ho RRI-96)</sup> After a few years, the relentless ash storms from the ever-erupting Red Mountain transformed Solstheim's southern reaches into pure ash wastes reminiscent of those present on Vvardenfell itself. The storms would leave behind deep dunes of ash that made life exceedingly difficult in Raven Rock. In order to protect the town from these drifts, Brara Morvayn proposed that the East Empire Company construct a large wall of her own design to protect the east end of town. The company quickly agreed and provided the necessary funds. After almost a year, the construction was complete and the huge edifice was named "The Bulwark." The wall proved to be extremely effective.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-HORR2-104)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/SR-npc-Lleril_Morvayn.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Lleril_Morvayn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lleril_Morvayn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lleril_Morvayn.jpg) Councilor Lleril Morvayn of Raven Rock In [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16), the High King of Skyrim gave the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) to Morrowind as a refuge, and it passed into the control of House Redoran fully (though the Telvanni established their own settlement, [Tel Mithryn](Lore_Tel_Mithryn.md), on the southeast coast).<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-DBLoad-105)</sup> This changing of the guard prompted the majority of the remaining Imperial population to depart the settlement.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-HORR2-104)</sup> Though a House Sathil settled in Solstheim around or before [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), their relationship with the Redoran rulers of the island is unknown and no trace of the House remained by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-LOS-106)</sup> Raven Rock continued to prosper under the rule of Brara Morvayn until her passing in [4E 65](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_65). Her son [Lleril](Lore_Lleril_Morvayn.md) promptly took on his mother's mantle as Councilor and ruler of Solstheim.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-HORR2-104)</sup>

Lleril would find his rule challenged on two occasions. In [4E 95](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_95), an assassination attempt was carried out against the Councilor by agents of House Hlaalu led by Vilur Ulen, but was stopped by House Redoran guards. Decades later, a band of Argonian raiders would land on Solstheim in [4E 150](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_150) with the intention of wreaking havoc. Fortunately, the raiders were swiftly defeated by the Redoran guard stationed in Raven Rock under the command of Councilor Morvayn.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-HORR3-103)</sup>

An all-Dunmer gang of bandits from Morrowind would surface in a ruined fort outside of the Cyrodiilic settlement of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) in the year [4E 180.](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180.) The gang's leader had come into possession of a Redoran Watchman's helm. On behalf of ex-member Brals Nelvani, a [fugitive member](Lore_Hero.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) slew the bandits and recovered the helm.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Redoran Helm-107)</sup> That same year, a Dunmeri woman named Dalsa Veleth fought as an arena gladiator near Rivercrest, claiming to be of House Redoran.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-Dalsa Veleth-108)</sup>

House Redoran continued to benefit from the mining of [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) in Raven Rock until [4E 181](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_181), when the mine at Raven Rock was closed.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-HORR3-103)</sup> The House's neglect of Solstheim increased, as without the mine, the island not only held little economic or strategic importance compared with the other seventy-three outlying settlements of Morrowind, but was a significant source of casualties for the [Redoran Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Redoran_Guard) (they lost twenty-six guards in a two-year span).<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-HRR-109)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) traveled to Solstheim and assisted Councilor Morvayn in uncovering and foiling a second Hlaalu plot on his life, this time by the Morag Tong and Vilur Ulen's son Vendil.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-SC-110)</sup> The Last Dragonborn similarly saved the citizens of Raven Rock from attacks carried out by [Ash Spawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Spawn) under the control of the undead General [Falx Carius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Falx_Carius),<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Mot D-111)</sup> and lifted the nocturnal control of [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md) by cleansing the Earth Stone in town.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Ct S-112)</sup> That same year, new ebony deposits were discovered and the mine reopened.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-TFD-113)</sup> The resumed flow of ebony from Raven Rock prompted House Redoran to take a renewed interest in Raven Rock and Solstheim.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-102)</sup>

House Redoran remains the dominant power in modern Morrowind. The Redoran Guard continue to serve as Morrowind's protective and peacekeeping force, and are found assigned as far away as Mournhold, though by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) the local garrison was reassigned to other parts of Morrowind.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-TMFN-114)</sup> As "the mightiest" of the Great Houses, it sits at the head of the Grand Council "by virtue of our preeminence in battle, wisdom, and ancestral glory" and keeps the "rabble" of the other Great Houses "organized."<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-102)</sup> The Redoran rule from their capital of Blacklight, now the capital of all Morrowind. It is said that House Redoran has improved Blacklight substantially over the last thousand years, and it now rivals the splendor of Mournhold in its prime. In particular, the Rootspire where the modern Council of Great Houses meets is said to be "something to behold." <sup>[[110]](#cite_note-DBCindiri-115)</sup>

## Culture

### Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg/200px-MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg) The settlement of Maar Gan, an example of typical Redoran architecture Redoran settlements are designed in the Dunmer village style, built of local materials, with organic curves and undecorated exteriors inspired by the landscape and by the shells of giant native insects the Redoran claim to have killed in the distant past. Redoran villages are typically centered on Temple compounds and their courtyards, with huts and tradehouses gathered around a central plaza, as in the West Gash village of Gnisis, which was, for a time, rented to the Imperial Legions in the Third Era prior to being destroyed during the Red Year.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup>

### Equipment
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/ON-concept-Redoran.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Redoran.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Redoran.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Redoran.jpg) Examples of Redoran arms and armor during the Second Era In the Second Era, House Redoran warriors donned armor inspired by the claws and chitin of gigantic arthropods. It was crafted from [Kagouti](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagouti) (or premium [guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guar)) leather, and covered in layers of medium-sized overlapping chitin (or chitin-looking) plates. The armor of the time sported helmets ornamented with tall horns that resembled the mandibles of shalk beetles.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-CM52R-41)</sup> By the Third and [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), this armor had been replaced by bonemold. In Third Era Vvardenfell, the Gah-Julan style was the preferred of House Redoran.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-MWBonemold-116)</sup> The Redoran bonemold utilized by the Fourth Era Redoran Guard was considered impressive, and was crafted from actual bone that was reinforced with a resin-like material and then shaped to form the armored plating. Though it appeared brittle, the armor was said to be capable of stopping a blow from weapons superior to iron and steel.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-DBVeleth-117)</sup> Redoran boots were made of the toughest available leather, with thick soles for protection against the land's rugged terrain. Gloves were designed to facilitate grasping and grappling, even in the heaviest armor sets. Redoran gauntlets eschewed rigid components over the hands and fingers, with gauntlet-plates typically pointed as an enhanced danger to enemies who got too close.

Redoran weapons likewise emulated the organic chitin of Morrowind's native fauna. Swords and daggers were designed to be broad, straight, and double-edged, with a murderous flaring double prong at the tip. This double prong could inflict savage wounds on an unarmored opponent, and the Redoran blade-fighting style emphasized cutting over thrusting, ensuring the twin prongs were no liability against armor. The crossguards were narrow, given Redoran swordfighters were trained to parry with the blade's forte, a hand's-width above the grip. Blunt weapons such as axes and maces were forged from the most durable metal available, ideally carbon steel, but substitutes such as iron, steel, and even Dwarven metal were not unheard of. Both maces and axes were additionally accented with bronze. These weapons evoked the claws and mandibles of the mammoth Morrowind insects whose fierceness the Redoran strove to embody, axes featuring double-edged blades and maces featuring a heavy head that sprouted sharply edged flanges on all sides, curved like insect claws. The classic Redoran bow is a composite of native funguswood and springy leg-chitin, faced with chitin plates on the limbs as they radiate from the grip. The quiver is of light chitin plates accented with bronze; arrows have broad, barbed heads. [Staves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staves) wielded by Redoran mages echo the shalk-mandible horns of Second Era Redoran helmets. To keep these twin prongs from getting entangled with enemy armor in close combat, they were designed to easily break away without damage, fitting back onto the staff's shaft when the battle was over.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-CM52R-41)</sup> Spears are also counted among House Redoran's favored weapons.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-MWSkills-118)</sup>
