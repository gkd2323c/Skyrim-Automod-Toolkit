Andel Indarys was the Count of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTC-1)</sup> Indarys was originally a [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) trader in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), but at some point after [3E 380](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_380), he immigrated to Cheydinhal in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to search for greater opportunity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBMN-2)</sup> He eventually gained his footing in the city and became the Count of Cheydinhal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTC-1)</sup>

It was believed that Indarys' sudden rise from a virtually unknown Hlaalu trader to the Count of Cheydinhal was because [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) and his friend [Hlaalu Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) used their considerable influence to put him on the throne.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Obrs-3)</sup> His rise into the highest ranks of Cyrodilic nobility was not well received by most of the old families of Cyrodiil and other [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), who regarded him as a presumptuous upstart.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBMN-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBMA-4)</sup> As of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), he was the only non-Imperial count in Cyrodiil.

Around [3E 413](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_413), he married Llathasa, and they had one son, Farwil.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBCAI-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBBt O-6)</sup> Circa 3E 433, Llathasa's battered corpse was discovered at the foot of the stairs of Castle Cheydinhal's county hall, immediately attracting scandal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTC-1)</sup> The Count's rumored struggles with debauchery, intemperance, and infidelity led some to speculate about his involvement.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Obrs-3)</sup> After Llathasa's entombment in the undercroft of the Great Chapel of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), Andel commissioned a bust of the Countess, which he put near her tomb.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBBt O-6)</sup>

It was said that Indarys knew of the existence of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) sanctuary in Cheydinhal, but bribes and threats of death kept him quiet.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ob LS-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBT-8)</sup> He was seemingly unaware of (or unconcerned with) the heavy fines that the captain of the Cheydinhal city guard, Ulrich Leland, imposed on the citizens of the city.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SL-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBLN-10)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), he was a staunch supporter of High Chancellor [Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md), as he believed that no one was better suited to guide the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) through the difficult times.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBCAI-5)</sup> When an Oblivion Gate opened outside the western gates of Cheydinhal, Farwil and his chivalrous order, known as the [Knights of the Thorn](Lore_Knights_of_the_Thorn.md), entered and launched an assault.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBAG-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBBS-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CHS-13)</sup> However, after two days with no contact with Farwil and the knights, the Count feared the worst, so he posted guards outside of the gate and offered a reward for the recovery of his son or confirmed news of his demise.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBAG-11)</sup> The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md) eventually rescued Farwil from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and closed the gate, and Andel rewarded them with [one of](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Indarys) [the heirlooms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thornblade) of the [Indarys family](Lore_Indarys.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBCAI-5)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CHS-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TWK-14)</sup> Additionally, with the Oblivion Gate outside of Cheydinhal closed, he provided military aid for the defense of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Af B-15)</sup>

Circa [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), the Knights of the Thorn were led by Andel's descendant, Ilver Indarys.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lo S-16)</sup><sup>:205</sup>
