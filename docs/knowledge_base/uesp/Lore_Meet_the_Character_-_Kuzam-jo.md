*A teary-eyed recounting of a certain Priest of [Mara's](Lore_Mara.md) [wedding](Lore_Marriage.md) ceremony speech.*

Zamiri-dra,

Your granddaughter missed you terribly at the [wedding ceremony](Lore_Marriage.md), but we understand how difficult travel is on your tired claws. There is so much to tell you, and I will when we return, but more than anything I wanted to share the words our new priest friend, Kuzam-jo, spoke at the wedding. Forgive the tears that will no doubt spot this letter.

* * *

*Often I am asked, 'Kuzam-jo, how do I know if my love is real? Will I see Mara's light in their smile? Will I feel the Divine Mother's warmth in their embrace? Please, Kuzam-jo, tell me.'*

*These questions spill most readily from those on the precipice of marriage. Nervous smiles, fur on end, claws chewed blunt. I tell them all the same thing.*

*Love is not a gift that you are blessed with. It is not the result of a divine presence.*

*I say this because love is not something that can be taken from you. Nor can it be denied. Love is a garden. Yes, it may have been nourished by Mara's light, but no garden grows by warmth alone. It is only through a mutual tending that a garden may bloom.*

*When I look at our soon-to-be-married, I do not see Mara herself, no. I see the delicate petals of intimacy. The thorns of lessons learned. The fruit of unrelenting friendship.*

*Is Mara with us here today? I believe so. But we should not thank her for the beautiful love that stands before us. We should thank her for the patience and strength and yes, the bravery, these lovers have shown.*

*This union does not prove love. It merely says, bright as Mara's light, 'please, join us in our garden.'*

* * *

I hope your heart is warmed by these words. We make for [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md) when [Jone](Lore_Jone.md) is full, but be warned. I suspect the festivities will continue long after we arrive. Bright [moons](Lore_Moons.md) wash away your aches so that we may dance beneath their light.

See you soon, mother, Sarimazi
