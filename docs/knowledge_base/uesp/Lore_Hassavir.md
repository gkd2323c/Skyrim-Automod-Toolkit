*Knowledge is only lost to those unwilling to seek it.*
—Hassavir<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup> **Master** Hassavir was a renowned and powerful [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) Kogo-Hi, a masterful practitioner of [weather](Lore_Weather_Magic.md) [magic](Lore_Magic.md) and author whose works inspired many [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) mages throughout history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/ON-quest-The_Desert_and_the_Sea_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Desert_and_the_Sea_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Desert_and_the_Sea_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Desert_and_the_Sea_02.jpg) A modern Redguard mage performing Hel Anseliak for Hassavir *I held a deep fascination for reading as a boy. A blade stood no contest. It was then I discovered the works of countless mages. They guided me to this path. My only wish is to give back to them.*
—Hassavir<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup> Little is known about Hassavir's life. He originated from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup> and lived through its fall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup> From an early age, he was deeply fascinated by books, even as a boy finding little appeal in the blade. Through his reading, he discovered the works of countless mages, and it was their writings that ultimately guided him onto his journey.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup> He eventually became a formidable Kogo-Hi, mage of great renown.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup> He mourned the fall of Yokuda and was among the survivors who migrated to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He grieved the fall of many of his people's more advanced creations and ideals, including their respect for magic. Though he remained proud of how far the Redguards had come in Tamriel, he could not help but lament the fading respect for magic among them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup>

Though Yokudan by birth, he regarded the deserts of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) as a second home. Hassavir was said to command the sands and winds themselves, manipulating the Alik'r gales to his will and summoning storms even under the fiercest sun.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup>

Hassavir was also the author of numerous works on magic that continued to inspire and influence Redguard practitioners of magic.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup> His body was interred in the Motalion Necropolis near Satakalaam alongside many other venerated Yokudan ancestors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDATS-3)</sup>

## Legacy
*There is one that I've always come here to visit. A Yokudan mage said to have controlled the sands and winds: Hassavir. His works made me who I am.*
—Jahesi<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup> Long after his death, his magical works continued to inspire modern Redguard mages throughout the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup> The practices of air and sand magic endured within Redguard culture. Treatises and writings on sand and wind magical traditions were expanded upon by other Redguard authors and remained in circulation even as late as the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WAS-4)</sup>

Hassavir's spirit remained wandering through the sands of the Alik'r Desert long after his death. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), that spirit guided a pilgrim seeking to perform the Redguard ritual of [Hel Anseilak](Lore_Hel_Anseilak.md) in honor of the deceased ancestors of Yokuda. As mages gradually fell out of favor among the Redguards, the ancient mages were neglected and often omitted when Anseilak was observed, leading contemporary Redguard mages to undertake it themselves. One such mage, Jahesi, deeply inspired by Hassavir's scholarly works, ultimately performed Anseilak for him, granting his spirit the peace.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hassavir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jahesi-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDATS-3)</sup>
