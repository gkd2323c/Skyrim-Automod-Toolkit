# Satakals

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/LO-misc-Colovian_Prince.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Colovian_Prince.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Colovian_Prince.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Colovian_Prince.jpg) A naked bodyguard from Rihad (back) *Just trust me on this: nothing good ever comes from snake worship. People bound to this idea of death and rebirth always wind up as wild-eyed cultists. Just the way of the world.*
—Ugron gro-Thumog<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Satakal Coil-1)</sup> The Satakals or **Sataks**<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-2)</sup> are a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) cult of devotees to [Satakal the Serpent God](Lore_Satakal.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-3)</sup> While they have resided in the cities of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), most notably [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), many live among the largely traditionalist nomads of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> These Devotees of Satakal have been seen rolling in the desert sand sidewinder-fashion in continuous, hundred-mile stretches, from [Balhar](Lore_Balhar.md) all the way to the [Nohotogrha oasis](Lore_Nohotogrha.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) ruler King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) was a deathless wizard rumored to actually *be* the Serpent God of the Satakal.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1W-5)</sup> Traditional Redguards not of the cult view Orgnum's connection to Satakal as blasphemous, and maintain that Orgnum will not be spared at the coming of the true Satakal.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TUAAYQ-6)</sup>

Many of the Satakals, such as those that inhabit Rihad, like to go naked in the streets, slithering in the dirt, and nipping at passersbys like [snakes](Lore_Snake.md). They even perform exhibitions of "shedding their skin".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-2)</sup> As of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), modern devotees of Satakal were said to shed their skin in gruesome rituals with winding daggers, with a possibility of self-consumption (though this has never been confirmed). Flensing rituals of Satakal were suggested to be more restrained in earlier eras.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Satakal Skinrazer-7)</sup> It is said the Satakals had hidden and well-guarded skin-archives detailing various traditional legends and myths from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). One such legend tells the tale of [Tall Papa](Lore_Tall_Papa.md)'s War with the [Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and how the conflict was settled when [Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) defeated Boethiah's son in a game of limb-riddles.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Forum Madness-8)</sup>

According to a pamphlet published by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the Satakals have historically caused the A'likr border-states no end of strife. These "revered madmen" are said to have sometimes formed perilous bands, terrorizing the countryside in "old [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) fashion." Despite this they are said to depend entirely on the charity of the other Redguards. When the Third Empire first controlled [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the Satakals proved resistant to transitioning to worship of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md). The Satakals began biting Imperial civil servants, forcing the Provisional Governors to run them out of the cities, protecting both locals and the [Imperial garrisons](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-3)</sup>
