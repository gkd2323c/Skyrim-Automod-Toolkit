## Cultural Beliefs and Traditions
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-activity-Dunmer_Ash_Garden.jpg/200px-ON-activity-Dunmer_Ash_Garden.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-activity-Dunmer_Ash_Garden.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-activity-Dunmer_Ash_Garden.jpg) An ash garden in a Tribunal Temple Each House [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) family has a shrine. In poorer homes, it may be no more than a hearth or alcove where ancestors' relics are displayed and venerated. In wealthy homes, a room is set aside for the use of the ancestors. The most powerful families possessed ancestral tombs dedicated uniquely to the worship of their ancestors. These shrines are called the Waiting Door, and represents the door to Oblivion. There the family members pay their respects to their ancestors through sacrifice and prayer, through oaths sworn upon duties, and through reports on the affairs of the family. In return, the family may receive information, training, and blessings from the family's ancestors. The ancestors are thus the protectors of the home, and especially the precincts of the Waiting Door.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> The Shrine of the Dead in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) was once the source of great power. It allowed the living to speak to the ancestors and harness their power. As Temple tradition steered away from ancestor worship, it was abandoned and remained hidden in [Old Mournhold](Lore_Old_Mournhold.md). Over the years, the power it radiated attracted the undead until it was eventually cleansed in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRGavas-2)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/ON-place-Andrano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-ON-place-Andrano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Andrano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Andrano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) The entrance to an Ancestral Tomb During the decades between the construction of the Great [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md) around the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) and the rise of the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md) and the fall of the False [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), there were many changes in the practice of ancestor worship among Dunmer Houses. With the vast majority of ancestors' remains going to strengthen the Great Ghostfence, there remained very few clan ghost fences in Morrowind. The Temple discouraged such practices among the Houses as selfish. The upkeep of family tombs and private Waiting Doors also fell into disfavor. During those years most Dunmer venerated a small portion of their ancestor's remains kept at a local temple.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> The Great Ghostfence was deactivated in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWGen Ghostfence-3)</sup> and a short time after the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the traditions came back with the approbation of the New Temple, and new Ancestral Tombs were built.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DBOthreloth-4)</sup>

On the 3rd of [Hearthfire](Lore_Hearthfire.md), the Dunmer celebrate [Ancestor's Day](Lore_Ancestor%27s_Day.md). Family and friends reunite comfortably around the clan hearth to give each other food and small gifts, and to be quiet and respectful to the hearth spirits.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IWADE-5)</sup>

## Dunmer Afterlife
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg) A House Dunmer consulting an honored ancestor The departed spirits of the [House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) Dunmer, entombed in their ancestral tombs, persist after death. The knowledge and power of departed ancestors benefits the bloodlines of their descendants. The bond between the living family members and immortal ancestors is partly blood, partly ritual, partly volitional.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLlevule-6)</sup>

Both House Dunmer and [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) do not emphasize the distinction between Mundus, Aetherius and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). They regard all these planes as a whole with many paths from one end to the other rather than separate worlds of different natures with distinct borders. This philosophical viewpoint may account for the greater affinity of elves for magic and its practices.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> Dark Elves don't believe that death is the end, but the beginning.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DBOthreloth-4)</sup>

Ancestral tomb-bound spirits will always recognize their own kind, regardless of time passed since the last time they communed with the living. However, if they are angry with their descendants, they may attack them,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODrelyth-7)</sup> although even a stranger could gain their trust if first pay the proper respects in the family shrines within the tomb.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESODranoth-8)</sup> Spirits do not like to visit the mortal world, and they do so only out of duty and obligation. For them, the otherworld is more pleasant, or at least more comfortable for spirits than Mundus, which is cold, bitter, and full of pain and loss.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> The ectoplasmic remains of spirits are sometimes used in the creation of wraithshrouds, which may retain its ghostly appearance. Ashlanders consider this a sign of favor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOWraithshroud-9)</sup>

Certain Dunmer have their skin covered in [magic script](Lore_Magic_Script_Alphabet.md) seen as white tattoos, which are wards against the [undead](Lore_Undead.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-10)</sup> It's thought that, during times of great trouble, Dunmer interred in ancestral tombs are restless and aggressive.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOHIC-11)</sup>

Like House Dunmer, Ashlanders understand that their departed kin rest alongside their ancestors and guide their people from beyond;<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Tharas-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOHDW-13)</sup> a dreaming place they call the 'world unseen' that wise-women can see into and discern visions from.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWGen WW-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ZN-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-MM-16)</sup> Respect for the departed ones is a central part of Ashlander culture. If an outlander wants to be respected, they should honor them as well.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOAsani-17)</sup>

## Dunmer Funerary Customs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) A Dunmer ancestral tomb [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/ON-prerelease-Necrom_Zone_Preview_03.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Necrom_Zone_Preview_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Necrom_Zone_Preview_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Necrom_Zone_Preview_03.jpg) Necrom, the most famous of Morrowind's Cities of the Dead When a Dunmer passes, his body is given to fire, so *"he might return to the ash from whence * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * he came"*.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DBOthreloth-4)</sup> It is a House Dunmer family's most solemn duty to make sure their ancestor's remains after cremation are interred properly in necropolises called a City of the Dead.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-HONTCOTD-18)</sup> Some examples of this include [Othrenis](Lore_Othrenis.md) in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md)<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ONUnused-19)</sup> and most famously, "the" city of the Dead, [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md) in eastern Morrowind. There, the spirits draw comfort from one another against the chill of the mortal world. <sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-HONTCOTD-18)</sup>

However, as a sign of great honor and sacrifice, an ancestor may grant that part of his remains be retained to serve as part of a ghost fence protecting the clan's shrine and family precincts. Such an arrangement is often part of the family member's will, that a bone shall be saved out of his remains and incorporated with solemn magic and ceremony into a clan ghost fence. In more exceptional cases, an entire skeleton or even a preserved corpse may be bound into a ghost fence. These remains become a beacon and focus for ancestral spirits, and for the spirit of the remains in particular. The more remains used to make a ghost fence, the more powerful the fence is. And the most powerful mortals in life have the most powerful remains.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> A city of the dead can also contain the remains of a group of significance. This was the case with Othrenis, which holds the mages that sealed the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(creature).md) in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Some Dunmer may have beloved pets or animal companions receive funerary rites with their masters<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESORizaaddru-21)</sup> and have them interred with them in ancestral tombs.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TLS-22)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/MW-creature-Bonewalker.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Bonewalker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Bonewalker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Bonewalker.jpg) A Bonewalker To protect the burial grounds of their ancestors, the Dunmer create and bind guardians<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Bonewalker-23)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWGhost-24)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWBonelord-25)</sup> through ritual and magic understood as "sacred necromancy" by natives that defend their tombs and necropolises against intruders.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Sharn-27)</sup> Ths is often done against the will of the spirits in question, and is reserved for Dunmer who have not been faithful in their service to their family. Dutiful spirits may lend their aid in the binding of more rebellious spirits. These spirits often go mad in their defense of their tombs, and become fearsome opponents and terrifying guardians. They are ritually prevented from hurting living relatives, but that does not keep them from behaving mischievously or irritably. Though dangerous to intruders, if one can penetrate the spirit's madness and play upon the spirit's resentment of their own clan, the angry spirits may be manipulated.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> Dunmer consider it blasphemy to fight their ancestors within their tombs, but strangers have no such limitations.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOTanval-28)</sup> Nevertheless, the Dunmer take grave objection to strangers trespassing on tombs.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Sharn-27)</sup> Further defenses within ancestral tombs and crypts also include elaborate traps of magical<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MWATombs-29)</sup> and even mechanical design.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-NDAA-30)</sup> The remains of some ancestors, such as skulls, have runes engraved into the bones.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Legacy Quest Item-31)</sup>

Ashlanders simply bury their cremated bodies in the ground and mark the place with a cairn, identified by the possessions of the departed. Honored ancestors usually receive offerings, mostly based on the deeds of that particular Dunmer. The Four First Ashkhans (Nalor Ahemmusa, Shadar Erabenimsun, Lammak Urshilaku, and Akami Zainab) were buried in and around [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) and had to be ritually honored with specific presents (an ashen fern, an enemy's weapon, the bones of a fallen [guar](Lore_Guar.md), and a jeweled cuttle).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOKuamta-32)</sup> In contrast with House Dunmer, Ashlanders think ancestral tombs tend to go feral in time, because their spirit inhabitants used to be forgotten and suffer imprisoned without the proper communion, so they elude them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOAsani-17)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOIbaal-33)</sup> However, some tribes like the [Ulath](Lore_Ulath_Tribe.md) and the [Urshilaku](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md), use a series of [ruin-filled caves](Lore_Urshilaku_Burial_Caverns.md) as a burial ground, where their ancestors are mummified and interred with artifacts,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-MWBurial-34)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONMemories-35)</sup> or bound as guardian spirits,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-MWSul-36)</sup> as their Chimer ancestors before them had done.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-NDAA-30)</sup> Most Ashlanders revile guardian spirits as devils and daemons.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MWGen Ash Salts-37)</sup>

Dunmer regard Necromancy upon any of the accepted races as an abomination. However, before the Third Era, it was acceptable to practice it on other races, especially those of slaves. [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) mages were especially skilled necromancers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AATD-1)</sup> After the outlawing of Necromancy and before the abolition of [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md), Necromancy thrived by taking slaves at a modest rate. Most assumed the slaves escaped or died. However, by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), this situation changed dramatically.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CP-38)</sup>
