[![ON-render-Divayth Fyr.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/ON-render-Divayth_Fyr.jpg/300px-ON-render-Divayth_Fyr.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Divayth_Fyr.jpg)
- Esteemed Ritemaster [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md),

- I apologize for the lateness of this missive. My wanderings took me far beyond the boundaries of civilization, where the fabric of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) grows thin indeed. I only recently returned to the land of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).

- I encountered a fellow traveler on my journeys through the veil, Ritemaster—the [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) sorcerer, [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).

- I'm sorry to report that his temperament has not improved. When Divayth left our isle of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), he was a sharp-elbowed upstart. In the intervening millennia, his power and vanity have grown a thousand-fold. Thankfully, his ambitions remain very narrow. He does not desire wealth or influence in his [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) Telvanni. He has no interest in accumulating followers or expanding his holdings beyond [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md). He seeks forbidden knowledge, and little else. This of course poses its own dangers. Forbidden knowledge is forbidden for a reason, after all.

- We found each other on the Isle of [Dranil Kir](Lore_Dranil_Kir.md). I went to the island to investigate an ancient [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) scrying device. When I entered the ruins, I found Divayth Fyr engaging in some profane [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) ritual. I interrupted it, of course—not wanting to be devoured by whatever fellcreature he intended to pull from the [void](Lore_Void.md). He was far from pleased.

- After a bout of cursing, he explained his intentions (in the most patronizing terms possible). Apparently, he intended to open a rift to a pocket dimension using a bizarre [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) device. I asked him why. He made a sour face and sighed. "Why do we do anything, my dear [Lilatha](Lore_Lilatha.md)? To prove that we can, of course."

- He went on to ask me about a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) he's keen to acquire. Well, not "ask," exactly. Fyr would never ask for help outright. No, he twisted his words to make it clear he was doing me a favor by bringing me into his confidence. I told him I hadn't heard anything, and urged him to conduct his Daedric experiments elsewhere.

- In truth, I do know about this artifact, and Divayth is smart enough to know I was lying. I urge you to keep a close eye on him, Ritemaster. Divayth is not nefarious, but his motives remain too murky for my taste. Trifling with Daedric artifacts is a dangerous proposition, even for mages as powerful as Fyr. Perhaps one day he will temper that power with humility. I'm not optimistic.

- Yours in magic,

- Lilatha
