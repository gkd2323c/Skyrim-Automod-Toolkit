# Mantling

— [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) in *[Where Were You When The Dragon Broke](Lore_Where_Were_You_..._Dragon_Broke.md)* Mantling, also known as mantle-taking or [apotheosis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Apotheosis),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup> is the process of attaining godhood through the use of one or more of the esoteric methods known as the [Six Walking Ways](Lore_Walking_Ways.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LOV6-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOV32-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NHOTSIT-4)</sup> Summarized with the phrase "walk like them until they must walk like you",<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NHOTSIT-4)</sup> the concept of mantling entails the manipulation of [mythopoeia](Lore_Mythopoeia.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup> reenacting the Mythic patterns established by the [Et'Ada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Et%27Ada) until their power is surrendered to the mantler.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-7)</sup> In the process, the mortal and the [deity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) become metaphysically synonymous with one another, allowing the mortal to claim the office and sphere of the mantled deity for themselves, reshaping them in the process.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup> This is explicitly juxtaposed with [Incarnation](Lore_Reincarnation.md), another kind of Apotheosis where the mortal is born with an existing divine mantle (as opposed to lacking one)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MDC1-8)</sup> and knowingly or unknowingly acts in the manner of the god they embody.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NHOTSIT-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-FPgoodall-9)</sup>

As the [Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md) touches all Mantles of Heaven,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup> mantling falls under the concept of the [Psijic Endeavor](Lore_Psijic_Endeavor.md), which defines Divinity as attaining the Tower<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOFDE-10)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-VT-11)</sup> and suggests the end goal of mantling to be mantling the [Godhead](Lore_Godhead.md) itself.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BBWD-12)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## Known Examples
- [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) is a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) with many associations with the concept of mantling. He himself speaks to the concept of different individuals encompassing his godhood.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SISheogorath-14)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRSheogorath-15)</sup> The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) interpret these events as Sheogorath being a dead god who has been replaced by something Other.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAS-16)</sup> - His chamberlain [Haskill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haskill) claimed to be a [Vestige](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md) of a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) man who mantled Sheogorath in the past.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CHAYQ-17)</sup>
- [Arden-Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arden-Sul) was a Prophet who resided in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). He was considered the personification of Sheogorath's mortality by some denizens of the Isles (particularly [Zealots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zealots)), seemingly attesting to Arden-Sul as one of his past mantlers.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Z-18)</sup>
- The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) entered the Shivering Isles and became the champion of Sheogorath at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SIDoor-19)</sup> Sheogorath prepared them to succeed him as the new Prince of Madness and despite a setback that saw Sheogorath unable to give the Hero [his staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Sheogorath), the Hero was able to create a new staff with the assistance of [Dyus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dyus) and mantle the Madgod.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SISymbols-20)</sup> The Hero of Kvatch also achieved something that other participants in the prior [Greymarches](Lore_Greymarch.md) had not; they defeated [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag) in combat and ended the Greymarch cycle, fully separating Jyggalag and Sheogorath from one another.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SIEnd-21)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-22)</sup>
- The [Wilderking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wilderking) was a deity residing in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). There are said to have been many Wilderkings and Wilderqueens over the course of Valenwood's history,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONHen-23)</sup> though only two are known.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONMar-24)</sup> - [Ostion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wilderking) was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) who had the power to shape the land ever since he was a young boy. Powerful individuals in his homeland of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) learned of his unique abilities and sent him to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Ostion and Valenwood would become one and he was known henceforth as the Wilderking.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SB-25)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONSumiril-26)</sup>
- [Aranias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aranias) was an Altmer who, much like Ostion, displayed tremendous power over the land at a young age.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TWOS-27)</sup> Originally being part of a contingency that was to assassinate the Wilderking, Aranias eventually realized her true destiny and replaced the dying Ostion's station, becoming the Wilderqueen.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TOTWK-28)</sup>
- The Necromancer God [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) has attempted mantling twice: - Mannimarco made a failed attempt to use the Amulet of Kings to soul trap [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) so he could mantle his station and take his place among the gods.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Mann So ST-29)</sup> This plot proved successful in an [alternative fate](Lore_Many_Paths.md), where Mannimarco took the mantle God of Schemes, ruling Nirn from a throne of bone and souls.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
- Mannimarco would later on achieve apotheosis by finding his Mystery, "As Above, So Below,", attaining a Mantle from Above, bringing it Below. He claims this is how Apotheosis is done.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NM-31)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-WWYDB-32)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>

## Ambiguous Examples
- The Missing God [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) and his various aspects have a strong association with mantling, tying back to the narrative that the Mundus was created as a means of transcending the divine:<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOFDE-10)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-VT-11)</sup> - [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) was an [Ada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada) with heavy ties to Lorkhan and his various aspects. He was referred to as the [Shezarrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TSOP5-33)</sup> and likened to [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor)<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TSOP4-34)</sup> while also sharing physical characteristics with these figures such as having a gaping chest with [no heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TSOP6-35)</sup> It is thus possible Pelinal mantled this figure.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- Upon the mortal death of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), he was said to have ascended to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), transforming the face of the Divine, making the [Eight](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) become [Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-OBMD-36)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TTM-37)</sup> According to some sources, Tiber Septim achieved this apotheosis by mantling Lorkhan through the Fourth Walking Way.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NHOTSIT-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- The Dragon God [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) has numerous ties to mantling: - When [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) shattered the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) and rose up as the avatar of Akatosh to banish [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) he was said to have achieved apotheosis,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-OBLight-38)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-GIKurt Kuhlmann-39)</sup> namely by mantling the God of Time himself.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- The [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md) Emperor [Tosh Raka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tosh_Raka) managed to transform himself into a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), supposedly the largest one in the [world](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MA-40)</sup> Some sources suggest this process actually involved Tosh Raka mantling Akatosh and becoming his rival mirror-brother of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- The Dragon God-King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), is suggested to possibly be an attempted mantler of Akatosh, taking the role of his mirror-brother of the Nords.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup> [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md) claims Alduin tried to claim the Lordship that properly belongs to Akatosh.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-41)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TDW-42)</sup> Similarly Khajiit myth claims [Alkhan](Lore_Alduin.md) ever hungers for [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh)'s Crown.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TWa S-43)</sup>
- The Yokudan Make-Way God [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding), bears connections to both mantling and incarnation: - When [Cyrus the Restless](Lore_Cyrus.md) freed [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) from occupation by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md),<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Redguard-44)</sup> it was said he may have been a manifestation of the Hoon Ding, accidentally mantling the Spirit by fulfilling its' role.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Vo F-45)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- Within the same conflict as Cyrus,<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Redguard-44)</sup> it has been claimed [Prince A'tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A%27tor) also became a manifestation of the Hoonding, in the form of a [sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit_Sword).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Vo F-45)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), also known as ALMSIVI, all have individual ties to mantling: - Within the apocryphal events of his Trial, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) claims ALMSIVI to have taken on mantles upon Apotheosis, springing from the basis of their [Anticipations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra). However Vivec also clarified the Tribunal never fully claimed the Mantles of the [Good Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra), due to the latter's unwillingness to fully relinquish their realms.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-RPTrial Of Vehk-46)</sup>
- The God of Life and Death [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) appears to demonstrate mantling or incarnation: - According to High Rock myth, the god Arkay was originally a shopkeeper by the name of Ark'ay, who is said to have discovered a tome detailing the secrets of life, death, and the purpose of existence. After devoting his life to studying it, he ascended to godhood on his deathbed as Arkay, the God of Life and Death.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-47)</sup> This myth is corroborated by Runil, an Arkayn priest in the Fourth Era who teaches that Arkay was once mortal and gave up that existence that mortals might better understand the vagaries of life and death.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-48)</sup>

## Notes
- The phrase "As Above, So Below" is often used by various sources to describe mantling and the duality of mortals and gods as a whole.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-WWYDB-32)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-V6-49)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-CM-50)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup> This quote is a paraphrase of a [lengthier verse from the Emerald Tablet](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/As_above,_so_below), a real-world esoteric text which describes the principles of hermetic alchemy, with the second verse in particular explicating the connection between the microcosm and the macrocosm.
- According to developer Ted Peterson, the myth of Ark'ay describes the god Arkay intentionally incarnating as a mortal in order to experience his sphere of Life and Death firsthand, before reascending to godhood to take his rightful place.<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-MUWRU-51)</sup>
