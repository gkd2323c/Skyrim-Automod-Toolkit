[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/SKY10_Cause_in-body_2.png/200px-SKY10_Cause_in-body_2.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SKY10_Cause_in-body_2.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SKY10_Cause_in-body_2.png) Wights in Rielle (Skyrim) Wights are an emaciated form of [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BLWight-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cause-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Undead-3)</sup> The name is usually associated with a form of reanimated [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) undead, commonly found in their ruins in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BLWights-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cause-2)</sup> Wights can also be found in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Shadowkey-5)</sup> in the caverns under [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> and even in [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md)'s [Pits of Pestilence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Pits).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Blades Pits-7)</sup> Wights are typically seen equipped with [Elven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elf) equipment made of [quicksilver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quicksilver).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cause-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BLWight-1)</sup> Some wights have taken on unusually monstrous forms or strange colors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Shadowkey-5)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-8)</sup> there was a strain of [vampirism](Lore_Vampire.md) spread by wights that were present in Stormhold's caverns.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Stormhold Vampirism-9)</sup> An ancient Ayleid warlord from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) called [Haeliaran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haeliaran) became a wight in their undeath and remained a source of evil until being defeated by [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BLQuest-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BLTomb-12)</sup>

Some [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) are also referred to as wights,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> with wight occasionally being used as a term in [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) tales.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The [Ash Vampires](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Vampire) of the [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House) were often known as "Heartwights".<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Vivec-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
