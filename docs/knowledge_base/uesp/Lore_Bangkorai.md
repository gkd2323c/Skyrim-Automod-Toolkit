[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-place-Mournoth.jpg/200px-ON-place-Mournoth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mournoth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mournoth.jpg) Bangkorai circa 2E 582 Bangkorai is a region east of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) which straddles the border between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). It takes its name from the famous Bangkorai Pass. The [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Garrison.md) located there has served as High Rock's defense against the wild raiders of Hammerfell for countless generations. The thickly wooded uplands in the northern [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) region give away to the blistering heat of the southern [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md), which are part of the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md). In between them lies an area known as [Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus.md), where the Pass is located. The headwaters of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) are found in Bangkorai, as well as the legendary [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) crypt the [Hall of Heroes](Lore_Hall_of_Heroes.md). The two largest settlements in the region are [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) and [Hallin's Stand](Lore_Hallin%27s_Stand.md). Bangkorai is ruled from Evermore.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

Many different races have called Bangkorai home, including the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). In modern times, it is predominantly inhabited by the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), and the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) —the ancient pariah tribe of the Redguards—also maintain a presence in the southern wastes. Due to Bangkorai's strategic importance to the Breton homeland, it is sometimes called the "Shield of High Rock".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> A longstanding belief in northern Bangkorai is that their settlements are protected from the dangers of the wilderness by the [Viridian Sentinel](Lore_Viridian_Sentinel.md), a mystical office of guardianship which has been passed on to an anointed successor since the days of fall of the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TVS-2)</sup>

The Sunken Road is a subterranean pass linking Mournoth and the Fallen Wastes. It is far less famous than the Bangkorai Pass, known mainly by the peasants in the region, who sometimes use it to avoid paying the toll at the Pass.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

## History
The lands in northern Bangkorai, encompassing the place where the city of Evermore was built, and the areas extending northward, were once a part of the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [homeland](Lore_Western_Reach.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MZ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Brinarch-4)</sup> The region was also home to the [River Horse Bretons](Lore_River_Horse_Bretons.md), who later established Evermore, a prominent city in the region in [1E 983](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_983).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-5)</sup> According to *[The Mirror](Lore_The_Mirror.md)*, these cultures coexisted, at times as allies and at other times as enemies.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TM-6)</sup>

Many times throughout history, the soldiers of the Bangkorai Garrison have successfully stopped invaders from pouring through the Bangkorai Pass, protecting the lands beyond it from military conquest. In [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874), a horde of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) which had been displaced by the Redguards flowed west, but were denied passage at the Bangkorai Pass. They were forced to head northeast into the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) in order to reach [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md). Then, in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029), when the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) under Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) deposed King [Styriche](Lore_Styriche.md), the [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) of [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Hammerfell).md), he and his [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) fled west, burning and killing as they came, but were successfully stopped and routed at the Pass.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup> Their success is attributed to the sacrifice of [Saint Pelin](Lore_Pelin.md), who is still praised in the region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOSP-8)</sup>

Hestra's legions caught and killed the stragglers from the Gray Host, and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) was annexed into the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup> When High Rock decided to secede in [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305), the Legions of Piety and Grace under the command of Abbot-General [Priscus Mactator](Lore_Priscus_Mactator.md) were sent to quell them. Though the legions are said to have filled the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) from end to end, they found themselves unable to get past the Bangkorai Garrison,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup> which then under the rule of Baroness Falinne Guimard.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-5)</sup> After a five-month siege, they gave up and returned to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup> On Sovereignty Day, the people of the region traditionally celebrate this victory by going for a picnic.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PAPAHS-9)</sup>

Only once did the Garrison fail to protect High Rock: when [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md)'s horde of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) poured down the south shore of the Bjoulsae circa [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), they found a way to filter through the Northwest Spine of the Dragontail Mountains. The Garrison was left unprepared, as the mountains had always served as a barrier to invading armies before.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup> After a three-day siege,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOAM3-10)</sup> Evermore was sacked, and the Garrison was taken from behind.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BSOHR-7)</sup> Hallin's Stand held out longer, but nevertheless, Durcorach's horde sped through Bangkorai in a matter of days.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOAM3-10)</sup>

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), besides trouble from the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), there were fears of an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) invasion from the south. The populace looked to powerful rulers around the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) to help drive out the troublemakers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>
