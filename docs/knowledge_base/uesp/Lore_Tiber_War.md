# Tiber War

—One of the [loading screens](Skyrim_Loading_Screens.md) in *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)* The Tiber War<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARredguard-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-2)</sup> (sometimes **Talosian conquest**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> was a series of conflicts spanning through the second half of the last century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), started by the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) warlord [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) and continued by his general [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). The results of the wars were manifold, most important of them being the end of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) with the founding of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) with Septim as its first Emperor, the proclamation of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), and the unification of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for the first and only time in its history.

## Timeline

### Human Kingdoms
*The coastlands of Tamriel, to the northwest of our desert homeland. Mighty Tiber Septim bids fair to become Clan-Mother to them all.*
— J'ffer The Tiber War began when Cuhlecain, the King of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), supported by [Chevalier Renald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chevalier_Renald) —a member of a [knightly order](Lore_Blades.md) descended from [Reman's bodyguards](Lore_Dragonguard.md) —decided to wage war to unify the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Reman-4)</sup> To secure his northern borders in his future bid for conquest, Cuhlecain allied himself with [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) against the forces of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). Leading his forces was a young general called Hjalti Early-Beard, later on named [Talos](Lore_Talos.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>

After the [successful pacification](#Battle_of_Old_Hroldan) of [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md), Nord and [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobles, rightfully fearing the ambitious Colovian king and his undefeatable general, formed an alliance and invaded northern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The [resounding victory](#Sack_of_Sancre_Tor) at [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor) was the turning point of the conflict, as the Nordic generals, suspicious of their Breton allies, flocked to join Talos' armies.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bo ST-5)</sup>

Finally able to turn his attention to internal affairs, Cuhlecain unified the Colovian Estates in under a year. Not much more passed before the rest of Cyrodiil shared a similar fate, with the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) itself [being captured](#Taking_of_the_Imperial_City) in [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854). Just as he was preparing to crown himself Emperor of All Cyrodiil, Cuhlecain was assassinated, circumstances of which are subject to much controversy. In his stead, general Talos was crowned by the Imperial Battlemage [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus) as Tiber Septim, founding the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md). Over the next ten years he waged war with most of the neighboring nations, to claim them for his [new Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), but never again doing it personally, preferring to work on administration of the newly conquered territories.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> The jarls of Skyrim and the petty kings of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) were the first to fall. Following the occupation of Skyrim, many young men would leave their villages to join the Legions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-S-6)</sup>

### Hammerfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg) Tiber Septim and Amiel Richton receiving the Forebear delegation *As the Empire of Tiber Septim sweeps through the kingdoms of Tamriel in a glorious bid for conquest, Septim is opposed on all sides, but never more fiercely than by Hammerfell the ancestral home of the Redguards.*
—Intro of *[Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)* The best understood episode of the Tiber War was the conquest of the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) kingdom of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Despite observing the might of the quickly expanding Empire, the already aged and ill High King of Hammerfell [Thassad II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thassad_II) resisted all forms of Imperial invasion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> It was only after his death in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1-H-8)</sup> that Hammerfell began to weaken, but not from an external invasion, but rather from an internal revolution—a civil war between the two most prominent factions: the rebellious [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) and the loyalist [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) supporting Thassad's heir [A'Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A%27tor). After initial defeats, the Forebears signed a [pact](#Forebear_Truce) with the Emperor, granting him minor territory concessions in exchange for helping them in the civil war. The already weakened Crowns still provided heavy resistance to invading Imperial forces, until at last they were cornered at [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). The [Battle of Stros M'Kai](#Battle_of_Stros_M.27Kai) was won thanks to the shrewd tactics of Admiral Richton and the aid of Septim's vassal dragon Nafaalilargus. The Empire swiftly took control over all of Hammerfell, imposing several Provisional Governors to rule its parts. After three months of brutal occupation, both Redguard factions revolted one after another against their new Imperial masters, forcing Tiber Septim to sign a more favorable treaty with both of them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>

### Aldmeri Realms
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg/200px-MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) Designs of Numidium, weapon that ended the Wars *The Mantella was infused with the life force of Tiber Septim's Imperial Battlemage, and with it, Septim crushed all who stood in his way.*
—A [letter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:The_Mantella_Revealed#Letters_You_May_Receive) received by the [Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) After the pacification of Hammerfell following the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](#First_Treaty_of_Stros_M.27Kai), Tiber Septim found himself left with opponents that were far stronger and more unified than the petty human statelets. It was said that he considered whether further expansion was even an option, seeking peace with the royal court of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TEPIC-9)</sup> Nevertheless, military expeditions and skirmishes in both [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were recorded circa 2E 861<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup> and with the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) even earlier.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1-AD-11)</sup>

By the time a viable invasion of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) was put into motion (sometime after [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882)<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DUP-12)</sup>), the Betmeri territories were already conquered. Black Marsh, however, was never successfully invaded,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ASHo M-13)</sup> with its strategically unimportant inner swamps carefully avoided by Septim's troops.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-14)</sup> Even though his generals feared the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) as dreadful and fanatic foes, Septim realized Morrowind's weakness in the in-fighting of its most prominent factions—the [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses). Despite expecting a more prolonged conflict, Septim was offered peace by the Dunmer after only a few brief skirmishes.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bo QB-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OM-16)</sup> One of the terms was rumored to have been allowing Septim to use [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) to crush the forces of the Aldmeri Dominion,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OM-16)</sup> whose navy was regarded to be superior to the Imperial Navy.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3-S-17)</sup> Without the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), Tiber was forced to find another source for the Numidium, which he found in the [Mantella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantella),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> around which many historical controversies have arisen. After the brief conquest of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3-S-17)</sup> the official integration of Morrowind as an Imperial province<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ASHo M-13)</sup> and the destruction of Numidium at the hands of [a rotting undead wizard](Lore_Underking.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> the whole of Tamriel was finally united, putting an end to the Tiber War, which had raged unabated for 44 years.

## Major Events

### Major Battles
- **Battle of Old Hroldan**, [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852). [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md)'s general [Hjalti Early-Beard](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) successfully led his king's [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) troops and [their allies](Lore_Nord.md)' berserkers into breaking the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) lines, forcing them to fortify themselves at [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md). The next day Hjalti approached the gates of the besieged city, protected by the winds created by a storm following his trail. Reports say that the ancient Nord art of [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) was used to shout down the walls of Old Hroldan, after which Hjalti's army took the city with relative ease by force. As a result, he was nicknamed Talos, meaning Stormcrown, by his Nordic troops.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>
- **Sack of Sancre Tor**, 2E 852. While his army was distracting the majority of the defenders, Talos made his way inside the fortress of [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor), capturing the Nord and Breton leaders and making them order their troops to surrender. St. [Osla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Osla) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) has a statue dedicated to her for her actions in the battle, where she healed the warriors that had fallen in battle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bo ST-5)</sup> Sancre Tor was abandoned and Talos recovered the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TLST-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-OBPrima-19)</sup><sup>:361</sup>
- **Battle of Five Bridges**. After the initial success of his foes, Tiber Septim commanded his reserves to victory, attacking only after his foes were completely victorious, as advised by his Battlemage Zurin Arctus. Having been lulled into a false sense of security by their victory, Tiber took the enemy army by surprise, routing an army twice the size of his own.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TAo WM-20)</sup>
- **Taking of the Imperial City**, [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854). After a siege of [the capital](Lore_Imperial_City.md), the battlemages of the [Eastern Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) surrendered before Cuhlecain's armies. Before he could crown himself Emperor, he was assassinated, and Talos was crowned in his stead as Tiber Septim.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>
- **Moth Rebellion**, [2E 857](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_857).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Moth)</sup> An uprising in the Imperial City. The Third Legion, mostly made out of newly-recruited Colovian peasant boys, was sent from nearby [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) to quell it. There the Legion earned its sobriquet "The Faithful" while besieged in the Hesod Barracks by the [Cultists](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md). After the rebellion ended, Tiber Septim triumphantly reentered the capital.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup>
- **Battle of the Bjoulsae**. Under the command of [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton), an Imperial squadron crushed [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md)'s naval power in a surprise dawn attack.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Richton-21)</sup>
- **Sack of Senchal**. After the besieged [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) refused to surrender, General Pottreid ordered the Third Legion to attack the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), with free reins. Due to heavy civilian casualties, including children, the battle was omitted from officially-sanctioned Imperial history.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup>
- **Battle of Black Rocks**, [2E 863](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_863).<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Blackrocks)</sup> The forces of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) caught the Third Legion by surprise and almost completely destroyed it. The remnants of it made the core of the newly created Ruby Legion stationed in [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup>
- **Battle of Stros M'Kai**, [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Also known as [Battle of Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Hunding_Bay) by Imperials.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE1-Pro-22)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif/300px-LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif) Battle of Stros M'Kai, the penultimate battle of the Crowns Fleeing from the capital of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), Prince [A'tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A%27tor) rallied the remaining [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) for one final battle. The navies of both sides clashed near the isle of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in the [Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hunding_Bay). Leading the Imperial New West Navy was Lord Admiral Amiel Richton, who brought in a dragon called [Nafaalilargus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nafaalilargus). The turning point of the battle was Prince A'tor's death by a poisonous arrow from Dram, a Dunmer assassin. [Archmage Voa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voa) tried to save the prince, but due to the strength of the poison he was forced to place his soul in a soul gem and to put his body in a magically induced stasis before being burned by the dragon. After their defeat, the remaining Crowns fled to Stros M'Kai, most of them to the Old Quarter of the city, which was promptly burned to the ground by the dragon, killing them all as a result. Some of the survivors took refuge at the local temple of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay). The priests there gave them asylum, hiding the body of prince A'tor in the temple and healing the wounded, only to be forced to surrender the latter to the Imperials. The result of the battle was that the Redguard forces, aside from the Crown-allegiant [Restless League](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restless_League), were either completely annihilated or subdued.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> Following the crowning achievements of his career, Richton was made the Provisional Governor of Stros M'Kai, reigning over the city with terror for the next three months.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Richton-21)</sup>
- **Stros M'Kai Uprising**,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Wit W-23)</sup> 2E 864. Led by siblings [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), the Restless League came out of hiding and stormed the Palace, with Cyrus himself cornering the Provisional Governor Amiel Richton and the Imperial Emissary Dram aboard a [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) [dirigible](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Airships). Cyrus' sword, enchanted with the soul of Prince A'tor, then killed them both.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> This act was allegedly the third incarnation of [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-2)</sup>
- **Rebellion of Sentinel**, 2E 864. After hearing of the Crown rebellion in Stros M'Kai, Baron Volag, leader of the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), came out of hiding and retook Sentinel<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> from the Provisional Governor [Senecus Goddkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senecus_Goddkey).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1-H-8)</sup> As a result of these two rebellions, the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](#First_Treaty_of_Stros_M.27Kai) was signed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>
- **Sack of Mournhold**. One of the several battles that occurred after Septim's legions invaded [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and before the [Armistice](#Armistice) was signed. The city was razed and the royal family of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) was wiped out, except for the five-year-old heiress [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bo QB-15)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRB-24)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_Barenziah)</sup>
- **Surrender of Alinor**, [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BHot E1-25)</sup> Given to the Empire as a part of the terms of the Armistice by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), Numidium besieged the [capital](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) of [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), its fall happening within an hour.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKposts-26)</sup> Alinor's fall marked the end of the Tiber War, finally unifying [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), in turn leading to the proclamation of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Ft MHT-27)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BHot E1-25)</sup> The great Altmer battlemage [Areldur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Areldur) died during Tiber Septim's conquest of the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Areldur-28)</sup>

### Peace Treaties
- **Forebear Truce**. After the Crown forces regrouped at Stros M'Kai and even got close to retaking the capital of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), the Forebears, desperate at the prospect of losing the civil war, asked Tiber Septim for aid. The Empire was to lend its armies to the Forebear cause in return for a swath of holding titles along the southeast border of Hammerfell. After the Crowns were crushed and the Forebear leader [Baron Volag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volag) went into hiding not a day later, rumored to be dead, the treaty was revised, and Imperial garrisons were stationed across Hammerfell *"until such a time as the native rulership can see fit to reinstate itself safely on its rightful throne."*<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>
- **First Treaty of Stros M'Kai**, [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Following the [double](#Stros_M.27Kai_Uprising) [rebellion](#Rebellion_of_Sentinel) of both most prominent Redguard factions, the Empire was forced to draw a treaty with the rebels, finally legitimizing their rule. It was signed by Tiber Septim, [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), and Baron Volag, representing the Empire, the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), respectively.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> While Hammerfell's status as a province remained, a remark from almost two hundred years later stated that the Redguards felt that they *"are part of the Empire, but not a subject."*<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TWQ5-29)</sup>
- **Armistice**. After the Imperials amassed their forces at Morrowind's western borders and had several skirmishes with Tribunal-led Dunmeri forces, [Lord Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) himself proclaimed that he would initiate peace talks with Septim's Empire. The terms of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) were culturally very favorable to Morrowind, such as the freedom of [religion](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and perseverance of the ancient Dunmeri right to own and trade slaves. Strategically, the Armistice was very favorable to the Empire, most importantly the access to Morrowind's rich [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) mines and to the [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) Brass God [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bo QB-15)</sup>

## List of Septim's Subordinates
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg/200px-LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg) Titus Alorius, wearing the uniform of a high-ranking Imperial officer of the Tiber War period Septim's subordinates were sometimes called the **Dragon-Tribe of the Emperor**.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dreekius-30)</sup> This is a short list of the most notable officers and associates of Tiber Septim involved in the Tiber War:

- **Imperial Battlemage [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus)** — Septim's closest subordinate, responsible for the excavation of the Numidium and its empowerment via the Mantella. Died just prior to the invasion of Summerset Isles, either betrayed by Septim or assisting him in subduing the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>
- **Lord Admiral [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton)** — A naval officer who started his service as a captain under Cuhlecain. Rose in rank rather quickly during the Tiber War. He commanded the Imperial New West Navy against Hammerfell, dealing a crushing blow in the [Battle of the Hunding Bay](#Battle_of_Stros_M.27Kai). Appointed Provisional Governor of Stros M'Kai and killed three months later in [an insurrection](#Stros_M.27Kai_Uprising).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>
- **General [Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus)** — A high-ranking non-Imperial officer, involved in the invasion of Morrowind, from [where he hailed](Lore_Dunmer.md). Received the rank of a general after the [Sack of Mournhold](#Sack_of_Mournhold), having a personal grudge against the ruling family of [the city](Lore_Mournhold.md). Lived to see the end of the Tiber War, becoming the Prime Minister of Morrowind and later on king consort to Barenziah.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bo QB-15)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRB-24)</sup>
- **General Pottreid** — Commander of the Third Legion. According to an account of one of his legionnaires, he, along with his legion, was deployed all over Tamriel. It is uncertain if he shared the fate of the Third Legion at the [Battle of Black Rocks](#Battle_of_Black_Rocks).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup>
- **Admiral [Vasi Hadrach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vasi_Hadrach)** — Known only as Richton's predecessor. Murdered in a brothel in [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) by rebel Reachmen.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Richton-21)</sup>
- **Provisional Governor [Senecus Goddkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senecus_Goddkey)** — Appointed the Provisional Governor of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) after Baron Volag went into hiding, who cast him out of Sentinel a couple of months later in [a planned insurrection](#Rebellion_of_Sentinel).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1-H-8)</sup>
- **Provisional Governor [Titus Alorius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Titus_Alorius)** — Appointed the Provisional Governor of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), a region that had always had poor relations with the Empire.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PGE1-HR-31)</sup>
- **Commander Beatia of Ylliolos** — Officer in charge of a legion stationed in Porhnak. Known for her tactical brilliance with which she survived an ambush by the Nords in [2E 855](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_855).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Ia C-32)</sup>
- **[Nafaalilargus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nafaalilargus)** — Tiber Septim's "proud soldier" and "loyal vassal", actually a well-paid mercenary. Being a [red dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), he was a living symbol of the rapidly expanding Empire.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>
- **Count Sven Advensen** — Known only as a retired Nord officer. Lived to see the end of the Tiber War and was given the county of [Blackmoor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackmoor) to rule as a reward for his loyalty.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bo QB-15)</sup>
- **Imperial Emissary [Dram](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dram)** — A former assassin of the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong), served as Tiber Septim's personal emissary. Heavily involved in the campaigns in Hammerfell, where he was killed in [an insurrection](#Stros_M.27Kai_Uprising).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>
- **[Chevalier Renald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chevalier_Renald)** — A member of a knightly order that were predecessors of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md). Supported Cuhlecain to get to Septim, then Hjalti Early-Beard, believing him to be the foretold Dragonborn.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Reman-4)</sup>

## Provinces
- Cyrodiil — Now that Tiber Septim possessed the Amulet of Kings<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bo ST-5)</sup> and could don it as a true [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) ruler, he held undisputed claim over the Ruby Throne.

- Skyrim — During the Battle of Sancre Tor, an alliance of Bretons and Nords intended to stand against General Talos and his armies. The Nords, who were suspicious of High Rock's aristocracy, heard the Talos's Thu'um and recognized him as the Son of Skyrim and the Heir to the Empires of Men. Thus they joined his armies.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bo ST-5)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1C-33)</sup> Skyrim itself was invaded and occupied afterwards,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-AH-34)</sup> with its Jarls believing they would outlast Septim's Empire as they had endured previous ones.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Tobias-35)</sup>

- High Rock — Following the Battle of Sancre Tor the Empire rounded up the High Rock enemies. The battlemage command was summarily executed, and the Breton captives were imprisoned or sold into slavery.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bo ST-5)</sup> With the help of a self appointed Provisional Governor,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36)</sup> Tiber Septim subsequently solidified the witch-kings of High rock and consolidated their borders as much as possible, merchant lords and mercenaries took advantage of the waning border laws that would come to be replaced by Imperial law. Nevertheless, High Rock became yet another province to come into the Empire.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Tobias-35)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-S.27rathra-37)</sup>

- Hammerfell — The best-understood episode of the Tiber War was the conquest of the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) kingdom of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Despite observing the might of the quickly expanding Empire, the already aged and ill High King of Hammerfell [Thassad II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thassad_II) resisted all forms of Imperial invasion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> It was only after his death in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1-H-8)</sup> that Hammerfell began to weaken, but not from an external invasion, but rather from an internal revolution—a civil war between the two most prominent factions: the rebellious [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) and the loyalist [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) supporting Thassad's heir [A'Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A%27tor). After initial defeats, the Forebears signed a [pact](#Forebear_Truce) with the Emperor, granting him minor territory concessions in exchange for helping them in the civil war. The already weakened Crowns still provided heavy resistance to invading Imperial forces, until at last they were cornered at [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). The [Battle of Stros M'Kai](#Battle_of_Stros_M.27Kai) was won thanks to the shrewd tactics of Admiral Richton and the aid of Septim's vassal dragon [Nafaalilargus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahfahlaar). The Empire swiftly took control over all of Hammerfell, imposing several Provisional Governors to rule its parts. Later that same year, an uprising led by siblings Cyrus and Iszara saw the Restless League come out of hiding and storm the Palace, with Cyrus himself cornering the Provisional Governor Amiel Richton and the Imperial Emissary Dram aboard a [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) dirigible. Cyrus' sword, enchanted with the soul of Prince A'tor, then killed them both.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup> This act was allegedly the third incarnation of [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-2)</sup> Thereafter Tiber Septim came to Stros M'Kai personally to sign a treaty that held more favorable terms for the Redguards, representing Hammerfell was Iszara who was guided by the wisdom of Prince A'Tor via the Soul Sword.

- Elsweyr — Tiber Septim and his armies did a great number on the [cat folk](Lore_Khajiit.md) of Elsweyr, years before incorporating them into the Empire General Pottreid accompanied by the young war prodigy [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(soldier).md) led an attack on the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) that was so devastating to the Khajiit people it was stricken from official Imperial records.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup> Ultimately the Khajiit became yet another province to fall to the ever growing Empire, although much like under the banner of Reman, the Khajiit rebelled.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-PGE3E-38)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/GEN-misc-King_of_Atmora.jpg/200px-GEN-misc-King_of_Atmora.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-King_of_Atmora.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-King_of_Atmora.jpg) Talos and the King of Atmora - Black Marsh — Before it became a province of the Empire, a skirmish in Black Marsh over a captive named Reekee saw an important member of Tiber's legion nearly lose a leg to an Argonian feather-serpent.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup> Ultimately Black Marsh was never successfully invaded fully like the majority of the other provinces,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ASHo M-13)</sup> this was largely because the savage, dangerous inner swamps of the province dissuaded Tiber, as the strategically unimportant nature of it was deemed best to avoid. The outer borders however quickly fell to his armies.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-14)</sup>

- Morrowind — Morrowind was the only province to never be incorporated into the two prior Empires of Cyrodiil, therefore scarcely little was known of the province by official Imperial records.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE1-P-39)</sup> Tiber Septim possibly under the guidance of Ysmir Wulfharth was advised to incorporate the province of Morrowind into the Empire, Tiber was very hesitant as he was aware of the divine power of [the Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almsivi), the living trio of Dunmer gods, however he coveted the rich source of Ebony located in the province as he needed a source of capital to rebuild Cyrodiil which had been ravaged for 400 years of war during the Interregnum. Ultimately, the need for Ebony and the dream of a fully united Tamriel won out and Tiber set his sights on the land of the Dark Elves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> The rulers of the Dunmer and the Empire could not reach initial agreements and Tiber's armies laid waste to the capital of Morrowind, [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) in the wreckage of the great city, Tiber and his Dark Elf general [Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) came across the adolescent [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) who they agreed might prove valuable some day and took into Imperial care.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRB-24)</sup> Amidst all the skirmishes between the Empire and the Dunmer, mutual reservations were growing for both parties. Unbeknownst to the populace the Tribunal's power had been waning with the return of the sharmat [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) who had cut them off from their source of divine power, because of this Vivec saw fit to reach an agreement with Tiber.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OM-16)</sup> On the Empire's side, Tiber Septim felt similarly compelled to reach a truce instead of dealing with the might of three living gods and the lingering threat of the returning Dagoth Ur.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-PGE3M-40)</sup> Thus Vivec and Tiber Septim signed the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) a treaty that saw Morrowind maintaining near full autonomy, a right no other province possessed, in exchange for provincial status into the Empire and also the all powerful brass golem [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) which Vivec gifted to Tiber per terms of the treaty.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OM-16)</sup> Tiber also approached the leaders of the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) personally and reached an agreement that no non Dunmer citizen could use their services, or have a mark placed on them, likely so he did not have the worry of facing the same threat that eliminated the majority of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Po M-41)</sup>

- Valenwood — The elven provinces of Valenwood and Summerset Isle had formed an alliance, the second [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) the government of Valenwood under this alliance was known as the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) which consisted of Bosmeri chieftains and Altmeri diplomats. This governing body was annihilated by Tiber Septim and his armies, however Tiber allowed them to retain certain traditions such as a tribal council and a figurehead [Camoran](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) king.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-42)</sup>

- Summerset Isles — The Altmer of Summerset were incorporated into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) of Reman Cyrodiil under extremely loose circumstances. They were very resistant to the taint of humanity on their sacred island of [Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alinor) and per their own account merely accommodated Reman. They expressed that Tiber Septim would receive no such luxury.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1Pro-43)</sup> Tiber Septim knew that acquiring the province in totality would be next to impossible and early on in his conquest had plans to send Zurin Arctus to make peace with the King of Alinor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> Another telling reminder of their might came at Black Rocks where the Aldmeri Dominion nearly destroyed the entire Third Legion with a strategic trap.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Attrebus-10)</sup> This all changed after Tiber acquired the Numidium from Vivec per terms of the Armistice. As he had it pieced back together in either the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus)<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Po M-41)</sup> or in Cyrodiil at the headquarters of the Imperial Battlemages<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Po M-41)</sup> he had his battlemage Zurin Arctus look into the potential of the Numidium and what its purpose was, after learning what power it possessed Tiber realized it was the key to his ultimate solidification of Tamriel. The only issue he faced was the lack of a power source, as its original power supply, the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) was being carefully guarded by the mighty Dagoth Ur.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> What follows is a point of much dispute, some sources claim that from the heart of either Zurin Arctus, or Ysmir Wulfharth the Ash-King, sprouts an artifact known as the [Mantella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantella) which while not as adequate as the Heart of Lorkhan, is able to power the brass golem. Along with this another artifact known as the [Totem of Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Totem_of_Tiber_Septim) crafted by Zurin Arctus is used to allow one to control the Numidium. What is agreed upon is that both Ysmir and Zurin were vanquished by this point and Tiber endured out of the ashes to finally move onto the Summerset Isle.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-DFMantella-44)</sup> After activating the Numidium near [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) Tiber wrought trauma, possibly testing the abilities of it on the Khajiit populace before marching towards Alinor.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-WWYDB-45)</sup> Numidium besieged the [capital](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) of [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), its fall happening within an hour.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKposts-26)</sup> Alinor's fall marked the end of the Tiber War, finally unifying [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Ft MHT-27)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BHot E1-25)</sup>
