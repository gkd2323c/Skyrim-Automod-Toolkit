- *"A powerful warhammer imbued with magic that places warding runes on the ground to obliterate it's * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * wielder's foes."* — [Florentius Baenius](Skyrim_Florentius_Baenius.md)

The Dawnguard Rune Hammer is a powerful warhammer received as part of the [Lost Relic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lost_Relic) quest. Its enchantment gives you the unique defensive ability to create a [Fire Rune](Skyrim_Fire_Rune.md) by bashing with the hammer while aiming at the surface the rune is to be placed upon, however the range at which the rune can be placed is not particularly long unless you have the Destruction perk [Rune Master](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rune_Master).

## Notes
- Use of the fire rune ability will level your skill in [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) magic. However, it will not expend any magicka, but it does use stamina each time you bash with it which limits how many times in succession you can place runes with it before allowing your stamina to replenish itself. Since the fire rune spell is so powerful as to warrant having one of the highest magicka costs of any non-continuous spell, this makes the rune hammer a very effective means of grinding that skill. - This was "fixed" by the Unofficial Dawnguard Patch, version 1.2.3.
- The Dawnguard Rune Hammer is affected by the Destruction perk [Rune Master](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rune_Master).
- [Ahzidal's Helm of Vision](Skyrim_Ahzidal%27s_Helm_of_Vision.md) also increases the Dawnguard Rune Hammer's effective placement range, and does so without the helmet's 25% increased "casting" cost being applied. - The bashing rune placement consumes a total of 60 stamina each use, with the initial bash depleting 30 stamina (identical to standard bashing costs of other weapons and shields) and then an additional 25 stamina for the rune placement. Like standard bashes, the bashing rune placement can still be used as long as the character has at least 1 point of stamina remaining.
- For an efficient close quarters technique that takes advantage of the two step nature of the bashing rune placement: - Bash the target directly and then quickly look at the nearest surface to apply the rune placement portion of the bash. From an enemy-facing starting position, this can be done in one smooth motion of rotating the camera down or to the sides after the initial bash connects. The floor at the target's feet is usually a reliable option for this, but in tight corridors, one may wish to quickly angle the camera at the walls instead. Done correctly, this technique prevents the bash itself from being wasted while potentially interrupting the target's action and causing additional damage.
- [Myrwatch](Skyrim_Myrwatch_(place).md)'s gallery<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Myrwatch.md)</sup> contains a special holder for this item.
- The weapon glows a bright golden light around its edges when equipped and drawn.
- The hammer's bash is a weapon attack, which does not get silenced by the [muffle effect](Skyrim_Muffle_(effect).md) or by [quiet casting](Skyrim_Quiet_Casting.md). This makes the hammer slightly less useful for laying traps in which to lure nearby enemies.

## Bugs
- If you drop the hammer from your inventory, it will disappear completely. - Putting the weapon on any weapon display furniture will also cause it to disappear, however, this bug does not occur on PS consoles with the most recent update.
- The hammer does not gain [extra damage](Skyrim_Specialty_Gear.md#Dawnguard_Weapons) against vampires. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.
- The rune may not cast when bashing. - Bashing right on the enemy might not cast the rune, so try to aim at their feet or the ground before them for best results.
