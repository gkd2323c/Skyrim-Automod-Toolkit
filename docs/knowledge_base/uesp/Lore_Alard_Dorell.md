Alard Dorell was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the Baron of [House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Following [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), Dorell served alongside the leaders of [Houses Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) as a member of the triumvirate Council of the North, which was formed to govern [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) until the selection of a new King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EOd-3)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), House Montclair's representative on the Council of the North, initiated a civil war in Rivenspire so he could become King of Shornhelm.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HRP-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BOR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lf LL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup> During the civil war, Alard and House Dorell allied with Houses Tamrith and [Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) to prevent House Montclair from conquering Rivenspire. Ultimately, Baron Dorell played a crucial role in defeating House Montclair and killing Baron Montclair at the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Rivenspire-8)</sup>

## Biography
Alard Dorell was a member of the prominent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble family, [House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) of [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md), and eventually became the house's baron.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup> He had a son named Ellic.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LEDd-10)</sup>

Following King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md)'s death and the conclusion of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), Baron Dorell and the leaders of [Houses Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) pledged their loyalty to King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup> In the aftermath of Ranser's death, [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) fell into chaos.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-11)</sup> To restore order to Rivenspire, Baron [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md) proposed the formation of a ruling triumvirate composed of the leaders of Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith to govern the region in the name of Emeric.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-11)</sup> Because Emeric did not trust any of Rivenspire's nobles to be King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), he approved of the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North to govern the region, but he also promised to eventually select a new king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HKEd-12)</sup>

As the leader of House Dorell, Dorell served as the house's representative on the Council of the North.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup> While serving as a member of the triumvirate, Dorell maintained a mansion in Shornhelm to stay closely involved in the affairs of the city's court.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup> When he was in Shornhelm, he entrusted the management of House Dorell's holdings in and around Northpoint to his son, Lord Ellic.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup>

Although Emeric promised to select a new King of Shornhelm, the Council of the North continued to rule Rivenspire during the fifteen years following Ranser's War.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EOd-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-11)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Baron Esmark Tamrith died of natural causes, so his daughter [Eselde](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md) succeeded him as the house's leader and took her father's place as a member of the triumvirate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> Dorell and Countess Tamrith had constant disagreements as members of the Council of North.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> Dorell considered Tamrith too young, inexperienced, and hesitant, while she perceived him as rash, pompous, and overly militant.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CETd-13)</sup>

In 2E 582, Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), House Montclair's representative on the Council of the North, initiated a civil war to take over Rivenspire following the death of his wife and his transformation into a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) due to the Lightless Remnant of the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HRP-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BOR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lf LL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup> House Montclair also planned to assassinate Baron Dorell.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo T-14)</sup> Baron Montclair's court magician Reezal-Jul and the House Montclair forces under his command captured Shornhelm's upper city,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LTRJ-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Rf RJ-16)</sup> aided by treachery from two of the city's guard captains.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CETd-13)</sup> Bickering between Baron Dorell and Countess Tamrith hindered their efforts to retake the upper city from House Montclair's forces.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CETd-13)</sup> After capturing the upper city, Montclair demanded that Dorell and Tamrith surrender and crown him king, a demand that they rejected.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CETd-13)</sup> Around this time, Baron Montclair's daughter, Lady Lleraya Montclair, arrived in Northpoint and used illusion magic and her vampiric powers to place Ellic under her spell and allow her troops to enter the city.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LEDd-10)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BQd-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Gd-18)</sup> Lleraya then had Ellic call a gathering in the city's square, where she used magic from the Lightless Remnant to turn many of the people of Northpoint into [bloodfiends](Lore_Bloodfiend.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BQd-17)</sup> Afterward, Lleraya sealed the city gates and took control of Northpoint.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BQd-17)</sup>

Following House Montclair's capture of Shornhelm's upper city, Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), a vampire and former friend of Baron Montclair, attempted to assist Baron Dorell and Countess Tamrith, but they did not trust him.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CETd-13)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CVRd-19)</sup> An agent of High King Emeric eventually arrived in Shornhelm to assist Dorell and Tamrith in retaking the upper city. After the agent entered the upper city and learned that Reezal-Jul had captured three nobles and was holding them captive in the Fevered Mews, a cave system in the hillside behind Shornhelm, Baron Dorell ordered the agent to rescue them. While the agent was in the Fevered Mews, Emeric arrived, and the forces of House Dorell, House Tamrith, and the Shornhelm Guard retook the upper city from House Montclair, although Reezal-Jul escaped.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SD-20)</sup> The agent eventually tracked down and killed Reezal-Jul in Shadowfate Cavern.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TBSS-21)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg) Baron Dorell has Ellic arrested By the time the agent killed Reezal-Jul, Dorell had learned that Northpoint had fallen to House Montclair, so he and his allies began preparing their forces to retake the city.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CVRd-19)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ni P-22)</sup> The agent then helped Dorell and his allies infiltrate Northpoint, kill Lleraya, and liberate the city.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TLo N-23)</sup> Following Northpoint's liberation, Baron Dorell, having discovered Ellic's involvement in the city's fall, had him arrested and imprisoned for allowing House Montclair to take over.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TLo N-23)</sup> Although Ellic had been under Lleraya's spell, Baron Dorell justified arresting his son by saying that he willingly welcomed her into Northpoint, and someone had to be held accountable for the deaths caused by allowing her into the city.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BADd-9)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg) Dorell trapped in a blood ward After liberating Northpoint, the forces of Houses Dorell, Tamrith, and [Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) traveled to the Shrouded Pass to confront Baron Montclair at the Doomcrag.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Pot P-24)</sup> After battling through the Shrouded Pass and establishing a base near the top of the Doomcrag, Count Ravenwatch attempted to dissuade Baron Dorell and Countess Tamrith from joining him in the final assault, but they refused to listen to him.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Pot P-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TLR-25)</sup> Dorell, Tamrith, Emeric's agent, Count Ravenwatch, and the Count's protégé, Gwendis, then ascended the Doomcrag and confronted Montclair. However, Montclair captured Dorell, Tamrith, and Gwendis, and imprisoned them within a blood ward, but Verandis and the agent managed to release them.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TLR-25)</sup> While Dorell and Tamrith descended the Doomcrag, the agent fought and killed Montclair, and Verandis made a deal with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to transport himself and the Lightless Remnant to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) so it would not destroy Rivenspire.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CVRd-19)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TLR-25)</sup>

After the defeat of House Montclair, a gathering took place at Shornhelm Castle to celebrate and select Shornhelm's new monarch. However, it is unknown whether Emeric, who based his decision on the recommendation of the agent, selected Baron Dorell or Countess Tamrith as the new ruler of Shornhelm.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TCo S-26)</sup>
