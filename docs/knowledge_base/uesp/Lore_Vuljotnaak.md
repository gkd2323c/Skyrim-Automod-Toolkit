Vuljotnaak was a green [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Dark Maw Eat" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

The circumstances surrounding his death are contradictory and not well understood. The [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) dated his death to during or just after the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), according to ancient documents recovered from the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). However, engravings found on an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) burial mask depict Vuljotnaak being "devoured" by a hissing serpent (likely a [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md)),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOCADBM-1)</sup> which would place his death sometime after [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703). Despite what this depiction would imply, his soul was not devoured by a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md). Instead, like many dragons, his remains were retrieved by members of the Dragon Cult, and he was buried in a dragon mound near the settlement of [Granite Hill](Lore_Granite_Hill.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The location of his grave was later documented by the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) in [2E 373](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_373).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AOD-2)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Vuljotnaak was returned to life by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). He was later killed by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) and his soul was consumed, preventing him from ever again being resurrected.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup>
