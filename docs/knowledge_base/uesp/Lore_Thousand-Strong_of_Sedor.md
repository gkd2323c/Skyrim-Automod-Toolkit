# Thousand-Strong of Sedor

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-place-Sedor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Sedor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sedor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sedor.jpg) Sedor circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) *He entered the Gate at ... to win back the hands of the Thousand-Strong of Sedor (a tribe now unknown but famous in those days), which the Ayleids had stolen in the night, two thousand hands that he brought back in a wagon made of demon-bone, whose wheels trailed the sound of women when ill at heart...*
— *[The Song of Pelinal](Lore_The_Song_of_Pelinal.md)* The Thousand-Strong of Sedor were an ancient tribe of [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) people. They lived in [Cyrod](Lore_Cyrod.md) during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). According to *[The Song of Pelinal](Lore_The_Song_of_Pelinal.md)*, their name has been lost to history, but they were famous during the days of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). They were enslaved and taken captive by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) who inhabited [Sedor](Lore_Sedor.md). During the Slave Rebellion, they were liberated by [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) and were "brought back in a wagon made of demon-bone, whose wheels trailed the sound of women when ill at heart".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOP-1)</sup> A text dedicated to the role of Shezarr in the survival of the spirit of the Ayleids' Nedic slaves speaks of a stone tablet found in the ruins of Sedor. This tablet depicts a bearded figure referred to as "the Shezzarine, Shor-Who-Lives, Teacher of Men".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOS-2)</sup>
