# Prophecy of the New Moon


## History

### Late Merethic Era
In the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), when [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) still comprised of sixteen kingdoms, the mighty [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) and his rage of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) flew to that land, seeking to establish their own domain and absorb the [power of the moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Core), an act which Kaalgrontiid believed would allow him to achieve apotheosis as the equal of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and fulfill the Prophecy of the New Moon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOKaalgrontiid-2)</sup> Kaalgrontiid would pursue the realization of his grand ambition from a temple on the island of [Dragonhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonhold), his seat of power, where his Khajiit followers would raise a number of tapestries depicting him achieving his desired ascension.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NMRTharn-1)</sup>

The ambitions of the dragons would be thwarted when the Khajiit hero and [demigod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demigod), [Khunzar-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khunzar-ri), brought together four champions to challenge them: [Nurarion the Perfect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nurarion_the_Perfect), [Flinthild Demon-Hunter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flinthild_Demon-Hunter), [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anequina_Sharp-Tongue), and [Cadwell the Betrayer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cadwell).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Kat D-3)</sup>

After days of terrible fighting where neither side managed to achieve victory, Cadwell the Betrayer, finally came up with a plan to use the dragon's ambition against them. Khunzar-ri and his companions, his Kra'Jun, feigned surrender, and [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anequina_Sharp-Tongue) performed the [Shadow Dance](Lore_Shadow_Dance.md) to align the moons into an eclipse, opening the [Moon Gate of Anequina](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md), allowing the dragons to reach the Plane of [Jode](Lore_Masser.md) and the lunar power they'd been seeking.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Kat D-3)</sup> Khunzar-ri convinced the dragons to not simply absorb the lunar core, but rather combine their own life force with it, claiming that in doing so they'd combine the power of the moons with their own.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOCadwell-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-5)</sup> This resulted in the core imprisoning most of their life force instead and, greatly weakened, the dragons were deceived into entering the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus), where they'd remain for millennia as the "Demon Weapon" of legend.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Kat D-3)</sup>

### Second Era
Following the retrieval of the [Dragonstone](Lore_Dragonstone.md) and inadvertent release of the dragons from the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus) by [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Kaalgrontiid and his rage of dragons continued pursuing the fulfillment of the prophecy once more.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOTHOC-6)</sup> The dragons initially allied themselves with the [army](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxians) of [Euraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn), usurper queen of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), but soon swayed the Euraxian [necromancers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) to serve them alone, abandoning the queen to be overthrown by the Elsweyr Defense Force.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-QU-7)</sup>

Kaalgrontiid had Zumog Phoon, leader of the necromancers, restore the life of [Cadwell the Betrayer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cadwell) who, wishing to share in the [lunar power](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Core) and the dragon's rule, used the Shadow Dance to open the [Moon Gate of Anequina](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md), allowing [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) to attempt to devour the Core's energies.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Betrayer-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Core-9)</sup> The attempt was foiled by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) and [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira), who used the Core to banish Kaalgrontiid from the Plane of [Jode](Lore_Masser.md) and into the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Core-9)</sup> but the dragon amassed incredible power, consisting primarily of the lifeforce of his fellow dragons and a lesser amount of lunar energy,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Nahf-10)</sup> enough to make his way back to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Heir-11)</sup>

Kaalgrontiid eventually made his way to [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md), where he tasked his ally [Laatvulon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laatvulon) and Laatvulon's chosen [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), Ra'khajin, to reconstitute the [Order of the New Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_New_Moon), and set about gathering massive quantities of [Aeonstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeonstone).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOZayri-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cult-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Aeon-14)</sup>

After Laatvulon's defeat Kaalgrontiid, who'd increased his power further by devouring the sacrificed life force of the New Moon cultists amplified by the amassed Aeonstone, attempted one last plan to achieve apotheosis and fulfill the prophecy.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Aeon-14)</sup> Kaalgrontiid performed a great ritual to funnel his amassed energy into an immense quantity of Aeonstone gathered at Dragonhold island, amplifying the power continuously until he achieved apotheosis and ascension to the sky as the New Moon. Through the combined efforts of the dragon [Nahfahlaar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahfahlaar) and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) empowered by the [Mask of Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mask_of_Alkosh), [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn), [Sai Sahan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai_Sahan) and Queen [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira), Kaalgrontiid was ultimately slain and Dragonhold was destroyed in a massive explosion unleashed by the concentrated energy, contained to the island through the seeming sacrifice of Abnur Tharn, who stayed behind to prevent the blast from destroying [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) itself. <sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESONMR-15)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NMRTharn-1)</sup>

## Notes
- According to [Ja'darri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ja%27darri), Forgotten [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) and champion of the [Pride of Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pridehome), the successful apotheosis of the New Moon would lead to the tapestry of time itself unraveling, which would result in nothing being left, as all things exist within time's domain.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOJ-16)</sup>
- The dragon Nahfahlaar expressed some skepticism over whether Kaalgrontiid could have truly achieved apotheosis and fulfilled the New Moon prophecy through the means he used, but believed he was so dangerous he had to be stopped regardless.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-17)</sup>
- A text by a Khajiit Moon-Priest, transcribed by a scholar of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) suggests that dragons, Clan Mothers of the Pride of Alkosh, and [Pridehome](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pridehome) exist in some sense outside the tapestry of time and of linear progression, and so have always existed and will always exist. Consequently, the "Doom to Come" the time when dragons return to bring imbalance to the world, will also always exist. The text mentions this now also applies to Abnur Tharn.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Pridehome-18)</sup>
- A new moon and god would eventually rise to the sky in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), with the apotheosis of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) in the form of the [Necromancer's Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancer%27s_Moon) during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), in which time was indeed disrupted. This was followed some years later by the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) and the fall of the Third Empire. However the Necromancer's Moon exists in opposition to the laws of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) rather than [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and there is no known connection between it and the New Moon of the prophecy. <sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Lot E-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Moon-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DFJourney-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Enemy-22)</sup>
