The **Warrior**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> also known as the **champion of Rivercrest**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CT-2)</sup> and as the **fugitive** or **fugitive Blade** by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Aranande-3)</sup> was a [hero](Lore_Hero.md) born in the small town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CT-2)</sup> in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BLDestane-5)</sup> part of the [kingdom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodfall_Kingdom) ruled by the [Bloodfall Queen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodfall_Queen).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Trained in combat by Henrik Seven-Swords since a young age,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> the Warrior left their hometown, either seeking adventure or to join the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Meet Elder Quest-8)</sup> and later became a member of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), but was forced to go into hiding after the end of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175) when the organization was formally disbanded and its members systematically hunted down by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The Warrior returned to Rivercrest circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180)<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> only to find it ablaze and reduced to rubble. The destruction of the town's Elder Statue caused an undead [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) Sorcerer-King named [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril_Light-Bringer.md) to be released from his ancient bindings in [the Abyss](Lore_The_Abyss.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> and the Warrior undertook to rebuild the town and save all of Tamriel from the reawakened Ayleid [Lich](Lore_Lich.md).
