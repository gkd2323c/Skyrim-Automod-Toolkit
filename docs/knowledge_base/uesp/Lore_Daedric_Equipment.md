# Daedric Equipment

There are many kinds of **Daedric equipment** used by the forces of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Among the most notable is simply dubbed **Daedric**, which is one of the strongest and rarest types known to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Mortals have struggled to replicate Daedric equipment, giving rise to various accounts on how to achieve its creation; however, the Dremora [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) believes their efforts remain pale imitations of true Daedric craftsmanship, limited by the inferior materials found on Nirn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-1)</sup> Most mortals agree that forging such items involves binding [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) with [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) essence<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Earnaana-2)</sup> through a [Daedra heart](Lore_Daedra_Heart.md). Some insist the process must be done at night, under a new or full moon, but never during an eclipse. A red harvest moon is said to be ideal. This, however, is likely myth, as these sinister items have been crafted without those conditions being met.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HAF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRDS-4)</sup> In truth, their creation demands the skill of a master blacksmith and [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRDS-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Earnaana-2)</sup> Mortal eyes usually perceive Daedric equipment as black, with red inlays that glow, a characteristic attributed to the Daedra Heart used to create it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BLDWA-5)</sup>

Daedric armaments are describable as outlandish or even corrupted. The appearance is said to be because Daedra are only being able to imitate and are unable to create anything new and as a result we see Daedric come in familiar Tamrielic shapes for their armor and weapons that have been exaggerated. <sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CMDS-6)</sup> Another belief is that the appearance is reflective of the Daedra's "echoes of preternaturally prolonged suffering endured during manufactur[ing]"<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWGaro-7)</sup>

What determines the aesthetics of Daedric armaments is unknown, though the appearance of the ebony used in the creation will be perverted to an otherworldly shape. For example, rings of ebony metal will take the form of Daedric Mail armor when it interacts with a daedric essence.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BLDM-8)</sup> It is also unknown if any external forces can have any influence on Daedric equipment.

The Dunmer have [Daedric masks styled in tribute to various revered figures](Lore_Daedric_Faces.md), such as the Daedric Faces of Inspiration ([Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md)), Terror ([Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md)), and God ([Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-9)</sup> Due to their rarity, Daedric equipment is typically only seen in the hands of the upper class, where they are passed on through generations of kin. Their price tag makes them a considerable portion of a Great House noble's personal wealth.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWGaro-7)</sup> Advanced enchanters typically reserve the greatest of enchantments for equipment of the highest tier, such as Daedric.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mo Armor-10)</sup> The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) goddess [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md)'s [War Mask](Lore_Mask_of_Almalexia.md) is Daedric.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-11)</sup><sup>:322</sup>

The well-researched historical fiction *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)* claims that during the war between [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) used Daedric armor along with other high quality equipment.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-2920V6-12)</sup>

## Other Types of Daedric Equipment
The titular Daedric is typically seen used by [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md), though not every Kyn can afford to wear Daedric equipment of the variety created by the combination of ebony and Daedric essence. They may instead opt for armor and weapons created from [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) and the skin of mortals.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CM:63-13)</sup>

The arms and armaments of the [Aureal](Lore_Aureal.md) are avian-themed and golden, while those of the [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) are dark and ribbed. The mortal smith Joften managed to reproduce Aureal and Mazken equipment, but he learned that it was a dangerous endeavor, as one could lose their mind attempting it. Mortal-made Aureal equipment is an expensive craft, requiring the use of gold metal as a base. As gold is far too soft on its own, multiple steps are required to strengthen them to be more nimble and harder than steel. The structure of the equipment is reinforced with refined [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md), and then the gold is hammered into place while the gold is heated just below its melting point. Finally, a Daedra's Heart is placed in the forge when the equipment is being heated, allowing them to be hammered into shape, and transforming the attributes of the metal, giving it an uncanny hardness.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TJ-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRCC-15)</sup> Likewise, the mortal imitation of the Mazken's armaments was equally taxing to produce. Though their armor was revealing, it was deceptively strong and the process to create it required a balance between shaping ebony and malleable quicksilver. Heat must be applied precisely, or the shape and integrity of the equipment will be ruined. As with the Aureal, a Daedra’s Heart was added during heating, adding to its hardness.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRCC-15)</sup>
