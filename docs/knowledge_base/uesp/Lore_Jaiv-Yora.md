## History
*She who oversaw Alduin's Wall
That recorded the Dragons' rise and fall
We honor her, this third Grandmaster
Who led us through victory and disaster*
— *[Grandmaster Jaiv-Yora](Lore_Grandmaster_Jaiv-Yora_(1E_2808_-_2835).md)'s commemorative plaque*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GJY-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg) Wind Scour Temple Little is known about Jaiv-Yora prior to her ascension as a grandmaster of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). According to some sources she succeeded the second leader of the organization [Vashu-Pir](Lore_Vashu-Pir.md) and became the third leader of the order in history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GJY-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GVP-2)</sup> Little is known about the leadership of the organization in the years [1E 2758](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2758) - [1E 2808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2808), but as of ca. [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804) an unknown male Grandmaster of the Dragonguard was stated to be active.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOTD-3)</sup> During Jaiv-Yora's time as grandmaster between [1E 2808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2808) - [1E 2835](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2835),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GJY-1)</sup> the Dragonguard was active in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). In [2809](Lore_Second_Era.md#2809) a Dragon was active in the east of the region. Scouts were sent immediately, and signs of it were discovered, but it fled when approached.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOTD-3)</sup>

In [1E 2812](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2812) Emperor Reman II permitted the construction of Alduin's Wall within Sky Haven Temple. Considered to be a wonder of the ancient world, its purpose was to record the accumulated dragonlore and prophecy that the Dragonguard possessed at the time. Its main focus was a pictorial representation of the Prophecy of the Dragonborn. The prophecy foretold of five key historical events that would preface Alduin's return after his expulsion during the Dragon War. Given that Alduin's return was inevitable, the Dragonguard believed that the wall was their gift to the generations that were to follow. Craftsmen from temples across the Empire were called upon to work on the wall, while Grandmaster Jaiv-Yora of the Dragonguard oversaw the construction of the wall,<sup>[[nb 2]](#cite_note-MASTER-7)</sup> and after six years it was completed. Emperor Reman II returned to the temple in [1E 2818](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2818) to officially dedicate the wall. The Blood Seal outside the temple was consecrated in the presence of all Akaviri Dragonguard in Skyrim. This allowed the temple to be sealed, only ever to be opened by a Dragonborn to unlock its knowledge and power. The entrance into the temple, opened by the blood seal, was a giant stone effigy of Reman I.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOTD-3)</sup>

After her death she was buried in [Wind Scour Temple](Lore_Wind_Scour_Temple.md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and honored with a [statue](Lore_Statues.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WST-8)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GJY-1)</sup>
