# Stormcrown Interregnum


## History
Following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) was unable to find a suitable heir to the Dragon Throne, so High Chancellor Ocato reluctantly agreed to become potentate under the terms of the Elder Council Charter.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup> The Elder Council was not as strong in leadership as they would have liked, and made efforts to consolidate and conserve the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and its provinces, though with perhaps little effect. [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) appeared to be on the brink of secession, and the other provinces each had their own internal and external problems to deal with.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPFTA-3)</sup>

Ocato's assassination circa [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15) marked the beginning of the Stormcrown Interregnum. The Elder Council fractured, leading to years of ruthless in-fighting, plots and backstabbing. Many tried to claim the Ruby Throne. Most were pretenders to the crown, a few had legitimate claims, and others were strongmen and warlords. Violent, unnatural storms reportedly lashed the Imperial City several times during this anarchy, always with the eye of the storm looking directly down upon the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT4-1)</sup>

With the Empire engulfed in chaos, the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) overthrew the kings and queens of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), allowing them to take control of the province.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup> Counties [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) and [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) became independent and were often at odds during the interregnum. The remnants of the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) protected the settlement of [Water's Edge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Water%27s_Edge) during this time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIC-5)</sup><sup>:223</sup>

At some point during the interregnum, [Thules the Gibbering](Lore_Thules_the_Gibbering.md), a [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) witch-warrior, became the Empire's ruler.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOS-6)</sup><sup>:96</sup> Circa 4E 22, the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) warlord [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md) captured the Imperial City with a force of less than a thousand men and deposed Thules.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT4-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIC-5)</sup><sup>:109</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOS-6)</sup><sup>:96</sup> Following the capture of the Imperial City, Titus routed the army of [Eddar Olin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eddar_Olin) with a force of around two thousand men.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIC-5)</sup><sup>:109</sup>

Thules the Gibbering was not a well-liked ruler, but since he was Nibenese, many on the Elder Council favored him over a Colovian usurper. [Hierem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hierem), who was from an old and well-connected Nibenese family and a minister to Thules the Gibbering, helped smooth over the conquest by convincing the Elder Council to accept Titus as a liberator rather than a conqueror.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOS-6)</sup><sup>:96</sup>

Titus I's accession marked the end of the Stormcrown Interregnum and the foundation of a new imperial dynasty, the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT4-1)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Stormcrown) | Ocato's death and the subsequent beginning of the Stormcrown Interregnum are never given precise dates. In *[Rising Threat](Lore_Rising_Threat,_Vol._IV.md)*, the accession of Titus Mede I and therefore the end of the Stormcrown Interregnum, which lasted for seven years, is placed around the same time as the Thalmor takeover of the Summerset Isles, which *[The Great War](Lore_The_Great_War_(book).md)* states occurred in 4E 22. Dating seven years back from this date yields the year 4E 15. |
