# Gargoyle

**Gargoyles** are creatures introduced by the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) official [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md).

They have the abilities to absorb health with their claws, and are immune to both [paralysis](Skyrim_Paralyze_(effect).md) and [poison](Skyrim_Poison.md). They have an additional ability: [Resist Damage](Skyrim_Resist_Damage.md), 250pts. They drop various [ores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ore) and [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md) when slain.

When stationary, gargoyles disguise themselves as [statues](Skyrim_Gargoyle_Statue.md). They can often be found beside regular statues, helping them blend in. While posing as a statue, they are immune to harm and cannot be killed. If they do not immediately burst from the rocks, magic (such as [Soul Trap](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md)) can be used to distinguish a stationary gargoyle from a regular statue. Occasionally you may encounter gargoyles in the wild or attacking cities, accompanied by [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md) and [death hounds](Skyrim_Death_Hound.md).

As a [Vampire Lord](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md), you may summon gargoyles to aid you in battle, much like a [Conjuration](Skyrim_Conjuration.md) spell. The power is only available through the Vampire Lord perk tree, by unlocking the [Summon Gargoyle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summon_Gargoyle) perk.

## Bugs
- Exploding gargoyle statues may be set to respawn when their associated actors aren't. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 2.1.1, fixes this bug.
- Some enemy gargoyles may follow you around forever due to improperly assigned AI packages. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.6, fixes this bug.
