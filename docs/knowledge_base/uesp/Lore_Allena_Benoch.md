*You always remember the mistakes of your first victim.*
—Allena Benoch<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AB-2)</sup> **Lady** Allena Benoch was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) [Jaqspur](Lore_Jaqspurs.md) who was a skilled swordmaster and archer. She was the former master of the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and the head of the [Emperor's](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [personal guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). She was active in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AB-2)</sup>

## History
Allena Benoch hailed from [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). As a Jaqspur, she developed her archery skills and joined her tribe's hunting party, where she began to hunt and kill their enemies and prey at the age of sixteen. In [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396), Allena Benoch fought against the Parikh Tribe who were aided by powers from [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), and killed many people during the battle. However, it was only when she killed an enemy scout in close combat that she realized the horror of killing another person. She began teaching herself swordsmanship and studied under the tutelage of Warday A'kor, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) swordmaster. She became a freelance adventurer, protecting caravans and dignitaries from danger.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AB-2)</sup>

She was an owner of an [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) [daedric](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) daikatana. Althought it was not of [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) design it was a formidable weapon, which she used to deliver as much damage with as little effort as possible. She dispatched a band of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) who had stolen gold from a local chieftain in [Meditea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meditea) using her swordsmanship.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/b/be/DF-npc-Akorithi_%28face%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Akorithi_(face).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Akorithi_(face).png) Queen Akorithi of Sentinel At some point, she became the master of the Valenwood Fighters Guild after defeating the previous master in a duel. It was disbanded two years later, though she was not responsible for it. Later, she gained the attention of the Emperor himself after defending Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) from a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) assassin, which led to her joining the Imperial Guard, and later becoming its head. Lady Benoch married her former servant Urken after he had been in her service for eleven years.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup>

A rumor circulated that Lady Allena Benoch, acting on the orders of [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md), carried out the assassination of the Archbishop of the One, [Calaxes Septim](Lore_Calaxes_Septim.md) in [his temple](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md). However, Lady Benoch denied knowledge of the affair, saying that the Imperial Guard's role is to protect the throne and not to carry out assassinations.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

At a later time, Lady Benoch arranged several interviews with an author. However, due to her responsibilities as the leader of the Imperial Guard and urgent summons from the Emperor, she had limited time and had to organize multiple meetings. She met the author on three occasions. It is unclear where the initial meeting between Allena Benoch and the author took place. However, after the first meeting, Lady Benoch's duties as the leader of the Imperial Guard called her away to important state affairs in the province of Skyrim and later in High Rock. The second meeting was held at the Blood and Rooster Inn, and the third, and final interview was held three months later in [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), a city in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). During the final meeting, Lady Benoch provided advice to young swordsmen who were just starting their careers, and quoted famed swordsman [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOP-1)</sup>
