# Bristleback

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/SR-creature-Bristleback.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Bristleback.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Bristleback.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Bristleback.jpg) A Bristleback Bristlebacks or **Tusked Bristlebacks** (also sometimes called **Razorbacks**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> are large tusked [boars](Lore_Boar.md) that can be found on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTG-3)</sup> and are known for their amazing ability to reflect magical effects used against them. The fur along their spines is barbed and stiff, lending to their name.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTG-3)</sup> They are often used by [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md), who have bred the creatures to be even more warlike and dangerous, as mounts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Their preferred climate is snowy mountaintops and temperatures well below freezing.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTG-3)</sup>

## Variants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/BT-concept-Boar.webp/200px-BT-concept-Boar.webp)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Boar.webp) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Boar.webp) Bristleback (Betrayal of the Second Era) Bristlebacks are known to come in varying sizes. From those expected of typical boars, to huge beasts bigger than a man. During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) huge bristlebacks with black and white fur were found on Solstheim and mainland [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTG-3)</sup> A smaller variant of these, found on [Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md), are known as Snowbarrow Boars.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

[Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) is home to bristlebacks with tan fur and of various sizes. Mountain Bristlebacks are the larger variety, found in [Southern Elsweyr](Lore_Southern_Elsweyr.md) and parts of the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). While the smaller variety are known as Lowland Bristlebacks.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

## Culture and Uses
The leather and tusks harvested from them are known to have alchemical properties, while their meat is used for food by both men and rieklings.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

Some Nords believe that slaying a bristleback boar when both moons are full leads to bad luck for the rest of the year.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> One of the many caves on Solstheim is named Bristleback Cave, after the beasts.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

The meat of bristlebacks is an ingredient of certain [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [dishes](Lore_Reachman_Cuisine.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOHroltar-12)</sup> Cured layers of bristleback hide are also sometimes used by Reachmen to create shields.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

In [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), the Bristleback hunters were famed for their prowess and distinguished for recounting lengthy tales in local inns during the night. Those stories were often filled with near-death experiences. Bringing down Bristlebacks was known to require both skill and a touch of luck.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BHS-14)</sup>
