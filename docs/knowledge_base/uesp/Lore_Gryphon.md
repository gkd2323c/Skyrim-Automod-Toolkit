*"Since the Altmer first looked up at the sky and saw the majestic gryphon soaring on the breeze, there have been dreams to tame the creatures, to ride upon them in times of battle or joy, and feel the freedom of seeing the world far below. But these were the dreams of children, who quickly learned that the gryphon is proud and fierce, and will not serve as mount to any lesser creature. Generations tried, but the gryphon would never succumb."*
— *[The Flight of Gryphons](Lore_The_Flight_of_Gryphons.md)* Gryphons (also spelled **Griffin**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> or **Griffon**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Tuminderion-2)</sup> are large winged creatures that live primarily on the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), within the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Sum Gryph-3)</sup> though they can also be found in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Els Gryph-4)</sup> and possibly the provinces of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), due to [Harpy](Lore_Harpy.md) clan leaders native to the provinces being known to wear dyed gryphon feathers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> They are generally found across the mountains of Summerset, primarily around [Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md), and specifically at the northeast point at Gryphon Aerie.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> They can be described as a combination of a [lion](Lore_Lion.md) and an [eagle](Lore_Eagle.md), though they bear more of a resemblance to an eagle on four legs. Some gryphons ressemble other bird species, such as the [lion ibis](Lore_Lion_Ibis.md). Gryphons are fiercely territorial.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Kinlady-8)</sup> They will attack anyone who intrudes upon their nests or hunting grounds.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JF-9)</sup> Gryphons are predators and eat live prey. Gryphons aren't picky eaters, and will capture anyone foolish enough to traverse their hunting grounds.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JF-9)</sup> Young gryphons are known as fledglings.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> [Salamanders](Lore_Salamander.md), [deer](Lore_Deer.md) and [cows](Lore_Cow.md) are among the animals that may find themselves hunted by a hungry gryphon.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Gryphons often make their nests on the sides of mountains or upon cliffs. They occasionally drag their prey back to their nests.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

Gryphons are revered by the [High Elves](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JF-9)</sup> The Altmer fear the gryphon's ferocity, but respect their strength and freedom.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JF-9)</sup> In some parts of Summerset, such as [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md) and [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md), the gryphon is sacred.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JF-9)</sup> In ancient times, many High Elves considered gryphons unsuitable to be mounted, but now the city-states of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) and [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md) are known for their tamed gryphons, which are a part of their iconography.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TWo EN-14)</sup>

[Gryphon's feathers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gryphon%27s_feather) are circulated in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region and have [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) properties.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The feathers can also be used to make tools such as brooms,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> and down feathers are used to make pillows.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Gryphon feathers have an iridescent vane, and are prized by the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Unfertilized yolks from gryphon eggs,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> as well as secretions including saliva, can be used to make skincare products.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Gryphon vellum is a popular material among Altmer monks for making illuminated texts, and it has a unique texture due to the feather-dimpling.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> In Summerset, it is illegal to collect gryphon eggs for the purpose of selling them somewhere off the island.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOMiranrel-22)</sup>

As many creatures are, the Gryphon is susceptible to a slew of diseases. One well-known example is a condition known as Wing Rot, which affects the wings of the afflicted.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ROWRCT-23)</sup> Wing Rot is known to be a chronic infection that likely affects a gryphons' ability to fly, and as such, young gryphons with the affliction are often kicked out of the nest and eaten by [welwas](Lore_Welwa.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ROWRCT-23)</sup> The conservatory at [Sil-Var-Woad](Lore_Sil-Var-Woad.md) began working on a cure for the disease during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), allowing young gryphons with the disease to live comfortable lives in captivity.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ROWRCT-23)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-creature-Darremalatu_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Darremalatu_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Darremalatu_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Darremalatu_02.jpg) One of the Welkynars next to her gryphon partner While not spoken of Altmeri records, the [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) tribes were the first on the Summerset Isles to tame gryphons. They were said to be quite adept at capturing and training the creatures.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

In early Altmeri history, the Altmer sought to tame gryphons but were unable to because of their ferocity. Early Altmer tribes lived in the mountains and crags of Summerset Isle where they would harvest gryphon eggs, hoping for them to hatch and thus later be tamed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup> However, these eggs would never hatch. All was not entirely lost, as these eggs would be used in large feasts, since they were considered delicacies for kings.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup> This lasted for an uncertain time, until one day when the Altmer tribesman Ulorome found an abandoned egg in the aftermath of a gryphon nest hunt.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup> He took the egg home, and knowing that it would most likely not hatch, prepared a feast for his people and placed the egg in a healthy fire. The tribe would sing and dance to bless their meal, but these factors would inadvertently lead to the hatching of the egg.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup> The light from the fire made the young fledgling look like a [phoenix](Lore_Phoenix.md) with red feathers. This gryphon would be named Cel-hinwe.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup>

This ancient tribe is the predecessor of [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md), and Ulorome's lineage would become the modern-day Kinlords of Sunhold. The gryphons descended from Cel-hinwe would become the city's protectors.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup> In [Maormeri](Lore_Maormer.md) legend, Cel-hinwe and his lineage are known as "the Red Wind." Many believe that the Kinlord and Gryphon cannot live without the other.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFo G-13)</sup> Since that time, taming gryphons is known as a possible feat.

The [Welkynars](Lore_Welkynars.md) of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) are known for riding their gryphons as part of their duties of protecting the Eternal Isle.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TWo EN-14)</sup> Because they respond to urgent calls across the sub-continent, they utilize gryphons for fast transport from their aerie in Eton Nir to as far as the southern coast. The Welkynar Gryphon Riders have long been a part of Summerset's history.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TWo EN-14)</sup> Gryphon plumage is used in crafting the Welkynars' weapons and armor.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CM67-26)</sup>

[Quasigriffs](Lore_Quasigriff.md) are large, feathered, wingless quadrupeds that are descended from Summerset gryphons. Created by [Irastimil Direnni](Lore_Irastimil_Direnni.md), they are the product of wizardry and selective breeding.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

## Training
The Welkynars are the only organization known to regularly tame gryphons. Training a gryphon is considered a dangerous task, because of how violent and deadly they can be. A fledgling could nip off a couple of fingers if one is not careful.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TGof W-28)</sup> Due to their snapping beaks, gloves are an essential part of training a young gryphon.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CM67-26)</sup> Every Welkynar must undergo a bonding process with their gryphon. Eggs are collected from wild gryphon nests, which itself is a dangerous task.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TGof W-28)</sup> An egg must be collected - an orphaned fledgling cannot be trained. Once the egg is hatched, the bonding process begins, which initially starts with a magical ritual to first create the bond.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TGof W-28)</sup> From then on, the process continues through familiarity, where the aspiring Welkynar must care for the fledgling, and expect many tiring days and nights. Once the process is complete, the gryphon becomes a loyal soldier to the Welkynar, and the experience is considered unimaginable. It essentially becomes an extension of the rider. Once the process is successfully completed, the aspirant becomes a true Welkynar.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TGof W-28)</sup>

A select group of members of [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md) active in the [Coral Aerie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Coral_Aerie), an islet off the northern shore of Summerset Isle <sup>[[29]](#cite_note-GWL-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Coral_Aerie-30)</sup> and [Sea Giant](Lore_Sea_Giant.md) Yandir who participated in raid of [Kyne's Aegis](Lore_Kyne%27s_Aegis.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) were also able to tame gryphons.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOYandir Skills-31)</sup>

## Variants

### Azure Gryphon
Azure Gryphons are found in the highest peaks of [Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md), and are distinguishable by the bluish tint on their plumage. Some of the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) believe that this is sky-blue camouflage, but priests of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) believe this is a touch from the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

## Notable Gryphons
- **Cel-hinwe** — The first tamed Gryphon and the progenitor of Sunhold's famed guardians
- **Sunnawel** — The guardian of Sunhold in the mid-Second Era
- **Belanaril** — The mount of Welkynar [Relequen](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Relequen)
- **Darremalatu** — The mount of Welkynar Olorime
- **Falarielle** — The mount of Welkynar Galenwe
- **Silaeda** — The mount of Welkynar Siroria
- **Haeliata and Nagravia** — A notoriously dangerous pair of Gryphons found roosting in northern Summerset Isle during the mid-Second Era
- **Avuuma the Whirlwind** — A powerful Gryphon found roosting in King's Haven Pass during the mid-Second Era
- **Quillmane** — A powerful Gryphon found roosting in northern Elsweyr during the mid-Second Era
