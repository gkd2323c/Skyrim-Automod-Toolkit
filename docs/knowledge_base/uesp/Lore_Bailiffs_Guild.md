# Bailiffs Guild

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/ON-npc-Chief_Inspector_Rhanbiq.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chief_Inspector_Rhanbiq.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief_Inspector_Rhanbiq.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief_Inspector_Rhanbiq.jpg) Chief Inspector Rhanbiq, a former member of the Bailiffs Guild The Bailiffs Guild was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) organization that existed during the reign of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). It served as a [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) -wide guild for bailiffs, sworn officers of the law.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCCIR-1)</sup> In its time, the guild was feared by professional thieves.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

## History
In [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321), the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) gave his approval for the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md), which sanctioned businesses such as the Bailiffs Guild to operate with Imperial authority.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HOTFG-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AP-4)</sup>

Bailiffs and other members of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s legal system follow the same universal laws, but each province may have its own set of rules and decrees. A book known as *[Legal Basics](Lore_Legal_Basics.md)*, penned by [Anchivius, M.Z.F.](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anchivius,_M.Z.F.), lays down the same laws and regulations observed all over Tamriel. The Tamrielic legal system also operates under the Prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md)'s famous credo, *"All are guilty until they have proven themselves innocent."*<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LB-5)</sup>

When the Second Empire's authority collapsed during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the Bailiffs Guild effectively collapsed. One member, Chief Inspector Rhanbiq, argued passionately against the disbandment of the guild's [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) branch. Having previously been part of the guild's much weaker [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) branch, Rhanbiq possessed an idealistic view of the Taneth branch's capability and expertise in comparison. He maintained the guild's former structure by forming the [Iron Wheel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Wheel), a for-hire organization of bailiffs headquartered in Taneth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCCIR-1)</sup> The Iron Wheel was still in existence circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when it became embroiled in a conflict with the burgeoning [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in the city of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) to the south of Taneth.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

## Notes
- Another guild associated with the law and legal system is the [Barristers Guild](Lore_Barristers_Guild.md).
- In an early version of [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)'s justice system, the player could join a successor to the guild called the Enforcers, who assisted local law enforcement by catching criminals.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONJustice-7)</sup>
