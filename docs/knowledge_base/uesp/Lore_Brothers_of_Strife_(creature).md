# Brothers of Strife (creature)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg) The Brothers of Strife at the moment of their creation The Brothers of Strife are two beings of [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) destruction that act as guardians of the [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They both have the appearance of flaming [bone colossi](Lore_Bone_Colossus.md), towering [undead](Lore_Undead.md) [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) usually assembled from the bones of many corpses. Although the Brothers are a powerful [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md), they cannot be controlled and thus were eternally bound.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBo S-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTBWR-2)</sup>

The Brothers were created in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the Daedra-worshipping [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) clans of the region after [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) tribes (most prominently the [Rontha](Lore_Rontha.md))<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Nedic Wineskin-3)</sup> from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) had successfully invaded Stonefalls.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SV-4)</sup> Faced with imminent defeat, the Chimer army made a last stand at a Daedric shrine far south of modern [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md). Despite the dangers, the Chimer performed a dark transformation ritual to create the Brothers of Strife, using [flame atronach](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) essences and the hearts of their Nedic enemies, and offering two of their leaders as sacrifices. The monsters succeeded in crushing the Nedes and liberating Stonefalls, but continued to rampage.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTBWR-2)</sup> Using flame atronachs as guardians, the Chimer managed to bind the brothers atop [Ash Mountain](Lore_Ash_Mountain.md) and the [Tormented Spire](Lore_Tormented_Spire.md), two large volcanoes that dominate the region. The responsibility of keeping the Brothers bound eventually passed down to [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), two [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), the descendants of the Chimer. These bindings were briefly broken in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDo B-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SFD-6)</sup>

## Balreth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/ON-creature-Balreth.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Balreth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Balreth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Balreth.jpg) Balreth **Balreth** came from General Balreth, the Chimer who was sacrificed during his creation. He was bound within the caldera of Ash Mountain, and eventually became the responsibility of the Indoril of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md). In 2E 582, the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) put the city under siege as part of a wide-scale invasion of [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) territory. Despite the warnings given by his ancestors, [Tanval Indoril](Lore_Tanval_Indoril.md) summoned Balreth to defend the city, wiping out the Covenant invaders.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> No longer bound, Balreth retreated to his prison atop Ash Mountain, although a contingent of Pact soldiers managed to rebind him there before he could go on a rampage.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDo B-5)</sup>

## Sadal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-creature-Sadal.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Sadal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Sadal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Sadal.jpg) Sadal's spirit **Sadal** came from Legate Sadal, the Chimer who was sacrificed during his creation.

During the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the [Coral Heart](Lore_Coral_Heart.md) was used to imprison him within [Sadal's Prison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sadal%27s_Prison), a small pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that could only be accessed from the caldera of the Tormented Spire. His imprisonment was the responsibility of the Dres of [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md), who limited access to the top of the volcano.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Reeling from his son's death at the hands of the Covenant invaders at [Fort Virak](Lore_Fort_Virak.md), Tanval Indoril sought to exact revenge by unleashing Sadal in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Realizing his mistake, Tanval aided the Pact in rebinding him before he could even leave the pocket realm. However, Tanval perished in the process.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SFD-6)</sup>

Sedura Morag is an enchanted warhammer made from a great bonespur of Sadal. It is also known as the "Unleashed Exalted Hammer".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONSedura-10)</sup>

## Notes
- The Daedric ruins where the Brothers were created would later be named "the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(place).md)".
- Sadal was considered the greater Brother of Strife.
