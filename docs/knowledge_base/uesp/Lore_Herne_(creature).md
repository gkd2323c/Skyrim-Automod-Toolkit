*What have we here? A mortal? Abroad in the great wide world with no one to save him? Oh, how tired he must be of the long days and nights. Come, fall upon your weapon, and end the pain. Else I fear we must feed you your own dainty parts, and spoil that pretty skin with teeth and fire.*
—A Herne Homeguard in the [Havoc Wellhead](Lore_Havoc_Wellhead.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Herne Dialogue-1)</sup> (listen Link ⏵) Hernes (also called **Mastema**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Mastema1-2)</sup> are a race of bipedal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), described as aggressive, orderly, and deliberate warrior-sportsmen.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:63</sup> These horned Daedra somewhat ressemble [Skaafin](Lore_Skaafin.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ghorev-4)</sup> but differ in that they possess furred digitigrade legs, cloven hoofs, and a pointed tail.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BS-5)</sup> Fitting to their nature, Hernes establish hunting groups in which they refer to each other as "brothers", "playbrothers", and "lapmates".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Herne Dialogue-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:148</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Balaherne-6)</sup> While some are entrusted with spears,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Egahirn-7)</sup> many simply use bones as blunt weapons.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BS-5)</sup>

Though Hernes commonly have a collected disposition and enjoy tormenting their prey through parley, they're self-admitted "sensitive creatures",<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Herne Dialogue-1)</sup> that are quick to take offense to personal slights and whose bonds between themselves and their leaders are felt deeply. When a Herne's oathkin or master is slain, sending their [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) back to the [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md), the Herne describe a physical feeling of an emptiness, pain, and change within themselves from the loss.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Herne Dialogue-1)</sup>

Hernes have often loyally served [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) such as [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ghorev-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ez-8)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BS-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BPTMR-9)</sup> and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Noonan)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Stormhaven CC-10)</sup> Many Hernes were known to serve as retainers of [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md)'s [clan](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md), in service to [Clan Dagon](Lore_Clan_Dagon.md). These Hernes would participate happily in the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](Lore_Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry.md) following the invasion of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Egahirn-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BSLevel5-11)</sup>

## History

### Interregnum
The shape-changing Daedra Ezhkel claimed to have once been a Herne, collecting debts for Clavicus Vile.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ez-8)</sup>

Hernes such as Ghorev who served Clavicus Vile were known have assisted the [Shrike](Lore_Nocturnal.md) Dyzera, a servant of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). This was due to the machinations of the [Daedric Triad](Lore_Daedric_Triad.md), an alliance between Clavicus Vile, Nocturnal, and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Herne Ghorev specifically was known to have been defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) while he was stationed in Dyzera's [own realm](Lore_Dyzera%27s_Realm.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ghorev-4)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

### Invasion of the Battlespire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/BS-creature-Herne_Egahirn.png/200px-BS-creature-Herne_Egahirn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Herne_Egahirn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Herne_Egahirn.png) Herne Egahirn, taking command from the West Gate Barracks of [Granvellusa](Lore_Granvellusa.md) In [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398), after the invasion of the Battlespire was complete and the four occupying forces were left behind on cleanup duty, the armies of the clans led by Faydra and [Xivilai Moath](Lore_Xivilai_Moath.md) would retreat through the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) into [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), making their way to the [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md) where many Hernes would stay behind and become involved in the Ritual of the Innocent Quarry, a [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md), in which they took on the role of Huntsmen. During the ritual, the Hernes are granted immunity from all weapons and magics except from the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md), the hunting weapon of their leader.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TPH-13)</sup> They followed the command of Select Huntsman Egahirn, the youngest Herne brother,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:143</sup><sup>:150</sup> in his goal of taking the role of Huntsman Bold and slaying the Hare, the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md), according to the laws of the hunt.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TPH-13)</sup> While this was the stated goal, many of the Hernes were aware Herne Egahirn had taken possession of one of the six gatekeys the Hare needs to escape and kept it on his person, a direct violation of the rules of the hunt, making him an oathbreaker.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:143</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Balaherne-6)</sup> Another brother, Balaherne, had become aware of this, leaving behind a note for his brothers imploring them to make right of Egahirn's cheating, which ultimately would not be heeded.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Balaherne-6)</sup> These huntsmen were under the watch of Si'ess, the [Dark Seducer](Lore_Mazken.md) paramour of Mehrunes Dagon and Master of the Hunt, while they commanded the [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md) and [Frost Daedra](Lore_Frost_Daedra.md) as the Greater and Lesser Dogs of the Hunt, respectively.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSLevel6-14)</sup>

In preparation for the arrival of the Apprentice, the Hernes strategized; sending patrols of their hounds to sweep the hunting grounds while the huntsmen were stationed to defend points of interest determined to be valuable to the Hare. These included the five remaining gatekeys, pieces of the [Armor of the Savior's Hide](Lore_Savior%27s_Hide.md), and the spear case containing the Spear of Bitter Mercy.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BSLevel5-11)</sup> After the Apprentice's arrival and introduction to the Hunt by Si'ess, the Hare gathered these necessary materials while carefully avoiding the invulnerable Hernes; eventually being provided assistance by [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere_Graegyn.md), who was able to dispel the enchanted wards and seals on the spear case and open it.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Old Man Chimere Dialogue-15)</sup> With the spear in-hand, the Hare inversed the dynamic of the Ritual of the Innocent Quarry and the Huntsmen became the hunted, with many Hernes potentially slain on the Apprentice's journey to confront Egahirn and obtain passage through the Temple of the Daedric Rites; where the armies of the clans had passed through a magical gate to the [Havoc Wellhead](Lore_Havoc_Wellhead.md), the initial staging grounds of the invasion of the Battlespire.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSLevel6-14)</sup>

Although many Hernes were assigned to the Chimera, others were stationed within the Havoc Wellhead itself, guarding Faydra Shardai and given further orders such as patrolling the encampment and keeping watch over the plundered invasion materials obtained from the sack of Battlespire, which included [catapults](Lore_Catapult.md) and [battering rams](Lore_Battering_Ram.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:158</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSLevel6-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OHW-16)</sup> When not performing their duties, the Hernes here found time for leisure activities, joining with members of Clan Dagon and [Clan Dremora](Lore_Clan_Dremora.md) in games of sport which use the heads of slain [Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) as the ball.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:158</sup> They would also take part in the boxing arena, both as participants and members of the audience. Here, the Hernes and their rivals, the [Clannfear](Lore_Clannfear.md), engaged in the Trial of Honor to settle disputes through physical combat.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSLevel6-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Clannfear Dialogue-17)</sup> As the Apprentice made their way through the realm, the hero possibly convinced a few Hernes of Shardai Clan to accept an uneasy truce, allowing passage through their territory and even taking arms against rival Daedra if the Apprentice made an oath to wreak vengeance against Xivilai Clan and the Clannfear.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Herne Dialogue-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSLevel6-14)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Noonan) | Developer [Gary Noonan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Gary_Noonan) attributes the Chimera of Desolation's Great Hunt that Mehrunes Dagon's forces participated in as hosted by Hircine.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-SSLW2-18)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_Rishaal) | The look of the Hernes in *Battlespire* was initially intended for [Rishaal Tamir](Lore_Rishaal_Tamir.md) during early development, but that visual concept was recycled for Hernes and Rishaal was redesigned as a "cute little girl demon".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BS-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-RTCA-19)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- The morphology of Hernes resembles that of other Daedra, such as [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md), [Morphoid Daedra](Lore_Morphoid_Daedra.md), [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md), and [Daedra Counts](Lore_Daedra_Count.md). It is unknown if there is any substantial relationship between them.
- In early versions of the Stormhaven zone in *[The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*, Hernes were [originally planned](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Cut_Content#Stormhaven.27s_Plot) to be included as a favored Daedra of [Hircine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Hircine).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Stormhaven CC-10)</sup> During the development of *[Blackwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Blackwood_(Chapter))*, the developers also looked at Hernes for inspiration when designing the [Ruinachs](Lore_Ruinach.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BPTMR-9)</sup>
