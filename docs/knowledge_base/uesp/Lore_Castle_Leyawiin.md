[3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) (*Oblivion Remastered*) Castle Leyawiin (also called **Leyawiin Castle**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> is a large [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) stone castle built on an island in the [Lower Niben](Lore_Lower_Niben.md), straddling the deep channels of the river and guarding the entrance to [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup> It is named for the surrounding walled city of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and is considered to be one of the most notable castles in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATGt TC-5)</sup>

The castle structure is located within the inner keep on the eastern side of the city and possesses staunch circular towers, a soaring main hall, an enclosed courtyard, side corridors, dungeons, guard barracks, private quarters, and secret passages.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATGt TC-5)</sup> The castle also contains poorly organized collections of books and written documents dating back to the early years of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

## History
The exact date of the castle's construction is unknown, although a settlement has existed at Leyawiin since the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Following the collapse of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), a number of councilors from the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) fled the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and came to Leyawiin to seek refuge from the chaos that engulfed the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). These bureaucrats formed the Chamber of Legates, an administrative body which gradually took over the running of County Leyawiin with the approval of Countess [Nevenia Caro](Lore_Nevenia_Caro.md). The Chamber of Legates based themselves out of Castle Leyawiin at this time.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGTL-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AIWCC-9)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Chamber of Legates were betrayed from within by Councilor [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md), an agent of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) who arranged for the systematic assassination of his fellow councilors by [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) cultists. The surviving councilors took refuge within the walls of Castle Leyawiin until the threat had passed.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> This plot to kill the councilors related to Dagon's plans to harvest the power of the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md) and merge [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) with his realm of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md). Dagon's plans were ultimately thwarted by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), with the aid of allies from across [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). This otherworldly band of allies were hosted within the east hall of Castle Leyawiin for a celebration after Dagon's defeat at [Sundercliff](Lore_Sundercliff.md); assembled guests included individuals from across Tamriel, as well as [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) from the Deadlands and from [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

Circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), Lady [Alessia Valga](Lore_Alessia_Caro.md) of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) became Countess Leyawiin upon her marriage to Count [Marius Caro](Lore_Marius_Caro.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3C-12)</sup> Upon her arrival in Leyawiin, Countess Alessia Caro set about implementing a policy of racial suppression against the city's historical [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) minority populations in an attempt to establish a bland Imperial-dominated culture.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The Countess went so far as to take Argonian prisoners from the castle's cells to personally oversee their torture within a secret chamber connected to her private quarters.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> This hidden chamber was used by the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) to steal back a ring from the Countess after a freelance thief attempted to sell it back to her and was apprehended.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The Countess was humiliated that same year by [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md), who tasked the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) with interrupting a society dinner being held in the castle dining room. The Hero cast a spell on the Countess and her guests which rendered them naked, resulting in a widely-reported scandal.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>
