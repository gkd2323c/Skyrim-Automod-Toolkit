[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone.jpg) Idgrod Ravencrone **Idgrod Ravencrone** is an [elderly](Skyrim_Elder.md) [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) [mystic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mystic). She is the [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) of [Hjaalmarch](Skyrim_Hjaalmarch.md) and runs the administration with her husband, [Aslfur](Skyrim_Aslfur.md), who serves as her [steward](Skyrim_Steward.md). Her [housecarl](Skyrim_Housecarl.md) is [Gorm](Skyrim_Gorm.md). She is the mother of [Idgrod the Younger](Skyrim_Idgrod_the_Younger.md) and [Joric](Skyrim_Joric.md). According to the townsfolk, Gorm, and Idgrod herself, she sees visions granted to her by the [Eight](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), and seems to rely on these more than the opinions of her people, as evidenced in her dialogue about [the resident wizard](Skyrim_Falion.md), whom she trusts even if nobody else does.

Although Idgrod is unwilling to reveal which side she is allied with in the [civil war](Skyrim_Civil_War.md) —stating that she stands with Morthal—she remains aligned with the [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) in practice. If Morthal ends up under control of the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md), through your actions actions in either the civil war or [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md), Idgrod will be replaced as Jarl by [Sorli the Builder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sorli_the_Builder). If Hjaalmarch is captured by the Stormcloaks, Idgrod, her court and her children will be exiled to the [Blue Palace](Skyrim_Blue_Palace.md) in [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md) like most of the Imperial-aligned Jarls, and stay there for the rest of the game (unless given over during [the main quest](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md) and later retaken if you're Imperial-aligned, at which point she will resume her post as if nothing ever happened).

She wears a rare assortment of upper-class attire, consisting of a [fur-trimmed cloak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fur-Trimmed_Cloak), [pleated shoes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pleated_Shoes), and a pair of [fine armguards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Armguards). She is equipped with a [steel dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger) and also carries a selection of [common items and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common).

## Conversations
Like most Jarls of Skyrim, Idgrod will spend almost all of her time indoors. She will only leave Highmoon Hall on two occasions, unlike her children. While within the Hall, she may have conversations with her [daughter](Skyrim_Idgrod_the_Younger.md), her [housecarl](Skyrim_Gorm.md), or her [husband](Skyrim_Aslfur.md).

Idgrod the Younger has only inherited a bit of her mother's gift. She has been tasked with taking care of her younger brother, Joric, who apparently inherited all his mother's talent and more. While not outside hanging around the [Thaumaturgist's Hut](Skyrim_Thaumaturgist%27s_Hut.md), she may have one of two conversations with her mother inside the Hall:

**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"You are looking after your brother, yes?"*
**Idgrod the Younger**: *"Yes, mother."*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"Good. He must be kept safe from harm."*
**Idgrod the Younger**: *"What sort of harm? Have you had a vision?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"No, no. Just watch over him."* **Idgrod the Younger**: *"I will be Jarl someday, mother?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"That is what the Divines have planned for you, yes."*
**Idgrod the Younger**: *"Have you... Do you see that future? Will I be a good leader?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"All things are possible, my child. But I believe it is within you to succeed."* Gorm is Idgrod's housecarl. He appears overprotective of his Jarl, as well as a bit nervous when her visions are mentioned:

**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"You are troubled, Gorm."*
**Gorm**: *"No, no. I'm fine. Is there something you need?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"No, Gorm. I am simply telling you what I see."*
**Gorm**: *"Yes, okay. Well thank you."* **Gorm**: *"How are you feeling, Jarl Idgrod?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"The visions continue to hold me in balance, Gorm."*
**Gorm**: *"So, the same then."*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"Do not worry, my friend. All is well."* Aslfur is both Idgrod's husband and her steward, and is protective of his wife in conversations with you, the reasons for which come to light in one conversation:

**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"The people still doubt me, don't they."*
**Aslfur**: *"No, not at all. It's just..."*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"It does not surprise me. They do not share in what I see, and I cannot explain it to them."*
**Aslfur**: *"Just guide them well, and all will work out."*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"I have never doubted that, husband."* **Aslfur**: *"Have you made a decision about Falion?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"What decision is there to be made? I allowed him to live here. I stand by that."*
**Aslfur**: *"Yes, but there are concerns. Rumors that he's up to something."*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"We are all up to something, my dear. All is as it should be."* Idgrod may also ask him about something that seems to trouble him, although if they're both in Highmoon Hall, he will unconvincingly shrug it off:

**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"What troubles you, husband?"*
**Aslfur**: *"What? Oh, nothing. All is well."* However, if Idgrod, Aslfur and the children are in exile in the Blue Palace, Aslfur will elaborate:

**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"What troubles you, husband?"*
**Aslfur**: *"It's Joric. I fear his condition is worse. He calls out in his sleep sometimes. I've heard Falion's name. What does it mean?"*
**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"Joric will be what Joric will be. Give it time."* **Aslfur**: *"Still, I fear there is some connection."*
## Quest-Related Events

### Falion's Secret
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone_05.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Idgrod_Ravencrone_05.jpg) Up to something... Though previously confident that [Falion](Skyrim_Falion.md) would be accepted by the residents of Morthal, when you inform her of [more strange happenings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falion%27s_Secret) in Morthal, she will be less tolerant herself. Telling her that Falion is up to something will result in the response:

**I thought you should know Falion's up to something.** *"Ah, Falion. Always looking where he shouldn't for things he doesn't need. What has he done now?"* **Never mind. It's not important.** *"I see. If something does happen, you know where I am."* **He's casting spells out in the marsh in the middle of the night.** *"Ah, so it is not just dreams then. I have seen this. I will see that it is dealt with. Thank you for bringing clarity."* She then rises from her throne, exits the building altogether for the first time in the game, and proceeds directly to Falion's location, wherever that may be. She will proceed to scold him for his actions:

**Idgrod Ravencrone**: *"Falion, I am disappointed."* **Falion**: *"What is the meaning of this?"* **Idgrod**: *"You've been exploring places you shouldn't, and you know it."* **Idgrod**: *"It stops at once, or else there will be consequences of an unpleasant nature. Do you understand?"* **Falion**: *"I... Yes, I understand."* However, due to a [bug](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falion%27s_Secret#Bugs), she may not actually speak, but instead follow Falion around indefinitely.

**Is there a story behind that burned down house?** or **I hear you want someone to look into that house fire.** *"Hroggar's house? He lost his wife and daughter in the blaze. My people believe it to be cursed now. Who am I to gainsay them?"* **How did the fire start?** *"Hroggar blames his wife for spilling bear fat in the fire. Many folk think he set the fire himself."* **With his own wife and child inside?** *"Lust can make a man do the unthinkable. The ashes were still warm when he pledged himself to Alva."* **So why haven't you arrested him?** *"On rumor and gossip? No. But you, a stranger, might find the truth for us. Sift through the ashes that others are too fearful to touch. See what they tell you. Should you prove him guilty or innocent, I will reward you."* The quest [Laid to Rest](Skyrim_Laid_to_Rest.md) will then begin. **I saw Helgi's Ghost.** *"The spirit world is strong in this place. Look to the graveyard. That is where you'll find her."* When you have broken into [Alva](Skyrim_Alva.md)'s house and found the necessary evidence, report back to Idgrod. Can't go making accusations like that without proof."* **I have Alva's journal.** *"So it's true. That traitorous bitch! Morthal owes you a debt. Here. You were promised a reward for solving the crime, but I need one more favor from you. Morthal is still in danger. The journal mentions Movarth, a master vampire I thought was destroyed a century ago. I'll gather together some able-bodied warriors to clean out Movarth's lair. They'll be waiting outside for you to lead them."* When you have returned:

**The master vampire Movarth is dead.** *"By the Eight, I didn't think you could do it! Now maybe we can put all this behind us. Take this as a token of our gratitude."*

## Notes
- In the *[Prima Official Game Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V__Skyrim__Prima_Official_Game_Guide)*, the summary for her reads:

*"Idgrod Ravencrone is unlike the other Jarls of Skyrim: She is a hunched old woman, who speaks in riddles and parables and rarely ever directly. However, her wisdom is sometimes misunderstood as the ramblings of an old woman, and some are beginning to lose faith in her. Idgrod spent her younger years wandering Tamriel in search of wisdom. Some say that she returned from her trip "touched." Gorm is actively plotting to remove her."* - - Gorm's own summary states that Idgrod and Aslfur are both aware of his plotting, but have not tried to stop him at Idgrod's own insistence.
- She will not attack you if you are a stage four vampire.
- Idgrod was supposed to have a sister named [Argi Farseer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Argi_Farseer) residing in [Stonehills](Skyrim_Stonehills.md), but the latter was not implemented in the final game.
- Jarl Idgrod is the only jarl in the final game that doesn't have a unique jarl outfit assigned to her. She instead uses a default template outfit (noticeable due to the lack of a circlet). There is one person that *does* have a unique jarl outfit without actually being a jarl, this being thane [Bryling](Skyrim_Bryling.md) of [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md). Based on unused content found leftover in the files, Bryling was seemingly at one point considered for jarlship of both Morthal and Solitude.
