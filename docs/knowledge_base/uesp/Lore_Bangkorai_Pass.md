[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/ON-place-Bangkorai_Garrison.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bangkorai_Garrison.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bangkorai_Garrison.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bangkorai_Garrison.jpg) The garrison at the Bangkorai Pass circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Bangkorai Pass is a mountain pass on the border between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), in the [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) region (which was named after the pass). The pass is home to the **Bangkorai Garrison**, which has played a crucial role in defending High Rock against invaders from Hammerfell and the eastern lands for centuries.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ON-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSo HR-2)</sup> According to tradition, the kings of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) are entombed at Bangkorai Garrison so they can protect their borders for "all eternity."<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAdrien-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/ON-tribute-pelin-Knight%27s_Commander_02.png/200px-ON-tribute-pelin-Knight%27s_Commander_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-pelin-Knight%27s_Commander_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-pelin-Knight%27s_Commander_02.png) The Knights defeat the Gray Host Many great battles took place at Bangkorai Pass throughout its history:

- In [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874), Warlord Thulgeg's army of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), driven from [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), was stopped at the pass and forced to flee northeast.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSo HR-2)</sup>

- In [1E 973](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_973), King [Joile](Lore_Joile.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) betrayed his Redguard allies and attempted to invade Hammerfell. A group of [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) defended the pass against his army, but eventually all of them fell, except [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md). In desperation, Leki was able to summon her spirit sword, which allowed her to single-handedly defeat the Breton army and slay King Joile. However, she died of her wounds shortly afterwards. Before her death, she was able to record her [memories](Lore_From_The_Memory_Stone_of_Makela_Leki.md) in a magical [memory stone](Lore_Memory_Stone.md), which was later recovered.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FTMSo ML-4)</sup> Some Breton historians and political commentators of the time characterise King Joile's actions not as a betrayal, but from goodness of heart and a desire to bring solid safety and comfort to his peoples across all of High Rock.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aminyas-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Morgaulle-6)</sup>

- In [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029), Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) deposed King [Styriche](Lore_Styriche.md), the Vampire of [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Hammerfell).md). Styriche fled west, destroying everything in his path, until he was defeated at the Bangkorai Pass. Pelin, a priest of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) who would later be known as [Saint Pelin](Lore_Saint_Pelin.md), died during that battle.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMo SP-7)</sup> Empress Hestra was so impressed by the bravery of the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) that she decided to add [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) to the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSo HR-2)</sup> In honor of Saint Pelin's actions, the annual Red Parade celebration is held at the garrison where the priest's sacrifice is reenacted.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGH1-8)</sup>

- In [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305), after High Rock seceded from the Empire, the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) invaded the province to reclaim it by force. The Alessian Legions of Piety and Grace under Abbot-General [Priscus Mactator](Lore_Priscus_Mactator.md) laid siege to the garrison for five months with no success. The Abbot-General was forced to admit defeat and retreated. Baroness Falinne Guimard of House Guimard was among the defenders.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSo HR-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RHKE-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Desynan)</sup>

- In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), [Durcorach the Black Drake](Lore_Durcorach.md) laid siege to the Bangkorai Garrison during his invasion of High Rock. His army poured into [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) and [Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus.md) from the eastern mountains, sacked [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) and attacked the garrison from behind.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-10)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSo HR-2)</sup> Accounts speak of a relentless daily slaughter at the gates for five months, as the Black Drake sent wave after wave of Reach warriors against the garrison walls.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGH1-8)</sup>

- In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) , High Rock was threatened again, this time by the [Seventh Legion](Lore_Seventh_Legion.md) under the command of [Septima Tharn](Lore_Septima_Tharn.md). The Imperial forces even managed to capture the garrison itself and open a [Dark Anchor](Lore_Dark_Anchor.md) to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) for [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) reinforcements, but were ultimately defeated by the forces of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), including the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), and Queen Arzhela of Evermore.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSo HR-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ON-1)</sup>
