[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/DF-map-Cybiades.png/200px-DF-map-Cybiades.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Cybiades.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Cybiades.png) Map of Cybiades Cybiades is an island off the northern coast of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), within the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Map-1)</sup> It has a tropical coastal climate with a vibrant variety of plantlife.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFClimate-2)</sup> The people of Cybiades create an oil condiment from hot peppers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AFATD3-3)</sup>

By [the fifth century of the Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md#Fifth_Century), the only notable structure on the island was the ruins of Cosh Hall, where armed [human](Lore_Human.md) squatters had taken up residence.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DF-4)</sup> Although otherwise uninhabited, Cybiades was considered a separate political entity until the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), when it was most likely subsumed by the massive expansion of the Kingdom of Sentinel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWINTW-5)</sup>

## Notes
- In early cut drafts for ESO's [Thieves Guild DLC](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Thieves_Guild_(DLC)), Cybiades was originally intended to be the setting, described as having "god-blessed shores" and featuring locations such as the Cybiades Atoll, Gray Barrows, and Headman Cosh's Manor (derived from the Ruins of Cosh Hall).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Much of this was later scrapped and remade when the setting changed to [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md), though [glimpses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Screenshots#Thieves_Guild) of a more plant-filled [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) in Cybiades were previewed in an episode of [ESO Live](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:ESO_Live).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Additionally, the character [Cosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Cosh) continued to play a role in the DLC's storyline.
- Though neither native to nor a resident of Cybiades, [Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md) took the isle's name to signify a new identity, distancing himself from his youth in [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAC-8)</sup>
