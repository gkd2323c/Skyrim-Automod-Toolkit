[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Fort Amol (or simply called **Amol**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BF4-1)</sup> is a fortified settlement found in the region of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), within the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Fort Amol was a noteworthy settlement that was occupied by a Jarl, but it would soon fall into disrepair, and become the regional stronghold for Eastmarch. Also, Amol was at times the name of a town in northeastern Skyrim on the road between [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup>

## History
During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Fort Amol was a castle-town that was occupied by Jarl Ivannar.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> It consisted of the Jarl's Manor, an Inn, and a couple of homes. The rest was encampments that were utilized by the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).

[Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) would utilize Fort Amol as a meeting ground for the Ebonheart Pact. On one occasion, Jorunn had gathered the militaristic leaders of the Pact, [Keshu the Black Fin](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md), Kora Greatstorm, and Yeveth Noramil, and had proclaimed war for [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), with each leader now focusing on the war effort. This conflict would become known as the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md), against the developing [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), the remaining [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), and eventually, the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BF4-1)</sup>

Following his [Konunleikar](Lore_Konunleikar.md), Jorunn the Skald-King would gather the Ebonheart Pact again at Fort Amol, to discuss the future of the alliance, as well as, solidify their bond. But at this time, [Fildgor](Lore_Fildgor.md), Jorunn's exiled brother had returned to Eastern Skyrim, to usurp his brother's throne. In doing so, he employed the [Stormfist Clan](Lore_Stormfist_Clan.md), sabotaged the Konunleikar in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), and attempted to foil the meeting in Fort Amol. An [Agent](Lore_Soulless_One.md) of the Pact would investigate the Stormfist Clan in Fort Amol, as well as, attend to the Pact delegates.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> With the help of their friend, [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md), they are able to uncover the Stormfist's plot to assassinate the King with Daedric poisoning. Once the Stormfist agents were dealt with, the Pact made it a priority to cure Jorunn of his severe poisoning. The meeting in Fort Amol was interrupted, and High King Jorunn would be taken to the [Ternion Monks](Lore_Ternion_Monks.md) of [Mistwatch](Lore_Mistwatch.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the town of Amol<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Eastmarch)</sup> was an active settlement, being ruled by Baroness Helle. It had a rivalry with [Vernim Wood](Lore_Vernim_Wood.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Laintar Dale](Lore_Laintar_Dale.md) in the southwest, [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in the east, and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) in the north.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Map Arena-7)</sup>

During the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the ruins of Fort Amol were occupied by a local wizard's coven. But regardless, Fort Amol would play a pivotal in the Skyrim Civil War, serving as Eastmarch's regional bulwark. It was used by the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) as their defense in the greater Eastmarch wilderness, against the Imperial Legion, should they invade the area. Should the Legion gain control over Fort Amol, it would have left Windhelm in a rather compromised position.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>
