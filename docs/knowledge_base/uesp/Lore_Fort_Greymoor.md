[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/SR-place-Fort_Greymoor.jpg/200px-SR-place-Fort_Greymoor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Greymoor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Greymoor.jpg) Fort Greymoor circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) **Fort Greymoor**, formerly known as **Black Moor**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> and **Darkmoor**,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is a partially ruined fortified settlement that dominates the Western Plains of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md). The central fort (originally called Black Moor Keep)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt C:A-2)</sup> guards a strategic crossroad which connects Whiterun to [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), and [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ho JG-4)</sup> A [lone mountain](Lore_Lone_Mountain.md) lies to the west of the imposing fortress.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-5)</sup>

## History
Black Moor Keep was in existence since at least [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), when it was occupied by Arkasis the Mad Alchemist while he experimented on the citizens of Whiterun, transforming them into [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) using [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) concoctions. When his actions were discovered, Arkasis was chased back to the keep by an angry mob and was forced to flee the hold. These events later went on to be dramatized and reimagined in an [Investigator Vale](Lore_Investigator_Vale.md) novel known as *The Hounds of Black Moor*.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt C:A-2)</sup>

In the early years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Darkmoor was ruled by Count [Sven Advensen](Lore_Sven_Advensen.md) and his wife. Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) granted Sven this title and fiefdom upon his retirement from the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOQB-6)</sup> The previous rulers there had been distant cousins to the new Count and his wife, Lady Inga.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRB-7)</sup> The couple famously became the guardians of Princess [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), and the future Queen of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) was raised in the small Skyrim village, unaware of her birthright.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOQB-6)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Black Moor was an active settlement, being ruled by Lord Torben. It had a rivalry with [North Keep](Lore_North_Keep.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md) to the southeast, and [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) to the east.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Map Arena-8)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the ruins of Fort Greymoor had been ravaged by war, and collapsed parts of the stone walls were fortified with wooden barricades. The occupants changed regularly, with [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and bandits all having occupied the ruins.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> During the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), the fort served as a point of contention between the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), with both sides struggling for control.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> The [Staff of Hasedoki](Lore_Staff_of_Hasedoki.md) was smuggled into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) the same year,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> and the Gilgar family dispatched Modig Gilgar to recover the staff, who was ambushed by smugglers intending to rob him at the road next to Fort Greymoor. He quickly dispatched them, with the Last Dragonborn arriving to the scene shortly after, being attacked by a wary Modig, whom they killed.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
