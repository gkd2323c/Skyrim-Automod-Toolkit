# Surilie

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/OB-sign-Surilie_Brothers_Vineyards.jpg/200px-OB-sign-Surilie_Brothers_Vineyards.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-sign-Surilie_Brothers_Vineyards.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-sign-Surilie_Brothers_Vineyards.jpg) Surilie Brothers Vineyards Branding The Surilie family are [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) vintners whose history stretches through at least three [eras](Lore_Era.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOAMA-1)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRSBW-2)</sup> They produce some of the best wines in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), with it being particularly famous in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBRCS-3)</sup> The family is synonymous with their brand name, the **Surilie Brothers Winery** (**Surilie Brothers** for short),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBRCS-3)</sup> or the **Surilie Farms**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONALP-4)</sup> By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), their base of operations was located at the wine-producing capital of Tamriel, [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBGS-5)</sup> where they established the Surilie Vineyards. At that time, they had a friendly rivalry with the Tamika Winery. When asked if the wine from the Surilie or the Tamika Vineyards were better, Skingrad's citizens said that [Tamika's](Lore_Tamika.md) was "every bit as good as the Surilie Brothers wines".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBRCS-3)</sup> However, Davide Surilie believed his creations were not as good as Tamika's wine but were more affordable.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBDS-6)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/OB-quest-Paranoia_07.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Paranoia_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Paranoia_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Paranoia_07.jpg) Glathir attempting to murder Davide During the later years of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), beverages were already being produced at the Surilie Farms.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONALP-4)</sup> By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), they developed the Surilie Syrah Wine, and Surilie Bros. White Merlot recipes,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> whose key ingredient was the Surilie Grape. Originating from the family itself, Surilie Grapes<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOAMA-1)</sup> saw great use during the Second Era and it was not uncommon to find it in stored in containers throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSGR-8)</sup>

As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), wines bearing the Surilie Reserves label were not forcibly plucked from the vines and were therefore considered [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) -friendly by some Bosmer of Skingrad.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Maltholbor-9)</sup> By then, the family was already in business,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOAMA-1)</sup> although some members pursued other professions. Stephenn Surilie worked as a merchant in [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Stephenn_Surilie-10)</sup> while Simone Surilie served as an agent for [House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Simone_Surilie-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DBC-12)</sup>

When the Surilies arrived in Skingrad is unknown, but by the time of the Interregnum they were already an established brand, and their prominence would eventually grow in the years to come.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOAMA-1)</sup> With [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md)'s prosperity for most of the Second Era, wine production motifs would populate the overland,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONDIGR-13)</sup> and immigrants from other provinces settled and brought their own techniques and recipes to the area.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWines-14)</sup> Surilie grapes were grown in bountiful harvests in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) along with other kinds of grapes such as the [jazbay](Lore_Jazbay.md), pinot, and allegran variants.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWines-14)</sup> During this time period, Surilie Grapes saw great usage in Cyrodiilic recipes such as the Cheydinhal Sherry, Skingrad Muscat, and the Kvatch Watch Grenache. It would also be incorporated as an ingredient in the dishes in many cultures, such as the Orsinium Pink Zinfandel, Sour Guar Shein, and the Xanmeer Brandy.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSGR-8)</sup> A shade of purplish pink dye associated with [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md) has been dubbed as "Surilie Wine".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Surilies were already established within Skingrad, and the heirs to the family legacy were Davide and Gaston Surilie. That same year, a crazed [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) named Glarthir became paranoid that a powerful organization that may be either the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), [Marukhati Selectives](Lore_Marukhati_Selective.md), [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), or the [Ayleid Revivalists](Lore_Ayleid_Revivalists.md) was out to get him. Glathir believed Davide to be the ringleader of the people out to get him. He is believed to have tried to convince someone to spy on him or even kill him. Some accounts say he finally snapped and attempted to murder Davide with an axe, though it is unknown if Davide or Glathir was the victor.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Later that year, the master alchemist [Sinderion](Lore_Sinderion.md) was a connoisseur of wines, and was known to provide alchemy training in return for both Tamika and Surilie Brother's vintage wine from [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> It is said that Surilie Brother's wine from [3E 415](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_415) was well received, while those from 399 were a "once-in-a-lifetime experience".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBGS-5)</sup>

The Surilie Brothers Vineyard at Skingrad was still active into the [third century](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#Third_Century) of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), where clientele would drink within its boundaries.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), bottles of Surilie Brothers Wine was in circulation in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where some innkeepers and food vendors sold it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRSBW-2)</sup> According to Delvin of the [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), the rare [Shadowbanish Wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowbanish_Wine) tastes better than anything the Surilie Brothers make.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup>
