# Mace of Molag Bal

*The Mace of Molag Bal! I give you its true power, mortal. When your enemies lie broken and bloody before you, know that I will be watching.*
— [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/ON-skill-Swing.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Swing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Swing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Swing.jpg) Molag Bal wielding his mace The Mace of Molag Bal, also known as the **Vampire's Mace**, is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) attributed to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), the Lord of Domination and Enslavement. Its enchantment drains the stamina and magicka of its victims and transfers them to the bearer. It also has been known to have the ability to transfer an enemy's strength to its wielder or trap their soul. It has been said to be a good weapon of choice for vanquishing wizards.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup>

Many legends surround the mace, but its origins can be traced back to when Molag Bal deceived an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) blacksmith and enslaved him in pits of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). The blacksmith was turned into a [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md) and forced to forge the mace.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Schick-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RM14D-4)</sup>

Due to Molag Bal's penchant for meddling in mortal affairs, he grants the mace to those he deems worthy but has also been quite free with his artifact. Molag Bal assumes that when such a powerful artifact falls into mortal hands, a trail of death and destruction is sure to follow.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Schick-3)</sup> Some believe the mace to be an object of Daedra worship.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MH-5)</sup> Molag Bal's followers refer to it as **the Master's Mace**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bt MM-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOJavad-7)</sup> and often swear by it.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## History
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWYB-9)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-10)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Mace of Molag Bal among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TA-11)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Mace of Molag Bal and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWFR-12)</sup>

During Molag Bal's attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he was witnessed wielding the mace himself, and utilized it while dueling the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ON-13)</sup>

In the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) an [agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) received the Mace of Molag Bal in exchange for eliminating a heretic mage.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DF-14)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the mace was also awarded to a supplicant who dispatched a lazy [Daedroth](Lore_Daedroth.md) minion named [Menta Na](Lore_Menta_Na.md) for not wreaking the havoc and terror he was created for.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MW-15)</sup> During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), Molag Bal spread corruption by having the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md#Hero_of_Kvatch) incite a local pacifist to murder; again, the mace was given as a reward.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup>

The artifact would later come into the possession of two distinct individuals from the Blades, both which went into hiding from the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) after the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> One belonged to a party known as the [Sheathed Blades](Lore_Sheathed_Blades.md), and they looted the Mace of Molag Bal sometime between [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) and [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) off the corpse of an Arch Necromancer they defeated in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> The second was the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md), who is thought to have looted the mace around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180) from an extravagant chest.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Molag Bal presented the mace to the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) for helping to capture the soul of a priest of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup>
