The Ayleid Empire (or ***imperatum saliache***)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TA-3)</sup> was the original [dominant power](Lore_Empires.md) in [Cyrod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrod).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCKN-4)</sup> It was composed of multiple city-states, ruled by the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) sorcerer-kings.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOP-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1C-6)</sup> The empire's roots began in the [Middle Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Middle_Merethic_Era), when established Ayleid settlements flourished in the Heartlands. Though they were ostensibly a tribute-land to the [High King of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md), the distance between Cyrodiil and [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)'s communication lines led to the Ayleids' isolation and eventual detachment.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup> Their fall is attributed to their arrogant pride of their achievements and smug self-assurance that their empire would last forever, which doomed them to fail and fade into obscurity.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ga L-8)</sup>

## History

### Origins
The Elves who left the Summerset Isles to create splinter cultures in central and southwest [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) sometimes did so to evade the strictures of Aldmeri regulation, which forbade the worship of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) among other things. As Ayleid culture flourished in the last millennium of the Merethic Era, drawing ever further from Alinor, Daedric worship took hold and spread among the Heartland High Elves. The [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) were still widely revered; unlike the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), the Ayleids made no distinction between [Good](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra) and [Bad Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bad_Daedra).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DW:TA-9)</sup> The [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), the capital of the Empire,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Umbacano-10)</sup> was founded circa [1E 0](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup> The height of their civilization was [ME 900](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_900) – [850](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_850), before it succumbed to inner turmoil.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Tor Antiq-11)</sup>

### Internal Conflicts
Sometime in the Merethic or early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the city-states of [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas) and [Delodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Delodiil), led by respective sorcerer-kings [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) and [Cenedelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cenedelin),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CJ-12)</sup> engaged in a series of conflicts over their religious patronage. While Abagarlas revered [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), Delodiil - in addition to the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) - worshipped [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWo D-13)</sup> Meridia and Molag Bal were rivals,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TLO-14)</sup> resulting in dark manipulations from Anumaril on behalf of Bal.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LJ-15)</sup> In both religious pride and envious provocation from Cenedelin's dismissal of his shrine,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TUT-16)</sup> Anumaril went mad, destroying the statue of Meridia within Abagarlas and murdering a visiting family of Delodiils.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWo D-13)</sup> In order to ensure the destruction of Delodiil, Anumaril retreated to the heart of his temple, pleading for help from Molag Bal.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TUT-16)</sup> Bal answered the request with the [Mortuum Vivicus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mortuum_Vivicus), a magical relic capable of storing thousands of souls.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Colors-17)</sup>

Gathering his army of [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) and bannermen, Anumaril marched for Delodiil, intent on harvesting their souls for the Mortuum Vivicus.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Bt MM-18)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CJ-12)</sup> Meridia learned of the plan and warned Delodiil of the threat, prompting Cenedelin to issue a holy task to Curano, an Exarch and Brightblade.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CJ-12)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Kot GB-19)</sup> After the willing sacrifices of several powerful priestesses, the [Prismatic Weapon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prismatic_Core) was forged, a blade capable of destroying the Mortuum Vivicus.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VJ-20)</sup> The newly formed Knights of the Gleaming Blade, afforded the entirety of Delodiil's armory,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Kot GB-19)</sup> infiltrated Abagarlas while Cenedelin rode against the city's dark walls with his full host. The Knights killed Anumaril's family, who were protecting the Mortuum Vivicus, and were successful in preventing the soul-collecting ritual.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CJ-12)</sup> Though Molag Bal saved the Mortuum Vivicus from destruction, Abagarlas was soon destroyed by the lightning of Meridia.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CJ-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWo D-13)</sup>

The city's destruction could be seen and heard from Delodiil, where Anumaril and his forces had arrived. However, the entire city had mysteriously disappeared, leaving not a single brick present where it had once stood. Upon their hasty return to Abagarlas, Anumaril found only ruins. The war between the two city-states was the last that either of them appeared in history, as Abagarlas was never rebuilt and Delodiil was never seen again.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWo D-13)</sup>

The Empire suffered a civil war known as the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md) starting in the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era) and lasting over 200 years into the First Era. The conflict saw battles between the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) - and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping people of the Empire. The war reached its conclusion in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198) with the Scouring of [Wendelbek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wendelbek), with Sorcerer-King [Glinferen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glinferen) of [Atatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atatar) leading a combined force of [Daedraphile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedraphiles) warriors against the traditionalist, Aedra-worshipping [Barsaebics](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barsaebics) of Wendelbek, driving them south into [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The war marked the end of all organized opposition to Daedra worship within the empire.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ASIV-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CATAYQ-22)</sup>

During the First Era,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Founder-23)</sup> Sorcerer-King [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril.md), along with his undead forces, waged war across Tamriel,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Telarendil-24)</sup> striving for its destruction.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Shulkunaak-25)</sup> His brother Valentis, a prince,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Founder-23)</sup> gathered several other sorcerer-kings to stop Celemaril, leading a rebellion against him. With [varla stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varla_Stone) and an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), Valentis donned Celemaril's crown and spoke a ritual incantation, sealing his brother within a crypt beneath a [marble](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marble) statue bearing his likeness.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Shulkunaak-25)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Saashi-27)</sup> After successfully binding Celemaril, the victorious kings wiped his name from all records, treating it as cursed to speak.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Telarendil-24)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Valentis-28)</sup>

### Alessian Slave Rebellion
Circa [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-SATD-29)</sup> [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) slave of the Ayleids, launched a [rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) against the Ayleid Empire. Alongside her [Demigod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demigod) lover, [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus), and the legendary warrior [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), she operated from [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor), fighting to topple the Empire and release the enslaved Nedic peoples across Cyrod.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TLST-30)</sup> Pelinal challenged various sorcerer-kings to duel: [Hadhuul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hadhuul) of [Ceya-Tar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceyatatar), [Haromir of Copper and Tea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haromir), and [Gordhaur the Shaper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gordhaur_the_Shaper) of [Ninendava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ninendava) all fell to him. He then rampaged through the city-states of Narlemae and Celediil before heading to Heldon. Though the Ayleids holding Heldon Bridge pushed back the advancing [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) forces, Pelinal's interference turned the tide of the battle and won the bridge for the Alessians—an act which became known as "[Pelinal's Midyear Massacre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelinal%27s_Midyear_Massacre)" in later Eras.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONMaera-31)</sup> Afterward, the Crusader met with the rebel army to breach the [Temple of the Ancestors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_Ancestors).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOP-5)</sup>

During the rebellion, King Glinferen of Atatar, who had commanded the Scouring of Wendelbek a half-century prior, called upon the Barsaebic Ayleids for assistance. The king of the Barsaebics, still stung from the humiliation of exile, sent him back a blunt refusal.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CATAYQ-22)</sup> The Barsaebics distanced themselves from their Heartland kin, who served darker masters, hearkening back to the Narfinsel Schism.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONAyleid Stat-32)</sup> Despite their shared animosity toward the Empire, the Barsaebics did not ally themselves with Alessia.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CATAYQ-22)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg/200px-OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) A depiction of Pelinal and Umaril's duel Some city-states joined Alessia in rebellion against the Empire, most of them being holdover Aedra worshippers from the Narfinsel Schism.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DW:TA-9)</sup> However, some of the allied city-states worshiped Daedra, most notably [Nenalata](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nenalata).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TLKot A-34)</sup> [Laloriaran Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar), the prince of Nenalata, fought alongside the Nedes, believing their cause to be true.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Dynar-35)</sup> Some of these Ayleid supporters were given land and titles for their assistance.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TLKot A-34)</sup>

Fearing the gathering army outside of the Temple of the Ancestors, the Ayleids made a pact with the [Aurorans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auroran) of Meridia and appointed [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril), an immortal half- [ada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Et%27Ada),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOP-5)</sup> as their liege-lord.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ui U-36)</sup> Pelinal alone stormed through to reach Umaril, as the other rebel leaders thought it was too dangerous.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOP-5)</sup> Eventually, he met Umaril at [Vanua](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vanua), challenging him to face him in combat. Umaril obliged, but only after sending underlings to weaken the Crusader. Still, Pelinal emerged victorious over the Sorcerer-King. Weakened from the fighting, Pelinal was killed by the remaining sorcerer-kings, who descended upon him and cut him into eight pieces in mockery of the Eight Divines.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOP-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TA-3)</sup> Although Pelinal was slain, Umaril's defeat and the fall of the White-Gold Tower marked the end of the Ayleid Empire and the start of the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ASIV-21)</sup>

## Leadership
Though the Ayleid Empire was referred to as 'the first Tamrielic empire' and Umaril the Unfeathered was referred to as the 'liege-lord' of [other Ayleid kings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Narilmor),<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-WGTAP-37)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ui U-36)</sup> [Herminia Cinna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Herminia_Cinna), a published scholar of the Ayleids, insisted there was never one King of the Ayleids, and that instead, the Empire was divided into independent city-states, each ruled by a sorcerer-king. Each sorcerer-king's power waxed and waned over the course of the Empire,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Herminia-38)</sup> and city-states often did not allow their neighbors within their walls.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCKN-4)</sup> The position of sorcerer-king was known to be passed hereditarily upon death,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-33)</sup> but reign rarely passed to heirs without some kind of arcane intrigue.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

Before his appointment as champion, Umaril the Unfeathered was a king with a reputation for leadership in battle.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-URW-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-UC-41)</sup> He commanded military conquests into neighboring regions, including Black Marsh<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-URG-42)</sup> and the territory of the [Sunnatren](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunnatren).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-URE-43)</sup> Additionally, he would disrupt the activities of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) nomads of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) that his enemies traded with.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-URS-44)</sup>

The Empire, though united at times, [warred within itself](#Internal_Conflicts) on multiple occasions, with some notable conflicts being the Narfinsel Schism,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ASIV-21)</sup> the war between Abagarlas and Delodiil,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWo D-13)</sup> and the rampage of Celemaril Light-Bringer.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Saashi-27)</sup> The lasting effects of these conflicts, adding up to be comparable to a full civil war,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TLKot A-34)</sup> was said to have contributed to the fall of the White-Gold Tower and the Empire's collapse at the hands of the Alessians.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ASIV-21)</sup> This trend was exacerbated during the Slave Rebellion as many Ayleids swiftly allied with Alessia, either out of sheer opportunism to wage war against their neighbors, or out of genuine frustration over the enforced worship of Daedra.
