[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-place-Eltheric_Ocean.jpg/200px-ON-place-Eltheric_Ocean.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Eltheric_Ocean.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Eltheric_Ocean.jpg) The Eltheric Ocean, as seen from the coast of the Alik'r Desert The Eltheric Ocean is a vast body of water encompassing the seas west of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It meets the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) at two points within the Padomaic: the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) in the north<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOContraband-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt GB-2)</sup> and the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) in the south.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Blue Divide)</sup> Its waters touch at least four continents: [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to the east, [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) to the west,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Map-3)</sup> [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Pearls-4)</sup> and, allegedly, [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) to the south.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Seat-5)</sup>

## History

### Origins
According to myth, during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), shortly after the creation of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) had interspersed seas, but no oceans. After the war between the Wandering and Old [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), the land was reshaped, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans, forming the Eltheric.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAA-6)</sup>

### Exploration
In the [Early Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Early_Merethic_Era),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AE12-7)</sup> Aldmeris suffered a catastrophe, leaving the continent doomed.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-8)</sup> The native [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) fled across the Eltheric,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Seat-5)</sup> then inhabited extensively by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TLo F-9)</sup> before making land on northern [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) at Nine Prows Landing.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AE12-7)</sup> Later, in the [Middle Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Middle_Merethic_Era), [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md), along with Illio and an unknown third captain, sailed out from Firsthold in search of Aldmeris. Using navigational devices known as [waystones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waystone), the three set sail in various directions, each heading out into the Eltheric Ocean. Illio's sailed southward toward Pyandonea, whereas the unknown ship sailed northwest, toward Thras and Yokuda.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FOTN-10)</sup>

Topal sailed northeast, coincidentally taking the longest path possible through the Abecean Sea without striking land. Eventually, after entering the Iliac Bay, Topal made land - near what would later be known as [Anticlere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anticlere) - after a two-month voyage. From there, Topal charted and mapped the coast of Tamriel, continuing in the Eltheric along [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) before entering the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). Topal would later return to Firsthold with treasures, the only of the three Aldmeris-bound vessels to return, sparking wonder in the faraway land<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FOTN-10)</sup> and prompting other Aldmeri sailors to follow in his footsteps.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-8)</sup> Among these subsequent Aldmer explorers was [Direnni Cygnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Direnni_Cygnus), who discovered the Isle of Balfiera and Adamantine Tower.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Once-11)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-8)</sup>

Around [1E 330](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_330), the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md), after being deemed a target by virtually every faction in northwestern Tamriel, fled their native province of High Rock. According to some legends, the druids may have been following a song that the elder druids heard on the wind, while others attest that the Druids navigated by way of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md). When the druids made land, they found themselves on the island of [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md) before spreading to the nearby islands.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SH-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-So A-13)</sup>

Many dissidents of the Yokudan Empire during the [Singer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers) Period sought refuge on the countless rocky islands east of the continent. Squatters that had already inhabited the islands, some being remnants of the Lefthand Empire, were forced to vacate to evade the incoming refugees. Some sailed south toward Summerset and some sailed toward the Iliac Bay, but those who found the most success were those that arrived at the Systres, aiming to - and eventually succeeding in - conquering the archipelago. However, the Lefthanded reign of the islands was relatively short-lived.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SH-12)</sup> The first [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) warrior-wave from Yokuda, leaving their homeland following the [War of the Singers](Lore_War_of_the_Singers.md),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RTHa TH-14)</sup> arrived on the Systres in [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785) and annihilated the remaining Lefthander forces before continuing east across the ocean to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SH-12)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), several expeditions into the Eltheric Ocean were conducted. One such journey was led by Captain [Wereshark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wereshark), under the employ of Duke Gignac of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). The crew set out to plunder the isles of Pyandonea, eventually arriving at the archipelago and discovering [sea serpents](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Serpent), [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) settlements, and previously unknown races of [lizard bulls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Bull).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSo CW-15)</sup> The famous [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) merchant-explorer Captain Margaux sailed into the Sea of Pearls on her ship, the *Golden [Spriggan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan)*. By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she had been declared missing and her house was up for sale.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> In the same year, the infamous Graven Deep was explored by Dhulef, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) sailor, who discovered an uncharted island within the region.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Dhulef-17)</sup>

## Composition
The Eltheric Ocean contains a variety of currents of both wind and sea, such as the maritime Herne Current, which runs offshore from High Rock, and the western-originating Yokudan Zephyr, which steadily but slowly passes over the Glenumbra Banks in the summer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt GB-2)</sup> The waters of the Eltheric are described as warm, particularly when contrasted with the frigid waters of the Sea of Ghosts.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> While the Abecean Sea is calm, the neighboring Cursed Sea is prone to rolling waves capable of afflicting seasoned sailors with sea sickness.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Jo T2-19)</sup> The Thrassian Archipelago within the Cursed Sea surrounds a brackish lagoon.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-20)</sup>

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), within Cursed Sea - the waters that surround Thras - a magical whirlpool larger than [Alten Corimont](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alten_Corimont) tore ships into a portal to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). It was capable of suddenly spinning up before ships could react.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-1I-21)</sup> A very similar whirlpool, known as the Maelstrom of [Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), was said to exist within the Abecean Sea,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TDo O-22)</sup> where some sources place Thras.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMot SS-23)</sup> Maormer have referred to a mysterious Mother Sea,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> but it is unknown if the term refers to a body of water.

Among these are the [Dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh), who inhabit the Abecean and Iliac, losing their intelligence when they migrate to land and regaining it upon returning to the ocean.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-39)</sup> Though unrelated to the Dreugh, the similarly crustacean [Hadolids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hadolid) migrate between sea and land in decade-long rotations.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-HRJ-40)</sup> [Sea Sload](Lore_Sload.md) come from the underwater kingdom of [Ul'vor Kus](Lore_Ul%27vor_Kus.md), located in the Sea of Pearls, and occasionally invade Summerset.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMot SS-23)</sup> [Yaghra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yaghra), who take various forms, serve the Sea Sload<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESODS-41)</sup> and have assisted them in their land assaults.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-42)</sup> [Ornaugs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ornaug) take to the seas not for food, but only to reach the littoral regions of new reefs or islands.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Eot O-43)</sup>

### Creatures of Legend
Though thought to be a myth by some, veteran sailors have attested that [Ithguleoir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithguleoir), an immortal leviathan, haunts the depths of the Eltheric Ocean, dining on its sailors one by one. He is described as having an oily back and blowing sea-mist like a whale, releasing a foul stench akin to those from Oblivion. It is reportedly intelligent, setting traps in the [Glenumbra Banks](Lore_Glenumbra_Banks.md) designed to run ships ashore.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt GB-2)</sup> Pyandonea is said to provide the perfect environment for southern water spirits.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PGE1TWR-30)</sup>

During the Thrassian War,<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-HRJ-40)</sup> the Sload of Thras released huge sea-monsters to attack the Systres Archipelago. The mightiest of these beasts was the [Molmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molmor), variously described as a massive whale or an octopus with grasping tentacles, with an array of long, sharp spines and dripping, slimy skin. It was said to turn the water it swam in black and boiled, capable of heaving itself ashore and crushing everything in its path. It ravaged the coast of the island of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) for seven years before it was killed. Its remains were overtaken by [the Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green) and became [Molmor Islet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molmor_Islet).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Bat M-44)</sup> During the Interregnum, old sailors would swap stories about the mysterious Graven Deep. Among the explanations for the disappearance of ships within the region was a giant whale.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Dhulef-17)</sup>
