*I exited the tent to find a scene of carnage. A larger, angrier ogre was fighting the other ogres. The large one had bony claws strapped to his arms. He slammed them into the ground. Ripples of ice shot out from the impact sites, slamming into the others and knocking them down. The ogre who had healed my leg tossed what appeared to be a glowing ball of snow at the larger one. When it hit him, he reared back and shouted. He gestured and the snow around him rose up and formed into crude simulacrums and joined him in the fight.*
— *[The Ice Elder of the Ogres](Lore_The_Ice_Elder_of_the_Ogres.md)* Urkazbur, otherwise known as **Mad Urkazbur**, was a powerful and intelligent [Snow Ogre](Lore_Ogre.md) shaman, known as the "Ice Elder of the Ogres" or the "mad Ogre wizard".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mad-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-2)</sup> Urkazbur resided in the wilds of northern [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), where he had gathered a small army of fellow Ogres.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Effigy-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Book Quest-4)</sup> Urkazbur was considered unusually intelligent for his kind,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bigot-5)</sup> even seemingly knowing how to read [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bigot-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Book Quest-4)</sup> He was a master of powerful totemic frost [magic](Lore_Magic.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-2)</sup> his impressive abilities included the power to create enchanted [duplicates](Lore_Ice_Golem.md) of himself made entirely of ice. These effigies started out small, about the size of a child's doll, and would grow large enough to fight at his side.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Effigy-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Urkazbur was known to regularly practice his ice spells.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mad-1)</sup>

## Biography
Urkazbur once attacked a camp of fellow Ogre elders soon after some of them healed the leg of an injured researcher with ice magic. In the fight, Urkazbur displayed dominance over them with his own ice magic. The researcher was able to escape the Ogres unharmed,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-2)</sup> though they were killed when they attempted to return. By the time they did, Urkazbur had succeeded in killing the rival Ogres and turned their camp into an altar for performing his magic.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he was considered a danger by the inhabitants of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), but his skills were still admired by the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md). The museum known as the "House of Orsimer Glories" desired one of his not completely enchanted ice-effigies for their collection, as they considered it "history in the making".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Effigy-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Desiring more culture for the city, [King Kurog](Lore_King_Kurog.md) commissioned a [High Elf](Lore_High_Elf.md) scholar named [Cirantille](Lore_Cirantille.md) to deliver some of her books to the city, but her shipment was attacked by Urkazbur's Ogres.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arz-9)</sup> Among the shipment was several dozen copies of her book "[The Ogres of Wrothgar: A Continuing Treatise](Lore_The_Ogres_of_Wrothgar__A_Continuing_Treatise.md)",<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bigot-5)</sup> said book being a bigoted text on Ogres, dismissing their intelligence and magical capabilities and claiming they deserve to be killed.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Urkazbur had his Ogres destroy these books, leading to a contract being put out for him to be killed and the pages recovered.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arz-9)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Book Quest-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bigot-5)</sup>

[The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) fulfilled this contract, recovering the book pages and killing Mad Urkazbur.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Book Quest-4)</sup> They would also recover one of Urkazbur's ice-effigies which would go on display in the Orsimer House of Glories. The bigoted and supposed expert on Ogres, Cirantille, who ordered for his death, considered Urkazbur simply the exception that proved the rule when it came to Ogre intelligence, calling him a brutish freak, cursed with intelligence above his station. Being quoted as saying "Can't have ogres thinking they're people!", as one of her reasons for why he had to be slain.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bigot-5)</sup>
