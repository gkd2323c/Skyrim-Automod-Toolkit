—The Eternal Champion before escaping the [Imperial Dungeons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Dungeons)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARManual-1)</sup> The Eternal Champion, purportedly named **[Talin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talin)**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARManual-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> (b. [3E 370](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_370)),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DC-2)</sup> was an otherwise unknown [hero](Lore_Hero.md) best known for ending the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399) by bringing together the pieces of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), killing the impostor [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HT-3)</sup> and rescuing the true emperor, [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), and general of the [Imperial Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Guard), [Talin Warhaft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Warhaft),<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> from a [dimension of Tharn's choosing](Lore_Oblivion.md) where they had been imprisoned for ten years.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARIntro-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TET-5)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/AR-menu-Character_Creation_House.jpg/200px-AR-menu-Character_Creation_House.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-menu-Character_Creation_House.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-menu-Character_Creation_House.jpg) The Champion's childhood home The Champion's father was General Talin Warhaft, who fought in a [battle](Lore_Wars.md) at [Borim Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Borim_Valley). Along with having a mother and a sister, the Champion also had a great-aunt and a great-uncle who each lived in separate villages close to the Champion's home village. During their childhood, the Champion worked at the stables of their home village and did various other chores. The Champion also attended regular a weapons class conducted by [Armsmaster Festil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Festil), an elderly warrior who had been fighting since the day of the Champion's grandfather.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> They became friends with [Ria Silmane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ria_Silmane) after they were summoned to the [Imperial Court](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Court) to learn under her tutelage the ways of [sorcery](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARManual-1)</sup> At the Imperial Court they were considered only a minor member of no real consequence.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARRia-7)</sup>

### Alternate Backstories
Outside of the purported background of the Eternal Champion as Talin, various cultures throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) have conflicting claims about the hero. Supposed identities for the Champion include **Grachta** the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [Knight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knight), **Dunastyr** the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) Sorcerer, **Shathra** the female [Ohmes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ohmes) [Khajiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) Assassin, and **Tyronicus** the [Cyrodilic](Lore_Imperial.md) Battlemage.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-IWDG-8)</sup> Other unnamed identities include the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) account who claim in public the Champion was an enlightened wizard, but in private admit he was an outcast. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) see them as the son of a proud king. The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) have legends that they were the daughter of the living [goddess](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia). The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) say she was an [Ayleidion](Lore_Ayleid.md) Aurehliar. Even some [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) clans have their own legends for the Eternal Champion, with the [Lysrezi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyrezi) believing him to be a vampire from a [realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) outside of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MAS-9)</sup>

### Imperial Simulacrum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/29/AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) The Staff of Chaos In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), Jagar Tharn overthrew Emperor Uriel, imprisoned him and General Warhaft in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and killed Ria Silmane. The Champion was imprisoned by Jagar Tharn in the [Imperial Dungeons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_City_Prison) and left to die. Using what little of her life force remained, Silmane contacted the Champion from between [the mortal world](Lore_Mundus.md) and [the afterlife](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) and guided them to freedom through a shift-gate, tasking them with reassembling the Staff of Chaos and rescuing the emperor.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bio-10)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> Silmane aided the Champion in this task by using her magic to mask the Champion's identity,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARRia-7)</sup> which successfully concealed the Champion's name from Tharn until seven of the Staff's eight pieces had been recovered.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ARTharn-11)</sup>

Using Silmane's guidance to find the location of the pieces, with covert assistance from [Queen Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) and [King Eadwyre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eadwyre), who through contact with Silmane gathered intelligence on the locations of the pieces of the Staff of Chaos to be passed on to the Champion,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bio-10)</sup> the Champion journeyed to [Stonekeep](Lore_Stonekeep.md), [Fang Lair](Lore_Fang_Lair.md), the [Fortress of Ice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortress_of_Ice), [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md), [Selene's Web](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene%27s_Web), the [Elden Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Grove), the [Temple of Agamanus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Agamanus), the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus), the [Temple of Mad God](Lore_Sheogorath.md#Notes), the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), the [Mines of Khuras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mines_of_Khuras), the [Crypt of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md), the [Vaults of Gemin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaults_of_Gemin), [Murkwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Murkwood), [Black Gate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Gate), and [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) on a quest that spanned the course of a decade, before ultimately descending into the depths of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md) to face Tharn. The Champion defeated Tharn in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), with their battle doing considerable damage to the Imperial Palace,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup> by draining the [Jewel of Fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jewel_of_Fire) of its power and restoring the Staff of Chaos to its full power, killing Tharn in the process. The Champion then freed Emperor Uriel and General Warhaft from Oblivion and was proclaimed by the Emperor as the Eternal Champion. They then took their place at the emperor's side, tasked with seeing to the needs of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Uriel-13)</sup>

### Legacy
The Eternal Champion became somewhat of a cultural hero to Imperial Loyalists, many of whom retracing their steps as part of a pilgrimage.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AMM-14)</sup> Some remember them as one of the greatest warriors in the modern eras.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HT-3)</sup> Their identity remained unknown to the public for years after their quest's completion,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HT-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BHot E4-15)</sup> which some claimed was at the will of Emperor Uriel Septim VII.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-IWTW-16)</sup>

The name, race, and gender of the Eternal Champion are ultimately player determined. The [introduction](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Arena_Player%27s_Guide_Introduction) to the [official game manual](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Arena_Player%27s_Guide) refers to them as a male named Talin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARManual-1)</sup> Additionally, a pre-release screenshot depicts the Eternal Champion as a [Breton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Breton) male named Talin.<sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb4)</sup> Some in-universe references to the Champion describe them as an "unknown hero"<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HT-3)</sup> and "enigmatic"<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BHot E4-15)</sup> whilst some others refer to them as male.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bio-10)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRB5-17)</sup> [Alternate backstories](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md#Alternate_Backstories) were provided in [unofficial lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Unofficial_Lore).
- | [^2.](#note_nb2) | |
| --- | --- |

In the original release of [*Arena*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena), General Warhaft is shown leaving Oblivion with Uriel VII, and thanks the Champion for their service. In the [Deluxe Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Deluxe_Edition), Uriel VII alone is seen leaving Oblivion, with no mention of Warhaft whatsoever after the game's introduction.
- | [^3.](#note_nb3) | |
| --- | --- |

The events of *Arena* start in 3E 389, however, according to the [*Biography of Queen Barenziah*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Biography_of_Queen_Barenziah,_Vol._III), this happened after the death of [Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus), which occurred in [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DC-2)</sup>
- In [*Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), a [background history](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Background_History) would have been used for a cut feature allowing players to import their character from *Arena* which would have made [the Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) and the Eternal Champion one and the same.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Background Game-18)</sup>
