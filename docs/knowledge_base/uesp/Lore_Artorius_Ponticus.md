Artorius Ponticus (né **Ancrus**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup> served as the Primate of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and the leader of the [Akatosh Chantry](Lore_Akatosh_Chantry.md) in [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Based in the Great Cathedral of Akatosh, he was a very powerful and influential figure in the years leading up to the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup> A deeply spiritual man, Artorius spent much of his life in service to Akatosh, who he believed guided and supported his efforts. He was considered the primary religious authority of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TP1-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWATD-4)</sup> As Primate, Artorius oversaw all aspects of Akatosh worship in the Cathedral. He was supported by Grand Chanter Surus and Grand Sermonizer Fithia, as well as the warrior-priests of the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CH-5)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup>

Despite his religious station, Artorious had a vast network of spies and informants throughout the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) and sought to shape its political landscape.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup> Nevertheless, many heralded him as a stabilizing force in the region for most of his primacy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt CPA-6)</sup> In his later years, he was involved in a conflict with the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) and a three-way power struggle for control of the Gold Coast.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Online DB-7)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
Artorius Ancrus was born into the wealthy Ancrus family. He was the youngest of four brothers. With his two eldest brothers being expected to take control of the family's holdings and his other brother, Angelus, being an officer in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), Artorius grew up without a clear place in the world. As the youngest of the four brothers, tradition dictated that Artorius would live his life in service to the [Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), much to his disdain.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TP1-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt CPA-6)</sup>

He was fascinated by the ongoing battles between [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) and [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542), so he scoured the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) for anyone who could tell him more about the conflict and usually found them in the most unsavory sections of the city. It was during this time that he became acquainted with the notorious crime boss, Vodunius Monrius, and began to run errands for him and his lieutenants, much to the chagrin of his father and older brothers. Artorius relished the role and the excitement that accompanied it until one day when he was collecting gold from the various merchants and artisans working in and around the [Market District](Lore_Market_District_(Imperial_City).md). Upon entering the shop of a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) baker named Sticky Paws, he found four officers of the city's guard waiting for him. The guard captain told Artorius that they could bring the full weight of the law down on him, but that thanks to his father's wealth and influence, he would be allowed to serve his penance in the Temple of the Divines under the supervision of its priests.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TP1-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TP2-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt CPA-6)</sup>

By the end of 2E 542, Artorius found himself in the Temple of the Divines serving his penance of one year and a day. Artorius was placed under the watch of Ilavius Alfeno, a priest of Akatosh, who eventually became his mentor. At the temple, Artorius performed an endless array of chores that included cleaning, food preparation, and helping to care for the shrines each day. He was also prescribed periods of study, meditation, and prayer. Artorius quickly became fascinated with the tales and tenets of the Divines. He visited each shrine of the Eight Divines and questioned the priests endlessly about their holy sphere and rituals. Of all the Divines, however, the one that Artorius felt the strongest connection to was Akatosh.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TP2-8)</sup>

When [Jonas Coventine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jonas_Coventine), the Primate of Akatosh, arrived for his annual visit to the temple, he took an immediate liking to Artorius. After leading the temple in worship, the Primate invited Artorius to a private meeting. The details of the meeting are unknown, but shortly after, Artorius pledged himself to the Divines. When his probation was over, he took the vows of the priesthood and devoted himself to serving Akatosh.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TP2-8)</sup>

After becoming a priest, Artorius continued to serve in the Temple of the Divines, learning more and more as he split his time between the eight shrines there. Artorius longed to be assigned to the Cathedral of Akatosh in Kvatch, which he believed was the best place in Tamriel for him to serve and worship the Dragon God of Time. He used every option at his disposal to make himself an appealing candidate to the high priests and the Primate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup>

### Miracle of the First Shrine
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-place-Cathedral_of_Akatosh.jpg/200px-ON-place-Cathedral_of_Akatosh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_Akatosh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_Akatosh.jpg) The Great Cathedral of Akatosh in Kvatch When a man named Casiras the Hunter brought his young son to the temple with the shaft of an arrow jutting from his chest, Artorius immediately began rendering aid. Even though the wound had clearly killed the child, Artorius led the grieving father to Akatosh's shrine and began to pray. Artorius reportedly prayed for three straight days, never pausing to eat, drink, or even sleep. At the end of the third day, Artorius began to speak rapidly in a tongue that no one in the temple could understand. Then a burst of brilliant light emanated from the shrine, which struck Artorius and flowed through his body, down his arms, and into his hands before passing on to the dead boy. When the glow subsided, the wound was healed, and the child was alive. It was claimed that Akatosh had answered Artorius' prayers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup>

Word of this reputed miracle, called "the Miracle of the First Shrine", spread throughout the Empire, quickly reaching Primate Jonas. Jonas decided that if Akatosh was indeed performing miracles through one of his priests, then that priest needed to be at the Cathedral in Kvatch. Soon, Artorius was serving under the Primate in Kvatch.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup> Jonas became Artorius' mentor, and he was never far from the Primate's side.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLV-9)</sup>

Artorius rose through the hierarchy of the Cathedral, eventually becoming Grand Sermonizer of Akatosh, second only to the Primate of Kvatch himself. However, at some point after the outbreak of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), the relationship between the Primate and Artorius became strained. Artorius wanted the clergy to become more involved in the growing political unrest spreading throughout the Empire and with the increasing threat of the Knahaten Flu, but Jonas wanted the Cathedral to remain distant from secular issues. The two argued constantly about the subject, reaching the point where Jonas was prepared to put an end to the disagreement through any means at his disposal—including sending Artorius to a distant posting.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup>

### Accession to the Primacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_06.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_06.jpg) A memory of Artorius' encounter with Lyra Viria Before Jonas could carry out his threat, Artorius performed the [Black Sacrament](Lore_Black_Sacrament.md) to contract the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) to kill Jonas so that he could become the Primate of Akatosh.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONPA-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONST-11)</sup> The Dark Brotherhood accepted and sent an assassin to Kvatch to carry out the contract. The Brotherhood assassin [Lyra Viria](Lore_Lyra_Viria.md) killed Jonas in the Cathedral of Akatosh, and immediately after the Primate's murder, Artorius confronted the assassin. Artorius sought his own personal champion and exploited Viria's disillusionment with the Brotherhood to try to convince her that Akatosh wanted them to join forces. Artorius told Viria that only through him and Akatosh could she find purpose and achieve redemption.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONPA-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONGFTP-12)</sup> Although Viria was hesitant to align with Artorius, she ultimately yielded and pledged her sword and life to him and the Dragon God.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONGFTP-12)</sup> She became known as the [Black Dragon](Lore_Black_Dragon.md) and became the First Sword of the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONGFTP-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TBDJ1-13)</sup>

Although it was claimed that Jonas died of natural causes, rumors circulated throughout Kvatch that he was assassinated by the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONGVT-14)</sup> Regardless of the rumors, Jonas' death paved the way for Artorius to advance to the top of the Akatosh Chantry's hierarchy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup> Artorius was elected the Primate of Kvatch, becoming the primary religious authority of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWATD-4)</sup> He also changed his surname to Ponticus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TP3-1)</sup> The famous loom artist Gloria Medilus created tapestries depicting Artorius' investiture to commemorate the ceremony.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CT-15)</sup>

### The Wolf and the Pirate Queen
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/ON-npc-Count_Carolus_Aquilarios.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Count_Carolus_Aquilarios.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Count_Carolus_Aquilarios.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Count_Carolus_Aquilarios.jpg) Count Carolus Aquilarios [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/ON-npc-Governor_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Governor_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Governor_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Governor_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.jpg) Governor Fortunata ap Dugal Artorius had a grudgingly civil relationship with Count [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md). When Varen initiated a [revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) against Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), his nephew [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md) was left behind to protect Kvatch. Still loyal to Leovic, Count [Ephrem Benirus](Lore_Ephrem_Benirus.md) of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) sent the Imperial forces that were stationed in Anvil to seize Varen's property and holdings, but Carolus' troops repelled the attacks. Although Artorius sought a more influential position in the city's hierarchy, he put aside his ambitions while Carolus worked to protect Kvatch.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWATD-4)</sup>

In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Varen killed Leovic and declared himself emperor, so Carolus was named Count of Kvatch. However, as soon as Carolus was named count, Artorius began working to undermine his authority and carve out more power and influence for himself.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWATD-4)</sup> In the same year, [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) led a fleet of merchant warships and pirate vessels into Anvil Harbor and seized control of the weakened city. After capturing the city, Fortunata took the title of Provincial Governor of Anvil and declared the Gold Coast to be a free and independent state.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TWATPQ-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rot RS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-19)</sup> After this, Fortunata requested that Kvatch join Anvil as part of an independent Gold Coast, but Carolus refused, preferring to remain loyal to Varen. Fortunata did not feel confident enough at this time to press the demand, but when Varen disappeared in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), she took advantage of Kvatch's sudden isolation and made her demands more forceful. Ultimately, Fortunata's pirates defeated Carolus' troops, leaving him with no choice but to swear fealty to an independent Gold Coast.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TWATPQ-16)</sup>

With his back against the wall, Carolus reluctantly agreed to an alliance with Artorius and the Order of the Hour to protect Kvatch.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCCA-20)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup> After the outbreak of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) and the subsequent collapse of the Empire, Artorius set out to bolster the power and scope of the Order of the Hour. He instructed Commander Marcus Scipio to increase the size of the Order and work to expand its responsibilities beyond the boundaries of the Cathedral and into the rest of Kvatch. Artorius maintained that this was done to reinforce the capabilities of the Kvatch Guard in the dangerous and troubling times.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OOTH-21)</sup>

### The Dark Brotherhood
Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Anvil and Kvatch experienced a notable increase in murders, which some suspected to be the work of the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCCA-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-19)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONCF-22)</sup> In response to the increase in murders and the other threats to the city, Carolus granted the Order of the Hour expanded rights and duties to supplement the efforts of the Kvatch Guard.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OOTH-21)</sup> Carolus also granted Artorius the powers to once again convert the Order into an army, provided the Order returned to the Cathedral once the threat was resolved.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OOTH-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCCA-20)</sup> The Order's numbers expanded quickly, growing larger than it ever had in its history.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OOTH-21)</sup> The expansion of the Order unsettled Fortunata, who believed the Primate and Carolus were building an army to eventually attack Anvil.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-23)</sup>

Around 2E 582, due to the volatile political situation, Artorius became concerned that the organization he had used to help him become primate might be used against him, so he decided to move against the Dark Brotherhood and eliminate them.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONST-11)</sup> To achieve this, he needed to locate their sanctuary on the Gold Coast. The Black Dragon was tasked with capturing suspected Brotherhood members and taking them to the At-Himah Family Estate, where Grand Sermonizer Fithia would interrogate them.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TGSJ-24)</sup> They interrogated and tortured several Brotherhood members, but none of them revealed the location of the sanctuary before dying.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TBDJ1-13)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TGSJ-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONGSF-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONLIS-26)</sup> Fithia ordered her sermonizers to deliver sermons urging the people of Kvatch to stand against the Brotherhood.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TGSJ-24)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Wot GS-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONAC-28)</sup> The Brotherhood responded to the sermons by murdering not only the chantry's street preachers speaking out against them but the Grand Sermonizer herself.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONLIS-26)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-quest-Filling_the_Void.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Filling_the_Void.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Filling_the_Void.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Filling_the_Void.jpg) Artorius awaits the Brotherhood assassins [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-npc-Primate_Artorius_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Primate_Artorius_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Primate_Artorius_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Primate_Artorius_02.jpg) Artorius impaled on his staff After the Brotherhood killed Fortunata at the behest of Carolus,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONASR-29)</sup> Artorius capitalized on the situation and offered pardons to any Red Sail pirates who agreed to abandon their criminal ways and join the Order of the Hour. As a result, many members of Fortunata's old crew began flocking to the Order's headquarters, the Enclave of the Hourglass, to join.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCCA-20)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-JTO-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-CMELV-31)</sup>

After Fortunata's assassination, Carolus informed the Brotherhood that the Black Dragon was the First Sword of the Order of the Hour.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCCA-20)</sup> Around that time, the Black Dragon learned the location of the sanctuary by interrogating an assassin named Brema, which she then revealed to Artorius.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLV-9)</sup> By this time, the Black Dragon had become disillusioned with Artorius and the Order.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLV-9)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TBDJ2-32)</sup> The Black Dragon journeyed to the Order of the Hour's ancient headquarters, Knightsgrave, where the Brotherhood killed her.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONWOS-33)</sup> Before she died, she revealed that she had informed Artorius of the location of the Dark Brotherhood sanctuary and that he planned to attack it.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLV-9)</sup> In response, the Brotherhood decided to preemptively attack Artorius and the Order before they could attack the sanctuary.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONST-11)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONFTV-34)</sup> Several Brotherhood assassins stormed the catacombs below the Cathedral of Akatosh and dispatched the forces of the Order of the Hour guarding the tunnels. Artorius confronted the assassins once they reached the main hall and fought them using [time magic](Lore_Time_Magic.md), but they defeated him. After being defeated, he kneeled before the cathedral's altar, where a [Brotherhood assassin](Lore_The_Vestige.md) impaled Artorius with his staff, killing him.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONFTV-34)</sup>
