*In my quest to write this book, I accomplished more than I could have ever dreamed at the start. I learned how to set myself up for success. I discovered the difference between expensive tools and true skill—not that you can’t have both. I stopped using history as an escape and began to explore it as a foundation for culture and courage. I grew communities around my cooking! I had wild, death-defying adventures and made friends I will keep for life. I gained the priceless ability to prioritize myself when I need to—a skill that I hope to continue honing. I fanned the flames of my own inspiration.*
—Urzhag-gro Larak<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:201</sup> Urzhag gro-Larak was an [Orc](Lore_Orcs.md) [culinary](Lore_Cuisine.md) master, author of *Tastes and Tales from Tamriel*, a traveler and adventurer, and the former apprentice of the legendary Gourmet, [Balagog gro-Nolob](Lore_Balagog_gro-Nolob.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/SR-npc-Chief_Larak.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Chief_Larak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Chief_Larak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Chief_Larak.jpg) Chief Larak, Urzhag's father [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/SR-place-Mor_Khazgur.jpg/200px-SR-place-Mor_Khazgur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mor_Khazgur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mor_Khazgur.jpg) Mor Khazgur Uzhag was born and raised in Mor Khazgur, an Orcish stronghold known for its mines. His father, Chief Larak, governed with an emphasis on resilience and martial prowess, virtues expected of every son of the clan. Slighter than most Orcs and lacking natural talent with weapons, Urzhag found himself outmatched in the training yard and quietly ridiculed for it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup> Growing up, Urzhag held little fondness for breakfast. Each morning brought unseasoned deer intestine and bland gruel, forced upon him by the elders who believed it would strengthen him for long days in the mines. Though meant to fortify him, the meals only left him heavy and sluggish, and he often wished he could forgo them entirely.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:9-11</sup>

Where others devoted themselves to drills and contests of strength, Uzhag sought refuge in the longhouse, burying himself in whatever texts he could find. He was particularly drawn to chronicles of Tamriel's past: the conquests of Tiber Septim, the rise of the Knights of the Nine, and the calamity of Baar Dau that reshaped Vivec City into the Scathing Bay. Through these accounts, he began to assemble a broader understanding of the world beyond the stronghold. When taunted for his frailty or poor aim, he escaped into imagination, picturing himself as an Aldmeri scholar among the Sapiarchs or a learned Breton mage wielding elegant spells.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup>

Everything changed the day he discovered *Uncommon Taste*, the first cookbook he had ever read. Its pages stirred something in him that no war chronicle ever had. Seizing a rare moment away from the mines, he slipped into the kitchen to experiment. He chopped carrots, blended broths, and stirred with determined focus. The finished dish was exquisite and before long, a group of miners burst in, drawn by the scent. His fellow kinsmen found his dish delicious and wanted more. For the first time, he had earned admiration. Encouraged by this unexpected praise, he resolved to master every recipe in Uncommon Taste. Soon he began devising dishes of his own, experimenting with flavors and techniques.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup>

When Urzhag was still a child, one of his father's friends brought a small chest to Mor Khazgur, filled with Orsimer artifacts reclaimed from Breton soldiers. Among the items were writings describing grand feasts held at the Orsinium in the Second Era, including a recipe for [Orzorga's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orzorga) Tripe Trifle Pocket. Urzhag spent weeks experimenting to balance the flavors correctly, and when he finally succeeded, with a few personal adjustments, it became one of the favorite meals in Mor Khazgur.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:33</sup>

### Apprenticeship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/SR-place-Solitude.jpg/200px-SR-place-Solitude.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Solitude.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Solitude.jpg) Solitude [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/SR-npc-Balagog_gro-Nolob.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Balagog_gro-Nolob.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Balagog_gro-Nolob.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Balagog_gro-Nolob.jpg) Balagog gro-Nalob - Urzhag's mentor Circa [4E 197](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_197), the news eventually reached Mor Khazgur that the elusive Gourmet, the author of Uncommon Taste, the cookbook that had changed Uzhag's life, was visiting Solitude. Though the chef's identity was concealed and a meeting seemed unlikely, Uzhag set out for the northern city in search of his inspiration. At the Winking Skeever, he met a finely dressed Orc who introduced himself as Balagog gro-Nolob. After discussing cuisine and confessing his admiration for Uncommon Taste, Uzhag learned near midnight that Balagog himself was the Gourmet. In the days that followed, Uzhag alternated between apologies and pleas for mentorship. Though Balagog refused to teach him, he allowed him to sample his dishes. Determined to prove himself, Uzhag decided to cook for the master. After dismissing more elaborate ideas, he chose a simple comfort dish: a bratwurst in a bun with cabbage and mustard. Once Balagog finished the meal he agreed to teach Uzhag for three months.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup>

During his apprenticeship, Balagog fundamentally changed Urzhag's understanding of food. Under his mentor's guidance, recipes became more than simple instructions: they became expressions shaped by history, magic, politics, and even music. Balagog recounted the origins of his techniques and the lessons he had gathered throughout Tamriel, yet he returned time and again to a single principle: he could teach the craft, but true understanding would only come once Urzhag had cooked for Tamriel himself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup> During his apprenticeship, whenever Urzhag presented a dish, Balagog followed the same ritual: a slow inhale, three deliberate bites, and a measured pause. If satisfied, he would laugh and remark that it was high-quality kindling.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:201</sup> During his stay in Solitude, Urzhag enjoyed rare moments away from training with Balagog, strolling along the docks and marveling at the exotic ingredients imported by the East Empire Company. He watched Imperial soldiers indulging in local Solitude Full Breakfasts, deeming them the perfect remedy for a morning hangover.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:25</sup>

When the three months drew to a close, Balagog sent him forth with a challenge: to travel, to learn, and to discover his own path. Thus Urzhag's four-year journey across Tamriel began.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup>

### Journeys
Urzhag's adventures took him across Tamriel, from Northpoint to Sunhold, from Senchal to Vvardenfell and the Imperial City. Along the way, he developed a passion for researching and recreating historic dishes, uncovering the unexpected origins of meals such as Gold Coast Mudcrab Fries and the layered wartime legacy hidden within a Colovian War Torte. These explorations ultimately inspired him to write a book of his own, all the while imagining the pride Balagog might feel when it was finally placed in his hands.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:1-7</sup> Early in his travels, Urzhag learned to value the comfort of a well-made appetizer, often welcomed to modest but generous tables as he moved from village to village.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:41-44</sup>

After completing his apprenticeship under Balagog, Urzhag made his way to Riften with plans to cross into Cyrodiil and properly begin his travels. But when morning came, anticipation gave way to unease. Exhausted and unsettled, he drifted through the streets, consumed by doubt, and failed to sense danger until he was suddenly attacked and captured. He regained consciousness in a frigid cell, hunger twisting in his stomach. From the muffled conversation beyond the door, he gathered that his captors believed him to be the Gourmet, having heard him speak the name and discovered his cookbooks and frying pan. When the sack was pulled from his head, three Nords regarded him uncertainly. Thinking quickly, Urzhag rasped that they had the wrong man, subtly encouraging their skepticism that an Orc could be a master of refined Nordic fare. Their disagreement ended with his release, and he was thrust back onto the streets. Though rattled by the ordeal, Urzhag returned to the inn and gathered himself. The next morning, he made certain to prepare a hearty breakfast before finally stepping beyond Skyrim's borders, resolved and properly fortified for the road ahead.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:9-11</sup>

Urzhag sought the wandering city of Falinesti in Valenwood but was unable to reach its current location; nevertheless, he learned the recipe for the Falinesti Forbidden Fruit, a rare plant-based Bosmeri dish.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:47</sup> As he was leaving Valenwood, Urzhag passed through Arenthia and stopped at a small tavern called the Empty Tankard, run by a Khajiit family. There he learned of a local frittata that had once been unpopular among the Bosmer, who found it overly sweet. Over time, however, the Khajiiti proprietors adapted the recipe, adjusting its flavors to better suit Bosmeri tastes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:13</sup>

By the time Urzhag reached Daggerfall, he had grown wiser in the ways of travel and secured passage aboard a merchant vessel trading along the Iliac Bay. The voyage proved captivating, but nothing impressed him more than the sight of the Adamantine Tower, whose origins and long history stirred his curiosity. In exchange for preparing hearty plates of Bubble and Squeak, he persuaded the sailors to share the legends and lore they knew of the ancient spire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:15</sup>

While journeying along the Bitter Coast, Urzhag met an Argonian who believed to be the reincarnation of the ancient chef Chow-Chow, and shared with him the recipe for the legendary fungus omelets.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:17</sup> Still in Morrowind, he journeyed with a caravan for safety, riding past the ash-filled skies of Red Mountain. He often traveled with an older Dunmer, Synali, who shared stories of her youth and of how kwama eggs reminded her of her lost home. The next morning, Urzhag located a kwama nest, and Synali's joyful reaction became one of his fondest memories of the road.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:19</sup>

Urzhag recorded information about the Old Aldmeri Orphan Gruel, a traditional elven porridge once served at New Life Festivals, noting its role in feeding orphans and soldiers alike amid rising tensions, and preserving it as a hope for future peace.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:21</sup> He also documented Cyrodiilic pear sweetcakes, which gained popularity after 4E 174 when the White-Gold Concordat was signed, as the pears were specially ripened to white and gold colores to celebrate the treaty.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:23</sup>

During his travels, Urzhag spent the 26th of First Seed in Rihad for the Festival of Blades. During the celebrations, he met a young boy who couldn't afford the festival delicacies, so Urzhag prepared Toad Muffins for him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:29</sup> While travelling through the Alik'r Desert of Hammerfell, Urzhag survived a bitterly cold night thanks to the aid of a goat herder named Rissan. The two formed a lasting friendship and remained pen pals thereafter, and the cherished recipe Rissan shared with him eventually found its way into Urzhag's book.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:45</sup>

Urzhag often spent the end of the year and the beginning of the new one on the road. On one such occassion, he encountered a group of travelers from Elsweyr who welcomed him into their spontaneous New Life Festival celebrations. He prepared Khazgur-style Stonetooth Bash Chicken for them, and in return, they attempted to teach him the traditional coin-nicking techniques of their Trial of Five-Clawed Guile. At the next morning, the Khajiit offered him a breakfast pastry paying homage to Rajhin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:23</sup>

In Skingrad, on a certain Loredas he entered a luxury cuisine competition whose prize was a set of ornate kitchen tools. Though many competitors were wealthy and highly trained, Urzhag practiced diligently and presented a dish taught to him by Emeloria, an elderly innkeeper. He won, but chose to leave the extravagant tools with her, knowing skill mattered more than finery. She sold some to repair her inn and put the rest to good use in her kitchen.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:41-44</sup> During his stay in the Gold Coast, Urzhag learned from a wealthy man in Anvil how the Great Anguish of the Third Era had the unexpected effect of sparking a boom in seafood. After discovering Urzhag's connection to the Gourmet, the man invited him to dine with his family at an elegant dinner.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:49</sup>

### Balagog's Demise
Four years later, in 4E 201, Urzhag made arrangements to reunite with Balagog in the Pale, only to learn that his mentor had died shortly before reaching the destination. The news left him hollow. He returned to his family home in Mor Khazgur in grief, withdrawing from the world, until he slowly noticed that the plain gruel left by his bedside had begun to taste surprisingly good. The meals were the work of his sister, a battle-hardened warrior who had never shown interest in cooking, quietly experimenting with his recipes. Night after night, they worked side by side in the kitchen, this time with Urzhag as the teacher and his passion gradually returned. In time, he came to understand that his journey had never been solely for Balagog's approval, but to carry forward the stories and inspiration his mentor had kindled in him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:37</sup>

When Urzhag brought a nearly finished copy of his book to Balagog's memorial, he intended to cast it onto the pyre as a final offering. In that moment, the meaning behind Balagog's words about the high-quality kindling finally became clear. The years spent writing and wandering had given him far more than culinary mastery. He had found discipline beyond fine tools, treated history as a foundation rather than an escape, forged enduring friendships, faced hardship, and returned home to earn his father's quiet pride. Kindling was not an end, but the promise of a greater flame. His work was fuel, not ashes, his journey was not yet over.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:201</sup>

When his father asked whether he would remain in Mor Khazgur, Urzhag knew he could not, at least not yet. There were still distant shores to see, promises to keep, and lessons waiting to be learned. He intended to rest briefly in the stronghold before setting out once more, perhaps to write another volume, to study the fungi of Black Marsh, or to seek the long-lost temple of Y'ffelon. He also intended to return to Turnpoint in the autumn, arrange a desert-sampling tour along the coast of Elsweyr, and perhaps seek further knowledge from the Sapiarchs.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-1)</sup><sup>:201</sup>
