Numara was the Queen of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), the northern kingdom of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). She was a [Moon-Singer](Lore_Moon-Singers.md). She was an owner of the amulet of [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](Lore_Anequina_Sharp-Tongue.md), which served as the key of the Moon-Gate, and Numara was possibly her direct descendant.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Khamira-1)</sup> During her queenship, the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) and [Varen's Rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md) took place.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOSilhu-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFC-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/LG-cardart-Frostfall_Coup.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Frostfall_Coup.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Frostfall_Coup.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Frostfall_Coup.jpg) Frostfall Coup; Euraxia Tharn standing over the corpses of Numara and Hemakar At some point before [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), she became the Queen of Anequina after marrying [King Hemakar](Lore_Hemakar.md). Before this, she was a Moon-Singer, a [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) lore keeper and historian.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Khamira-1)</sup> She was also a skilled archer.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-QN-4)</sup> In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), she gave birth to her daughter and Hemakar's heir, [Khamira](Lore_Khamira.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Khamira-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LGR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-EC-6)</sup>

Numara and the royal family were murdered by the soldiers of the Imperial envoy [Euraxia Tharn](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md), specifically the Imperial assassin Saulinia,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Zamarak-7)</sup> in the [Frostfall Coup](Lore_Frostfall_Coup.md) of [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576). Euraxia seized the throne thereafter.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFC-3)</sup> Numara's daughter Khamira, however, managed to escape. Before that happened, Numara entrusted her daughter with Anequina's amulet.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Khamira-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Zamarak-7)</sup>

She and her husband were mummified and entombed in the prepared royal crypt beneath Rimmen Palace.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Zamarak-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-2Q-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-KHG-9)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Euraxia's necromancers reanimated Numara and Hemakar in an attempt to defend Rimmen Palace against Khamira and the Northern Elsweyr Defense Force. During the assault on Rimmen Palace, Khamira and the Northern Elsweyr Defense Force rekilled Numara and Hemakar and overthrew Euraxia.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-2Q-8)</sup>
