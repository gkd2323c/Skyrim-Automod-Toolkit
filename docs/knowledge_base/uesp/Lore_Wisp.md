# Wisp

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/SR-creature-Wisp.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Wisp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Wisp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Wisp.jpg) Wisp [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/SR-wallpaper-steam-Wispmother.jpg/200px-SR-wallpaper-steam-Wispmother.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-wallpaper-steam-Wispmother.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-wallpaper-steam-Wispmother.jpg) Wispmother and wisps Wisps, also known as **Witchlights**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> are enigmatic, floating entities often found in the vicinity of [Wispmothers](#Wispmother), although they can also exist independently.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup> Current consensus on wisps is divided, with it being suggested they are scavengers that lure prey to the Wispmother and share in the psychoetherial energy released by her kills. Another theory states that they are merely emanations or conjurations of the Wispmother and not free-living creatures, which is supported by the fact that killing a Wispmother also kills the wisps surrounding her, leaving only [glow dust](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glow_Dust) behind.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TW-3)</sup>

[Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) is known to send wisps to herald the storms of Rain's Hand. To see one is to see the promise of new growth, new life, and a new chapter in nature's cycle.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Galen_Wisp-4)</sup> The followers of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) consider Wisps to be gifts from Y'ffre, signaling periods of change, and believe they carry fortunes and omens of what is yet to come. Some Bosmer were known to commune with the Wisps to conduct fortune readings.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Phythia-5)</sup>

Powdered remains of wisps are a key component in the construction of [stonewisps](Lore_Stonewisp.md), the constructs created by the clergy of [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SOTL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PWR-7)</sup>

## Wispmothers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/SR-misc-Mothers Day_Art22.jpg/200px-SR-misc-Mothers Day_Art22.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Mothers Day_Art22.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Mothers Day_Art22.jpg) Wispmother **Wispmothers** (sometimes **wispfolk** or **wispfathers**)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> are magical-energy beings thought to be either [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md) or [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGT-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TW-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASpell-10)</sup> and they can be found in the wilds of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Wispmothers are capable of speech and are known to refer to wisps as their children.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Killing a wispmother causes all of its attendant wisps and shades to die. Legends in Black Marsh state that wispmothers cannot be truly destroyed, and that their essence must be sealed, or else they will rise again eventually.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> In [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) they are known as the **Amronal**,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASpell-10)</sup> though they are much rarer than the wispmothers of Skyrim.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TW-3)</sup>

Wispmothers can use illusionary magic to alter their appearance to appear like mer.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> They are able to teleport and restore their health. Wispmothers can also summon multiple shades, which are weak clones of themselves. More powerful wispmothers are also capable of some form of necromancy, where they send out wisps to possess and reanimate nearby corpses.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup>

Many legends and theories exist which seek to explain the true nature of wispmothers. Nords variously believe them to be [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md) of the ancient [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) (as supported by their tapered ears) or natives of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TW-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASpell-10)</sup> Master Sadren Sarethi proposed that they are a forgotten type of [lich](Lore_Lich.md) from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Lydette Viliane of the [Synod](Lore_Synod.md) proposed that they are elemental personifications of snow or mist.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TW-3)</sup>

The spirits of some dead mortals can take on the form of Wispmothers.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONLost-15)</sup> At least one wind spirit also manifested in a similar form.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TFOT-16)</sup>

Wisp wrappings and glow dust, both used in alchemy, can be gathered from vanquished wispmothers.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Additionally they are capable of leaving behind physical remains, such as their heads.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
