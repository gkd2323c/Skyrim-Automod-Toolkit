[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/OB-place-Colovian_Highlands.jpg/200px-OB-place-Colovian_Highlands.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Colovian_Highlands.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Colovian_Highlands.jpg) Colovian Highlands [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/OB-map-Colovian_Highlands.jpg/200px-OB-map-Colovian_Highlands.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Colovian_Highlands.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Colovian_Highlands.jpg) Map of the Colovian Highlands The Colovian Highlands are a hilly, forested region on the northwest outskirts of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The region is known for its ancient forest,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTR-2)</sup> and prominent [wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wine) country, specifically for its famously robust red blends.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WOB-3)</sup> The Colovian Highlands are a far-off region from the centers of [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) civilization but some people, such as the well-known Imperial historian Dubicius call the region their home.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CACIII-4)</sup>

The Colovian Highlands runs along the northwest provincial border, between the southern-innermost banks of the [Brena River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brena_River), the western [Imperial Reserve](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Reserve), and the westernmost [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). Colovian cities such as [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) and [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) lie on the edge of their respective regions before they lead into the highlands, but their county districts extend out there. The [Black Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Road), one of Cyrodiil's major colored thoroughfares runs through the Colovian Highlands before it disappears into the mountains.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBMap Colovian-5)</sup>

## Society and Geography
The Snow-Blanket Sorrel [Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse) is native to the Colovian Highlands, as well as a common sight between Chorrol and [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md). It is known for its intelligence and high spirits.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSnow-6)</sup> The Highland Spotted [Lynx](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lynx) is a species native to the region. They were previously uncommon, but an opportunistic hunter brought cubs with him and they have since become premium vermin killers in the area.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONLynx Bundle-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONLynx-8)</sup> The brown Highland Wolf is a common creature found between here and the Great Forest. Local farmers and shepherds fear their wild packs, but some foresters have domesticated them and turned them into mounts.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONHighland-9)</sup>

A Colovian Highland style of [ale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ale) is strong and sweet, with just a taste of [juniper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Juniper). By the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), it was popular all over Colovia but it was seldom seen in [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIC-10)</sup><sup>:Part 2, Chapter 7</sup>

## History
The Colovian Highlands are riddled with rocky trails, caves, and woodlands with scant examples of civilization. The more south you travel, the more ruins, specifically [Ayleid ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins) can be found. The exact history of these ruins before abandonment is unknown, but the clans of the former cities, [Talwinque](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talwinque) and [Varondo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varondo) were able to escape the [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) and found refuge among the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) during the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ASIV-11)</sup>

Some of the forts date back to the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when Colovia was ruled by kingdoms such as Kvatch. In one of Colovia's many incursions into [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March), the Hastrel, [Linchal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Linchal), and Ontus cohorts led by the famed General Thibaut of Kvatch traveled south into the region. The details of Thibaut's death are unknown but while the Linchal and Ontus cohorts returned north, the Hastrel cohort stayed and built the modern-day township, [Greenhill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenhill).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TCAIH-12)</sup>

After his successful ambush against Emperor [Gorieus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorieus) in a low-barring pass, King [Rislav Larich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rislav_Larich) of Skingrad and his Colovian forces fled north through the West Weald and eventually, the Colovian Highlands with the [Imperial Army](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Army) following suit. In the highland forest, Gorieus and his exhausted army was confronted by King Justinius of Kvatch, Rislav's father-in-law, and were defeated within an hour. Disheveled, the Imperial Army fled north into the Imperial Reserve and eventually back into the Nibenay.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTR-2)</sup>

When the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) broke out and sundered central Cyrodiil, the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) advanced onto the region through the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) and the Colovian Highlands. The Covenant built their southern gate stronghold on the edge of the Colovian Highlands west of Lipsand Tarn and Fort Rayles,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONSouth Gate-13)</sup> as well as a temple to house the [Elder Scroll of Alma Ruma](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONYseline-14)</sup> Central Cyrodiil was split three ways between the alliances with the northwest taken over by the Daggerfall Covenant. With that being said, only a fraction of the Colovian Highlands fell inside the border, only around [Hrotanda Vale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrotanda_Vale) and the aforementioned Lipsand Tarn.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONTerritory-15)</sup> At the time there was a rumor of plague in the highlands.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFirilanya-16)</sup>

Before the [Battle of the Red Ring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Red_Ring.md) in the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), General Decianus and his [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) from [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) were amassed in the Colovian Highlands, near Chorrol while Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) and General Jonna's armies were gathered. On the 30th of [Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rain%27s_Hand), General Decianus descended onto the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) from the west, taking Lord [Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md) by surprise while Jonna's forces were counterattacked from the south by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). In the end, the Imperial Army was able to re-capture the Imperial City and seize the war from the Dominion.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TGW-17)</sup>

## Notable Locations
[Battlehorn Castle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battlehorn_Castle) The last stronghold of the [Knights of the True Horn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_True_Horn), held by the order until their demise in the twilight years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). [Cloud Top](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Top) A mountain summit in the Colovian Highlands. [Fort Linchal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Linchal) A former Imperial outpost and a historical location established back when Kvatch was a kingdom. [Lipsand Tarn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lipsand_Tarn) Known as the Elfs Haunts to the locals of nearby Chorrol, it is a ruin built into the hills that people tend to avoid. Shrine of [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine) The province's shrine to the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of Debauchery and Revelry. [Talwinque](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talwinque) A large Ayleid ruin northeast of Kvatch, whose clan managed to avoid the Alessian Slave Rebellion and flee south into [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).
