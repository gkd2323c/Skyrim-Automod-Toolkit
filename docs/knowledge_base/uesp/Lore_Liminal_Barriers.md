# Liminal Barriers


## Invasions
Numerous Daedric invasions have occurred over the course of [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history, due to the pact between [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and mortals being temporarily broken. These include:

### The Planemeld
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg) Dark Anchors surrounding the Imperial City The [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) was a monumental [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) invasion of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) that took place sometime between [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) and [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Planemeld)</sup> during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). The [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) used [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md), huge Daedric interplanar machines of his own design, in an attempt to drag [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), which had been "set adrift" and become "unmoored from the fabric of the [multiverse](Lore_Aurbis.md)" as a result of the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md), out of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and into his realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). Rifts of darkness that lead from Tamriel directly to Coldharbour appeared across the continent. Each death in Tamriel weakened the barriers between Nirn and the [realms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) beyond, and the concurrent [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) only accelerated the Planemeld. The scale of the Planemeld is comparable to that of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). The Planemeld was ultimately stopped by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) with [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia)'s assistance.

### The Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/OB-creature-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg) Mehrunes Dagon on Nirn during the Oblivion Crisis The [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), also known as the **Great Anguish**, was a total war between [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) hordes and the population of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It began with the assassination of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and all of his known heirs in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) at the hands of a fanatical cult of worshippers known as the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md).

Shortly afterward, Oblivion Gates to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) began opening, unleashing Daedra across Tamriel. With each Gate that opened, the [barriers](Lore_Dragonfires.md) between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [Firmament](Lore_Astronomy.md), would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined. Widespread devastation and casualties resulted across entire provinces. The Daedra besieged [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and laid waste to the [Old Holds](Lore_Hold.md). In [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) called back many [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to fight off the Daedra. According to the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), the Daedra were forced to close the Gates in Black Marsh due to an Argonian counter invasion. In [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the city of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) was entirely destroyed.

### Martin's Sacrifice
The Oblivion Crisis was ended when [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) shattered the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) and turned into the Avatar of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), defeating Dagon in a final battle in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md)'s [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One). This sealed the liminal barriers permanently, preventing any future invasion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBMQ-7)</sup> The new barrier was noted to be too powerful for even a [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md), an object in the presence of which it was said even "a thousand [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) would likely dissolve", to breach for more than fleeting moments.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBLight-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Excavation-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBMartin-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-11)</sup>

Martin's sacrifice did not prevent portals to Oblivion from being opened, provided they posed no danger to Mundus. This was evidenced when [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) opened a portal in [Niben Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_Bay) that same year, to serve as an invitation rather than an invasion.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) cultist [Vonos](Lore_Vonos.md) devised a profane ritual which would shatter the liminal barrier and bypass Martin's sacrifice. Vonos managed to open a permanent Oblivion Gate in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) by tricking the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) into killing him while holding a [Great Welkynd Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Welkynd_Stone). The Dragonborn thereby inadvertently participated in the ritual, with Vonos' death serving as a mortal sacrifice. By destroying the Gate's [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) in advance, Vonos ensured that the portal could not be closed. Despite this, the planned Daedric invasion was foiled when the Dragonborn ventured into the Gate and slew Dagon's generals, [Valkyn Methats](Lore_Methats.md) and Valkyn Gatanas. The [Vigil of Stendarr](Lore_Vigil_of_Stendarr.md) subsequently seized control of the Gate from the remaining cultists.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TC-13)</sup>

## Invitations
Although the liminal barriers prevent Daedric invasions, they do not altogether prevent Daedric presence on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). This is because mortals may invite Daedra into the Mundus by summoning them or opening portals.

- Despite the strength of the liminal barriers, practitioners of [Conjuration magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) regularly crack open the doors of Oblivion to summon lesser Daedra to do their bidding. However, summoning greater Daedra runs the risk of making the crack impossibly difficult to shut.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-14)</sup>

- [Oblivion Gates](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) are portals opened between Nirn and Oblivion using [Sigil Stones](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md). These are gates that connect to Daedric realms based on the realm of origin of the Sigil Stone, and may last for minutes at a time.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LB-6)</sup>

- Additionally, while Daedric invasions are prevented by the liminal barriers, this does not mean the Daedra can't be summoned en masse by mortals. One such attempted instance is the Culling by [Lord Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SD-15)</sup>

- In the year [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#_1E_2920), both [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) were directly summoned to Nirn to destroy cities. Molag Bal destroyed the town of [Gil-Var-Delle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gil-Var-Delle) upon request of the then mad King of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), Dro'Zel. Similarly, Mehrunes Dagon destroyed [Old Mournhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Mournhold) upon the request of [Turala](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Turala), a [witch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witch) of the [Skeffington Coven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skeffington_Coven).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LYof FE-16)</sup> These Princely summonings were banned by the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), a deal between multiple [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) and the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TCC-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-CWCSotha Dialogue-18)</sup>

## Notes
- The [Moonlight Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonlight_Blade) had the ability to cut through the liminal barriers.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONShando-ri-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONVastarie-20)</sup>
- According to [Demiprince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demiprince) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen), [Slipstream Realms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Slipstream_Realm) are transitional realms between Mundus and Oblivion where "transliminal forces balance in standing waves", with the [Battlespire Academy](Lore_Battlespire.md) being within one of these realms.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Fa Nuit Hen-21)</sup>
- Some sources also speak of [the aurora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aurora) or "Kyne's lights", and the [Lunar Lattice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons), also serving as boundaries that protect [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) from harmful influence from beyond.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Celestial-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-23)</sup> Some sources describe the Lunar Lattice as burning spirits that touch it with moonlight fire, causing even [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to be set aflame and fall into the darkness below when pressed against it. It is said by those sources that no spirit had ever managed to damage it until [Merrunz](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), released to serve as a weapon against the Lattice, cracked it with his axe.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Boethra-24)</sup>
- Those [Daedric Princes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) that were signatories of the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md) also had certain restrictions placed upon the interactions of their [realms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) with Nirn by the agreement.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TCC-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-CWCSotha Dialogue-18)</sup> The Compact seemingly placed certain restrictions on how mortals could interact with the realms of those Princes as well.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SOi A-25)</sup>
