#### Gabrielle Benele (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md) was a prominent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [author](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Gabrielle_Benele) and [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) member of first rank active during the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). Before her tenure with the Mages Guild, she was part of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md), which she left and later rejoined through the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md), the University's most prestigious historical society, whose members scour the continent for lost antiquities to collect and catalogue. There she became the most accomplished antiquarian and their leader. She participated in the [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) Invasion of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), where she played a major role in aiding in the defeat of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and ending the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). She subsequently became the acting guildmaster for the Mages Guild in [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md)'s absence, and worked with the Stirk Fellowship to take down the remnants of the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md). Gabrielle had a sister named [Sara](Lore_Sara_Benele.md), who also accomplished notable feats of her own. Sara played a key role in preventing [the Mage's Staff](Lore_The_Mage%27s_Staff.md) in the [Spellscar](Lore_Spellscar.md) from causing a catastrophe. Like Gabrielle, Sara was a member of the Mages Guild, though the two sisters were quite different in many ways.

#### Blademaster Gaiden Shinji (1E? b - 970)
[Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) hero of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the leader of the [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md), who was killed during the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md). He also founded the [Arena](Lore_Arena_District.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), and was its original Blademaster. He is best known for his famous proverbs such as "the best techniques are passed on by the survivors" or "honor wears the face of your next opponent". The combatants of the Arena refer to him in a manner most would reserve for gods, often saying things like "by Shinji", "Shinji be with us", and "for Shinji's sake". He is believed to have taken influence from [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) culture, in addition to his own.

#### Gallus Desidenius (?b - [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176))
[Gallus Desidenius](Lore_Gallus_Desidenius.md) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) thief who became Guild Master of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He purportedly maintained strong ties with many influential families in Skyrim, which allowed the Guild to operate relatively safely. He was also a member of the [Nightingale Trinity](Lore_Nightingales.md), along with [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), with whom he had a romantic relationship, and [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md). The three of them swore an oath to [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) to protect the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md), which holds the [Ebonmere](Lore_Ebonmere.md) portal open at the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md). Gallus was the author of *[The Nightingales](Lore_The_Nightingales.md)*.

Over time, Gallus became suspicious of Mercer's behavior. Mercer, in turn, became aware of Gallus' scrutiny. Though he was unable to find hard proof, Gallus became convinced that Mercer was responsible for desecrating the Twilight Sepulcher and stealing the Skeleton Key. Before he could dig any further, Mercer arranged a meeting, where he betrayed and murdered Gallus. Gallus' spirit became the last of the Nightingale Sentinels and defended the Sepulcher alone for about 25 years. For more information, see the [lore article](Lore_Gallus_Desidenius.md).

#### Galyn the Shelterer (fl. [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Galyn the Shelterer](Lore_Galyn.md) was a Dunmer [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) within the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). At some point in time Galyn became the leader of the Clockwork Apostles of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). Galyn and the Apostles were aware of the death of their god at the hands of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Despite Sotha Sil's death, Galyn and the Apostles continued to inhabit the city, which continued to function and in fact thrive thanks to Sotha Sil's [Mechanical Heart](Lore_Mechanical_Heart.md) contingency.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Galyn and his Apostles would face adversity when a former Apostle and apprentice of Sotha Sil [Mecinar](Lore_Mecinar.md) returned to the Clockwork City. Mecinar was previously banished from the city by Sotha Sil for profane experiments Mecinar had committed. Mecinar used [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md) to access the realm. Pursuing Mecinar into the realm was the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) who intended to stop Mecinar's nefarious plans to overtake the Clockwork City.

Galyn and the Apostles realizing the common enemy they shared with the Forgotten Hero would help the hero in their quest to take down Mecinar. The Forgotten Hero would ultimately defeat Mecinar although it's unclear what the fate of the Clockwork City was following these events.

#### Gauldur (fl. early 1E)
[Archmage Gauldur](Lore_Gauldur.md) was a powerful and renowned [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [conjurer](Lore_Conjuration.md) who lived in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the "dawning days" of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). His aid and counsel were sought by many. A major source of his power was his [magical amulet](Lore_Gauldur_Amulet.md).

Though Gauldur was wise, wealthy, and honorable, his sons Jyrik, Mikrul, and Sigdis Gauldurson resented living in their father's shadow. They killed Gauldur in his sleep and split his amulet between them. Consumed by power, the three brothers laid waste to the surrounding villages until High King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) sent Archmage [Geirmund](Lore_Geirmund.md) and a company of battlemages to stop them, which they finally did after a great effort and many casualties. Because of the destruction caused by Gauldur's family, King Harald ordered the Gauldur name struck from all historical records. But despite this age-long campaign to wipe out all mention of Gauldur, whispers of the legend survived, although bards still refused to tell the tale four thousand years later.For more information, see the [lore article](Lore_Gauldur.md).

#### Archmage Geirmund (fl. early 1E)
[Geirmund](Lore_Geirmund.md) was an Archmage of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and personal battlemage to [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md). He does not appear in the historical record until the reign of High King Harald in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when Jyrik, Mikrul, and Sigdis Gauldurson killed their father, Archmage [Gauldur](Lore_Gauldur.md), stole his powerful [amulet](Lore_Gauldur_Amulet.md), and began to terrorize [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) villages throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The Gauldursons caused so much havoc across Skyrim that Harald sent a company of his best warriors and battlemages led by Geirmund to stop them. Geirmund and his men fought the Gauldursons in a devastating battle that ended with the brothers fleeing the field. A chase ensued, from the wilds of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the glacial north. Geirmund eventually tracked down and killed Mikrul in [Folgunthur](Lore_Folgunthur.md), near [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), and Jyrik in [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), sealing them in those ruins. Geirmund's forces suffered great losses in each encounter, but finally, only the middle child, Sigdis, was left standing. When he could flee no more, Sigdis challenged Geirmund to a duel in the fields near [Ivarstead](Lore_Ivarstead.md), a challenge that Geirmund was honor-bound to accept. Geirmund and Sigdis both died in the duel, bringing an end to the Gauldursons' rampage.

To commemorate his courage, Harald ordered a tomb built for Geirmund. Harald also had Sigdis interred there, so Geirmund could forever guard him. The tomb was built on an island in [Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md), which takes its name from the fallen Archmage. Harald then began an age-long campaign to erase all knowledge of the Gauldur tale so that his powerful amulet would not be reforged and used to threaten Skyrim once more. Harald's campaign did not fully succeed, as in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), bards still sang about Geirmund's sacrifice, and there was even a festival to his memory, regularly held at the site of his tomb. In time, however, the story became gradually forgotten, and by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), it was known as the obscure "Forbidden Legend" and few sources mentioned it. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) learned of the legend and disturbed Geirmund's tomb to retrieve Sigdis' portion of Gauldur's amulet.

#### Crown Prince Geldall Septim ([3E 377](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_377) - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png/160px-RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png) [Red Diamond heraldry](Lore_Red_Diamond.md), badge of the Septim Dynasty [Geldall Septim](Lore_Geldall_Septim.md) was the eldest son of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and [Caula Voria](Lore_Caula_Voria.md) and crown prince of the Empire. An able heir to his long-ruling father, he had taken over many administrative responsibilities in the years leading up to the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). Following the Restoration in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), rumors circulated that Geldall and his two brothers were actually [doppelgangers](Lore_Doppelganger.md) placed in the household during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), leading to riots in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). For decades, Geldall and his brothers had not taken wives, which is attributed to fear of showing favoritism to the province their spouse would be from, thereby alienating the others, and marrying someone from Cyrodiil would be a missed opportunity for a good alliance. He eventually became engaged to a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) woman named Tamrialle, and they held an engagement ball with many royal guests in attendance, including Queen [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md). It would turn out that Tamrialle was secretly a [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) agent, and Geldall was assassinated by her at the age of 56, in a coordinated strike against the Emperor's family.

#### Knight-Paladin Gelebor (?b - ?d)
One of the last known [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) to exist. He and about a hundred other Snow Elves took refuge from the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) and [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) in the massive yet well-hidden [Chantry of Auri-El](Lore_Chantry_of_Auri-El.md), but time and Falmer attacks dwindled their numbers to only two, Gelebor and his brother [Vyrthur](Lore_Vyrthur.md). Gelebor believed his brother had been taken and corrupted by the Falmer. However, Vyrthur had actually contracted [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md) from an initiate, and had started a plot to cause [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) as much suffering as he could. Gelebor's duty as Knight-Paladin was to guard the wayshrine at the Chantry of Auri-El in the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md), and he stayed there for millennia. When the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) and [Serana](Lore_Serana.md) went to the Vale in search of [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), Gelebor agreed to help, but first demanded that they kill Vyrthur. After the deed was done, he helped them with the bow and stayed in the Inner Sanctum of the Chantry, determined to continue his service to Auri-El for the rest of his days.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-4)</sup>

#### High King Gellir (fl. early 1E)
[Gellir](Lore_Gellir.md) was a High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Little is known about him or his reign, except that he ruled at least several decades after [King Harald](Lore_Harald.md), whose reign ended in [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221). He led the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) to conquer several [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city-states, including [Mzulft](Lore_Mzulft.md), though the Dwemer would retake them about a century later.

Nordic chroniclers have ascribed his surprising success to inspired tactics and the blessings of [Shor](Lore_Shor.md). Others assert the Dwemer were in a weakened state due to an ongoing civil war, which paved the way for Gellir's subsequent conquests.

#### Patriarch Gherig Bullblood (?b - 2E 582)
**Patriarch** [Gherig Bullblood](Lore_Gherig_Bullblood.md) **of the Dreadhorn Clan** was a [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) [Blood-Forged](Lore_Blood-Forged.md) clan leader active in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). He made an alliance with [Domihaus the Bloody-Horned](Lore_Domihaus.md) and led the [Dreadhorn](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) to an ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) site—the [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md). One of his ambitions was to conquer the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) with the power granted to his clan by the [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) and [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) and armor forged in the ancient forge. He was also a knowledgeable [smith](Lore_Smithing.md) and armorer, and penned instructions for his clansmen for crafting their arms and armor.

#### Master Gilvas Barelo (fl. 3E 427)
Master [Gilvas Barelo](Lore_Gilvas_Barelo.md), Abbot of [Holamayan](Lore_Holamayan.md), was the head of the secret order of [Dissident Priests](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). In other words, during the fruition of the [Nerevarine Prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine.md), he was the most prominent skeptic of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md)'s explanation of how the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) gained their divinity and the events surrounding their rise to power.

The Nerevarine came to him to learn more about the crisis [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) was facing. Barelo helped the Nerevarine find and interpret the Lost Prophecies of the [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) in order to understand how to fulfill them and defeat [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md). Gilvas saw the return of Saint [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) as the last and best hope of ending the threat posed by Dagoth Ur, and he understood that helping the Nerevarine do this was the only way to end the persecution of the Dissident Priests and advance their beliefs. Following the defeat of Dagoth Ur, the Temple attempted to make amends with the Dissident Priests and re-integrate the estranged order. With the disappearance of the Tribunal and the events of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the Dissident Priests seized power in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) and formed the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md), although it is unknown what role Barelo played in these affairs.

#### Gjadil the Keel-Hauler
Gjadil the Keel-Hauler was an ancient Atmoran explorer who sailed from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) to [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). She was said to have discovered new lands and cultures, but at the cost of half of her crew falling mad along the way. Her greatest adventure was supposedly raiding the palace of King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) himself. According to legend, after she fled [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) with the treasure the whole [Maormer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Maormer) fleet chased her to a cove on Summerset Isle. She buried a single chest of treasure and enchanted it so that only her descendants could open it, before she made her way back home. Gjadil settled down and the story of her hidden treasure was passed along to her descendants.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> During the mid-Second Era one of her descendants, known as Igeke Rat-Bite, searched for the treasure and eventually found it. Instead of great riches the chest contained a scroll that spoke of family being the most precious treasure of all, much to Igeke's dismay.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONGL-7)</sup>

#### King Glinferen (fl. 1E 198 - 242)
[King Glinferen](Lore_Glinferen.md) was the last known monarch of [Atatar](Lore_Atatar.md), an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [city-state](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) in northern [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). Glinferen was a [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphile.md) and fought against the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) -worshipping [Barsaebics](Lore_Barsaebic.md) in what would become known as the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md). This conflict culminated in the Scouring of [Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md) in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198), when King Glinferen led a combined force of Daedraphile warriors against the traditionalist Barsaebics. His victory drove the Barsaebics out of the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) and into inhospitable [Argonia](Lore_Argonia.md), effectively ending organized Ayleid opposition to [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

In [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242) the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) began when [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) made Covenant with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to gain the favor of the Aedra. In desperation, King Glinferen sent an envoy to the Barsaebic city of [Twyllbek](Lore_Twyllbek.md) in Argonia seeking aid against the Alessians. The Barsaebic king, still stininging from the humiliation of exile, bluntly refused to help. These events ultimately led to the downfall of Ayleid civilization and the abandonment of Atatar.

#### Gogh (fl. 4E 201)
[Gogh](Lore_Gogh.md) is a [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) hunter and an apparent champion of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). He was part of a [Cyrodiilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) Goblin tribe that originally resided near [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), possibly the [Three Feather Tribe](Lore_Three_Feather_Tribe.md). Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), his tribe migrated out of Cyrodiil using subterranean tunnels beneath the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). They emerged into [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and took up occupation of a cave known as Gromm's Pass. At some point he obtained the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md), presumably a gift from Hircine himself, as those who tried to to take the spear from him seemed to be rejected by the weapon, finding it impossible to lift. Gogh was also fiercely protective of the weapon.

His tribe subsequently encountered a helpless [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) from the nearby stronghold of [Largashbur](Lore_Largashbur.md), who happened to be stained blue by the [Steel Blue Entoloma](Lore_Alchemy_S.md#Steel_Blue_Entoloma) mushrooms which grow in abundance within Gromm's Pass. Most of the tribe believed him to be the [Blue God](Lore_Blue_God.md), an aspect of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) worshipped by goblins, the tribe quickly made him their shaman and began to obey him as their god. However, the tunnel to Cyrodiil collapsed soon after, cutting the tribe off from any reinforcements. Gogh then spotted the Orcish shaman applying blue dye to maintain his appearance and was thus sentenced by the Orc to death in a gladiatorial pit, the rest of the tribe defended the Orc not knowing this. Gogh was able to successfully defend himself for a time, but the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who had heard rumors of Goblins in Skyrim, eventually saved Gogh from the pit by killing the Orc and his remaining goblin followers. Gogh, despite the language barrier, entered into the Dragonborn's service and continued to wield the Spear and its powers in battle.

#### King Golkarr (1E? b - ca. 1E 980)

#### Gratia Arquitius (fl. 2E)
[Gratia Arquitius](Lore_Gratia_Arquitius.md) was a legendary chef that lived during the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) known for her Colovian War Torte recipe, a robust yet lavish dessert designed to facilitate camaraderie among [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) during their war marches. Known ingredients include [nutmeg](Lore_Nutmeg.md), citrus, but flour, [carrots](Lore_Carrot.md), [frost mirriam](Lore_Frost_Mirriam.md), and [honey](Lore_Honey.md) have also been known to be used. She elevated the dessert into the Molten War Torte by adding Rubyblossom Extract, a rare and robust sweetener once cultivated by [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md) [alchemists](Lore_Alchemy.md). The final evolution to her confectionary creation was the White-Gold War Torte, achieved by adding rare Mourning Dew on a Molten War Torte.

Her torte recipe was misplaced by her in the wilds of Cyrodiil, and subsequently utilized by the alliances participating in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). The legacy of Gratia Arquitiu lasts much longer than the Interregnum of the Second Era. [Urzhag-gro Larak](Lore_Urzhag_gro-Larak.md), apprentice to the famous Gourmet, [Balagog gro-Nolob](Lore_Balagog_gro-Nolob.md), recounts in his book *Tastes and Tales from Tamriel* how sharing a Colovian War Torte helped connect him with two total strangers. Stuck in a cave in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) duiring a blizzard, Urzhag sharing his torte with the strangers led to them joking and conversing like they had known each other for years, when earlier they had been suspicious of each other.

#### The Gray Fox
The [Gray Fox](Lore_Gray_Fox.md), a mysterious master thief, had been the leader of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for three hundred years by the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). Although some doubt his existence, it was commonly believed that he was a single person, while in fact a series of different people had actually held the title throughout the years. The persona of the Gray Fox is created by the [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md), which was originally stolen from [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) by Emer Dareloth, the first Gray Fox. Nocturnal cursed the cowl to steal the identity of the wearer. Once a person dons the cowl, the curse transforms them into the Gray Fox and their original identity is lost. The cowl had been passed from one guildmaster to the next, preventing the guildmaster from appearing in public due to the infamous reputation of the Gray Fox.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Oblivion-13)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Hieronymus Lex, a Watch Captain of the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), began a personal vendetta to bring the Gray Fox to justice, although the Thieves Guild eventually conspired to put an end to his hunt. The current Gray Fox, previously Count Corvus Umbranox of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), had spent 11 years attempting to break Nocturnal's curse, and eventually succeeded. His best thief stole an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) from the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md), and used its power to rewrite history. Without the curse, the Thieves Guild had flourished, and the Cowl's power to hide one's identity could be fully manipulated. The persona of the Gray Fox continued to lead the guild, with the Cowl being passed down from the redeemed Count Umbranox to the master thief who aided him.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Oblivion-13)</sup>

As a member of [Ysgramor's](Lore_Ysgramor.md) [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Companions.md#Five_Hundred_Companions), he took part in [the Return](Lore_Merethic_Era_Wars.md#The_Return), driving the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). He searched for the hidden path to [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) afterlife, betraying his fellow Companions in an attempt to create one for himself.

#### Grjotgaror (?b - [3E 366](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_366))
[Grjotgaror](Lore_Grjotgaror.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) in the fourth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

He became Chieftain of Thirsk after slaying the white witch Katla and presenting her staff as a battle trophy. He reigned as chieftain for two years before being killed by [Amelie Bontecou](Lore_Amelie_Bontecou.md) who would take his place as Chieftain.

A famous Orcish champion who lived in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) before [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was founded, traveling to secure territory for the ostracized Orcish people to live.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16AOM12-14)</sup>

#### Grosta (fl. Merethic Era)
[Grosta](Lore_Grosta.md) was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior and a member of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Along with her sister [Froa](Lore_Froa.md), they were known as the **great Shield-Sisters**. They were credited with the creation of the [Woeful Bowstring](Lore_Woeful_Bowstring.md), an essential part of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s great bow, the [Long-Launcher](Lore_Long-Launcher.md).

#### Grundwulf (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Grundwulf](Lore_Grundwulf.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [tongue](Lore_Tongues.md) and member of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) of Emperor [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md). He was contemporary with the notorious [Chevalier Renald](Lore_Chevalier_Renald.md) and the two grew to become great friends. Early on In Grundwulf's recruitment into the Dragonguard, Renald approached the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) of [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md) as he wished for himself and those of his order to be taught the secrets of [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to help with their efforts but this was largely refused save one member, the newly recruited Grundwulf.

#### The Gryphon
[The Gryphon](Lore_The_Gryphon.md), also known more specifically as the **Luminary Gryphon**, was one of the [Luminaries of Magic](Lore_Luminary.md), mysterious immortal beings of pure [magic](Lore_Magic.md), spirits likened to beams of sunlight from [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) himself, his chosen form was that of a [Gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md). The Gryphon was considered part of the Scholarium's heart, he was a combative being described as having feathers like armor, talons that could cut stone, and a beak that could rend the sky, and viewed himself as the Scholarium's guardian. He played an important role in the life of [Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md), collaborating with her alongside the other Luminaries to develop the system of Scribing, a "metamagical theoryset" that was the precursor to the modern [Spellcrafting](Lore_Spellcrafting.md) system.

The Gryphon believed that it was not how one was born that defined them but what one chose to do. Though he considered the circumstances of his existence strange, he viewed his strength as a gift from Magnus and [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), and so chose to devote himself to living as a "bulwark against the hordes", someone who took action when he saw injustice against the weak, knowing his strength could make a difference. The Gryphon believed that differences from others do not weaken but rather make one stronger, and so viewed his companion, [The Fox](Lore_The_Fox.md), not only as a creature to whom he offered shelter beneath his wings, as he would to any other, but as a fiendishly clever partner who was a joy to spend time with. To the Gryphon, the fight for justice was never-ending and a reward in itself.

As with all of the original Luminaries, the Gryphon himself was not aware of his true origins, and had his own theories on his birth. The Gryphon believed himself to have been born as a result of the death of another, a great sage who stood against a terrible enemy and burned them out from within using a mighty and beautiful [spell](Lore_Spells.md) that cost them their life. He thought that the casting of the fatal spell required a lengthy ritual and a vast amount of magicka accumulated during that process, so great that, when the spell was finished, a great reservoir still remained to coalesce into the Gryphon himself. The Gryphon's thoughts on his possible origins helped give Ulfsild the inspiration to attempt to create a Luminary herself, using part her own [soul](Lore_Souls.md) as material, which eventually resulted in the creation of the [Crow](Lore_The_Crow.md).

The Gryphon was viewed as having had a complicated relationship with Ulfsild herself, though by his own admission they had also been great collaborators during the creation of Scribing. The Gryphon's beliefs were viewed with some skepticism by the other Luminaries, who viewed him as obstinate to a fault and pretentious in behaving as if he was the only one with a moral compass. They perceived the Gryphon's conviction that he was a shield for the others to hide behind as having gotten him into nothing but trouble, and likened him to a "grizzled old knight who has fallen off his horse one too many times". Of the other Luminaries, [The Netch](Lore_The_Netch.md) had the highest opinion of him, and viewed him as a heavy soul that had lost sight of what he wanted, but nonetheless meant well.

As with all Luminaries, The Gryphon was said to possess a mastery of magic that made the average archmage look like a neophyte. As a Luminary, he saw the world and magic in a way that was different from mortals, and so was not particularly fond of mages and would only lend them his power if his challenges were completed. As beings of pure magic the Luminaries were said to have understood magic in a way mortals never could and so, their power, freely given, allowed one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of. Indeed, Scribing is said to have been somehow destined to channel the power of the Luminaries from the start.

The Gryphon had a negative view of [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) and considered him an "old fool", he also had a dim view of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), who he derided as the "Daedric Prince of Nonsense". According to [The Netch](Lore_The_Netch.md), of the Luminaries, people were drawn to The Gryphon due to being of like mind.

#### Gudrun (fl. before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Gudrun](Lore_Gudrun.md) was a member of the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) who introduced the [martial art](Lore_Claw-Dances.md) called the Way of the Peaceful Fist into the practice of the Moth Priests.

#### Guthrag Stone-Talker (fl. Late 9th Century of the First Era - [1E 950](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_950))
[Guthrag Stone-Talker](Lore_Guthrag_Stone-Talker.md) was a renowned [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) diplomat. He gained fame for his formidable negotiation abilities and a magical mask that bore his name. Through his compelling oratory skills, he successfully postponed the siege of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) for over a decade.

#### Green Lady Gwaering (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Gwaering](Lore_Gwaering.md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) woman who served as the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was the niece of [Finoriell](Lore_Finoriell.md), the previous Green Lady. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), after the assassination of [Edhelorn](Lore_Edhelorn.md) —who was not only [the Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) but also the husband of Finoriell—and the subsequent death of Finoriell herself, Gwaering was chosen to become the new Green Lady.

#### Gwyna (?b - ?d)
[Gwyna](Lore_Gwyna.md) was a legendary figure from the oral tradition of the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). She was an eponymous heroine of *The Song of Gwyna*, an epic poem told by Reachfolk [Vateshrans](Lore_Vateshrans.md) throughout generations in numerous different forms. In some versions of the tale, she was a huntress of the [Horn-Stride clan](Lore_Horn-Stride_Clan.md), in other retellings, she fought against them and the 'king below the rock' who ruled from the Understone Keep in the City of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) as an ally and kindred and ally of the Chief [Rowolan](Lore_Rowolan.md).

#### Lord Gyrnasse (fl. early 2E)
Lord of [Sollicich-on-Ker](Lore_Sollicich-on-Ker.md), a town on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The future Archmagister [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) was born there as a poor serf, but was taught to read by his father, a forbidden act at the time. Upon being discovered, the book smugglers, including Galerion's father, were executed by Lord Gyrnasse and left to hang in the sun for weeks.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GTM-15)</sup>

#### Gyron Vardengroet (fl. early 2E)
[Gyron Vardengroet](Lore_Gyron_Vardengroet.md) is an immortal wizard of legend that was born in the early years of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and was prophesied by the [Gods](Lore_Gods.md) as a champion that would be sent down to guide others and bring wisdom. His exploits would earn him the title of **the Sage**, and **Great Sage**. Physically, he is described as a tall, bald, and bearded [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) with dark eyes, an indigo robe trimmed with gold, a pointed wizard's hat, and an intricately carved staff. He is the primary figure in the books *[The Sage](Lore_The_Sage.md)*, *[Feyfolken](Lore_Feyfolken.md)*, and *[The Final Lesson](Lore_The_Final_Lesson.md)*. Gyron has monuments erected in his reverence in both [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). He was listed alongside [Voernet](Lore_Voernet.md) and [Druid](Lore_Druid.md) [King](Lore_Druid_King.md) [Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md) as one of the most powerful Breton mages in history.
