*Av molag anyammis, av latta magicka. - From fire, life; from light, magic.*
—Ayleid phrase<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup> The Ayleids, also known as the **Heartland High Elves**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAOK-2)</sup> **Wild Elves**, or **Saliache**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOot O-3)</sup> were the first race to establish an empire in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWE-4)</sup> and had ruled modern-day [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for countless years dating back to before recorded history.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-5)</sup> They were thin and lean with pointed ears and angular facial features. Their complexion was lighter than that of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), but they were darker than [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md);<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920L5-6)</sup> their skin tone being a light tan to a dark bronze in color and their eyes ranging from white to turquoise.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Models-7)</sup>

[Ayleidoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleidoon), their language,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TVC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSOP-9)</sup> is similar to [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), the ancient language of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BJ2-10)</sup> The Ayleids were the original founders of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). They constructed the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), which they called the "Temple of the Ancestors", in emulation of the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BOTZ-12)</sup> The [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) collapsed in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) when it was overthrown by the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-5)</sup> While it's believed that the Ayleids remain in the wilds of every province on Tamriel, sightings have been few.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWE-4)</sup> There have been no known sightings in the [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md) or [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

## History

### Merethic Era
*"Wear lore as your armor."*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png/200px-LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png) A depiction of the Aldmer with the White-Gold Tower in the Merethic Era The Ayleids began as groups of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) who left the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) to create splinter cultures in central and southwest Tamriel.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> They first left in the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), after the area had been charted by [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) and others,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BTAOM-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3C-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-16)</sup> In some cases, they left specifically to evade the strictures of Aldmeri regulation, which forbade (among many other things) the worship of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> The Ayleids were in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) as early as the First Era,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Ayleid HR)</sup> and their ruins can be found throughout the province, including the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains) and as far as the island of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Enduum-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ARLIESO-18)</sup>

Though they settled predominantly in Cyrodiil, the [Barsaebic Ayleids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barsaebic_Ayleids) also had settlements in modern-day [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The Ayleid rulers of Black Marsh were renowned as necromancers and titled the Fenlords.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Zahra-19)</sup> The cities [Gideon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gideon) and [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold) were originally Ayleid settlements whose names have been lost to history.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3A-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Loriasel-21)</sup> The earliest Aldmeri account of the [Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) of Cyrodiil from Topal the Pilot speaks of friendly feathered [Bird Men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird_Men) who resided there,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-FOTN-22)</sup> but these natives disappeared from history, only to be replaced by the Ayleids. It is also alleged that the Bird Men were enslaved by the Ayleids.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OBDecentius-23)</sup>

Far away from [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md), Ayleidic culture flourished. In the last millennium of the Merethic Era, Daedra worship took hold and spread among the Heartland High Elves. The cults devoted to the various [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) which had sprung up across Cyrodiil, once merely tolerated, became celebrated.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> Unlike the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) who left Summerset in the Late Middle Merethic Era,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BTAOM-14)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LOTS-24)</sup> the Ayleids made no distinction between "good" and "bad" Daedra. Even some of the more heinous Princes received mass veneration, especially when their worship was adopted and endorsed by Ayleid kings and aristocrats.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup>

The Ayleids made deals with Daedra for more power, blessings, and other advantages.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Oblivion-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TOAC-26)</sup> They employed entire armies of Daedra to conquer, subjugate, and enforce their rule.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAOK-2)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-COTF-27)</sup> In the earliest Cyro-Nordic stories, [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr) fought against the Ayleids on mankind's behalf, then vanished, presumably to go help humans elsewhere. Without his leadership, the Cyrodiils were dominated and enslaved by the Ayleids.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SATD-28)</sup> At first this enslavement of the [Nedic peoples](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) was occasional, but eventually this became a systematic, widespread institution of Ayleid society.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> They eventually controlled the entirety of modern-day Cyrodiil, and kept the Nedic peoples there enslaved for generations.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TA-29)</sup> The Ayleids are also said to have enslaved beastfolk.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OBDecentius-23)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7d/ON-npc-Master_Stormwarden_Faranwe.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Master_Stormwarden_Faranwe.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Master_Stormwarden_Faranwe.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Master_Stormwarden_Faranwe.jpg) Master Stormwarden of Ilayas The fortress of [Ilayas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ilayas) was built on a seaside hill of [Malabal Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malabal_Tor), in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), many years into the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), and it was also active during the golden years of the Ayleid Empire, roughly between [ME 850](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_850) and [ME 900](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_900). The Ayleid lords of Ilayas were part of an ancient order of lightning wizards called the Stormwardens, who defended the coast from foreign invasion and operated an Ayleid artifact called the Sphere of Storms, a potent orb that utilizes [weather magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weather_Magic) to manipulate the waves of the coast and supply water for crops.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONCirnean-30)</sup>

In the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), at the height of Ayleid rule, Abagarlas was a wealthy city which prospered on account of its large population of mercenaries.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWOD-31)</sup> Located close by was the city of Delodiil, which was home to a glorious temple dedicated to the worship of [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), the Lady of Light. Growing envious of Delodiil's temple, King [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) of Abagarlas ordered the construction of a far more massive temple dedicated to Molag Bal. Although Anumaril's temple dwarfed Delodiil's, its design was crude and aesthetically displeasing, leaving Delodiil's people, including their king, [Cenedelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cenedelin), unimpressed. Hearing of Delodiil's dismissal of his temple enraged King Anumaril, and after profaning Abagarlas' small shrine to Meridia, he ordered the destruction of Delodiil and proclaimed that he would sacrifice its populace to Molag Bal.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWOD-31)</sup> Anumaril, calling upon his dark master, was granted an army of undead and the knowledge to construct a weapon known as the [Mortuum Vivicus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mortuum_Vivicus), which would drain all life from Delodiil, and convert its citizens to undead servants of Molag Bal.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CJ-32)</sup> Meridia, knowing of Molag Bal and Anumaril's twisted plans, warned her subjects in Delodiil, and in response a small group of priests were dispatched to Abagarlas to destroy the weapon. Upon reaching Abagarlas the priests tore a bloody swath through the city, putting both Anumaril's wife Queen Palolel and his son Prince Malyon to the sword. Soon, the priests successfully reached the Mortuum Vivicus, in time to stop the contraption's activation. However, Molag Bal pulled the device into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) before it could be entirely destroyed. After the priests fled the city, undead in tow, Meridia completely destroyed the city through divine lightning.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LJ-33)</sup>

The [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md) was a civil conflict that spanned the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era) to [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198). The war was held between the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping Ayleids of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The war's conclusion, brought in by the Scouring of [Wendelbek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wendelbek), marked the end of any organized resistance to Daedra-worship in Cyrodiil. The lasting effects of the Schism were theorized to have greatly contributed to the fall of the Ayleid Empire and the White-Gold Tower, nearly half a century later.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ASIV-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-CAATAYQ-35)</sup>

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/LO-misc-Blades_Mural.png/200px-LO-misc-Blades_Mural.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Blades_Mural.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Blades_Mural.png) Celemaril's rise and fall In the First Era, together with his undead forces, the immortal [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril_Light-Bringer.md) waged war across Tamriel.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Telarendil-36)</sup> His brother Valentis led other Ayleid kings in a rebellion against Celemaril. With [varla stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varla_Stone) and an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), Valentis donned Celemaril's crown and spoke a ritual incantation, sealing his brother within a crypt beneath a [marble](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marble) statue bearing his likeness.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup> As king, Valentis would then erase much of the records of Celemaril and his conquest.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Telarendil-36)</sup> Valentis also founded the town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) around his statue which sealed Celemaril.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/ON-interior-Vakka-Bok_Xanmeer_Golden_Path.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Vakka-Bok_Xanmeer_Golden_Path.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Vakka-Bok_Xanmeer_Golden_Path.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Vakka-Bok_Xanmeer_Golden_Path.jpg) Ayleid nexus gates in Eld Angavar At some point prior to the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), a clan of Barsaebic Ayleids led by an Ayleid mage known as Myndhal invaded the region of Murkmire in southern [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) in order to steal the souls of the native [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) The Ayleids launched a series of attacks on the [Root-Whisper Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Root-Whisper_Tribe). The tribe was unable to resist the invaders, forcing the tribe's [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree to take a drastic measure: the creation of a "seed", an amber gem which would later be known as the [Remnant of Argon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remnant_of_Argon), which was capable of holding the souls of the tribe, preventing the Ayleids from harvesting them. In this way, the Hist planned to preserve its tribe and become dormant, only to be awakened when the Ayleid threat to Murkmire was ended.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESODreaming-40)</sup> Upon learning of the Remnant's creation, and having failed to secure it during its formation, Myndhal attempted to locate it by any means necessary in order to make use of its power. An Ayleid mage named Houtern began to construct [Welkynd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welkynd_Stone) -powered Nexus Gates that all connected to [Eld Angavar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eld_Angavar) to traverse Murkmire in search of the Remnant. Over time, this resulted in the creation of a "golden path", a sea of azure gates linking to one another within an otherwise-empty void. However, the Ayleids were ultimately unsuccessful in their search, and were forced to turn to Argonian technology to locate the Remnant.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESORemnant-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Myndhal-42)</sup> Through the use of Argonian [vakka stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vakka_Stone), Myndhal theorized that he could create a link to wherever the Hist had hidden the Remnant, and swore to find a way to it even if it would take "hundreds of years". In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) traversed Eld Angavar and killed Myndhal, who had become undead and seemed to still be working on a way to uncover the Remnant.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESORemnant-41)</sup>

#### Alessian Slave Rebellion
The example of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) to the north inspired the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) of [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-PGE3All-43)</sup> Alessia's rebellion coincided with a civil war within the Ayleid Empire which led to many rebel Ayleid lords joining forces with Alessia and aiding her rebellion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-5)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-OBHerminia-44)</sup> These allies of Alessia were largely Aedra-worshippers,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> indicating that the schism among the Ayleids was likely related to religious differences.

[Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), of course, lent help to their enslaved relatives under the Ayleid Empire, which also played a part in the Ayleids being overthrown.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-FC-45)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-46)</sup> In only a year, the Slave Queen [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and her supporters had taken the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), founded the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), and secured the subsequent shift in power from mer to men on Tamriel.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-FC-45)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3C-15)</sup>

Sometime after the Alessian Slave Rebellion,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-FBJ-47)</sup> various Ayleid kingdoms launched a desperate search for the fabled [Wrathstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrathstone), a weapon that they believed would be their salvation against the encroaching forces of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). An expedition marched to the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city of [Mzulft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mzulft), in the hold of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where it was rumored the Wrathstone was held. The Ayleids besieged Mzulft, but failed to breach it or retrieve the artifact.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-IPOM-48)</sup> At the time, the Ayleids were unaware that the Dwemer only possessed the left half of the Wrathstone, and that half was stored not at Mzulft, but at nearby [Kagrenzel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenzel).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-IPOM-48)</sup> After the failure of the raid on Mzulft, [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia) appeared to her faithful with a cryptic warning that the Ayleid Empire would soon fall, and entrusted the right half of the Wrathstone to Garlas Malatar and King Narilmor. To keep his piece of the Wrathstone safe,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-FBJ-47)</sup> Narilmor denied both aid and asylum to the desperate Ayleid refugees from other kingdoms, and locked them out of his city.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MTCKN-49)</sup> The inhabitants of Garlas Malatar were not heard of again. Although Garlas Malatar was eventually besieged by [Men](Lore_Men.md), some accounts have suggested that the attackers did not even make it inside Narilmor's city before it collapsed into ruin; it was speculated that the city fell from within, brought down by internal conflict or sabotage.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MTCKN-49)</sup>

#### The Late Ayleid Period
*"Our exiled Elven ancestors heard the welcoming gifts of peace in the streams and beech trees and stars."*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup> The Alessian Slave Rebellion was devastating to the Ayleids. Legend says entire settlements were slaughtered at the hands of Alessia's champion, [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSOP-9)</sup> Though the Ayleids would continue to have a presence in Cyrodiil for several hundred years, the fall of the White-Gold Tower in 1E 243 signaled the beginning of the Late Ayleid Period and the start of the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md). The Temple of the Ancestors was turned into the Imperial Palace by Alessia and her descendants, and it remains so to this day. The [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) region was one of the very last areas to be liberated by the Alessian army. It took Alessia's forces, led by Bravillius Tasus, four attempts to finally defeat them.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-DOTN-50)</sup> In these early years, the Alessian Empire forbade the worship of the Daedric Princes and focused on hunting down and exterminating Daedra-worshipping Ayleids.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup>

Ayleidic culture lingered in the Aedra-worshipping city-states that survived as client kingdoms of Alessian Cyrodiil, but resentment at the continued presence of Ayleid nobles within the Empire was a contributing factor to the rise of the so-called [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) founded by [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md). In [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361), the Alessians gained control of the Empire and enforced the Alessian Doctrines throughout its domain. The Ayleid lordships were abolished. Enforcement of this decree does not appear to have required much direct violence — it seems that by this point the balance of power was so overwhelmingly against them, and their fate so long foreshadowed, that most of the remaining Ayleids simply left Cyrodiil to join the Ayleid Diaspora, eventually being absorbed into the elven populations of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-5)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-PGE3V-51)</sup> Some Ayleid clans were known to have settled in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-52)</sup> It's speculated that the exodus of the Ayleids from Cyrodiil may have vastly strengthened the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni.md) in High Rock - in which case, the Ayleids had their revenge, as the war with the Direnni eventually crippled the Alessian Empire and brought about its fall.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-5)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-DRD-53)</sup>

The only known Ayleids who remained in Cyrodiil served as mentors and tutors for the new human nobility.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-RTR-54)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg/200px-ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg) Ruins of Bisnensel, a city founded in High Rock by King Dynar In the years [1E 372](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_372) - [374](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_374), King Dynar was given an ultimatum by the [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) [Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ami-El). He and his people were eventually forced to abandon Nenalata and flee to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in order to escape the anti-Ayleid pogroms.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-55)</sup> During their exodus, hunters among the Nenalata Ayleids used senche mounts daily to track and kill game to help feed the evacuees. <sup>[[56]](#cite_note-NA-56)</sup> In High Rock, King Dynar and his people were welcomed by the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan. After displacing previous Orc residents, they settled on the shores of the [Halcyon Lake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halcyon_Lake). There, King Dynar founded the new city of [Bisnensel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bisnensel), which was a miniature copy of Nenalata.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-55)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-BOAR-57)</sup>

In the years [1E 453](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_453) - [460](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_460), the Primeval Seekers, a cult devoted to [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), was formed in Bisnensel. Their leader, High Priest [Uluscant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uluscant), demanded more of a say in municipal affairs as the cult grew in power. King Dynar denied these demands. As a result, after a few months Dynar was deposed and forced to flee from Bisnensel.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-55)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-BOAR-57)</sup> King Dynar and his family fled to [Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balfiera), where they were granted asylum among the Direnni. Later, Dynar became a highly regarded tactician and strategist in the Direnni army.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-55)</sup>

In the late 400s, the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) invaded High Rock. The invasion culminated in the great [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors), in which King Dynar participated.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-55)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-5)</sup> The battle was won by the Direnni and was a fatal blow to the Alessian Order.

In the years [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482) - [484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_484), King Dynar returned to the [Heartland](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartland) and was lured to Nenalata, where he fell into [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s trap and was imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), in a [replica of the White-Gold Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Lightless_Oubliette).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-55)</sup> There he remained for over three thousand years, until he was freed.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-ESOKLD-58)</sup> Other sources state that King Dynar was not captured by Molag Bal in Nenalata, but instead was present in the [Hollow City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hollow_City) while it was still in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), was pushed into Coldharbour along with it, and captured shortly afterwards.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ONGroundskeeper-59)</sup>

It is possible that some tribes of Ayleids may continue to live deep within the forests of Cyrodiil even into modernity, yet the most recent reported sightings (as of the Late Third Era) are more than 1000 years old.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWE-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920L5-6)</sup> These Ayleids, or Wild Elves, are at best a rarity and may not have actually survived into modern times.

## Legacy

Ayleid sages were present at the turn of the Second Era. [Tjurhane Fyrre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tjurhane_Fyrre) founded the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) and studied the history of his people, publicly speaking about their religion and culture, such as when he released a book during the reign of [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) in [2E 12](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_12) known as the *Nature of Ayleidic Poesy*. He passed away in [2E 227](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_227) at the age of 357.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWE-4)</sup>

In the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), King Dynar was finally freed by the [Vestige](Lore_Hero.md), who infiltrated Coldharbour with the [Fighters](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Mages Guilds](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in an effort to stop Molag Bal's terrible [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). The Ayleid king assumed command over the Fighters Guild forces, who had been leaderless after their captain had been slain. The invasion force, led by the Vestige and aided by [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), eventually managed to stop the Planemeld inside the Planar Vortex, but King Dynar was mortally wounded while trying to close a Daedric portal. He died soon afterwards in the Hollow City, in Meridia's Chapel of Light.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ESOTLA-60)</sup> His spirit is believed to have gone to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-61)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

King Hadhuul's spirit, along with spirits of his army and some [Flame Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Atronach), reappeared at Ceyatatar during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), but these invaders were banished by soldiers fighting in the ongoing [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) before they could harm the nearby settlement of [Vlastarus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vlastarus).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ON-63)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-render-King_Narilmor_02.jpg/200px-ON-render-King_Narilmor_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-King_Narilmor_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-King_Narilmor_02.jpg) King Narilmor Around [2E 283](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_283),<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-64)</sup> an entire Imperial expedition from Fort Mistwatch was forcibly converted into Meridian Purified when they uncovered the power hidden within Garlas Malatar, serving as unwilling protectors of the Wrathstone.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-FMMWTDOM-65)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the treasure hunter Tharayya accompanied by the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) uncovered the horrors that took place in Garlas Malatar. ## Society
*"In the caverns of lore, ugly and obscure, shines the gold of truth."*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup> [![ON-furnishing-Ancient High Elf Statue 02.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/ON-furnishing-Ancient_High_Elf_Statue_02.png/140px-ON-furnishing-Ancient_High_Elf_Statue_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Ancient_High_Elf_Statue_02.png) Ayleidic society was an alliance of kingdoms united under one empire, which ultimately ended in 1E 243 with the death of their champion, [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril_the_Unfeathered), and the fall of the White-Gold Tower.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWE-4)</sup>

The impact of the loss of the Ayleids' tower, what they called the "Temple of the Ancestors", should not be underestimated. They had built it in emulation of the Adamantine Tower,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BOTZ-12)</sup> and made the [Chim-el-Adabal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chim-el_Adabal), which they believed to be the crystallized blood of the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), into the tower's Founding-Stone.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-AE4TET-74)</sup> It is the site where the [Ten Ancestors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ten_Ancestors), statues sacred to the Ayleids, were traditionally held. They were spread to various other settlements during the siege of the White-Gold Tower for safekeeping, but were not reunited again until the late Third Era, long after the fall of the Ayleids.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-11)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Oblivion-25)</sup>

Ayleids frequently adorned themselves with feathers and magic beads.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSOP-9)</sup> They are said to have been "obsessed" with the number eight (which may have been why the pantheon Saint Alessia created was made up of [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md)).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TA-29)</sup>

Most humans, at least, agree that the Ayleids' fall from power, if not their extinction, is for the better, as the portrayals of them passed down by their former slaves are not kind. Their scholars label the Ayleids as bellicose and arrogant, and point to this being the ultimate reason for their downfall.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TOAC-26)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-PGE3All-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-OBHerminia-44)</sup> Slaves were utilized by the Ayleids for a variety of purposes, including agricultural work, entertainment, and the building and maintenance of their empire's infrastructure. According to legend, the Ayleids literally turned cruelty into an art form, and many Daedra-worshipping Ayleids across Cyrodiil derived strange pleasures from "art-tortures".<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TA-29)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup>

Typically, Ayleid kingdoms where the most malevolent Daedric Princes were worshipped also proved to exhibit the worst treatment of their slaves.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> Various settlements became famous for their particular, elaborate method of torturing their slaves, such as "wailing wheels", "gut gardens", "flesh-sculptures", and forced ingestion of hallucinogenic [drugs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drugs), as well as nighttime "tiger sport", which involved the immolation of human children.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TA-29)</sup>

As the kingdoms of the Ayleids dwindled, they apparently adapted to a more rural, elusive, and possibly nomadic lifestyle. Any remaining Ayleids are unknown; if they continue to exist, they are likely living in hiding. The Ayleid Sage [Tjurhane Fyrre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tjurhane_Fyrre) ([1E 2790](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2790) - [2E 227](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_227)) talked and wrote extensively of the state of the Ayleids' culture. According to Fyrre, the Ayleids of his time lived in a tribal society, with each "tribe" being substantially different from the next.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWE-4)</sup> The Ayleid settlements in modern-day [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were particularly far removed from the culture of the heartlands, and were never attacked by the Alessian armies.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3A-20)</sup> It's not clear how these settlements met their fate.

### Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/SR-quest-The_Cause_05.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Cause_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Cause_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Cause_05.jpg) A Great Welkynd Stone in Rielle Magic was an enormous part of their society. In accordance with High Elf religion, they believed [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) was composed of four basic elements: earth, water, air, and light (as opposed to the four elements of modern natural philosophy: earth, water, air, and fire).<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-GAL-75)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-MFTS-76)</sup> Some of their magic tapped into the power of the weather.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Oblivion-25)</sup> The most sublime form was starlight, as the stars were the link between [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).

They used aetherial fragments which fell from the heavens, particularly [meteoric iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Iron), to construct receptacles which are believed to harness energy from starlight. These "Ayleid Wells" are scattered all over Cyrodiil, and can still be used by mages to replenish their energy. The logic used in their placement is a mystery, and none are known to be associated with any particular Ayleid settlements. They kept specially cut pieces of [meteoric glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Glass) known as Welkynd Stones (meaning literally "sky children") as storage containers for magical energy, as well what are called Varla Stones (or "star stones").<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-MFTS-76)</sup> This was still an emerging technology when the Alessian Slave Rebellion brought about the decline and disappearance of their society.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-LRN-77)</sup> Some of these crystals had the capacity to store and release memories directly to the reader; overuse of these crystals can cause a 'problem of capacity' for mortals.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ESOFlooded-78)</sup> Varla gems were occasionally used to imbue the diadems by Barsaebic for that purpose.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Contraband B-79)</sup>

They preserved many of the ancient runes and [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) magics of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) that other elves had forgotten.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BTAOM-14)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-AD-80)</sup> They also developed what came to be called the school of [Alteration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alteration), as they were skilled in magics such as shapeshifting and levitation.

### Aedra
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg) Auri-El Few blanket statements can be made about Ayleidic religion. They began as splinter groups, and they were never totally united in their beliefs.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> But while they are most well-known to historians for being "godless" and "[Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -loving",<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-GTTIC-82)</sup> their society was fundamentally dedicated to the veneration of the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra), not the Daedra, until the last millennium of the Merethic Era.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWOD-31)</sup>

Even after the Daedric cults rose to power (discussed [above](#History)), many Ayleidic kingdoms continued to honor the Aedra.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> Many slaves in Cyrodiil became Aedra-worshippers by adopting many of the beliefs of their masters,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAOK-2)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SATD-28)</sup> and it's suspected that Alessia incorporated elven Aedra into her Eight Divines pantheon in order to appease her Ayleid and Ayleid-influenced vassals.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> Ayleids venerated their ancestors,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-11)</sup> and Aedra is an Elven word for "ancestor".<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-AAD-83)</sup>

### Daedra

#### Blood-Made-Pleasure
To the Ayleids, [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine) was known as the **Blood-Made-Pleasure**.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Mot I-85)</sup> Those from the kingdoms that worshipped the more malevolent Daedric Princes were known to indulge in the pleasures of art-torture, such as flesh-sculpting and gut-gardening.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWTA-13)</sup> An ancient Ayleid manuscript of Eldhaal the Inducer describes him being overseen and guided by Sanguine in these heinous arts, particularly in the creation of the "Messenger Beast Redeemed".<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Mot I-85)</sup>

#### Hyrma Mora
The city of [Bisnensel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bisnensel) began to worship Hyrma Mora under the lead of High Priest Uluscant, who established a congregation in Bisnensel that attracted many of the learned and most influential people in the community. As their numbers became more numerous, on their behalf Uluscant requested more of a say in municipal affairs, a request that was denied by King Dynar. Within a few months of his rebuff, Dynar was deposed.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-86)</sup> The Ayleids of Bisnensel turned the city into a shrine and stored forbidden knowledge of the natural order, which could allow one to manipulate the very fabric of reality.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-87)</sup>

#### Mehrunes Dagon
In ancient times, Dagon created a construct known as the Nefarivigum to challenge those who drew near to undertake a trial. One who passed the trial would be deemed worthy of wielding [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md). Located in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the Nefarivigum was discovered by the ancient Ayleids, who arrogantly believed they could turn away any who sought the Razor. To this end, they built the vast, ringed city of [Varsa Baalim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varsa_Baalim) during the height of Ayleid civilization. For many years, pilgrims came to Varsa Baalim seeking the Razor, but the Ayleids turned them away. One day in the early years of the First Era, a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) infiltrated the city. Before long, the city was infested with undead and thrust into chaos. The city suddenly and mysteriously disappeared. None know what happened, but Varsa Baalim seemingly vanished into the mountains of the Eastern [Nibenay Basin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Basin).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TOAC-26)</sup>

#### Mephala
The Ayleid city of [Erokii](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Erokii) was ruled by princess Anurraame, whose wisdom, grace, and beauty were unsurpassed. She married a prince from another state, and their relationship was happy for a time. However, Anurraame's husband was away for extended periods of time. The princess's passion died, and she took a younger warrior as her lover. Her husband eventually caught wind of the affair, and gathered his army to storm the city. Anurraame pledged to face him in battle, and her lover promised to muster his own forces in defense of the city. When the day of the battle arrived, Anurraame saw that her lover had betrayed her for a bribe, as his legions had joined her husband's. The spurned princess called upon the Daedra to help defend her city, and Mephala answered her plea. She commanded Anurraame to pour all her tears in a basin, then imbued them with the power of Anurraame's hatred for her traitorous lover, which was only strengthened by the genuine love she had for him before he turned on her. Her tears hardened into a powerful artifact known as the [Tears of Anurraame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tears_of_Anurraame), which the princess used to destroy both armies and her city in the blink of an eye.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-88)</sup>

#### Merid-Nunda
[Merid-Nunda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia) (known as the **Bright One** and **Rainbow of Light**)<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-RDP-89)</sup> was an extremely important figure for the Ayleids, as she was considered the personification of their fourth element, light.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-EOMN-90)</sup> The Ayleids often depicted Meridia with wings and a cowl.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ESOAS-91)</sup>

Merid-Nunda was the patron deity of Garlas Malatar, a city which was built upon the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). Its ruler, [King Narilmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King_Narilmor), was a devout worshiper of Merid-Nunda who dedicated his life and city to her veneration. He was considered a favorite of the Daedric Prince, second only to her champion, Umaril the Unfeathered. She gifted the city with a relic known as the Guiding Light, which shone above the city with her unquenchable light. It was a literal and metaphorical beacon, visible for great distances.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MTCKN-49)</sup>

She is remembered by the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) as a patron and mentor to the Heartland High Elves, and complicit in the bondage and oppression of their ancestors.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-AYQ-92)</sup>

#### Mola Gbal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg/200px-OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg) Ayleid statuette of Mola Gbal Molag Bal was known as **Mola Gbal** to the Ayleids,<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ESOHo SLoad Txt-93)</sup> and was the patron deity of [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWOD-31)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-94)</sup>

King [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) of Abagarlas was granted an army of undead and the knowledge to construct a weapon known as the [Mortuum Vivicus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mortuum_Vivicus), which would drain all life from Delodiil, and convert its citizens to undead servants of Molag Bal.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CJ-32)</sup> Meridia, knowing of Molag Bal and Anumaril's twisted plans, warned her subjects in Delodiil, and in response a small group of priests were dispatched to Abagarlas to destroy the weapon. Upon reaching Abagarlas the priests tore a bloody swath through the city, putting both Anumaril's wife Queen Palolel and his son Prince Malyon to the sword. Soon, the priests successfully reached the Mortuum Vivicus, in time to stop the contraption's activation. However, Molag Bal pulled the device into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) before it could be entirely destroyed. After the priests fled the city, undead in tow, Meridia completely destroyed the city through divine lightning.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LJ-33)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg/200px-OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) One of the Ten Ancestors A deity whose name was lost to history, known only as the [Insect God](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Insect_God), was venerated by Flower King [Nilichi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nilichi). The [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) [Men-of-ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Men-of-ge) were eventually "destroyed" when the Flower King made a great sacrifice to this long-forgotten insect god.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TA-29)</sup>

The Ayleid Diaspora was allowed to settle in Valenwood on the condition of adopting aspects of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). Having little choice, the Ayleids agreed and refrained from harming the forest, which probably contributed to the dilution of their culture and their eventual integration with the local [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) population.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ASIV-34)</sup>

The [Ten Ancestors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ten_Ancestors) are a set of ten Ayleid [statues](Lore_Statues.md). They consist mostly of [meteoric iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Iron) prongs, with a shard of [meteoric glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Glass) in the center. The statues played an important role in Ayleid religion and were displayed in the [Temple of the Ancestors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_Ancestors), now known as the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). The temple of Loriasel was also built in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) by the [Barsaebic Ayleids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barsaebic_Ayleids) in veneration of the Ancestors.

The Ancestors themselves were believed to worship the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), and [Daedraphile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedraphile) Ayleids accordingly followed the *Eleven Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*. The Heretic of [Lindai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lindai) was known for writing the *Eleven OTHER Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*, a parody that supposed the Ancestors worshipped [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) instead.
