# Legend of the Lovers

The **Legend of the Lovers** is ancient holiday tale often recited on [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md), the 16<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Dawn. It is a bardic tale concerning two Lovers, **Polydor** and **Eloisa**. Although the names and people were real, the legend itself is fantasy, spun to mythologize a true and tragic story.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup>

Every Heart's Day, in homes all across Tamriel, the Legend of the Lovers is traditionally sung to the younger generations. Inns also offer free rooms for visitors on this day in honor of the Lovers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Although most live performances tend to focus on the carnal aspects of the legend,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup> the truth of the tale focuses on the wickedness and perversity found in mortal man. Some bardic performances of the legend are therefore decidedly depressing, with the two Lovers doomed due to the cruel nature of the world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920SD-4)</sup> This telling is the closest the legend gets to reality; the original bardic rendition ends with the apparent death of the Lovers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup>

According to the enigmatic immortal entity known as [the Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md), the truth of Polydor and Eloisa's story can be found in the ancient pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) writings of [Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup>

In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), King [Dro'Zel](Lore_Dro%27Zel.md) of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) summoned [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to the town of [Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md) after hearing a story from a local bard, resulting in the town's destruction.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HOSAO-5)</sup> The historical fiction series *[2920, the Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)* suggests that the story was the bard's retelling of Polydor and Eloisa's tale. Normally the King ended most Lovers' Day in a happy mood, but the story left him depressed and vengeful.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920SD-4)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the famous Argonian stage director Dreams-in-Scenes set out to give the tale new staying power. Aiming to challenge modern audiences' evolving tastes and their tendency toward surface-level criticism, she re-envisioned the Legend of the Lovers as a sweeping epic to be performed during a Hearts Week festival in [Belkarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Belkarth). She successfully reshaped its traditionally somber mood into a more dynamic and accessible production while preserving its artistic core.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RLFCA-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DIS-7)</sup>

Polydor's Grotto is a cave that shares its name with one of the Lovers. It was cleared out one Heart's Day by warriors from a nearby [kingdom](Lore_Dynasty_of_Odar_the_Brave.md) who were investigating monsters multiplying at an unprecedented rate, possibly due to the coming of spring.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PG-8)</sup>

## Verses
*And so the couple stood / atop the castle wall / when for the greater good / parted wide curtains of light
Their love would never rest / the blood would tell it true / and as their families fest / they vanished to the night*
—Final lines of the original bardic rendition<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LATH-1)</sup> *A hero returns home to find his meadow ablaze and cottage sundered.
Beams of moonlight cut a path through the graveyard fog. They carry too, in my desperate imaginings, your touch on my weary skin.
Release them, vile fiend.
And so we slept soundly atop a bed of flowers, never to be separated again.*
—A collection of verses from Dreams-in-Scenes' stage production.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GGS-9)</sup>

## Notes
- The Legend of the Lovers is not to be confused with *[The Legend of Lovers Lament](Lore_The_Legend_of_Lovers_Lament.md)*, which relates to the lake of [Lover's Lament](Lore_Lover%27s_Lament.md) and a different pair of lovers.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TLOLL-10)</sup>
- The names of Polydor and Eloisa are likely derived from the phrase "[Abelard and Heloise](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abelard_and_Heloise)".
