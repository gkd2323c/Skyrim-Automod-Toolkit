Katariah Ra'athim (also enumerated as **Katariah I**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> was the thirteenth [empress](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for approximately forty-six years, from [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153) to [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200). Katariah was originally the consort of the mad emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md), but in [3E 147](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_147), he began biting and attacking visitors to the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), resulting in his commitment to an asylum and Katariah being named empress regent.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> After Pelagius' death in 3E 153, Katariah was proclaimed Empress of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> As the first and only [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) to rule all of Tamriel, she faced opposition from the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), and the council assumed much greater power and responsibility during her reign.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-5)</sup> Nonetheless, Katariah's forty-six-year reign was one of the most celebrated in Tamriel's history, and she was popular with the common people.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> She was preceded by her husband, Pelagius III, and was succeeded by her son, [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg/200px-SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Katariah.jpg) The Katariah, ship of Emperor Titus Mede II, named after Katariah Katariah was a member of the prominent [Ra'athim](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) Clan of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> At some point prior to [3E 141](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_141), she was appointed Duchess of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ8-6)</sup> In 3E 141, on the orders of Emperor [Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), Katariah was married to the Emperor's son, [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_III.md), King of [Solitude](Lore_Haafingar.md), who was known for his eccentric behavior.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ8-6)</sup> Magnus arranged the marriage of Katariah to Pelagius to try and cement relations with Ebonheart, the seat of power for the Ra'athim Clan.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> Magnus also arranged the marriage because he hoped that Katariah, who was both a shrewd diplomat and beautiful, would be able to hide the madness of Pelagius.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup>

### Empress Consort and Empress Regent
On the 8th of Second Seed, [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145), Emperor Magnus died quietly in his sleep.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ8-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TET-7)</sup> [Jolethe](Lore_Jolethe.md), Pelagius' sister, took over the throne of Solitude, and Pelagius and Katariah rode to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), where they were crowned emperor and empress.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TET-7)</sup> A legend holds that at Pelagius III's coronation, he fainted when the [Red Dragon Crown](Lore_Red_Dragon_Crown.md) was placed on his head, but Katariah held him up so only those closest to the thrones could see what had happened.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup>

Due to Pelagius III's madness, Katariah and the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) made all the decisions and tried to keep him from embarrassing everyone.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> In [3E 147](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_147), Pelagius began biting and attacking visitors to the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), resulting in his commitment to a private asylum and Katariah being named empress regent.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DC-8)</sup> Katariah visited Pelagius in his cell shortly after his commitment, and she became pregnant with his child.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> She later gave birth to their son, [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> Her pregnancy occurring shortly after Pelagius' commitment sparked rumors of infidelity; however, Katariah proved that her pregnancy was the result of her visit to Pelagius' cell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> Some absurd rumors emerged around this time suggesting that the Emperor was actually sane and that she was part of a conspiracy to keep him locked away.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup>

In [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153), Pelagius died on a warm night in Sun's Dawn after a brief fever in his cell at the [Temple of Kynareth](Lore_Temple_of_Kynareth.md) on the Isle of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md), and Katariah was proclaimed Empress of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TET-7)</sup>

### Empress of Tamriel
Some consider the accession of Katariah to be the end of the Septim bloodline.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> While Katariah was not a blood relative of the Septims, the father of her son Cassynder was Pelagius III, which meant that her successor was a blood descendant of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-4)</sup> After the death of Pelagius, she took a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman named [Gallivere Lariat](Lore_Gallivere_Lariat.md) as her imperial consort.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-4)</sup> With Lariat, Katariah had her second son, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_IV.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-4)</sup> Katariah was uncomfortable in the Imperial City, so she traveled extensively throughout the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), more than any emperor had since the days of Tiber Septim.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> She repaired many of the broken alliances and poor diplomatic relations caused by previous emperors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup>

During her reign, Katariah appointed [Jovron Direnni](Lore_Jovron_Direnni.md) as the first [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) since the days of Tiber Septim.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DRD-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-5)</sup> Direnni assisted the Empress in creating peace during the tumultuous moments of her reign.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DRD-9)</sup> Katariah faced opposition from the Elder Council, and the council assumed much greater power and responsibility during her reign.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-5)</sup> One member of the Elder Council, Thane Minglumire, Duke of [Vengheto](Lore_Vengheto.md), took a particular delight in exposing Katariah's lack of practical knowledge.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAC-3)</sup> The people of Tamriel purportedly came to love her far more than the nobility did.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup>

After a massacre of [Imperial troops](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) outside the village of Armanias in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), Katariah and the Elder Council discussed which armorer should receive the commission to design the armor and weaponry for the Imperial legionaries being sent to quell the unrest in Black Marsh. Katariah favored a clever armorer that she had met in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), the former slave [Hazadir](Lore_Hazadir.md), while the Elder Council favored the finest armorer in the Imperial City, [Sirollus Saccus](Lore_Sirollus_Saccus.md). After much debate, it was decided to have both armorers outfit a champion for a duel, and whichever armorer outfitted the winner of the duel would receive the commission. Katariah's champion, [Nandor Beraid](Lore_Nandor_Beraid.md), was outfitted by Hazadir, and the other champion, Raphalas Eul, was outfitted by Saccus.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HT-10)</sup> In the duel, Beraid defeated Eul, so Hazadir received the commission, and Katariah won the respect of the Elder Council.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAC-3)</sup>

In [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200), Katariah was killed in a minor skirmish in Black Marsh, and she was succeeded by Cassynder.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Death)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TET-7)</sup> Katariah's death is a favorite topic of conspiracy-minded historians. The sage [Montalius](Lore_Montalius.md) later discovered that a disenfranchised branch of the Septim family was involved in the skirmish, which came as quite a revelation.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup>
