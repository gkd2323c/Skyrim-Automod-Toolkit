[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-College_of_Winterhold_02.jpg) The College of Winterhold [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/SR-banner-College_of_Winterhold.png/120px-SR-banner-College_of_Winterhold.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-College_of_Winterhold.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-College_of_Winterhold.png) College banner The College of Winterhold is a school for [magic](Lore_Magic.md) in the [city of Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md). Its campus is imposing and fortress-like, arranged around a large central courtyard and, following the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md), reachable only across a narrow stone bridge.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup> It functions much like the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in other parts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), but is a resolutely independent establishment separate from the Guild. The College holds documents dating back as far as the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRSavos-2)</sup> though the College itself is even older.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRMirabelle-3)</sup> It is said to have been founded in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the legendary mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md), although it is one of many dubious feats attributed to him.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AMM-4)</sup> One of the oldest reliable mentions of the College comes from the early Second Era.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ao D-5)</sup>

The College is a selective institution, which allows entrance only to those students who prove themselves adept at the magical arts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup> However, once a student earns admittance, they are granted more freedom of experimentation than in the Mages Guild, with even [Necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) regarded as an acceptable arcane practice.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRPhinis-6)</sup> The College's main building is the Hall of the Elements, which is used for all kinds of magical practice. It holds the famous Arcanaeum library as well as the Arch-Mage's quarters, while accommodations for students and faculty are provided in separate buildings. Beneath the college campus lies the Midden, a remnant of the college's past where many of its greatest secrets lie.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup> The [Eye of Shalidor](Lore_Aurbic_Eye.md) is the symbol of the institution and is seen on many banners and stone reliefs across campus.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Eo SBM-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-STN-8)</sup> It symbolized leadership over both mundane and arcane mysteries.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DEC-9)</sup>

## History
The Rings of Preservation were fabled pieces of jewelry argued by some scholars to have been crafted in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) for the Arch-Mages of Winterhold, to help with their personal protection.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BLRing-10)</sup> Although it is unknown if he was the chief librarian of the College as of then, [Urag gro-Shub](Lore_Urag_gro-Shub.md) already worked in the Arcanaeum during the Second Era, ensuring the survival of the rare books.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-UGS-11)</sup>

According to [Taleon Mythmaker](Lore_Taleon_Mythmaker.md), during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), three enigmatic individuals were associated with the College and wore crowns with its emblem. Shroud was linked to pursuing worldly agendas in hidden spaces,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SC-12)</sup> Shadow was the master of hidden arcane knowledge in Winterhold,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ASC-13)</sup> and Dawn, who led both Shroud and Shadow, who was known for mastering both magic and people to uncover secrets.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DEC-9)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the College became home to the Ysmir Collective, a library rescued from destruction in the east. It became the cornerstone of academic life at the College, and scholars from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) descended on Winterhold to study its contents.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Being an independent organization, the College was not affected by the dissolution of the Mages Guild following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md),<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRLoading-15)</sup> and remained studiously neutral despite pressures from the Imperial [Synod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Synod) and the [College of Whispers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Whispers).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Faralda-16)</sup>

The College has struggled to maintain its prominence in Skyrim in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) for a number of different reasons. The local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), who remained skeptical of magic despite the prestige the College brought to their city, became even more distrustful after it came out conspicuously unscathed during the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md), perched on a surviving, inverted pyramid of rock that juts out of the sea.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OTGC-17)</sup> The College maintains that it was spared thanks to ancient magical protections not afforded to the surrounding city.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OTGC-17)</sup> However, the many troubles of the Fourth Era resulted in a significant loss of their history.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRSavos-2)</sup> The war against the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) caused much magical knowledge and talent to be destroyed, and so the College has dedicated itself to preserving what it can and passing it on to future practitioners of the arts.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EP-18)</sup> By the Fourth Era, the fabled Ysmir Collective library has shrunk in size and importance to a collection of rare books on display within the Arcanaeum.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-19)</sup><sup>:778</sup>

Despite a fallen prestige after the Great Collapse, several high-profile mages study at the College in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Some of these mages include the [Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) instructor Phinis Gestor, who was said to be one of the greatest conjurers in all of Skyrim, and Tolfdir, the seemingly foolish [Alteration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alteration) professor that is actually one of the best Alteration scholars in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRMirabelle-3)</sup> In addition, the mage [Arniel Gane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arniel_Gane) was a researcher of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) artifacts that managed to use [Keening](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keening), one of [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac)'s Tools, in conjunction with a specially designed [Soul Gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) to recreate the events surrounding the disappearance of the Dwemer. Unfortunately, Gane was too successful, as he disappeared as well, taking the knowledge of his great discovery with him.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

In 4E 201, the College discovered the ruins of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md) and found the [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md), revealing ancient secrets of such great magnitude that members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) returned to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and journeyed to the College for a rare intervention into the affairs of Tamriel. A Thalmor "advisor" to the College, [Ancano](Lore_Ancano.md), attempted to harness the power of the Eye for himself, killing several senior College faculty in the process. Ancano was eventually defeated and killed by the [soon-to-be Arch-Mage](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), as the [previous Arch-Mage](Lore_Savos_Aren.md) was among those killed by Ancano's treachery.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup>
