In the present day, it has come to refer to **Understone Keep** and the excavation site of the even deeper ruins found beneath the city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> The underground ruins were built in the Hoagen Kultorra style and contained dwellings, an armory, and a control room for the automated defenses.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SD-3)</sup> The modern-day Keep features the Jarl's Palace (christened by the Mournful Throne), a Dwemer Museum, and a wizard laboratory in the connected towers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup>

## History
Nchuand-Zel was built by the Dwemer, with the Understone Keep containing the "Mournful Throne" for the city's ruler.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Guard Dialogue-4)</sup> An area where two of the city's families lived held a tree outside the dwellings, mysteriously imported from the lands around Whiterun, with an unknown significance attached to it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SD-3)</sup> The clan inhabiting Nchuand-Zel was among those that took in the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) after the war with the Atmorans, recording their deal to blind them and subjugate them into servitude onto [a stone](Lore_Calcelmo%27s_Stone.md) in both the Dwemer and Falmer languages.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CS-1)</sup> When the entire Dwemer race disappeared in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), the Falmer gradually took control of the ruins as their own.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SD-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLE-5)</sup>

Around [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) took a caravan to [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), which briefly visited [Murtag Stronghold](Lore_Murtag_Stronghold.md) before being set upon by [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), forcing the survivors to flee towards [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) and abandon Terentius to the raiders. [Javad Tharn](Lore_Javad_Tharn.md) suggested that they take him down into Nchuand-Zel and sacrifice him to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). In return for a promise to paint the Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), he was spared the fate of his fellow captives, who were released and hunted by the Reachmen to honor [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) subsequently raided the Dwemer ruins in pursuit of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), where he swiftly killed Terentius' tormentors in the halls before teleporting them safely away to [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-6)</sup>

In the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Lady Belain, a Nighthollow vampire, staged an attack on Markarth by gathering her forces in lower Nchuand-Zel. She clashed with the forces of [Ard Caddach](Lore_Ard_Caddach.md), and, while her forces were temporarily fought off, the Reachman Arana was injured. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) learned of this from the ard and descended down into the ancient city to meet up with Arana and confront Belain. Arana tells the Vestige that Belain had escaped to the orrery in the Library of Arkthzand, but they need a keystone to follow her inside. After exploring the ruins of the ancient Dwemer city, The Vestige retrieved the Nchuand-Zel keystone, and journeyed back to the Library.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the scholar [Calcelmo of Markarth](Lore_Calcelmo_of_Markarth.md) opened an excavation into the derelict parts of Nchuand-Zel,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLE-5)</sup> and opened up the Dwemer Museum of Markarth to display noteworthy findings.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> [Stendarr's Hammer](Lore_Stendarr%27s_Hammer.md) resurfaced in Calcelmo's possession and was put on display in his private museum, though it was stolen soon after he acquired it.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
