| --- |
| [![SR-concept-Alduin's Wall (lo-res).jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/SR-concept-Alduin%27s_Wall_%28lo-res%29.jpg/800px-SR-concept-Alduin%27s_Wall_%28lo-res%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Alduin%27s_Wall_(lo-res).jpg) Skyrim |
| Alduin's Wall is a large [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) bas-relief found within [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md). It is considered to be one of the best preserved examples of early Akaviri sculptural relief, as well as a wonder of the ancient world. Although Akaviri in origin, craftsmen were said to have incorporated the flowing Nordic style into its design.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SREsbern-1)</sup> <br> <br> It is noted for recording the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md), which foretells five key historical events that would preface Alduin's return. The first half of Alduin's Wall depicts the historic events of the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) and the banishment of Alduin by the ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) heroes. The other half foretells the omens required to fulfill the prophecy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRAL-2)</sup> <br> <br> Emperor [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md) granted the Akaviri [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) permission to begin the construction of Alduin's Wall in [1E 2812](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2812). Its purpose was to record all of the dragonlore the Dragonguard had accumulated, and to preserve this knowledge against the forgetfulness of time. Akaviri craftsmen and artisans were summoned from temples across the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) to build the wall and after six years it was completed. Reman II visited the Sky Haven Temple to officially dedicate the wall and consecrate the Blood Seal at the temple's entrance in the presence of all the Dragonguard of Skyrim. Despite being a wonder of the ancient world, the location of Alduin's Wall was lost over time, and its very purpose was ironically forgotten.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Aot D-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SREsbern-1)</sup> The wall was re-discovered by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) and the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) during their quest to stop [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRAlduins Wall-4)</sup> |

| Battle of the Bones Memorial is a memorial depicting an axe wielding Nord Warrior found in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It marks one of [Unnvald Ironhand](Lore_Unnvald_Ironhand.md)'s most well-known battles. In a pass through the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md), he defended Fullhelm Fort from the [undead](Lore_Undead.md) conjured by necromancers of [Fort Virak](Lore_Fort_Virak.md), a keep on the other side of the pass in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BOTBM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TEGS-6)</sup> <br> <br> After the Battle of the Bones a memorial was built just south of Fullhelm Fort. Unnvald claimed that it was built to honor the deceased but his granddaughter believed it was made to taunt his enemies since it cannot be seen from Fullhelm Fort but is particularly visible from Fort Virak. After the battle, Unnvald left his sword, Bonebane, in a chest near the memorial, vowing to never pick it up again.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONHallfrida-7)</sup> | [![ON-place-Battle of the Bones Memorial.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/b/b3/ON-place-Battle_of_the_Bones_Memorial.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Battle_of_the_Bones_Memorial.jpg) Skyrim |

| ### Broken Statue of Fanacasecul | |
| --- | --- |
| An enchanted [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) statue was discovered outside the ruins of [Fanacasecul](Lore_Fanacasecul.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The ruined sculpture contained the spirit of an Ayleid who was magically trapped inside during the reign of the Ayleids in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). According to the spirit, the sculptor bore a grudge against them and bound their spirit to the statue during its unveiling. In its prime, Fanacasecul was a pleasure garden of the Ayleids, replete with flowering trees and plants. For many years the statue served as a source of amusement in these gardens and the spirit was frequently tormented by the citizenry.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONFanacasecul-8)</sup> <br> <br> After the defeat of the Ayleids in the First Era, Fanacasecul fell into ruins and the statue was largely destroyed except for the plinth. When it was re-discovered during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the trapped spirit spoke of its sole desire to be whole again, however, to do so required a skilled sculptor.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONFanacasecul-8)</sup> At some point between the mid-Second and late Third Era the statue was indeed restored to its former glory.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBFanacasecul-9)</sup> | [![ON-npc-Broken Statue.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/d/de/ON-npc-Broken_Statue.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Broken_Statue.jpg) ESO |

| ### [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(place).md) | |
| --- | --- |
| The Brothers of Strife is the name of the enormous stone statue towering over ancient [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) ruins in the [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It was named after the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(creature).md), two [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) creatures created there. The ruins around the statue was the location of the Chimer army's last stand after [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) invaded Stonefalls in the First Era. In desperation, the Chimer sacrificed two of their leaders, General [Balreth](Lore_Balreth.md) and Legate [Sadal](Lore_Sadal.md), in order to summon the Brothers of Strife. The Brothers drove the Nedes from Stonefells and the statue was later built to commemorate the Chimer's sacrifice.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SV-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> <br> <br> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a researcher from [House Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) named Sorcerer Vunal traveled to the Brothers of Strife to learn what had happened during the battle. The [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) later helped Vunal complete his research by establishing a link to the distant past and viewing past events.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONWindow-12)</sup> Around this time, large feldspar [crystal](Lore_Crystals.md) sculptures and chrysolite carved bookends depicting the Brothers of Strife could be found.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cont Brothersof Strife_Sculpture-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Cont Brothers Strife Bookends-14)</sup> | [![ON-place-Brothers of Strife.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/8/86/ON-place-Brothers_of_Strife.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Brothers_of_Strife.jpg) ESO |

| Two large [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) skull figureheads are found back-to-back at the apex of the bridge that crosses into the Nordic town of [Dragon Bridge](Lore_Dragon_Bridge.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The ancient and ornate bridge predates the settlement by untold years, and some scholars believe the skulls are in fact petrified skulls of actual dragons, due to the difference in stonework detail between the bridge's stone and the skulls. Other scholars however deride this as "poetic nonsense". Dragon Bridge is symbolic for the Nords and the skulls and bridge have inspired the imaginations of travelers for centuries.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> It also acts as a crossroads for the northern region, with it being the first line of defense for [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) and the neighboring holds.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> | |

| ### Great Statue of Irkngthand | |
| --- | --- |
| The Great Statue of Irkngthand, also called the Falmer Statue, is an enormous statue of an unknown [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) figure. It was built in the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city of [Irkngthand](Lore_Irkngthand.md) in ancient times, deep beneath Skyrim. During this time the remnants of the Snow Elf race had been enslaved by the Dwemer, but were able to build this statue in secret. The statue is the only known visual representation of a Snow Elf, before centuries of subterranean slavery twisted the race into the Falmer.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SRloading-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PRIMA-19)</sup><sup>:218</sup> A skullcap "crown" was carved atop its headpiece.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Falmer Statue Concept-20)</sup> <br> <br> In each of its eye sockets was a large priceless jewel, known later as "Eyes of the Snow Elves".<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TLW-21)</sup> Legends grew around the valuable gemstones and some believed that just one of the eyes was worth more than some thieves could earn in a lifetime.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRDelvin-22)</sup> <br> <br> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Guildmaster [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md) of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) gained access to the ruins of Irkngthand and attempted to steal the eyes after he betrayed his own comrades, but was stopped by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), and [Brynjolf](Lore_Brynjolf.md). The eyes were recovered and later put on display in the Riften guildhall.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRBlindsighted-23)</sup> | [![500x](https://images.uesp.net/c/c2/SR-misc-Snow_Elf_Statue.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Snow_Elf_Statue.png) Skyrim |

| ### [Leki's Blade](Lore_Leki%27s_Blade.md) | |
| --- | --- |
| According to legend, the enormous statue of a hand holding aloft a broken sword in the middle of the Alik'r Desert dates back to a mythical duel in ancient times. In the early First Era, a great Yokudan Ansei warrior named [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md) traveled to the surrounding oasis, claiming to match the gods in the way of the sword. Here he met the Yokudan goddess Leki, who had taken the shape of a warrior of sand, wielding a blade of the hardest rock. Some say that Leki herself drew him to the school to challenge and humble him. <br> <br> Rada al-Saran bowed to his opponent, and when it bowed back, he knew found an opponent worthy of his skill. For three days and three nights, they dueled with neither gaining the upper hand. Rada al-Saran was becoming tired from fighting, so in a last-ditch attempt he struck the weak point of Leki's sword. The blade broke in two, and a great sandstorm split the horizon. When it cleared, Leki had miraculously erected a shrine from the sands, and the surrounding oasis was said to have held the purest water in all Alik'r. A training school was later founded beneath the shrine and was open to students until at least the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONRah-30)</sup> | [![ON-place-Leki's Blade.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/3/35/ON-place-Leki%27s_Blade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Leki%27s_Blade.jpg) ESO |

He then put them on display in his private manor in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), before his untimely death that same year while exploring the ruins of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-38)</sup> | [![OB-quest-The Collector 02.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/e/e1/OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) Oblivion |
