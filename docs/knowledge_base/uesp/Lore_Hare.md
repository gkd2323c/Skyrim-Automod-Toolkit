Hares are small, long-eared animals similar to [rabbits](Lore_Rabbit.md), though they are sometimes incorrectly conflated with them by the denizens of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PPMH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> They can be found in [Summserset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KI-3)</sup> [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UOH-4)</sup> [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Brogdul-5)</sup> and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PPMH-1)</sup> Hares are also tangentially associated with the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FAHM-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TPOTH-7)</sup>

## Variants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-crown_store-Pale_Pass_Mountain_Hare.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Pale_Pass_Mountain_Hare.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Pale_Pass_Mountain_Hare.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Pale_Pass_Mountain_Hare.jpg) Pale Pass Mountain Hare [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-crown_store-Understone_Obsidian_Hare.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Understone_Obsidian_Hare.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Understone_Obsidian_Hare.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Understone_Obsidian_Hare.jpg) Understone Obsidian Hare Hares come in a variety of fur colors, including shades of brown and grey,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Carcass-8)</sup> as well as white with black spots and solid black.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PPMH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UOH-4)</sup>

Pale Pass Mountain Hares are found in the region of [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) of northern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). They were often sought out and captured by enterprising merchants and then sold, sometimes inaccurately referred to as bunnies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PPMH-1)</sup>

The Understone Obsidian Hares, common in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and named after [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md)'s [Understone Keep](Lore_Understone_Keep.md), were known to be kept as a pet by some [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). Similarly to Pale Pass Mountain Hares they were ocassionally incorrectly labeled as rabbits.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UOH-4)</sup>

## Culture

### General
Hares are sometimes associated with luck and have lent their name to various businesses.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LHW-9)</sup> They are frequently hunted for their meat,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-GH-10)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Carcass-8)</sup> and prized for their pelts.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Brogdul-5)</sup> Hare meat is used in a variety of dishes, including being served grilled or prepared with garlic sauce.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-GH-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LOS-11)</sup> Idioms comparing hares and [tortoises](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tortoise) to describe quick and slow individuals were in use.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AJ-12)</sup> The term "hare-brained" was used across many races and cultures of Tamriel, including the Altmer and the Khajiit, to describe foolish or reckless individuals.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Tufeh-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Vinilsare-14)</sup> Hares were also featured in jokes, such as: *What do you call a line of rabbits hopping backward? A receding hare-line.*<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CJ-15)</sup>

### Altmer
Hares were found in the Summerset Isles, where some dogs were known to chase them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KI-3)</sup> Idioms such as "quick as a spooked hare" were sometimes used to describe swift individuals.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-JS-16)</sup>

### Imperials
Idioms involving hares, such as "coursed down like a hare" were present in Imperial culture.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DOB-17)</sup>

### Nords
Idioms involving hares, such as "I run like a hare from the hound" were present in Nordic culture.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FFTT-18)</sup> Nords were known for shoes designed to track hares through the craggy terrain.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-HTB-19)</sup>

### Orcs
Orcs were known to craft shoes from hare leather, though they generally expressed a preference for echaterre leather.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Brogdul-5)</sup> This general sentiment is echoed in various texts, including a message authored by [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TWEM-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LE-21)</sup>

### Reachfolk
One Reachfolk [saying](Lore_Exclamations.md), *The swift hare survives where the dull ox cannot*, was used to emphasize the importance of a warrior's quickness.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Aydolan-22)</sup> They also sometimes described someone as aspooked hare, meaning fast and cautious. "Hare-bladder coward" was one of the insults used by the Reachfolk.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Arana-23)</sup> Some Reachfolk would sometimes compare a timid person to a hare turned human by a witch.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Eslyn-24)</sup>

### Daedra
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg) A Hare associated with Herma-Mora (ESO) Hares are mentioned frequently in the context of the Daedra. *[Spirit of the Daedra](Lore_Spirit_of_the_Daedra.md)*, a tome authored by a Daedric entity, compares the Daedra's view of mortals to the way men and mer regard animals like hares or [foxes](Lore_Fox.md). This perspective, focused on both the cunning and fragility of prey, was echoed by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) himself.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SOTD-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MB-26)</sup> The hare is also closely associated with [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), who is said to assume this form,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FAHM-6)</sup> as well as with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), who often designates the target of [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md) as the Hare.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TPOTH-7)</sup>

#### Hermaeus Mora
In one Nordic myth surrounding Ysgramor, Herma-Mora took the form of a hare.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FAHM-6)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HAFB-27)</sup>

#### Hircine
The Great Hunt, a sacred event dedicated to Hircine, is a grand chase in which the chosen prey is designated as the Hare, named after the animal hunted by the mortals.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TPOTH-7)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-THP-28)</sup> The term refers to anything that is hunted, including other animals like indriks,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Hanu-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-AUH-30)</sup> and even people.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-AOLH-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DTB-32)</sup> According to the rites of the Hunt, the Hare must be given at least a small chance of escape.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LFB-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-HH-34)</sup>

Some of Hircine's werewolf followers, such as [Vykosa the Ascendant](Lore_Vykosa.md), viewed their condition as that of an eternal Hare, forever hunted in an endless chase.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-THCC-35)</sup> Hircine himself referred to some mortals as hares, even when they were not targets of the Great Hunt.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Hircine OB-36)</sup>
