The borders can barely even be considered Argonia. The maps cannot be correct. The roots grow too deep and too wide for us to know the true Argonia.*
—Junia Severa<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Black Marsh is a dense swampland region of southeastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), home to the reptilian humanoid race of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and a race of sapient trees known as the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) refer to the region as **Argonia**, named after an ancient battlefield.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIEGTTBM-3)</sup> The Argonians do not have a word for their homeland in their [native language](Lore_Jel.md), in part because they do not consider it a singular nation. A member of the Argonian Gee-Rusleel tribe once used the wide-swamp gesture alongside the word **kronka-thatith** (roughly meaning "everything-egg") to describe the region, which is probably the closest non-Jel speakers will ever get in the way of a "true name" for the region. Many Argonians who live there struggle with the concept of it being a "province", as there is so much varied difference in each part of Black Marsh.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAMurk2-4)</sup>

The Argonians thrive in the foreboding swamps of Black Marsh, a lush and threatening land teeming with poisonous plants and violent predators. The region's tropical climate lends its plants the capacity to overturn all attempts to cultivate them. Foreign agricultural, colonial, and commercial ventures beyond the slave-trade have met with abject failure.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAA-5)</sup> The native Argonians organize themselves on the tribal level with success and efficiency, and were only loosely integrated into the ruling [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) when Cyrodiilic Emperors deigned to lay claim to the land.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIEGTTBM-3)</sup>

Aside from the Argonians, Black Marsh is host to a slew of other sapient peoples. The human inhabitants of Black Marsh ([Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) settlers from the Cyrodiilic Empire notwithstanding) include the [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md), the [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md), and the silver-skinned [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md). The [Orma](Lore_Orma.md), who are predisposed to blindness, are another Mannish race that inhabits the marsh.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLYFE-6)</sup> The serpent-bodied [Lamias](Lore_Lamia.md) and the vulpine [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md) (the latter of which lived along the southern coasts) are notable instances of humanoid and semi-humanoid races that live in the region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WWBM-7)</sup> The Kothringi and the Lilmothiit were driven to extinction by the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-9)</sup>

## Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-place-Shadowfen.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shadowfen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shadowfen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shadowfen.jpg) A view of the Shadowfen region in northern Black Marsh Black Marsh contains various regions, such as [Arnesia](Lore_Arnesia.md), the Blackwood Bog,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> [Gloomire](Lore_Gloomire.md) (or Gloommire),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BOG-11)</sup> [Middle Argonia](Lore_Middle_Argonia.md), [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md), [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md) and the [Ultherus Swamp](Lore_Ultherus_Swamp.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SFOT-12)</sup> [Norg-Tzel](Lore_Norg-Tzel.md) is a small island off the coast of the [Oliis Bay](Lore_Oliis_Bay.md), with similar climate and terrain to Murkmire.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) is an isolated tropical island within the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Justice The Fallen-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Jubilee2025-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Ta S-16)</sup> located south of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESODirect2025-17)</sup>

Black Marsh is located in the southeastern part of Tamriel, bordering [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) to the north and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to the west.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-POT-18)</sup> Most of the Argonians reside in the inland waterways and swamps of the southern interior. There are few roads, and the principal method of travel is by boat.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-POT-18)</sup> The [Panther River](Lore_Panther_River.md) starts in southern Cyrodiil and flows past the provincial border to the Black Marsh side of Blackwood.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONPanther-19)</sup> The [Onkobra River](Lore_Onkobra_River.md) is another river in that starts from the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) and runs eastward into central Black Marsh.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TAA3-20)</sup>

The soil composition of Black Marsh is varied. The roots of the Hist penetrate dark soil and grow through Black Marsh's soft, white limestone.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:34</sup><sup>:54</sup> Red clay is prevalent in Blackwood, particularly northeast of Gideon.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TAA3-20)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> [Washtongue powder](Lore_Washtongue_Powder.md) is a mineral native to Black Marsh that is used in pottery during the firing process.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MTC-23)</sup>

[Fort Swampmoth](Lore_Fort_Swampmoth.md) is an Imperial keep found somewhere in the province.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OBRadiant Convo-24)</sup> Other locations in Black Marsh of unknown placement include Agia Nero,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Hallgerd-25)</sup> Armanias,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TAC-26)</sup> the Lost City of Black Marsh,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> [Noota Nara](Lore_Noota_Nara.md),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dreekius-28)</sup> Seekhat-Xol,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-KFETA-29)</sup> Toll-Town,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-2)</sup> and Zurook.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-KTBTV1-30)</sup> [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) is perhaps Black Marsh's southernmost settlement, situated along the Oliis Bay.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup>

Gideon is a city with stone walls and largely Imperial architecture that was built atop the sunken Ayleid ruin of [Twyllbek](Lore_Twyllbek.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

In *[The Argonian Account](Lore_The_Argonian_Account.md)*, the poor condition of Black Marsh's roads is said to prevent food from arriving fresh at any destination.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TAA2-32)</sup> The main character, Decumus Scotti, describes the goods (grain, meat, and vegetables) of his caravan as being in various stages of rot.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TAA1-33)</sup> In the novella, impediments to road travel are abound: fast-growing grasses that cover important trade routes as quickly as they are cut down, insects known as fleshflies feast on the soft skins of non-natives, rivers that seasonally flood several feet,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TAA1-33)</sup> and bands of Naga raiders that target caravans.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TAA4-34)</sup>

Jarth's novella finds it a solely Imperial desire to make trade land-based, and to build large-scale plantation complexes, denoting it as a desire that has done little good for Black Marsh itself. The novella finishes by having Scotti clear up most issues relating to Imperial interests in Black Marsh, returning travel to its historical form, by boat and Underground Express, and by ceasing Imperial efforts to change Black Marsh's economy from one oriented on subsistence agriculture into one oriented on export crops. As Jarth writes, the situation is that "Black Marsh simply was, is, and always shall be unable to sustain a large-scale, cash-crop plantation economy." In returning to the traditional methods, Jarth's summation of Scotti's accomplishments concludes that the province "was better off than it had been in forty years".<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TAA4-34)</sup>

## History

### Merethic Era
The earliest inhabitants of the Black Marsh and its environs are believed to have been the Hist, great life-giving trees of unknown capacities. The region presently known as Black Marsh was once part of a much greater landmass within the domain of the Hist, but the greater part of the region was flooded during the wanderings of the humanoid Mer races. *[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)* states "The Hist were bystanders in the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) war, but most of their realm was destroyed as the war passed over it. A small corner of it survived to become Black Marsh in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), but most of their realm was sunk beneath the sea."<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Black Marsh also became home to groups of [Men](Lore_Men.md), including the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), as well as the fox-like [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/ON-place-Hissmir.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hissmir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hissmir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hissmir.jpg) An intact xanmeer in the village of Hissmir in southern Shadowfen At some point, the Argonians developed a unique civilization that inexplicably collapsed long before their first encounters with the other races of Tamriel. A number of forgotten Argonian tribes constructed countless elaborate stone structures across Black Marsh, ranging from solitary statues and [Wayshrines](Lore_Wayshrines.md) to sprawling stepped pyramids known as [xanmeers](Lore_Xanmeer.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIEGTTBM-3)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ESO-36)</sup> While the purpose of many of these structures, as well as the techniques used to carve and build them, have long been lost to history, many of these structures have been identified to be shrines erected in both reverence to and fear of [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), such as the Teeth of Sithis in central Murkmire<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-To S-37)</sup> or the vaults beneath the Sunscale Strand ruins in southern [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SS-38)</sup> It is wholly unknown how or why this ancient Argonian civilization disappeared, and the apparent disparity between its enduring, elegant stonework and the makeshift, bulbous mud structures and thatch houses that many subsequent Argonian tribes adopted has perplexed foreign scholars.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIEGTTBM-3)</sup> The mysterious event that caused this collapse and brought about a plethora of changes for Argonian civilization has come to be known as [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SWETL-39)</sup>

Following Duskfall, the Argonians of Black Marsh no longer worshipped Sithis the Destroyer and instead chose to follow the teachings of Raj-Sithis the Changer.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Waku Met-40)</sup> The Nisswo-Kings lost power and were replaced by the Clutch of Nisswo, a priesthood devoted to collecting and spreading the many contradictory truths of Sithis throughout Black Marsh.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Xukas-41)</sup> The majority of Argonians ceased building with stone to instead allow the marsh to reclaim what they built.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Talen Jush-42)</sup>

Perhaps most drastically, however, the Hist trees ceased communicating with Argonians altogether following Duskfall. Though the two parties still cared for one another, the Hist remained silent until an Argonian named Jaraleet finally renewed their relationship through re-discovering the practice of ingesting [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TLC-43)</sup> While outsiders may be perplexed and pity the Argonians for the loss of this grand civilization, the Argonians themselves viewed this as a just and fitting end for a society that worshipped only destruction. The majority of modern tribes freely chose to reject mimicking the masonry of their ancestors altogether so that they could fully embrace the change that is Sithis.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Uaxal-44)</sup>

### First Era
Some Argonians began to migrate throughout the rest of Tamriel in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), but these rare expatriates usually assimilated into their adopted homelands and shared little of their native ways with others. Following the expulsion of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) -worshipping [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) from Cyrodiil in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198), the Barsaebics took refuge in Black Marsh and established a number of colonies, including what would later become the cities of [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md) and [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ASIV-45)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SCOS-46)</sup> Consequently, the Barsaebics survived the wrath of [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) decades later, becoming one of the few remaining enclaves of Ayleid civilization in Tamriel before they too dwindled.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-place-Silyanorn_Ruins_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Silyanorn_Ruins_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Silyanorn_Ruins_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Silyanorn_Ruins_02.jpg) Silyanorn, a Barsaebic Ayleid ruin in Shadowfen Centuries later, the fringes of Black Marsh became safe havens for thieves, pirates, and other outlaws. The problem became so severe that in [1E 1033](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1033), Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) launched a campaign to root out the notorious human pirate ["Red" Bramman](Lore_Red_Bramman.md). The Imperial Fleet delved deeper into Black Marsh than any outsider had ever explored in their pursuit of Bramman, eventually cornering and killing him at his bandit kingdom near modern-day [Blackrose](Lore_Blackrose.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIEGTTBM-3)</sup> The Empire attempted to civilize the pirate-infested south, gleaning the first detailed accounts of native Argonian culture in the process, but ultimately settled on eradicating the pirates before building prisons for their least desirable convicts, and later abandoned Black Marsh entirely.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup>

In [1E 2811](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2811), the last organized army of Argonians was easily defeated by the legions of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) at the [Battle of Argonia](Lore_Battle_of_Argonia.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup> Within the next few decades, Black Marsh was nominally incorporated into the Second Empire as a province. Emperor [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md) squandered thousands of troops and years of effort in his attempts to conquer Black Marsh; in [1E 2837](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2837) he declared the region officially annexed, despite only extending the Empire's reach into the northern and eastern marches.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-RTLOA-47)</sup> Black Marsh remained a province until the Empire's collapse in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-2)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg) Stormhold, a Dunmeri town built atop an Argonian village built atop an Ayleid ruin The Knahaten Flu started in [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md) and persisted for 41 years, from [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560) to [2E 601](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_601). The flu took its toll on the region, driving the Kothringi and the Lilmothiit to extinction. The native Argonians were largely immune to this plague, which led some to falsely believe it was fabricated by an Argonian shaman.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup> To the Argonians, the Flu was merely a mild sickness which they called the Half-Swim Sniffles.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAMurk2-4)</sup> The last recorded case of the flu was in [2E 603](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_603), after a group of Kothringi tribesmen fled Black Marsh by boat and disappeared.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TDC-48)</sup>

During the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) of [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), remembered as the "Liberation War" among the Argonians, a phalanx of Argonian Shellbacks unexpectedly came to the aid of an allied force of beleaguered [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and Dunmer who were struggling to contain the cornered [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) army.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-JTSK-49)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TSAI-50)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FATS-51)</sup> With the Argonians' help, the Akaviri were slaughtered to the last man before their ships could arrive to evacuate them.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TSAI-50)</sup> The battle engendered a newfound respect between the three races, which resulted in the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-UA-52)</sup>

Under the Pact, Argonian slaves in Morrowind were freed and Black Marsh gained a level of autonomy not seen in centuries. Despite all Argonians being welcomed into the Pact, only the tribes of [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md), [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), and [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) decided to join.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FATS-51)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-GTTEP-53)</sup> The Argonians established a more formal government in the form of the vicecanons, magistrates who administered major settlements in Black Marsh and coordinated with emissaries from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and Morrowind.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SCOS-46)</sup> Shadowfen was invaded in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), which sought to sever the Argonians' connection to the Hist and thereby drive the entire race to extinction. This plot was foiled, and the allied Argonian tribes later reaffirmed their commitment to the Pact.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ESO-36)</sup> It is unknown how long this period of relative independence for Black Marsh lasted, but the Ebonheart Pact ceased to exist by the end of the Second Era.

[Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) brought Black Marsh into the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). Though it is not directly indicated, a passage in the book *[A Short History of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Short_History_of_Morrowind)* implies that Black Marsh was acquired by treaty or pact rather than by military dominion. "[Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) was the last of the provinces to submit to Tiber Septim; like Black Marsh, it was never successfully invaded, and was peacefully incorporated by treaty into the Empire as the Province of Morrowind."<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ASHOW-54)</sup>

Imperial rule changed little of the tribal organization of Black Marsh's society, but wrought disastrous changes to its economy as the small farms of Argonian peasants were replaced with massive cash-crop farms and traditional forms of transportation were usurped by foreign methods unsuited to the native terrain. These changes were eventually remedied as the land's administration chose to return to previous methods of business. There have been some small attempts to convert the residents of Black Marsh to the Cyrodilic pantheon of the Nine Divines, but most have been unsuccessful.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TAA4-34)</sup>

### Third Era
During the later years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Black Marsh came into conflict with its Dunmer neighbors to the north in the bloody [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md). The conflict started as a slave revolt in the lands of [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), and escalated into a full war when the Argonians captured, tortured, and murdered the Dunmer merchant [Roris](Lore_Roris.md), who was declared a saint by the Dunmer Temple.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PGE3M-55)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-LOTS-56)</sup> However, the armies of Black Marsh were defeated, allowing Morrowind to expand its territory into the south.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup> Interestingly, documents prepared for [Lord Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) by the Tribunal Temple imply that Black Marsh retains control of some historically Dunmer provinces, despite the Dunmer victory in the Arnesian War. In detailing Dagoth Ur's plans, the Tribunal Temple noted that he wished to "recover ancient territories stolen by [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and Argonia".<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-DUP-57)</sup> The Dunmer and the Argonians, perhaps unsurprisingly, have a "long standing and bitter hatred for one another".<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-POT-18)</sup>

### Fourth Era
After the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) and the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) during the beginning of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Black Marsh seceded from the Empire along with [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-RT-58)</sup> Morrowind was substantially weakened when the [Ministry of Truth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Vivec_Ministry) became unstable and crashed into [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), destroying much of the island. The Argonians of Black Marsh invaded, at first successfully pushing deep into the country, but then being driven off by the forces of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md). In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), a floating, soul-fueled city known as Umbriel moved over the city of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) after being summoned through Black Marsh by the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md) and a rogue Hist tree, destroying and killing all foreign entities and 'assimilated' Argonians.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup> In the late 2nd century of the Fourth Era, the [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md) served the [king of Black Marsh](Lore_Kingdom_of_Black_Marsh.md), who ruled over the province.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-SRVeezara-59)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-60)</sup>

## Fauna
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/LG-cardart-Shocking_Wamasu.png/200px-LG-cardart-Shocking_Wamasu.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Shocking_Wamasu.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Shocking_Wamasu.png) A wamasu Black Marsh generally has a southerly marsh-like climate along with the flora and fauna that would be associated with such an environment. Waughin Jarth's *[The Argonian Account](Lore_The_Argonian_Account.md)* suggests a climate similar to that of neighboring Blackwood, the swampy region east of Leyawiin, though with denser vegetation and less hospitable wildlife.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TAA1-33)</sup>

Black Marsh is home to many different reptilian species. The [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md) is a large predatory reptile with a tough hide that spits lightning. They can be found in many regions throughout the swamp.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-61)</sup> [Bog dogs](Lore_Bog_Dog.md) are mammalian pack animals found in Blackwood,<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-62)</sup> though they aren't related to actual dogs.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-63)</sup> [Crocodiles](Lore_Crocodile.md), known locally as *rormasu*, are common throughout Black Marsh.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-64)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-KCAHC-65)</sup> [Snakes](Lore_Snake.md) are prevalent across the province, including the [giant snake](Lore_Giant_Snake.md) of Shadowfen,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-66)</sup> and the [pukta snake](Lore_Snake.md#Pukta_Snake) and [wasso hedge snake](Lore_Snake.md) which are used in various Argonian dishes.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-67)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ACA3-68)</sup> The lethally poisonous [red hooped tree-viper](Lore_Snake.md#Red_Hooped_Tree-Viper) looks nearly identical to the wasso hedge snake, so picking a trustworthy snake dealer is paramount.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ACA3-68)</sup> [Willow vipers](Lore_Snake.md#Willow_Viper) are good at camouflage, and best left alone.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Alten-69)</sup> [Moon-adders](Lore_Snake.md#Moon-Adder) are venomous vipers. Argonians are resistant to their venom, and use it to make [daril](Lore_Drugs.md#Daril).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup> [<sup>what page?</sup>](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Pages_Missing_Data) The hulking [haj mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md) and the meat-eating [painted tortoise](Lore_Turtle.md) are animals commonly found in Black Marsh.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-70)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TFOSC4-71)</sup>

Lizards are diverse in Black Marsh. Some scholars believe the tree-dwelling [ancestor lizards](Lore_Ancestor_Lizard.md) are the ancestors of Argonians.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-72)</sup> Strange scaly quadrupeds known as [bear-lizards](Lore_Lizard-Steed.md) (or "lizard bears"<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-73)</sup>) reside in south and western Black Marsh.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-74)</sup> Argonians call these animals "useful lizards", believing that these lizards underwent evolutionary changes similar to themselves.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-75)</sup> Other lizard species include Murkmire's [hedge gecko](Lore_Hedge_Gecko.md),<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-76)</sup> the [water lizard](Lore_Bestiary_L.md#Lizard)<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-KTRM2-77)</sup> and the tiny [xaht lizard](Lore_Bestiary_L.md#Lizard) of Blackwood.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-78)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg) Swamp jellies of various breeds, related to netches Some of the creatures that live in Black Marsh are theorized to be related to animals found in Morrowind.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-LAMQA-79)</sup> [Hackwings](Lore_Hackwing.md) are giant birds with long, saw-like beaks nearly the size of the rest of their bodies. They are similar in appearance to [cliff striders](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md), and are vicious predators that take advantage of lazy prey.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TAA2-32)</sup> [Swamp jellies](Lore_Swamp_Jelly.md) are floating creatures with elastic, gelatinous bodies that are related to [netches](Lore_Netch.md). Argonians give the various breeds flavor-themed names based on their hue.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-80)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-LAMQA-79)</sup> [Alits](Lore_Alit.md), [Kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md), and [guar](Lore_Guar.md) can also be found in Shadowfen, close to the Morrowind border.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-81)</sup> [Mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md), [burglar crabs](Lore_Crab.md#Burglar_Crabs),<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-82)</sup>, [swamp shrimp](Lore_Shrimp.md)<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-83)</sup> and [dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) are crustaceous creatures that live in Black Marsh.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-84)</sup> The [three-clawed mudcrab](Lore_Mudcrab.md#Three-Clawed_Mudcrab) is a rare variant of mudcrab in Black Marsh.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-KTRM2-77)</sup>

[Fleshflies](Lore_Fleshflies.md), blood-colored, sand-grain-sized insects which feast on human flesh, inhabit Black Marsh and southeastern Cyrodiil. Those unlucky enough to be caught by their swarms may find their faces deformed as the creatures eat their skin.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TAA1-33)</sup> [Giant wasps](Lore_Giant_Wasp.md) can be found in the northern region of Shadowfen,<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-KCAHC-65)</sup> while [hoarvors](Lore_Hoarvor.md) and [kotu gava](Lore_Kotu_Gava.md) live in the Blackwood Bog to the southwest.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-85)</sup> [Spear-ants](Lore_Ant.md),<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-86)</sup> [wood ants](Lore_Ant.md),<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-87)</sup> [bog ants](Lore_Ant.md), and [marsh centipedes](Lore_Centipede.md)<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-KTRM2-77)</sup> are other bugs found in the province.

Black Marsh is also home to [rootworms](Lore_Rootworm.md), which are referred to as the "Underground Express" by the locals. Rootworms are useful to travelers, swallowing them whole and carrying them with the current. The digestive systems of rootworms are apparently quite slow, and travelers "could live in a rootworm's belly for months." All one needs to do to get off the Underground Express is keep an eye out for pockets of air (signified by bubbles) through the beast's transparent flesh, and push upwards to break through their malleable skin.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TAA3-20)</sup>

[Slugs](Lore_Slug.md) are common in Black Marsh. The Saxhleel use them in cooking, as well as an art known as "slugshaping".<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-FWTL-88)</sup> Known species include the Jassa Red,<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-FWTL-88)</sup> Bearded Blue, Black-Banded Slider, and King Yellow.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ACA1-89)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg) A voriplasm [Voriplasms](Lore_Voriplasm.md) are blobs of green slime that can strip creatures down to the bone in seconds. Also of note are [swamp leviathans](Lore_Swamp_Leviathan.md), which are massive reptiles with tusks and sharp teeth.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TAA4-34)</sup> [Will-o'-wisps](Lore_Will-o-the-Wisp.md) are deadly glowing orbs of light found in the deep swamps, and shines are their wispy, yellow, and less dangerous cousins from Murkmire.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:17</sup> Coral reefs grow along the coast of Black Marsh, west of Lilmoth. [Venin-bats](Lore_Bat.md) and [blood moths](Lore_Moth.md#Blood_Moth) are a nuisance to the residents of Murkmire.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:46</sup> [Miregaunts](Lore_Miregaunt.md), great behemoths woven from roots, wander the deep swamps.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-90)</sup> [Marsh Giants](Lore_Marsh_Giant.md) are woodland spirits native to Black Marsh, described as "the [spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md)' prodigious cousins". Marsh Giants are essential to the ecosystem; as they till soil, fell dead trees to allow new vegatation to flourish, encourage vital moss growth, encourage avian diversity by warding off feral cats, combat invasive fungi, and most importantly, hinder loggers, miners and other tradesfolk from harming the marsh.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-91)</sup>

[Crows](Lore_Crow.md) are prevalent in Gloomire, like most everywhere else in Tamriel. They make nests on treetops, as their black feathers allow them to blend well into the dense forests and marshlands.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BOG-11)</sup> [Black cockerels](Lore_Chicken.md#Black_Cockerel), which live near White Rose, eat toxic [beetles](Lore_Beetle.md) that turn their flesh black.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TFOSC4-71)</sup> Betva-hens are possibly large birds, whose feathers are often worn by Argonian *naheesh* elders.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Antiquity PWF-92)</sup> Another avian species found in Black Marsh is the [marsh-robin](Lore_Marsh-Robin.md).<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-93)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/ON-creature-Death_Hopper_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Death_Hopper_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Death_Hopper_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Death_Hopper_02.jpg) A death hopper, about to spit poison Many [frog](Lore_Frog.md) and toad species can be found in Black Marsh. [Aojee toads](Lore_Aojee_Toad.md),<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-94)</sup> [dragon frogs](Lore_Dragon_Frog.md), [indigo tree frogs](Lore_Indigo_Tree_Frog.md), [moss-foot croakers](Lore_Moss-Foot_Croaker.md), [ruby hoppers](Lore_Ruby_Hopper.md), and [sun-blessed toads](Lore_Sun-Blessed_Toad.md) can be found near Lilmoth;<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-95)</sup> while [glowing frogs](Lore_Glowing_Frog.md) and the large, carnivorous [death hoppers](Lore_Bestiary_D.md#Death_Hopper) can be found near Gideon.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-96)</sup> The nettle-toad is a species that lives in Murkmire and eats slugs; the Saxhleel who leaves a bowl of slugpaste unattended may return to find that a nettle-toad has eaten their fare.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-ESOBeehuna-97)</sup> Singing frogs are found near the Panther River. These frogs make unique chirping calls, and are prized by Argonian musicians who use them for *vossa-satls*.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-98)</sup>

[Flounder](Lore_Flounder.md), [trogfish](Lore_Trogfish.md),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:197</sup> [harpoon-fish](Lore_Fish_H.md#Harpoon-Fish),<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-99)</sup> [slaughterfish](Lore_Slaughterfish.md), [hog-fish](Lore_Fish_H.md#Hog-Fish),<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-100)</sup>, [bog-eels](Lore_Fish_B.md#Bog-Eel)<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-101)</sup> and [swamp eels](Lore_Fish_E.md#Eel)<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-102)</sup> are all examples of fish that live in Black Marsh. [Osheeja gars](Lore_Fish_G.md#Gar) are large river fish used by the [Naka-Desh](Lore_Naka-Desh.md) tribe to catch smaller fish.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-103)</sup> [Ripper eels](Lore_Ripper_Eel.md) are trained to hunt Argonians illegally crossing canals in Lilmoth, though this can be prevented by rubbing oneself with eel-slime.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup> [<sup>what page?</sup>](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Pages_Missing_Data) The [geels-ha butcher eel](Lore_Geels-ha_Butcher_Eel.md) is a massive river predator of Kothringi folklore, that could swallow haj mota whole. [Bog-blue jasper](Lore_Jasper.md) is said to come from their gizzards.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-104)</sup> [Sea-drakes](Lore_Sea-Drake.md) and [sharks](Lore_Fish_S.md#Shark) also swim the southern seas.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:52</sup> [Pad-sa](Lore_Pad-sa.md) are a species of scrappy sharks usually found along the coast, from the mouth of the [Niben River](Lore_Niben_River.md) to the Topal Bay.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-105)</sup>

[Black marsh cucumbers](Lore_Sea_Cucumber.md) and [black marsh-squids](Lore_Squid.md) are some examples of the aquatic animals native to Black Marsh that aren't fish.

The [Helstrom crocodile](Lore_Crocodile.md), a much larger species of crocodile; the [wraxu](Lore_Wraxu.md), a cousin of the [terror bird](Lore_Terror_Bird.md) described by *Varid's Guide to Unusual Birds* as being as tall as [mammoths](Lore_Mammoth.md) with tails as long as wagon tongues; and a species of [long-legged swamp wolves](Lore_Wolf.md#Long-Legged_Swamp_Wolf) all went extinct following the [Blackwater War](Lore_Blackwater_War.md) and the Great Burn of [1E 2828](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2828).<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-106)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Antiquity PWF-92)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ESOWTS-107)</sup> Features of the extinct swamp wolf could be seen in the [Blackwood dire wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ESOWTS-107)</sup>

[Swamp-foxes](Lore_Fox.md)<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-108)</sup> and [mud-moles](Lore_Mole.md)<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Alten-69)</sup> are examples of other animals that live in Murkmire.

## Flora
[Dragon's Tongue](Lore_Dragon%27s_Tongue.md) and the [Somnalius Fern](Lore_Somnalius_Fern.md), which are used in alchemical brews, are native to Black Marsh. Dragon's Tongue is a fern-like herb lethal to the touch. It gains its name from the fire-red fronds that surround its golden efflorescence. The Somnalius Fern is light green and delicate and crumbles at the touch. It has the effect of fatiguing the creature that inhales it.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SFOT-12)</sup> Scaldroot is an herb that, when brewed, produces foul-tasting tea that aids in cleaning the teeth.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ETJ-109)</sup> [Dragonthorn](Lore_Flora_D.md#Dragonthorn) also grows in Black Marsh. The ancient Argonians believed that chewing dragonthorn leaves helps the body prepare healthy eggs.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-110)</sup>

[Flint vine](Lore_Flora_F.md#Flint_Vine) is a sturdy plant from Murkmire that can be woven into ropes or ladders.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-111)</sup> [Mangrove](Lore_Flora_M.md#Mangrove) trees thrive along the western coast of Black Marsh.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:50</sup> A thick wall of mangroves thrives along the shore about ten miles from Lilmoth.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:53</sup> They form a nigh-impenetrable wall, and look like thousands of giant spiders with their legs interlocked. An Argonian folktale claims that's exactly what they once were. These giant spiders apparently garnered the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md)'s wrath during an ancient conflict, and were transformed into trees.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:52</sup> [Sleeping palms](Lore_Flora_P.md#Palm_Tree) and [bamboo](Lore_Flora_B.md#Bamboo) thrive in and around Lilmoth, as does [lucan mold](Lore_Flora_L.md#Lucan_Mold), which glows with pale phosphorescence.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:17</sup> Cattails grow near the water in Murkmire.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Alten-69)</sup> Stink weeds and swamp grass are plants native to Murkmire that grow back later when cut down.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-112)</sup> [Nightbloom](Lore_Flora_N.md#Nightbloom) vine, which grows in secluded bogs, is precious to the [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md).<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-113)</sup>

[Plums](Lore_Flora_P.md#Plum) grow near White Rose, known to Argonians as "lizardfruit".<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-114)</sup> Krona nuts are a nut whose husk is used in Saxhleel coming-of-age rituals.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-KTRM2-77)</sup> Wasso nut leaves are used in cuisine, and worn as protective padding.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ACA1-89)</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-115)</sup> Wasso fruit is a delicacy in Blackwood.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-116)</sup>

[Bog willow](Lore_Flora_B.md#Bog_Willow)<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-117)</sup> and [cypress](Lore_Flora_C.md#Cypress)<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-118)</sup> are two species of tree that grow in the marsh.
