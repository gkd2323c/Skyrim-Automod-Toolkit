[4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Raldbthar is an ancient [dwemer ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) found on the slopes of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Built amidst the snowy heights of [Shearpoint](Lore_Shearpoint.md), Raldbthar was once a powerful city-state that served as the primary source for the blue crystal, [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md). It was one of four city-states in a pocket empire that spanned across modern-day Skyrim, led by the [Kragen Clan](Lore_Kragen.md) of [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAW-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KJ-2)</sup> Raldbthar is one of four cities that contains a great lift to Mzark Cavern in the underground world of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md).

## History
[![SR-book-Dlc1ld mapsmall.png](https://images.uesp.net/2/2a/SR-book-Dlc1ld_mapsmall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Dlc1ld_mapsmall.png) Raldbthar was built sometime in the early First Era, quite possibly as early as [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAnti Tool-3)</sup> by a clan of Dwemer that expanded into Skyrim following the Kragen Clan.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AP-4)</sup>. Along with the aforementioned clan and two other cities, [Bthar-Zel](Lore_Bthar-Zel.md) and [Mzulft](Lore_Mzulft.md), they formed an alliance to harvest and utilize Aetherium. Raldbthar served as the main mining operation for the Aetherium, building a mine that ran deep into Shearpoint, where it would send the raw material to the Aetherium Forge of Bthalft, a magical forge in [the wilds](Lore_Ragged_Hills.md) of [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KJ-2)</sup> Raldbthar Dwarves were believed to experiment with devices designed to extract the Aetherium before devising heavier machinery. By roughly 1E 240, they used personal extraction tools as well aside from the tools for large-scale extraction.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAnti Tool-3)</sup>

This alliance ended shortly after and each of the four cities waged conflict in what is known as the Aetherium Wars, as each city sought ownership of the Forge. The decades of battle had severely weakened the nations, paving the way for High King [Gellir](Lore_Gellir.md)'s conquest over the Dwarves, and the Dwarves of Raldbthar were forced out of their city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAW-1)</sup> The Dwemer were able to return to their homes, but like the rest of their race, they disappeared, leaving behind whatever Aetherium they acquired.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAW-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AP-4)</sup> Raldbthar was subsequently sacked and later abandoned much like other Dwemer cities.

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the ruins were occupied by a large bandit clan led by the crime lord, Alain Dufont, who managed to steal a large sum of wealth from the [Shatter-Shield Clan](Lore_Shatter-Shield_Clan.md) of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), including their family heirloom, the enchanted war hammer, Aegisbane. Dufont's streak of luck ended when he was murdered by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) for a contract from his former deceived lover.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRMourning-5)</sup> The Last Dragonborn traveled to this location and discovered a shard of the Aetherium Crest within the Deep Market of the ruins.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LTTA-6)</sup>
