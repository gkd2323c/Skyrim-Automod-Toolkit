[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-place-Dragonstar.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dragonstar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dragonstar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dragonstar.jpg) Dragonstar circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/SK-misc-Dragonstar_Banner.png/150px-SK-misc-Dragonstar_Banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Dragonstar_Banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Dragonstar_Banner.png) Banner of Dragonstar circa [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397) Dragonstar (historically called **Drangstor**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGMap-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-HRP-2)</sup> is a city-state in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), just south of the border with [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It is located on the foothills of the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains), in the region of [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn). Like much of Craglorn, Dragonstar is very cosmopolitan compared to the rest of Hammerfell, often inhabited by those deemed "unfit" for life in the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Dragonstar is neighbored by several settlements, including Dragon Gate to the southeast and [Heldorn Mount](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heldorn_Mount) to the southwest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Map Arena-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/SK-place-Dragonfields.png/200px-SK-place-Dragonfields.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-place-Dragonfields.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-place-Dragonfields.png) Dragonfields - Entrance to Dragonstar In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it was a small, fortified settlement dominated by [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) miners and mercenaries. The Dragonstar Caravan Company began operations there, but later moved its headquarters to [Ska'vyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ska%27vyn). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the local miners were contracted with extracting fast amounts of [nirncrux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirncrux), known as "red brittle" to the locals. Although abundant and seemingly inert, the stone is very dangerous to mine due to the toxic fumes released. The patron of this operation was [Cassipia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cassipia), who aided and later betrayed the dangerous [Scaled Court](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scaled_Court). Nirncrux mining presumably halted following her death and the destruction of the Court. The entrance to an [otherworldly arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonstar_Arena) also appeared in the mountains north of the settlement, and before the trade routes to Dragonstar were cut off, the site was used to host a grand melee every year.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESO-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONLothakar-7)</sup>

By the time of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Dragonstar was an active settlement, being known as the *"home of the Sacred Rage."* It was ruled by King Dhatan, and it had a rivalry with [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [Skaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skaven).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Arena-8)</sup>

During the [War of Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md), the forces of Skyrim advanced into Hammerfell and besieged the city, eventually establishing a separate government within the city. During this time, the city was divided into western and eastern sections, walled off from each other, each with their own government. Dragonstar West was still under the control of Hammerfell, while Dragonstar East was controlled by Skyrim. An atmosphere of deep mutual distrust prevailed between the two sides. The city had an arena pit, a prison and guild halls for the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md). [Shadow Gates](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Magic) could also be found in the city.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

The occupation persisted after the end of the war, and by [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432) the situation remained unchanged,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3S-10)</sup> although [contemporary](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maps#Anthology_Map) [maps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maps#Oblivion_Codex_Map) continued to show the whole city as belonging to Hammerfell *[de jure](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/de_jure)*. The history of Dragonstar after the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) is unknown.

## Notes
- The [Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ansei) warrior [Satameh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satameh) was known as the "Tigress of Dragonstar".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONSai-12)</sup>
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Dragonstar's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Sacred Rage".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-13)</sup> This was finally used in *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)*, where the Dragonstar Sacred Rage arena team competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
