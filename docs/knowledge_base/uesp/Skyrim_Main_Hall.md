# Main Hall

[![SR-book-Byohhub.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/SR-book-Byohhub.png/200px-SR-book-Byohhub.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Byohhub.png) The **Main Hall** is the first step in expanding your home after [building](Skyrim_Construction.md) the basic [Small House layout](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Small_House_Layout). It contains three separate areas: the [Entryway](#Main_Hall:_Entryway), the general space of the Main Hall, and the [Cellar](#Main_Hall:_Cellar).

The following is a list of possible furniture items and decorations which can be added by using the carpenter's workbenches in the Main Hall. It is not necessary to purchase or build all furnishing options; you can pick and choose to better dictate style. However, all furnishing options are in a pre-arranged location and only ingredients and standard items can be moved. Oftentimes, the name of the furnishing option only indicates the primary piece of furniture, and actually includes various additional [containers](Skyrim_Containers.md) and accessories.

## Main Hall
The general space of the Main Hall is split up into three areas, distinguished by their own individual carpenter's workbenches. The first area is the large two-level room with the staircases. The carpenter's workbench in the southwest corner of the first level controls the furnishings for both levels of this room. There is a second carpenter's workbench on the north side of the first level (which contains the door in the floor which leads to the Cellar). A third carpenter's workbench can be found on the north side of the second level, which actually contains three of its own distinct sections, notable by the walls dividing them.

Each of the three areas distinguished by their own individual carpenter's workbenches have been organized into three separate tables which include the count of total materials for furnishing options in that specific area.

## Main Hall: Cellar

- *"Cellar" redirects here. For other uses, see [Cellar (disambiguation)](Skyrim_Cellar_(disambiguation).md).*

The Cellar is accessible via a small trapdoor in the floor of the small north room on the first level of the Main Hall. The cellar itself is effectively divided into two rooms, one on the north and one on the south. The north room contains the ladder which exits to the Main Hall.

A number of miscellaneous items are added with various furnishing options, but only notable items are specified on this table. Common items and ingredients are not listed here. Note that the icon displayed for each furnishing option does not necessarily represent what is actually built in your home.

## Notes
- After building the Cellar addition, you may experience a random [skeever](Skyrim_Skeever.md) infestation in that part of your home. This is the only type of enemy spawn which occurs indoors, as all other random enemies spawn outside (see [Lakeview Manor](Skyrim_Lakeview_Manor.md#Bugs) page for a related bug). When this happens, other residents of your home may comment on the noise coming from downstairs. When you have killed all but one of the skeevers, the last skeever will not attack and simply run from you until you either kill it or sheathe your weapons. If you sheathe your weapons, it will become non-hostile.
- After the roof of each Main Hall is built, a hawk nest will appear on it, giving you access to free [hawk feathers](Skyrim_Hawk_Feathers.md) and [hawk eggs](Skyrim_Hawk%27s_Egg.md) at Lakeview Manor and Heljarchen Hall, and a Rock Warbler nest with access to free hawk feathers and [Rock Warbler eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rock_Warbler_Egg) at Windstad Manor. You can build a tower add-on, jump from a nearby rock formation, or use [Whirlwind Sprint](Skyrim_Whirlwind_Sprint.md) to get to them. - Version 3.0.11 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch) replaces the Rock Warbler nest at Windstad Manor with a Hawk's nest. You can get to it using the 3 methods mentioned above.
- You can pay your [Steward](Skyrim_Personal_Steward.md) to furnish the Main Hall, but you'll still need to build all furnishings in the Cellar by yourself.
- Quests that flag a common item as a quest item can prevent you from building furniture that requires that item. This can include named items, such as those created by [The Fishing Job](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Fishing_Job) and similar quests.

## Bugs
- The Skeever infestation activates in all Hearthfire houses even if you build the cellar in only one of them. Your spouse/children will comment about strange noises, even if that house has no cellar. To make them stop you need to build the cellar in the house they are currently living in and get rid of them.
- The pair of wall sconces on either side of the doorway in the north room will not have the sconce built on the west side of the doorway at Lakeview Manor.
- The carpenter's workbench for the cellar at Windstad Manor will not be removed on selecting the remove workbench option at the drafting table.
- You cannot mount fish on either of the two fish plaques added by the [Fishing](Skyrim_Fishing.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) if you have already done so in another house. See [this section](Skyrim_Fishing.md#Bugs) for details. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.629, fixes this bug.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) This bug is also fixed by the [Fish Plaque Fix](https://www.nexusmods.com/skyrimspecialedition/mods/67227) mod, but in a different way.
- When building the Cellar, crafting the Shrine Base and some of the individual deity shrines *before* other furnishings can result in the workbench subsequently only listing the remaining non-built shrines. <sup>**?**</sup> - This can be circumvented by activating (praying at) one of the shrines and then reloading the workbench interface immediately afterward. In most cases, crafting *all* the possible shrines will restore the normal functions of the workbench.
- The safes in the cellar can remove the stolen tag from items. <sup>**?**</sup>
- With the [Fishing](Skyrim_Fishing.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed, the second set of Wall Shelves (2) for the Cellar is broken: the center shelf is no longer present (the entrance to the east hallway is in its place), while the side shelf is, oddly, already present when first entering the cellar; this upgrade option remains available at the workbench, and its entry will be removed from the list when selected for construction, but doing so will provide no new furniture or items. <sup>**?**</sup>
