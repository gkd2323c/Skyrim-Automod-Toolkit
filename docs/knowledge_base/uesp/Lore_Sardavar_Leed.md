[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/OBR-place-Sardavar_Leed.jpg/200px-OBR-place-Sardavar_Leed.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Sardavar_Leed.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Sardavar_Leed.jpg) Sardavar Leed at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) Sardavar Leed (also **Sardarvar Leed**),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAa-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:123</sup> sometimes shortened to **Sard**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAa-1)</sup> was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) settlement located south of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md) in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), in an area known as the Bloody Grasslands.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBSL-4)</sup>

Sard was known for the breeding of [men](Lore_Man.md) by the Ayleids. Many tribes of men were brought from all across the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md), and some were even imported from southern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They were bred for slave labor or to be tortured for the amusement of the Ayleids. It was here that the future Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) was brought as a youth and raised.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAa-1)</sup> She prayed to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) for freedom from Ayleid slavery,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAo K-5)</sup> and in [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242), she escaped from captivity and launched the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), which ultimately led to the fall of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3C-6)</sup> At some point the settlement's name changed to the modern "Sardavar Leed", and it was eventually abandoned.

An [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) fort was built east of the ruins and was named [Fort Alessia](Lore_Fort_Alessia.md), likely in Alessia's honor.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBFA-7)</sup> By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the ruins were occupied by a group of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) summoners led by Velandare.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins of Sardavar Leed had been taken over by a hostile group of conjurers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBSL-4)</sup>

In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), Crown Prince [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) and his guards set up camp by the springs near the ruins when they were betrayed. Led by one of Attrebus' guards and lovers, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) named Radhasa, the group was massacred by a group of mercenaries hired to assassinate the prince. Radhasa spared Attrebus' life and brought him south, intending to sell him in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:124-127</sup> The ghosts of those massacred helped the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md) in eventually discovering that Attrebus still lived.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:139-141</sup>
