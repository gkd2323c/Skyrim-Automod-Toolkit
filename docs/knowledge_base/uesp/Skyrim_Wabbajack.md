[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg/200px-SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg) Wabbajack Wabbajack is a Daedric artifact given to you by [Sheogorath](Skyrim_Sheogorath.md) in [The Mind of Madness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness) quest. The staff applies a variety of random effects on the target it is used against. It can randomly cause 60 points of [fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage), [frost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Damage), or [shock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shock_Damage) damage, absorb attributes (damaging the target's Health, Stamina and Magicka by 50% of their maximum value, and restoring the player's attributes by 50% of their maximum values), make the target [invisible](Skyrim_Invisibility_(effect).md), [heal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Heal_Other) the target by 75 points, and in some cases instantly kill the target. Along with its offensive abilities it can also randomly transform a target into a [chicken](Skyrim_Chicken.md), [rabbit](Skyrim_Rabbit.md), [mudcrab](Skyrim_Mudcrab.md), [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora.md), [goat cheese wheel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Goat_Cheese_Wheel), more rarely into a [sweet roll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sweet_Roll) or a shower of 50 [gold](Skyrim_Gold.md#Currency) coins, along with a pile of ash. Transforming the target into a goat cheese wheel, sweet roll, or gold coins usually terminates the target. However, if the Wabbajack is used to transform a target into an animal, it only delays the fight as they often transform back a short time later with full health (using it on the same target will return them to their true form where the fight can continue as normal).

## Related Quests

## Notes
- The Wabbajack could be used to reset your [followers](Skyrim_Followers.md), allowing their level and stats to scale with yours, or reach their max level. Since patch 1.6, this has become unnecessary as your followers level with you. - To do this, take your follower to a secluded area, save (since it could kill them or turn them hostile), dismiss them, use the Wabbajack on them until they transform into something, and then punch them to break them out of it. This will cause them to respawn, which will fix their level and stats.
- When used on [dragons](Skyrim_Dragon.md), [essential](Skyrim_Essential_NPCs.md) NPCs, [giants](Skyrim_Giant.md) or [dead thralls](Skyrim_Dead_Thrall.md), it will exclusively cause elemental explosions.
- The Wabbajack can be a useful tool against strong opponents due to its range of debilitating and/or instantly fatal effects.
- While you are in the [Mind of Pelagius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness), you are given a different version of Wabbajack with a description reading *"It's all you've got..."* that is tied to various quest triggers, and unlike the normal one, has unlimited charges (vital since you can't access your inventory to recharge it if needed). This alternate Wabbajack's Form ID is `0009b2b2`, but does nothing if acquired through the console once outside of the Mind of Pelagius. It will be counted as a weapon and does 1 damage, but it won't give you a bounty if used nor will the "victim" of the Wabbajack become hostile. The only thing it will do is give a reddish aura to the NPC that the Wabbajack hits for a few seconds.
- The majority of the Wabbajack's effects are considered harmful, thus making it a [crime](Skyrim_Crime.md) to use it against villagers.
- Once you have acquired the Wabbajack, a [Sheogorath](Skyrim_Sheogorath.md) [worshipper](Skyrim_Madwoman.md) may randomly run up to you and ask you to "wabbajack" her.
- The Wabbajack will not work when wielded by a follower.
- On the top of the Wabbajack are three faces with varying expressions; this is also present at the portal to the [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles) in [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion).
- When used on carriage drivers, the Wabbajack will transform the cart as well.
- While the Wabbajack is governed by the Destruction skill, and its charge cost will be affected by your skill level and enchantments that reduce the cost of Destruction spells, the actual damage the Wabbajack does when doing a burst of elemental damage isn't governed by anything. This means targets that are otherwise immune to frost, for example, will still take damage from a Wabbajack frost burst. This also makes the Wabbajack a particularly valuable item for anyone on higher difficulty levels, as its damage is not affected by your difficulty setting.
- Using the Wabbajack on plot-related NPCs can produce highly unpredictable results, as their scripts may interact with the Wabbajack's effects in ways that cannot be anticipated. An essential NPC, for example, cannot be killed but will be removed from the game if transformed into a sweetroll and eaten by the player.
- You can loot conjured and reanimated creatures by casting the appropriate Conjuration spells until you hit your summon limit, then using the Wabbajack on the creature that's about to disappear. If the creature is transmogrified, punch it before it disappears and it'll die like a normal creature, allowing you to loot it. This can get you a variety of interesting items that you wouldn't normally be able to acquire. A video showing the process can be found [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T7f CLo Fnzcg).
- During the quest, the Wabbajack will have infinite charges.
- [Myrwatch](Skyrim_Myrwatch_(place).md)'s gallery<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Myrwatch.md)</sup> contains a special holder for this item.
- If the transmogrification happens, it will reset the apparel and weaponry of the affected NPC, though only with randomly generated ones such as [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md) or [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md). For example, if a Bandit Marauder has mismatched steel armor and an orcish waraxe, using the Wabbajack on him and he becomes transmogrified, then changing back he could have an elven waraxe and steel plate armor instead. With *Dawnguard*, vampires will change which color (and perhaps enchantment) of vampire armor they wear, or change to vampire robes. In this way, it's possible to "cycle" loot of enemies to get a certain armor or weapon you want, which can appear in their leveled lists.

## Bugs
- During the quest, the Wabbajack may not auto-equip itself on you once Sheogorath gives you it. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.4, fixes this bug.
