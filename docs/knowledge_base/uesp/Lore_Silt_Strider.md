Silt Striders are giant arthropods native to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), which can grow to at least twenty feet tall,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> and commonly grow much taller.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> They have been used as a common means of transport in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and in the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), they are controlled by their caravaners by manipulating exposed organs and tissues with special hooks.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWClasses-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gen-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Hook-5)</sup> Silt Striders are a resilient species whose tough carapace helps them survive the adverse environment of the island.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RSDB-6)</sup> Silt Striders survived the [Ash Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) that exterminated many of Vvardenfell's native creatures, such as the [Cliff Strider](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VTAN-7)</sup> However, their numbers would eventually dwindle due to the coming of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), when the eruption of the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) killed most of them and destroyed their native habitat.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RSDB-6)</sup> A small population of the creatures was imported to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RSDB-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Wiz-8)</sup>

## Biology
Silt Striders are very docile creatures, without many predators.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Faven-9)</sup> They are very hardy, and well adapted to the harsh weather of Morrowind.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RSDB-6)</sup> They possess special glands that produce a unique musk, used to attract mates.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Musk-10)</sup> Their mating is described as involving "awkward flailing" and is known to be dangerous if one gets too close.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Silt Striders start out as larvae, but eventually encase themselves in a tough cocoon to mature.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RSDB-6)</sup> A juvenile Silt Strider is known as a calf.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SSCR-12)</sup> Silt Hoppers are a smaller variety of Silt Strider,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Hop-13)</sup> though it is unclear if they are young Silt Striders or simply a miniature species.

When used for transport, a compartment is carved out from the creatures shell and they are controlled by manipulation of their exposed organs and tissues.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gen-4)</sup> While this typically seems to cause no meaningful harm to the creature in standard operation, inexperienced control of a Silt Strider can result in major harm coming to the creature. In one account of a novice repeatedly making mistakes; when they pulled the left antennal lobe it caused a minor seizure. When the novice stepped on the right suboesophageal ganglion, it prompted violent discharge of the strider's gastric system and musk tubules. Lastly when they poked the ocellar nerve with a curved vedran-prod, the strider began screeching immediately. These examples make it evident there are many ways to accidentally harm the Silt Strider.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/MW-Screenshot42.jpg/200px-MW-Screenshot42.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-Screenshot42.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-Screenshot42.jpg) A caravaner welcomes a passenger aboard Silt Striders are a common method of transport for passengers and cargo in various parts of Morrowind, and have been used as such for generations.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gen-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Faven-9)</sup> Silt Hoppers are used as beasts of burden by [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Hop-13)</sup> A Silt Strider will commonly cross its route several times in a day, and are capable of moving considerably fast.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Faven-9)</sup> Silt Striders are known to be loyal to their owners.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RSDB-6)</sup> The Dunmer see Silt Striders as majestic creatures, but non-Dunmer have trouble acclimating to them, and are even known to express fear of them.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Non-Dunmer may also fall for lies about their danger, such as them being willing to eat people.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

Silt Strider limbs can be attached to ships for deep sea fishing; while their young's legs can be used for fishing rods.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SSCR-12)</sup> Their musk is used as a perfume, but it is dangerous to use in the wilds.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Musk-10)</sup> Silt Striders are the inspiration behind some toys.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

Silt Striders are the subject of Dunmer song and sayings: "That and a few coins will get you a ride on a silt strider".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Among the many absurd requests made by Vvardenfell [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) Archmage [Trebonius](Lore_Trebonius_Artorius.md) was that a subordinate "learn the language of the Silt Striders so we could question them about who they took to different cities". The mage in question chose to ignore the request, and recommended everyone else do the same.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
