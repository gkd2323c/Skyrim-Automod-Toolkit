[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg/200px-LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg) Plague doctors carting away corpses (foreground) and boarding up condemned houses (background) The Knahaten Flu (also known as the **Knahaten Plague**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-C-1)</sup> or the **Crimson Plague**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Crimson Quest-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-3)</sup> was one of the deadliest [diseases](Lore_Disease.md) to infect the populace of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), lasting for 43 years, from the year [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWG-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1-TEC-6)</sup> to [2E 603](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_603).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-7)</sup> The dangerous disease, once widespread across Tamriel, was thought to have been stamped out during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Crimson Quest-2)</sup> Some [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) referred to it as the **Half-Swim Sniffles**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LA:Murkmire2-8)</sup>

## History

### Origins
There are two theories about how the Knahaten Flu was introduced to Tamriel. One account speaks of an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) shaman<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup> who, in retaliation for the awful treatment of his people by the other races, created the disease "*through a manipulation of their cherished spore-trees*"<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWG-4)</sup> – the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Another account attributes the disease to natural causes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup> Since Argonians were widely reported to be immune to the Flu,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWG-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup> the former story was popularly accepted.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AAU-9)</sup> Some Argonians say that they were not responsible for the flu as they consider the harshly impacted Khajiit to be their friends, and that Argonians can contract the flu, albeit it is a very mild disease for them compared to others.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LA:Murkmire2-8)</sup> However, an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) spirit implies that the flu was instead created by the Ayleids as a weapon against the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), and was chagrined that it had left Kothringi survivors.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOMorilye-10)</sup>

The Knahaten Flu began in the city of [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md) in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), and quickly spread to every corner of Black Marsh and beyond, killing all infected non-Argonians. It continued to plague Tamriel for over four decades, the last known cases of the Flu being documented in the year [2E 603](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_603).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-7)</sup>

### Aftermath
There is no definitive death toll of the disease. However, the Flu almost completely destroyed the non-Argonian population of Black Marsh, and instilled outsiders with a fear of the province for centuries. Only [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) slavers of [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), with their entire economy dependent on the slave workforce, remained there. It was said that even [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) himself thought twice before invading Black Marsh.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-So R-11)</sup> Fear of the disease persisted late into the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), when a mass panic was caused by an alleged relapse of the Flu.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-C-1)</sup>

Regardless of the Flu's source, the situation in Black Marsh became increasingly favorable to the Argonians after the disease struck. After the assassination of [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) and the dissolution of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), Cyrodiil lost its grip over most of the province.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3-At Eo M-12)</sup> Once the Flu struck, the Empire lost any hope of controlling Black Marsh.

The Knahaten Flu was, indirectly, one of the main causes of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). By destroying the royal family of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), it brought King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) to the throne from which he forged the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ta Toa M-13)</sup> It was a crisis in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), motivating the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) to join the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) out of respect and gratitude for the many elves who had rendered aid during the worst years of the Flu.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TEat C-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Itt Lo TESO-15)</sup> And of course, it provided the Argonians with enough independence to join the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) as equals to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), rather than as slaves.

The Flu also motivated changes to enforce higher standards in food quality and safety; for instance, it made the Summerset Navy rethink their regulations concerning rations.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TEot GF-16)</sup>

## Symptoms, Treatment and Transmission
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/ON-creature-Ogre_03.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Ogre_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ogre_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ogre_03.jpg) An infected [ogre](Lore_Ogre.md) in the ruins of [Orcest](Lore_Orcrest.md) ca. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-npc-Flu_Victim.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Flu_Victim.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Flu_Victim.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Flu_Victim.jpg) A Flu victim, reanimated after death Early symptoms of the Flu include "*general malaise, loss of appetite, and fatigue,*" followed shortly by weakness, headaches,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRCrimson Disease-17)</sup> aching muscles, sharp pain in the shoulders, chills, and watering eyes. Late-stage symptoms include wracking cough, high fever, and blood flowing out of the nose, eyes and eventually the mouth. The most prominent sign of the disease was a red, granular rash, which did not itch but spread all over the body.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ot KF-18)</sup> Infection can result in muteness.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Crimson Quest-2)</sup> Death usually occurred within three painful days of infection, but some victims were able to survive for up to a week.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

While the worst of the symptoms could be alleviated by sorghum-tea<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TEat C-14)</sup> and chicken broth,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Jo TM-20)</sup> the search for a cure was impeded by many questionable alleged treatments. For instance, a Redguard named Perizada supposedly saved an entire village with a potion made by boiling [clannfear](Lore_Clannfear.md) claws in salt water, but she later succumbed to the plague herself.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ot KF-18)</sup>

The common disease tends to spread easily and acts quickly.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Crimson Quest-2)</sup> How the disease spread is not definitively known.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ot KF-18)</sup> One source claims it was airborne,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1-TEC-6)</sup> while another claims that it was spread through physical contact with the victim's particular rash. The latter theory is supported by reports that infected people who covered their rashes with clothing and bandages were less contagious.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ot KF-18)</sup> Though they were immune to the Knahaten Flu themselves, Argonians were often suspected to be carriers of the disease, perhaps due to the common belief that they had created it.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AAU-9)</sup>

## Victims
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/ON-quest-The_First_Step_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_First_Step_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_First_Step_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_First_Step_02.jpg) Victims of the Knahaten Flu, brought back as undead [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/ON-place-South_Guard_Ruins.jpg/200px-ON-place-South_Guard_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-South_Guard_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-South_Guard_Ruins.jpg) South Guard in ruins as a result of the Scorch At its height, the Knahaten Flu engulfed most of Tamriel. It spread from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), where it started, to southern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup> and across the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) to [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1-TEC-6)</sup> and from there to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md)<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-21)</sup> and even [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ta Toa M-13)</sup> It has been claimed not a single inhabitant was left untouched by this scourge and it is said that the Knahaten Flu was dominating every aspect of Tamrielian life.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BSAthe-22)</sup><sup>:16-17</sup>

The Flu managed to destroy cities, religions, and even entire cultures. The high and mighty were not exempt, as it consumed the entire royal family of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ta Toa M-13)</sup> ending the Gardner Dynasty,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-23)</sup> as well as the hero [Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md), who performed many good deeds in Valenwood before succumbing to the disease.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TL-24)</sup> Also in Valenwood, the Flu nearly wiped out worshipers of [Z'en](Lore_Z%27en.md), leading to the Bosmer abandoning his worship entirely soon afterward.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-21)</sup>

The entirety of Elsweyr suffered greatly from the Flu, though the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) suffered the most. A strain of the disease crossed the channel in 2E 560 and infected most of the populace. Soon there was damage to the city itself, as paranoia raged rampant, and entire sections of the city were burned to slow down the Flu, never to be rebuilt again. An account from three hundred years later said that visitors to Senchal could still see the "*charred skylines on the periphery, as black and jagged as the teeth of the nearest sugar junkie, begging for cake.*"<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1-TEC-6)</sup> The disease in Elsweyr was alleviated only with the help of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), who saved many lives, indebting the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) to them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Itt Lo TESO-15)</sup> Despite heavy loss of life, the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md)'s Legion managed to reimpose order and implement medical discoveries made by various Clan-Mothers.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TEat C-14)</sup>

In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563) the Knahaten Flu reached the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md), slaying commoner and noble alike. Duke Avrippe Mornard ordered the archipelago sealed off in [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565), causing trade to stagnate, which caused Duke Avrippe to fall into disfavor in Wayrest's court.

### The Stricken
The Stricken were [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) whose bodies were ravaged by the Knahaten Flu but ultimately survived their infection with the disease. The Stricken were cast out of Khajiiti society due to rampant fear and hatred of those infected with the Flu. When the outbreak began, Khajiit flocked to S'rendarr's Cradle in small numbers. The adepts tried their best to heal everyone, but many died. Rumors spread that healing could be found within S'rendarr's Cradle, and those afflicted by the Flu came by the dozens. King [Hemakar](Lore_Hemakar.md) viewed the large numbers of sick people in such close quarters as a breeding ground for the disease. Despite these fears, the adepts of S'rendarr's Cradle tried their best to accommodate everyone and keep the adeptorium sanitary.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOSilhu-jo-25)</sup> Many orphaned children were taken in by the adepts during this time.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOVazbi-26)</sup>

All was going as well as it could until Sutabi, vizier to King Hemakar, arrived at S'rendarr's Cradle with a host of war-cats. Sutabi threatened to raze the adeptorium in order to contain the infection. If S'rendarr's Cradle continued to harbor Flu victims, the adeptorium would burn. Thus, the sick were led to nearby Baan Dar's Litter by the adepts and left to die. Those who survived their affliction became known as the Stricken, and they lived in the canyon east of S'rendarr's Cradle for over eighteen years.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOSilhu-jo-25)</sup>

While it was known that those who had survived the flu could no longer transmit it, many residents remained indifferent. Several Khajiit survivors were reported to still reside in Rimmen following their recovery from the flu.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Bindabi-27)</sup>

### The Kothringi and the Crimson Ship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg) Stillrise Ritecallers Those most affected by the Knahaten Flu were the human population of Black Marsh. Most notable among them were the native silver-skinned [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), whose settlements of [Stillrise Village](Lore_Stillrise_Village.md) and [Zuuk](Lore_Zuuk_(place).md) were already lifeless by [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup> The Kothringi are thought to have been completely exterminated by it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWG-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-5)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Kothringi)</sup>

One of the most famous stories from those troubled times is that of the Crimson Ship. Filled with Kothringi refugees, it left the shores of Black Marsh on the 9th of First Seed, [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), only to be turned back at every port. After a year on the sea, they reached Hammerfell, and with no one to shelter them, they sailed west<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-7)</sup> to the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). The book, [*Pirates of the Abecean*](Lore_Pirates_of_the_Abecean.md) tells a tale of the discovery of the ship, found adrift by a group of pirates, its entire crew dead.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-30)</sup> The tale appears to be fictional, as no official records exist that confirm the discovery of the ship. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a Khajiit beggar claimed to have been on a ship that encountered the vessel during his service in the [Imperial army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), blaming the event for his misfortunes. However, the reliability of his testimony is questionable.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Kuralit-31)</sup>

To memorialize this event, the people of Hammerfell honor the 20th of Rain's Hand as the [Day of Shame](Lore_Day_of_Shame.md). Nobody leaves their house, for it is told that the Crimson Ship will return on this day.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Hi D-32)</sup>

### Attempted Resurgence
In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the tomb of [Gedna Relvel](Lore_Gedna_Relvel.md) in the ruins of [Old Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) was unsealed. Relvel, once a powerful sorceress condemned for studying and practicing forbidden magics, had cheated death and become a lich. She then attempted to spread the Crimson Plague throughout the city of Mournhold by infecting [rats](Lore_Rat.md) with the disease. The lich was destroyed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) before the disease could spread to more than a few people.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Crimson Quest-2)</sup>
