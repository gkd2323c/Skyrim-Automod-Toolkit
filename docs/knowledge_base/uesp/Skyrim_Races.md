## Non-Playable Races
Other NPC races exist in the game but are not available for you to play:

- [Child](Skyrim_Child.md) (including Breton, Imperial, Nord, and Redguard children)
- [Elder](Skyrim_Elder.md) ("Old People Race" in the game data)
- [Dark Seducer](Skyrim_Dark_Seducer.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Saints_%26_Seducers.md)</sup>
- [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora.md)
- [Golden Saint](Skyrim_Golden_Saint.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Saints_%26_Seducers.md)</sup>
- Invisible Race (the race of the [Ancient Dragonborn](Skyrim_Ancient_Dragonborn.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>)
- [Mannequin](Skyrim_Mannequins.md) (categorized as an NPC race to allow mannequins to equip the armors and outfits they are meant to display)
- [Snow Elf](Skyrim_Snow_Elf.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>

## Notes
- For each playable race, there is also a different race used by [vampires](Skyrim_Vampirism.md). For example, non-vampire Bretons use "Breton Race", whereas Breton vampires use "Breton Race Vampire". However, this is not apparent in-game, as the traits of each race are usually the same for its vampire counterpart (except for a number of oversights).
- The `[setrace](Skyrim_Console.md#setrace)`, `[setplayerrace](Skyrim_Console.md#setplayerrace)`, and `[showracemenu](Skyrim_Console.md#showracemenu)` [console](Skyrim_Console.md) commands can be used to change your race after character creation. Only `[player.setrace](Skyrim_Console.md#setrace)` will not break your skills after switching a race.
- The [movement](Skyrim_Movement.md) speed of each race is dependent on its height. As shown above, High Elves have the largest speed multiplier due to being the tallest race. This was likely done so the same animations (with the same speed) could be used for every character without them appearing to moonwalk.
- There is a persistent but false rumor that [melee damage](Skyrim_Damage.md) is also affected by height, apparently based on a misunderstanding of the [`setscale`](Skyrim_Console.md#setscale) command. See [this post on the Bethesda forums](https://web.archive.org/web/20200226050502/http://forums.bethsoft.com/topic/1286166-hidden-stat-differences-based-on-race-selection/page-2#entry19603197) for reference.
- There are many minor dialogue differences depending on the race you choose. However, the only two instances where an NPC's reaction to your race actually matters to gameplay are your [Blood-Kin](Skyrim_Blood-Kin.md) status and the quest [Diplomatic Immunity](Skyrim_Diplomatic_Immunity.md).
- Although the in-game text says *"Khajiit claws do 15 points of damage."*, the actual effect magnitude is only 12 points.
- Certain races (Argonians, Khajiit, Nords, and Imperials) are able to perform an alchemy loop through [superstacking](Skyrim_Glitches.md#Infinitely_Powerful_Items) without being a Werewolf or Vampire Lord, if they have a specific item that the player enchanted with a Fortify Alchemy effect. Khajiit vampires can superstack with Gold Rings, Nord vampires can superstack with the Bone Hawk Amulet, Imperial vampires can superstack with Forsworn Gauntlets, and Khajiit and Argonians can superstack with the Worn Shrouded Cowl, whether or not they're vampires.
- Along with information like height and skill bonuses, a weight value can be found in the game data. The meaning or impact of this value is unclear; however, it seems to affect how easily a race is ragdolled or moved.

| *Weight* | Argonian | Breton | Dark Elf | High Elf | Imperial | Khajiit | Nord | Orc | Redguard | Wood Elf |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Female | 1 | 1 | 1 | 0.5 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Male | 0.5 | 1 | 1 | 0.5 | 1 | 0.5 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 |

All other races in the game are considered to be [creatures](Skyrim_Creatures.md), with every variety of creature having its own race; these creature races are not detailed here.
