Jylkurfyk was a coastal city in southern [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). It was known for its large shipyard and was active in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup>

## History
Following the events of the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md) and Ysgramor's retreat to Atmora, and before the Return of [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md), Ysgramor expressed a desire to travel to Jylkurfyk and take a ship back to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to avenge his kin. However, before he returned to Skyrim, he codified the written language in Atmora to preserve human history.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TOOTO-2)</sup>

[Ylgar](Lore_Ylgar.md) commissioned two ships for himself and his brother [Yngol](Lore_Yngol.md), the *[Darumzu](Lore_Darumzu.md)* and the *[Harakk](Lore_Harakk.md)*, named after the [stars](Lore_Stars.md) favored by the Atmorans from the local shipyard for [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s legendary voyage to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup>

Due to an inexplicable shift in the climate of Atmora territories once known as a green, albeit cold land were gradually overcome by "the frost fall" and rendered largely barren and uninhabitable,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOTR7-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-5)</sup> becoming a "frozen continent".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PBE-6)</sup>

Although there is some suggestion that, as of the early [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Atmora was still inhabited,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup> the fate of Jylkurfyk is unknown. It is believed that Atmora no longer supports any civilized life, and those people who did not leave it for Tamriel ultimately succumbed to the ever-worsening climate.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-5)</sup>

*[The Ship of Ice](Lore_The_Ship_of_Ice.md)* tells the story of the crew of the last ship to leave Atmora. As they were preparing to leave Jylkurfyk, on the day they were ready to sail, a [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) came to them. A child dressed in a thin robe, unbothered by the cold, announced that the frostfall of Atmora was their revenge for the destruction of the Snow Elves by the Nords.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSOI-8)</sup>
