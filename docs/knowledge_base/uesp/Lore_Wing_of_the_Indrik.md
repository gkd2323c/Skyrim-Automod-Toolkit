[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg) Wing of the Indrik The Wing of the Indrik is a [pocket realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) belonging to the [Luminary Indrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary_Indrik). It was shaped and hewn into being in the same way he chose his form. He considers it a pleasant respite from the larger world, a place to find peace and clear his mind of stray thoughts. He believed that realms of other [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md) could serve different purposes. Like other Luminaries, he formed his own pocket realm at birth, though the reason remains unknown.The Indrik felt that [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), in its wisdom, did not wish the Luminaries to be both alone and wandering.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup>

This realm resembles the climate of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), with falling petals and verdant greens that made the Indrik feel at home. [Ulfsild](Lore_Ulfsild.md) decided to bind the Indrik's domain to the Scholarium, leading to great achievements. This connection is facilitated through large doors, with the Indrik's power channeling into the very heart of the Scribing Altar, enabling incredible feats of spellcraft. The Indrik believed a Luminary's power was practically destined for this purpose.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup> As with the realms of all Luminaries, it had a warded door and a key which one had to use to enter it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik_06.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik_06.jpg) The Gate to the Wing [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/ON-creature-Indrik_Fawn.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Indrik_Fawn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Indrik_Fawn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Indrik_Fawn.jpg) Indrik Fawn The Wing of the Indrik is as old as the Luminary himself, created at the time of its birth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup> Circa [1E 2150](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2150),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LAS-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MGC-4)</sup> Ulfsild came into possession of the *[Fable of the Indrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fable_of_the_Indrik)* and annotated it as she searched for the gateway to the Indrik's realm.

In her pursuit, Ulfsild obtained keys to the realm from various locations on the island of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md): near the mundus stone dedicated to [the Lady](Lore_The_Lady.md) south of [Silsailen](Lore_Silsailen.md), in Ondil, and [Buraniim](Lore_Buraniim.md). Ultimately, she found the door on the island of the South Beacon lighthouse,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FOTIA-5)</sup> broke the wards, trespassed into the Indrik's domain,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ULTI-6)</sup> and passed the trials.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup> Ulfsild formed a bond with the Indrik, which eventually led to the connection of his realm with the Scholarium.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup>

As beings of pure magic, the Luminaries were said to have understood magic in a way mortals never could and so, their power, freely given, allowed one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup> Before the doors to their wings were sealed, the Luminaries, including the Indrik, fed the altar their strength, allowing it to "shape the very heart of magic itself" and translate its very essence into mortal hands with the Luminary as intermediary. The practitioner scrawling their [soul](Lore_Souls.md) upon the page could change and rewrite both the magic and the mortal.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup> The power of the Indrik himself infused entirely into every aspect of the texts used for the Scribing process.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TI-1)</sup>

Eventually, the Scholarium was lost along with [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md), transported to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup> due to a bargain [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) made with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in exchange for the [Folium Discognitum](Lore_Folium_Discognitum.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Shalidor-7)</sup> With the doors closed, the altar became dormant. Without that beating heart, the mastery of Scribing was believed to stagnate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup> The Scholarium itself was not discovered, however, for it had been warded against mages, Princes, and powers long ago, and those defenses held strong even in the Shivering Isles.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Scholarium was rediscovered by a group of [knights](Lore_Knights.md) from the [Order of the Lamp](Lore_Order_of_the_Lamp.md). During their arrival, complications arose, necessitating the sealing of the doors to prevent a catastrophe, as the once dormant altar became unstable. This led to the return of Scribing to Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TC-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCOS-8)</sup>

The [Inheritor](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Scholarium retraced Ulfsild's footsteps, discovering the keys, breaking the wards, and entering the realm to reconnect the Scholarium with the Wing of the Indrik. The fable was placed on a dais, and after passing the trials of the Indrik, the wing was properly connected to the Scribing Altar once more.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWOTI-9)</sup> This reconnection led to the great power of the Indrik setting up harmonic reverberations across Tamriel. Long dormant [ley lines](Lore_Ley_Lines.md), similar to those in the Scholarium itself, began to awaken, revealing the true nature of the Scripts and Luminous Ink. These were now recognized as portents of future power rather than merely scraps of paper or dull and lifeless writing materials.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SOTIA-10)</sup>
