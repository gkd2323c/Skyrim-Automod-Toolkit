# Graht-oak

— *[A Dance in Fire, Chapter 2](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Dance_in_Fire,_Chapter_2)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ADIF2-1)</sup> The graht-oaks are massive, ambulatory trees native to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) are known to build entire cities in the branches of these great trees, most famously [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ADIF2-1)</sup> In their mighty boughs, Bosmer live alongside the [ape](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ape) -like [Imga](Lore_Imga.md) in a mobile ecosystem, coaxing the living wood into pathways winding around hide structures nestled in hollows.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AA-3)</sup> The canopy of the trees is so large that it is sometimes called the *second sky*, or the *middle green*.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGFalicia-4)</sup> Emblematic of their [reverence for nature](Lore_Green_Pact.md), the Bosmeri [Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tower), [Green-Sap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green-Sap), is represented in each and all of the graht-oaks, rather than in a single erected structure as with [White-Gold](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and [Ada-Mantia](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AE4TET-5)</sup>

The **Elden Tree** is an even larger subspecies of the graht-oak and one of the oldest living things in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They are generally worshipped as focal points of the Green's essence. The cities, [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root) and Falinesti are the only settlements built from the Elden Tree.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONLoad Elden-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONLoad Autumn-7)</sup> The southeast region of [Grahtwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahtwood) is known for its abundance of graht-oak trees, more than any other parts of Valenwood, hence its name.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONLoad Grahtwood-8)</sup>
