[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/SR-place-Fort_Hraggstad.jpg/200px-SR-place-Fort_Hraggstad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Hraggstad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Hraggstad.jpg) Fort Hraggstad **Fort Hraggstad** is a medium-sized [fort](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md) west of [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md) initially occupied by [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md). It contains two zones: *[Fort Hraggstad](#Fort_Hraggstad)* and *[Fort Hraggstad Prison](#Fort_Hraggstad_Prison)*.

The fort will be taken over by whoever wins the [Civil War](Skyrim_Civil_War.md), or by the [Imperials](Skyrim_Imperial_Soldier.md) if you first clear it of bandits and/or complete the quest [Joining the Legion](Skyrim_Joining_the_Legion.md) (see [bugs](#Bugs)).

## Related Quests

- **[The Battle for Fort Hraggstad](Skyrim_The_Battle_for_Fort_Hraggstad.md)**: Prepare for the final assault on [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md) by taking this fort for the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md).

## Walkthrough

### Exterior
The sizable courtyard and walls are patrolled by up to seven [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md). A [forge](Skyrim_Forge.md) is along the southwestern wall, with an unlocked [chest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chest) to its left. On your right as you pass the arch into the courtyard is an empty stable, with several food barrels found around the courtyard. On top of the two intact towers are random, possibly enchanted [bows](Skyrim_Bow.md) and quivers of [arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Arrows) on tables, with the top of the tallest tower also having a [long bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Long_Bow).

### Fort Hraggstad
As soon as you enter, unless your sneak skill is very high, you are likely to be confronted by a lone bandit in the first room, standing in front of the fireplace directly opposite the entrance. However, even the sounds from a loud battle with him are unlikely to reach the ears of the next bandit upstairs, so you can generally deal with the two bandits one at a time. To the left of the fireplace is a cupboard holding clutter, a garlic bulb in a wooden bowl, and three common books, while leaning against the fireplace is a woodcutter's axe. To the right of the fireplace is a doorway leading further into the fort, and in the northeast corner is a wooden table with [iron armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Armor), helmet, [sword](Skyrim_Iron_Sword.md), and battleaxe all on top.

With the [Netch Leather Armor](Skyrim_Netch_Leather_Armor.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed, there is an additional locked chest here which can only be opened with a key obtainable during the quest [More Than You Can Chew](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:More_Than_You_Can_Chew).

Through the doorway is a cart at the bottom of a flight of stairs, containing an [iron shield](Skyrim_Iron_Shield.md), a long bow, iron gauntlets, and a [potion of minor healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing). Climbing the stairs leads to a large room, with a bandit leaning against the wall, and the [bandit chief](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bandit_Chief) at the back of the room in an area being used as sleeping quarters. The front half of the room is being used for storage, with several food barrels under a wooden structure in the northwestern corner. On this structure is an adept-locked chest and a small wooden table holding three potions of minor healing and some foodstuffs. Against the north wall are two sets of shelves containing three bottles of [alto wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Alto_Wine), a bottle of [wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wine), some food, a [gourd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gourd), an iron sword, and eight common books. Opposite the shelves is another wooden structure, with a food barrel and a table holding a small mead barrel and three potatoes.

There is a raised section in the middle of the room with a small wooden table holding two bottles of wine. Against the south wall is a single bed and a dresser. On the dresser are three loose [coins](Skyrim_Gold.md), a bulb of [garlic](Skyrim_Garlic.md), and some bread and cheese. In front of this is an end table with a gourd on top, and against the north wall is another single bed and another table with a [leg of goat roast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leg_of_Goat_Roast) and a loaf of bread on top.

The western end of the room contains four more single beds, an unlocked chest, and a table holding a tactical map of Skyrim which the bandit chief was studying, a [baked potato](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Baked_Potatoes), a garlic bulb, a gourd, an iron helmet, and a copy of the [Archery](Skyrim_Archery.md) [skill book](Skyrim_Skill_Books.md) *[The Gold Ribbon of Merit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Gold_Ribbon_of_Merit)*. Against the north wall, between two of the beds, is a cupboard holding an iron helmet. Between the other two beds against the southern wall are an end table and a table with a set of iron armor, iron gauntlets, and an [iron mace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Mace) on top, while leaning against the end table is an [iron greatsword](Skyrim_Iron_Greatsword.md), with a pair of [iron boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Boots) next to the nearest bed. None of the beds in the fort are owned.

### Fort Hraggstad Prison
Upon entering, you are in a room with a bandit roaming about and a second bandit sitting at a table to the left. On the table is a small [coin purse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse) and a [satchel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Satchel). There are a couple wooden benches and a set of shelves holding only clutter behind the table, with a [knapsack](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Knapsack) on top of the shelves. Against the northeast wall, almost opposite the entrance, is a second set of shelves holding a [woodcutter's axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe), [iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Armor) or [banded iron armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Banded_Iron_Armor), [iron gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Gauntlets), and an [iron helmet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Helmet). There is a door leading downstairs in the southeast corner.

At the bottom of the stairs is a room containing a set of shelves full of more clutter against the northeast wall, and a small wooden table in the southeast corner with a [rock warbler egg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rock_Warbler_Egg) on top. To the southwest are stairs down to the cells, which are guarded by another bandit. There are a total of seven cells, with four against the southwest wall, and three against the northeast wall. All of the cells are empty and unlocked. On your left is a table holding an [iron battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe) and a common book. Behind the table is a chair, and against the southeast wall is an unlocked chest to the right of two sets of shelves holding several pieces of [bread](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bread) and cheese as well as clutter.

## Notes
- Earlier in the game's development, the quest [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md) was tied to the factions of the Civil War, and you seemingly chose a side before you could first encounter dragons. If you chose the [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md), [General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md) would send you with [Captain Metilius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Metilius) to investigate reports of a dragon at Fort Hraggstad. If you instead joined the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md), [Ulfric](Skyrim_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) would send you along with someone named Sifnar (this person might have been [Sifnar Ironkettle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sifnar_Ironkettle), but the latter may have been named after the former) to investigate a similar disturbance at [Fort Amol](Skyrim_Fort_Amol.md). Little evidence of this remains in the game's files, with only a few markers (`MQ104Fort Hraggstad Move To Marker`), AI packages, and leftover scripts (`MQ104Script`) still referring to this iteration. The Dialogue Views files hold dialogue for General Tullius and Captain Metilius that are not viewable in the [Creation Kit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Creation_Kit) in the final game's files.

## Bugs
- If this is not the first [Civil War Fort](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md#Civil_War_Forts) you clear, after clearing it of [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md), soldiers will never garrison and enemies respawn instead. This is due to a hidden quest not ending after clearing the first Civil War Fort. See this [talk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:talk_Fort_Hraggstad) section for info. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.7, fixes this bug.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) The console command `resetquest CWFort Observer` can be used to reset the hidden quest, then clear the fort again. Repeat this for every Civil War Fort you want to be garrisoned.
- The exterior weapons and the bandit chest aren't properly linked to enable their parent markers. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.2, fixes this bug.
