# Souls


Souls are generally immortal though there have been a few cases of spirits that got erased from existence, like [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril_the_Unfeathered). In death, a mortal's soul usually journeys to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), where it remains forever. Mortals who made a binding pact with a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince), who retain strong emotional ties to the mortal realm, or who are affected by certain spells, may instead find their souls on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) or one of the planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). It is possible to trap and manipulate souls through [necromantic arts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) and spells like [Soul Trap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Trap).

It is possible that souls are composed of various parts and influenced by variables that are yet to be understood, for example a [Shadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Magic) of the soul can be separated from it and transformed into an [independent entity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow), leaving the original soul in a weakened state.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fyr Shadow-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lilatha Shadow-3)</sup> Other anomalies of soul mechanics include the odd nature of [Cadwell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cadwell), who shared his consciousness between his old body and the one formed by his Vestige, resulting in two distinct versions of the same person. While the precise nature of the souls is uncertain, what can be known is that souls are a source of mystic energy.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SBAW-4)</sup> [Soul Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic), [Enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md), [Necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy), [Mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism), and [Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) all make use of this energy. [Soul gems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems) are the most common method of trapping souls, especially for enchanting, although items such as animus geodes and auricular geodes<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SFTD-5)</sup> can be used in some cases.

## Mortal Souls

### Characteristics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg) An Argonian's soul being torn from his body as he dies [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/RG-scene-Iszara_Restored_02.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Iszara_Restored_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Iszara_Restored_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Iszara_Restored_02.jpg) A soul's essence surrounds [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), restoring her The Anuic animus of mortals, commonly referred to as a "soul",<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CCTAP-1)</sup> is described as having certain characteristics. It has been described as the piece of every mortal which comes from [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), the realm of pure light and magic, and a "tiny [star"](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars) which flares into a ["sun"](Lore_Sun.md) when magic is invoked.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LOS-6)</sup> Souls can accumulate magicka over time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOFaindor-7)</sup>

The spirits of mortals are noted for their mutability, in contrast to the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who are described as largely immutable and static, incapable of experiencing change in spiritual nature or certain aspects of character,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDungeons-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Scruut-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mora-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Leramil-12)</sup> or to [Nature Spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirits) who are said to always return to a core nature,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Laurel-13)</sup> mortals can change, in their nature, their ways, their names,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Nature Nymics-14)</sup> which makes them stand out. Because of this difference in mutability, even a mortal who's received power from a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) to achieve transformation into a "mock-Daedra" remains mortal in terms of nature.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDungeons-8)</sup> This mutability is said to extend to a soul's vessel, where a mortal might change their spirit's vessel through some basic magic, a change a number of mages have undergone, taking on forms such as that of a Voriplasm , a tree, a [Tomeshell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tomeshell), a [Giant](Lore_Giant.md), a [Golem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) or [Flesh Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flesh_Atronach), a [Soulrazer Knight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soulrazer_Knight), or even a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) skeleton,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Rectavius-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vorm-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Orryn-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Thallik-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sapling-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESORD-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AHing-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GH-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOLeobert-23)</sup> a Daedra's form is defined by their [Nymic](Lore_Nymic.md) or "incantatory true name" in its one changeless form.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Nature Nymics-14)</sup> In some cases souls have jumped between different hosts in quick succession.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Armaltol-24)</sup>

According to [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), it is the nature of mortals to always have a chance to succeed and reach a desired outcome even when it would otherwise be impossible, a quality not possessed by immortal beings like himself who would inevitably fail in the same circumstances.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mora-11)</sup> The unique ability of mortals to choose their own [fate](Lore_Fate.md) is also affirmed by the Daedric Prince [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia), according to whom mortals, unlike immortals such as the Daedra, possess the ability to change who they are and alter their own fates naturally, without the need of magic or divine power, something which beings such as herself or her servants are incapable of.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup>

Unlike the Vestiges of Daedra, the souls of mortals are described as not fettered to existence, with the proper rites they can be "decoupled" from the body entirely and persist as immortal spirits that sustain themselves by consuming other souls. This unmooring process is not the same as the soul being separated from the body at death because then the resulting spirit is dead, a [Ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost), whereas if separated prior to death the mortal becomes a living spirit.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Uldazaan-25)</sup>

### Recuperation and Reincarnation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) Moon-Singer Talbira, a spirit who reincarnated herself into a living body [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg) Cantor Krin'ze, the Twilight Cantor whose soul Talbira merged with Even when a soul has been sundered by powerful magic, it is said it can eventually recoalesce in the same location, given enough time.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-26)</sup> Indeed, the fragments of a spirit shattered by magic need to be bound by spells to prevent them from seeking each other out and reforming into the whole, and will proceed to recoalesce once the spell keeping them apart is lifted.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOAlanwe-27)</sup> Magic can be used to bind a spirit from moving on to the afterlife, but it is said even such souls can find a way to move on eventually.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESOAnconath-28)</sup> There have been cases where the spirit of a dead mortal has entered the body of a living being and integrated itself with the living soul residing there. Though ghosts have the ability to possess and animate both living and dead bodies as well as inanimate objects by entering them,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONJanne-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONGalga-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONUldor-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESORun-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONGhora-33)</sup> such phenomena are said to not be the same thing as this spiritual integration. Whereas in a possession the soul of the spirit remains distinct and either exerts direct control over the host body or influences the host's thoughts and emotions, with the host soul sometimes being expelled or having its identity erased outright,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOFarwen-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOAwa-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Spirithunter-36)</sup> the integration phenomenon results instead in the soul of the spirit merging with the soul of the living host without exerting control, thus the spirit is effectively reborn or [reincarnated](Lore_Reincarnation.md) into the living host, who will than start to experience the memories of that spirit as if they'd lived through them, manifesting the knowledge and abilities the soul reincarnated within them possessed and feeling its emotions as their own. The phenomenon has also been described as a spirit being resurrected as the "twin soul" of its host. The initial entry of the soul into the living body is accompanied by a brief bout of intense pain, after the rebirth, other spirits who knew the reincarnated soul in life can sense its presence within its reincarnated form and realize the host soul and the reincarnated soul as now being the same. Though such spiritual rebirth is possible, it is said that it is relatively rare for spirits to behave in that manner.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Zerith-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cantor-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Notes-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Journal-40)</sup>

### Composition
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/ON-npc-Divayth_Fyr%27s_Shadow.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Divayth_Fyr%27s_Shadow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Divayth_Fyr%27s_Shadow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Divayth_Fyr%27s_Shadow.jpg) A Shadow, aspect of a mortal's animus [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg) Magicka, an aspect of a mortal's animus According to the [Order of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), mortal souls are the [Aedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) essence at the core of every mortal's existence. They can be likened to the wings of a butterfly, scaled and full of vessels which become filled with the experiences of mortal existence. It is said that upon death a process of dissipation whereby some of these accumulated contents disperse. These essences, the [Fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md) or "will to peace", are believed to retain a connection to both the grand fabric of [creation](Lore_Aurbis.md) and to all aspects of the soul in all their [destinations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence). Thus because, all aspects of a soul remain connected, tapping into the Fjyrons allows one who knows how to receive the erudition of the ancestors. Knowledge and guidance from beyond mortality, from the existence beyond the present and the past and the known world, where time is irrelevant, thus allowing one to glimpse the cosmic tapestry and its threads. The [Ancestor Moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md) of the Order are said to imbibe the Fjyrons, described by some as the memories of the dead,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TA-41)</sup> becoming animated by the Fjyrons of the ancestor spirits, shepherding that wisdom and passing it through the generations. Thus the moths represent a connection to the Aedra and the "endless Aedric coil of mortal souls" through the experiences of every mortal who has ever lived, and their trilling forms a sort of primal augur which allows the Order to read and interpret the contents of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) which contain knowledge said to exceed even the [gods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TDS-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-POTM-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-PGE1-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-LAESMP-45)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-AMS-46)</sup>

The mortal soul contains certain facets, the [Shadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow) is said to be the darkness which innately belongs to mortal souls, an integral part of a creature's animus, it being damaged while attached to the rest of the soul is equivalent to the soul being damaged and can lead to death.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Blade-47)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fyr Shadow-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lilatha Shadow-3)</sup> In at least one case, a mortal's essence was separated into their aspects of Magicka, Health and Stamina through magic.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Unleashed-48)</sup> A mortal's emotions such as fear, anger, happiness, and sadness make up different aspects of their animus and can express their own will when allowed to by magic. Certain types of magic such as the Silaseli Dream State of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) can allow these emotion-aspects to separate themselves from their soul of origin and manifest in spectral forms, rendering the mortal incapable of feeling those emotions until they are returned. Emotion aspects separated in this manner retain the ability to use magic.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Arnitole Anger-49)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Arnitole-50)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Arnitole Sadness-51)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Arnitole Happiness-52)</sup> Though souls can be sundered through the use of powerful magic, it is said the spirit can eventually recoalesce, given enough time,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-26)</sup> in addition to their fragments eventually reconstituting on their own, shattered spirits can also be restored through the use of [Soul Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic), a process which can also allow for souls that have been altered as in the case of the [Dro-m'Athra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra) to be restored to their original state.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Vastarie-53)</sup>

The mortal soul is said to contain tremendous power, but it also places certain checks on mortal will which obstruct the mortal from accessing it, separating the soul from the body, as is done through the process of transformation into [Lichdom](Lore_Lich.md), is said to remove such boundaries, opening the door to a virtually limitless magical horizon.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Vastarie-53)</sup> According to the necromancer [N'Gasta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:N%27Gasta) the mortal soul holds immense power which mortals themselves remain generally unaware of, an "unearned grace" granted by the [Outer Gods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) who "give too plenty and without good guidance" which, if it was understood, would make them place more importance in safeguarding their souls over their bodies. Thus N'Gasta likens the soul itself to a flame and the body to an easily slipped skin akin to paper, which gradually withers not able to long withstand the fire within it.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Gasta-54)</sup> Some sources claim that spiritual corruption manifests within the soul as a tangible thing, something which those with the required knowledge and skill can isolate and remove, leaving the soul itself alive and healthy. Such an ability is said to have been possessed by [Saint Veloth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saint_Veloth), whose ability to distinguish good from bad and skill as a healer were combined in his symbol of power, the artifact [Veloth's Judgment](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veloth%27s_Judgment), which could cleanse souls of corruption by siphoning it from the spirit in a precise manner. Whatever the case, using the same process to siphon a living creature's entire soul instead, can grant the one using it immense power.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Jo SV-55)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Veloth-56)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Vox-57)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Boethiah-58)</sup> It is said that even a magically fractured mind retains subconscious memories of past experiences such as echoes of past trauma, which are written in the fault lines of the soul itself, and can be read to reveal the truth by those who know how.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Mirrormoor Art-59)</sup> According to some accounts, the Hunger of [Sep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sep) exists deep within the core of every mortal, it is a thing that can never be sated and is related to mortals being overtaken by greed. It is said that those who pursue avaricious desires can be taken body and soul by the Hunger of Sep, leading to calamity. Being taken by this "hunger" is said to be what caused the [Samara](Lore_Samara.md) Scarabs to transform into the [Assassin Beetles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Assassin_Beetle) of today, growing larger and sprouting rending claws and mandibles due to its influence.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-THOS-60)</sup>

### Transition to the Afterlife
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/ON-npc-Jahones_Valain_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Jahones_Valain_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jahones_Valain_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jahones_Valain_02.jpg) The spirit of a living individual on the precipice of death The soul of an individual on the precipice of death can manifest in a spectral form, similar to a ghost. A soul burdened by strong emotions such as regret can splinter, its parts manifesting simultaneously in multiple locations tied to those emotions.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Jahones-61)</sup> Such souls, caught between life and death, can be prevented from moving on to the afterlife through the use of powerful magic. However, even the power of a [Luminary](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary) can't prevent a soul from passing on to Aetherius indefinitely, and powerful [wraiths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wraith) might eventually arrive to force the transition into Aetherius.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Netch-62)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-End-63)</sup> It is said that after death a soul can wander for a time, its path forward darkened by its transgressions in life. A soul can sense the afterlife calling to them when its time for them to move on. Such spirits, held back by guilt and unresolved affairs, might refuse entry into the afterlife even if they have been accepted into it, holding themselves back as long as their unresolved affairs remain. Being summoned to provide aid to the living, through lending their strength or inhabiting and animating bodies willingly, is said to be one of the ways through which spirits can resolve the issues that keep them from the afterlife, once employed by certain branches of necromancy such as that used by the Order of the Hidden Moon. In some cases these powerful unresolved emotions might cause the soul itself to crack and be wounded, allowing for a [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) to be created within it. Forgiving one's self can heal the Void-wound, allowing that spirit to proceed to the afterlife beyond.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Notes-39)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Eifa-64)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cantor-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Zerith-37)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONLi De-65)</sup> One Argonian spirit claimed to be able to hear the call of the Void beckoning him through sounds of chaos and destruction when it was time to move on.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Akech-66)</sup>

### Related Arcane Phenomena
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/ON-quest-The_Dark_Behind_the_World.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Dark_Behind_the_World.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dark_Behind_the_World.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dark_Behind_the_World.jpg) The Void within the soul of Zerith-var manifested as a planar rift to Azurah's Crossing Souls are described as being linked to the origin of certain phenomena. When a mortal dies their memories become [Water](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Water_Magic),<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Herald_Kixathi-67)</sup> with rivers of memory even connecting Nirn to realms beyond,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Vistha-Li-68)</sup> and the oceans being described as extending beyond the three dimensions mortal senses perceive, and as having a true nature that can be fatal to witness.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Augur-69)</sup> Because of this, with the proper knowledge, the memories drawn from mortals can be coalesced to create a Water Stone, an artifact capable of creating an endless supply of water, said to be very close in nature to that of a nature spirit of water.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Lorelia-70)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Herald_Kixathi-67)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Water Stone-71)</sup> According to some sources all waterways connect the realms of the living and the dead, being the means by which spirits cross over into the living world.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ONDomitia-72)</sup> The memories a mortal accumulates throughout life, much like their lifeforce, can be tapped into through magic, potentially serving as a source of power for spells and curses. Absorbing a spirit's memories is equated to absorbing its essence itself, thus lifeforce drawn from the body can manifest as memories, and memories either manifested through magic or tied to an object with emotional significance can provide the one they originate from with lifeforce. A spirit's memory is known to serve as a potent source of mortus energy, the magical energies of necromancy.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Mjolen-73)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Maxten-74)</sup> Through the use of magic, a mortal's memories of another individual can even be extracted to create a version of that individual that can go on to substitute the original in the role they were meant to perform.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-ONNaanurrel-75)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ONLandolvel-76)</sup> In addition, a mortal experiencing suffering or death is said to produce necromantic energies which can stain the area where such events occurred and trap the souls of those present to haunt it, or serve as a reserve of magic for necromancers.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Stain-77)</sup> It is said that those who died within a perilous area such as a swamp never really leave, thus their tormented ends can infuse the very land itself, haunting and transforming the living things that reside there, and causing them to take on spectral traits themselves.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Sepulchral-78)</sup> A soul's essence can be given fully in order to cast especially powerful magic, sacrificing the spirit's independent spectral existence in the process. Such an event occurred when the spirit of the mage Alanwe sacrificed herself to banish the fully powered [Ebony Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Blade) from Tamriel for a long time and cursed it to never stay with any one wielder for long, despite the will of its creator [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and the Blade's magic attempting to resist.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOAlanwe-27)</sup>

A soul is said to develop arcane connections to people and objects interacted with, and indeed it is said that because of this all things are magically connected, practicioners of [Soul Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic) can perceive these connections and even influence them should they desire to, touching or strengthening them with focus, this is described as "deep magic" compared to something like weaponizing soul essence for use in combat.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-79)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-80)</sup> As emotions of love are said to create a real arcane connection between two souls, one who has such a bond with another soul can draw upon the loved one's soul for power should they know how. In cases where a soul's strong unresolved emotions have caused a Void to form within it, this arcane link can allow for that Void to be tapped into, serving as a source of great power. A soul's Void can even be used to create a rift across the [planes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence), a shortcut allowing one to bypass planar boundaries to the afterlife. <sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Notes-39)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cantor-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Zerith-37)</sup> It is said that siphoning a soul's essence can prolong one's life.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-SJ-81)</sup>

### Souls with Distinct Characteristics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-creature-Rakkhat_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Rakkhat_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Rakkhat_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Rakkhat_02.jpg) A Dro-m'Athra, a spiritual state of Khajiit souls [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/ON-creature-Siluran_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Siluran_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Siluran_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Siluran_02.jpg) The soul of an Altmer Because of their connection to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), the souls of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) are said to have unique qualities not shared by souls of other races of Tamriel.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-82)</sup> Normally when they die they are able to return to the Hist tree which is bound to their tribe, but if that is prevented or the tree is suffering, the soul may remain as a ghost.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-TMDeyapa-83)</sup> The souls of Khajiit are also described as having unique qualities, an innate duality or choice to be made not found in other souls, which is what allows for their transformation into [Dro-m'Athra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra), dark spirits who have been described as "bizzare mutants" of a Daedric nature by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) themselves. It is said by master necromancers that to view Dro-m'Athra as either just a form of Daedra or as just corrupted mortal souls is not quite accurate.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Vastarie-53)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Era-84)</sup> Khajiit [Twilight Cantors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Twilight_Cantors) have described the Dro-m'Athra as the "shadowed face" of the Khajiit which echoes the light and dark facets of the [moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons), a form the Khajiit takes after succumbing to the Dark Heart's call, which the Khajiit can naturally hear, due to rage and anguish.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Viti-85)</sup> A Dro-m'Athra's formation is described as the result of the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) seeping into cracks which are created in the soul when one hears the beat of the [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Zerith-37)</sup> Such cracks which can invite the Void are also said to form when one feels an emotion so strong it fills the spirit so completely as to make it crack.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Notes2-86)</sup> The Bent Dance calls to the spirits of the Khajiit even in death, and spirits which have not yet reached [Azurah's Crossing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow) can still hear and be lured by it.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Eifa-64)</sup> One Khajiit account describes how a Khajiit souls travels to [Azurah's Crossing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow) after death, and encounters Azurah herself. At that point the spirit remembers that it has lived and experienced the passage to the afterlife many times, and mentions it to Azurah. To this observation Azurah responds that the spirit has "walked [many paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md)" to stand before her.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Crossing-87)</sup> The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) view themselves as "true children of the Et'Ada", descendants of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) and of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), which is to say of the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) or "Divines" that preceded the Aldmer, in an "unbroken line" dating back to [Nirn's](Lore_Nirn.md) creation. Thus they believe that the "divine spark" is housed within each of them.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Oghma-88)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mystery-89)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-90)</sup>

### Nature and Spiritual Transformation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-npc-Cipher_Akacirn.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Cipher_Akacirn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cipher_Akacirn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cipher_Akacirn.jpg) A living soul in the process of becoming decoupled from the corporeal Though certain aspects of souls, such as them being a source of magical energy, are understood, their true nature has been described as ultimately unknowable, with every mage who has attempted to discover it vanishing without a trace.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SBAW-4)</sup> Indeed, the Numinous Grimoire, an arcane tome held in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) that is said to be a dissection of the mortal soul in both life and death, and contains rites that allow one to "decouple" their soul from the physical and become an immortal living spirit, is said to destroy the minds of any mortal who reads it, with the knowledge within only being transmittable through the aid of immortals who've read it first.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Tracts-91)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Plautis-92)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Uldazaan-25)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg) The Dragon Luminary, theorized to be an Ascended Ancestor [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-creature-The_Crow.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Crow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Crow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Crow.jpg) The Crow Luminary, born from part of the soul of Ulfsild the Evergreen Some sources, such as [Spinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinner) [Girnalin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Girnalin), claim that mortal spirits experience a change upon reaching Aetherius, with many souls of mortals becoming spirits of a different sort in that place.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ONGirnalin-93)</sup> Many reports from across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) describe a phenomenon where the souls of the dead adopt a variety of forms to return and speak with the living. Animals, plants, or even fearsome visions, are all reported manifestations. It is theorized that the Luminaries, immortal beings of pure magic, might have originated as mortal souls, being the spirits of Ascended Ancestors that transformed into their current state after death due to this described phenomenon. As the Luminaries themselves can't recall their origins, this theory remains unproven.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-LAS-94)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-95)</sup> Whatever the case, however, a Luminary can indeed be created out of the essence of a mortal soul, as was the case with the Crow Luminary, who was created through a ritual devised by [Ulfsild the Evergreen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulfsild) out of a portion of her own essence. Though the process is straining and the restoration of the spirit takes time, a soul that was used in such a manner will eventually recover.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Crow-96)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-ONU-97)</sup><sup>[[98]](#cite_note-Fable-98)</sup>

The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) maintains that souls that cross over into the afterlife can grow to gain great power and rule there, with a particularly malevolent tyrant for example, feeding into the power of Daedra like [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) through their acts in life, and going on to "rule in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md)" after death. The Psijics believe that the "Daedra and gods to whom the common people turn" are themselves "no more than the spirits of superior men and women whose power and passion granted them great influence in the afterworld", and that while living they too were "bewildered" by their own ancestors going back to the original progenitors, the [Acharyai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Acharyai).<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Old-99)</sup>

[Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) likewise maintains that the spirits of the dead continue to exist in the world, being able to "know and affect the future, speak with other spirits and work great magic". They believe that recently deceased spirits are weaker but close to the living in spirit, quick to guide and protect, whereas older spirits are stronger but stranger and more distant, the Daedra being the oldest and greatest but also strangest of these. Malevolent spirits are considered "daemons and "devils" from whom benevolent " Hero" spirits protect.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-Ancestor-100)</sup>

Similar beliefs are found among the [Tsaesci](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tsaesci) who believe ancestor spirits grow more powerful the more they are revered, thus allowing a well-honored ancestral spirit to cross from the afterlife into the living world with greater ease, take on different forms such as becoming a black bladed "golden warrior" that's "swift as the wind", grant great fortune or misfortune, commune through and alter dreams, and drag the living to the afterlife if angered.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-Clever-101)</sup><sup>[[102]](#cite_note-ONProconsul-102)</sup><sup>[[103]](#cite_note-Proving-103)</sup>

### Role
According to the Daedric Prince Ithelia mortals, by their very existence and lives, help preserve and maintain [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md), every child born, war fought, or field plowed by mortals, helps stabilize reality itself. It is said that mortals were designed as servants to perform tasks that maintain reality and so, the limitations that constrain them, such as the inability to comprehend and traverse the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), were placed upon them intentionally. This truth, Ithelia claims, is generally kept from mortals, to minimize the risk of them disrupting the plans of those who benefit from this secret. Despite this, there are certain [individuals](Lore_Hero.md) who have been shown to not be bound by at least some of the usual mortal limitations, being able to comprehend and traverse the Many Paths without issue.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup>

Some sources claim that the reason for the cycle of life and death is that there exist too many souls for the physical world to hold, thus necessitating the existence of a process through which souls can spend some time living, learning, and progressing, before being ushered away to the spiritual realms so other souls can take their place. Keeping this balance of life and death is said to be the role of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) as a [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods).<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Birthand Death-104)</sup>

The essence of a mortal soul is one of those influences that, when joined with purest [creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) from the realm of a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) or [Daedra Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Lord), can give rise to a [Demiprince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demiprince).<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Whim-105)</sup>

## Vestiges
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-creature-Soul_of_Dusk.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Soul_of_Dusk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Soul_of_Dusk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Soul_of_Dusk.jpg) The Soul of Dusk, a Daedric animus [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-npc-Ithelia_and_Torvesard.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ithelia_and_Torvesard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_and_Torvesard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_and_Torvesard.jpg) A Dremora having his essence returned to nothingness—effectively the closest thing a Daedra can have to a peaceful death Daedra lack an Anuic Animus, also known as a mortal soul,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CCTAP-1)</sup> each one instead possessing a Daedric soul known as a **Vestige** (sometimes also called an Animus).<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Dylora-106)</sup> When a Daedra is destroyed, either in Mundus or in Oblivion, its Vestige is banished to the "[Waters of Oblivion](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md)", and than attracted back to the plane of Oblivion where it originated.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-TBOD-107)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Dylora-106)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-OBProphet-108)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-OBStaada-109)</sup> A Daedra reforms according to the pattern within its Morphotype (a concept which seems to be interchangeable with the Vestige though it more closely relates to the form of a Daedra) using [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) (e.g. the [Azure Plasm](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md) of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md)).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CCTAP-1)</sup> The reconstitution process has been described by at least one type of Daedra as being both irritating and unpleasant, involves action on the part of the discorporate Daedra, and can result in minor variations in the final reconstituted form, such as a body part that seems "shorter than it used to be."<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-ONScruut-110)</sup> A [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) can create a new Daedra by replacing an entity's original Animus with a Vestige, a process which was used to give rise to the original [Daedric Titans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Titan).<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-DDTT-111)</sup>

Unlike mortals, Daedra and their Vestiges are described as largely immutable and static, incapable of experiencing change in spiritual nature or certain aspects of character. It is said that they never grow or change, as that is the nature of Daedra who are "immortal" and "forever". This unchanging nature is described as applying even more so to Daedric Princes, with even the Princes themselves expressing inability to truly destroy or permanently remove the power of another Prince, even if that Prince desires it to be so. A case of this phenomenon was seen with the Daedric Prince Ithelia, who was stripped of her power and rendered "as powerless as a mortal" when first imprisoned, but couldn't be destroyed and gradually regained her power upon being released. Later, when Ithelia willingly relinquished her power to rid herself of the destructive rage it caused to grip her, it was determined that her nature as a Prince would always restore her to power and freedom sooner or later, whether she desired it or not, also causing the rage to return. This, the true nature of a greater Daedra's "princely disposition" made it necessary for Ithelia to be exiled to a [different reality](Lore_Many_Paths.md) due to the threat she posed to [existence](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDungeons-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Scruut-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mora-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Leramil-12)</sup>

This immutability is reflected in some aspects of a Daedra's nature. A Daedra's form and nature are said to be defined by their [Nymic](Lore_Nymic.md) or "incantatory true name" in its one changeless form, described as a "pattern or formula that defines the being created when it manifests itself from the eternal chaos of Oblivion".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Nature Nymics-14)</sup> According to [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), immortals such as the Daedra do not share the mortal quality of always having a chance to succeed and reach a desired outcome even when it would otherwise be impossible, and would inevitably fail in the same circumstances.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mora-11)</sup> This limitation of Daedra is also affirmed by the Daedric Prince Ithelia, according to whom the natural ability of mortals to choose who they are and alter their own [fates](Lore_Fate.md) naturally and without the need of magic or divine power, is something which beings such as herself or her servants are incapable of.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup> The Vestiges of Daedra are unlike the souls of mortals in that they are fettered to existence.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Uldazaan-25)</sup>

According to the [Mazken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mazken) Madam Whim, a renowned information broker operating out of [Fargrave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave), the original formation of the Vestiges of lesser [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) is generally the work of a progenitor Daedric Prince, who forms them out of the Creatia of their realm and sets their nature and path at the time of their making.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Whim-105)</sup> There have been cases of lesser Daedra that were created by their progenitor Prince to accomplish a specific purpose or task, such as the scions of Ithelia, who were created by their Prince to help avert her foreseen imprisonment after a conflict with the other Daedric Princes.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup>"

Though normally unchanging, a Daedra's Vestige and therefore its Morphotype can be altered and given new qualities through vestigial hybridization, a process through which a Vestige is given traits originating from a different Vestige thus allowing a new type of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to be created. Examples of this process include the creation of the [Xivkyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivkyn) by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) through the combination of elements from both [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) and [Xivilai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivilai) within the [Vile Laboratory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vile_Laboratory), and the transformation of [Valkyn Skoria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valkyn_Skoria), who became a being of elemental fire through the addition of elements from [Flame Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Atronach) to his Vestige by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Questions-112)</sup>

It is possible to trap the animus of a Daedra by magically infusing it into an item.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-Lyranth-113)</sup> It is not known what happens to a Daedra killed in Aetherius,<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Quest-114)</sup> though it has been suggested that this may be one way to permanently kill a Daedra.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-DOME-115)</sup> While normally considered immortal, even by their own reckoning, a lesser Daedra can experience a form of death through the rare method of its Vestige being changed by a [Cataclyst](Lore_Cataclyst.md), a device designed by the [Moth Priest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moth_Priests) Elegian which permanently merges multiple Daedric Vestiges into one to create an [Incarnate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Incarnate), a more powerful Daedra which is said to be the incarnation of a natural disaster, effectively eliminating the original identities of the combined Daedra and killing them even though their essence persists.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-Dothaz-116)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-Lyranth Disaster-117)</sup> There is one known way for a lesser Daedra to be rendered incapable of ever returning, and that is for their own progenitor Prince to consign their essence to nothingness. The [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) [Torvesard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torvesard) met his final end at the hands of his Prince, Ithelia, in this manner.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ithelia-9)</sup>

According to Daedric sources, the manner in which the Daedra perceive each other's essence can differ from how mortals view them; sources from [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) describe them as viewing others of their kind as a "belligerent collision of acute hyper-angles."<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-Dremora Style-118)</sup>

Some sources claim that Daedra do not inhabit their bodies in the same manner mortals do, but rather their consciousness floats adjacent to their physical form, allowing them to influence it, or even another object, to move as they desire from the outside by exerting their will.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-True Daedra-119)</sup> Daedra have been known to sometimes possess other beings such as mortals, requiring that they be exorcized. In such cases, the possessing Daedra can be forced out of the body it is occupying and into a physical form through the use of certain holy items.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Obsessed-120)</sup> Daedra affiliated with [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) have been known to infiltrate the minds of their target through their dreams, warping their mindscape to influence them or even seize full control, abilities also exhibited by mortals blessed by that Prince.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-Betrayal-121)</sup><sup>[[122]](#cite_note-Galthis-122)</sup><sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Omen-123)</sup><sup>[[124]](#cite_note-Ansuul-124)</sup> It has been said that the minds of mortals possessed by a Daedra in this manner will never again be fully their own.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-Bogdan-125)</sup> In some cases Daedric beings have been shown to possess and animate inanimate objects in addition to living forms,<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-Ukaezai-126)</sup> having a Daedric spirit take over a body is one of the ways in which [necromancers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) reanimate bodies.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-Summoning-127)</sup> Daedric Vestiges are also one of the means used to animate [Flesh Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flesh_Atronach) and [Golems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem).<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-KCAHC-128)</sup><sup>[[129]](#cite_note-SIRV-129)</sup> Daedric Princes will occasionally possess a mortal body in a similar manner.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-Sheogorath-130)</sup><sup>[[131]](#cite_note-Azura-131)</sup><sup>[[132]](#cite_note-Vaermina-132)</sup><sup>[[133]](#cite_note-Lo SVile-133)</sup>

#### Soul Shriven
[Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven) are the [vestiges](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md) of mortals who have had their souls taken by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Cultists devoted to him ritually sacrifice victims,<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-HLBEF-134)</sup> who are pulled into Coldharbour,<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-Lyris-135)</sup> their souls stolen and replaced by a Vestige at the moment of death.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CCTAP-1)</sup> This process leaves a soul shriven's mind and body intact and allows it to regenerate after death as the Daedra do. However, as they are not native to Oblivion, their bodies are imperfect imitations of what the soul shriven had on Nirn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CCTAP-1)</sup> It also reduces the victim's willpower and causes their sense of self to degrade, allowing Molag Bal to enslave the soul shriven for eternity.<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-HLBEF-134)</sup> However, the process is imperfect, and over time both the mind and body of a soul shriven decay, resulting in a violent, feral, decrepit creature.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-Lyris-135)</sup> Some soul shriven also claim to still feel a connection to their souls, believing that they can feel sensations that correspond to the treatment of the soul gems in which their souls are kept.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-SD-136)</sup>

It has been suggested that the soul shriven experience this effect because mortals require an Anuic principle to maintain their forms. In this case, there might exist "paragon" mortals with a different Anuic aspect, who would retain their forms and sanity after becoming soul shriven, while still becoming immortal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CCTAP-1)</sup> This hypothesis was confirmed during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), during which a [mortal](Lore_The_Vestige.md) became capable of fighting to defend Nirn even without a soul.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-137)</sup>

## Dragon Souls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/SR-creature-Queen_Potema.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Queen_Potema.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Queen_Potema.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Queen_Potema.jpg) The soul of Potema Septim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/SR-quest-At_the_Summit_of_Apocrypha_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-At_the_Summit_of_Apocrypha_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-At_the_Summit_of_Apocrypha_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-At_the_Summit_of_Apocrypha_03.jpg) Dragon soul being absorbed [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) souls are unique in that they generally persist eternally due to the link between a dragon's soul and its physical remains being far stronger than that of a mortal.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-LADit SE-138)</sup> The souls of dragons are said to be exceptionally resilient, making influencing them through [necromantic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) magic a difficult task which requires great power and preparation.<sup>[[139]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-139)</sup><sup>[[140]](#cite_note-Val-140)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-141)</sup> However, it is possible to use powerful [soul magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic) to sever the connection between a dragon's soul and its physical remains, although the effects of this are the subject of fierce scholarly debate, with some speculating that a dragon soul once severed may simply dissolve over time or return to join Akatosh.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-LADit SE-138)</sup> After severing the dragon soul from the remains, a mage may attempt to capture it, but it will be lost to them should they fail to contain it.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-Caluurion-142)</sup>

Dov (Dragons and [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md)) can absorb the souls of fellow dov.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-143)</sup> This grants the recipient a portion of the knowledge and life essence of their fallen opponent, but it also destroys the dov permanently, rendering it beyond the ability of any ritual to resurrect.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-144)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-145)</sup> In addition, certain dragons have claimed to possess the ability to "shred" the unabsorbed souls of other Dov so they may never rise again. <sup>[[139]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-139)</sup>
