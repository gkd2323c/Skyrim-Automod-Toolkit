[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg) Volenfell circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Volenfell ("City of the Hammer" in [Dwemeris](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemeris)) was the capital of the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) clan of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), located in eastern [Alik'r Desert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alik%27r_Desert), southeast of the city of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup>

Volenfell was founded by the Rourken clan, exiled from [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) because they refused to join the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council) and make peace with the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md). The legend holds that the [Rourken chieftain](Lore_Rourken_(person).md) threw his warhammer, [Volendrung](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volendrung), towards the sky and said that his clan would settle wherever it fell. The Rourken subsequently left Resdayn, crossed [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and arrived in the southeastern Alik'r Desert, where they established the city of Volenfell as their capital. Later, the city gave its name to the entire province as well.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/ON-load-Volenfell.jpg/200px-ON-load-Volenfell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Volenfell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Volenfell.jpg) Volenfell At some point between [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) and [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), when the Rourken inhabited Hammerfell,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3H-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DI1-4)</sup> a race of [giant Goblins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giant_Goblin) invaded via their [Goblin gate](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-5)</sup> They possessed a huge army of fighters, and appeared in the middle of the province, where they would pillage and raze towns to the ground, driving the Dwarves out of the province.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RGH-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-5)</sup> The dispossessed Rourken ultimately shared the fate of all Dwemer, disappearing mysteriously around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DI1-4)</sup>

When the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md) conquered Hammerfell, they made use of the abandoned Dwemer cities, taking them as their own. By the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Volenfell, as well as every other former city except for [Dwarfhome](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarfhome), were still inhabited by the Redguards.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RGH-7)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-creature-Guardian_Trinity.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Guardian_Trinity.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Guardian_Trinity.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Guardian_Trinity.jpg) Guardian Council The location of Volenfell was eventually lost, but the ruins of the city were rediscovered shortly before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), prompting a race between scholars and treasure-hunters to be the first to search its storied depths.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONLoading-8)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a Redguard treasure hunter Tharayya and her ex-husband Quintus Verres were working on competing studies of the Dwemer. When Tharayya discovered the lost ruins of Volenfell, Quintus poisoned her and stole her journals in order to get to the ruins first. Tharayya survived, gathered an army of mercenaries and followed Quintus; however, her group was ambushed by Quintus' own mercenaries in Volenfell. Soon afterwards, a group of [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) adventurers entered the ruins. Tharayya offered them a reward for helping her defeat Quintus, recover her research notes and find an ancient Dwemer artifact called [Guardian's Eye](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guardian%27s_Eye). The adventurers defeated Quintus' army and recovered the notes, but they failed to get the Guardian's Eye, which disappeared when Tharayya tried to release it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> The same year, the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) launched an invasion of [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) lands, led by Magus-General [Septima Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septima_Tharn). The Second Cohort of the [Seventh Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion) was ordered to march on Volenfell in order to locate an "item of significance" to the Legion.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> This was likely the Guardian's Eye, although the orders were replaced with forgeries before the Cohort had even reached the Alik'r.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

The mystery of the Eye or its relationship to the undead that overran Volenfell was never solved. No one was willing to head back into the ruins, meaning any clues Tharayya's team left behind were beyond reach.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-11)</sup>
