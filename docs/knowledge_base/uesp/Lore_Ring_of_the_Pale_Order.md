# Ring of the Pale Order

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_the_Pale_Order.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_the_Pale_Order.png) Ring of the Pale Order *Victory unaided, death unsung.*
—Pale Order popular motto The Ring of the Pale Order is a signet ring once belonging to a prominent member of the now-defunct [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) Knights of the Pale Order, who were notorious for their persecution of the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) during the final stages of their flight to Balfiera, around or after the ninth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[nb 7]](#intnote_Pale)</sup> Its enchantments shape the wearer’s behavior, driving them toward isolation and ritualized violence to draw out the ring’s full power. It is said to radiate a cold surge of energy and a deep, nagging paranoia, invigorating the wearer as they inflict damage in combat. The enchantment, however, weakens in the presence of allies, becoming less potent the more companions accompany the wearer. Consequently, members of the Pale Order were known for keeping their own company, and rumors persist that they and their associates were [vampires](Lore_Vampires.md) or practitioners of dark magics. It was not uncommon to see knights of the Pale Order drenched in blood, their strength growing with each passing battle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RPO-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RPOCodex-2)</sup>

The artifact’s power derives from its intricate design. The band itself is broad, heavy, and golden, forming the ring’s sturdy base. A groove along the interior bears an elegy for all those slain by the wearer in unassisted triumphs, along with the engraving, “Killed at my hand,” reflecting the deeply personal and ritualized nature of violence within the Order. The gallery rail, a style dating to the fifth century of the First Era, carries the inscription, “Vigor through Blood,” suggesting that physical strength, endurance, or even vitality itself was believed to increase through killing. Polished onyx stones flank the ring’s signet, engraved with “Austerity, Autonomy, and Ferocity,” a behavioral mandate that explains why the knights neither [married](Lore_Marriage.md), traveled together, nor maintained close social bonds. The ring thus reinforced isolation and self-surveillance, turning its wearer inward and away from society. Though it is unknown whether the original owner of the Ring of the Pale Order was a vampire, the signet identifies them as a member of [House Aurmine](Lore_House_Aurmine.md), vassals to the despotic king of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), Festrien Spenard I, who is believed to have been a vampire posing as all six rulers of the Spenard dynasty.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RPOCodex-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONRKT-3)</sup>
