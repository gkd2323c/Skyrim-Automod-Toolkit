## Lame Corprus

They are more dangerous than their [Corprus Stalker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus_Stalker) counterparts. ## [Lamia](Lore_Lamia.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/ON-creature-Lamia.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Lamia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Lamia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Lamia.jpg) A lamia [Lamias](Lore_Lamia.md) (or **Lamiae**) are a species of amphibious [reptilian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md). They have a serpentine appearance, with the torso of a woman and the tail of a [snake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snake). Despite appearing to be exclusively female, it is assumed they are hermaphroditic or sexless. They repoduce by laying eggs. Lamias are larger than most other mortal [races](Lore_Races.md), and according to one unconfirmed fable, are capable of interbreeding with them. They appear to be cold-blooded and are known to lounge in the sun.

Found in various parts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), they can live in water or on land, although they rarely venture far from the water, and are known to take up residence in abandoned ruins. The smaller, more aquatic Lamia of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) are considered a distinct variety from the larger scaley "desert Lamias" found in other parts of Tamriel such as [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Lamia), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lamia), Betrayal of the Second Era

## Lampen
Lampens are creatures found in [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) that have a [clam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clam) -like taste. They were used by the chefs of Umbriel to make a type of sauce.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-1)</sup><sup>:Part 2, Chapter 1</sup> They were occasionally known to invade Umbriel's cilia tubules, its living filtration system. The master chef Toel claimed his nose would ache if even a single lampen had invaded the cilia tubules.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lo S-2)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 9</sup>

**Found in:** - [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Creatures#Notes)

## Lark
Larks are a type of [songbird](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird). Snow larks are a variety of larks found in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Hilde-6)</sup> while meadow larks were known in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SJ-7)</sup>

Larks are often associated with beautiful singing voices, and skilled bards were sometimes said to possess the voice of a lark.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Hilde-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SGD-8)</sup> Some ships were named after them, such as the *River Lark*.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AJ-9)</sup> Idioms involving larks were present in the cultures of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Nazdar-10)</sup> [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SJ-7)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FFF-11)</sup> and [Nords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nords).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Hilde-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SGD-8)</sup> An infamous thief from [Rosgard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rosgard), active during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), adopted the nickname Lark of Rosgard. He was known for his mask featuring avian motifs.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LOR-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AR-13)</sup>

## [Leaper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leaper)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-statue-Saint_Stental_02.png/200px-ON-statue-Saint_Stental_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Saint_Stental_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Saint_Stental_02.png) Statue of the Leaper and Saint Stental [Leapers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leaper) or **Leaper Demons** are a type of being spoken of in the myths of [Men](Lore_Men.md). They are considered a type of "demon" though what exactly this appellation signifies is unclear. One Leaper was portrayed as a [gargoyle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gargoyle), though it is unknown if this is what the creature truly looked like.

Leaper Demons are spoken of in a dubious [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth, which portrays the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) as having been the "Leaper Devil King" or "King of Leapers". He was described as benevolent creature who leaped around [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and would preserve pieces of [kalpas](Lore_Kalpa.md) before they were consumed by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). Though Alduin was said to have eventually cursed the Leaper Demon King to become Dagon, making him unable to leap anymore and condemning him to eventually destroy all the pieces of kalpas he had once saved. Other myths portray Mehrunes Dagon as created by the [Magna Ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge) to destroy [Lyg](Lore_Lyg.md) in the prior kalpa.

Saint [Stental](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stental) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [saint](Lore_Breton.md#Saints) who is best remembered for slaying the demon of Overlook Hill, rescuing the three Sisters of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) from a terrible fate. A memorial, named "The Saint Slays the Leaper", was erected in [Stormhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven) to honor his courage in [2E 388](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_388), which depicts Stental armed with a sword facing the Leaper demon, depicted as a crouched gargoyle.

## [Leopard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leopard)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-creature-Senche-Tiger_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Senche-Tiger_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Senche-Tiger_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Senche-Tiger_02.jpg) A Senche-Leopard [Leopards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leopard) are a type of elusive, large, predatory [feline](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cat) found in the jungled regions of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), with **Snow Leopards** being found in colder regions such as [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Leopards are known for their distinctive spotted patterns, which aid them with blending into their surroundings in their forest homes.

Many of the cats known as leopards in southern Tamriel are actually a type of [Senche-Cat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-Cat), known as a **Senche-Leopard**, differentiated by their larger size. The two species may often be confused however due to their similar appearance. Leopards and senche-leopards are among the most feared predators of the [Tenmar Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest) of Elsweyr.

Many [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) have leopard-like fur patterns. Some breeds of [snowy sabre cat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snowy_Sabre_Cat) are named for their resemblance to snow leopards.

## [Lich](Lore_Lich.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/LG-cardart-Miscarcand_Lich.png/200px-LG-cardart-Miscarcand_Lich.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Miscarcand_Lich.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Miscarcand_Lich.png) A lich [Liches](Lore_Lich.md) are undead [necromancers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) who have embraced the power of lichdom, the most sophisticated form of Undeath. It is commonly done by transferring their [soul](Lore_Soul.md) into an object called a 'Phylactery'. They are typically selfish and power-hungry, destroying all in their search for souls to repair the Phylactery. Extremely intelligent and powerful, a commonly held myth is that if the Phylactery is destroyed the lich will also be destroyed; however, the Phylactery simply serves as a vessel during the transference, and once the necromancer has transcended the Phylactery is of no consequence.

Though destroying the Phylactery will not automatically destroy a lich, a number of liches have used their Phylacteries as an anchor to allow them to revive after being slain. In such cases the destruction of the Phylactery is required in order to permanently slay the lich itself, with the Phylactery being described as an object to which the lich is bound due to their unique nature, and a tether along which the lich can return to the world, thus making their death no longer a concern. Phylacteries have also been likened to a spiritual focus, like an arcane vessel or container that contains larger portions of one's soul, thus making them powerful but dangerous tools. The form a Phylactery takes can vary, and there have been cases of liches using their towers, or even an entire [realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arum-Khal%27s_Realm) they created themselves as a Phylactery, to anchor themselves and revive after death.In some cases, permanently destroying the lich might require shattering its otherwise impervious Phylactery with tools imbued with the lich's own power, and physically killing it before it can recreate a Phylactery while in its "hemomorphic spiritual form". In others one might have to shatter multiple Phylacteries and vanquish the slivers of the lich's soul guarding them in order to destroy the lich itself. There have been liches that achieved such a form of immortality that they could not be permanently slain and had to be sealed away through the use of a sealing ritual and an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) that would bind them to the one future where they failed to escape.

There have been cases of slain liches whose [souls](Lore_Souls.md) were extracted through rites of cleansing possessing their would-be killers and consuming their spirits from within, instead, merging the lich and its vanquisher into a composite being. Such was the case for lich Erandur-Vangaril, a lich formed when the spirit of the former [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) wizard Vangaril, was consumed from within by the spirit of his former friend whom he had slain, the lich Erandur.

- Variations: [Ancient Deathpriest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancient_Deathpriest), Ancient Lich, Ayleid Lich, [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), Nether Lich

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Lich), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Lich), [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Lich), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Lich), [Oblivion Mobile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/OBMobile:Lich), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Ayleid_Lich.md)<sup>([CC](Skyrim_The_Cause.md))</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lich), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Miscarcand Lich), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Liches), [Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Lich), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Lion Ibis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion_Ibis)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-crown_store-Glinting_Talon_Quasigriff.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Glinting_Talon_Quasigriff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Glinting_Talon_Quasigriff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Glinting_Talon_Quasigriff.jpg) A Glinting Talon Quasigriff The [Lion Ibis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion_Ibis) (often referred to informally as a **bird-beast**) is a unique form of [gryphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gryphon) native to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). They differ from normal gryphons in that their head resembles that of an [Ibis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ibis), a [bird](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird) considered sacred to [Tu'whacca](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tu%27whacca). Winged lion ibises are depicted often in ancient [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) statuary dating back to the time of the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada). Gryphons such as these were considered sacred to the Glinting Talons, an ancient group of [sword-singers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers) devoted to [Tava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tava) and [Diagna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diagna), who thought that the bird-beasts served Tava "in all things".

Wingless [quasigriff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quasigriff) varieties of the lion ibis were used as mounts by the Glinting Talons. These are referred to as **Glinting Talon Quasigriffs**, and possess a brilliant bright red-orange plumage. The Glinting Talons seem to have ridden them in the Battle of Six Hands, depicting them in ancient carved doors. Some of the Glinting Talons magically took on the plumage of the lion ibises in the name of Tava and Diagna. They also raised what appeared to be normal gryphons.

## [Living Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Living_Armor)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-card-Dragonclash_Living_Armor_Senche.png/200px-ON-card-Dragonclash_Living_Armor_Senche.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-card-Dragonclash_Living_Armor_Senche.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-card-Dragonclash_Living_Armor_Senche.png) A suit of Living Armor [Living Armors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Living_Armor) are [animated armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem#Armor) [constructs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) of [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) origins, associated with cults of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). Living Armors exist in various forms, ranging from humanoid-shaped suits of armor, to large, [feline](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-Cat) -like mounts.

They were forged by master Nord [smiths](Lore_Smithing.md), designed to offer both swiftness and warmth. Their overlapping plates moved with tireless grace, animated by the fire burning within. The religious text *Dedication Ceremony* describes armor consecrated to Akatosh as allowing one's inner flame to shine brightly through the forge's creation, spreading light and guiding others toward wisdom. In contrast, armor dedicated to Alduin is said to channel the fury in the World-Eater's belly and the rage in his visage, allowing the creature's wrath to be felt by all who stand against them.

Magic that allowed mortals to assume forms resembling the humanoid-shaped Living Armors existed. The Akatosh armor, Time's Herald Living Armor, was described as a golden-scaled suit, wreathed in righteous flames and untouched by the passage of time. In contrast, Alduin's form, World-Eater's Living Armor, evoked the razor-sharp hunger of the World-Eater himself, its shadowy scales inspired dread, and its presence stirred a scorching, apocalyptic rage.

## Lizard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-creature-Lizard.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Lizard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Lizard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Lizard.jpg) A lizard Lizards are small [reptiles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) that can often be seen basking in the sun, their habitats include deserts or more tropical environs.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Lizards are typically harmless, living on a diet of small bugs, but some are poisonous, or feast on live flesh. It can be nearly impossible to tell harmless ones apart from the deadly species.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Lizards are able to smell with their tongues and can make good trackers.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> [Argonians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Argonians) regard lizards as clever and lucky creatures.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SNTI-23)</sup>

## [Lizard Bull](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Bull)
The [Lizard Bulls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Bull) are a mysterious [race](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Race) of [beasts](Lore_Beastfolk.md) indigenous to the islands along the coast of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md). They were accidentally discovered during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) on an ill-fated expedition to Pyandonea by a crew of pirates, who were led by the infamous Captain Wereshark. Due to both the immediate hostility between the two parties and their sheer remoteness from Tamriel, little is known about them beyond what was documented during this expedition.

Captain Wereshark's first mate described them as massive quadrupedal monstrosities, wielding "glittering [trident](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trident) (s) shiny as one you'd see carried by some stuffy [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) Queensguard". The first mate theorized that they may have killed the inhabitants of the island who lived within the stick huts scattered throughout, though whether any other race ever truly lived there was left unclear.

Lizard Bulls are extremely durable and skilled at combat, with just eight of them of defeating an entire [Maormeri](Lore_Maormer.md) armada. Their level of intelligence is unfortunately unknown, as Wereshark's crew never had a chance to speak to them prior to their mage assaulting one with a fireball to the face.

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Saga_of_Captain_Wereshark)

## [Lizard-Steed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard-Steed)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/ON-creature-Senche-Lizard_Steed.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Senche-Lizard_Steed.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Senche-Lizard_Steed.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Senche-Lizard_Steed.jpg) A Senche-Lizard **Lizard-Steeds** (sometimes spelled **Lizard Steeds**) are [Useful Lizards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Useful_Lizard) shaped by the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) to look like large animals commonly used as mounts by the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The Hist shaped them as such to allow the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) people to travel long distances in the swamps. [Horses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse), [wolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf), and other creatures traditionally used as mounts are not suited for long travel through the deep swamp; these mounts can get stuck in the mud, catch [sloughskin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sloughskin) from [fleshflies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fleshfly), or eat poisonous flora and die. Lizard-Steeds do not have such issues.

The origin of the Lizard-Steeds is similar to the Argonians' own origins. The Argonian Parable of Becoming states that the Hist found the humanoid forms of Men and Mer useful, molding the form of the swamp's lizards after them to create Argonians.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Senche-Lizard_Steed)

## [Lizard Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Man)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/AR-creature-Lizard_Man.gif/200px-AR-creature-Lizard_Man.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Lizard_Man.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Lizard_Man.gif) A lizard man [Lizard Men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Man) (or **lizardmen**) are bipedal [lizard](Lore_Lizard.md) -like creatures, though once thought to be distant cousins of the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), it was soon evident they had nothing "human" within them whatsoever. They are described as a race of carnivorous [reptiles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile), whose use of language stretches only far enough to communicate the location of their prey to the rest of their hunting party, which then move in to attack and feed. These creatures are easily identified as cold-blooded reptiles due their distinctive eyes, claws, spiked tails, fangs, and scales which glisten in the light. They have been identified with various descriptors such as "reptilian humanoids", "man-beasts", and have been described as "a [hybrid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Multiraciality) of a [man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Human) and a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md)". They are known to posses the ability to paralyze their opponents, and despite their bestial nature, they are capable of wielding axes and shields which they use to attack with and defend themselves.

Various factions and forces were known to utilize lizard men as foot soldiers or guards. Lizard men were found all throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), and were known to be among [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s legions. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) were known to use lizard men to patrol their guild halls across Tamriel during night hours. Various criminal groups such as the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md), [the Necromancers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult), and the [Wharf Rats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wharf_Rats) were known to utilize the aid of lizard men to deal with their enemies. The [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) was known to use lizard men as minions, with them also being among the beasts that infested [Stonekeep](Lore_Stonekeep.md) during its occupation by [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) working for [Golthog the Dark](Lore_Golthog_the_Dark.md). One lizard man was among the specimens contained in the Bestiary of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md).

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Lizard_Man)

## [Lurcher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lurcher)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-creature-Heart_of_Rootwater.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Heart_of_Rootwater.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Heart_of_Rootwater.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Heart_of_Rootwater.jpg) A Lurcher [Lurchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lurcher) (or **Log-striders**) are lumbering [plant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flora) -like creatures of multiple origins. They often created as a form of wooden [golem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem), described as constructs made from bark, moss, and evil [magic](Lore_Magic.md). Lurchers often attack by expelling clouds of choking pollen from their glowing core. This pollen has been known to spread [disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diseases) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Within the torso of Lurchers are knotted hearts, which are described as fist-sized acorns that beat like a heart.

[Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) create them by binding evil [spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit) to crudely made bodies of vines, twisted branches, and knurled bark. There also have been instances of [Briarhearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briarheart) from the [Winterborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterborn) clan transforming themselves into [briar heart tree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briar_Heart_Tree) lurchers through [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) rituals involving fire, vines, and blood. Lurchers have been described by [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as the "accursed brothers" or "polluted cousins" of [Spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan). Their crude artificial bodies are said to contrast them with the lithe forms of the Spriggans created by [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md). The [Bloodthorn Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodthorn_Cult) performs a perverted ritual using Spriggans as a base, twisting the creatures into lurchers and leaving them as shells of their former selves. Members of the [Beldama Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beldama_Wyrd) considered these lurchers abominations.

Lurchers are looked upon positively in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), where they are created by the local [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) frequently. The Bosmer create Lurchers using the local flora as a base, adding other ingredients such as bug parts to give their lurchers special [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) qualities. The Shimmering Kollopi Tree produces seeds that can be grown into vengeful lurchers. In [Greenshade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenshade), the tribal Bosmer see lurchers as gifts from the [Wilderking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wilderking), and "embodiments of the raw energy of the forest". The lurchers that resided within [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen) were known to coexist peacefully with the native wildlife and [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit), some of them having resided in the area since ancient times.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lurcher), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Lurcher), Betrayal of the Second Era

## Lynx

There are large species of lynxes that can be used as mounts.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-46)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-47)</sup> Most notably, tiger-lynxes that can be found in the [West Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Weald).
