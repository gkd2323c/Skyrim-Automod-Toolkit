*Ah, sure. They're all outside having a good time -- WITH THE PIXIES AND THE FAIRIES!*
—A remark made by the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) to Old Man Chimere<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OMC-1)</sup> Pixies (known as **Whilloki** to [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md)) are a type of [faerie](Lore_Faerie.md) described as appearing as glimmering sparks with the personality of whimsical pranksters.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TF-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OMC-1)</sup> Ahrtabazus included them in his controversial "Faerie Chain" theory from [2E 456](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_456), which connected all the types of fay in a hierarchal chain. The chain began with simple pixies and ended with the faeries considered to be "god-like". Most controverisally, he included Elves as part of this chain, placing just above above whilloki and below [nephrine](Lore_Nephrine.md). Many Elves found it offensive to be placed only slightly above whimsical pranksters such as whillwoki.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TF-2)</sup>

Several small or flame-related creatures have been compared to pixies. Flame pixies are a tiny form of [Flame Atronach](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The Magma Queen was a giant [Nixad](Lore_Nixad.md), another form of fay, that served as a champion of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and was referred to as a pixie.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The burrowing pixie owl is a rare species of [owl](Lore_Owl.md) native to western Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
