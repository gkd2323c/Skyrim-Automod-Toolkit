# Wayshrines


[Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) wayshrines are perhaps the most ancient, built in the early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) in the same style as the ancient Argonian [xanmeers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xanmeer). The stonework used to create these structures has been lost to time, forgotten even by the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Each of the major [races](Lore_Races.md) of Tamriel have a distinct style of construction, generally reminiscent of the regional architecture. Even in Valenwood, where dwellings are generally shaped from living wood in accordance with the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), ancient crude shrines built from large monolithic slabs of rock are found. It is unknown who built them, as both the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) seemed to be unlikely candidates, and the engravings were seemingly meaningless apart from theories that they were made in devotion to [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Estaamo-2)</sup> In the depths of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), menacing wayshrines built from [cold iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron) and dedicated to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) can be found.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

Wayshrines have historically been associated with various religious rites and rituals. The [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) shrines found in the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) were dedicated to [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), and initiates of the [Chantry of Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chantry_of_Auri-El) would ceremoniously fill a ewer with water from each of the wayshrines' basins. Using Snow Elf teleportation magic, these wayshrines also served as a basic portal network throughout the vale, although they were magically sealed for a time following the death of their respective prelates.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

The various wayshrines found throughout [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) were dedicated to the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines). Crusaders and worshippers traditionally performed a pilgrimage to a wayshrine of each Divine to be absolved of sin. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Divines allowed the spirit of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) to speak with a [pilgrim](Lore_Hero.md) who had prayed at each shrine in order to stop the reincarnation of [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril_the_Unfeathered). The pilgrim went on to mantle Whitestrake's position as the Divine Crusader.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

As first theorized by [Corvus Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corvus_Direnni), the wayshrines create an interconnected "web of sojourn" which could be manipulated to create a permanent portal network. In order to do this, the "fast traveler" would have to attune himself to each wayshrine in order to create a "node". However, to test this theory the potential traveler would have to unmoor his soul from the Mundus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Wo T-1)</sup> This theory was proven correct during the time of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), when a number of [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven) escaped from Coldharbour. These soulless mortals, animated by a Daedric vestige and formed from [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md), were capable of using the wayshrines in this manner. Attunement to a wayshrine involved the lighting of a cold flame (or regular fire) in the central brazier. Along with smaller common shrines, the wayshrines also allowed these Soul Shriven who had been re-attuned to [Anuic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) magic to reform in death, being reborn at the nearest shrine instead of being banished to Oblivion like a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

The wayshrine web of sojourn is similar in function to a [Transitus Network](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Transitus_Network), a teleportation network also based on flaming braziers but usable by regular mortals.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
