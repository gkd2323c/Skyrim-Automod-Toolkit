[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/OB-misc-Akatosh_Statue_02.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Akatosh_Statue_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Akatosh_Statue_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Akatosh_Statue_02.jpg) The Temple of the One after the Oblivion Crisis The Temple of the One<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is the premier religious institution of the [Dragonborn Emperors](Lore_Dragonborn.md#Dragonborn_Emperors) and the overall [Imperial Church](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), located in the Temple District of the [Cyrodiil City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTTIC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBJauffre-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Temple IV-3)</sup> The structure itself is dedicated to the ineffable deity of the old [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) named "[the One](Lore_The_One.md)", also known as [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBJauffre-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ao K-4)</sup> the "Supreme Spirit",<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Exclu Man-5)</sup> though other stories conflate or combine this patron deity with [Shezarr](Lore_Shezarr.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOP-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-XL-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Since the time of Empress Alessia, the Temple of the One has served as site of ceremony for the lighting of the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) ritual, which traditionally involves the covenantal bequeathment of the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTTIC-1)</sup>

The Temple's religion is honored throughout all of Tamriel and has a number of different missions throughout the continent, including: the **Brotherhood of the One** and the **Conclave of the One**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARTemples-9)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WAP-10)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> The **Archbishopric of the One**, sometimes referred to as a Primate, serves a powerful religious role throughout Cyrodiil City and over all of Tamriel.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WAP-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Faction IV-11)</sup> The Temple houses Cyrodiil's [Communion of Saints](Lore_Communion_of_Saints.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTTIC-1)</sup> Additionally, the Tamrielic Empire's city-state Kings and Queens are expected to be active members of the Temple.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Real Barenziah-12)</sup>

## History

### Origins
The Temple of the One is said to have originated from an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) cult,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Real Barenziah-12)</sup> but has been more heavily codified as Alessian in nature.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBJauffre-2)</sup>

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/LO-misc-Monkey Truth-%28cropped%29.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Monkey Truth-%28cropped%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Monkey Truth-(cropped).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Monkey Truth-(cropped).jpg) The Prophet Marukh Upon her death, it was said that Empress [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) was buried on the site of the Temple of the One.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TLST-13)</sup>

The Alessian Order founded after her death traces its origins to the prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md), who preached of a Single God and questioned the legitimacy of [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) rule. This depiction of a Single God became increasingly abstract and unknowable, as the Alessians realized they had to incorporate existing ancient polytheistic elements into their new religion for it to find a wide acceptance. This eventually led to the Order declaring itself the authority on every religion in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), leading to a massive growth in power and influence.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1-14)</sup>

Although the [Remans](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) were not known to follow Alessian teachings, they regularly used the temple as a site of worship. Every year for the [South Wind's Prayer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:South_Winds_Prayer), Emperor [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md) always visited the Temple of the One for the yearly service. On the other hand, his son and heir [Juilek](Lore_Juilek.md) visited different temples such as the [Benevolence of Mara](Lore_Benevolence_of_Mara.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-2920V1-15)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/ON-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg/200px-ON-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg) The Amulet of Kings The [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) occurred here in [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), when [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) purposefully corrupted the coronation ritual of [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup> When the Imperial City was captured by the forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) during the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md), the temple was converted to a torture chamber used by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to extract information from the city's residents before executing them. These Daedric forces were cleared out by Alliance War soldiers circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

### Third Era
[Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) is said to have revived the cult from its "ancient Nordic origins" when he took the Imperial Throne.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Real Barenziah-12)</sup>

In [3E 41](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_41), [Tiber Septim's](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) heir, [Pelagius I](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_I.md), was murdered by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) while praying in the Temple of the One.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BHOTE-18)</sup>

Shortly after taking the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md) for himself by treacherous and magical means, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) quickly had Empress [Caula Voria](Lore_Caula_Voria.md) exiled from the palace at the beginning of the Imperial Simulacrum in the early 3E 390s. Empress Voria was given sanctuary at the Temple of the One.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Real Barenziahv5-19)</sup> Later in the reign of [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) after Jagar Tharn had been dispatched,<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> the Archbishop of the One was [Calaxes](Lore_Calaxes_Septim.md), the bastard son of Uriel Septim VII, who attempted to overthrow the Septims and install a theocratic administration. But one night during his revolution, Calaxes disappeared from the world and was never heard from again. It was speculated that the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md)'s [Allena Benoch](Lore_Allena_Benoch.md) was responsible for assassinating him, but these rumors were never confirmed or denied.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WAP-10)</sup>

The Temple was the scene of the fiery resolution of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md): [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md)'s sacrifice of the [Amulet of Kings,](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) the banishment of [Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) from Mundus, and the creation of the Statue of Akatosh.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-OBDragonfires-20)</sup> The Temple structure was heavily damaged during the bought, with the Statue of Akatosh's wings extending beyond the structure itself.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-OBDragonfires-20)</sup>

### Fourth Era
During the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Imperial City was captured by the forces of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) under the command of [Lord Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md). It is said Naarifin was a worshipper of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and planned to bring about a prophecy known as "[the Culling](Lore_Great_War.md#The_War)", which involved the mass summoning of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Naarifin took control of a building which appeared to be the Temple of the One for this purpose and opened a portal to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), but was confronted and defeated by a small group led by the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Showdown-21)</sup> Notably, the Temple's structure seemed to have been repaired since the time of the Oblivion Crisis.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Showdown-21)</sup>
