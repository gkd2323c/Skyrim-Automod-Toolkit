# Dark Brotherhood

—Description of the Dark Brotherhood in the *[Arena Player's Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Arena_Player%27s_Guide)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARFaction-1)</sup> The Dark Brotherhood is a guild of assassins shrouded in shadow and mystery which has been active all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). While their business is certainly not legal, their existence has typically been tolerated or ignored.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup> They operate from a number of hidden sanctuaries throughout the continent, with their secret headquarters said to be the [Nightside Asylum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nightside_Asylum).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFMephala-3)</sup> Whenever an individual with legal authority takes an interest in their dealings, the Brotherhood relies upon a combination of bribery, blackmail, coercion, and murder to keep their activities hidden. They have been bitter rivals of the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) since the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The Brotherhood is also known for their worship of the Dread Father [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) (personified chaos, also known as the Void).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AKSM-5)</sup> Dark Sisters are accepted into the Brotherhood as equals of Dark Brothers (though members of either gender are often referred to as Brothers).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup>

## Organization
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/SR-place-Dawnstar_Sanctuary_Fivetenets.jpg/200px-SR-place-Dawnstar_Sanctuary_Fivetenets.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Dawnstar_Sanctuary_Fivetenets.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Dawnstar_Sanctuary_Fivetenets.jpg) Ancient tablet of the [Five Tenets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Five_Tenets) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-quest-The_Dark_Brotherhood_Forever.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Dark_Brotherhood_Forever.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Dark_Brotherhood_Forever.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Dark_Brotherhood_Forever.jpg) The Night Mother, as seen in [*Skyrim*](Skyrim_Skyrim.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/ON-concept-achievement-Litany_of_Blood.jpg/200px-ON-concept-achievement-Litany_of_Blood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-achievement-Litany_of_Blood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-achievement-Litany_of_Blood.jpg) The Speaker talks to an assassin near a statue of the Night Mother Like any organization, the Dark Brotherhood has some rules that all members must abide by, though the demand to adhere to them can vary with the leadership.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRLoading-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup> These interrelated rules are the Five Tenets: members must never dishonor the Night Mother, betray the Dark Brotherhood, disobey or refuse to carry out an order from a superior, steal the possessions of a Dark Brother or Dark Sister, or kill a Dark Brother or Dark Sister.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFT-8)</sup> Breaking one of these tenets invokes the Wrath of Sithis, which, it is rumored, is not just a turn of phrase.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFT-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oblivion-9)</sup>

In extreme circumstances, the purposeful extermination of an entire sanctuary can be ordered. This ancient rite, known as [Purification](Lore_Purification.md), is carried out by the [Black Hand](Lore_Black_Hand.md) in order to cleanse the Brotherhood of mistrust and treachery, with the souls of those killed offered to Sithis as a symbol of fealty, while hopefully eliminating traitors in the process.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Lucien-10)</sup>

In contrast to the Morag Tong's veneration of the Daedric Prince [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), the Brotherhood worship the Void – [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNMT-11)</sup> However, they see their murders as more of a business, viewing their duty through the lens of economics rather than religion and honor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNMT-11)</sup> They are thus frequently viewed as being more pragmatic than the Morag Tong.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TOC-12)</sup> These differences have caused bitter enmity between the two organizations.

The Dark Brotherhood has always been led by the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup> Some stories suggest that the Night Mother is the ghost of the first, who became the wife of Sithis and murdered their five children in his honor.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNMT-11)</sup> Others say that the title is merely an honorific given to the current leader.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SW-13)</sup> Some even consider her to be none other than Mephala,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup> who is commonly thought to be the "Patroness of the Dark Brotherhood".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-14)</sup> Regardless, the most well-known and infamous method of acquiring the services of the Brotherhood is by performing the Black Sacrament, a profane ritual that purportedly allows those who perform it to be heard directly by the Night Mother.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AKSM-5)</sup> The ritual involves stabbing some actual body parts (including a heart, skull, bones and flesh) within a circle of candles using a dagger rubbed with the petals of a Nightshade plant while whispering the plea, "Sweet Mother, sweet Mother, send your child unto me, for the sins of the unworthy must be baptized in blood and fear".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AKSM-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-JOCA-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NMR-16)</sup> Next in the command hierarchy is the shadowy Black Hand, which is composed of a Listener and four Speakers.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oblivion-9)</sup> The Listener, of course, listens to the Night Mother, and relays her orders to the Speakers, who then dispatch a Brother to make specific arrangements and carry out the murder.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oblivion-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AKSM-5)</sup> Their identities are kept well-hidden, even within the organization.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oblivion-9)</sup>

For much of the Brotherhood's history, they have had a special relationship with [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), who form a unique sect within the Brotherhood known as the [Shadowscales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowscales).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OBTeinaava-17)</sup> The Brotherhood usually recruits by directly contacting murderers who show promise,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRLoading-6)</sup> but in the Argonian homeland of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), those born under the sign of [The Shadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Shadow) are taken at birth and presented to the Dark Brotherhood. A Shadowscale hatchling is then trained in the arts of stealth and assassination, and lives a life in service to the mighty kingdom of Argonia. Any Shadowscale who lives to come of age is accepted into the Dark Brotherhood as a full member. If a Shadowscale deserts the Dark Brotherhood, the penalty is death.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OBTeinaava-17)</sup> During the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), however, the tradition apparently died out.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup>

## History
[![ON-banner-Dark Brotherhood.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/ON-banner-Dark_Brotherhood.png/150px-ON-banner-Dark_Brotherhood.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Dark_Brotherhood.png)
### Origins
*You inspect the note. There's a bloody handprint and the words "We know." We know what, you wonder?*
—About a Re-Gifted Note<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c5/LG-cardart-Assassin%27s_Ritual.png/200px-LG-cardart-Assassin%27s_Ritual.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Assassin%27s_Ritual.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Assassin%27s_Ritual.png) The Assassins's Ritual, as seen in *[Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)* The Brotherhood's origins are connected with the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong), though how is a topic still debated by scholars.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNMT-11)</sup> But it is widely believed that in the year [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the Morag Tong assassinated Emperor [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup> likely at the behest of [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie), his successor.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-2920v12-19)</sup> In [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324), they evidently assassinated Versidue-Shaie himself, setting off a frenzy among the nobles across Tamriel. The Morag Tong was soon outlawed across the continent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup>

One popular theory holds that the Brotherhood split away for religious reasons. It posits that during the backlash, the Morag Tong appealed to the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), the highest power in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) at that time, to ensure their existence.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup> [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), whom the Tong worshipped as their patron goddess, was seen by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) as the Anticipation of the Tribune [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TA-20)</sup> The idea is that in exchange for tolerance of their continued existence, the Tong ceased their worship of Mephala in exchange for the worship of Vivec. This is where the break would have occurred - the Morag Tong agreed and worshipped Vivec, also agreeing to limit their activities to the First Era level of traditional inter-House rivalry, whereas the Dark Brotherhood apparently objected to both.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup> A similar but distinct theory is that, following Versidue-Shaie's assassination and the subsequent persecution of the Morag Tong, Sithis spoke to a former member and demanded the formation of a new guild of assassins, a guild which would worship him, not Mephala, aim to "marry business with death" and better satisfy the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void)'s hunger for souls.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNMT-11)</sup>

Another story, taken from a purported interview between author Enric Milnes and the Night Mother, says the Dark Brotherhood was an offshoot of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), not the Morag Tong. Milnes was assassinated shortly after he had it published. This account asserts that, some time in the Second Era, a few members of the Thieves Guild found it efficacious to strangle the occupant of the house when performing a burglary.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SW-13)</sup> The woman, who would later become the Night Mother, thought they should formally recognize and allow murder, but the leadership of the Thieves Guild at the time disagreed (and still do<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FOTGF-21)</sup>), thinking it would be bad for their core business. Undeterred, she founded the murder syndicate which would become the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SW-13)</sup> The connection to the Morag Tong only emerged after the assassination of Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak) in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup> or [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431),<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CM1-22)</sup> when several Tong assassins quit that struggling organization and joined the upstart syndicate.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SW-13)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3All-23)</sup> Who murdered the last Potentate has never been clearly established, though some accredit the Brotherhood with the "accounting" of the [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup>

In addition to all this conjecture, a rough date for the formation of the Dark Brotherhood has been advanced. In [2E 358](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_358), a note addressed to "Brother" was found beneath the floorboards of an abandoned house in the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) village of [Jallenheim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jallenheim) which depicted a war going on between two shadowy organizations. Scholars speculate that this note is a rare piece of evidence about the war between the Morag Tong and the Brotherhood.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAD-4)</sup> This would make this note the first recorded evidence about the existence of the Dark Brotherhood. About fifty years later, in [2E 412](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_412), the Blood Queen [Arlimahera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arlimahera) of [Hegathe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hegathe) wrote a short piece in her journals, mentioning that her family had enlisted the services of the Dark Brotherhood since her grandfather's time. Together with the note found in Jallenheim, this puts the formation of the Dark Brotherhood around 2E 358 or 360 at the latest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBOD-2)</sup>

### The Wrath of Sithis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/LG-cardart-Black_Hand_Messenger.png/200px-LG-cardart-Black_Hand_Messenger.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Black_Hand_Messenger.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Black_Hand_Messenger.png) Black Hand Messenger, as seen in *[Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)* In the centuries after the fall of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), the Dark Brotherhood showed just how powerful they really had become. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) they prospered in the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), killing many notable nobles including the self-declared "provincial governor" of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), Fortunata Ap-Dugal and Primate Artorius of the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESO-24)</sup> That same year, a group of Brotherhood assassins led by Elam Drals were sent to re-establish the Dark Brotherhood sanctuary within [Bloodrun Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodrun_Cave).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AUA-25)</sup> Bloodrun Cave served as an honorable tomb for deceased Dark Brotherhood members, including esteemed Shadowscales. Formerly, the Dark Brotherhood had a sanctuary located deep within the cave.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Mim-Jasa-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> However, the Brotherhood were then framed for the murder of several [Elder Councilors](Lore_Elder_Council.md) in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) by the [Order of the Waking Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Waking_Flame). The Vestige and [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eveli_Sharp-Arrow) entered the sanctuary as agents of Leyawiin and spoke with Elam Drals, who confirmed that the Brotherhood was not behind these assassinations. However, the Waking Flame chose this moment to attack the sanctuary, teleporting inside using [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) portal magic. The group managed to escape with their lives, but the attack resulted in the deaths of many Dark Brothers at the hands of the cultists.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AUA-25)</sup> Elam Drals later helped defend the [Ambitions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Four_Ambitions) at [Fort Redmane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Redmane) in order to exact his revenge on the Waking Flame for this attack.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

In [3E 41](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_41), [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s heir, [Pelagius I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_I), was murdered by the Dark Brotherhood while praying in the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BHOTE-29)</sup>

Circa [3E 325](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_325), the Dark Brotherhood was decimated by a vicious internal power struggle. Some suspect that the conflict involved [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md), an artifact long sought by the Brotherhood because of its ability to kill any target in a single strike.

### 3E 405 and the Iliac Bay
[![](https://images.uesp.net/4/4f/DF-NPC-Flat_182_44.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-NPC-Flat_182_44.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-NPC-Flat_182_44.png) A Dark Binder of the Dark Brotherhood [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/DF-place-Dark_Brotherhood_Guildhall.png/200px-DF-place-Dark_Brotherhood_Guildhall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Dark_Brotherhood_Guildhall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Dark_Brotherhood_Guildhall.png) A typical Dark Brotherhood guild hall Shortly after this time the Brotherhood had a strong presence in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) region, having a guildhall in almost every town and city by [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405). The organization's headquarters at this time was the [Nightside Asylum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nightside_Asylum). This version of the Dark Brotherhood were explicit [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) worshippers, with members consistently worshipping her name. Its political structure was also generally different from all other versions of the Dark Brotherhood, with no mention of Speakers, Listeners, Keepers or Silencers. Instead, The Night Mother answered to Mephala, and the Dark Brotherhood to her. Contracts were given out in a standard guild hall by figures called the [Dark Slayers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Dark_Slayers), and members were grouped through a hierarchical ranking system based on level of skill. It was highly intolerant of failure, with members who failed assassinations or went missing punished severely and sometimes hunted down themselves. As the premier assassination guild of the region, it held large influence and was regularly contracted by rulers<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-DFCoa N-30)</sup>, peasants and even the Mages Guild<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DFRM-31)</sup> to carry out accountings, it sought a monopoly on assassination and allowed no rival groups to exist, sometimes executing assassins and their employers for daring to do contract killings without their involvement.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DFDC-32)</sup>

Before the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), an official of one of the cities in the Iliac Bay was close to discovering the Nightside Asylum's secret location. An assassin was sent to dispatch the official, and it was rumored that this assassin was sent by the Dark Brotherhood. In fact, the assassin was actually an [adventurer](Lore_The_Agent.md) who summoned Mephala and was given this task by the Daedric Prince in return for the [Ebony Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Blade).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFMephala-3)</sup>

In [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421), a Dark Brotherhood member named Greywyn Blenwyth began his own crusade against the Brotherhood. Believing to have been ordered by Sithis to remove all non-vampires from the Brotherhood, Greywyn founded an organization known as the [Crimson Scars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crimson_Scars) in hopes of eliminating the Black Hand and taking control of the Brotherhood, before turning or killing the remaining members. His attempt failed when a member of the Crimson Scars betrayed him to the Black Hand, who swiftly retaliated and declared the second Purification of the Brotherhood's history, killing the Crimson Scars except for Greywyn Benwyth and Rowley Eardwulf, who managed to escape.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-GJ-33)</sup> The final fate of Greywyn and his supposed sanctuary remains unknown.

### Foothold in Vvardenfell
Despite having a large contingent in mainland Morrowind, Dark Brotherhood activity on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) was virtually unknown.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TRMatius-34)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Brotherhood attempted to gain a foothold on the island, and allied themselves with the Dagonites, cultists of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) who provided shelter for them. Both the Brotherhood and the Dagonites were repelled fiercely by the Tong; the Night Mother, Severa Magia (one of the cases where a leader of the organization, or of a local chapter of it, claimed that title for themselves), had taken up insufficiently-secret residence on the island, and was assassinated,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MWESM-35)</sup> as were many of the other Brotherhood members in the province. From their corpses, the Morag Tong recovered the [Threads of the Webspinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Threads_of_the_Webspinner) that the Brotherhood had stolen from them.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MWTWS-36)</sup>

In the same year, [King Hlaalu Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth) of Morrowind hired the Brotherhood to assassinate the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), who he feared might challenge his rule. In response, the Nerevarine tracked down the assassins to their sanctuary in the Manor District of [Old Mournhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Mournhold), and killed their local leader, the fairly high-ranking [Dandras Vules](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Dandras_Vules), discovering the latter to be in possession of a contract signed between "the Night Mother" (almost certainly Magia in this case) and a "H" who was relatively easily identified as King Helseth.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TRDB-37)</sup> Helseth later explained to the Nerevarine that the attacks were due to misinformation.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TRHelseth-38)</sup> Although Vules was dead and their hideout cleaned out, the Dark Brotherhood were not completely destroyed: they later made an attempt on [Queen Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah)'s life, but the assassins were killed by the Nerevarine. There is some evidence to suggest that the assassination attempt was a ruse and that the real target was the Nerevarine once again.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TRAAA-39)</sup> Whether or not Helseth was involved in this, or whether the Brotherhood was attempting to take revenge against either him (through Barenziah), or the Nerevarine, or both, is unknown.

### The Purification
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/OB-npc-Lucien_Lachance_03.jpg/200px-OB-npc-Lucien_Lachance_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-npc-Lucien_Lachance_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-npc-Lucien_Lachance_03.jpg) A Speaker for the Black Hand, as seen in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Dark Brotherhood in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) was betrayed from within. Mathieu Bellamont, a trusted Speaker, was secretly attempting to destroy the Brotherhood as an act of revenge for the assassination of his mother.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TD-40)</sup> A new member of the Brotherhood quickly rose in rank and became a Silencer of the Black Hand, purifying the [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) sanctuary and being deceived by the traitor into assassinating most fellow members of the Black Hand.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TD-40)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oblivion-9)</sup> The remaining Speakers, including Bellamont and the newcomer, went to the Night Mother's crypt beneath the Lucky Old Lady statue in [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) to seek her guidance. There, Bellamont revealed himself, killing two of the four Speakers before being slain by the newcomer. The Night Mother made the newcomer her Listener.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oblivion-9)</sup> The Brotherhood began recovering, but the loss of half its Black Hand and the Purification of the Cheydinhal sanctuary left the Cyrodiil branch crippled at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

### Fall of the Dark Brotherhood
The next two hundred years did not treat the Brotherhood well. A combination of external and internal factors not only prevented the Brotherhood from truly recovering, but crippled them further. The [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) strained the organization along with most of Tamriel, and the riots in Bravil, [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), and [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) became significant problems. Several Sanctuaries and bases of the Brotherhood across Tamriel were destroyed or evacuated. The Great War in particular proved costly, as the organization became secondary targets for both major political parties, and their presence in provinces like [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) was eliminated.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup> This made it almost impossible for the Brotherhood to organize contracts in these regions, and further weakened the organization's reputation, power, and influence across the continent.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRLoading-6)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup> The Shadowscale training facility at [Archon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Archon) in Black Marsh was abandoned, and with no influx of new recruits, the Shadowscale tradition itself began to die out.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/LG-quest-Uther%27s_Story_07.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Uther%27s_Story_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Uther%27s_Story_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Uther%27s_Story_07.jpg) Uther Nere, the man responsible for the fall of the Dark Brotherhood (Legends) The Black Hand became divided on the direction of the Brotherhood, with some pushing for expansion while others wanted consolidation. In [4E 186](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_186), the Bruma sanctuary in Cyrodiil was lost with almost no survivors, which served to further disorganize and demoralize the Brotherhood. One by one, other sanctuaries in other countries and provinces fell. By the end of [4E 187](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_187), only four known active sanctuaries remained in all of Tamriel: the Brotherhood's headquarters in the Cheydinhal sanctuary in Cyrodiil, the [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) sanctuary in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), the [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md) sanctuary in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and the [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) sanctuary in the Pine Forest of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup>

In [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188), the Dark Brotherhood would be infiltrated by the spy known as the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md); who was hired by [Alisanne Dupre](Lore_Alisanne_Dupre.md)'s father, Uther Nere, to infiltrate the Dark Brotherhood. He plotted the ultimate destruction of the Dark Brotherhood by killing the Listener, who he claimed slew his wife.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-US-42)</sup> After the Forgotten Hero successfully infiltrated the Dark Brotherhood, one of the first tasks assigned to the hero was to assassinate King Barynia of Wayrest. At the time Wayrest was attacked by corsairs, who King Barynia was secretly working with as part of his efforts to destroy his own enemies within Wayrest, and the sanctuary there would be raided and destroyed as a result.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-CWTR-43)</sup> It's unknown if the Forgotten Hero went through with killing the king or if they spared the king's life.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-CWTR-43)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/LG-quest-Assassins_Found.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Assassins_Found.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Assassins_Found.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Assassins_Found.jpg) Members of the Brotherhood meet up in the ruined Bravil The surviving Black Hand leadership later shut down the Corinthe sanctuary and moved its members to reinforce the crucial Cyrodiil faction. According to [Cicero](Lore_Cicero.md)'s journal, the political and social chaos that was engulfing Cyrodiil and the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) allowed a massive gang war in Bravil to rage unhindered, leading to the death of a few members of the Brotherhood, including the Listener, as well the destruction of the Night Mother's crypt.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup>

The destruction of the Night Mother's crypt wasn't caused by a gang war as claimed by Cicero. After Uther was successful in gathering all the information he needed from the Forgotten Hero, he gathered an army of mercenaries, and he personally led his army to assault the Night Mother's crypt.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-SHo TW-44)</sup> Uther's ultimate goal was to destroy both the Night Mother and kill his daughter and end the Dark Brotherhood once and for all. It is unknown if the Forgotten Hero chose to side with the Dark Brotherhood in the end or stayed loyal to Uther. Either way, Alisanne Dupre died defending the Night Mother's corpse, while also dying at the hands of her own father.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-NMC-45)</sup> During the battle, an assassin named Garnag was able to retrieve the Night Mother's corpse before it could be destroyed and ultimately foiling Uther's plot to destroy the Night Mother.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/SR-interior-Dark_Brotherhood_Sanctuary_04.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Dark_Brotherhood_Sanctuary_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Dark_Brotherhood_Sanctuary_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Dark_Brotherhood_Sanctuary_04.jpg) The Falkreath sanctuary, as seen in [*Skyrim*](Skyrim_Skyrim.md) With the Listener dead and no new Listener chosen by the Night Mother, the Dark Brotherhood was forced to rely on word-of-mouth to gather new contracts. In order to preserve the Night Mother's quickly deteriorating corpse, the surviving Black Hand re-instituted the ancient position of Keeper, charged with keeping the Night Mother's corpse intact so that her spirit could remain in Tamriel and speak to the Listener. However, the Night Mother still did not speak, demoralizing the surviving membership in Cheydinhal further, and the Black Sacraments went unheard. Eventually Rasha, the leader of the Cheydinhal sanctuary and only remaining member of the Black Hand, proclaimed that he was the Listener. However, when he failed to repeat the words that the Night Mother taught to all Listeners and confirmed by all Keepers, he was killed.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup> The Sanctuary was reduced to just three members - Cicero, Garnag and Pontius: then finally to two, when Pontius was slain by a common bandit in the streets, and then to one, when Garnag disappeared without a trace, either dead or deserted.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup>

Soon, the only known remaining member in Cyrodiil was Cicero, the Keeper, who went mad from a combination of isolation, demoralization, and desperation.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), only the Falkreath sanctuary of Skyrim remained active, so Cicero took the Night Mother there, despite the fact that they did not hold to the old ways of veneration of the Night Mother, and their leader Astrid ran it purely as an organization of murderers for hire.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRLoading-6)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CJ-41)</sup> The Falkreath sanctuary was destroyed around this time, and most if not all of its members killed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup> It's unknown whether Cicero reached the sanctuary before its destruction. Some reports out of Skyrim claim the Brotherhood was wiped out. Conflicting reports assert that the Brotherhood not only survived, but succeeded in assassinating the reigning Emperor, [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md), that the Night Mother chose a new Listener, and that the remaining members rebuilt an old sanctuary in [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SRWith-46)</sup>

However, whether or not the Dark Brotherhood succeeded in assassinating Titus Mede II, between two and four members and the body of the Night Mother did survive. Some reports describe Babette, Nazir, and the Listener moving the Night Mother's corpse to the Dawnstar Sanctuary, where they may or may not have been joined by Cicero, before completing their contract on the Emperor. Other, conflicting reports indicate that Cicero did not reach the Falkreath Sanctuary with the Night Mother's body in time to join the others, and was thus not present for the attack. Babette, the child vampire, was also not present in the Sanctuary at the time of the attack.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-SRdestroy-47)</sup>
