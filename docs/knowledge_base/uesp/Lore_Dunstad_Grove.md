[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/SR-place-Fort_Dunstad.jpg/200px-SR-place-Fort_Dunstad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Dunstad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Dunstad.jpg) Dunstad Grove circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Dunstad Grove (also called **Fort Dunstad**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup> is a fortified settlement located in a mountain pass of the main road, directly south of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The fort itself was built around [the Pale's](Lore_The_Pale.md) main road by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) to serve as a highly effective checkpoint station and prison for some of Skyrim's worst criminals.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>:643</sup>

## History
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Dunstad Grove was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Bjarne, and it had a rivalry with [Markarth Side](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Markarth_Side).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena-3)</sup> Dunstad Grove was neighbored by several settlements, including Lainalten to the southwest, [Snowhawk](Lore_Snowhawk.md) to the west, and [Stonehills](Lore_Stonehills.md) to the northeast.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Map Arena-4)</sup>

During the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the ruins of Fort Dunstad were occupied by a local bandit clan, but at this point, an inn known as the *Stumbling Sabrecat*, was found in Fort Dunstad, and it was run by a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) farmer. But the farmer was killed off by the bandits.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup> As the civil war escalated Fort Dunstad would play a pivotal role in the conflict, serving as the Pale's regional stronghold. It was used by the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) as their defense in the greater Pale wilderness, against the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), should they invade the area. Should the Legion gain control over Fort Dunstad, they would have control over the Pale.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
