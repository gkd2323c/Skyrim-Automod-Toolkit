# Construction


With the [Farming](Skyrim_Farming.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md), several construction options become available for [Goldenhills Plantation](Skyrim_Goldenhills_Plantation.md). This requires the use of the [Farmer's Workbench](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Farmer%27s_Workbench) and is slightly different from the methods of construction listed here. For full details, see the **[related page](Skyrim_Farming_Construction.md)**.

## Related Quests
- **[Build Your Own Home](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Build_Your_Own_Home)**: Construct a house for yourself.

### Purchase
Before any building can be done, the first thing you need to do is purchase a plot of land for 5,000 gold. Afterwards, you'll receive a deed to the property, and the home's name and location will be marked on your map. Check [this page](Skyrim_Houses.md#Hearthfire_Houses HF) for requirements you need to meet first.

### Planning & Preparation
After you have purchased a plot of land from the steward of one of the three holds, you will be [directed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Build_Your_Own_Home) to travel to your new property. When you first arrive, you will find a **Drafting Table**, **Carpenter's Workbench**, anvil, chest, and log pile to aid you in construction. The Carpenter's Workbench contains a copy of the book *[The Beginner's Guide to Homesteading](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Beginner%27s_Guide_to_Homesteading)*. The chest contains thirty [clay](Skyrim_Clay.md), one [corundum ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ingot), six [iron ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ingot), and thirty [quarried stone](Skyrim_Quarried_Stone.md), while the log pile contains twenty [sawn logs](Skyrim_Sawn_Log.md). The provided materials are enough to build, but not furnish, the Small House. You can either purchase additional lumber from merchants at wood mills or cut it yourself if given permission from the mill owner, or give your [steward](Skyrim_Personal_Steward.md) gold to purchase it for you; the sawn logs used in construction do not show up in your inventory, appearing instead as a "Log Pile" on your property. If you own more than one property, the same log supply will be shared between them; and when you buy a new plot, if you already have sawn logs in your log pile, then those logs are not lost and you gain an additional twenty logs. Clay and quarried stone can be obtained by mining clay deposits and stone quarries (both of which provide an infinite supply); these are present near your home and throughout Skyrim. You must use iron ingots to craft [hinges](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hinge), [iron fittings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Fittings), [nails](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nails), and combine them with corundum ingots to create [locks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lock) at a [forge](Skyrim_Forge.md) or anvil. [Glass](Skyrim_Glass_(item).md), [goat horns](Skyrim_Goat_Horns.md), and [straw](Skyrim_Straw.md) may be purchased from general goods [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md) or [Khajiit traders](Skyrim_Khajiit_Traders.md); goat horns can also be obtained by looting the bodies of [goats](Skyrim_Goat.md).

The visual design of each house is the same no matter where you choose to build, with each of the three houses requiring the same materials. The Small House contains basic living amenities, including a place to sleep and a chest for storage. The Main Hall provides furnishing options for the three [crafting](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Crafting) skills, so it is not necessary to choose a wing addition specifically for this purpose.

All furnishing options for each room are in predefined locations and, with the exception of standard [items](Skyrim_Items.md) which appear in your inventory, they cannot be moved. However, you may choose not to purchase or create certain furnishings in order to dictate your own style in each room, allowing a moderate level of flexibility in design.

The Small House layout must be built before adding a Main Hall, and the Main Hall must be built before adding a Cellar or any additional wings. Each house can have a maximum of three additional wings added to a completed Main Hall, with three different options for each wing, allowing 27 different wing combinations. Six of the nine options for wing additions include an exterior rooftop area, either a patio or balcony depending on the addition.

Once you've built all available options at a specific Carpenter's Workbench, you can choose to remove it.

### Building Options
- [Small House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Small_House_Layout): A small one-room house that comes furnished with one [chest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chest), with options to build a bed for [sleeping](Skyrim_Sleeping.md), a firepit and [cooking](Skyrim_Cooking.md) pot, one display case, and additional storage (seven usable [containers](Skyrim_Containers.md)). After completion, you may add exterior amenities detailed in the [Exterior](#Exterior) section below. These can be added at any time, whether you expand with further additions to your home or not.
- [Main Hall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Main_Hall): A much larger two-level building that is added on to the initial Small House. It contains two staircases, one on either side of the hall, leading up to the second level, with separate areas in the back on each level. You may add a large bed and two small beds, as well as an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Labs.md), an [anvil](Skyrim_Anvil.md), an [arcane enchanter](Skyrim_Arcane_Enchanters.md), a bookshelf with space for 54 [books](Skyrim_Books.md), a fireplace with cooking pot, a mannequin and two weapon plaques, and eight display cases. The Main Hall can include up to 66 usable containers for storage, excluding the Entryway and Cellar. After completion, you may remodel the original Small House room into an Entryway, add a Cellar, and construct additional wings on three sides of the home. - [Entryway](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Main_Hall#Main_Hall:_Entryway): After building the Main Hall, you may choose to remodel the Small House room into an Entryway, with options to build two display cases, four single weapon racks, and eight usable containers. If you choose to remodel the original Small House into this area, then most improvements made to it will be distributed throughout the rest of the house. It is possible to build these "redistributable" improvements (e.g. Double Bed) for the Main Hall before remodelling the Small House, thus wasting some materials.
- [Cellar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cellar): An optional addition to the Main Hall, which can include all [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) facilities, an archery target and practice dummy, an armor mannequin, a set of five weapon racks, two weapon plaques, one display case, two mead barrels, an altar for holding your own personal [shrines](Skyrim_Shrines.md) to the [Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines#Nine_Divines), a coffin for sleeping (if a [vampire](Skyrim_Vampirism.md)), and 43 usable containers.
- [Aquarium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Aquarium)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Fishing.md)</sup>: An optional addition to the Main Hall. This room in the Cellar includes space for extra storage and for fish tanks and plaques to put your [catches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fishing_Items) on display.

**East Wing Additions**

- [Armory](Skyrim_Armory.md): Includes an exterior deck area on the roof. You can build five armor mannequins, two weapon plaques, two sets of four weapon racks, four display cases, two [tanning racks](Skyrim_Tanning_Rack.md), a [workbench](Skyrim_Workbench.md), a [grindstone](Skyrim_Grindstone.md), and two archery targets. The armory can include up to 18 usable containers for storage.
- [Kitchen](Skyrim_Kitchen.md): Includes a fireplace with cooking pot and the only available oven in the game for [baking](Skyrim_Baking.md), as well as the only churn for providing [butter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Butter). You can add up to 16 usable containers for storage. A number of [food](Skyrim_Food.md) items are added along with other furnishing options.
- [Library](Skyrim_Library.md): A tower including a rooftop balcony area. You can build five tall bookshelves (each with four shelves that can hold eleven books each) and four smaller bookshelves (each with two shelves holding eleven books each) with storage for 308 books total. An additional 8 usable containers can be added for storage.

**North Wing Additions**

- [Trophy Room](Skyrim_Trophy_Room.md): Allows the display of three large and four small trophies. You can also add one display case and up to 16 usable containers for storage.
- [Storage Room](Skyrim_Storage_Room.md): Includes an exterior deck area on the roof. You can build three usable display cases, and add up to 46 usable containers for storage.
- [Alchemy Laboratory](Skyrim_Alchemy_Laboratory.md): A tower including a rooftop balcony area. You can build an alchemy lab and up to 42 usable containers for storage. A number of food items and [ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md) are added along with other furnishing options.

**West Wing Additions**

- [Bedrooms](Skyrim_Bedrooms.md): Includes an exterior deck area on the roof. You can add one double bed and two child beds, two display cases, and up to 18 usable containers for storage.
- [Greenhouse](Skyrim_Greenhouse.md): Allows for the planting and growing of certain plants indoors. You can build three large planters (four plants each) and six small planters (one plant each) with room for 18 plants total. You can add four display cases and up to 25 usable containers for storage.
- [Enchanter's Tower](Skyrim_Enchanter%27s_Tower.md): A tower including a rooftop balcony area. You can build an arcane enchanter, four [soul gem](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md) braziers, two display cases, an armor mannequin, a weapon plaque, and a set of three weapon racks. You can add up to 13 usable containers for storage.

## Main Hall
If you wish to expand your home past the Small House layout, you can do so by choosing the Main Hall plan at the drafting table, then constructing parts at the carpenter's workbench. The Main Hall is considered complete after steps 1-6 in the table, but you can expand further by adding a cellar. After building the Main Hall, you have the option to remodel the Small House layout portion of the house into an Entryway if you choose. The Main Hall consists of two floors, with a firepit on the first floor, bedrooms on the second floor, and options to furnish the inside with an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md), an [anvil](Skyrim_Forge.md), and an [arcane enchanter](Skyrim_Arcane_Enchanter.md).

## Additions
After completing the Main Hall (no cellar required), you have the option of building up to three additions to your home: the East Wing, North Wing, and West Wing. Each wing has three style options: a two-level tower with access to a small rooftop balcony, a single-level interior with a large rooftop patio, and a high-ceilinged single-level interior space.

### East Wing
You can choose one of three options for the East Wing of your home: the Armory, the Kitchen, or the Library. The Armory has a rooftop patio, the Kitchen is a single-level high-ceilinged room, and the Library is a tower. See individual pages for details on furnishing choices for each option.

## Notes
- Building and fully furnishing a house requires a lot of supplies. In particular, you will need a *large* supply of [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md). While you can construct the Small House and Main Hall layout with just 17.5 iron ingots, it will cost you an additional 139.5 ingots to fully furnish them, and building the additions will cost between 24 and 37.2 ingots to construct (depending on your choices), or up to a total of 80.7-143.5 ingots to construct fully furnished, for a grand total when all is said and done of 237.7-300.5 ingots. The Library, Trophy Room, and Enchanter's Tower are the least expensive additions to build and fully furnish, while the Armory, Storage Room, and Bedrooms are the most. Multiply these numbers by three if you plan to build all the houses, and you can see they can get quite high. - This means that you should be stockpiling iron at every opportunity. Visit every blacksmith on a regular basis and buy out their entire supply of iron ore and ingots. Carry around a [pickaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pickaxe) or [Ancient Nordic Pickaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nordic_Pickaxe)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> at all times and [mine](Skyrim_Mining.md) every iron ore vein you see. [Halted Stream Camp](Skyrim_Halted_Stream_Camp.md), [Fort Fellhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Fellhammer), [Embershard Mine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Embershard_Mine), and [Stonehills](Skyrim_Stonehills.md) are excellent places to stock up. Try to avoid using the [Transmute Mineral Ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Transmute_Mineral_Ore) spell too much - silver and gold are useful, but you'll never need nearly as much of these as you need iron. Also, don't waste your iron and corundum ore making steel ingots. You should be able to easily get enough steel from vendors, but iron (and to a lesser extent [corundum](Skyrim_Corundum.md)) are needed in large quantities in home construction. However, keep in mind that all vendors refresh their inventory every 48 hours, and so you can wait in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) for example for two days and buy all iron ore and ingots from [Eorlund Gray-Mane](Skyrim_Eorlund_Gray-Mane.md), [Ulfberth War-Bear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ulfberth_War-Bear), and [Adrianne Avenicci](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Adrianne_Avenicci) (be sure that Adrianne is outside, see the notes section for [Warmaiden's](Skyrim_Warmaiden%27s.md)). You will also need to [smelt](Skyrim_Smelting.md) your iron ore into iron ingots - there is a smelter outside of Warmaiden's. - Since all 4 craftable housebuilding materials (hinges, iron fittings, locks, and nails) require 1 iron ingot each (and the lock also requires 1 corundum ingot), forging them is an excellent way to improve your [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill. - The hinges recipe produces 2 per ingot, and the nails recipe produces 10 per ingot. The other two recipes (iron fittings and locks) produce one at a time.
- Killing the bandit bosses of [Halted Stream Camp](Skyrim_Halted_Stream_Camp.md), [Fort Fellhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fort_Fellhammer), and [Embershard Mine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Embershard_Mine) clears their respective dungeons, but you may wish to avoid this, as it will force a 30-day waiting period for the ore veins to respawn. If you leave the boss alive, you can visit the mines every ten days for more iron ore, making them a prolific source of iron ore.
- You can pay your [Steward](Skyrim_Personal_Steward.md) to deal with the furnishings process, and the furnishings will appear in their respective rooms over time. The cost to furnish each room is: - Small House: 1,000 gold
- Entry Room Upgrade: 1,000 gold
- Main Hall: 3,500 gold - The exception is the Cellar, which requires you build all furnishings by yourself.
- East Wing: - Armory: 2,000 gold
- Kitchen: 1,500 gold
- Library: 1,500 gold
- North Wing: - Alchemy Laboratory: 2,500 gold
- Storage Rooms: 1,000 gold
- Trophy Room: 1,500 gold
- West Wing: - Bedrooms: 2,000 gold
- Enchanter's Tower: 2,500 gold
- Greenhouse: 1,500 gold
- You can ask your Steward where to buy building materials ([straw](Skyrim_Straw.md), [glass](Skyrim_Glass_(item).md), etc), and they'll tell you where the nearest shop is. You can also pay them directly for these: - [Lumber](Skyrim_Sawn_Log.md): 20 for 200 gold
- [Stone](Skyrim_Quarried_Stone.md): 20 for 100 gold
- [Clay](Skyrim_Clay.md): 20 for 20 gold
- General goods [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md) and [Khajiit traders](Skyrim_Khajiit_Traders.md) sell building materials, but it may take up to 30 days to restock – it is unknown whether this is intentional or not.
- Once a steward has been chosen for a particular homestead, the choice is permanent; you cannot replace that steward with someone else later, though you can sill ask them to [follow](Skyrim_Followers.md) you.
- There is no plot of land available for purchase in [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold_(region).md), even with *Hearthfire* installed, making construction there impossible. It also doesn't matter if you helped [Korir](Skyrim_Korir.md) or not.
- It's advised to finish building or furnishing each part at one time to avoid some unnecessary bugs.
- It's advised to monitor your gold when stocking up on crafting equipment, as you could quickly be drained if you spend too much.
- It's advised that you get rid of heavy clutter before building, otherwise you could quickly be overencumbered by your crafting equipment.
- Conversely, when furnishing a house, you should add each item separately or in small groups to avoid bugs that will cost you extra resources.
- The music that plays inside of each property is the same music that plays in walled cities, such as [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) and [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md).
- You can build the outdoor accessories before drafting the Main Hall, and you can finish the Cellar before adding any wings to your property.
- If you remodel the Small House Layout into an Entryway, the Wall Shelves and the Tall Shelf With the Display Case will be transferred to the west bedroom in the Main Hall, the Square Table and Chairs will be moved to below the Main Hall's western staircase, and the Double Bed will be transferred to the east bedroom in the Main Hall. However, the Firepit, Hanging Rack, and the Sack on the corresponding Wall Shelf will disappear. No other furnishings will be affected. - With the [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch) installed, the End Table will also disappear when remodeling. It will be replaced with a bench in the same location, and its contents will be transferred to the entry hall chest. In addition, the Mounted Bear Head in the Main Hall, the Mounted Bear Head in the Armory, the Mounted Bear Head in the Bedrooms, and one of them in the Cellar will all change into Mounted Cave Bear Heads, and one of the Mounted Wolf Heads in the Main Hall will change into a Mounted Ice Wolf Head, and their respective furnishing requirements will change accordingly.
- After the roof of each Main Hall is built, a hawk nest will appear on it, giving you access to free [hawk feathers](Skyrim_Hawk_Feathers.md) and [hawk eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk%27s_Egg) at Lakeview Manor and Heljarchen Hall, and a Rock Warbler nest with access to free hawk feathers and [Rock Warbler eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rock_Warbler_Egg) at Windstad Manor. You can build a tower add-on, jump from a nearby rock formation, or use [Whirlwind Sprint](Skyrim_Whirlwind_Sprint.md) to get to them. - Version 3.0.11 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch) replaces the Rock Warbler nest at Windstad Manor with a Hawk's nest. You can get to it using the 3 methods mentioned above.
- If you build the Bedrooms addition, you must build 2 Child's Beds and 2 Child's Chests in order to [adopt](Skyrim_Adoption.md) children into your property. If you do not build the Bedrooms addition, you must build the two single beds and the associated dresser in the Main Hall in order to adopt children.
- If you leave the canopies off of the tower add-ons, you'll be provided with a full, unobstructed view of the surrounding landscape. The tower will still function like normal, and anyone living on the property will still use it as part of their daily routines.
- Your adopted children will comment on the properties, depending on which one you send them to: - Lakeview Manor: - *"I like living here. The lake is so pretty."* (girls)
- "*I went fishing at the lake. I almost caught something!"* (boys)
- Heljarchen Hall: - *"Look at all the snow! I want to go sledding! Or skating!"* (girls)
- "*It's so c-c-cold here! If I play outside for too long, I'm going to freeze solid!"* (boys)
- Windstad Manor: - *"This place is scary. There are monsters in the swamp, I just know it."* (girls only)
- All: - *"Did you really build this house all by yourself? Wow!"* (boys only)
- If you ask your spouse "How are the kids?" they will reply with one of the following, depending on their [voicetype](Skyrim_Generic_Dialogue.md#Adoption_Dialogue). Their responses will be the same for all 3 properties: - *"Fine, when we're not fending off attacks by wolves. Or bears. Or giant spiders. Honestly, what were you thinking, sending us out here?"* (Arrogant)
- *"Fine, love. I think the fresh air does wonders for all of us, and this house... it's just perfect."* (Neutral)
- *"Fine, fine. Living out here on our own will take some getting used to, but I think it'll be good for us."* (Nord)
- *"Better than ever. Nothing like living off the land to teach you how to fight, how to survive. We'll have a little warrior on our hands in no time."* (Orc)
- The construction chests outside each house are [safe](Skyrim_Containers.md#Safe_Locations) for storage.
- Building a house was originally developed as part of the [Game Jam](Skyrim_Game_Jam.md).

## Bugs
- Shelves in the bookcases of these homes may display a larger capacity than the shelf is supposed to have. <sup>**?**</sup>
- The menu for the exterior carpenter's workbench may be blank, even if there are items available. - Exiting the menu and selecting the workbench again may fix this. If the problem persists, try this fix multiple times.
- The carpenter's workbenches may have items that you already have built listed as being available. - Exiting the menu and selecting the workbench again may fix this. If the problem persists, try this fix multiple times.
- The railings on the deck of the Storage Room will not be removed properly and will block the path to connecting decks if it is built before the Bedrooms or Armory additions. - Building the Storage Room addition last will fix this.
- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) Open the console, click on the blocking railing (ID = xx010E80) and type `Mark For Delete`. After reentering the area, only this railing will be gone and passing through to the other deck is possible.
- After furnishing a room by using a steward, the furnishings may not actually appear.
- The double beds where you and your spouse sleep in Windstad Manor and Heljarchen Hall aren't set as owned by the Player Bed Ownership faction which would deny the Well Rested bonus when used. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch), version 1.0.1, fixes this bug.
