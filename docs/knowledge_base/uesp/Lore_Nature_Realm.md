[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/ON-place-Nature_Realm.jpg/200px-ON-place-Nature_Realm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nature_Realm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nature_Realm.jpg) The Nature Realm The Nature Realm is a realm of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) which exists in parallel with the physical realm. It is a realm of spirits. The Nature Realm is a mirror image of the physical world, but tinged green. The spirits of physical beings can be seen in the Nature Realm, but cannot be interacted with.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho E-1)</sup>

The [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md) possess the ability to enter the Nature Realm as [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) spirits, and can use this to bypass obstacles in the physical realm.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho E-1)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) named Uela and her Dark Witnesses occupied Jackdaw Cove, a forest in the [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) region of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Using dark [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) magic, Uela corrupted the forest, weakening the local Glenmoril wyresses in the process. Uela specialized in utilizing [crows](Lore_Crow.md) as spies and messengers, magically possessing their physical bodies. These crow spirits were helpless and could do nothing but watch from the Nature Realm as she controlled their physical forms.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> In order to reach Uela and kill her, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) wyress named Rashan guided [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) into the Nature Realm in order to commune with the crow spirits and bypass her defenses.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho E-1)</sup>

## Notes
- The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) believe that the sacred [Caenlorn Wolves](Lore_Caenlorn_Wolf.md) typically reside in the "world of the spirit" with the [All-Father](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Father). They are only summoned by the Skaal for certain ceremonies.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>
- The skald [Morachellis Hag-Husband](Lore_Morachellis_Hag-Husband.md) speculated that [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) use their [third eye](Lore_Third_Eye.md) to view an "overworld of spirits" coterminous with the mundane world, although this claim is considered dubious.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TFG-4)</sup>
