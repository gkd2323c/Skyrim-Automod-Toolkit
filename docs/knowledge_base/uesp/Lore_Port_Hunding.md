Situated north of [Hunding Bay](Lore_Hunding_Bay.md), the city takes up a large portion of the island's area.

## Layout
Port Hunding is divided into roughly four districts: the harbor, market, town square, and old quarter. Ships entering the harbor are greeted by a large metal statue of [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md), with his sword extending out to sea. The town square has also been called the Plaza of the Three Princes and features the Monument of the Three Princes, standing over the Regent's Pool.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> The Draggin' Tale Inn was established here in [2E 284](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_284) and was still in existence 580 years later.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> It derives its name from the head of a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), which was brought from [Noota Nara](Lore_Noota_Nara.md) in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and mounted on a wall within the establishment.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> A temple dedicated to [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) was constructed sometime after [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and a [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) guildhall in [2E 638](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_638).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Another tavern, the Screaming Mermaid, was notable for using [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) piping in its kitchen.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

The old quarter lies northeast of the town square. A large palace is built north of the square on a section of land separated by a river. Beneath the palace is a series of catacombs, once known as the Grave, where the worst criminals are kept. The palace's treasure vaults are also contained within. On a small island southeast of the city lies Hunding's Palatial Hall, once the home of the legendary Captain Izad.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> It is a mansion rivaling the grandeur of the palace. Both of these structures are accessible by bridges.

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/RG-place-Palace.jpg/200px-RG-place-Palace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-place-Palace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-place-Palace.jpg) The palace The modern structure of the Palace dates to the early [Common Era](Lore_Common_Era.md), though its foundations are much older, connected to a maze of catacombs which may predate the arrival of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Jeff-7)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Port Hunding was ruled by Headman Bhosek, a universally-disliked and severe ruler.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> His Bloody Fists kept order in town. Captain Kaleen of *The Spearhead* organized a heist to steal shipping logs from Bhosek, which she sold to King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) for gold and as evidence that Bhosek was still raiding [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) ships, despite being part of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

[Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md), the son of High King [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md), was born in Stros M'Kai. Following his father's death in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862), the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) led an uprising in [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), forcing A'tor to retreat to his ancestral home with other loyal [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md). From his palace, he led a counterattack that became the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) civil war. The war came to a close when the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) allied with the Forebears and crushed the Crowns at the [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md). [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) Admiral [Amiel Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md) was made provisional governor of the island. The old quarter was burned to the ground during the battle.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) arrived in Stros M'Kai. During his adventures he gathered a great deal of information from the townsfolk and acquired the pieces of a map leading to the [Flask of Lillandril](Lore_Flask_of_Lillandril.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> His adventure ended when he ordered the [Restless League](Lore_Restless_League.md) to attack the harbor while he infiltrated the palace. Richton was defeated and Cyrus' sister [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md) became the city's ruler. She immediately began reconstruction of the old quarter, and celebrations for the victory were held there after Cyrus left the island.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>
