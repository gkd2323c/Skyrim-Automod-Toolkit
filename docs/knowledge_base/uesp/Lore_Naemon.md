Naemon was a prince of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) who was the heir to the Throne of Alinor for seven years. Naemon became the heir to the Throne of Alinor following the disappearance of his older sister, [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md), in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), but when Ayrenn returned in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), she was crowned Queen of Alinor over Naemon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup> Naemon outwardly took Ayrenn's return well, but in actuality he was resentful.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-3)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), during the ratification ceremony at the Orrery of [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md), Naemon betrayed Ayrenn, but the Orrery transformed him into an [Ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md), and he was killed by an agent of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TOo ER-4)</sup>

Following the events in Elden Root, Naemon's devoted advisor and attendant, Vicereeve [Pelidil](Lore_Pelidil.md), absconded with his body, stole the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), and resurrected the prince as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Greenshade-5)</sup> Naemon then took command of an undead army and attempted to corrupt the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md) by using the Staff of Magnus, but his lich was ultimately destroyed by the Aldmeri Dominion in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin of [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Greenshade-5)</sup>

After his death, Naemon's [soul](Lore_Soul.md) was claimed by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) before he was later resurrected once again, this time by the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GOS-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ca C-7)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
Naemon was the eldest son of [Hidellith](Lore_Hidellith.md), King of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md), and Kinlady [Tuinden](Lore_Tuinden.md). He was the younger brother of [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md), the heir apparent to the Throne of Alinor. He also had at least one younger brother.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup> He was a cousin of [Alwinarwe](Lore_Alwinarwe.md), who served as proxy queen of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) following the formation of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt CPQA-8)</sup> Around [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Naemon married High Kinlady [Estre](Lore_Estre.md) of [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TIEG-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Estre-10)</sup>

One day in Evening Star [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), the entire royal family of Alinor gathered at the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) to celebrate Ayrenn's matriculation to the [Sapiarchs' Labyrinth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapiarchs%27_Labyrinth), where she was to study [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) Regal Praxis and Ceremoniarchy for the requisite three thousand five hundred and fifty-five days. However, Ayrenn did not arrive—somewhere between the royal palace and the tower, the princess disappeared.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup>

In due course, Naemon was named heir to the Throne of Alinor,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOL-11)</sup> and in 2E 573<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CN-12)</sup> or [575](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_575),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup> he matriculated into the Sapiarchs' Labyrinth. Naemon, like his father, was a natural ceremoniarch, who seemed to genuinely relish the rites and duties ordained by tradition for the heir. When Hidellith died in 2E 580, Naemon immediately began preparing to speak the eighty-eight-day Coronation Liturgy that was required to take the throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup> Naemon was more than halfway through the eighty-eight days of liturgical chanting when word came from [Velyn Harbor](Lore_Velyn_Harbor.md) that Ayrenn was traveling to [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> Naemon and the rest of the Court of Alinor then journeyed to [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), arriving just in time to welcome her. Ayrenn announced her readiness, as the eldest heir, to assume the Throne of Alinor, which the High Justiciar affirmed was her right, displacing Naemon as heir. Ayrenn was crowned Queen of Alinor on the 7th of Frost Fall, 2E 580.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATUQ-1)</sup>

Naemon outwardly took Ayrenn's return well, but in actuality he and many of his supporters were resentful, as they believed that he was the rightful king.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Pelidil-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Tt VM-14)</sup> After Ayrenn's return in 2E 580, Estre formed the Altmeri supremacist group, the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md), in opposition to Ayrenn and the newly formed Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-WDt V-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lit ES-16)</sup> As the leader of the Veiled Heritance, Estre sought to overthrow Ayrenn, destroy the Aldmeri Dominion, and expel all non-Altmer from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Estre-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-WDt V-15)</sup> Naemon believed that Ayrenn prioritized the needs of the Dominion above those of the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> He appeared to hold Altmeri supremacist views,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> although the extent of his knowledge regarding the plans of Estre and the Veiled Heritance is unclear.

### Royal Progress in the Aldmeri Dominion
In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Naemon and Estre accompanied Ayrenn to [Vulkhel Guard](Lore_Vulkhel_Guard.md) and [Tanzelwil](Lore_Tanzelwil.md) during her sojourn in Auridon.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MDAA-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AHS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-It Not Q-19)</sup> While Ayrenn was visiting Skywatch, the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md) discovered that Estre was the Veiled Heritance's leader, which they then revealed to Ayrenn, prompting Estre to begin an open conflict with the Dominion across northern Auridon.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Wt V-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TVF-21)</sup> Ayrenn declared Estre a traitor and ordered her death.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> The Dominion eventually killed Estre when she attempted to capture Firsthold, and Naemon was saddened and distressed by her death.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SAT-22)</sup>

After Naemon learned of Estre's death, he traveled to [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) to prepare for Ayrenn's ratification ceremony at the city's Orrery to prove her worthiness to rule.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> Naemon was also involved in the investigation into the delays in the construction of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) Embassy, which revealed that Tarinwe, the Altmer ambassador to Elden Root, was a member of the Veiled Heritance and had sabotaged the project, leading to the ambassador's arrest.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-THot Q-23)</sup> After the items required to operate the Orrery were recovered, Naemon accompanied Ayrenn, King [Camoran Aeradan](Lore_Camoran_Aeradan.md), and Daraneth into the Orrery's main chamber for the ratification ceremony. Once the Orrery was activated, Naemon betrayed Ayrenn by using a spell to render her and the other onlookers immobile; however, when he entered the Orrery in order to confirm his worthiness to rule, it transformed him into an [Ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md), and he was killed by an agent of the Dominion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TOo ER-4)</sup> Naemon's treachery was covered up by claiming he died a hero, and Ayrenn asked the prince's devoted advisor and attendant, Vicereeve [Pelidil](Lore_Pelidil.md), to travel with his body to [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) and make the funeral arrangements.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TOo ER-4)</sup>

Although Pelidil did travel to Marbruk, he absconded with Naemon's body, stole the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md) from the city's [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and assumed control of the remnants of the Veiled Heritance with the goal of resurrecting the prince as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) to lead an undead army and corrupt the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Razum-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Telonde-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Fariel-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Indaenir-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TSo M-28)</sup> After the Veiled Heritance and the Sea Vipers of the [Pyandonean](Lore_Pyandonea.md) Armada, with whom Pelidil had signed a treaty, captured the settlement of [Seaside Sanctuary](Lore_Seaside_Sanctuary.md), the vicereeve performed a ritual to raise Naemon from the dead as a lich.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Telonde-25)</sup> When Naemon was resurrected, he spat in Pelidil's face and then left, infuriating the vicereeve.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Telonde-25)</sup>

### Lichdom
After Pelidil was killed in a naval battle against the Dominion, Naemon used the Veiled Heritance and the undead army that he raised to continue fighting the Dominion.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Henandan-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Rt P-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TBot B-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Ro T-32)</sup> Naemon used the Staff of Magnus to corrupt the Heart of Valenwood in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin of [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md) and create a new realm, the [Shadow Wood](Lore_Shadow_Wood.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Indaenir-27)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Thraul-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Ollion-34)</sup> He planned to transform all of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) in this manner, supplanting it with the Shadow Wood.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Indaenir-27)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Thraul-33)</sup> Naemon envisioned that the Shadow Wood would ultimately consume all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Thraul-33)</sup> However, before he could fully achieve his goal, Naemon's lich was destroyed by an agent of the Dominion and the future [Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md) when they assaulted Hectahame.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Sat H-35)</sup>

After Naemon's second death, Ayrenn officially exonerated him.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-3)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Henandan-29)</sup> Ayrenn claimed that Naemon's lich was only a pawn, as the Dominion had supposedly discovered papers belonging to Pelidil and Estre, which revealed that they had conspired with the Veiled Heritance to resurrect the prince and use him as a figurehead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-3)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Henandan-29)</sup> Ayrenn also issued a proclamation that he would receive a royal funeral.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Henandan-29)</sup>

Following his second death, Naemon's [soul](Lore_Soul.md) was claimed by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), who imprisoned him in [Heart's Grief](Lore_Heart%27s_Grief.md) in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), where he was tortured by being sealed behind a sheet of ice that suffocated him without allowing him to die.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GOS-6)</sup>

### Resurrection on Solstice
Naemon remained trapped in Coldharbour until Dark Adept Vairisse, a cultist of the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md), offered him a way to escape.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> Vairisse wanted to resurrect Naemon because he was the only one who could control the Staff of Magnus, which the dark adept believed could be used to enhance the shackles involved in the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-PNat So M-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Rexus-37)</sup> Although he did not trust the Worm Cult, he saw Vairisse's offer as his only choice to escape the torment in Coldharbour.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Naemon-2)</sup> Naemon's soul escaped Coldharbour through a Daedric Gate,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONSA-38)</sup> and Vairisse and her worm cultists resurrected him on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Cariel-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Gaelle-40)</sup>

After his resurrection, Naemon took up residence with Vairisse's worm cult nest at Aldwilne Citadel.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Vairisse-41)</sup> Naemon summoned his loyalists to Solstice.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Cariel-39)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup> Although they were allied with each other, there was significant tension between the Worm Cult and Naemon and his loyalists.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Cariel-39)</sup>

Along with resurrecting Naemon, the Worm Cult also acquired the Staff of Magnus by ambushing and slaughtering a group of Mages Guild members to whom Ayrenn had loaned the staff.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Rexus-37)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Cariel-39)</sup> The Worm Cult then gave the Staff of Magnus to Naemon.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-PNat So M-36)</sup> Ayrenn had ordered an Eye of the Queen named Cariel to shadow the Mages Guild, and she eventually tracked the Staff of Magnus to Aldwilne Citadel.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Cariel-39)</sup> At Aldwilne Citadel, Cariel recruited the Vestige to infiltrate the fortress in order to gain the trust of Naemon and Vairisse.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ca C-7)</sup>

Once they gained their trust, the Vestige was tasked with exploiting the mistrust between Naemon and Vairisse in order to allow them to stop the prince, regain possession of the Staff of Magnus, and sabotage the Worm Cult's plans. After earning the trust of Naemon and Vairisse and convincing the prince that the dark adept was involved in a plot against him, they sabotaged the Planemeld shackle and placed a loyalist dagger near the machinery to frame Naemon and his loyalists. The Vestige's efforts ultimately led to the start of a brief conflict between Naemon's loyalists and the Worm Cult in Aldwilne Citadel. Naemon attempted to recruit the Vestige to assist his loyalists; however, their decision and the ultimate outcome for Naemon is unknown.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ca C-7)</sup>

Naemon's spirit is believed to have eventually passed on to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup>
