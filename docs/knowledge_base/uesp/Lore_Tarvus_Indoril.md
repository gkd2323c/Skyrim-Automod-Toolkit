Tarvus Indoril was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) priest who served as the Archcanon of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Born into [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), Tarvus was highly devoted to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and became the youngest Archcanon in history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCAT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATI-2)</sup> His peers described him as a good man and an excellent priest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CL-3)</sup> He played an important role in the foundation of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), but at some point he had died in his sleep. Seeing an opportunity, he was subsequently impersonated by [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md) in the events leading up to [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s plot to drain Vivec's divine energy.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONBarbas-4)</sup> Following the end of the crisis, he was succeeded by his pupil [Llevule Andrano](Lore_Llevule_Andrano.md) as the Archcanon of the High Fane. Tarvus was subsequently revived by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and became a Proctor of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). He took on much of the same duties he had in his previous role under Vivec, redirecting them in service of Sotha Sil.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LACW-5)</sup>

## History
Tarvus was born to Dunmer parents around [2E 362](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_362), and was 220 years old during the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive-6)</sup> He grew up learning the teachings of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), the dominant [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) throughout the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1M-7)</sup> and initially sought to join one of the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) or even the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATI-2)</sup> However, after personally speaking to Vivec directly, he was persuaded to choose a path as a priest in the Temple. Tarvus began to constantly work hard to prove his devotion to Vivec and the Tribunal as a canon, and was chosen by Vivec to become his Archcanon, the youngest in history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCAT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATI-2)</sup>

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md) invented a plot to get his master [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) into Sotha Sil|'s Clockwork City. The [Nycotic Cult](Lore_Nycotic_Cult.md), who worshipped a twisted form of Vile, performed a ritual to summon [Sunna'rah](Lore_Sunna%27rah.md), a staff created by Sotha Sil that could drain the divine energy from the [Tribunes](Lore_Tribunal.md). On the Forty-third Day of Vivec City's construction, one of the lift-ropes had broken and two tons of stones crashed, causing injuries. It was the work of Barbas' tampering, and Tarvus witnessed the hound at the scene as he went to help the injured. He went to bed so that he could decide on whether to report what he had seen to Vivec, but unexpectedly died in his sleep shortly after.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONBarbas-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AJ-8)</sup>

Later on, the Warrior-Poet convinced the Vestige to investigate what was happening to his divine powers, while Llevule stayed at Vivec's side as the god was growing weaker and weaker. Unbeknownst to everyone, Archcanon Tarvus, who was aiding the Vestige with the investigation, had been secretly replaced by Barbas. After the Vestige finally retrieved Sunna'rah from Chodala, Barbas tricked them into using the staff on Vivec, which drained most of his remaining energy, caused the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to erupt, and almost made [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) fall from the sky. Vivec was unaware of the impersonation until that point, and believed that Barbas killed Tarvus,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONVivec-9)</sup> though Barbas stated Tarvus peacefully died in his sleep and Vivec was too "stupid" to notice.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONBarbas-4)</sup> Barbas subsequently went to the Clockwork City to prepare for Vile's arrival, but the Vestige and the wizard [Barilzar](Lore_Barilzar.md) followed and stopped him, then returned the stolen energy to Vivec. With the threat over, Llevule was named the new Archcanon of the Tribunal Temple.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONDivine Blessings-10)</sup>

Tarvus was subsequently revived by Sotha Sil, and became a Proctor of the Clockwork City. During his recovery time in the Clockwork Basilica, he accepted an interview from [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md) of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md), where Tarvus answered questions on behalf of Sotha Sil.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LACW-5)</sup>

Due to the procedure responsible for his resurrection, Tarvus believed that he had been granted immortality to witness Sotha Sil's ultimate plan for [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) -prior.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LACW-5)</sup> His fate following Sotha Sil's death<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRTMG-11)</sup> is unknown.
