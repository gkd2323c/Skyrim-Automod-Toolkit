Carolus Aquilarios (known as the **Wolf of Kvatch**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCN-1)</sup> was the Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) during the late sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). When his uncle [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) initiated a [revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) against Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), he left Carolus in Kvatch to defend the city. He defended the city until [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), when Varen overthrew Leovic and declared himself emperor, which resulted in Carolus being named Count of Kvatch. However, as soon as Carolus was officially named Count of Kvatch, [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md), the ambitious and powerful Primate of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), began working to undermine the Count's authority and carve out more power and influence for himself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWat D-2)</sup>

Before Artorius' challenge could come to a head, [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) led a fleet of merchant warships and pirate vessels into [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) Harbor, took control of the city, and declared the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) to be a free and independent state.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PQot GC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Rot RS-5)</sup> Fortunata's pirates subsequently slaughtered Carolus' cohort of the Second Legion in the Gottshaw Massacre, leaving him with no choice but to swear fealty to an independent Gold Coast.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup> These events marked the start of a three-way power struggle over the Gold Coast between Carolus, Artorius, and Fortunata.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCN-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup>

## Biography

### Background and the Colovian Revolt
Carolus Aquilarios was born in [2E 550](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_550) to a sibling of [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-6)</sup> He was the grandson of a [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) duke and was proud of his heritage.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCN-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CFC4-7)</sup> Carolus was considered honorable and was known as a man of faith and conviction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCN-1)</sup> He idolized his uncle Varen, the Duke of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) and Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWat D-2)</sup>

In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) issued a [decree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic%27s_Great_Spirits_Proclamation) that legalized [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), which prompted Varen to initiate a [revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWat D-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SHot LE-8)</sup> Carolus was eager to fight alongside Varen, but Varen instead wanted Carolus to stay behind and protect the home front. Although disappointed not to be able to fight alongside Varen, Carolus nevertheless vowed to keep Kvatch and its people safe.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWat D-2)</sup>

Shortly after Varen and the majority of the [Second Legion](Lore_Second_Legion.md) departed the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), the Imperial Prefect of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Ephrem Benirus](Lore_Ephrem_Benirus.md), the Count of Anvil, sent the Imperial forces that were stationed in Anvil to seize Varen's property and holdings. In response, Carolus rallied the single cohort of the Second Legion that Varen had left in his command, as well as the Kvatch Warders—the famed defenders of the city. In a short and fierce battle, Kvatch's defenders met the Imperial forces on the plains below the city, ending with the remnants of Anvil's forces retreating. Before 2E 576 was over, Anvil sent its dwindling Imperial forces against Kvatch three more times, and each time Carolus' troops defeated them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWat D-2)</sup>

In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), word reached the region that Varen had overthrown Leovic and declared himself emperor. Carolus, who was being called the Wolf of Kvatch for his bravery and leadership, was named Count of Kvatch and took the wolf's head symbol as his own. However, as soon as Carolus was officially named Count of Kvatch, [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md), the ambitious and powerful Primate of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), began working to undermine the Count's authority and carve out more power and influence for himself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWat D-2)</sup>

### Conflict with Fortunata ap Dugal
Before Artorius' challenge could come to a head, [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) led a fleet of merchant warships and [pirate](Lore_Piracy.md) vessels into Anvil Harbor, took control of the under-defended city, and deposed Ephrem Benirus. Fortunata then took the title of Provincial Governor of Anvil and declared the Gold Coast to be a free and independent state.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PQot GC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Rot RS-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-9)</sup> After this, Fortunata requested that Kvatch join Anvil as part of an independent Gold Coast, but Carolus refused, preferring to remain loyal to Varen. Fortunata did not feel confident enough at this time to press the demand, but she would not forget the Count's lack of support.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup>

After Varen disappeared in the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), Fortunata took advantage of Kvatch's sudden isolation to make her demands more forceful. Anvil began pressing Kvatch to join the independent Gold Coast, both diplomatically and by increasingly underhanded means. Marauders and bandits began to appear along the [Gold Road](Lore_Gold_Road.md) around Kvatch, disrupting trade and harassing travelers. The Kvatch Warders were dispatched to deal with the bandits, but each time they were able to escape to the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md). The bandits returned in greater numbers, amplifying their attacks by raiding outlying farms and sending frightened refugees into the city for protection.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup>

Despite these acts of aggression, Carolus remained staunch and refused to negotiate with the Pirate Queen. Infuriated, Fortunata announced her intention to annex Kvatch, and she sent the Anvil Guard to take control of the city. Carolus responded by putting the Kvatch Warders upon the city walls and sending the cohort of the Second Legion to confront Fortunata's forces. The opposing forces met on the Gold Road, near the [Gottshaw Inn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gottshaw_Inn), where the Second Legion cohort scattered the undisciplined Anvil guards. However, an army of pirates then ambushed and slaughtered the cohort in what became known as the Gottshaw Massacre.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup>

Without the support of the cohort, Carolus had no choice but to swear fealty to an independent Gold Coast.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-3)</sup> With his back against the wall, Carolus reluctantly agreed to an alliance with Artorius Ponticus and the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) to protect Kvatch.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCN-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup>

### The Dark Brotherhood
Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Anvil and Kvatch experienced a notable increase in murders, which some suspected to be the work of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONCF-11)</sup> In response to the increase in murders and the other threats to the city, Carolus granted the Order of the Hour expanded rights and duties to supplement the efforts of the Kvatch Guard.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCN-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OOTH-12)</sup> The Count also granted Artorius the powers to once again convert the Order of the Hour into an army, provided the Order returned to the Cathedral of Akatosh once the threat was resolved.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OOTH-12)</sup> These actions unsettled Fortunata, who believed that Carolus and the Primate were building an army to eventually attack Anvil.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-13)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg) Carolus at the Jarol Estate meeting In 2E 582, Carolus attended a meeting at the [estate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jarol_Estate) of Lord Quintus Jarol with Fortunata to discuss the Dark Brotherhood threat and the expansion of the Order of the Hour.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONSi B-14)</sup> Primate Artorius was also supposed to attend, but Cathedral business forced him to remain in Kvatch.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup> In this meeting, Carolus gave his word that the Order of the Hour would return to the Cathedral once the Dark Brotherhood threat was resolved.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup> However, the meeting was interrupted when an assassin penetrated the estate, but Jarol had a contingency that allowed Carolus and Fortunata to depart safely.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONSi B-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/ON-quest-A_Special_Request.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Special_Request.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Special_Request.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Special_Request.jpg) Carolus performing the Black Sacrament Despite Carolus' assurances at the Jarol Estate meeting, Fortunata still viewed the expansion of the Order of the Hour as a threat.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-13)</sup> Therefore, not long after the meeting, she contracted the Brotherhood to kill Commander Marcus Scipio to weaken the Order and show Carolus that they could not protect him.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-13)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONASR-15)</sup> The Brotherhood murdered Scipio in Castle Kvatch; however, in an unexpected turn of events, Carolus performed the [Black Sacrament](Lore_Black_Sacrament.md) to have the Brotherhood kill Fortunata, as he believed this was the only way to protect Kvatch.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONASR-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CC-16)</sup> The Brotherhood accepted the contract, and Carolus offered to pay with gold and provide information about the [Black Dragon](Lore_Black_Dragon.md), who was responsible for killing Brotherhood assassins.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONASR-15)</sup>

The Brotherhood subsequently killed Fortunata in Anvil.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONASR-15)</sup> After Fortunata's assassination, Carolus still owed the Brotherhood information regarding the Black Dragon, so he requested to meet them at his camp in the ruins of [Dasek Moor](Lore_Dasek_Moor.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONAC-17)</sup> At Dasek Moor, Carolus informed two Brotherhood assassins that the Black Dragon was the First Sword of the Order of the Hour.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONDR-18)</sup> He also informed them that the Order was amassing an army and siege weapons at their old headquarters, the Enclave of the Hourglass, which threatened both Kvatch and the Brotherhood.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCCA-10)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONDR-18)</sup> Later, the Brotherhood learned that Artorius planned to attack the Dark Brotherhood sanctuary on the Gold Coast.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONAC-17)</sup> In response, the Brotherhood preemptively attacked the Cathedral of Akatosh in Kvatch, where they killed Artorius.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONFTV-19)</sup> With the deaths of Artorius and Fortunata, Carolus became the only remaining power on the Gold Coast.
