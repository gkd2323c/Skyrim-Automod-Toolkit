## History
*Magic is a word used by cowards who fear its power or fools who think they can control it. We are connected to the land. Nothing more, nothing less.*
—Shaman Ulvoch<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup> Little is known about Ulvoch's past. At some point, he became the Shaman of the Crow-Eye Clan and took Fialyn as his apprentice, after she petitioned for the position. Since then she assisted Ulvoch with every ritual and clan ceremony, listening and observing him and receiving teachings. As an apprentice, she was chosen as his successor after his death.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AF-3)</sup> They were responsible for the sacred rites of the clan.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Eamon-4)</sup>

One of the rituals he and his apprentice were responsible for was maintaining the protective wards designed to guard the village of [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md). It was maintained through offerings to the spirits with which the clan was known to bargain with: their patron [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) and other Daedra: [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), Spirit Queen [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AF-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATS-5)</sup> It is unknown if he was born blind or lost sight at some point in the past, but as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) he was sightless.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup>

During the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) invasion of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and western holds of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in 2E 582, Karthwasten was one of the targets of the attack. Thanks to the protective wards the village was not utterly destroyed, unlike the neighboring [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) settlement of [Karthwatch](Lore_Karthwatch.md). Numerous inhabitants of the village were still affected by the [harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorms.md) and turned into harrowed, however.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AF-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Karth-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Harrowed-7)</sup> In the same year, the village was visited by [an outsider](Lore_The_Vestige.md) who was willing to aid locals with their problems related to the Gray Host activity in the region. Fialyn introduced the outsider to Ulvoch, who accepted their help and requested them to help Fialyn with the renewal of the wards. The outsider and Fialyn collected materials with a connection to Karthwasten and the surrounding countryside and offered them to the spirits. However, the final part of the ritual was disturbed by the [Netherroot](Lore_Netherroot.md), a plant that was cultivated by the Gray Host in the Sanuarach Mine. After his apprentice and the outsider cleared the mines, the ritual was finished successfully and the wards restored.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AF-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATS-5)</sup>

The village thrived once again and the outsider was named a Crow-Eye in spirit and a friend to the Clan.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AF-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATS-5)</sup> Shaman Ulvoch attended the moot during which regional leaders Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of the Reach, and Jarl [Svana](Lore_Svana.md) and King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) of Skyrim signed the peace treaty between the Reach, Eastern Skyrim, and Western Skyrim.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SC-8)</sup>
