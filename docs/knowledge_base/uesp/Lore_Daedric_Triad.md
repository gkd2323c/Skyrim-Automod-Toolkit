The Daedric Triad (or simply **the Triad**) was an alliance between three [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) who were not bound by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), that formed in the Second Era. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the three Princes enacted a plot gain control of the means via which to remake reality.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup>

## History

### Initial Attempt on the Crystal Tower
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/ON-npc-Clavicus_Vile_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Clavicus_Vile_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Clavicus_Vile_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Clavicus_Vile_03.jpg) A memory of Clavicus Vile bargaining with the Sea Sload K'Tora At some point in the Early Second Era, the three Princes formed a secretive alliance known only as 'The Triad'. Their goal was to acquire the means to remake reality by any means possible.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup> Their first known target was the mythical [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) on Summerset. As part of their scheme, Clavicus Vile made a deal with the [Sea Sloads](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) of [Ul'vor Kus](Lore_Ul%27vor_Kus.md) —he would give them the island of Summerset in exchange for a piece of Transparent Law, the 'Stone' of the Crystal Tower. The Cabal of Sea Sloads brainwashed the Ritemaster of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md), into stealing Transparent Law, but Nocturnal secretly betrayed Clavicus Vile and Mephala and helped Iachesis hide the Stone from the rest of the Triad, providing him with a spell that would hide the Heart from everyone, until such time as she could move forward with her own plans.

Iachesis then moved the island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) off of Nirn entirely to avoid falling under the influence of the Princes yet again. With the Triad's plan seemingly foiled, they moved on and began to plot their next move.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Summerset-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOPO-3)</sup> As part of the Triad's deal with the Sea Sload, they promised them the island of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) itself in exchange for the Heart, something which would've been achieved through the [Abyssal Geysers](Lore_Abyssal_Geysers.md) activated by the followers of the Princes, the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md), that were intended to sink the island into the sea.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlereeve-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KTora-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Indirim-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Grog-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sinking-8)</sup>

### Vvardenfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-npc-Barbas_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Barbas_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Barbas_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Barbas_03.jpg) Barbas within the Clockwork City Following this, the Triad found that their next target would be the fabled [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). While the Triad began their attempts to locate it, with Barbas enacting his own plan in an attempt to please his master. Barbas began tampering with the construction of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md). He was noticed by Archcanon Tarvus of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), who saw evidence of sabotage and had planned to tell Vivec of the potential canine culprit he spotted.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> According to Barbas himself, he found that Tarvus had passed in his sleep, and at that point decided to impersonate him.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Morrowind-10)</sup>

Concurrently, the [Nycotic Cult](Lore_Nycotic_Cult.md) was somehow able to acquire the artifact known as [Sunna'rah](Lore_Sunna%27rah.md), a staff that was created by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) God [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) as a tool for him to experiment with his own powers.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOBarilzar-11)</sup> [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md), the Ashkhan of the [Urshilaku Tribe](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md), discovered through a benefactor about the staff and wished for it to be acquired, with the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) later offering a proposal to the [Red Exiles](Lore_Red_Exiles.md) who were sent by him in order to retrieve the staff from the cult, but not before the cultists first prepared a ritual that was able to allow the object to channel unfathomable power, with the plan calling for the exiles to let the cultists start the ritual but not allow it to be finished.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-REI-12)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Morrowind-10)</sup>

After Chodala was killed by the Vestige and the staff was returned, Barbas still posing as Archcanon Tarvus, used it to steal [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md)'s divine energy and discover the Clockwork City's location. He succeeded, and Barbas entered the City to arrange Clavicus Vile's arrival, but was stopped by the Vestige and the mage [Barilzar](Lore_Barilzar.md), who pursued him into the City.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Divine_Intervention-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Divine_Restoration-14)</sup> The Vestige found themselves transported to the shrine of Clavicus Vile, where the Prince threatened to imprison them. However they channeled the power of Vivec contained within Sunna'rah against the Prince through his statue, allowing them to escape. Thus Vivec's stolen energy returned seconds before [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) crashed into Vivec City. Had this plot not been foiled this would have caused great devastation to Vvardenfell. Barbas nevertheless succeeded in locating the Clockwork City for his master Clavicus Vile.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Morrowind-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Barbas-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOClavicus Vile-16)</sup>

### The Clockwork City
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg) Nocturnal's shadows rushing forth to consume the Throne Aligned as Luciana struggles to hold them back [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG/200px-ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG) The freed Sotha Sil banishes Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City Now knowing how to enter the Clockwork City, the Triad quickly launched a full-scale coup within it. Nocturnal replaced [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) with a shadowy replica and imprisoned the Clockwork God within [his own throne](Lore_Cogitum_Centralis.md). Meanwhile, one of the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostle.md), Chancellor Gascone Dusant, sought to deliver the Skeleton Key, which had been accidentally found by a Factotum beneath the Brass Fortress, to Clavicus Vile, only for the Key to be stolen by the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md). The Vestige, assisted by [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) and the Clockwork Apostles, unveiled the plot, but were unable to reclaim the Skeleton Key before it was taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow.

Using the key, Nocturnal and the Shadow attempted to seize control of the Clockwork City by unlocking the Throne itself, but were stopped by the Vestige, Divayth Fyr, and Proctor [Luciana Pullo](Lore_Luciana_Pullo.md), who rescued Sotha Sil by defeating his Shadow and repelling Nocturnal's darkness through a special light spell Sotha Sil had once taught Luciana. Once freed Sotha Sil banished Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City. Sotha Sil then gave the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping. In reality, however, Nocturnal had tricked Clavicus Vile and Mephala once more and succeeded in stealing crucial information for the final part of her plan.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOCWC-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Shadow Sil-19)</sup>

### Final Attempt on the Crystal Tower
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-quest-Lost_in_Translation_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Lost_in_Translation_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Lost_in_Translation_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Lost_in_Translation_04.jpg) Mephala and Clavicus Vile attempt to remove the Transparent Law from Ritemaster Iachesis [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg) The Crystal Tower under Nocturnal's control, within the Fields of Regret The Triad turned their attention once more to the Crystal Tower following this failure, seeking some way to find the missing Heart of Transparent Law or otherwise gain entrance to the Tower. Clavicus Vile would send the Abyssal Cabal to Summerset once more, to work with his agents in the Court of Bedlam in an attempt to hunt down Iachesis and pull the location from his mind. In preparation for the completion of their plan, the Triad invaded Meridia's realm, the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md), and she was imprisoned within Nocturnal's darkness to prevent her from interfering further.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Meridia-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESONocturnal-21)</sup>

This chase would eventually lead into Nocturnal's realm, [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), where her treachery was revealed and it was discovered that the Heart was hidden within Iachesis himself. Clavicus Vile and Mephala both immediately manifested within the realm to claim it, only for Nocturnal to banish them both and rip it out of the Ritemaster herself. With the Heart, Nocturnal briefly took control of the Crystal Tower, hoping to use its properties of existing in every [reality](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) simultaneously, enhanced by the life force of Nirn which she'd drain through the use of a secret life draining technique she'd stolen from Sotha Sil's Clockwork City, to achieve omnipresence in the entire [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md).

In this manner she'd absorb the power of the other Daedric Princes as she invaded their [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), thus gaining infinite power through the infinite connections made, and ultimately unravel reality itself to wait for the next one to arise. Nocturnal's shadow enveloped the Tower and transformed the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) inside into shadows, while she attempted to repair Transparent Law by reconnecting it with its Heart, making the Tower whole to complete her plan.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOPO-3)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-18)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup> At the same time, her forces started invading the realms of the other Princes.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Barbas-15)</sup>

Clavicus Vile begrudgingly allied himself with the Psijic Order and the Vestige in order to stop her, granting them access to the Crystal Tower's counterpart in his own realm and having Barbas lead the charge against Nocturnal's invading forces, though not before extracting the promise of a favor at some unspecified point in the future from the Vestige. After the Vestige succeeded in finally putting a stop to Nocturnal's plot, Clavicus Vile joined the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md) alongside Mephala and disbanded the Triad for good.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Summerset-2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Barbas-15)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Mephala-22)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOClavicus Vile-16)</sup>

Other events the Triad was involved in during this time included Nocturnal taking control of the Abyssal Cabal's Abyssal Geysers for some time, following the death of the Sea Sload K'Tora,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONGeysers-23)</sup> and Nocturnal's possible backing of the Sea Sload Z'Maja's conquest of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md), through an enchanted amulet that gave the sorceress the powerful [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md), which she used to kill countless of Cloudrest's inhabitants via a fatal mist that enveloped the city, and to make shadowy clones of its protectors, the [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md) -riding [Welkynars](Lore_Welkynars.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-WPA-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-WOTW-25)</sup>

## The Court of Bedlam
During the course of their plans to remake reality, the Princes of the Triad employed a cult consisting of worshippers of all three, called the Court of Bedlam. The court was lead by three of the most favored servants of Nocturnal, Mephala, and Clavicus Vile, each titled as the "Earl" of their respective Prince.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Aldarch-26)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup>

Some of the members of the Court of Bedlam consisted of [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) who rejected [Queen Ayrenn's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Queen_Ayrenn) decree to open the borders of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) to visitors, traders, and immigrants of all races, and believed they were going to become gods in the Triad's new world.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Nedoril-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Razum-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Val-29)</sup> Leythen, Earl of Mephala, and Veya Releth, Earl of Nocturnal, believed that the Triad was going to remake the world into a place without suffering. Though, unlike Leythen, Veya planned for the current world to be unmade for that transition to occur.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-VPTP4-30)</sup>

The Court worked with the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) of the Abyssal Cabal, allies of the Triad, in order to unleash the [Abyssal Geysers](Lore_Abyssal_Geysers.md) throughout the Summerset Isle, through the use of the [Abyssal Pearls](Lore_Abyssal_Pearl.md). These mystical phenomena summoned forth [Yaghra](Lore_Yaghra.md) and other creatures associated with the Sea Sload, and would have eventually caused the Summerset Isle to sink into the sea, thus fulfilling the Triad's pact with the Sload.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlereeve-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KTora-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Indirim-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Grog-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sinking-8)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOClavicus Vile-16)</sup> Other schemes of the Court during this time included attempting to create an unnatural sinkhole that'd swallow up all of [Rellenthil](Lore_Rellenthil.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Val-29)</sup> calling forth a powerful [Hunger](Lore_Hunger.md) called the [Insatiable](Lore_The_Insatiable.md) from the realm of [The Gorge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Gorge) within the Cey-Tarn Keep,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TG-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup> and carrying out an attack against the [College of the Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) in an attempt to claim the Resolute Diamonds that served as the keys to the Crystal Tower.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup>

In preparation for their patron's attempt on the Crystal Tower, the Court of Bedlam started eliminating the cults of other Daedric Princes on the Summerset Isle, hoping to eliminate their mortal followers before the Triad made its move to attack the other Princes themselves. Among the cults that were attacked was that of Meridia,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup> whose realm would also be attacked directly some time later.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Meridia-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESONocturnal-21)</sup>

At least one of the Earls, former Psijic Order member Leythen, the Earl of Mephala, exhibited knowledge of the rites required to conjure an aspect of his Prince in order to communicate with her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLey-1)</sup>
