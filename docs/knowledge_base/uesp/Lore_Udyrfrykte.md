# Udyrfrykte

The Udyrfrykte<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BM-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> (also spelled **Uderfrykte**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TV-4)</sup> or **Udefrykte**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-5)</sup> is the name given to members of a bloodline of legendary [trolls](Lore_Troll.md). Initially thought to be a unique individual,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup> there are now at least three known trolls that have been recorded in history under the name.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BM-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-5)</sup>

Two of the known trolls with the name are believed to be the offspring of the **Uderfrykte Matron**, also known as the **Horror of Dive Rock**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup> While her origins are unknown, it is said trolls were more impressive in ancient times,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Serpent-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TAO-8)</sup> and her ancient lineage has plagued the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) for centuries.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SS-S-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TV-4)</sup> Common traits between her offspring include glowing green eyes, brownish fur, and a preference for lairing in ice caves.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BM-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-5)</sup>

## History

### The Udyrfrykte of Solstheim
*His ancient name was the Udyrfrykte, though the settlers knew him only as death.*
—An excerpt from *[Thirsk, a History](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thirsk,_a_History)* The first, and best known, Udyrfrykte to have been slain, once lived in the ice caves beneath the mead hall of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). He was described by the locals as "a remorseless monster with a taste for human flesh".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SS-S-9)</sup> According to legend, the ancient beast was first awoken from his long hibernation beneath the ice due to the noise and commotion made during the original construction of the mead hall, during the time of [Hrothmund](Lore_Hrothmund.md). The Udyrfrykte came to the newly completed mead hall and wreaked vengeance upon those who had shattered the peace of his long, cold sleep. He killed without warning or mercy, reducing the Thirsk Nords to half their number. A sorcerer known as Eldrid Ice-Light managed to drive the beast back to his lair beneath the frozen lake, and used his magicka to seal the entrance with a great wall of ice. It took the survivors two months to fix the damage done by the Udyrfrykte.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TH-10)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SS-S-9)</sup>

The Udyrfrykte stayed sealed in his lair for a century or more, until he escaped in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) during the fulfillment of the [Bloodmoon Prophecy](Lore_Bloodmoon_Prophecy.md), when a pillar of fire emanated from [Lake Fjalding](Lore_Lake_Fjalding.md). The fire melted the ice covering the entrance to the ice caves on the shore of the lake; the Udyrfrykte, still wanting vengeance, attacked the mead hall, slaying the warriors within and leaving few survivors.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SS-S-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TH-10)</sup> [Svenja Snow-Song](Lore_Svenja_Snow-Song.md) survived the attack, and urged the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) to slay the monster. The Nerevarine killed the Udyrfrykte and took his heart as a trophy. Svenja then appointed the Nerevarine as the new chieftain of Thirsk.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TMHM-11)</sup>

This Udyrfrykte had a unique appearance compared to common trolls. It had only two eyes, elongated teeth, and was overall more [baboonish](Lore_Baboon.md) in build. It was also amazingly resiliant, as it was completely immune to the cold, electricity, poison, weapons lesser than [silver](Lore_Silver.md) quality, and remarkably for a troll, fire. In its battle with the Nerevarine, it wielded a severed Nord leg as a weapon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BM-1)</sup>

### The Uderfrykte Matron
*Failure and horror! We engaged the monster with all the force we could muster, but it was a travesty beyond comprehension. Svenja... My beautiful Svenja! My dear wife was killed instantly, consumed by the beast nearly whole!*
— [Agnar the Unwavering](Lore_Agnar_the_Unwavering.md) Svenja married [Agnar the Unwavering](Lore_Agnar_the_Unwavering.md), the next chieftain, following the Nerevarine's departure. Haunted by memories of the Udyrfrykte's attack, she and Agnar set out to find the ancient Uderfrykte Matron, the mother of the Udyrfrykte. Unlike her offspring, the Matron moved from location to location, preventing her killing or capture. The couple tracked the monster from the remote highlands of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) into the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), where local legends credited the monster with the slaying of over a dozen people and [cattle](Lore_Cow.md). They confronted the Matron on the [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) side of the border, near [Dive Rock](Lore_Dive_Rock.md), the highest natural observation point in the Imperial Province. The monster devoured Svenja and killed Agnar.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup> The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) stumbled upon their campsite in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), and managed to kill the monster.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-6)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

In appearance, the creature was described as "a giant, troll-like beast that seems to waver and shimmer in the cold", "like the feral form of winter itself".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup> It was completely immune to the cold, but extremely weak to fire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AJ-3)</sup>

### The Udefrykte of Dawnstar
*Brrr! Chilly! You'll enjoy this. Not an original part of the Sanctuary, per se. Let's call it a 'forced addition.' Forced by what? Oh, come and see!*
— [Cicero](Lore_Cicero.md) Another Udefrykte lived in the enclosed ice caves beneath the settlement of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) in Skyrim. The monster broke through into the abandoned [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) sanctuary beneath the city.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DS-14)</sup> It was slain by [the Listener](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), and the ice caves were then resealed when the Dark Brotherhood reclaimed the sanctuary.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCFM-15)</sup>

In comparison to its sibling and mother killed centuries prior, this Udefrykte more closely resembled common trolls.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-5)</sup>

## Notes
- In Norwegian Bokmal/Nynorsk, [Udyr](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/udyr) means *"Monster"* or *"Beast"* and [Frykte](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/frykte) means *" To fear."*
- Omluth the Fool was honored with a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) for his attempt to feed the Uderfrykte and becoming a meal instead.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TV-4)</sup>
- According to cut content, the sea troll [Skullbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Skullbreaker) in the *[Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:ESO)* was originally meant to be known as the Sea Troll Uderfrykte.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
