# Ithguleoir

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/RG-map-West_Tamriel_%28cropped%29.jpg/400px-RG-map-West_Tamriel_%28cropped%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel_(cropped).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel_(cropped).jpg) A possible depiction of Ithguleoir Ithguleoir is a mythical creature that lives in the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md), not far from the coast of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is said to be an immortal [leviathan](Lore_Leviathan.md) that stalks the depths of the ocean, particularly in the waters north of [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOContraband-1)</sup> and the outer reaches of the [Glenumbra Banks](Lore_Glenumbra_Banks.md). It is described as having an oily back and a demonic stench that smells like it's blown from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt GB-2)</sup>

The nature of Ithguleoir is largely unknown, but it is known to prey on people that sail in the water. Some have tried to destroy the beast, but none have succeeded.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ithguleour-3)</sup> It is theorized that Ithguleoir is responsible for the constant change in the Glenumbra Banks every season. It would fill in the old channels and create new ones, and if a ship was stuck on the shifting sands, then the leviathan would snatch them up.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt GB-2)</sup>

## Notes
- A cut quest from the *[Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:ESO)* would have involved slaying Ithguleoir in a cave near Northpoint with the aid of a Whalebone Talisman.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-4)</sup>
- One account from a sailor mentions a huge sea creature, said to be so massive it dwarfs even the largest islands. The old sailor claimed it has at least one massive bright green eye, larger than any structure built by men or mer. It was said to prowl the depths between High Rock and the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Whether this creature was related to Ithguleoir is unknown.
