# Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel


The **Imperial Battlemage** (also stylized as the **Imperial Battlemage of [Tamriel](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md)**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOMT-1)</sup> the **Imperial Battlemage of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md)**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> the **Imperial Battlemage of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md)**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena Intro-3)</sup> or the **Grand Battlemage**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAH-4)</sup> serves as the chief advisor to the [Tamrielic Emperors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrielic_Emperors) on varying matters (such as: governance, warfare, battle magic, occult sciences, et al).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOMT-1)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup> When the role is extant, the Imperial Battlemage typically heads the [Imperial Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-6)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup> The [Nibenese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenese) government of [Cyrodiil City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodiil_City) has featured an "age-old aristocracy of battlemages" since the early [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1C-7)</sup> but the Imperial Battlemage as the designated title for battlemage advisors didn't arrive in its current form until the [Reman Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_Empire).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AT2-8)</sup>

During the reign of the [Septim Emperors](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), it was customary for the future Imperial Battlemage to be chosen from the [Spire Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Battlemages) (a elite clique of five powerful battlemages who administer and maintain the [Imperial Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) —an elite Mages Guild and Imperial War College).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-9)</sup><sup>:17</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FRG-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-11)</sup><sup>:56</sup> A strong relationship of some sort seems to exist between the Imperial Battlemage and the [Imperial Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), with the Imperial Battlemage having the direct power to promote or dismiss members of the guild.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWArch-Mage-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LTO-13)</sup> Another example of this relationship can be seen in the case of a regional Archmage of the Mages Guild seeking to undergo the Battlespire Test to become a member of the [Tamrielian](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md) elite.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FRG-10)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-JRG-14)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1C-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-9)</sup><sup>:18</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-11)</sup><sup>:56</sup> Finally, [Imperial Battlemage Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) of the [Septim Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septim_Empire) was himself a "Master-Wizard" of the Mages Guild,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Ocato4-15)</sup> and also Ocato's battlemage bodyguard, Evangeline Beanique, was a "Warlock" of the Mages Guild.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Evangeline4-16)</sup>

## Notes
- The Spire Council that administered the Battlespire was known to also operate facilities outside the Battlespire itself. One such location was the Dread Cellar, a magically warded prison where the Empire's worst enemies were held and interrogated, or tortured, for information. The Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel, traditionally selected from among the administrators of the Battlespire, was also aware of the existence of the facility and held visitation rights to it. The Dread Cellar operated for centuries until its eventual abandonment during the tenure of Abnur Tharn as Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel due to the collapse of the Empire, and it is unknown if its operation ever resumed.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Lucilla-23)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Martus-24)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-VL-25)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DCLS-26)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-9)</sup>
