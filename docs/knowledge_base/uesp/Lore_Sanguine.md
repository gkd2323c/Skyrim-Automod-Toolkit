# Sanguine

[![Attention.svg](https://images.uesp.net/3/3f/Attention.svg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Attention.svg) **Warning:** *This article contains mature subject matter. Reader discretion is advised.* [![Sanguine (Daggerfall)](https://images.uesp.net/8/87/DF-prince-Sanguine.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-prince-Sanguine.gif) Sanguine, known to his worshippers as the **Lord of Revelry**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:293</sup> **Blood-Made-Pleasure**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONMI-2)</sup> and he **who tastes the Shaven Fruit**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1I-3)</sup> is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose sphere is hedonistic revelry, debauchery, wild orgies, and passionate indulgences of darker natures.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBOD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-D4C-5)</sup><sup>:11</sup> As the **Daedra Prince of Lust, Perversity, and Unnatural Sexual Relations**, anything in excess is his motto.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFSanguine-6)</sup> Sanguine is depicted as a portly yet muscular crimson-skinned man with a demonic head with horns, tusks, usually with a [bottle](Lore_Alcohol.md) in his hand or a [whore](Lore_Prostitution.md) under his thumb.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFSan-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBSan-8)</sup> He is thought to control thousands of small realms called the [Myriad Realms of Revelry](Lore_Revelry.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LFNHATRAYQ2-9)</sup> He is known to be allied with [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFFactions-10)</sup> while his enemies are [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFFactions-10)</sup> the [House of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFHouse-11)</sup> and the [Benevolence of Mara](Lore_Benevolence_of_Mara.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFFactions-10)</sup> His summoning day coincides with [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md), which falls on the 16<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Dawn.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-12)</sup> Celebrations associated with Sanguine include the vulgar Crendali Festivals, which he is said to have helped [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) with creating,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-13)</sup> and [Carnaval](Lore_Carnaval.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Po DV-14)</sup>

Sanguine has a reputation among mortals for not being "as bad" as other Daedric Princes and is likelier than most Princes to take an interest in mortal affairs.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRLoading-15)</sup> Some see him as a harmless, inveterate drunk due to his outgoing nature and his propensity for partying and drinking [alcohol](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TVTo B-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOBaseekus-17)</sup> Despite his vulgar nature, many consider Sanguine one of the more "sophisticated" Princes.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LA:ABayq-18)</sup> He is described by [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) to be one of the weaker Princes.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lt Fayq-19)</sup> Those who worship him or appreciate his nature claim that Sanguine has never hurt anyone maliciously.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Argues-with-Frogs-20)</sup> He enjoys playing pranks on others.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBSan Quest-21)</sup> He is reputed for throwing the best parties in Oblivion and celebrates at the drop of a hat. One instance of these spontaneous celebrations is the 400th anniversary of Sanguine's [dog](Lore_Dog.md) learning to play "[undead](Lore_Undead.md)," which occurred sometime around [2E 182](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_182).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOChee-Saak-22)</sup> All manner of entertainment can be found at Sanguine's parties, such as stage plays, orgies,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-D4C-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFSanguine-6)</sup> bardic concerts, dancing, skooma,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRN-23)</sup> arena fights, and of course, refreshments.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LOTP-24)</sup> Food and drink are usually present, but [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) is not.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SISheogorath-25)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Partying with Sanguine can be a dangerous affair, as those who stay too long may not want to leave or are enticed to stay and may end up dying there.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOSings-with-Drink-27)</sup> It is in Sanguine's nature to indulge the natures of others, particularly their darker desires—so to Sanguine, 'absolute control' is anathema.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LFNHATRAYQ2-9)</sup> The Daedra in attendance may also become violent towards mortal guests.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESODeroh-Metaku-28)</sup> Sanguine welcomes all mortals to his shindigs. Still, those who enter must partake in the drinks there if they wish to remain. Those who drink from Sanguine's goblet will be bound to the party and unable to leave.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-FV-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESOAldmeri_Spy-30)</sup> Sanguine's cultists have tricked entertainers into signing contracts that force them to perform endlessly for Sanguine's parties.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-GN-31)</sup> Some desperate mortals who have been forced to stay have committed suicide to escape.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-GN-31)</sup> Mortals with businesses or families on Nirn may be tempted to abandon their families and responsibilities to stay forever.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOVivrun_Radus-32)</sup>

One of his possessions includes a red hat.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-FNH-33)</sup> There exists wine in Sanguine's private stock called 'Sanguine's Reserve' that allows one to control the dead. When poured over a corpse, the spirit of the mortal that the corpse belongs to can be commanded.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOSings-with-Drink-27)</sup> Scarlet bottles of a rare and expensive 'Sanguine's Rose' perfume are reputed to have aphrodisiacal properties.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-34)</sup>

## Religion and Culture

### Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-crown_store-Saturalia_Ice_Steed.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Saturalia_Ice_Steed.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Saturalia_Ice_Steed.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Saturalia_Ice_Steed.jpg) Saturalia Ice Steed associated with Sanguine (ESO) The [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) holiday of [Saturalia](Lore_Saturalia.md) has a story where during a winter storm, a lost and abandoned mercenary prayed for rescue, and Sanguine answered it by [creating](Lore_Golem.md) a frozen steed for her, which allowed her to take vengeance against the lord that betrayed her on Saturalia morning.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-SIS-35)</sup> Saturalia was originally a holiday for a "long forgotten god of debauchery", though it is unknown whether this refers to Sanguine or a different deity.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SDF-36)</sup> However, elements of Saturalia have been adopted by other races and cultures for their [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) thanks to merchants from [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) capitalizing on the holiday through consumer goods. Widespread adoption of Saturalia-themed products and practices across Tamriel for usage in the New Life Festival make for a booming market during this time of year, touching even Tamrielans that grew up with their own New Life traditions.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LATH-37)</sup> Gift giving is something shared among these two holidays.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CWCFP-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-39)</sup> Relevant to Sanguine is the youth of the gentry in [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) giving each other licentious presents for the New Life Festival that are intended to dare the recipients into lascivious behavior, though the giver's name is never revealed—it's up to each recipient to guess who their 'Secret Sanguine' was.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-NLFI-40)</sup>

— *[Mad God's Masque and Bellicose Ball](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Mad_God%27s_Masque_and_Bellicose_Ball)* According to some sources, Sanguine has been a favorite for several Emperors since the foundation of the [first Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). During the reign of [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md), records indicate Sanguine resided in [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) where he is said to have helped Reman with the dubious draftsmanship of the Crendali Festivals. The vulgarities found within these festivals did nothing but hinder Imperial expansion efforts within the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) during Reman Cyrodiil's tenure.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-13)</sup> In more modern history however, Cyrodilic Daedra worship was conducted only in secret.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-MH-41)</sup>

In [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), those seeking to speak with Sanguine usually offer a bottle of Cyrodiilic Brandy to one of his shrines.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Engorm-42)</sup> The early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) master vase painter Martina Russus of [Cropsford](Lore_Cropsford.md) specialized in black figure pottery and created a [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) relic of Sanguine worship that combines their love of weald [wines](Lore_Wine.md) and Daedric worship into a pitcher depicting kneeling figures accepting a red liquid into their goblets, with the tip adorned with a red rose.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PSR-43)</sup> Followers of Sanguine have created objects such irregular five-sided [ivory](Lore_Ivory.md) dice, where the [sigils](Lore_Sigil.md) act as numbers, and small pots of balm made from human blood called "Sanguine Smiles", used at formal occasions.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-WWT-44)</sup>

### Khajiit
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-npc-Nisaazda_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Nisaazda_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nisaazda_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nisaazda_02.jpg) A vampiric Clan Mother summons sanguine blood creatures Sanguine is known to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) as "**Sangiin**": the **Blood Cat**, the **Blood God of the Second Litter**,<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TWS-45)</sup> or the **God of Death and Secret Murder**.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-VOFTK-46)</sup> According to the writings of [Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md), Sangiin is not evil by nature, but his sphere distracts Khajiit from the Path.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TWS-45)</sup> His sphere encourages people to seek out the urges of blood and pleasure without purpose.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TWS-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-47)</sup> "Sangiin's Midnight" is the term for when both moons are dark, and bright crimson red claw polish is used during this time.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-48)</sup> His worship is hidden from the Cat's Eye, [Magrus](Lore_Magrus.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-VOFTK-46)</sup> He is prayed to in secrecy in forbidden shrines.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOEGTE-49)</sup> Sangiin grants those born under the sign of [The Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) the gift of swift mending.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Hanubina-ko-50)</sup> Wrist sheaths crafted from black leather have prayers to Sangiin written with metallic ink on the inside.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Cont Shawl-51)</sup> The trickster god [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md) is said to have had a drinking contest with Sanguine.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-52)</sup> It is alleged that Sangiin was the one who gave Khajiit the secret of wine by spilling the blood of the grapes.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ONSereyne-53)</sup>

He is said to tempt Khajiit by [making their flesh immortal](Lore_Vampire.md), likely implying he grants a form of vampirism to his followers.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TWS-45)</sup> Though darker, they are still drawn by his promise of filling their cups and tasting life as Sangiin desires.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-EKP-54)</sup> The vampires of the [Hollowfang Clan](Lore_Hollowfang_Clan.md) are devoted followers of Sangiin.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOHFC-55)</sup> Clan members are known to bind their spines in leather and gird their loins, as Sangiin does before a tempestuous night.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-CM78:MF-56)</sup> Hollowfang vampires devoted to Sangiin often perform blood sacrifices. Their thralls may be driven to kill themselves<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-sh-57)</sup> to bring forth a Hemo Helot, an orb of blood used in rituals or to empower other vampires.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TSS-58)</sup> Hollowfang vampires use [Blood Magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md) to summon blood specters, and may do so while invoking Sangiin's name.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Nisaazda-59)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/1/1b/ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png) An emblem representing Sanguine To the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), Sanguine was known as the **Blood-Made-Pleasure**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONMI-2)</sup> Those from the kingdoms that worshipped the more malevolent Daedric Princes were known to indulge in the pleasures of art-torture, such as flesh-sculpting and gut-gardening.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-DWTA-60)</sup> An ancient Ayleid manuscript attributed to Eldhaal the Inducer describes himself as being overseen and guided by Sanguine in these heinous arts, particularly in the creation of the "Messenger Beast Redeemed".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONMI-2)</sup>

The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) have no use for Princes such as Sanguine and [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LA:ABayq-18)</sup>

Sanguine worship has a strong presence on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), with the Revelers of the Rose cult drawing a large following. This is partly due to the influence of [Sunport](Lore_Sunport.md), whose people enjoy celebration and are not overly judgmental. It helps that the revelers are also open to anyone who wishes to join their gatherings, though some believe their festivities can sometimes go too far.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Vo FS-61)</sup>

Some of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md)'s worshippers were once devotees of Sanguine. One follower of Dibella saw the two deities as stark opposites: Dibella teaches that love and beauty are meant to be treated with care and respect, whether toward people or objects, while Sanguine perverts these ideals into thoughtless indulgence that leaves only emptiness.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ONHPC-62)</sup> Another perspective comes from a former follower of Dibella who turned to Sanguine, believing Dibella was too restrictive. For her, beauty is freedom and the ability to revel in whatever brings joy, such as dancing however she wishes. Under Dibella, she felt constrained by needing to conform to appearances and grace, believing this caused her sense of beauty to become faded.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-ONHraewin-63)</sup>

[Carnaval](Lore_Carnaval.md) is a holiday associated with Sanguine, whose exact date is unknown, save for the fact that it is a party thrown by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Po DV-14)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ESOCarnaval-64)</sup> It is a portmanteau of [carnal](Lore_Sex.md) and carnival. Indeed, people participating in Carnaval honor Sanguine with unrestrained celebration, boundless enthusiasm, and surrender their mind, body, and spirit to the rhythm of everlasting revelry.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ONCC-65)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONFD-66)</sup> [Tamrielian](Lore_Tamriel.md) customs for Carnaval can vary depending on region. For example, participants from the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region decorate themselves in elaborate swirling and twirling body markings believed to draw Sanguine's gaze.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONMarks-67)</sup> If a reveler is not careful, there is a danger that they may not leave with their soul intact.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ESOCarnaval-64)</sup>

[Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md) is a holiday celebrated throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) on the 16th of [Sun's Dawn](Lore_Sun%27s_Dawn.md), honoring love wherever it is found, among friends, lovers, and even passing strangers<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-ONRL-68)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Event Announcement-69)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-DF-70)</sup> Ironically, Heart's Day originated as the Summoning Day dedicated to Sanguine.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-DF-70)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LATH-37)</sup> As newer religions rose to prominence, they appropriated the holiday and sanitized Sanguine’s influence in order to strengthen devotion to their own traditions.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LATH-37)</sup>

### Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/79/ON-crown_store-Sanguine%27s_Rose_Red_Lip_Tint.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Sanguine%27s_Rose_Red_Lip_Tint.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Sanguine%27s_Rose_Red_Lip_Tint.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Sanguine%27s_Rose_Red_Lip_Tint.jpg) A Sanguine's Rose Red Lip Tint Sanguine's worshipers drink, dance, wear red lipstick,<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-SRRLT-71)</sup> and make love to honor him.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Engorm-42)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBSan Quest-21)</sup> According to his [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md), worshipers must revel in the moment as they've never done and fix on the passionate excess in their minds, then let it go to fully enjoy the next moment, and so on. Some say that with practice, one can see the very debauchery of Sanguine gleaming from their eyes in the form of a vermilion red color.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Eo S-72)</sup> Some worship Sanguine because they think "normal" life is too boring, and they would simply rather be partying than going to work or being with their families.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOVivrun_Radus-32)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-OBAshni-73)</sup>

During Sanguinary feasts, revelers may wear masks to conceal their identity. Though not usually violent cultists, they are willing to assassinate any uninvited guests who witnessed them partake in such events.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ONST-74)</sup> Certain extremist cult branches promise the blood of human sacrifices to Sanguine, and go to great lengths to chase down escapees to fulfill their oaths.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TFG-75)</sup> He inspires perversions in the wild and meek to seek illicit pleasures, such as convincing virgins to take part in taboos.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-76)</sup> When the first roses of the season bloom, the followers of Sanguine gather in candlelit gardens to caress each other's faces and mark them with markings described as "a rose-shaped path of pain and pleasure". <sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TOEM-77)</sup>

They use sacred, floral-based inks and their markings are floral in design as a reminder of Sanguine's great gifts: both bliss and agony.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TOEM-77)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-TOEBM-78)</sup> At his celebrations, shadowy goats called Rams of Dark Delights are fitted with the symbols of the party: sweet drinks and heady rose perfumes, and lead revelers through the streets.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-79)</sup>

## History
Although the term "sanguine" means a crimson or bloody red liquid,<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-SIDervenin-80)</sup> the definition of the word is actually based off Sanguine's name, as the names of Daedra predate the concepts they connote.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-RGNGasta-81)</sup>

### First Era
King [Hale the Pious](Lore_Hale_the_Pious.md) introduced four of the Daedric Princes to the written record almost a thousand years after the publication of the [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md) in one of his tractates when he wrote that the wickedness of his political enemies was comparable to the depravity of Sanguine.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-OO-82)</sup> Sanguine has been a favorite for several Emperors since the foundation of the first Empire. During the reign of [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md) records indicate Sanguine resided in White-Gold Tower where he is said to have helped Reman with the dubious draftsmanship of the Crendali Festivals. The vulgarities found within these festivals did nothing but hinder Imperial expansion efforts within the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) during Reman Cyrodiil's tenure.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-13)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-npc-Samara_Gautier.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Samara_Gautier.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Samara_Gautier.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Samara_Gautier.jpg) Sanguine, disguised as a Breton under the alias "Samara Gautier"
(ESO) Around [2E 53](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_53), a growing Sanguine cult began gaining footholds in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PSR-43)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), several [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) spies entered Sanguine's Demesne and drank from Sanguine's goblet, binding them to the party and rendering them unable to escape.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-FV-29)</sup> That same year, Sanguine was recognized as a public menace by the authorities in [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md), and a bounty was put out for his cultists.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-83)</sup> Stormhold's citizens knew to stay away from the nearby temple to Sanguine.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ESOBeek-Nei-84)</sup> When the dragon Sahrotnax crashed into Moongrave Fane, the Hollowfang Clan considered it a great boon from Sangiin and performed many rituals in his name to prepare feasting upon the dragon's blood.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-NJ-85)</sup>

Sanguine himself manifested in Tamriel for the [Jester' Festival](Lore_Jester%27s_Festival.md), taking on the alias of a Breton named "Samuel Gourone". Seeking to liven up the celebrations for his fellow Prince [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), he had one of the festival goers compel a group of Daedra to perform as minstrels. Naturally, after the performance, the gathering almost turned into bloodshed before an act of absurd divine intervention turned the Daedra into confetti, presumably banishing them back to where they came.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ONGBT-86)</sup> During [Heart's Week](Lore_Heart%27s_Week.md), Sanguine took on the form of a Breton named "Samara Gautier" and sought out festivalgoers to teach them of the old Sanguine traditions of Heart’s Day. These rituals included inducing intoxication through a frog’s kiss to embrace risk and indulgence, sanctifying revelry with a blood toast, and nudging lovers to seize the moment rather than leaving love to chance.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-ONTM-87)</sup>

### Third Era
Just before the events of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), an [unknown agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) was sent by Sanguine to kill a monk who had slain his worshippers. Sanguine promised the Sanguine Rose as a reward, despite the fact that it had been set aside for his favored disciples. The agent accomplished the task, and was given the Rose by a worshipper of the Prince.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-DFSanguine Quest-88)</sup>

Sometime before the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) was involved with Sanguine's cult and obtained the Sanguine Rose. An incident in the cult, presumed to have been caused by the dangers of the Rose, left many of his friends dead, and Martin thereafter dedicated himself to Akatosh. The forbidden knowledge on Daedric magic he had gained from the cult, however, was instrumental in bringing about an end to the Crisis.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-OBMartin-89)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Sanguine enlisted the Hero of Kvatch to sabotage an excruciating uptight party of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) Countess [Alessia Caro](Lore_Alessia_Caro.md), where she and her guests suddenly found themselves stripped of all their possessions—clothes included, leaving a lasting impression on her otherwise formal public image.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBSan Quest-21)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-PSSG-90)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/SR-npc-Sam_Guevenne.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Sam_Guevenne.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Sam_Guevenne.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Sam_Guevenne.jpg) Sanguine, disguised as a Breton under the alias "Sam Guevenne"
(ESO) Circa [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176), the [Sheathed Blades](Lore_Sheathed_Blades.md) came across a drunken beggar in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) named Otto who claimed to be a Prince. He begged the adventurers to escort him to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and he would pay them later. Upon arriving to the city, the Sheathed Blades encountered a group of thugs that knew Otto and attempted to kidnap him. In exchange for the Prince, the thugs were willing to offer a reward. The stories diverge from here, but one account states that the adventurers refused to give up Otto, and due to the display of their loyalty, the beggar revealed himself to be Sanguine. The Daedric Prince stated his wants clearly, he wanted blood and fun, and chose [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) as his target. The Sheathed Blades therefore are rumored to have reveled in and ransacked the city, and Sanguine gave his blessing to his wine and blood covered participants.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-91)</sup>

The apprentice of [Balagog gro-Nolob](Lore_Balagog_gro-Nolob.md), [Urzhag gro-Larak](Lore_Urzhag_gro-Larak.md), made camp with some mercenaries near [Kynesgrove](Lore_Kynesgrove.md) circa [4E 197](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_197). Sanguine came into the camp and introduced himself as a mortal named "Sam" and offered his supplies in exchange for having a place to stay. While the mercenaries helped themselves to Sanguine's seemingly unending supply of alcoholic beverages, Urzhag only took some of his sweet peach treats. Sanguine's "Shaven Fruit" is a recipe Urzhag invented in an attempt to replicate the snack, though he never managed to make it taste as sweet.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-92)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Sanguine, in the guise of a Breton named "Sam Guevenne", challenged the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to a drinking contest. The events that followed culminated in the Last Dragonborn entering one of the Myriad Realms of Revelry and receiving the Sanguine Rose. This is one of the most recent recorded instances of Sanguine manifesting on Nirn.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-SRSanguine-93)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Death Dealer's Fete
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Death_Dealer%27s_Fete.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Death_Dealer%27s_Fete.png) Death Dealer's Fete The [Death Dealer's Fete](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Death_Dealer%27s_Fete) is a signet ring associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Sanguine. The ring is cursed, allowing one to hear a glimpse of some revelry in the dead of night, full of laughter and anguished screams. Its enchantment, Escalating Fete, invigorates the wielder during conflicts, increasing higher the longer they are locked into combat. Its intended purpose is believed to be for preventing exhaustion while partying, fighting, or other rigorous activities.

It is believed to have been forged in the mid- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and like many rings of that time, has a crude fastening system with heavy handed smithing that makes its silver prongs near unbreakable. The artifact's power comes from the various components that make up the ring's anatomy. The prongs themselves serve as conduits for siphoning [magicka](Lore_Magicka.md). They hold the center stone, an excessively huge and asymmetrical blood-red [ruby](Lore_Ruby.md) that is warm to the touch and resonates with great power. The ring's shank is designed for discomfort, as it features minuscule barbs. This design choice is also present within other rings that Sanguine gives to mortals that attend his parties. These rings were enchanted to unexpectedly tighten to prevent exhaustion, though it is unknown if the Fete itself also has this enchantment. The base contains a faded inscription which, when touched, inspires reckless indulgence. The bridge was sculpted to resemble a fanged maw; its open spaces are thought to have once held jewels.

### Sanguine Rose
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg/150px-SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg) Sanguine Rose (Skyrim) The [Sanguine Rose](Lore_Sanguine_Rose.md) (sometimes called **Sanguine's Rose**) is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Sanguine. It can take on many forms, including that of an actual [rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rose), a wooden stave carved like a rose or a staff-sized rose. Although powerful, the Rose is not an artifact most would care to possess. It can be used to summon a lesser [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), who will attack all except the holder. The Daedra is uncontrollable, unlike those summoned by [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), but as with conjured creatures it is only bound to the mortal plane temporarily. Each time the Rose is used it wilts a little, and when all the petals fall off, it loses its power. When this happens, a new rose blooms somewhere in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and is plucked by Sanguine to be given to another champion.

### Threads of the Webspinner
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/56/MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg/150px-MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg) Threads of the Webspinner The [Threads of the Webspinner](Lore_Threads_of_the_Webspinner.md) are 27 pieces of extravagant enchanted clothing and jewelry associated with both [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and Sanguine. The story goes that Mephala sought to reward her devoted followers within the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), and turned to Sanguine to create them. Each Thread enhances a single skill, such as weapon skills, speechcraft, and magic.

### Rod of Revels
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/af/ON-item-Rod_of_Revels.jpg/150px-ON-item-Rod_of_Revels.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Rod_of_Revels.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Rod_of_Revels.jpg) Rod of Revels The Rod of Revels is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [Artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by Sanguine. It has the ability to turn any location into a small part of Sanguine's [Realms of Revelry](Lore_Myriad_Realms_of_Revelry.md).<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-ESOGoat-94)</sup> It was used in Sanguine's Fair on [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) until being broken by the Lady of Riot. It was rebuilt with the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to overthrow the usurper and return the fair to Sanguine's control.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ESOFair-95)</sup>

## The Myriad Realms of Revelry
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg/200px-SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg) One of the Myriad Realms of Revelry The [Myriad Realms of Revelry](Lore_Revelry.md), also called the **Realms of Revelry** or **Revelry** for short, are a congeries of one hundred thousand pocket and sub-realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Indulgence, Sanguine. The realms are used mainly as pleasure pockets, refashioned to meet the needs and desires of its visitants. As such, Sanguine exerts minimal control over them, which aligns perfectly with his preferences.

The waters found within the Realms of Revelry were documented in *Sanguine's Rites and Realms* by the Prisoner of the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md). This text documented a phenomenon in which visions of ill-fated, prince-less realms of Oblivion colliding with each other could be seen within the waters' reflections.
