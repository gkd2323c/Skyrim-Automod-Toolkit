# Tiger God

The Tiger God was an obscure figure once worshipped by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Bantha](Lore_Bantha.md) on the 14th of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md). It was speculated that the [Feast of the Tiger](Lore_Feast_of_the_Tiger.md), held on this day, may have originally been a religious observance in honor of the Tiger God, rather than a harvest thanksgiving. By the time of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), however, the feast had evolved into a more festive occasion, still celebrated in the rainforest, but now resembling other harvest festivals. It was not a solemn time of reflection and gratitude, but a lively celebration marked by grand festivities across villages.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOTIB-2)</sup>

## Notes
- [Tigers](Lore_Tiger.md) have been associated with other gods by other cultures as well at times - [Tosh Raka](Lore_Tosh_Raka.md), the leader of [Ka Po' Tun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ka_Po%27_Tun), is referred to as the divine Tiger-Dragon.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MA-3)</sup> In the [Nedic](Lore_Old_Nedic.md) language, the word 'Tosh' could mean either time, tiger, or [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), depending on its context. It was also a component of the name of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the god of time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-APAYQ-4)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), who would later be revered as the Ninth Divine, [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), was known for his fondness for tigers, a passion that began in his youth.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MK-5)</sup>
