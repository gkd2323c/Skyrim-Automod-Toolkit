# Talos

— *[Ten Commands: Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ten_Commands__Nine_Divines)* Heir to the Seat of Sundered Kings, **Talos** (meaning **Stormcrown** in the [language](Lore_Ehlnofex.md) of the ancient [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> also called **King of Earth and Sky**, **Dragon of the North**, **the Dragon Prince**, **the Most High**, and **Lord of Shining Hosts**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-2)</sup> is the most important hero-god of Mankind. In life, he was Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), who conquered all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel_(continent).md) and founded the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), ushering in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Talos, or Stormcrown, is the name given to him at birth in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> Also called [Ysmir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods_Y#Ysmir), 'Dragon of the North', and General Talos. He is also referred to as 'the Grey Wind' and 'the Storm of Kyne' by the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AH-3)</sup>

Amulets of Talos, traditionally crafted with Dragonbone and scales,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> are used in worship of the God.

## History
Tiber Septim died in [3E 38](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_38),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHot E1-5)</sup> and it is believed he ascended to godhood and became the ninth aspect of the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines), the major gods of Tamriel. These gods are alternatively referenced as the "eight and one" because of his distinct origins, the eight other [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) arising from the interplay of [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) and [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) before the creation of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) or man.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AA-6)</sup> The Divine Talos is the patron of questing adventurers, warriors, and generals, and was perhaps the most glorified god of human races, as Tiber Septim was a man and united much of the lands now comprising the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). Less prominent sects, such as the [Talos Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talos_Cult) and [Order of Talos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Talos), have also been devoted to the worship of Talos. Tokens commonly associated with the original Eight Divines were hybrids of Nordic and Aldmeri gods, often thought to be the same entities (e.g., [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SATD-7)</sup>

In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), worship of Talos was banned as a result of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) between the Aldmeri Dominion and the Empire, with the Dominion claiming that a man couldn't achieve apotheosis.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTM-8)</sup>

The ban on Talos worship was initially only loosely enforced by the Empire, with people still having their shrines to Talos, until the Markarth Incident. In the chaos of the Great War, the Reachmen seized Markarth and declared independence, hoping to rejoin the Empire as an Imperial Province. However Ulfric Stormcloak led a Nord Militia to crush this uprising, and for compensation demanded a repeal on the ban of Talos Worship, which the Jarl of the Reach was forced to grant. This in turn brought the attention of the Aldmeri Dominion who demanded the right for the Thalmor to hunt and arrest those under the suspicion of Talos worship, or else the Empire would be seen as violating the treaty. The issue of Talos worship continued to divide the Nords, and would ultimately lead to the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) that erupted into the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Wars.md), led by Ulfric Stormcloak.

## Artifacts
The levitating [Boots of the Apostle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boots_of_the_Apostle) are attributed to the Emperor, though it remains veiled in mystery.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TL-9)</sup> The [Armor of Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Armor_of_Tiber_Septim) played an important role in helping to end the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBBot D-10)</sup>
