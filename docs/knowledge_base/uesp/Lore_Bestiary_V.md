## [Vahath Behemoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vahath_Behemoth)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/ON-creature-Vahath_Behemoth.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Vahath_Behemoth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Vahath_Behemoth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Vahath_Behemoth.jpg) A Vahath Behemoth [Vahath Behemoths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vahath_Behemoth) (or simply **Vahaths**) are giant feathered [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) beasts found on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) that were created by ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to act as guardians. They are in actuality thought to be an offshoot of Argonians themselves, modified in their eggs through ancient [arcane](Lore_Magic.md) meddling in the days of myth and legend. Most accounts claim they were created when Stone-Nest tribe turned to [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) powers when the [Corelanyans](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) invaded Solstice in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and used dark rituals to change hatchlings in their eggs into beasts to drive the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) away. Other tales suggest Vahath behemoths are even older, though it is uncertain if these are true. While they have certain similarities, they are not to be confused with [Xal-Krona](Lore_Xal-Krona.md), better known simply as Argonian Behemoths, who are created through the ingestion of [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md). The Vahath is suspected to be the more ancient variety of transformed Argonian.

Vahath behemoths are described as warriors cursed to relish excessive violence and death. They often attack by rolling around or throwing rocks. The process of their creation was said to have turned them into brutes, unlike the similar Xal-Krona they have lost the power of speech, but they still are said to retain a clever malice. Some are intelligent enough to tame and keep [voriplasms](Lore_Voriplasm.md) as pets, and are even seemingly capable of using [magic](Lore_Magic.md). Some have been known to defend themselves by creating clouds of [disease](Lore_Disease.md). They have been to known to try and destroy ancient Corelyanan ruins, with some believing this is them following their nature of trying to drive out invaders.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Vahath_Behemoth)

## [Vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/SR-creature-Dwarven_Horse.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Horse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Horse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Horse.jpg) A Dwarven Horse [Vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md) is the [Dwemeris](Lore_Dwemer_Language.md) word for "mount". It is used to refer to the many different types of [animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) constructed by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) for use as steam-powered mechanical mounts. Many of these constructs were made to imitate nature, taking the form of animals commonly used as mounts by the other [races](Lore_Races.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Like other animunculi, vamidiums appear to utilize [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) and [dynamo cores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dynamo_Cores) as a power source.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Dwarven_Horse.md)<sup>([CC](Skyrim_Forgotten_Seasons.md))</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Dwarven_Horse), [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Skyrim_-_The_Adventure_Game)

## [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/SR-npc-Rargal_Thrallmaster.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Rargal_Thrallmaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Rargal_Thrallmaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Rargal_Thrallmaster.jpg) A vampire A [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) is a preternatural being, commonly believed to be a reanimated corpse, which consumes the blood of living creatures. The vampires of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) are [undead](Lore_Undead.md), diseased persons who are hated, hunted, and misunderstood by the living. Whether they consider themselves cursed or blessed, or whether they have given into their animalistic instincts or have sought to rid the world of the disease, vampires are nonetheless considered abominations.

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Vampire), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Vampire), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Vampires), [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Stormhold:Vampirism), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Vampire), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Vampire.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Vampire_(NPC)), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Vampire), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Enemies#Vampire), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models), [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Dungeons#Encounters), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Vampire Lord](Lore_Vampire.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_10.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_10.jpg) A vampire lord The Vampire Lord is a powerful type of [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md), being much closer to being pure-blooded, although they do not necessarily have to be. Vampire Lords are tall humanoid [bat](Lore_Bat.md) -like creatures with sharp, sometimes poisonous talons and small wings. These creatures can shift between their original forms and the Vampire Lord form at will. They are also capable of using magic, and can also transform themselves into a cloud of bats to pass small distances or even mist to become temporarily invincible.

## [Vermai](Lore_Vermai.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/BS-creature-Vermai_02.gif/200px-BS-creature-Vermai_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Vermai_02.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Vermai_02.gif) A vermai [Vermai](Lore_Vermai.md) are a type of gaunt and sharp-toothed [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that serve as dark minions of the lesser [Daedra lords](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md). They are aggressive and mindless, and typically attack all who approach. It is said that the Vermai have no thoughts, and therefore cannot fear. They have sometimes responded to attempts at communication, but never in any intelligible way, considered to be one of the most idiotic Daedra.

Those who summon them can control them through pacifying helmets. At some point during the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the prodigal student Faindor of [Agea Relle](Lore_Agea_Relle.md) summoned Vermai through alchemy to serve him; out of spite, Nerien'eth had those same Daedra torture him. [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md), a servant of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), summoned Vermai to guard his tower and kept them under control through helmets. The "Vermai Clan" served Zenaida Nacarra, aiding [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) in his invasion of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Vermai), [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Vermai)

## [Verminous](Lore_Verminous.md)
The [Verminous](Lore_Verminous.md) are bipedal [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) beasts that were once common across [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md) [lands](Lore_Morrowind.md), but went extinct long before the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) created [Verminous Fabricants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Verminous_Fabricant), artificial [imitations](Lore_Fabricant.md) that bore the name of the Verminous, to preserve part of the past and serve as a reminder of things that once were. The original Verminous were perceived as a "small", as in relatively unnoteworthy species. Knowledge of the Verminous had long faded from public consciousness following their extinction, as the residents of modern-day [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), and even some residents of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) were unfamiliar with the species the fabricants were based on.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Loremaster%27s_Archive_-_The_Clockwork_City.md)

## [Verminous Fabricant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Verminous_Fabricant)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-creature-Verminous_Fabricant.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Verminous_Fabricant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Verminous_Fabricant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Verminous_Fabricant.jpg) A Verminous Fabricant [Verminous Fabricants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Verminous_Fabricant) are [Fabricants](Lore_Fabricant.md) created by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) that resemble lean bipedal [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md). They have long necks, long tails, a horn on their noses, and various metallic limbs and spikes, which they utilize to attack their prey. Sotha Sil created them as imitations of [extinct creatures](Lore_Verminous.md) of the same name that once roamed across [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md) [lands](Lore_Morrowind.md), to serve as a reminder of a thing that once was. He sought to perfect them (in his eyes) with an amalgam of flesh and metal. Aside from being quick, agile, and being able to fit into small spaces, they also are capable of spinning into their target, generating shockwaves, and as a last resort, self-destructing. They are powered by an [animo core](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Animo_Cores), and can be contained within fabrication tanks. Verminous Fabricants dwell in [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), but have in occasion made it to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

**Found in:** - [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Verminous_Fabricant), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Verminous_Fabricant), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Verminous Fabricant)

## [Voriplasm](Lore_Voriplasm.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Voriplasm.jpg) A voriplasm A [Voriplasm](Lore_Voriplasm.md) (a.k.a. an **Ooze** or **Slime**) is an amorphous blob of sentient green mucus, mostly found in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Known for having an insatiable hunger, these creatures can strip down their victims to the bone within moments, or can form spikes to pierce their target. Voriplasms can then envelop and puppeteer the remaining skeletal structure of their victims as a "voriplasmic corpse". The word for their name in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) is unknown, but it translates to "death-mud".

- Variations: Bile Glob, Giant Voriplasm, Monstrous Ooze, Peryite's Glory, Riven Ooze, Spattering Ooze, Swamp Rot

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Voriplasm), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Vulk'esh](Lore_Vulk%27esh.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/ON-creature-Rocky.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Rocky.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Rocky.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Rocky.jpg) A vulk'esh [Vulk'esh](Lore_Vulk%27esh.md) are creatures of fire and lava that can be found all across the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md), such as in [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md), and [Galen](Lore_Galen.md). They are known to live in underground volcanic depths, swimming through seas of fire. Some have even described them as being born in magma, though they do possess actual flesh. They have been observed emerging from volcanic vents, and [Ash Hoppers](Lore_Ash_Hopper.md) can often be found in their wake.

Vulk'esh and the conditions that resulted in their creation are considered somewhat mysterious. The idea of them being created by mages is considered unlikely, while other passing theories include the idea of them originating in some fiery realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Given how they thrive in volcanic depths, it is speculated they may simply be natural residents of the deep underground of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). It is suggested there could be thousands of the creatures beneath the crust, with the their range theoretically extending as far as [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md).

Despite their strange morphology, they have some traits akin to [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) like [lizards](Lore_Lizard.md), such as laying eggs to proliferate. They are known to shed their outer layers of rock like a [snake](Lore_Snake.md). After being shed, the cooled molten crust of these creatures is known as "Vulk'esh [Scoria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scoria)", and it is believed to have magical properties. It is speculated Vulk'esh eat [minerals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minerals), but there is no actual evidence for this. Their excrement has a tendency to harden into dense, heavy, igneous [coprolites](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coprolite).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Vulk%27esh)

## [Vvardvark](Lore_Vvardvark.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-pet-Vvardvark.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Vvardvark.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Vvardvark.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Vvardvark.jpg) A vvardvark [Vvardvarks](Lore_Vvardvark.md) are primarily small creatures found in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) that were the result of a failed experiment by the [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) magister [Varkenel](Lore_Varkenel.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They are a magical hybrid that possesses scales, fur, and a long tongue, with their diet consisting of small insects. They are popular pets, and some Vvardvarks are even large enough to act as pack animals or riding mounts.

They were prevalent on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), but by the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Vvardvarks found on Vvardenfell were wiped out by the [Ash Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) phenomenon. Though due to be being imported elsewhere, they were also widespread on the mainland, notably in the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Vvardvark), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Vvardvark Experiment)
