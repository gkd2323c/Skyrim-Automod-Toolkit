#### Caccino Aurelia (?b - [3E 393](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_393))
[Caccino Aurelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caccino_Aurelia) was a chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the fourth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

He became Chieftain of Thirsk after slaying the Imperial Champion Claudius Anzione and presenting his sword as a battle trophy. He would, however, rule for only three weeks, as he was revealed to be a fraud and killed by [Eldjar Bear-Skinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eldjar_Bear-Skinner), who took his place as chieftain.

#### Ard Caddach (fl. 2E 559 - 582)
[Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of the [Blackdrake Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackdrake_Clan) (also known as the **Despot of Markarth**) was a [Reachman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) who was the Imperial Governor of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and later the Ard of Markarth during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During the reigns of his kinsmen, Emperors [Moricar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moricar) and [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic), Caddach served as the Imperial Governor of Markarth. After Leovic was overthrown in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Caddach set aside his imperial title and took instead the old [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) title of ard, thereafter ruling Markarth as an independent city-state. As ard, Caddach consolidated power and worked to unite the [Reachman clans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Clans) under his authority. In 2E 582, he played a role in fighting the reemerged [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) and contributed to its defeat.

#### Sir Cadwell (fl. ME)
[Sir Cadwell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cadwell), also known as **Cadwell the Betrayer**, **Hero of Cyrod**, **The Villain of Elsweyr**, and **Champion of the Third Nedic Massacre**, is an ancient [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) knight turned [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven). In life, he was a cruel, power-hungry man who claimed the horrors of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) pale in comparison to his heinous deeds. He helped [Khunzar-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khunzar-ri) and his kra'jun seal the dragons away in the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus), only to betray him in an attempt to take the power sealed away in [Jode's Core](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Core). He died at a last stand in the Shadow Dance Temple during the Merethic Era. According to **Cadwell the Soul Shriven** his head was "quite unceremoniously" separated from his body via decapitation. His remains were scattered across the land and his name wiped from all records. His soul was thereafter sent to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and he became a [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven), now being the oldest among the Soul Shriven to have not gone feral.

#### High Astrologer Caecilus Bursio (fl. 2E 582)
**High Astrologer** [Caecilus Bursio](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caecilus_Bursio) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) member of the [Star-Gazers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Star-Gazers) of [Belkarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Belkarth). He held a senior position within the order and was renowned for his prolific writings, as well as his expertise in star navigation and the study of constellations.

#### Calani Direnni (fl. 1E 482)
[Calani Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calani_Direnni) was one of the prominent members of the influential [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md), and the pioneer conjuration mage. Along with their contemporary, Arch-Conjurer [Corvus Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corvus_Direnni), Calani was a pioneer of the modern [School of Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration). Their binding rituals are still used when summoning lesser [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).

Calani took part in the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors) circa [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482), where creatures conjured by them and Corvus played a pivotal role. Direnni mages mounted on flaming horses first appeared during this battle.

#### Vateshran Calbokh (fl. 2E 582)
**Vateshran** [Calbokh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calbokh) was one of the revered [historians and storytellers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vateshrans) among the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk). He was a wandering storyteller, who dedicated himself to the preservation of tales from across the Reach. He possessed extensive knowledge of tales featuring prominent figures such as King [Faolan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Faolan), Chieftain [Fiasof](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fiasof), and [Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrielic_Emperors) [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic). He was known for his captivating recitals and lived during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), specifically in the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a period known as the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). He was contemporary with other notable Vateshrans such as [Barth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barth) and [Ovra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ovra).

#### Camhain Tharn (fl. 2E 558 - 577)
[Camhain Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camhain_Tharn) was a [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) member of [House Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharn) and a nephew of the first of the [Longhouse](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) [Emperors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrielic_Emperors) [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) of the [Blackdrake Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackdrake_Clan), and possibly [Veraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veraxia_Tharn). He was an author of the *[A Reach Travel Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Reach_Travel_Guide)* and was known for his pro-Longhouse sentiments.

#### Chief-of-Chiefs Cannear (fl. 2E 578 - 582)
**Chief-of-Chiefs** [Cannear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cannear) was chief of the [River-Elk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:River-Elk_Clan) [Reach](Lore_Reachmen.md) clan in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). In the years leading up to [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), she attained the title of [Chief-of-Chiefs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Clans#The_Chief-of-Chiefs), a position of great authority in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), and held it for at least four years.

#### Caius Cosades (fl. 3E 427 - early 4E)
[Caius Cosades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caius_Cosades) was the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) Grand Spymaster of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) that were stationed in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). He played a major part in the defeat of [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) and in the [Nerevarine prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md). Caius' cover story in [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora) was that of an old man with a [skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skooma) problem; however, at some point, he genuinely became addicted to skooma, resulting in the higher-ups becoming concerned about his addiction.

#### Calantius of Skingrad (fl. 2E 582)
[Calantius of Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calantius_of_Skingrad) was the Count of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in 2E 582, he issued a decree that was ratified by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), redesignating [Arenthia Vale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Woods) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) as the [South Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Woods). He also ordered the [Legion of the West Weald](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) to annex this region. Calantius was also the author of *[The Fort Sphinxmoth Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Fort_Sphinxmoth_Ruins)*, a comprehensive text detailing the history of [Fort Sphinxmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Sphinxmoth).

#### Calaxes (?b - [3E 498](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_498) *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>)
**Calaxes** was the bastard son of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md). He was given the Archbishopric of The One in the Church of the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines). During Calaxes' archbishopric, tensions arose between himself and the Emperor over the Emperor's legislation to limit the Church's authority. It was rumored that Calaxes believed that the Gods were angered with the secular governments of Tamriel and the Emperor specifically. Rumors circulated that Calaxes was advocating a rebellion to establish a theocracy. Soon afterward, Calaxes suddenly disappeared, having supposedly been assassinated in the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One) by the Imperial Guard because of the threat he represented. His disappearance was dated to the 29th of Sun's Dusk [3E 498](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_498) *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*. However, as the Third Era did not last to that date, the death date as given is likely a mistake.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WAP-1)</sup>

#### Calcelmo of Markarth (fl. 4E 201)
[Calcelmo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calcelmo_of_Markarth), Scholar of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), is an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [conjurer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) of great renown who dedicates himself to researching the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). He and his nephew Aicantar study the [Dwemer ruins beneath Markarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nchuand-Zel). He is the author of the series of books known simply as *[Dwarves](Lore_Dwarves_(book).md)*. His next expected work is a book on the translation of the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) language which is tentatively titled *Calcelmo's Guide to the Falmer Tongue*. For other historical information, see the [lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calcelmo_of_Markarth).

#### Calixto Corrium (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Calixto Corrium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calixto_Corrium) was a retired [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) adventurer that was active in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). #### Master Bard Callisos (fl. before 2E 582)
**Callisos** was a famous traveling bard, and one of the most illustrious alumni of the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> The Flame of Callisos constantly flickering next to the steps of the College was named after him. It is said that as long as the flame burns, the College will stand.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WTW11-5)</sup> He was Nirn-renowned musician, who allegedly tricked Sheogorath himself into giving him Callisos' Lodestone. Callisos persuaded Sheogorath that his ordinary lyre had been graced by Dibella's unbridled passion. In return, Sheogorath offered Callisos an instrument he named "Lodestone." This lyre was tethered to Callisos' very soul, promising him glory and fame—promises fulfilled. Over time, Callisos' remarkable talent and heroic exploits made him a legend among Bards College alumni. Yet, one day, Lodestone was discovered in a college practice room, and Callisos vanished. It's widely believed that he constantly traveled Tamriel, trying to elude Sheogorath's vengeance, but eventually, his luck ran out.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CL-6)</sup> During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), murals depicting his travels and early life were frequently found all across Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TL-7)</sup>

#### Prince Camarril (fl. before 3E)
[Camarril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camarril) was a prince of an unknown Cyrodilic dynasty.

His tomb is located in the [Green Emperor Way](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Emperor_Way) district of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). [Mankar Camoran's](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) infamous [*Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md) contain a hidden message that points to the tomb of Camarril, upon which Mankar had inscribed a runic depiction of the symbol of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) and the location of their hidden base, which was enchanted to glow red at midday. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) was able to find this hidden message and infiltrate their base in the caverns beneath [Lake Arrius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Arrius) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).

#### King Camaron ([3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368) - [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403))
[Camaron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camaron) (also spelled **Cameron**), the **White Moon**, was a King of the Kingdom of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was the husband of Queen [Akorithi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akorithi), and father of [Arthago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arthago), [Aubk-i](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aubk-i), [Greklith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greklith), and [Lhotun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lhotun).

During Camaron's rule, a disagreement between Sentinel and the Kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) over the Isle of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony) sparked a [war](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) in [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402). He died at the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field) the following year, killed in a duel with Daggerfall's young King [Gothryd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gothryd) which ended the war.

#### Prince Camaron (fl. [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432))
**Camaron** was the prince of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) following the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). He was born to King [Gothryd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gothryd) and Queen [Aubk-i](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aubk-i), whose marriage established peace between Daggerfall and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). #### King Camoran Aeradan (fl. 2E 489 - 582)
[Camoran Aeradan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran_Aeradan) was the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) king of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). His reign began after a civil war for sovereignty with his cousin, [Gelthior Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gelthior_Camoran), but later, he was among the leaders that spearheaded the first iteration of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), as well as, part of the original Inner Council of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).

#### King Camoran Anaxemes (fl. 2E 830)
**Camoran Anaxemes** was the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) King of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) during the formation of the Second [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830). In 2E 830, Anaxemes and the other heirs of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) began to fight over the throne of Valenwood. When a faction of the Bosmer offered territorial concessions to the [Colovians](Lore_Colovia.md) in exchange for support for the faction's claimant, the Altmer cited a stewardship clause in a treaty from a thousand years before and quickly established a provisional government, the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), on behalf of their claimant, Anaxemes. After the Aldmeri army defeated the Colovians and silenced the other heirs to the throne, the Altmer reminded Anaxemes of the price of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md)'s aid: fifty years' fealty to the King of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md). These events marked the beginning of the Second Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-9)</sup>

#### Camoran Gelthior (fl. [2E 489](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_489) - 582)
[Camoran Gelthior](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran_Gelthior) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who was the leader of the Blacksap Movement and a member of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md). Gelthior led the Blacksap Movement in a civil war against his cousin, King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran), over his perceived leniency toward the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). Around [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) intervened in the civil war to support Aeradan, which led to the Battle of [Cormount](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cormount), where General [Endare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Endare) and the Jade Dragoons massacred many Blacksap rebels, and Gelthior was captured. After his capture, the [Vinedusk Rangers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vinedusk_Rangers) imprisoned Gelthior in a remote [Ayleid ruin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins), ending his involvement in the Blacksap Rebellion.

#### Sapiarch Camilonwe of Alinor (fl. 2E 582)
[Camilonwe of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camilonwe_of_Alinor) was a powerful [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [conjurer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration). He was also an accomplished scholar and author, who held the position of [Sapiarch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapiarch) of Transliminal Arts (or Transliminal Travel) in the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). #### Consul Cardea (fl. 2E 568 - 582)
[Cardea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cardea) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) consul and the administrator of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Cardea became the administrator of Markarth under the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), and after the dynasty's fall, she retained the position under the city's former governor, Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md). She was also a prolific writer. She was an author of two books related to the history and the politics of the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk): *[Politics of the Reach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Politics_of_the_Reach)*, and *[History of Markarth: A Story in Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:History_of_Markarth__A_Story_in_Stone)*. She also penned an autobiographical text *[An Imperial in Markarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:An_Imperial_in_Markarth)*, and numerous other documents of administrative nature, as well as a leaflet for contractors willing to aid Markarth.

#### Count Carolus Aquilarios (2E 550 - ?d)
[Carolus Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Carolus_Aquilarios) (known as the **Wolf of Kvatch**) was the Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) during the late sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). When his uncle [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) initiated a [revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) against Emperor [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), he left Carolus in Kvatch to defend the city. He defended the city until [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), when Varen overthrew Leovic and declared himself emperor, which resulted in Carolus being named Count of Kvatch. However, as soon as Carolus was officially named Count of Kvatch, [Artorius Ponticus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Artorius_Ponticus), the ambitious and powerful Primate of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), began working to undermine the Count's authority and carve out more power and influence for himself.

Before Artorius' challenge could come to a head, [Fortunata ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortunata_ap_Dugal) led a fleet of merchant warships and pirate vessels into [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) Harbor, took control of the city, and declared the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) to be a free and independent state. Fortunata's pirates subsequently slaughtered Carolus' cohort of the Second Legion in the Gottshaw Massacre, leaving him with no choice but to swear fealty to an independent Gold Coast. These events marked the start of a three-way power struggle over the Gold Coast between Carolus, Artorius, and Fortunata.

#### Cernunnon
[Cernunnon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cernunnon) is a spirit of importance to the [Dreadhorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreadhorn_Clan) [clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk_Clans) of [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk). Dreadhorn Clan, similarly to many other clans of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) venerated and made pacts with spirits, both lesser and greater. Cernunnon was summoned to aid them during the siege of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

Cernunnon was capable of granting immortality to their champions, also known as their oath-bound and the spiritmasters. So long Cernunnon was undefeated, their oath-bound were effectively immortal. It was Cernunnon who determined when they die. The oath made by their followers sustained them and protected them from death. Cernunnon could grant power to their followers, who held Cernunnon's power within them. The oath, also referred to as a compact made between Cernunnon and their oath-bound was believed to be unbreakable and eternal.

Cernunnon wielded powers associated with the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) and could manipulate it to pull the souls of their foes. Cernunnon could also possess and reanimate the corpses of the deceased. Oath-bound of Cernunnon were known for spilling their enemies' "blood for the Hunt-Father" and offering their "soul for Cernunnon".

In the Reachfolk culture the void is associated with [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) and the realm of spirits.

#### Empress Regent Clivia Tharn (fl. 2E 577 - 582)
[Clivia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn) was the ruler of the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). A member of the prominent [Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharn) family, born to [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) and his seventh wife, [Pulasia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pulasia_Tharn), and Abnur's sixteenth daughter overall. She was also the widow of former Emperors [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) and [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios). Abnur had plans for her first child to inherit the throne, but by the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) she was "comfortably lodged in Mannimarco's pocket", and had disappeared from the public eye months before the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). When she was 7-years-old, Clivia played a game of Imperial War-Chess with her younger relative [Euraxia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn) and played an illegal move. Outraged, Euraxia taunted her by saying that she would one day be queen of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), but Clivia remained unimpressed, stating she would be the Empress. Her sister [Septima](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septima_Tharn) noted that while she lacked instinctive prowess in the magical and political arts, Abnur groomed her for the Ruby Throne because she had an innate ability to command others, and they would have no choice but to follow. She married [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), and served as his consort. When Leovic was overthrown and killed in a violent [rebellion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen%27s_Rebellion) for legalizing [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), his usurper, Duke [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol), wed Clivia to consolidate his rule. When the [Five Companions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Five_Companions) embarked on a journey to locate the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), Abnur sent Clivia a letter instructing her to oversee the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) in his absence and saying that if [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)'s plan to relight the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) worked, her first child would inherit the whole of Tamriel.

In [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), Varen mysteriously disappeared during a calamity known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md), and Clivia was quickly declared Empress Regent of the Empire in his stead. This aroused widespread suspicions that Clivia and her father were responsible for Varen's disappearance, though it unbeknownst to even Clivia it was Mannimarco's doing. Mannimarco, who had orchestrated the Soulburst, became the power behind the Ruby Throne, turning Clivia to worship [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and manipulating her into expelling the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) so that his [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult) could operate freely. When the three alliances invaded [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), they quickly conquered much of Cyrodiil and confined the Empire to only the Imperial City and [Lake Rumare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Rumare)'s environs. The alliances were unable to take the Imperial City, and so Clivia continued to rule, but despite this, whenever one of the alliances gained supremacy over the other two alliances, they would choose an Emperor from their own ranks, although these Emperors were usually short-lived. This lasted until a [dark anchor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Anchor) opened over the Imperial City, heralding a Daedric invasion which quickly overran the Imperial forces, and resulting in the deaths and capture of many of its citizens who were unable to escape.

While absent from the Elder Council meeting at the start of the invasion, she was in charge of commanding troop movements, though her nonsensical orders for General Velarius suggest she was being impersonated even at the time. Elder Councilor [Itinia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Itinia) had not seen Empress Clivia for months even before the anchors fell, and had not seen her after. As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Clivia's fate is unknown; she is not among the known survivors of the Daedric invasion of the Imperial City, and one of Molag Bal's servants, [Molag Kena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Kena), impersonated her to gain access to the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in order to carry out the "[Planemeld Obverse](Lore_Planemeld.md)" ritual. It is unclear how long Kena had been impersonating Clivia.

The alliance spy networks tried to discover the true fate of Clivia Tharn. At some point after the defeat of the Molag Kena, Dhulef, a court mage of the [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) inquired his old associate, the leader of the [Ring of Daggers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Daggers), a [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) spy network, [Copper Dariah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Copper_Dariah) about the whereabouts of the Empress Regent. She responded in a cryptic manner: "Stop asking about the Tharns. They're graveyard dead, buried in the ground. We have enough unquiet corpses in the world. Why go making more?"

#### Saint Coellicia (?b - ca. [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242) – [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243))
[Saint Coellicia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coellicia) was a minor martyr of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), which took place between [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242) and [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243). She died by torturous starvation, and was later canonized as an [Imperial saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Saints) by [the Church](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Church).

Saint Coellicia is typically honored in the closing days of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md) with a fast. The only food that was traditionally allowed to be eaten during these fast days was [bread](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bread) crumbs. Usually only a single spoon of crumbs would be eaten after the local bishop's recitation of the Creed of Crumbs. At the end of each fast day, an unadorned religious wafer would be eaten to signify the end of the fast.

Coellicia is most often remembered for an infamous banquet known as the Feast of Saint Coellicia, which took place under the reign of Emperor [Brazollus Dor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazollus_Dor) at some point prior to [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877). Known for his debauchery, Brazollus sought to rekindle the adoration of his subjects by replacing Coellicia's fast with a feast. This resulted in a twelve-hour marathon of forty dishes, held at the Emperor's autumnal estate in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). The description of this feast's many courses counts among the most famous mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) culinary texts of the southeastern [Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_River) watershed. An apocryphal account suggests that Brazollus had Coellicia's bones disinterred, ground, and mixed in with the [flour](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flour) as an ingredient in his feast, although the practice of saint-eating had been banned almost a decade earlier.

#### The Crow
[The Crow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Crow), also known more specifically as the **Luminary Crow**, was one of the [Luminaries of Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary), mysterious immortal beings of pure [magic](Lore_Magic.md), spirits likened to beams of sunlight from [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) himself, her chosen form was that of a [Crow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow). As the looming threat of the loss of [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) left little time to plan the ritual to Ulfsild's normally exacting standards, the process was not fully refined, causing the Crow to have a chaotic arcane presence. The ritual to create the Crow took an enormous toll upon Ulfsild's spirit, siphoning a considerable amount of her soul, and required years of recovery. The Crow described itself as Ulfsild's [familiar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Familiar).

[Cuilean Darnizhaan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cuilean_Darnizhaan) was a great sage and member of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). In life, they were the Psijic Proctor of Abyssal Fragments. They were quoted by Loremaster [Celarus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Celarus) in his publication *[The Old Ways](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Old_Ways)*, as well as by scholarly institutions such as the [School of Julianos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:School_of_Julianos).

Cuilean believed that no subject is too trivial to warrant research and discovery, and that "the greater scheme of things" is too broad of a concept, hence the creation of their most recited quote. This belief led Darnizhaan down the path to study [Detritus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Detritus), a demi-plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and the realm of the [Daedra Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Lord), [Hollowjack](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hollowjack). Cuilean labeled people constantly targeted by Hollowjack's fear feeding as "Hollowjack's Haunted". Taking cases of his "hauntings" in the Psijic Order's library and their research, they wrote a treatise on Hollowjack called *[Lord Hollowjack's Dread Realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord_Hollowjack%27s_Dread_Realm)*.

#### Curalmil (fl. before 1E 140)
[Curalmil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Curalmil) was a legendary [alchemist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy), and a [Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tongue), who lived in an early age of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and is known for the creation of the [White Phial](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:White_Phial).

#### Cyndassa (fl. 3E 405)
[Cyndassa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyndassa) was a lady-in-waiting employed by Queen [Aubk-i](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aubk-i) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md); she worked in the kitchen of [Castle Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Daggerfall). She played a pivotal role in the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). As the maid of her Queen, she was the one that received [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md)'s letter intended for the Dowager Queen [Mynisera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mynisera) from the [Knights of the Dragon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Dragon) courier that the [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md) sent and transmitted it to Aubk-i herself.

Cyndassa claimed that she saw the Queen's jaw drop in amazement after reading the fateful letter sent by Uriel VII and that Aubk-i informed her that she would transmit the letter to the intended recipient, Myniseria, herself. Some sources claim Cyndassa was the reason the letter went missing, stating that she read the letter, and while she did not fully understand its content, she understood that it was important and sold it to the highest bidder, [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gortwog).

At some point, Cyndassa's brother became a [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md), and despite considering him as good as dead in that state, she grew to pity him. When [the Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) approached her for information, she sent them to kill her brother in exchange for information on the letter. Her brother was said to have been menacing the region he was found in for weeks by the time the agent slew him. The peasants who lived in the area were known to have delighted in his killing.

#### Governor Cyril Vinticae (fl. c. 3E 427)
The governor of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) when it was under siege by the [Ice Tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ice_Tribes) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Vinticae banished his four champions, correctly suspecting that one was a traitor in league with the Ice Tribes. He called for the [Hero of Dawnstar](Lore_Hero_of_Dawnstar.md) to interrogate the champions, ordering the local healer and [oracle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) [Eustacia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eustacia) to assist.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Eustacia's visions helped the Hero to determine the truth and reveal the traitor, for which the Governor gave the Hero [Starfrost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Starfrost).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

#### Cyrus ([2E 839](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_839) - ?d)
[Cyrus the Redguard](Lore_Cyrus.md), also known as **Cyrus the Restless**, and **Sura**, **Sura of the Bend'r-Mahk *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]***, or **Sura-do-Hega** in [Yoku](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yoku), (b. [2E 831](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_831)) was a [hero](Lore_Hero.md) best known for leading the [Stros M'Kai Uprising](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stros_M%27Kai_Uprising) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Having spent the early years of his career under the wing of the [Cyro-Nordic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyro-Nord) pirate captain [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tobias), Cyrus traveled to the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in search of his missing sister, [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), where he ended up leading his fellow [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) in a rebellion against the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) alongside her.
