# Travelers League

[![](https://images.uesp.net/c/c8/DF-npc-Malthar_%28face%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Malthar_(face).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Malthar_(face).png) A member of the Travellers League The Travelers League was a specialized branch of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) tasked with overseeing [teleportation](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) services.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TS-2)</sup> The League frequently found itself at odds with the Cabal, another faction within the Guild that managed sanctioned [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TC-3)</sup> Interestingly, the League also counted the [Vraseth](Lore_Vraseth.md) [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline in particular among its enemies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vraseth-4)</sup>

**Guild Guides** were active in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) and performed similar tasks.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MGG-5)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-crown_store-Mages_Guild_Recall.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Mages_Guild_Recall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mages_Guild_Recall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mages_Guild_Recall.jpg) Mages Guild Recall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/ON-misc-Group_Teleportation.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Group_Teleportation.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Group_Teleportation.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Group_Teleportation.jpg) Guild mages performing a group teleportation ritual In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), during the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the Mages Guild maintained an active portal network across Tamriel. These portals were specifically designed to transport both people and materials efficiently, allowing for large groups to travel en masse. Unlike personal teleportation spells, Guild portals were significantly less taxing on individual mages.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Laloriaran Citadel-6)</sup> For extended use or large-scale transport, the Guild employed Portal Anchors, arcane devices used to stabilize and sustain portals for longer durations, ensuring reliable passage during coordinated movements.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Portal Anchor-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Stirk-8)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/e/eb/DF-npc-Travelers_League.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Travelers_League.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Travelers_League.png) A league member offering services [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/MW-npc-Erranil.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Erranil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Erranil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Erranil.jpg) Erranil, one of the Guild Guides During the Third Era, the Guild Guides of the Mages Guild provided instant transportation between the primary halls for a modest fee. This service was accessible to both members and non-members, though Guild members typically received a substantial discount.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MGG-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GTSM-9)</sup> In addition, the Guild maintained a specialized branch known as the Travelers League, a more exclusive service reserved for high-ranking members. Unlike standard hall-to-hall travel, the Travelers League could teleport individuals to virtually anywhere in the world.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TS-2)</sup>

### Fourth Era
The Travelers League, as a subdivision of the Mages Guild, was presumably dissolved in the aftermath of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). It is unclear whether its functions were continued by the [Synod](Lore_Synod.md) or the [College of Whispers](Lore_College_of_Whispers.md), the two successor organizations to the Guild.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIC-10)</sup>
