[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/DF-map-Lainlyn.png/200px-DF-map-Lainlyn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn.png) Lainlyn ca. 3E 405 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/DF-map-Iliac_Bay_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-DF-map-Iliac_Bay_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_(Anthology).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_(Anthology).jpg) Map of the Iliac Bay Lainlyn (formerly known as **Tava's Blessing**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AUAL-1)</sup> is a region located on the north coast of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), on the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). Lainlyn is a fairly large coastal region located in the forest area of the Hammerfell-Iliac region, east of the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) and directly south of [Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFLainlyn-2)</sup> The region is affiliated with the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), the remnants of the noble class of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AUADC1-3)</sup> It is also known for its abundance of [harpies](Lore_Harpy.md), winged creatures that have a possible origin story tied to the region.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ghrae-4)</sup> The [Host of the Horn](Lore_Host_of_the_Horn.md) is the barony's knightly order and they are sworn to protect both the royal family and their country.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFHost-5)</sup>

## History

### Early History and the History of Ghraewaj
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/ON-creature-Harpy_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Harpy_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Harpy_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Harpy_02.jpg) The Harpy [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/ON-place-Ansei_Shrine_%28Tava%27s_Blessing%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ansei_Shrine_%28Tava%27s_Blessing%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ansei_Shrine_(Tava%27s_Blessing).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ansei_Shrine_(Tava%27s_Blessing).jpg) The Ansei Shrine in Tava's Blessing Before the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) settled in Lainlyn or nearby [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), there were outposts built by the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) and later the proto- [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md). They were used to protect their fisherfolk from raiders such as [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and even worse creatures like [giant scorpions](Lore_Giant_Scorpion.md), [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), and even elemental spirits.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> It is unknown when the settlement nor the barony were founded; however, one of its oldest known citizens was the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warrior, Maja. She was alive when the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) were still active and hunted down necromancers across the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AS-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EOTAW-8)</sup>

One of the oldest traditions in Lainlyn is the holiday, [Riglametha](Lore_Riglametha.md), which translates in the [Banthan](Lore_Bantha.md) dialect of [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md) as "grateful-offering". It is a large-scale festival that thanks the [gods](Lore_Divines.md) for the graces they bestowed on the people for centuries. Part of the celebration is a series of plays and performances that depict the long and storied history of Lainlyn. One such tradition is called [Ghraewaj](Lore_Ghraewaj.md), which translates in Banthan-Yoku as either "The Crows Who Were Punished" or "The Crows Who Punish".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ghrae-4)</sup>

The story revolves around a coven of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worshippers led by their mistress, Noctyr-a, who seduced the unnamed Baron of Lainlyn. Since then, Castle Lainlyn had become an orgy-filled den of decadence. Before Noctyr-a convinced the Baron to commit suicide, the Baroness gave her a white robe, which turned her into a giant black bird. No longer charmed by the witch, the Baron slew the bird and had the cook prepare a large meal with it. Unbeknownst to the Daedra worshippers, they consumed their mistress, which turned them into harpies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ghrae-4)</sup>

Details in the story have changed over time, such as when the fate of both Noctyr-a and the Baroness switch, and the witch and the Baron lived happily ever after. In the midst of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) in the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Barony of Lainlyn was ruled by the virgin Baroness, Viana the Pure, who reigned from [2E 120](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_120) to [148](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_148). During her tenure, Ghraewaj was altered so that the Baron character was a willing conspirator of Noctyr-a and the harpies consumed two birds instead.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ghrae-4)</sup>

### Lainlyn in the Late Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/8/80/DF-npc-Baron_Shrike_%28face%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Baron_Shrike_(face).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Baron_Shrike_(face).png) Baron Shrike [![](https://images.uesp.net/7/76/DF-npc-Baroness_Dh%27emka_%28face%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Baroness_Dh%27emka_(face).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Baroness_Dh%27emka_(face).png) Baroness Dh'emka By [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), the Barony of Lainlyn was jointly ruled by Baron [Shrike](Lore_Shrike.md) and Baroness Dh'menka. Shrike was known for his tyrannical rule and would often employ the Host of the Horn to intimidate peasants for tax money and harass the common folk.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFHost-5)</sup> At the time, Lainlyn's patron deity was the God of Wisdom, [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), who is represented through the temple, the [School of Julianos](Lore_School_of_Julianos.md). The region bordered several other nations at the time, such as the Dragontail Mountains to the west, [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset_(barony).md) and [Totambu](Lore_Totambu.md) to the south, and [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam_(county).md) to the east.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFLainlyn-2)</sup>

In response to the Baron's actions, his younger brother, Lord [Kain](Lore_Kain.md) and a noble from the Dragontail Mountains, Lord [Harth](Lore_Harth.md) formed the [Knights of the True Horn](Lore_Knights_of_the_True_Horn.md), who were dedicated to overthrow the Baron and restore peace to the region.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LKW-9)</sup> Sometime later, the Knights of the True Horn gathered enough momentum to stage their coup d'etat in Lainlyn. Their crusade ended in failure and the True Horn was exiled from the barony. Lord Kain's men were forced to spread out across the realms and await his message to rally their arms again, but it never came.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LKW-9)</sup>

The Baron's reign continued but it is unknown how he fared during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), when the Kingdom of Sentinel annexed all of the southern Iliac Bay, from [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md) to the west coast and Satakalaam to the east.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWITW-10)</sup> Because areas such as Lainlyn were affiliated with the Crowns, their acquisition by a [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) country kept King [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md) busy with military, diplomatic, and religious missions to keep them in check. As a way to mediate the situation, the King founded the [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md) party, a neutral third party between the Crowns and Forebears. However, it only made the situation worse, as a civil war between Sentinel and the Crowns commenced.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

## City of Lainlyn
Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Lainlyn_(city).png) The City of Lainlyn Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn_(city).png) Lainlyn city in the region [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn.md) (formerly known as **Tava's Blessing**) is the capital of the eponymous barony in south-central [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), in the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). It is a minor port city occupied by the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), who normally hold territories in the north and east. Lainlyn is most well-known for its shipyards, which have supplied vessels for the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md)'s royal navy and [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) in its heyday.

## Notes
- The Dragonsword of Lainlyn is a longsword that was last carried by the Knights of the True Horn, specifically the last remnant that lived in [Battlehorn Castle](Lore_Battlehorn_Castle.md), on the edge of the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBDragonsword-12)</sup>
- Sometime in the late-Third Era, the Breton battlemage and necromancer, Arielle Jurard had a sinister reputation in Lainlyn. One of her last known experiments was resurrecting Lord Kain, in the hopes of using him as a puppet king of Lainlyn and pleasing her lord, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LJJ-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DN-14)</sup>
- The [Montalion](Lore_Montalion.md) clan of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) are active in the region.
