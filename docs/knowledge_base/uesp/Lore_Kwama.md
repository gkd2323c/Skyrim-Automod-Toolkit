# Kwama

[![ON-prerelease-Kwama.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/ON-prerelease-Kwama.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Kwama.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Kwama.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Kwama.jpg) Kwama are large insectoids native to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), which are usually kept for their eggs (or "mined"). They live in underground communal colonies, where each kwama has a specific role. They are considered distant relatives of [nix-hounds](Lore_Nix-Hound.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> and as such may be considered to be kin to [nix creatures](Lore_Nix_Creature.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Nix-2)</sup>

## Hierarchy
At the top of the colony's hierarchy is the Queen. [Kwama Queens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Queen) are huge and bloated, and produce the nest's eggs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> They are too fat and heavy to move, so all their needs are attended to by the Workers. [Kwama Workers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Worker) dig the colony's tunnels and chambers and tend the queen and the eggs. They are usually docile, but not completely helpless.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup>

The workers also produce a pheromone unique to their colony. Different colonies compete over resources, making them natural enemies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONRD-4)</sup> [Kwama Warriors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Warrior) defend the colony's tunnels.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> They are aggressive and dangerous, with poisonous or shock attacks. They don't attack the miners because they are used to their odor, but will assault outsiders without hesitation.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Especially large warriors serve as the queen's consorts.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> They are said to sometimes fight each other for this role.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

[Kwama Foragers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Forager) hunt for prey and scout the surface and natural underground passages, searching for suitable locations for new colonies.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Foragers are aggressive, but not very dangerous.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup>

[Scribs](Lore_Scrib.md) (or squibs) are a late larval form of the kwama.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Co K-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> They are not very aggressive but can paralyze adversaries. Scribs are known to have a pupal stage; these pupae are sometimes harvested and roasted over an open fire by [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The hive can easily replace scribs, but colonies don't always recover from the loss of a Queen.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/MER-art-Kwama_Mine.jpg/200px-MER-art-Kwama_Mine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Kwama_Mine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Kwama_Mine.jpg) A Kwama Mine Kwama are exploited by both the native [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Kwama Cuttle is a tough, waxy substance that comes from the beak of kwama beasts.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Kwama Wax expands and hardens with heat, making it useful for plugging leaks or mending furniture.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONRD-4)</sup> It is also sometimes used to dress armor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GLV-9)</sup> The flesh of scribs is crushed down into the soft Scrib Jelly, a nutritious but sour-tasting gelatin with an unpleasant texture that is popular with the natives.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Scribs can also be cut into strips and dried in the sun to produce Scrib Jerky, a practical foodstuff for travelers as it doesn't spoil.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup>

Kwama Eggs are a rich, nutritious foodstuff and a principal agricultural commodity of the Vvardenfell District.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Eggmines are set up in kwama colonies, often in Morrowind, though there have been instances in [Valenwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Shadowfen.md). The miners protect the kwama from poachers, predators, and raiding kwama foragers from other colonies, and harvest the eggs judiciously, leaving enough eggs to sustain colony growth.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Kwama Eggs can be eaten boiled, roasted, or raw, and remain fresh for weeks. They are popular throughout the Empire, and are a major export for [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> The eggs come in two main sizes, large and small, with the large eggs having more alchemical properties than the small ones. The [Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) was a major threat to eggmines, making it unsafe for miners and killing the Queen if she is not cured.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup> Once the Queen is cured, the colony will recover. Kwama can also become infected with [droops](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Droops), a common [disease](Lore_Disease.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Kwama Council of Morrowind was the professional body for egg mining. It was responsible for defining commercial subsections to run mines across the different regions of Morrowind. The Kwama Consortium of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md) was one such subsection, and was responsible for running the mines on the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The Seven Eggs of Gold are seven legendary golden kwama eggs, which were laid by the Kwama Queen of the forgotten Pudai Egg Mine. The [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) were contracted to find these eggs, and in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) the mine was found on the island of [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Morrowind-12)</sup> The eggs were of solid gold and fetched a high price. Settlements such as [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) and Rethan Manor are dependent on eggmining, and [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) control a great many of the mines. The Shulk Egg Mine is the largest single source of Kwama Eggs on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-miner-3)</sup>

Kwama are also used for bloodsport, being placed in fighting pits for the amusement of onlookers.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

A symbol of a scrib is used to signify an inn or tavern in Morrowind, and one can usually be found hanging on a banner outside of the establishment. [Scrib Cabbage](Lore_Scrib_Cabbage.md), a domesticated plant found in southern Morrowind, is named after the larvae.

## Notes
- Kwama nests are sometimes home to a rare mushroom called [Kwama Caps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Cap), and kwama will become aggressive if these mushrooms are disturbed.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
- According to their [concept art](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Concept_Art) notes and a later comment from their designer, the original concept for Kwama Warriors in [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind) was that Foragers and Workers would "fuse" to become them, with the Foragers entering the tube that was present in the Workers.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Concept Art-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-16)</sup> However there are no mentions of this concept in the final game, and later designs of the creatures would drop most of the visual aspects that imply this connection.
