Pelagius Septim IV was the twentieth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for twenty-nine years, from [3E 339](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_339) to [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368). During his reign, he worked to reunite Tamriel, and by the time of his death, Tamriel was stable, prosperous, and closer to unity than it had been since the days of [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> Due to his efforts to reunite the Empire, he was considered a strong and capable ruler.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3AEM-2)</sup> He was preceded by his aunt, [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup>

## Biography
Pelagius Septim was born in [3E 314](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_314) to [Eloisa](Lore_Eloisa_Septim.md), a daughter of [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md) and sister of [Uriel VI](Lore_Uriel_VI.md) and [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> In [3E 339](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_339), Empress Morihatha was assassinated.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TET-4)</sup> Morihatha had no surviving children, and Eloisa had died of a fever in [3E 335](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_335), so Pelagius was crowned Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TET-4)</sup>

Shortly after his coronation, Pelagius IV and his family had dinner with Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and her consort [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRB5-5)</sup> In [3E 346](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_346), his son, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_VII.md), was born.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDC-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-EST-6)</sup> At some point during his reign, Pelagius IV hired Lord Vanech's Building Commission to build and improve infrastructure in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) in order to increase commerce in the province.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TAA1-7)</sup> During his reign, he was suspicious enough of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) to refuse to allow their ambassadors into the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FOA-8)</sup>

Pelagius IV continued Morihatha's efforts to reunite Tamriel, slowly bringing the rebellious kingdoms, duchies, and baronies back under imperial control. Like Morihatha, Pelagius IV was patient and careful in his efforts to reunite Tamriel, but he did not achieve the same success she did. The kingdoms, duchies, and baronies had been free of imperial control for so long that even a benign imperial presence was met with hostility. Nevertheless, by the end of Pelagius IV's reign, the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) was stable, prosperous, and closer to unity than it had been since the days of [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> In [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368),﻿ Pelagius IV died and was succeeded by his son, Uriel VII.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDC-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-EST-6)</sup>

## Notes
- Simocles Quo was Pelagius IV's Master of Cuisine. Pelagius IV reportedly called Quo "the finest connoisseur in Tamriel."<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TRKR-9)</sup>
