# Failed Incarnates

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-interior-Cavern_of_the_Incarnate.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Cavern_of_the_Incarnate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Cavern_of_the_Incarnate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Cavern_of_the_Incarnate.jpg) The Cavern of the Incarnate The Failed Incarnates (also called **"False Incarnates"** by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md)) were individuals who sought to fulfill the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md), but failed. The [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) once believed them possessed by the spirit of [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), but each had a critical flaw that made them destined to fail to fulfill the prophecy. Their spirits reside within the Cavern of the Incarnate on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESODovrosi-1)</sup> Some of the failed Nerevarines were buried with death masks resembling the visage of Indoril Nerevar.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TI-2)</sup>

## Aduri
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-npc-Incarnate_Aduri.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Incarnate_Aduri.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Incarnate_Aduri.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Incarnate_Aduri.jpg) Aduri [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate%2C_The_Warseeker.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate%2C_The_Warseeker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate,_The_Warseeker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate,_The_Warseeker.jpg) *"To seek only war is to find only death."* To fulfill the Nerevarine prophecy, Aduri followed a path of blood and war. Her love of senseless war proved to be her undoing; the path she followed led nowhere, and she was cast down as the hope of her people.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOAduri-3)</sup> She helped the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) deny Conoon Chodala's claim to the title of Nerevarine in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESODD-4)</sup>

## Ane Teria
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/MW-npc-Ane_Teria.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Ane_Teria.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Ane_Teria.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Ane_Teria.jpg) Ane Teria Ane Teria was a holy crusader of the [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) in the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)'s golden era. She contributed substantially to the writings that were suppressed by the temple and considered apographa. She followed the Tribunal unquestioningly, and didn't believe in the Nerevarine prophecies until it was too late. She offered her mace to the Nerevarine in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWAne-5)</sup>

## [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-npc-Chodala.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chodala.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chodala.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chodala.jpg) Conoon Chodala [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/MW-npc-Conoon_Chodala.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Conoon_Chodala.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Conoon_Chodala.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Conoon_Chodala.jpg) As a ghost [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md) was an [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) who was an ashkhan of the [Urshilaku](Lore_Urshilaku.md) tribe in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He falsely claimed to be the legendary [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), which made him one of the failed Incarnates.

## Danaat
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-npc-Incarnate_Danaat.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Incarnate_Danaat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Incarnate_Danaat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Incarnate_Danaat.jpg) Danaat [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate%2C_The_Fool.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate%2C_The_Fool.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate,_The_Fool.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate,_The_Fool.jpg) *"The fool hears only his own poor advice."* Incarnate Danaat refused to accept wise counsel, which led to the doom of his tribe. The Nerevarine must listen as well as proclaim, for wisdom ignored is ignorance.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESODanaat-6)</sup> He helped the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) deny Conoon Chodala's claim to the title of Nerevarine in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESODD-4)</sup>

## Elvil Vidron
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/MW-npc-Elvil_Vidron.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Elvil_Vidron.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Elvil_Vidron.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Elvil_Vidron.jpg) Elvil Vidron Elvil Vidron received waking dreams from Dagoth Ur. However, unlike other Dreamers who fell to Dagoth Ur's control, he instead believed these dreams to be prophetic and that he was the Nerevarine. He began walking around Suran, proclaiming himself Nerevarine, attracting the attention of the Tribunal Temple.

## Erur-Dan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/MW-npc-Erur-Dan.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Erur-Dan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Erur-Dan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Erur-Dan.jpg) Erur-Dan Erur-Dan the Wise was a wizard and counselor of Ashkhan Assur-Abanba of the Erabenimsun Tribe.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Manirai-7)</sup> Erur-Dan saw [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) fall to the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). He lived through Morrowind's surrender, and swore vengeance upon the Imperials and the Tribunal for their betrayal. In his later years, he left for [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), where he grew old and died fighting monsters and the [blight](Lore_The_Blight.md). He offered the Nerevarine his spear and cuirass in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWErur-Dan-8)</sup>

## Hort Ledd
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/MW-npc-Hort_Ledd.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Hort_Ledd.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Hort_Ledd.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Hort_Ledd.jpg) Hort Ledd Hort Ledd died four hundred years prior to the fulfilment of the Nerevarine Prophecy, in the final days of turmoil after the Empire came to Morrowind. He was a thinker, not a doer, and though he was chosen, he was not a hero. He offered the Nerevarine his robe in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWHort-9)</sup>

## Idrenie Nerothan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/MW-npc-Idrenie_Nerothan.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Idrenie_Nerothan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Idrenie_Nerothan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Idrenie_Nerothan.jpg) Idrenie Nerothan Idrenie Nerothan lived in the late years of the Tribunate, and helped demoralize and repel Akaviri invaders behind the scenes. She knew nothing of the Nerevarine prophecies before she took refuge with the [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md). She died attempting to loot the ruins of [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md). She offered the Nerevarine tools with which to pass locks in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWIdrenie-10)</sup>

## [Peakstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peakstar)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/MW-npc-Peakstar.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Peakstar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Peakstar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Peakstar.jpg) Peakstar [Peakstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peakstar) was an [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) woman who washed ashore near [Ald Redaynia](Lore_Ald_Redaynia.md) as a baby. She was found and raised by the [Urshilaku Tribe](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md), and became a figure of legend among the Ashlanders around [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397). Many believed this mysterious "girl child" was the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md). Though she survived [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md), she did not master the arts of war, and died while fighting an [Ash Vampire](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md). She became the last known failed Incarnate, and her spirit went to the Cavern of the Incarnate with the others. The [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) searched for her unsuccessfully for many years, and finally announced she was dead. Ashlanders were under the belief that she had been found and captured at some point. Rumors persisted that she was still alive, as the Temple could not produce a body. Her spirit helped guide the Nerevarine during the latter's campaign against [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md).

## Ranso
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/ON-npc-Incarnate_Ranso.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Incarnate_Ranso.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Incarnate_Ranso.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Incarnate_Ranso.jpg) Ranso [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate%2C_The_Brute.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate%2C_The_Brute.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate,_The_Brute.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tapestry_of_a_Failed_Incarnate,_The_Brute.jpg) *"There is no nobility in physical strength."* Ranso thought himself Nerevar, for he was the most powerful warrior in the land. Power alone couldn't save his people, and it couldn't save him.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESORanso-11)</sup> He helped the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) deny Conoon Chodala's claim to the title of Nerevarine in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESODD-4)</sup>
