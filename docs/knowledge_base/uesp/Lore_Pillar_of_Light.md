[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/BS-place-Pillar_of_Light.jpg/200px-BS-place-Pillar_of_Light.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-place-Pillar_of_Light.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-place-Pillar_of_Light.jpg) Pillar of Light The pillar of light is a large waygate that allowed one to travel from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to the [Weir Gate](Lore_Weir_Gate.md) of the [Imperial Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSIntro-1)</sup> The pillar itself is round and metallic, and featuring seven large spires and six sizeable metallic objects that face the central teleportal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSIntro-1)</sup> The pillar's exact location on Tamriel is unknown, but it appears on a shore near a large body of water that seemingly flows through a channel beyond.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSIntro-1)</sup>

Robed humanoid figures are witnessed attending to the pillar of light, and appear there when the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) is sent off to Battlespire for testing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSIntro-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | The [Letter to Mactana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Letter_to_Mactana) note from *Battlespire* refers to the **Assizes of Entry** who seemingly belong to the [Imperial Battle College](Lore_Imperial_Battle_College.md) and determine who gets to enter the Imperial Battlespire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LTM-2)</sup> The seven robed figures that appear around the pillar of light in the cutscene likely represent the Assizes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSIntro-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LTM-2)</sup> |
| --- | --- |

- Directly before *Battlespire* began, the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) is said to have participated in a competition to be selected to enter the Battlespire and eventually join the Emperor's personal Imperial Guard.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-3)</sup><sup>:18</sup> Given the linguistic defintion of this subgroup, the Assizes of Entry logically could have been the body which graded who could enter based on this competition.
- In English, "Assizes" is a plural noun mainly referring to the historical regional courts of [England and Wales](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/England_and_Wales) from the nineteenth and twentieth century.<sup>[[LANG 1]](#cite_note-wiktionary1-4)</sup> An "assize" singular however refers to "inquiry" or "verdict reached" and/or "an assembly of knights and other substantial men, with a bailiff or justice, in a certain place and at a certain time, for public business."<sup>[[LANG 2]](#cite_note-wiktionary2-5)</sup>
