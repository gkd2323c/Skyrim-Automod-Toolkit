# Air Transportation

There are many ways for the inhabitants of Tamriel to fly high in the sky.

## Aetherial Vessels
While there have been ships and vessels created capable of traveling across the void of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) to enter [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) by way of the [stars](Lore_Stars.md), they are incredibly uncommon due to the distance and amount of magicka required to make them function.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup>

### Aetherbells
The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) were rumored to have used vessels called "Aetherbells", that could "dive" through the realms of Aetherius and Oblivion. While some scholars believe in their existence, others believe there is no evidence the Dwemer ever made such things.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Furn Star Chart-2)</sup>

### Imperial Mananauts
The [Imperial Mananauts](Lore_Imperial_Mananauts.md) were a group who were known to travel to Aetherius during the days of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> In their cosmic ventures, they discovered that mortal minds must be acclimated to Oblivion by gentle degrees to prevent mental distortion. To remedy this issue, they utilized [Slipstream Realms](Lore_Slipstream_Realm.md), points within the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) where truths can co-exist without conceptual abrasion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LFNHa TR-4)</sup>

### Sunbirds
The [Sun Birds of Alinor](Lore_Sun_Birds_of_Alinor.md) were an order of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmeri.md) explorers of the Merethic Era who were known to have traveled to Aetherius in ships called sunbirds.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-5)</sup> They collected genuine celestial mineral that was used to create the [great Orrery](Lore_Great_Orrery.md) at [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md). They were dissolved due to the untenable expenditures required to reach magic by magicka.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup> Sunbird vessels have been described as "[birds](Lore_Bird.md) made out of the [sun](Lore_Sun.md)".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>

## Airships
[![RG-render-Dirigible.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-render-Dirigible.png/200px-RG-render-Dirigible.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-render-Dirigible.png) *[They](Lore_Star_Teeth.md) are fire gods, fire gods from the night sky. You haven't lived until you have chased them...* *Clouds beneath your feet, wind to your back, crew racing the rigging as you pursue that brilliant flare descending toward the horizon.*
—Captain Nym, recounting his experiences flying an airship<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SKNym-7)</sup> Airships (also known as dirigibles<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-8)</sup> or skyboats)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-9)</sup> are an uncommon method of transport in modern Tamriel, but have been used by a few civilizations.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGship-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

#### Dwemer
The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) were known to have had airships, with the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) later using them. Lord [Amiel Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md) possessed one in the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) that was subsequently crashed by [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGship-10)</sup>

Louis Beauchamp, a prominent member of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), created a patchwork airship out of Dwemer parts for an expedition to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). However, the airship crashed into the [Isinfier Plains](Lore_Isinfier_Plains.md) and all the crew died due to the cold and hunger.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The ruined remains of this same airship were home to a camp of [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md) circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRship-13)</sup>

## Balloons
Large balloons used for flight were first created by a group of inventors who worked for the great king of a faraway land. They were of such size that they could transport dozens of people through the air with ease.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-19)</sup> The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of [Caecilly Island](Lore_Caecilly_Island.md) were known to have hot air balloons. One was used by the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) in the [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

## Jumping
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png/200px-LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png) Tarhiel's ill-fated experiment Despite the existence of [levitation and flight spells](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md#Short_Range_Spells), some find the experience these spells provide disorienting.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TJ-21)</sup> Partially because of this, there have been several ill-fated attempts in Tamriel's history to develop a way to jump across great distances. Some of the earliest known attempts were in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Icarian was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mage who developed a device that utilized magicka in an attempt to jump all the way to Aetherius. He would fall to his death in northern Cyrodiil. Despite his failure, his name would eventually be recorded in the scrolls of history.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-22)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-23)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Another attempt that year was that of Tarhiel Farano, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) mage who tried jump to extreme heights with a spell, only to end up with the same fate as Icarian. After falling to his death in the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), he rose as an angry [spirit](Lore_Ghost.md), claiming that his descendants would perfect his spell.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

In [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421), the [Levitation Act](Lore_Levitation_Act.md) was passed, somewhat restricting the teaching of levitation magic.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OBDovyn-26)</sup> Following this, in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) a Bosmer enchanter known simply as Tarhiel would be the next known individual to attempt a feat of extreme verticality. This time intending to jump hundreds of miles with enchanted "Scrolls of Icarian Flight". Alas, his fate was the same as his predecessors, and he fell to his death north of [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md), leaving behind his experimental scrolls.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TJ-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-27)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-28)</sup> It had been noted that a wayward mage falling from the sky was an occasional sight in Tamriel.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

## Flying Mounts
Flying creatures are used as mounts by a few cultures in Tamriel. Not everyone is aware of this however, and some considered the prospect of flying mounts as nothing but a fantasy.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LHAYQ-30)</sup>

### Dragons
[Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) can be ridden by those they deem fit to do so.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Battlespire-31)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Skyrim-32)</sup> It is said that there was a time, long ago, when dragons and mortals took to the skies as bonded battle-mates, implying that these mortals acted as dragonriders in battle.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DRS-33)</sup>

According to some tales, ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) heroes could tame and ride dragons with the power of their [Voice](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-34)</sup> The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) is a known dragonrider, flying to [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md) on the back of [Odahviing](Lore_Odahviing.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-35)</sup> [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md) and the Last Dragonborn, at separate times, rode the dragon [Sahrotaar](Lore_Sahrotaar.md) within [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-36)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-37)</sup>

The [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) were known to have used enslaved red dragons as war mounts, which eventually inspired the red dragon symbol for the Empire.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

Samar Starlover, a member of the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) at the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), had a draconic mount named [Dragonne Papré](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonne_Papr%C3%A9) who was capable of flying all the way from Battlespire to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), seemingly able to cross the liminal barriers between them.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SL-39)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-40)</sup> One source indicates that the Empire may have an elite guard who keeps dragons as their mounts, and that Samar Starlover was one of their members.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-41)</sup>

### Gryphons
The [Welkynars](Lore_Welkynars.md) of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) tamed the native [gryphons](Lore_Gryphon.md) and used them as mounts. Given the city's central location on the island of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md), and with the help of these flying creatures, the Welkynars were able to quickly render aid across the entire realm.

### Parraptons
[Parraptons](Lore_Parrapton.md)<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-TPThe Blue Dawn-42)</sup> are insects typically the size of [horses](Lore_Horse.md) that are used as aerial mounts by [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Origin-43)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-TPThe Blue Dawn-42)</sup> Following a strict hierarchy, approval from the queen is needed to ride her children, who is a behemoth twice the size of her kin. Such an audience must not be taken lightly, for she may deny a request for fear for her children's safety. Furthermore, the queen also handpicks which of her children is best fit for the rider. Riders powder their hands and rub them together to signal to their parrapton, which depending on context, will signal when they are to fly or land.<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-TPThe Blue Dawn-42)</sup>

### Other Flying Mounts
There are legends involving flying [horses](Lore_Horse.md), though some believe them to be mythical.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-44)</sup> [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) once expressed disappointment that [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) didn't use flying horses as mounts.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-45)</sup>

Some have considered the prospect of using [giant bats](Lore_Giant_Bat.md) as mounts, though the idea is considered impossible as they are too light to support the weight of a person on their back while flying.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LHAYQ-30)</sup>
