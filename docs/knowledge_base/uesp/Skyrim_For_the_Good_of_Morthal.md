## Quick Walkthrough
1. Speak to [Gorm](Skyrim_Gorm.md) when he's apart from the [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md).
2. Deliver [Gorm's letter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gorm%27s_Letter) to [Captain Aldis](Skyrim_Captain_Aldis.md) in [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md).

## Detailed Walkthrough

### Unfit to Lead
[Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) [Idgrod Ravencrone](Skyrim_Idgrod_Ravencrone.md) governs [Hjaalmarch](Skyrim_Hjaalmarch.md) from her seat in [Morthal](Skyrim_Morthal.md). She is gifted with visions that seem to occupy most of her attention; she is less concerned with governing a people than she is with attending to her visions and passing on the wisdom she gleans. Her administration is under scrutiny as a result, as you will find out when visiting Morthal. If you speak to [Gorm](Skyrim_Gorm.md), Idgrod's [housecarl](Skyrim_Housecarl.md), inside of [Highmoon Hall](Skyrim_Highmoon_Hall.md) he will be very solicitous of Ravencrone and her visions. However, if approached while away from the jarl he will reveal that his concerns are not just about her safety, but rather the condition her gift has left her in. He believes that she may no longer be able to adequately act as jarl of Hjaalmarch.

Speak with Gorm while he is dining at [Moorside Inn](Skyrim_Moorside_Inn.md) at 6:00 p.m., or while he is walking to and from Highmoon Hall:

*"My mind weighs heavy enough. Leave me to my food."* **Is there something bothering you, Gorm?** *"She's so lost in her visions, and it's only getting worse. I've looked after her safety for so long... If she can't tell what's real and what's not, how will she continue to lead Hjaalmarch?"* **Is there anything I can do?** *"Yes, I suppose there is. There is a letter, one I have had for some time now. I think it is time it gets delivered. I cannot leave Idgrod's side, so I must stay here. Could you see that it reaches Captain Aldis in Solitude?"* **I'll see that your letter reaches Aldis.** *"Thank you. Please, do not open it. It is... private."* He will give you a letter to deliver to an [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) officer, Captain Aldis. Aldis can be found in Solitude at [Castle Dour](Skyrim_Castle_Dour.md). Reading Gorm's Letter reveals that there is seemingly a plan in the works to remove Idgrod from power. Aldis, however, seems far less sanguine at the prospect of removing a sitting Empire-loyal jarl during wartime; but, he will take the information under advisement:

**I have a letter for you.** *"For me? From whom?"* **It's from Gorm, in Morthal.** *"Well now, that is something. Hand it over, please.*"He will then read the letter, and continue, saying:

*"Oh, no. I hadn't heard from Gorm for a while, so I'd hoped he'd given up on his little scheme. The last time we'd discussed this issue was before this damnable war broke out. Things were different then. Now, what's he's asking, it's just impossible. Even if it worked, the questions and accusations it would cause... Well, I'll take it under advisement. Thank you."* He gives you 20 gold for delivering the letter.

## Notes
- Counts for assisting people for [Thane of Hjaalmarch](Skyrim_Thane_of_Hjaalmarch.md).
- The only way Idgrod is actually removed from power in game is if the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) take over Morthal.
- If siding with the Stormcloaks, this quest needs to be started before [The Battle for Fort Hraggstad](Skyrim_The_Battle_for_Fort_Hraggstad.md) and finished before the [Battle for Solitude](Skyrim_Battle_for_Solitude.md) to avoid issues. Also, [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md) can transfer Hjaalmarch to the Stormcloaks; if that happens, then this quest cannot be started until Hjaalmarch is reconquered for the Empire.
- Unlike many quest items, reading or obtaining Gorm’s letter by looting or pickpocketing will not allow the quest to begin. The letter will remain a quest item in your inventory but Captain Aldis will not show the dialogue to receive the letter. However, if you return to Gorm, he will respond as though he still has the letter.
