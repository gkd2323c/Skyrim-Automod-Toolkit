# Souls

| --- |

This article is about the souls in soul gems. For the NPCs in the Soul Cairn, see [Soul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul).

Every **[soul](Lore_Souls.md)** in Skyrim is one of six possible strengths: [Petty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Souls-Petty), [Lesser](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Souls-Lesser), [Common](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Souls-Common), [Greater](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Souls-Greater), [Grand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Souls-Grand), and [Black](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Souls-Black). More powerful creatures have more powerful souls. When a creature or NPC dies under a [Soul Trap](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md) effect, its soul is captured in the smallest sufficiently-sized [soul gem](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md) in the player's inventory. A soul may fill a gem larger than itself, which may be undesirable if one is in short supply of large gems. Filled soul gems may be used for [enchanting](Skyrim_Enchanting.md) items and [recharging weapons](Skyrim_Enchanting.md#Recharging_Weapons).

All NPCs, [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora.md), and [ghosts](Skyrim_Ghost.md) have Black souls, which are as powerful as Grand souls but only fit in Black soul gems or [The Black Star](Skyrim_The_Black_Star_(item).md). The soul level of any other creature is instead determined by its level as listed below; most creatures have a static level, but certain "leveled" creatures have a level equal to the player's level, possibly with a modifier (i.e. a multiplier or offset) applied.

| Soul Level | Charge Capacity | Creature Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Petty** | 250 | 1-3 |
| **Lesser** | 500 | 4-15 |
| **Common** | 1000 | 16-27 |
| **Greater** | 2000 | 28-37 |
| **Grand** | 3000 | 38+ or [NPC](Skyrim_NPCs.md) |
