# Bosmer

[![](https://images.uesp.net/c/c8/LO-race-Bosmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Bosmer.png) Illustration of a typical Bosmer male The Bosmer (formerly the **Boiche**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AU4TET-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWEDF-2)</sup> are the [Elven](Lore_Elf.md) clan-folk of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), a forested province in southwestern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POT-3)</sup> In the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), they are often referred to as **Wood Elves**, but they typically call themselves Bosmer<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1P-4)</sup> and the **Tree-Sap people**.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWdialogue-5)</sup> Bosmer rejected the stiff, formal traditions of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) high culture, preferring a more romantic, simple existence in harmony with the land. They are relatively nimble and quick in body compared to their more "civilized" [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) cousins (who often look down upon the Bosmer as unruly and naive). Their agility makes them well-suited as scouts and thieves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VAS-6)</sup> However, they are also a quick-witted folk, and many pursue successful careers in scholarly pursuits or trading.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ASGTN-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ADIF1-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TBG-9)</sup>

Bosmer possess a natural lifespan ranging from several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Though they are considered less influential than some of their Elven brethren, the Bosmer are also relatively prolific, with 12 children in a family considered average.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONEVE-12)</sup> As a result, they outnumber all other [mer](Lore_Mer.md) on Tamriel.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RM3TWE-13)</sup>

The best archers in all of Tamriel, the Bosmer snatch and release arrows in one continuous motion; they are even rumored to have invented the bow.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TGROM-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRG-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TML-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ADIF3-17)</sup> They have many natural and unique abilities; notably, they can command simple-minded creatures<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Racials Skyrim-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Racials Oblivion-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Morrowind Racials-20)</sup> and have a nearly chameleon-like ability to hide in forested areas,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE1W-22)</sup> as well as natural resistance to poison and disease.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Racials ESO-23)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Racials Skyrim-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Racials Oblivion-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Morrowind Racials-20)</sup> **Nature's Grace** grants Bosmer greater affinity with [healing magics](Lore_Restoration.md) and the capability to recover from harm quickly.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Racials-24)</sup> Many in the forests of Valenwood follow the tenets of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). These "Green Pact Bosmer" are religiously carnivorous and cannibalistic, and do not harm the vegetation of Valenwood, though they are not averse to using wooden or plant-derived products created by others.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ADIF1-8)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-GPBO-25)</sup>

## Appearance
As elves of the forest, Bosmeri appearance is said to match the life of Valenwood.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Bos App Arena-26)</sup> Bosmer are known for their slight stature,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VAS-6)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-BApp General-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CAOV-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> though their height appears to be sexually dimorphic, as women are often taller than men.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-BIESO-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-31)</sup> The eyes of Bosmer have star-like pupils<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-BIESO-30)</sup> and they're known to file their teeth to sharp points.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-32)</sup> Bosmer often sport antlers, which are typically glued on, although some are grown with [magic](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOAo CH-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PHat HMC-34)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-LEo ERPZI-35)</sup>

## History

### Origin

- *The Boiche Elves were of the Earth Bones who most hearkened to Jephre and his greensongs. They did not build a Tower, they grew it, a great graht-oak whose roots sprang from a Perchance Acorn. And this was their Stone. And because the Acorn might perchance have been elsewhere, thus was Green-Sap manifold and several. And each could walk.* — [Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree](Lore_Aurbic_Enigma_4__The_Elden_Tree.md)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg/200px-ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg) The Heart of Valenwood The earliest days of the Bosmer are lost in legend. Some speak of the [Ooze](Lore_Ooze.md), the shape-shifting formlessness from which they came before the Green Pact with Y'ffre. The forest was flourishing and primordial still, making shapeshifting a necessity.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Indinael Dia-36)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TOAF-37)</sup> More obscure legends also suggest some dissented to the Green Pact, with some Bosmer wishing to keep their shape-shifting abilities. According to legend, Y'ffre turned the Ooze into a kind of purgatory state for these individuals, where they would be stricken from Y'ffre's story upon death and unable to interact with any world or afterlife. Eventually, the dissenters were overcome by the other Bosmer faithful to Y'ffre. These "Oathbreakers" were then buried in [Ouze](Lore_Ouze.md) so that they could be imprisoned and protected until the time of renewal.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Indinael Dia-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-OOO-38)</sup> Some evaded capture and became known as [Changelings](Lore_Changeling.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-OOO-38)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Selene-39)</sup>

Khajiiti myth includes the Bosmer in their origins. [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) is said to have taken some of [Nirni](Lore_Nirni.md)'s favorite children, the shapeless forest people, and turned them into Khajiit. Y'ffer (the Khajiiti spelling of Y'ffre) learned how Azurah did so and told Nirni, which made her angry. Nirni separated her remaining children from those Azurah had changed, and allowed Y'ffer to change them so that they would always be mer and never beasts, and Y'ffer named them the Bosmer.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-WOCMA-40)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), the land of the Bosmer [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) myth holds the Bosmer descended from the first elves to arrive in Valenwood, long before the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VAS-6)</sup> The forests of Valenwood, or [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md) (though this term can mean more than just Valenwood), were so dense and inhabited by beasts that they could not hope to tame it, so they had to fight and adapt to become predators instead of prey.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup> This adaptation led to the creation of the Bosmer.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VAS-6)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE3All-42)</sup> It is said that the city of [Arenthia](Lore_Arenthia.md) is named after the first King of the Bosmer.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ARArenthia-43)</sup>

There's some suggestion that early Bosmer were known for intermarrying with humans.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TM-44)</sup> Another legend is that of the Boiche Elves, made from the [Earthbones](Lore_Earthbones.md) that followed Jephre, grew the first great [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) out of the Perchance Acorn, which would become [Tower of Green-Sap](Lore_The_Towers.md#Green-Sap). Due to the nature of the Acorn being, perchance, elsewhere, many different Green-Saps sprung in many different forms, forming the walking Graht-Oak forests of the Valenwood. Each one told its own stories of the Green, the forest home of the "Boiche-become-Bosmer".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AU4TET-1)</sup>

The establishment of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) in the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) brought increased trade to the Bosmer, and cities like [Woodhearth](Lore_Woodhearth.md), [Haven](Lore_Haven.md), and [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md) flourished.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ACOV-45)</sup> The ancient Ayleid site of [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md) is home to the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md), a magnificent tree of immense power which stands as the physical symbol of the soul of Valenwood. It's unclear exactly when Ayleids first started migrating to Valenwood, but it was around that time when a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) twisted the power of the Heart and used it to raise an undead army, forcing the Ayleids to seal off Hectahame, along with the Heart, in order to contain the horde.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-HOV-46)</sup> Ayleids and Bosmer during these early days also had to fend off numerous savage attacks by the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). They completely destroyed both Haven and Woodhearth, and sacked Elden Root.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ACOV-45)</sup>

### The First Era
Eventually, the Bosmer would be united under the leadership of [King Eplear](Lore_Eplear.md), whose founding of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) heralded the start of the First Era.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup> The [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) circa [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) (remembered by the Altmer as the "White-Gold Catastrophe") triggered the "[Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md)", which saw many Ayleids flee to Valenwood for safety. These migrants agreed to adopt aspects of the Green Pact and to refrain from harming the forest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AU4TET-1)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ASIV-47)</sup>

An infamous dissenter to this arrangement was the Ayleid sorcerer [Anumaril](Lore_Anumaril.md), who used powerful magic to convert the Perchance Acorn of the [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) Graht-Oak into a Definite Acorn, which prevented Elden Root from walking and gave a Green-Sap a single, definite form. He attempted to convert this Green-Sap into a new, singular [White-Gold](Lore_White_Gold_Tower.md), but due to the Perchance nature of the Bosmeri Tower, he could neither succeed or fail.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AU4TET-1)</sup> Though they found sanctuary in Valenwood, the Ayleids who migrated there seemed to fade from history. For reasons still debated by scholars, they apparently had little lasting impact on Bosmeri culture beyond imparting some stonework and masonry techniques.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ASIV-47)</sup> The Bosmer made a formal trade treaty with the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) in [1E 340](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_340), though it would only last for several decades before breaking down into on-and-off border skirmishes with [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) for most of the First Era.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup>

The earliest known Wild Hunt occurred in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369), which ended with the death of the [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-AHOD-48)</sup> This assassination can be viewed as an act of self-defense, though perhaps proactive, as Borgas had been journeying to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to rally support for a joint war against Valenwood. Another Wild Hunt would not occur again until the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup> though whether one or more occurred then is not clear. After the Bosmer were devastated by the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714), they were absorbed by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). The Empire, in their usual fashion, divided power in Valenwood among several districts to ensure they would not unite against Imperial rule.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup>

In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the last year of the First Era, on the 19th of Sun's Dawn, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) destroyed the town of [Gilverdale](Lore_Gilverdale.md) in [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) (eastern Valenwood). This prompted [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), the living wizard-mystic god of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), to negotiate an agreement with several Daedric Princes to forestall further attacks, though the details of this agreement are still unknown.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-2920L-49)</sup>

### The Second Era
In [2E 406](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_406), a salacious scandal rocked Valenwood: the sleepy trading post of [Cormount](Lore_Cormount.md) of Grahtwood was likely home to numerous potential heirs of the Camoran Dynasty, as Camoran rulers had evidently been journeying to the secluded town for untold years to engage in extramarital relations. Cormount's population nearly doubled in a week, and weddings became a daily occurrence for months, as many Bosmer sought to sire children with a noble lineage despite the ignoble circumstances. The news even brought many Bosmer who had never lived in Valenwood back to their spiritual home. These migrants, who largely came from Cyrodiil and thus did not follow the Green Pact, were shocking to the established Bosmer of Grahtwood, and tensions brewed for years as a result. Finally, in [2E 420](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_420), the migrants' village, New Joy, was burned to the ground for provocations that have been lost to history. The incident eventually inspired the formation of the Blacksap Movement in [2E 489](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_489), a Cormount-based cultural organization which hoped to formalize the Bosmeri approach to the Green Pact and thereby prevent any further tragic misunderstandings.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TROC-50)</sup> With the fall of the Empire in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), the regional powers went about warring among themselves, and [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) pirates were able to get a foothold along the coast which they used to harass the Altmer of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup>

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) The symbol of the Aldmeri Dominion During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Bosmer became part of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), along with their allies the Altmer and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). The alliance was rocky, though the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) Committee on Alliance Relations encouraged tolerance of the Bosmer's cultural eccentricities to help keep the Dominion together.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TH-51)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ASIV-47)</sup> [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) was made the capital of the Dominion, and drew many new migrants as a result (this also became the impetus for plans to establish a gladiatorial arena in the city).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-WMTM-52)</sup> Many Bosmer saw the Dominion as their only chance at survival.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-LTC-53)</sup> However, many others remained resentful of the alliance, believing the Dominion had "polluted" Valenwood.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-WCN-54)</sup> Resentment may have also festered due to the "Massacre at Cormount", wherein the Grahtwood city was apparently razed by Dominion soldiers utilizing particularly offensive tactics.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-TMAC-55)</sup>

In [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), to secure their border from attack, the Altmer, using an old treaty and encroachment from Colovia for justification, invaded Valenwood and reestablished the Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-56)</sup> Details on the circumstances leading up to this are disputed,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup> but the reformed Thalmor government apparently brought peace to Valenwood until the rise of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup> During the War of Rihad in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862), Colovian armies employed the Bosmer as archers, though they proved to be prone to desertion.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup>

### The Third Era
The Camoran monarchy, maintained by the [Septim Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) as a "figurehead", ruled over 250 years of peace in Valenwood. However, the Bosmer became increasingly alienated by Imperial neglect.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup> In [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249), a pretender to the throne, the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md), led an army of undead and mercenaries which devastated Valenwood, and most of Tamriel's western coast, until he was finally defeated in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TFOTU-57)</sup> Since then, the Bosmer have become more isolationist, traditionalist, and distrusting of central authorities, and this political weakness has led to significant losses of territory to their neighbors.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup>

The Bosmer would suffer heavily during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) (3E 389 - 3E 399), losing the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) against the Khajiit and the [War of the Blue Divide](Lore_War_of_the_Blue_Divide.md) against the Altmer. Both saw large territorial concessions and no intervention by the Empire.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ADIF7-58)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-PGE3SB-59)</sup> At the end of the Third Era, rumors surfaced that a prophet called the Precursor had emerged among the Bosmer promising new gifts from Y'ffre to reward their devotion.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup>

### The Fourth Era
Following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the Summerset Isles declared independence from the Empire. It's alleged that Thalmor agents, who had formed close ties to certain Bosmeri factions even before the Oblivion Crisis, conspired to overthrow Valenwood's government. In [4E 29](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_29), they succeeded, quickly overcoming Imperial resistance and reforming the Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TGW-60)</sup>

## Culture

### Art and Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/BL-quest-Pool_of_Despair_04.jpg/200px-BL-quest-Pool_of_Despair_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-quest-Pool_of_Despair_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-quest-Pool_of_Despair_04.jpg) A Bosmeri hunting camp in Cyrodiil (Blades) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-concept-Skull_decorations.png/200px-ON-concept-Skull_decorations.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Skull_decorations.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Skull_decorations.png) Depictions of Bosmeri skull and bone decorations The Green Pact has had an enormous impact on Bosmeri artwork, crafts and clothing. Their walls are often decorated with skulls and bones.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-CWS-61)</sup> It forces their architects to take great care in their work, apparently strengthening their craftsmanship. Inside tree hollows and residences, hides are stretched out over frames of bones to form partitions.

Traditional timber clanhouses can be found at sites scattered along the coast, where imported lumber is easy to find and cheap to purchase, and through the Valenwood interior, connected only by undeveloped foot trails.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-mwvalenwoodtopic-62)</sup> More of these types of buildings can be found on the forest floor of cities like Elden Root or Silvenar, typically to house foreigners like the Altmer, but Bosmer cities in the Green are more well-known for the many homes scattered throughout the canopy of the trees.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-AA-63)</sup>

Many "citified tree-folk" favor a life among the branches, and have woven them together to form limbed pathways without harming the trees. Trails of thick, living vines anchor dozens of platforms that carry goods and people among the graht-oaks. These platforms are hoisted by strong, often foreign laborers. Bone, resin, and sinew are employed by the Bosmer in a plethora of ways, including bridge design. A secondary market in such scraps allows the tree-dwellers to recycle their animal waste by simply tossing it to the ground below, where it is scavenged and reworked into a variety of items.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-AA-63)</sup> What is not scavenged is absorbed by the roots of the trees and used to keep the settlement healthy. These cities are grown by Bosmer [Greenspeakers](Lore_Greenspeakers.md), who use rituals of speech or song to grow entire villages in just a few days.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ONGloo-64)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-LMAGwaering-65)</sup> This magic can also be used to fashion wooden items, presumably without violating the Green Pact.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Cont W-66)</sup> Below ground, the trees share a common root system which delivers hot sap into each hut to keep them warm.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ONGloo-64)</sup>

Like the Nords, the Bosmer rely heavily on stylized, often interlocking natural motifs in their architecture, crafts, and clothing. However, while the Nords emphasize animals, Bosmeri designs are mainly floral.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-RM4TN-67)</sup> Since the Bosmer believe that a species' form is a sacred gift from Y'ffre, each species is depicted by a particular, idealized motif which represents the ur-form it was given by Y'ffre. Thus, while there a great plethora of designs in Bosmeri artwork, there is very little room for variation in these designs. Seemingly inconsequential details are rigidly scrutinized, and those which deviate from the norm are looked down upon as plain "wrong".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RM3TWE-13)</sup>

Border settlements often follow the architectural designs of other cultures.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

### Clothing and Fashion
The Bosmer have a sense of fashion that may seem strange to some of Tamriel's other inhabitants. For example, Wood Elves in the Second Era thought that shawls made of blue bunting feathers were the height of fashion, while people from most other cultures perceived them as tacky.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-68)</sup> Humor also plays a role in Wood Elf fashion, as some clothing items are simply worn as a joke.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ZVKMNHat-69)</sup> The Pod-Singers wear clothes made entirely out of animal leather and quills.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-70)</sup> On the whole, Wood Elves prefer clothing that allows their skin to breathe, and think covering oneself from head-to-toe doesn't make a whole lot of sense. Leather is a common component in Bosmer clothing: doeskin, chamois,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ESODoeskin-71)</sup> crocodile skin—if it has hide, it's free game for leather.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-72)</sup>

Their gravitation towards leather doesn't keep them away from fabric; wool is also a component in Bosmeri stitchwork.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ESOSpinner Cos-73)</sup> Felt is also used in clothing, with the Silvenar's personal guard being known to wear unique felt caps.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-ESOFelt Hat-74)</sup> Beading, turquoise, sea-beads, and even iridescent deep-forest beetle shells are common accents in Bosmer fashion, giving clothing extra flair.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-75)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ESODoeskin-71)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-76)</sup> Translucent cloths, and nothing more, are traditionally worn during ritual dances.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Transparent Shawl-77)</sup>

Cultural mixing between Bosmer and Khajiit has had an effect on fashion as well, such as unique hairstyles.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-LEBraid-78)</sup> A messy and dyed hairstyle popular in the Second Era was named after the Bosmeri drink, Rotmeth.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-RHBed-79)</sup>

### Weaponry and Armor
Due to the proscriptions of the Green Pact, forges are rare in Valenwood, and metal weapons have never been widespread among the Bosmer. They typically utilize bone clubs, or else axes and spears with blades of stone or obsidian. Their composite horn bows are arguably the finest in Tamriel. In coastal towns, imported metal weapons are more common, and Altmeri tutelage in these areas has benefited many Bosmeri swordsmen. However, Altmer who are schooled in "Proper Conflict", as they call their system of swordsmanship, typically refuse to adapt their techniques to the needs of their shorter cousins, and Bosmer students are typically less attentive to the finer philosophical points of the lessons, thus the experience has often proven to be mutually exasperating.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CAOV-28)</sup>

By the age of fourteen, Bosmer youth are typically proficient enough with the bow to accompany hunting parties.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CAOV-28)</sup> Long-distance archers are known as [Jaqspurs](Lore_Jaqspurs.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CAOV-28)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-WAP-80)</sup> It is their style of draw, "snatching and releasing in one continuous motion", which has made the Bosmer famous as archers. Their traditional bows are crafted from horn and sinew. Strings are also made of sinew; Khajiiti gut is said to work best, and is thus prized among Valenwood archers. Bosmeri arrows are carved from bone, and fletched with the feathers of various bird species. They believe the source of bone used for an arrow influences its characteristics: [Mammoth](Lore_Mammoth.md) bone arrows are thought to knock down a target, bird arrows fly faster and more accurately, and the [Senche-tiger](Lore_Senche-Tiger.md) brings extra damage. Much to the amusement of the Bosmer, outsiders have not been able to replicate these effects.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CAOV-28)</sup>

Beetle-chitin is often used in armor and weapons, as it as protective as iron at a much lighter weight. Likewise, beetle-hide is incredibly slick, making it difficult for a weapon to find purchase on armor made from it. It also isn't unheard of for these materials to be mixed with iron and steel.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-So ZB-81)</sup> Bone and antlers are also used to accent clothing and armor.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ESOJaqspur Cos-82)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ESOSpinner Cos-73)</sup>

[Darts](Lore_Throwing_Weapons.md) of Judgment are very rare and expensive Daedric projectiles fashioned by the Bosmer of Valenwood.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ATJ-83)</sup>

### Food

- *Main Article: [Bosmer Cuisine](Lore_Bosmer_Cuisine.md)*

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg/200px-ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg) A selection of meals from Valenwood The Bosmer are bound by the terms of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) to not harm the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The Bosmer do not eat anything made from plant life; fruits are only allowed if they have already fallen from the tree, and the seeds must be replanted, lest they face the wrath of the forest. As such, their recipes tend to include animal products.

The Green Pact is interpreted differently depending on the tribe—foodstuffs such as fruits that fell from the trees, dairy products, honey, mushrooms, and insects are eaten with zest by all but the most ultra-zealous of Pact Bosmer. Ritualistic cannibalism is a part of Bosmeri culture and a requirement for the upholding of the Green Pact; they traditionally eat their dead and even their enemies as part of upholding the meat mandate although the practice of cannibalism has fallen out of favor in Valenwood during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and is only practiced by the most zealous upholders of the Green.

## Society
The elves of Valenwood have always stood ready to defend themselves against aggression, and have done so on many occasions.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-MUT-97)</sup> They often rely on their forest-coupling skills to make use of guerrilla tactics. The title of their most famous poem, the Meh Ayleidion, means "The One Thousand Benefits of Hiding".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup> However, the Bosmer seem to be the most pacifistic of all the modern races on Tamriel, as they have never instigated war with other nations (leading some more militant cultures to criticize them as cowards),<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-FWOB-98)</sup> though they have been accused of needlessly escalating [some conflicts](Lore_Five_Year_War.md). They have never found a tried and true ally in any other Tamrielic nation. Mistrust has been engendered by the ritual cannibalism they practice, and it's often difficult for them to adjust their lifestyles to the expectations of other races and cultures. Nevertheless, the Bosmer of Valenwood are heavily dependent on good trade relations with others, particularly for timber.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POT-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ADIF2-90)</sup> Their own resources for trade include hides, river pearls, and finger-bone charms made from the magically-charged hands of their dead wizards. When they are at odds with Colovia or other trading allies, they are said to rely on piracy or banditry to get what they need.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup>

Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Wood_Elf_Deadeye.png) Bosmer victim of necromancy The Bosmer greatly value diversity. Other races are *generally* welcomed in most of Valenwood, though most foreigners stay in large cities.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-AMGTV-99)</sup> Travelers are encouraged to take care when imbibing drinks of fermented milk which are popular among the Bosmer, as non-Bosmer may have adverse reactions to them.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ADIF-100)</sup><sup>[[99]](#cite_note-FTNOACC-101)</sup> Their respect for life's many facets is inherent in many of their old sayings, like "One mans miracle is another mans accident".<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-AOOGAW-102)</sup> Conversely, their general animosity towards necromancy is said to be extraordinary,<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-CP-103)</sup> though this animosity for necromancy may be exaggerated, as many individuals show little concern over the reanimation of the dead.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-BOR-104)</sup> They have attempted to accommodate other races in Valenwood throughout history, though many remained wary of outlander encroachments into their home.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-OBRTTD-105)</sup> To this day, they have little more than foot paths connecting their tiny settlements in Valenwood's sparsely populated forests.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POT-3)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-FTJOAHES-106)</sup>

### Life in Valenwood
Though the Bosmer first united under the Camoran Dynasty, this form of governance has been called a "loose hegemony".<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TWEOV-107)</sup> There are in fact many leaders throughout Valenwood who wield varying degrees of authority, such as the treethanes.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TWEOV-107)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-DDTTH-108)</sup> Their territory has sometimes been referred to as the "Valenwood Nations",<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup> perhaps because of the autonomy practiced by various leaders.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TWEOV-107)</sup> At the clan-level, Bosmeri society is matrilineal.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TWEOV-107)</sup> Greenspeakers, mages who dedicate their lives to serving the Green, use their spells to guide and enhance the growth of trees and plants, and in so doing, create furniture and housing for their people.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ESODorinlas-109)</sup>

The Bosmer have adapted to life in the Green in many ingenious ways. For instance, in the heat and humidity of Valenwood, popular Altmeri ink refuses to dry. It is still prized, though, as it is perfect for writing temporary messages on certain types of porous stone found in the Green. But to write words permanently, the Bosmer uses a type of ink that can dry even if a parchment is submerged in water. Ink which glows faintly in the dark, perfect for writing in the shadows of the forest, is also prevalent. Writing on a leaf or a flat stone is common in Valenwood, and often preferable in a place where quality parchment is rare and even paper is uncommon.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-TC7-110)</sup> Green Pact typically does not allow the use of paper, which makes vellum made of animal hides more common in Valenwood.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Zuzik-111)</sup> They've also found means to both deter and eradicate [Hoarvor](Lore_Hoarvor.md) infestations.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-HF-112)</sup> Though they refrain from smoking plants, many enjoy smoking bugs from bone pipes.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RM3TWE-13)</sup> Finding light without resorting to the danger of fire is an issue on the perpetually shadowy forest floor, but as the moons rise, additional light is provided by luminous lichen, molds, and fungal growths at the base of the oaks. Higher up, nocturnal flowers feed from the graht-oaks, attracting [Torchbugs](Lore_Torchbug.md), whose hives light the branch platforms.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-AA-63)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/LO-race-Imga.jpg/200px-LO-race-Imga.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Imga.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Imga.jpg) A sketch of an Imga, the Great Apes of Valenwood The Bosmer share Valenwood with the [Imga](Lore_Imga.md) and so-called "Wood [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md)", along with the many beasts of the forest, including [Centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-21)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-CAEWO1-113)</sup> They have a long history of welcoming those who seek refuge within Valenwood.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VAS-6)</sup> They accepted slaves fleeing the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), and then Ayleids fleeing the slaves, without showing favorites. Around the time of the Three Banners War, they also freely welcomed [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) refugees who had left their homeland in protest over the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-ARD-114)</sup> Many foreigners visit [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) at some point, the marvelous traveling city of the Bosmer (though at last report, the city had mysteriously rooted itself).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3V-41)</sup> Cultural loyalties have tended to divide the Bosmer, such as the rivalry between the Blacksap and the Camorans, and the schism between followers of the [Green Pact](#The_Green_Pact) and "salad-eaters" who have no qualms about breaking the tenets of the Pact.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-OBRTTD-105)</sup>

### Laws and Traditions
The Rite of Theft is a cherished Bosmeri custom, though one which outsiders have trouble appreciating.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-TGPATD-115)</sup> The Bosmer steal from each other, and upon return of the stolen item, they demand a boon commensurate to the item's worth.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-HOTS-116)</sup> In ancient times, the Bosmer Vanirion the Thief was renowned for demanding over two hundred payments after invoking the Rite.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOTGPB-117)</sup> In more modern times, the Bosmer of Greenshade are particularly infamous for their thievery.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-AMGTV-99)</sup> Occasionally, some Bosmer have been known to observe the Rite of Theft even from beyond the grave, such was the case with the Blackroot Clan when their chief's bow was stolen. <sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ESORo T-118)</sup>

Outsiders who understand the Rite of Theft may use it to their own advantage as well. Three Altmer, the so-called "Heroes of the Sanctuary", once used the Rite of Theft to secure a safe port of Summerset ships. They successfully stole from various treethanes while keeping their own treasure secure, earning the right to build their own settlement, known as [Seaside Sanctuary](Lore_Seaside_Sanctuary.md), on the shores of Valenwood.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-HOTS-116)</sup>

Often when making contracts with the tribes of Valenwood, one will be expected to put up some sort of collateral to ensure equal treatment between unequal partners, such as when a mundane individual is dealing with a powerful mage. In traditional Bosmeri villages, this involves the more powerful party giving the weaker a loved one as a 'pact-hostage'. Should the contracted party complete their task, the pact-hostage will be returned. If they fail to uphold their end of the contract, the pact-hostage will be killed, cooked, and served up to them as Unthrappa, or Atonement. Eating the Unthrappa represents eating one's failure.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ESORanneth-94)</sup>

Nearly everywhere outside the cities of Valenwood, Bosmeri tribes in the wilds of Valenwood still practice the tradition of the "Mourning War". When a tribe member is slain, they are symbolically replaced via a hostage-taking raid on a neighboring tribe. If the deceased was an especially powerful or prestigious member of the tribe, multiple captives may be taken to replace them. The captive or captives undergo a period of physical torture, supposedly to test their worthiness, and then are joyously welcomed into the clan. Traditionally, the victim was given the deceased tribe member's position, possessions, and family, though this practice may be rarely honored in modern ages.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-WCOTTB-119)</sup> Mourning wars are an ancient tradition; scholarly references to them date back to the First Era,<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOTGPB-117)</sup> though when they actually started is not known.

The Rite of Propagation is an ancient rite invoked by villages to prevent stagnation against the Green.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-SBVOTV-120)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-VOTV-121)</sup>

## Religion
*Heavy-bearded Y'ffre, speak through me. Tell us of the time before time. Let the story grow in me. Let my heart echo to the pounding of your feet along the story-lines, the bones of the world. I will walk Your steps, and know Your story.*
— *[The Green Singing](Lore_The_Green_Singing.md)* The Bosmer venerate many other deities, from [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) to [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to other spirits unique to Valenwood.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-GTEV-129)</sup> In fact, few Bosmer outside the Empire accept the limitation of [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) to a mere eight or nine. Most Bosmer claim direct descent from [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), though Y'ffre the Storyteller is their most important deity. They venerate Y'ffre as the spirit of "the now".<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-VOFTWE-130)</sup> Like the Argonians, the Bosmer seem to prefer to live in the present, or the "Aurbic Now", showing relatively little regard for the ways of the past (besides when it comes to telling old tales or following the Green Pact, that is).<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-RM11AE-131)</sup>

#### [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png/200px-ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png) A possible depiction of Y'ffre (ESO) **Y'ffre** is the **God of Song and Forest** and is the chief god of the Bosmer people. They believe that Y'ffre's first tale was that of the world, giving shape to the primordial [Ooze](Lore_Ooze.md). Only after did the second tale come, that of the Bosmer people, and with it the Green Pact.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TOAF-37)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-VOFTWE-130)</sup> Ehlnofey, or "Earthbones," were created from Y'ffre's corporeal form to establish safety and the laws of nature. These laws took the shape of stories, and there is great debate between tribes competing to interpret Y'ffre's ways and knowledge of the times of chaos, before Y'ffre arrived on the mortal plane. This way, they became a vital part of Bosmeri religion to such an extent that the [Spinners](Lore_Spinner.md), priests of Y'ffre, are considered to hold real power in Valenwood, above treethanes and kings.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-GTEV-129)</sup> The Wood Elves regularly leave a variety of offerings at Y'ffre's temples, from beverages to gems to living animals.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-ROWO-132)</sup> Treethanes typically wear sashes adorned with thunderbug splints on Y'ffre's Naming Day.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-TThane Sash-133)</sup> Many religious decorations, symbols and fetishes are made in Y'ffre's name.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-FCWBowl-134)</sup><sup>[[133]](#cite_note-YFPDice-135)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-BHorker Tusk-136)</sup><sup>[[135]](#cite_note-HSYfrre-137)</sup><sup>[[136]](#cite_note-YCGuardian-138)</sup>

#### [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md)
**Auri-El** is a little worshipped god by the Bosmer. While the Wood Elves do claim descent from Auri-El, they have little affection for the god. At most, offerings and prayers are done at the start of the year, to make up for neglected charity work during months prior.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-GTEV-129)</sup>

#### [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md)
**Arkay** is the god of burials, funeral rites and is associated with the seasons. The Bosmer invoke him when solving offenses against the Green Pact.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-GTEV-129)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-VOFTWE-130)</sup>

### The Green Pact
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Bosmer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Bosmer.png) The symbol of the Green Pact The [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), also known as the **Treaty of Frond and Leaf**, is a strict code upheld by many of the Bosmer of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to preserve [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md). It is said to have guided their existence since the beginning of the "great story". Its rules are clear. Do not harm the forests of Valenwood. Do not eat anything made from plant life. Eat only meat. When enemies are conquered, their meat must be eaten, not left to rot. Do not kill wastefully. Do not take on the shape of beasts.

Green Pact Bosmer are said to attack any who would harm the forest in which they live, which they call the Green. However, they usually don't mind other races cutting trees down (most notably in the case of [Vinedusk Rangers](Lore_Vinedusk_Rangers.md) and [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md) in their respective territories), and are known to buy wood from them. The cannibalistic "Meat Mandate" of the Pact sets them apart religiously from other cultures. The requirement to eat fallen enemies before three days pass is perhaps the most shocking part of the Pact to outsiders. Though many Bosmer still try to respect the Green Pact, the practice of eating dead enemies diminished by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and is typically only practiced in the most remote villages. The family members of the warrior who slaughtered the enemy may help him with his meal. Bosmer who honor this facet of the Green Pact typically attempt to prepare themselves before large battles by fasting.

Despite its perceived simplicity the Green Pact's doctrinal details can be baffling to outsiders, its certain aspects are often matters of dispute among the Bosmer. According to the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md), the tenet "eat no plants" would be better rephrased as "harm no living plants". Picking up and consuming fallen fruits is not considered breach of the Green Pact. Dairy products, honey, mushrooms, and insects are generally accepted meals in the Valenwood, and eating them is not considered breach of the Green Pact by all but the most ultra-zealous of Pact Bosmer. Plants that are grown outside of Valenwood, but from seeds native to Valenwood are considered sacred and cannot be harmed.

In return for protecting Valenwood from harm, [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) allowed the Bosmer to make Valenwood their home and granted them his patronage and protection. As a result, they use bone, animal, and insect products widely; imported wood is used when necessary. Rather than drinking conventional liquor made from grain or fruit, the Bosmer consume [jagga](Lore_Jagga.md) (fermented [pig](Lore_Pig.md)'s milk) and [rotmeth](Lore_Rotmeth.md). Rotmeth is made from fermented meat seasoned with thunderbug organs, takes years to brew, and is considered a precious ritual beverage that symbolizes "the muscle of the boar, the power of the forest, the strength of the Bosmer people". No wedding can take place without it. Y'ffre is believed to have first blessed them with their form, thus their appearance is sacred, so although Bosmer may take the shape of beasts, the Pact normally bans this practice. However, the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) is considered a result of the Green Pact: in return for their patronage to Y'ffre, the Bosmer may perform a ritual in dire times that transforms the participants into a horde of wild, shape-shifting beasts, which go on to lay waste to the enemies of the Bosmer.

The Green Pact made the felling of Valenwood trees by anyone a crime against the Bosmeri religion, though this does not apply to non-Bosmer. There are almost no cities or towns built by the Bosmer themselves. Rather, the Bosmer's relationship with the Pact grants them magical forest-shaping abilities to *grow* their settlements. The exact process is unclear, but reports claim that it's often a highly ritualized affair which consists of slow, rhythmic chanting for several days. Much like the Meat Mandate, fasting is possibly involved prior to and during the process. Bosmer also believe Y'ffre has blessed them with the limited ability to ask the Green to shape itself to their needs to help them live within the restrictions of the Pact. Some Bosmer during the Second Era refused to follow the Green Pact, as they considered it hypocritical for it to not apply to non-Bosmer and because the Pact was made long before they themselves were born. Despite the fact that Green-Pact did not apply to foreigners, some Bosmer still considered foreigners harming plants as blasphemous crime against the nature. [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) was allowed to settle in Valenwood on the condition of adopting the Green Pact. Having little choice, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) agreed to adopt aspects of the Green Pact and refrained from harming the forest, which probably contributed to the dilution of their culture.

The tenets of the Pact have led to many tensions with allies and others who have disrespected their beliefs. More than once, the Bosmer have gone to war to protect the Green. While many "civilized" Bosmer don't adhere as closely to the Green Pact as their tribal brethren, they nevertheless often seem to be staunch advocates for respecting its tenets. Green Pact Bosmer are fanatical in their devotion to the Pact. They have been known to let loved ones die of illness even as the alchemical ingredients which could save them grow nearby. Nevertheless, these forest-dwellers are not savages: they have been described as a highly intelligent, curious, and rational people with rich cultural traditions vested in the forest and in their spiritual leaders, the [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) of Y'ffre. The Spinners are charged with enforcing the Green Pact, and are said to hold the real power in Valenwood. Accounts suggest they have great influence on the people, and that their sermons touch upon more than just spiritual matters. Every springtime, Green Pact Bosmer gather at the Den of the Eldest for a festival in which they drink, tell stories, and pay homage to [the Eldest](Lore_The_Eldest.md), an ancient strangler.

Placating the Green is a vital part of life in Valenwood, as the forests will often turn against the residents when the Pact is broken or the Valenwood is otherwise harmed. In order to attone and stop the Green's assault, the clan responsible for that area of land must hold a trial and the party found guilty of violating the Green Pact must be sacrificed to the Green itself, regardless of how limited their involvement was or even whether they were a Bosmer. Three kinds of proof are required for judgment: witness testimony, physical evidence, and a magical omen.

The Green Pact Bosmer are known as fierce warriors, but are also known for the hospitality they exhibit to outsiders who respect their ways. Their stories speak of the [Ooze](Lore_Ooze.md), a purgatorial state reserved for Bosmer who violate the Pact (who are called Apostates), as well as the harsh punishments the Green unleashes on those who would harm it.

### The Ooze
The **Ooze** is a Bosmeri term that refers to the formless chaos that existed prior to [Y'ffre's](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) Naming. They believe that, during the chaos of the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) there was a state of existence where all life and land on Nirn were formless and shifted constantly between shapes, unable to hold one form for more than a moment, acknowledged also in [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) myth. It was Y'ffre who granted all things their Names and gave them an enduring form. According to legend, Y'ffre would shape the Ooze into all of creation by telling their stories. The Bosmer thus believe that after they made the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) with Y'ffre, he taught them how to escape the chaos and retain their form, but also how to return to it by instigating the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md). Returning to the chaotic shifting of Ooze can also be done without the need of collective ritual, though that act is deemed a breach of the Green Pact and punished as such. Even after creating the [Green](Lore_The_Green.md), the Bosmer, and the rest of life, though, some formless nothing still remained. Y'ffre decided to speak one final story to give the Ooze purpose, saying that any being who violated their Green Pact with the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) would be cast into the Ooze and stricken from the story of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

Most Bosmer recognize the Ooze to be a sort of 'spiritual prison' for Oathbreakers who disobeyed some aspect of the Green Pact. Among such Oathbreakers are [Changelings](Lore_Changeling.md), shapeshifting [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md) that were once prevalent in Valenwood. When Y'ffre introduced the Green Pact many of them gave up their formlessness to become Bosmer, with those who refused it becoming known as **Oathbreakers** and being hunted down to be interred in the tarpits of [Ouze](Lore_Ouze.md). These spirits view Y'ffre's imposition of name and shape and of the Green Pact as a form of imprisonment, which they refused as they had no wish to give up their shapeshifting abilities. These individuals would be shunned by Y'ffre, who represents the living moment, and their spirits would be tied to their remains for eternity. These Oathbreakers could only play witness to all the transpires on Nirn and beyond, and were unable to participate or interact with Nirn in any meaningful way, stuck in a purgatorial state for all time. The rules that dictate whether someone will be trapped in the Ooze upon death are harsh, but clear. The moment someone breaks the Green Pact or otherwise displeases Y'ffre, no matter how inconsequential or accidental their crime, their soul will be bound for the Ooze. This fate can even be extended to outside intruders in the Valenwood under certain circumstances. Those trapped in the Ooze, however, are not without help. [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) have been known to communicate with the remains of those who rest in the Ooze and even free them if they so choose. Additionally, Bosmer in the Ooze are still bound to Bosmeri customs, and invoking one such as the Rite of Theft can grant complete control over the Oathbreakers, allowing even for someone to command them to be freed from the Ooze.

### The Silvenar and Green Lady
[The Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) is the Voice of the People for the Bosmer. While often viewed by outsiders as a title for the Bosmer's political representative for foreign affairs, the Silvenar is more than a mere politician. Each person who bears the title is thought of as merely the Silvenar's aspect. The Silvenar represents the Bosmer people legally, physically, and emotionally. He is said to be their spirituality, in contrast to [the Green Lady](Lore_The_Green_Lady.md), who is their physicality. It is said to be the union of the two that makes Valenwood whole. He reflects the state of the Bosmer people in his way of thinking, his health, and even his gender. He is inseparably tied to the Bosmeri people, and they to him, making him supernaturally empathic and aware of their concerns. They also believe that he looks after their spirits, as well as their bodies. As the Voice of the People, the Silvenar has many rights which grant him great authority in Valenwood. For one, only the Silvenar can grant foreign building and trade contracts; any foreign power that wishes to build in Valenwood or do business with the Bosmer must deal with the Silvenar first.

How the Silvenar and, by extension, Green Lady, came to be is shrouded in mystery. Some folktales claim that the first Silvenar and Green Lady were pilgrims, a childless couple who tended to [the Eldest](Lore_The_Eldest.md) like their own offspring. The Green Lady, however, insists that her memories date all the way back to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).

As the Silvenar exists primarily as a story, his only true origin is the tale of his meeting with the Green Lady. In the tale, the Silvenar begins as a being who can see the whole of Valenwood and is left saddened and empty by its purely primal nature. He eventually comes across a Bosmer unlike any he had seen before, whose raw strength and passion made all before him terrified. She begins to attack him, but he instead tells her to stay with him and see the beauty of the forest. Eventually, The Green Lady comes to see the Valenwood as the Silvenar does, while the Silvenar's emptiness became filled with her passion. The two became bound to one another, both taming the wilds and strengthening the Green in the process..

This story, in addition to functioning as the beginning of the Silvenar, also serves as the Silvenar's now and future. Each time a Silvenar dies, a new one is selected by the Green and will once again repeat this tale to unite with their Green Lady. As such, the Silvenar is a constant in Bosmeri society.

While the Silvenar represents the civilized nature of the current Bosmer, the Green Lady has the raw physicality and passion of their primal past. If the Silvenar dies, his Green Lady's bestial nature is irrevocably unleashed, and her fury turned on all those involved until she joins him in death. Upon the death of the Silvenar, the new Silvenar officially takes on the title at a wedding. This wedding, which is known as the Handfasting, is an important occasion for the Bosmer which only occurs once in a generation. It is responsible for both anointing and marrying the Silvenar and the Green Lady. Even before the Handfasting, the Silvenar may feel the new identity taking over. For the Silvenar doesn't just represent the will of the Bosmer to outsiders. The Silvenar actually feels the will of the people and acts upon it, and the people likewise can feel the Silvenar's influence and be swayed by it.

### The Wild Hunt
*It was as if a crack in reality had opened wide. A flood of horrific beasts, tentacled toads, insects of armor and spine, gelatinous serpents, vaporous beings with the face of gods, all poured forth from the great hollow tree, blind with fury. They tore the Khajiiti in front of the temple to pieces. All the other cats fled for the jungle, but as they did so, they began pulling on the ropes they carried. In a few seconds time, the entire village of Vindisi was boiling with the lunatic apparitions of the Wild Hunt.*
— *[A Dance in Fire, Chapter 4](Lore_A_Dance_in_Fire,_Chapter_4.md)* The [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md), also called the **Great Hunt**,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> (not to be confused with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s [Wild Hunt/Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md)) is a magical trait innate to all Bosmer. It is brought on by a collective ritual performed by many Bosmer, causing them to shapeshift into a horde of feral, eldritch beasts. This transformation cannot be undone. These monsters stampede, changing their form constantly, slaying and devouring all in their path, devouring the flesh of those in their way to the bone in seconds, and eventually, when left with no surviving targets, turn upon themselves in a "cannibalistic orgy". This transformation is seen by the Bosmer as a return to the chaos of the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) they call the [Ooze](Lore_Ooze.md), a state of existence where all life and land on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) were formless and shifted constantly between shapes, unable to hold one form for more than a moment, acknowledged also in [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) myth. It was [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) who granted all things their Names and gave them an enduring form. The Bosmer thus believe that after they made the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) with [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), he taught them how to escape that chaos and retain their form, but also how to return to it by instigating the Wild Hunt. As all life started as part of the shifting chaos of Ooze and had their shapes taught to them by Y'ffre, getting caught up in the vicinity of a Wild Hunt ritual can also lead to non-Bosmer forgetting their taught shapes, the bonds of the Name bestowed upon them unraveled, and transform into Wild Hunt creatures.

Some sources claim that the origins of the Wild Hunt might lie with the emergent elves' observation of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) during primordial times. It is said that, while the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) would learn how to defy the Earthbones by observing the Ehlnofey controlling the natural laws governing things such as decay and the [passage between](Lore_Portals.md) [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) clans from which the Bosmer would eventually emerge would witness the passing of Y'ffre, who was the first of the Ehlnofey to give of themselves to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) fully and become an Earthbone, learning how to defy the Earthbone he established to govern the physical form of things through observing him. It is said that this is the knowledge modern Bosmer invoke to enact a Wild Hunt, reverting to what life truly is beneath Y'ffre's imposed law. The reason for the seemingly undying nature of such Hunt creatures is said to be that in severing themselves from the control of one Earthbone, others also lose their power to impose their own laws on them, thus leading to the Earthbone governing lifespan also losing its power.

Shapeshifters of the Wild Hunt are stripped of their flesh from their bone instantaneously, and turn into creatures that "defy all attempts to identify them". Some may not even turn into creatures at all, and instead change into "great writhing tongues, unfettered by lips or teeth", or can take on a gaseous form which makes them impossible to contain without the aid of [magic](Lore_Magic.md). There's even some suggestion they can take on the form of water, or waves of blood. The forms they take are not static, however, as they may appear like a serpent one moment, and shift into another creature the next moment. When Bosmer invoke the Wild Hunt, it causes a change in wild animals as well. Beasts typically transform into beings that are part plant and part animal.

The purpose of the Wild Hunt varies with each separate Hunt, from an act of vengeance to a tool of war. In either case, the Bosmer are not proud of their ability and are unwilling to discuss the Hunt at great length. One of the rare accounts on the nature of the Wild Hunt states that "every monster in the world that has ever been comes from a previous Hunt". As such, the Bosmer are reluctant to use the Hunt, except as an act of desperation; the change is said to be irreversible.. The monsters that do get created by a Wild Hunt, all former Bosmer, are said to be ferocious and long-lived. Notable examples included [Willy the Bitten](Lore_Willy_the_Bitten.md) and [King Dead Wolf-Deer](Lore_Dead_Wolf-Deer.md), who were created by the end of the [fourth century of the First Era](Lore_First_Era.md#Fourth_Century) and lived at least until the beginning of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). They went on to plague [Silvenar Grove](Lore_Silvenar_Vale.md) and the [Lynpar March](Lore_Lynpar_March.md) respectively. There was also a [Hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) monstrosity captured by the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) during the Wild Hunt started to kill High King Borgas in 1E 369 in Skyrim.

#### [Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Helm_of_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md)
The [Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Helm_of_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md), also known as the **Helm of Kharag gro-Khar**, is a prized [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) with a history of conflicted attribution. The helm itself is an engraved skull, which is enchanted to improve the wearer's agility and endurance.

Those that claim it is a Bosmeri artifact know it by the former name, and attribute it to the legendary [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) hunter of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md) of the [Oreyn bloodline](Lore_Oreyn.md). Legends claim that Bearclaw single-handedly killed Glenhwyfaunva, the witch-serpent of the Elven wood, and thus brought peace to his clan. He would bring the helm and his name further reverence by performing many more great deeds, until he lost his life to the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md). After Bearclaw's demise, his helm stood as a monument of his stature, although it was eventually lost after his clan split.

Those that call the helm by its latter name believe that Oreyn was falsely credited, and that the feats were actually performed by his [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) friend, Kharag gro-Khar, hero of the [Shatul Clan](Lore_Shatul_Clan.md). Thus they consider it an Orcish artifact, and the relic has been claimed by [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) himself. Those that believe the former story however, state that Kharag stole both Oreyn's helm and reputation, and thus both camps have tried to claim it.

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_the_Wild_Hunt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_the_Wild_Hunt.png) Ring of the Wild Hunt
#### [Ring of the Wild Hunt](Lore_Ring_of_the_Wild_Hunt.md)
The [Ring of the Wild Hunt](Lore_Ring_of_the_Wild_Hunt.md) is an ancient ring which plagues the wearer's dreams with frightful images of hunters and prey. In exchange, it blesses the wearer with [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)'s swiftness, increasing the speed of whomever possesses it.

## Notes
- [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) often prefers to hire Bosmer and [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) as mercenaries to ensure they are left in peace.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Dialogue Races-165)</sup>
- The term "[Wood Elf](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundering_of_the_Elves#Nandor)" originates in the works of [J. R. R. Tolkien](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien)'s [legendarium](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tolkien%27s_legendarium).
- The word 'Bos' is Dutch for 'woods' or 'forest'.
- Early *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)* concept art depicted Bosmer women as beautiful [Swanmays](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swan_maiden) with black eyes, and the men as ugly Woodland [Imps](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imp) and Forest [Goblins](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goblin). The concept art showed the men were originally going to have several subtypes, those being [Satyrs](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satyr), [Redcaps](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redcap), Boarfolk, Tree- [trolls](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troll), and [Boggles](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bogle).<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-MWConcept Art-166)</sup>
- [^](#note_nb1) The in-game description in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* characterized the Bosmer as tall.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Bos App Dag-167)</sup>
