[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-place-Hakoshae.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hakoshae.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hakoshae.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hakoshae.jpg) Hakoshae Lein-Barduik was a former bodyguard of an heir of [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md), Proconsul [Beloren-Kaie](Lore_Beloren-Kaie.md), and the founder and first magnate of the settlement of [Hakoshae](Lore_Hakoshae.md). He was the ancestor of Magnate Feina-Darak, who governed Hakoshae as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DOTP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg) Proconsul Beloren-Kaie Little is known of the past of Lein-Barduik except that he served as the personal bodyguard of Proconsul Beloren-Kaie, an heir of [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> After the death of Savirien-Chorak in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBOD-3)</sup> or [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1ETWR-4)</sup> Beloren-Kaie fled [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), with Lein-Barduik serving as his only protector.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> When Beloren-Kaie was attacked by a [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) assassin named Methas Daram, Lein-Barduik fled, leading to the Proconsul being killed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ashur-5)</sup>

After Beloren-Kaie's death, Lein-Barduik looted his corpse and assumed his identity.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> Lein-Barduik fled with other Akaviri who sought refuge in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) during the brief reign of Emperor [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md), who expelled the Akaviri from Cyrodiil to rid the province of the foreigners who had ruled it during the first half of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ashur-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1E-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OABR-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTS-8)</sup> The Akaviri eventually took refuge in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), the northern region of Elsweyr, where the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) granted them asylum.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1E-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OABR-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTS-8)</sup> Under the guise of Beloren-Kaie, he founded the town of Hakoshae south of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) in the [Rim Territories](Lore_Rim_Territories.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MFD-9)</sup>

Shortly after Hakoshae's founding, Lein-Barduik held the town's first [Proving Festival](Lore_Proving_Festival.md), a festival where the people prove themselves to their ancestors.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TPF-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MFD-9)</sup> The ancient Akaviri rites invoked during the final stage of the festival allow ancestor spirits to enter the realm of the living and judge their descendants directly.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> During the first Proving Festival, Lein-Barduik summoned Beloren-Kaie, who attempted to kill him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> In response, Lein-Barduik cast him back into the realm of the dead.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> Summoning Beloren-Kaie caused Lein-Barduik to cancel future Proving Festivals, and it was eventually forgotten by the people of Hakoshae.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TPF-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MFD-9)</sup>

Lein-Barduik later passed away, and his descendants continued to rule Hakoshae.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-2)</sup> He was interred in the Hakoshae Tombs.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HT-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SOTF-12)</sup>

## Legacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/93/ON-load-Hakoshae_Tombs.jpg/200px-ON-load-Hakoshae_Tombs.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hakoshae_Tombs.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hakoshae_Tombs.jpg) Hakoshae Tombs, Lein-Barduik's place of rest [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-npc-Magnate_Feina-Darak.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Magnate_Feina-Darak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Magnate_Feina-Darak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Magnate_Feina-Darak.jpg) Magnate Feina-Darak Roughly a century after Lein-Barduik's time, Hakoshae was governed by his descendant, Magnate Feina-Darak.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MFD-9)</sup> She and her ancestors believed that they were descendants of Beloren-Kaie; however, this was mostly kept secret due to the threat of the Morag Tong.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LTFD-13)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Feina-Darak found a text referencing the Proving Festival and held the town's first festival since Lein-Barduik's time.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DOTP-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MFD-9)</sup>

During the festival, a grandson of Methas Daram, Ashur, arrived in Hakoshae to investigate a rumor that one of Savirien-Chorak's heirs had survived. Enlisting the help of a [traveler](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the two investigated Hakoshae, while the latter partook in the Proving Festival. The investigation eventually led them to the Hakoshae Tombs after Feina-Darak was taken by the spirit of Beloren-Kaie, whom she had summoned during the festival. In the tombs, they encountered the spirit of Lein-Barduik, who unsuccessfully attempted to stop Ashur and the traveler from learning the truth. After defeating the spirit of Lein-Barduik, they spoke with Beloren-Kaie's spirit, who revealed the truth behind the town's founding. Ultimately, Feina-Darak was spared, announced her true heritage, and attempted to renounce her title of magnate. The inhabitants of Hakoshae, however, persuaded her to continue as magnate because of her leadership skills, regardless of her heritage. The festival continued, Ashur left Hakoshae knowing that his grandfather had succeeded in killing Beloren-Kaie, and the truth behind Lein-Barduik's treachery was revealed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DOTP-1)</sup>
