# Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai


Critics of [Emperor Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md)'s acquiescence to the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) that ended the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) five years earlier took the Redguards' successful resistance as proof that the Aldmeri Dominion and the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) could have been defeated. However, although the White-Gold Concordat's terms were considered harsh, some accounts maintain that the Imperial armies were in no condition to fight on, and that prolonging the conflict against the Aldmeri Dominion would likely have ended in Imperial defeat.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Hammerfell's relations with the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) remained strained after the treaty despite their mutual opposition to the Aldmeri Dominion. The Redguards believed that they had been betrayed when Titus II first agreed to the White-Gold Concordat, which stipulated that a significant portion of Hammerfell be ceded to the Dominion, then renounced Hammerfell as an Imperial province when it protested the Concordat's terms. Following the Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai, Hammerfell was left an independent yet severely diminished nation.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> A secretive Hammerfell organization known as the [Remnants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remnants) was tasked with the responsibility of making sure the Aldmeri Dominion held up their end of the treaty, and had agents secretly looking into potential treaty violations.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FI-7)</sup>
