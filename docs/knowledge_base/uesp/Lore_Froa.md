*Arrangements having been made, Ylgar set forth to the academies of honored soldiers, seeking out his most trusted friends and advisors to join him on the venture of the Return. By now the stories of the new land to the south were spreading before him, and the mere emergence of his presence was enough to cause the finest warriors to lay down their present undertakings and follow him.
So was he able to call to his side the great Shield-Sisters, Froa and Grosta (who thought and spoke as one), and they brought with them the wise war-teacher Adrimk, who first taught them to dance among the blades.*
— *[Songs of the Return](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR-1)</sup> Froa was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior and a member of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Along with her sister [Grosta](Lore_Grosta.md), they were known as the **great Shield-Sisters**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR-1)</sup> They were credited with the creation of the [Woeful Bowstring](Lore_Woeful_Bowstring.md), an essential part of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s great bow, the [Long-Launcher](Lore_Long-Launcher.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FAHM-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) Ysgramor, the wielder of the Long-Launcher [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg) Ylgar, the captain of Darumzu *[Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus](Lore_Fragmentae_Abyssum_Hermaeus_Morus.md)*, a legendary account of Ysgramor's deeds from the frozen continent of Atmora, speaks of Froa. According to the text, Ysgramor gathered the laments of the [Giant](Lore_Giant.md) -Wives and brought them to Froa and Grosta. Together, the Shield-Sisters twisted the laments into the Woeful Bowstring, enabling Ysgramor to re-string his mighty bow, the Long-Launcher.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FAHM-2)</sup> The bow served Ysgramor well, as he wielded it during his legendary hunt of the White [Stag](Lore_Stag.md) of Forelgrim,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FAHM-2)</sup> the great Snow- [Goose](Lore_Goose.md) Faldrosta,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOOTO-3)</sup> and in his battle against the ancient [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) wizard named [Serenarth](Lore_Serenarth_(Snow_Elf).md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-4)</sup>

[Volume two](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return,_Vol_2.md) of the *[Songs of the Return](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return.md)* also recounts the sisters. According to the text, Froa and Grosta were so close that they thought and spoke as one. They studied under the great warrior and wise war-teacher [Adrimk](Lore_Adrimk.md), who first taught them to dance among the blades. Adrimk was a legendary warrior in her own right, shaping the Shield-Sisters into great fighters. [Ylgar](Lore_Ylgar.md), son of Ysgramor, set forth to the academies of honored soldiers, seeking his most trusted friends and advisors to join him on the venture of [the Return](Lore_The_Return.md). In this way, he was able to gather many loyal allies, including Froa and Grosta, their teacher, and numerous other students of hers. Together, they manned [Darumzu](Lore_Darumzu.md), one of the grand Atmoran ships, and successfully landed on the mainland of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR-1)</sup>

[Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) women continued to bear the name Froa as late as [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), though it is uncertain whether this was in honor of the legendary Froa herself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Iddra-5)</sup>
