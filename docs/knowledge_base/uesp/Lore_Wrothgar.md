[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-place-Grudge-Rock_Falls.jpg/200px-ON-place-Grudge-Rock_Falls.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grudge-Rock_Falls.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grudge-Rock_Falls.jpg) Wrothgarian Mountains The Wrothgarian Mountains, also known as **Wrothgar**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHi O-1)</sup> or the **Wrothgar Mountains**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARGS-2)</sup> are a large region in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located at the northeast corner of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). The region is inhabited mostly by [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md), and the region was once inhabited by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). [Riekr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riekr), the ice goblins, make their home in the Wrothgar Mountains as well.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBa R-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Dwemer Ruins-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3E-5)</sup> A portal to [Maelstrom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maelstrom) can be found within the foothills of the Wrothgarian Mountains.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LFa TRa YQ-6)</sup> The main vampire bloodline found here is the [Lyrezi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyrezi).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BLl-7)</sup>

It is bordered to the west by [Phrygias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phrygias), [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen), [Kambria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kambria), [Koegria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Koegria), and [Alcaire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcaire). The mountains are also bordered by [Menevia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Menevia), [Orsinium Area](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orsinium_Area), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), and [Gavaudon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gavaudon) to the south. The regional seat is the Orcish Kingdom of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OGTB-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-9)</sup> and in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Breton city of [Wrothgaria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgaria) became the regional seat of the Wrothgarian Mountains. After the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Wrothgaria became part of the kingdom of Orsinium.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Wb Co WM-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWit W-11)</sup>

Minerals and ores of all types fill Wrothgar's mountains, and metals such as [Gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold), [silver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver), and [iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron) can all be found in great quantities. There are also rare metals that can only be found in Wrothgar and nowhere else on Tamriel, such as the elusive [liquid silver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Liquid_Silver).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LS-12)</sup>

## History
In the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) there were 13 Orc strongholds in the Wrothgarian Mountains.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MOC-13)</sup>

In the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) the city of [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md) would be built within the Wrothgarian mountains, its ruler was Lord Storig, who commanded the vanguard of the [Imperial army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) at the Battle of Bodrum in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FSV3-14)</sup>

The Wrothgarian Mountains are best known for being home to the Orcish capital of Orsinium. It was razed by [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) and the [Order of Diagna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Diagna) in [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980), and at the time of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), it briefly became an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) territory.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-WJot B-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3O-16)</sup> At some point, Wrothgar came under the control of the first [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). When King [Ranser](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ranser) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) attacked [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Wayrest sent emissaries into Wrothgar and promised to return Orsinium to the Orcs in exchange for helping him defeat Ranser. The clan of [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog) answered his call and helped annihilate Ranser's forces at Markwasten Moor. After these events, Kurog was given control over Wrothgar, and his new Orsinium joined the second Daggerfall Covenant.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHi O-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TFo KR-17)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-9)</sup>

The deal made between Emeric and Korug gave the Orcs control of all Wrothgar as an independent kingdom separate from High Rock.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OGTB-8)</sup> Terms such as Breton High Rock and Orcish Wrothgar were used to distinguish the two as being independent.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-WHLS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-19)</sup> At this time Farrun was under the control of the Orcs, and was an Orcish Stronghold going by the name Fharun.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Fharun-20)</sup> The Orcs would also reclaim all of Wrothgar from the Reachmen, who had control of the Wrothgarian Mountains during the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONAlga-21)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Kurog would ask for adventurers to aid the rebuilding of Orsinium. One of them, the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), would aid King Kurog in driving out the last of the [Winterborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterborn) holdouts at [Frostbreak Fortress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbreak_Fortress), and later foiling a plot by the King to kill all the Orcish clan chiefs who refused to give up the worship of [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-It O-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-FKAG-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Boa KHs-24)</sup>

Sometime after [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Bretons settled in some parts of the Wrothgar, resulting in higher Breton populations among the kingdoms of [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md) and Farrun on its northern shores.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MAP-25)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-19)</sup>

The [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) and his horde of warriors marched through the Wrothgarian Mountains while his main forces were occupied to the west in the mouth of the Iliac Bay. This use of this strategy was attributed to the philosophy of his brother, Camoran Kaltos. With the [Battle of Firewaves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Firewaves) in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267), the Usurper's armies were quashed by the combined forces of the Iliac Bay's lesser known nations.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TR-26)</sup>

In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), the city of Jehanna had fallen under the control of Skyrim and the landscape was swiftly claimed by their Counts. By the waning years of the Third Era, resistance in the cities was feeble at best, but the fight remained strong toward the border.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3E-5)</sup>

After the Warp in the West, King [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gortwog_gro-Nagorm) of Orsinium gained control over a significant part of the Wrothgarian Mountains.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWit W-11)</sup>

In the Fourth Era Orsinium was once again razed, and the next incarnation of Orsinium was founded closer to the border between Hammerfell and Skyrim.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Lo S-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SRLoading-28)</sup>

## Geography
The Wrothgarian mountains have three different biomes. Frostbreak Ridge, also called Western Wrothgar, is home to milder climates than the rest of Wrothgar, and mainly consist of boreal forests found along the cove of the [Great Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Bay). What was called the Morkul Plains in the Second Era, also known as Central Wrothgar, consists mainly of tundra plains. Eastern Wrothgar, also called Icy Shores, has a frigid, snowy climate that results from the high mountains.

## Notable Places
Notable places in the Wrothgarian Mountains include:

- [Argent Mine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Argent_Mine) - Headquarters of the [Silver Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Silver_Dawn)
- [Cloud Spring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Spring) - A Breton town within the Wrothgarian Mountains.
- [Fharun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fharun) - an Orcish stronghold, and Breton city within the Wrothgarian Mountains.
- [Frostbreak Fortress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbreak_Fortress) - A fortress built during the Akaviri Potentate to defend Orsinium.
- [Honor's Rest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Honor%27s_Rest) - A tomb within Wrothgar, and final resting place of [Baloth Bloodtusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baloth_Bloodtusk) and [Gaiden Shinji](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiden_Shinji).
- [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md) - A city near the border of Skyrim.
- [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) - An Orcish city that has been razed and rebuilt several times.
- [Raven Spring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Raven_Spring) - A Breton village located in the foothills of the Wrothgarian Mountains near the border of Hammerfell.
- [Shedungent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shedungent) - [Nulfaga's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nulfaga) castle in the Wrothgarian Mountains in the Third Era.
- [Sorrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sorrow) - the tallest mountain in the Wrothgarian Mountains.
- [Wrothgaria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgaria) - A Breton city and the regional seat of the Wrothgarian Mountains in the Third Era.
