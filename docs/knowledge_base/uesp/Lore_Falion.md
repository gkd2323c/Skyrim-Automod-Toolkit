*I know many things. I have studied things beyond the reach of most humans, traveled the Oblivion planes, seen things one should not see. I have met Daedra and Dwemer and everything in between and I know enough to see a vampire where others would see a man.*
—Falion<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Falion-1)</sup> Falion is a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) wizard, master [conjurer](Lore_Conjuration.md), and planar traveler who claimed to have encountered both the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Falion-1)</sup> He was a former teacher at the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Phinis-2)</sup> and he was regarded as one of the only people in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) capable of curing [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Isran-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/SR-npc-Phinis_Gestor.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Phinis_Gestor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Phinis_Gestor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Phinis_Gestor.jpg) Phinis Gestor, Falion's student *Go to Morthal and find Falion. He's the only one I know of in Skyrim that can cure vampirism.*
— [Isran](Lore_Isran.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Isran-3)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/SR-npc-Agni.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Agni.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Agni.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Agni.jpg) Agni, Falion's apprentice Little is known about Falion's early life. He and his sister Jonna hailed from [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and traveled together in for most of their life.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PRIMA-4)</sup><sup>:724-725</sup> At one point, Falion served as a teacher at the College of Winterhold, where he mentored mages, including the expert conjurer Phinis Gestor. He eventually grew to resent the leadership of Arch-Mage [Savos Aren](Lore_Savos_Aren.md) and the direction of the College, and he departed many years before [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), leaving only a note behind.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Phinis-2)</sup> After further travels, he was drawn to [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) in [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), where he and his sister settled. Jonna remodeled one of the homes into the local inn, while Falion devoted himself to magical research,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PRIMA-4)</sup><sup>:724-725</sup> settling down in a house, which also served as his shop,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FH-5)</sup> where he was also providing magical training in the arts of conjuration.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Falion-1)</sup>

Falion's sister was fiercely protective of him, urging would-be students to seek out the College instead,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Jonna-6)</sup> and attempting to reassure the steward Aslfur, husband of Jarl [Idgrod Ravencrone](Lore_Idgrod_Ravencrone.md), that her brother's arcane pursuits posed no threat to the people of Morthal.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Jonna-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aslfur-7)</sup> Falion took in the orphaned [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) child Agni as both an apprentice and a ward of sorts. According to him, she was a quick and capable learner. Though he instructed her in magic himself, he discouraged her from seeking study at the College of Winterhold.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Agni-8)</sup>

Falion aided the [Sheathed Blades](Lore_Sheathed_Blades.md) in their pursuit of Oviron Salith by reanimating a fallen member of the [Chosen of Boethiah](Lore_Chosen_of_Boethiah.md). Though initially reluctant, he was persuaded to assist, enabling the Blades to uncover Oviron's intention to bring his scheme to its conclusion at the Sacellum of Boethiah. His involvement in the conflict extended no further than providing the agents with the necessary information.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TAG-9)</sup>

By 4E 201, many citizens grew uneasy about Falion's presence,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aslfur-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Benor-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Jorgen-11)</sup> and the steward raised the matter with his wife, Jarl Idgrod. She, however, saw no cause for action, maintaining that she had permitted him to live in Morthal and stood by that decision,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aslfur-7)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Idgrod-12)</sup> though it was viewed with distrust by some of her subjects.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Jorgen-11)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/SR-place-Falion%27s_House.jpg/200px-SR-place-Falion%27s_House.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falion%27s_House.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falion%27s_House.jpg) Falion's House in Morthal Jarl Idgrod dreamed of Falion casting spells in the marsh in the dead of night near a summoning circle.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Idgrod-12)</sup> Similarly, her son Joric, who shared his mother's prophetic sight, claimed that Falion was responsible for some unclear, but concerning deed, insisting that he had done it, though he could not say what it was.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Joric-13)</sup> Falion's own ward and apprentice also suffered nightmares of him abandoning her and bringing forth monsters.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Agni-8)</sup> Falion was indeed slipping out at night to perform secret magical rites. He was followed by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), whom he attempted to bribe in exchange for their silence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Falion-1)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FS-14)</sup> It was unclear whether his secret was kept or revealed to Jarl Idgrod, or to Jarl Sorli the Builder should Morthal have been under [Stormcloak](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) control, either of whom would have demanded that he cease his actions lest he face the consequences.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Idgrod-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FS-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Sorli-15)</sup>

Falion's research focused on vampires, [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) and all matter of [undead](Lore_Undead.md). He was correctly believed to possess the knowledge to cure vampirism. Many innkeepers,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RAD-16)</sup> as well as members of the [Dawnguard](Lore_Dawnguard.md), were aware of these rumors.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Sorine-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Florentius-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Gunmar-19)</sup> The order's grandmaster, [Isran](Lore_Isran.md), maintained that Falion was the only person in Skyrim that he knew of who was capable of curing the condition.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Isran-3)</sup>

In his pursuit of knowledge, Falion claimed to delve into matters beyond the reach of most mortals. He had traveled the planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), encountered Daedra and Dwemer alike, and seen things he believed were best left unseen. Such experiences, along with his study of [life-extending](Lore_Lifespan.md) [magic](Lore_Magic.md), allowed him to recognize a vampire where others would see only a mortal. Though he had long understood the ritual required to cure vampirism, which demanded a [black soul gem](Lore_Black_Soul_Gem.md) filled with a mortal [soul](Lore_Soul.md), he had not performed it. In 4E 201, he may have had occasion to enact the rite, curing the Last Dragonborn should they have contracted the affliction and sought cure.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Falion-1)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RAD-16)</sup> It is also possible that he extended this service to [Serana](Lore_Serana.md), an ancient [Daughter of Coldharbour](Lore_Daughters_of_Coldharbour.md) of the [Volkihar clan](Lore_Volkihar.md), and freed her from vampirism as well.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Serana-20)</sup>
