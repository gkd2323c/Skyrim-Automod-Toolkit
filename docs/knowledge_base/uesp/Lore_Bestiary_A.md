## [Abomination](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abomination)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/LG-cardart-Risen_Abomination.png/200px-LG-cardart-Risen_Abomination.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Risen_Abomination.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Risen_Abomination.png) An Abomination [Abominations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abomination) are mechanomagical lifeforms that were created by the former [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md), [Mecinar](Lore_Mecinar.md), and are a perversion of [Sotha Sil's](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) goal of creating the perfect lifeform. Early variants consisted of individuals modified against their will that were melded with steel and sorcery similar to [Fabricants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fabricants), but are a step further and are merged with beasts. The creations led Sotha Sil to exile Mecinar out of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).

## Aether-Hound
Aether-Hounds are creatures that feast on the [spirits](Lore_Soul.md) of the recently departed. They were described by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) [necromancer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) [N'Gasta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:N%27Gasta) as comparable to his soul snare. He described them as "broad shouldered and panting, patrolling the near-here gates of death, alert for the scent of the hapless spirit".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-N.27Gasta-4)</sup> It is unclear if they are literal [canines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dog).

## Air Atronach
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/ff/ON-creature-Air_Atronach.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Air_Atronach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Air_Atronach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Air_Atronach.jpg) Air Atronach [Air Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Air_Atronach) are a type of [Elemental Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elemental_Daedra) that possess the combined power of a [flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Atronach), [frost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Atronach) and [storm atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Storm_Atronach). As a result, Air Atronachs are capable of absorbing the element of atronachs slain nearby to them to temporarily increase their strength. They are able to use a variety of elemental attacks. [The Serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent) used them in his army in [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn), and caused the creation of the powerful "Air Monarch".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> They are known to be in service to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) have been known to summon unique variations of the atronach.

It is not know what realm they originate from, though there is said to be a chaotic elemental realm where fire, ice, and lightning continually clash.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Enchantment_Power#Soul_Bound_Table), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Air_Atronach), [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Followers#Isbrann_Lyn)

## [Aggression of Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aggression_of_Root)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/ON-creature-Aggression_of_Root.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Aggression_of_Root.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Aggression_of_Root.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Aggression_of_Root.jpg) An Aggression of Root [Aggressions of Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aggression_of_Root) are uncommon [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit). According to the [Druids](Lore_Druid.md), they speak with the voice of [plants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flora) and protect nature where it feels threatened. They are known for attracting a wide range of dangerous wildlife to any area they inhabit, creating a hazardous environment for nearby residents and travelers. They can command other nature spirits in battle such as [Forest Wraiths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forest_Wraith) and [Spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan), as well as vegetation such as [Stranglers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strangler) and creatures such as [Fauns](Lore_Faun.md). They are capable of speech and have a discordant tone.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Aggression_of_Root)

## [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/MW-creature-Akulakhan.png/200px-MW-creature-Akulakhan.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Akulakhan.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Akulakhan.png) Akulakhan [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan), also called the **Second [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md)**, is the name of the [machine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:God) built by [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) under [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). Using the divine influence of the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) to power Akulakhan, he planned to use it to drive all outlanders out of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), cast down the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), spread [Corprus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus), and eventually take over all [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

Dagoth Ur also planned to establish a theocracy based on the worship of Akulakhan with his [Ash Vampire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Vampire) brothers serving as the god priests of Akulakhan. But, in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) arrived in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), recovered [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac)'s Tools—the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) artifacts [Wraithguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wraithguard), [Sunder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunder), and [Keening](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keening) —and led an assault against Dagoth Ur's stronghold. Using the power of the tools, the Nerevarine was able to destroy the tonal enchantments on the Heart, killing Dagoth Ur and destroying Akulakhan.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](Morrowind_Dagoth_Ur_(facility).md#Akulakhan.27s_Chamber)

## [Alit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alit)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/ON-creature-Alit.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Alit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Alit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Alit.jpg) An alit [Alits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alit) (or **Dusky Alits**) are tailless, bipedal omnivorous [reptiles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) that are native to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). They also inhabit parts of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), such as the island of [Khenarthi's Roost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi%27s_Roost), and a species of brown **Desert Alits** live in [Northern Elsweyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Elsweyr). They are related to [guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guar), [kagouti](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagouti), and [wormmouths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wormmouth).

Built like the kagouti, the Alit has a large head and protruding jaw, and when running on its short, stumpy legs, it looks like a big toothy mouth with feet. While most lack the stature of their kagouti cousins, they possess a more vicious temperament and more cunning attack instinct.

## [Ammonite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ammonite)
[Ammonites](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ammonite) (also known as **Eltheric Ammonites**) are extinct aquatic [mollusks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mollusk) once native to the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) and known only for the beautiful shells they left behind. Although the true origin of these shells is unknown, the consensus among naturalists is that ammonites predate even the oldest of [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) ruins. Fossilized ammonites, along with [trilobites](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trilobite), could also be found in the stone walls of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).

Ammonites are most commonly harvested for use by mages and [druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) due to the fact that they contain stores of raw [magicka](Lore_Magic.md). The capacity varies depending on the shell, with some only holding trace amounts of magicka and others holding nearly as much as a lesser-grade [soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems). However, ammonites cannot be recharged and suffer from arcane decay, losing potency within a month of being harvested.

- See also: [Nautilus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nautilus)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Treasures)

## Ancestor Ghost
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/MW-creature-Ancestor_Ghost.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Ancestor_Ghost.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Ancestor_Ghost.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Ancestor_Ghost.jpg) An ancestor ghost Ancestor Ghosts are the [spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) or [wraiths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wraith) of deceased [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), Ancestor Ghosts commonly defend the tombs of clan and kin, but may also be summoned and controlled by sorcerers.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MWAnc Ghost-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Aat D-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-21)</sup> Ancestor Ghosts are aggressive, but not very dangerous to the prepared adventurer. These ghosts can curse those who attack them, and are immune to disease, poison, frost damage and mundane weapons.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MWAnc Ghost-19)</sup> Powerful Ancestor Ghosts are referred to as Ancestor Guardians.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

They are often brought about through the binding of a [spirit](Lore_Soul.md) against its will, which usually results in the spirit going mad. This unpleasant fate is reserved for those who have not served the family faithfully in life, so that they may guard the family shrine in undeath. Dutiful and honorable ancestral spirits often aid in the capture and binding of these wayward spirits. These mad spirits make for terrifying guardians, and are exceedingly dangerous to outsiders. Though they are ritually prevented from harming mortals of their clans, that does not necessarily discourage them from mischievous or peevish behavior.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Aat D-20)</sup>

The ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) were known to summon their own form of Ancestor Ghosts, bound spirits that resemble [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Ancestor_Ghost), [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Ghost#Ancestor_Ghost), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Ancestor_Guardian), [Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancestor_Ghost)<sup>([CC](Skyrim_Staves_(Creation).md))</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Indoril_Ancestor_Spirit), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Vigilant Ancestor)

## [Ancestor Lizard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancestor_Lizard)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/ON-pet-Lilmoth_Ancestor_Lizard.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Lilmoth_Ancestor_Lizard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Lilmoth_Ancestor_Lizard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Lilmoth_Ancestor_Lizard.jpg) An Ancestor Lizard [Ancestor Lizards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancestor_Lizard) (or **Useful Lizards**) are semi-aquatic, tree-dwelling swamp [lizards](Lore_Lizard.md) native to the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). They have webbed hands with help them swim in muddy water and hunt for [feathery-gilled wrigglers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm). They are known to hunt amidst twisting [cypress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cypress) roots, where predators from the deeper parts of the swamp can't reach them. After eating they bask in the [sun](Lore_Sun.md) on logs, amidst other members of their species.

Scholars believe that [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) were uplifted from these lizards. The Argonian allegory known as the *Parable of Becoming* states that the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) found the humanoid forms of [Men](Lore_Men.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) useful, and molded the form of the swamp's lizards after them to create Argonians. This theory appears to be an accurate account; in [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), the Argonian [Mere-Glim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels_Mere-Glim) received a vision while on [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) of life as a lizard, before the root and the taste of [Hist Sap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hist_Sap) came and transformed both his body and mind.

**Found in:** - [Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels_Creatures), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Helstrom_Ancestor_Lizard)

## [Ascended Sleeper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascended_Sleeper)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg) An ascended sleeper [Ascended Sleepers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascended_Sleeper) are highly intelligent and aggressive distorted half- [mer](Lore_Mer.md), half-beast [ash creatures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Creature). Originally [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) cultists, they have become powerful [magical](Lore_Magic.md) beings. They are among the most feared beings in [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur)'s revived [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House), alongside [Ash Vampires](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Vampire). They rank highly in the Sixth House and are the final and most powerful mutation of the common ash creatures transformed through Dagoth Ur's [Ash Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Blight) and [Divine Disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus). Stronger cultists who do not become simple Corprus beasts advance through the stages of mutation and enlightenment before eventually reaching the powers of Ascended Sleepers. These cultists start by becoming [Ash Slaves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Slave) and [Ash Zombies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Zombie), progressing into [Ash Ghouls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Ghoul), then finally becoming Ascended Sleepers.

In appearance their faces are covered in strange tentacles filled with holes, these tentacles are seemingly used as [musical](Lore_Music.md) [instruments](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Instruments). They can afflict their opponents with a variety of deadly Blight diseases as well as damaging frost, fire, and shock attacks. In personality these creatures act above petty concerns. They have little concern for their own deaths, believing that if they are slain they will return as if waking from a dream. When slain, these creatures' bodies disappear, [ash salts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy_A#Ash_Salts) can be harvested from their remains.
