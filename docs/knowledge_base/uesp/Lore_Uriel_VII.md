*I've served the Nine all my days, and I chart my course by the cycles of the heavens.
The skies are marked with numberless sparks, each a fire, and every one a sign.*
—Uriel Septim VII, the Emperor of Tamriel Uriel Septim VII ([/ˈ j ʊər i ə l ˈ s ɛ p t ɪ m/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***yer** -iel septm* Link ⏵) was the twenty-first [Dragonborn Emperor](Lore_Dragonborn.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBOTD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CHot E4-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DUG-3)</sup><sup>:3</sup> though some described him as the twenty-fourth of the Septim line.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue U-4)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Placement)</sup> He succeeded to the throne in [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368) after the passing of his father, [Pelagius Septim IV](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_IV). Uriel VII's 65-year reign was interrupted for a decade by the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), during which he was trapped in another [plane of existence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) while the impostor [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) ruled in his name.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CHot E4-2)</sup> After being restored to the throne, his reign saw the [Miracle of Peace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miracle_of_Peace) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and the fulfillment of the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DF-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MW-6)</sup> Uriel VII was assassinated in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), ushering in the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CHot E4-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ob MQ-7)</sup>

While the inheritance of the Dragon Blood is not strictly based upon bloodlines,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KKPosts-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Reman1-10)</sup> Uriel's birthright was such that he could don the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Reman1-10)</sup> It is said that the Dragon Blood which flowed through the veins of the Septims allowed them to "see more than lesser men", and only a true heir of the Blood could wear the Amulet.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Bau IV-11)</sup>

During his rule Uriel VII wed Princess [Caula Voria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caula_Voria), and sired at least four legitimate heirs: [Ariella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ariella_Septim),<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-12)</sup> [Geldall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Geldall_Septim), [Enman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Enman_Septim) and [Ebel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebel_Septim);<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Assas-13)</sup> though other direct sources indicate sons of his with other names exist: [Pelagius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prince_Pelagius), [Cephorus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prince_Cephorus), [Uriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prince_Uriel), [Cassynder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prince_Cassynder), [Voragiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voragiel), and [Trabbatus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trabbatus).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-14)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Heirs)</sup> Uriel VII fathered at least two children outside of wedlock: [Calaxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calaxes_Septim) (who was publicly acknowledged and participated in family affairs) and [Martin](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) (whose true lineage was hidden, even to him, until the Oblivion Crisis).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-WAP-15)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ob MQ-7)</sup> Additional unknown family members were around during this time,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-14)</sup> and Uriel was said to have some nephews as well (such as Pythus Angelicus).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-RFM-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Loranna-17)</sup> Regardless, when Uriel VII was assassinated, any suitable heirs known to the public were assassinated at nearly the same time, leaving only Martin to claim the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ob MQ-7)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life
Shortly after Uriel's father, [Pelagius IV](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_IV), was crowned Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), Queen [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) and Prime Minister [Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) of Morrowind attended a dinner with the Septim Royal Family. At that youthful age, Uriel insisted that a place be set at every meal for his imaginary female playmate, Justin. Of course, Uriel would soon grow out this. However, it would inspire a long running joke between Symmachus and Uriel throughout the monarch's reign; Symmachus would inquire into Justin's health whenever he met with the Emperor, and Uriel would respond mock-seriously on every occasion. One time Uriel elaborately explained to Symmachus that Justin had met an adventurous, though incorrigible, [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) youth, married him, and settled down in [Lilandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lilandril) to raise [fire ferns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fire_Fern) and [mugworts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mugwort).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRB-18)</sup>

### Emperor of Tamriel
When Pelagius IV died and Uriel VII succeeded him in [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368) after an astonishing twenty-nine-year reign, Tamriel was closer to unity than it had been since the days of [Uriel I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_I). According to historians, Uriel VII had the diligence of his grand-aunt [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md), the political skill of his grand-uncle [Uriel VI](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_VI), and the military prowess of his great-grandfather [Uriel V](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_V). For sixty-five years he reigned and brought justice and order to Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CHot E4-2)</sup>

### Imperial Simulacrum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/AR-npc-Uriel_Septim-Deluxe.png/200px-AR-npc-Uriel_Septim-Deluxe.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Uriel_Septim-Deluxe.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Uriel_Septim-Deluxe.png) Emperor Uriel during the Simulacrum In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), Uriel VII was betrayed by [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who imprisoned him in an [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) plane of his own creation <sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo USVII-19)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CHot E4-2)</sup> using "the most powerful spell conjecturable"<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum Denouement-20)</sup><sup>:16-17</sup>, with help from [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum Denouement-20)</sup><sup>:180</sup> Tharn then used [illusion magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion) to assume the Emperor's visage. Tharn held Uriel VII in a dimension where time ran slower, ensuring it would take centuries for him to die (at which point, the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) would somehow warn the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) of the Emperor's passing).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Arena Manual-21)</sup> For the next ten years, Tharn pursued his own agenda, not continuing Uriel VII's schedule of reconquest. It is not entirely known what Tharn's goals and personal accomplishments were during this time.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CHot E4-2)</sup>

Tharn was eventually defeated in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399) by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md), who freed Uriel VII from his other-dimensional cell.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-EA-22)</sup> After his release, Uriel VII worked diligently to heal and reunite Tamriel. Tharn's interference broke the momentum, but the following years proved that the lauded golden age of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) could return to Tamriel once more.

### Secret Meeting
In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), Uriel VII and his new Imperial Battlemage, [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md), met in the audience chamber of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) with an [agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md). Uriel dispatched this agent to the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) to investigate the restless [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of King [Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus) of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall), as well as to find and destroy a letter the Emperor sent to Queen [Mynisera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mynisera) of Daggerfall.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DUG-3)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Df IS-23)</sup> In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), this agent's choices would lead the many squabbling principalities of northwestern Tamriel to be magically reforged into four kingdoms. Those who celebrate the historic calm this event brought to the region remember it as the [Miracle of Peace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miracle_of_Peace). Those who recall the massive amounts of death, destruction, and fear wrought by the event itself would more likely refer to it as the Warp in the West.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWINT-24)</sup>

### Later Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OBR-npc-Emperor_Uriel_Septim_04.jpg/200px-OBR-npc-Emperor_Uriel_Septim_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-Emperor_Uriel_Septim_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-Emperor_Uriel_Septim_04.jpg) Uriel Septim VII in the Imperial City Prison [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png/200px-LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png) The Emperor is assassinated By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Uriel VII was dogged by rumors of age and illness, and that Ocato was making the real decisions of the Empire.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MSHH-25)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a member of the Blades wrote in a private letter that High Chancellor Ocato and the Elder Council had been running the Empire for fifteen years (i.e., since shortly after the Miracle of Peace).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Lt M-26)</sup>

Uriel took considerable personal interest in the Nerevarine Prophecy of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), and in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), he dispatched [an agent](Lore_Nerevarine.md) of obscure origins to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) with designs to fulfill the legend, an apparently successful venture.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo USVII-19)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DP-27)</sup>

Uriel VII was troubled all his life by dreams that showed him [visions of the future](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight), including his assassination and the ensuing Oblivion Crisis.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-28)</sup> On the 27th of Last Seed, [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), his premonitions came to pass: his heirs were assassinated by a Daedric Cult called the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) during Crown Prince [Geldall Septim's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Geldall_Septim) Engagement Ball. After midnight on the 27th, as Uriel VII was attempting to escape through the tunnels beneath the [Imperial City Prison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_City_Prison), the Mythic Dawn found and assassinated him.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Assas-13)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TOC-29)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TPWarofthe Wormgod-30)</sup> This left only the secret son, Martin Septim, to claim the throne and save Tamriel from Oblivion.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TOC-29)</sup>

- Additionally, some *Morrowind* sources (a new edit of *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire_v_1)* and the new book *[The Arcturian Heresy](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md)*) seemingly retconned that [Pelagius I](Lore_Pelagius_I.md) was the grandson of Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) rather than being Tiber Septim's son as *Daggerfall* indicated. Additionally, some instances of "Tiber Septim" were changed to "Tiber Septim I"<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRB-18)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-BOQB-32)</sup>, which may have impacted the count.
- | [^2.](#note_Heirs) | In *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, the player character is given a personal [Background History](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Background_History#Notes) which they may read in their character menu, determined by their chosen class. In certain scenarios—such as those for the Bard or Rogue—the player character encounters a male heir of Emperor Uriel VII. There are 6 possible names assigned to this prince, chosen from a unique pre-designed list specifically created for this single use in the Background History: Pelagius, Cephorus, Uriel, Cassynder, Voragiel, and Trabbatus (in that order). In the Ranger background, the prince you met mentions that he has brothers that are also on the run; the plural use of "brothers" indicates the existence of multiple other princes. The number of possible names could logically indicate 6 in total. These figures precede the later introduction of Geldall, Enman, and Ebel, the Emperor's sons coined in *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*. Ultimately, no information on the other names for princes given in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* is available, as they are not referenced in subsequent games.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-14)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- The name "Septim" as originally coined for *[The Elder Scroll: Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* presumably comes from the Latin "Septimus", meaning "Seventh".<sup>[[LANG 1]](#cite_note-Septimus-33)</sup> This would mean that Uriel Septim VII means Uriel the Seventh the Seventh, and/or that "Septim" is a synonym for the Seventh.
- According to dialogue from *Arena*, Uriel VII's real birthday is not on the 30th of [Frostfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostfall) as the holiday description for [Emperor's Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Emperor%27s_Day) implies. Instead, Emperor's Day represents a symbolic celebration of the [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Arena Holi-34)</sup> The *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* intro implies that Uriel VII's true birthday is sometime prior to the 1st of [Hearthfire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hearthfire) (the date the game begins).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Arena Intro-35)</sup>
- During his reign as Emperor, Uriel VII had two known secretaries. The earliest known secretary was [Lomegan Mariel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lomegan_Mariel), who was also the Emperor's personal liaison to the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) prior to its destruction.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BSPaxti-36)</sup> The next was [Glabrio Bellienus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glabrio_Bellienus), who sent correspondence to the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) Spymaster on Vvardenfell, [Caius Cosades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caius_Cosades), about his task on the island.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DP-27)</sup>
- The voice actor for Uriel in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* is unknown, although [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson) believes he was a local acting student. The actor for Uriel in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* was John Gilbert. The voice actor for Uriel in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* was [Patrick Stewart](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Stewart). In [*Oblivion Remastered*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion_Remastered), Uriel's face is more heavily modeled after Patrick Stewart's likeness. - Lead Designer [Ken Rolston](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ken_Rolston) had this to say about Uriel VII's portrayal in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*: "[Patrick Stewart] was selling the product like a reverend... standing in front of his flock... which is exactly the tone I wanted." According to Todd Howard, Rolston had "written a bunch of notes for Patrick Stewart on the character—very, very long, referencing things Stewart had done before. I thought it was embarrassingly long, actually."<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Oblivion Making Of-37)</sup>
- Uriel's five appearances make him tied with [Staada](Lore_Staada.md) for the character with the most [appearances](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Recurring_Characters) in the series, excepting [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).
