# Elsweyr Confederacy


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg/200px-ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg) [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance) of [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha) As the rebellion continued, [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta), the nonpartisan spiritual leader of the Khajiit, revealed the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) in the [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance) of [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha) in [2E 311](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_311).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLSRT-4)</sup> The Mane used Riddle'Thar to proclaim a new system of dividing the powers in Elsweyr. Bound to the dance of the two moons—their different phases—the power in a region shifted between the nobility of the cities and the chieftains of the deserts. The Mane itself was overseeing this shift of powers, and as this system was implemented it finally brought peace to Elsweyr some time around or after [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324) —lasting under many more Manes.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup> The edicts from this key event in Khajiiti theology however were not accepted by everyone at this time<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Shazah-5)</sup>, leading to those exiled and their temples deconsecrated.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONLSCS-6)</sup>

Two hundred years later during the chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) ravaged the land of the Khajiit. As the Khajiit were focused on fighting against their own extinction, they saw aggression from the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) and [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3V-7)</sup> Colovian armies, during the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), invaded northern Elsweyr and annexed it. Their invasion went as far as [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) and [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md), until they were pushed out around [2E 562](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_562).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Khali-8)</sup> This didn't stop the Colovians, and in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), [Euraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn) became Queen of Rimmen after staging the [Frostfall Coup](Lore_Frostfall_Coup.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTON-9)</sup> The occupation was short-lived after Euraxia was killed and Rimmen was brought back under the control of the Khajiit in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), although the Elsweyr Confederacy was left in shambles.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-10)</sup>

Knahaten Flu continued to spread across Elsweyr until aid came unexpectedly from the Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) and the [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md), bringing direly needed physicians, healers, and supplies. This aid gradually defeated the Knahaten Flu and saved the Khajiit. In return for, Queen Ayrenn asked the Khajiit to stand by her side as invading armies moved towards southwest Tamriel, to which the Elsweyr Confederacy agreed.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TEATC-11)</sup> The signing of the [Elden Accord](Lore_Elden_Accord.md) by Queen Ayrenn, [Lord Gharesh-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lord_Gharesh-ri) and King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran) formed the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gharesh-ri-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TEATC-11)</sup> Under the united banner of the Dominion, they entered the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TCC-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-WTNAI-14)</sup> The Elsweyr Confederacy contended with numerous other threats during the Three Banners War. Colovians once again invaded northern Elsweyr under [Javad Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Javad_Tharn) and worked together with the Stonefire Cult to corrupt the current Mane [Akkhuz-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Akkhuz-ri) and anoint his own Dark Mane, thus corrupting the spiritual soul of the Khajiit.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONJavad-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Bodring-17)</sup> The conflict came to a head in the city of [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), where Javed launched an attack by opening Daedric portals and allowing Daedra into the streets. The Khajiit fought back and his plan failed, ending the Daedric threat.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONFOD-18)</sup>

In an effort to stop the Three Banners War, [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) inadvertently unleashed the Dragons [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid), [Laatvulon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laatvulon) and their minions from their containment from the Halls of Colossus. Kaalgrontiid continued pursuing the [Prophecy of the New Moon](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_New_Moon.md) that would see him become the Dark Aeon and ascending to godhood. A Khajiiti dragon cult, known as the [New Moon Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Moon_Cult) appeared around this time to aid Kaalgrontiid's schemes. Queen [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira) and the recently reformed Dragonguard forged an alliance with the red dragon [Nahfahlaar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahfahlaar). Through their combined effort, the eventually went on to defeat Laatvulon and later Kaalgrontiid.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOJC-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESONMR-21)</sup>

Although Rimmen briefly seceded from the Elswey Confederacy in [2E 812](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_812), the ascending [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) began conquering parts of Anequina during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> In the Halls of Colossus, Tiber Septim reconstructed and reactivated the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SMIWDOT-22)</sup> Not long after, Senchal was besieged by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) troops under General Pottreid. The population did not surrender and was slain entirely.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Attrebus-23)</sup> Ultimately, Elsweyr was annexed to become a province of Tiber Septim's [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup>

While Elsweyr thrived under the Empire for most of the Third Era, in [4E 10](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_10) the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md) began - only ten years after the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-RT4-24)</sup> Elsweyr once again found itself independent of the Empire, but was granted no peace. In or around [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48) the Mane was assassinated, sinking the south of Elsweyr into chaos. During this time, an unknown figure proclaimed himself Potentate of Rimmen and surrounding areas.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TIC-25)</sup><sup>:179</sup> In [4E 98](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_98), Masser and Secunda vanished from the sky for two entire years. The so-called [Void Nights](Lore_Void_Nights.md) ended in [4E 100](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_100) with the return of the moons, while the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) took credit for bringing them back. Whether or not they were actually responsible for this turnaround remains unresolved. Nevertheless, as the Khajiit people and culture are tied inexorably close to the moons, the Thalmor were able to win great influence over them as their apparent saviors. Within the following fifteen years, until [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115), the influence of the Empire dwindled so much it was unable to stop the coup which dissolved the Elsweyr Confederacy and recreated the original kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine as client states of the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TGW-26)</sup>
