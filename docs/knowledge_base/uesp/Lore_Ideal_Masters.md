# Ideal Masters

—An Ideal Master (listen Link ⏵) The **Ideal Masters** are the malevolent revenant spirits that rule the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) known as the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md). Typically, they do not physically manifest themselves in the realm, instead preferring a type of omnipresence; however, Ideal Masters have been known to manifest through a crystal resembling a [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md), which can be used as a conduit through which the individual can communicate or drain the life essence of an approaching mortal. According to some sources the Ideal Masters are actually "disembodied entities living in a distant Platonic ideal" with their "only connection" to the realm being "small magical gems which provide a channel of communication and sensation with those bound in form to the Soul Cairn."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BA-1)</sup><sup>:115</sup> The Ideal Masters eternally seek souls to bring to the realm, where they become eternally trapped. The Masters view this as peaceful eternal life, although the undead who reside there view it as a curse. Individual Masters have names, so exalted that they cannot be spoken. They call themselves the **Makers of the Soul Cairn**, and were once mortals themselves.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LAFa Nuit Hen-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSIdeal-3)</sup>

## Overview

— *[The Master's Voice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Book_of_Life_and_Service)* The Ideal Masters originated early in [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s history.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LAFa Nuit Hen-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> as an early order of sorcerers who practiced [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) and trafficked in [souls](Lore_Souls.md). They grew to become very powerful, and eventually found their physical forms to be unacceptably weak and limiting. After transcending these forms and becoming beings of pure soul-energy, the sorcerers entered Oblivion as immortals and selected an area of [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) to create the Soul Cairn, a realm ideal for their role as soul merchants.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LAFa Nuit Hen-2)</sup> Although immortal, they are reluctant to expend magicka as this "diminishes their eternity".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSIdeal-3)</sup>

The Ideal Masters often come into contact with mortals. It is theorized that a soul trapped in a black soul gem is sent to the Soul Cairn if the gem is used to power an enchantment, there it must eternally pledge itself to the Masters.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Serana-5)</sup> Black souls (i.e. those belonging to the sentient mortal [races](Lore_Races.md)) are the most common type to be captured, although white souls and a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) soul are also known to reside in the Soul Cairn. Necromancers commonly summoned these trapped souls to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), although the art had been lost by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Necromancers seeking greater power would historically contact the Ideal Masters, and could gain access to armies of undead in return for souls, although oftentimes the necromancer would be deceived by the Masters who would then claim his soul as well. A notable early example of this would be the deal they made with the dragon [Durnehviir](Lore_Durnehviir.md), who became eternally trapped within the realm due to their deceit.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DG-6)</sup>

The Masters' only real connection to the Soul Cairn is through the magical gems that inhabit the realm. They generally manifest in one of two forms: as a small red crystal found "resting" in a coffin, through which the Master can communicate;<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Battlespire-7)</sup> or as a giant pink crystal, often found hovering above a ruined structure and seemingly protecting treasure, through which the Master can drain the life essence of anyone who approaches. The effect can be counteracted by ingesting a [Soul Husk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Husk), a plant native to the realm which can protect one's soul.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DG-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Old retainers of an ancient Daedric-Human alliance are said to serve the Ideal Masters. The retainers achieved their state through ritual, separating their spirits from their bodies. They strengthened and organized their spirits to the point where they achieved a form of eternal consciousness. Both the Ideal Masters and their servants are considered to be revenant spirits.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BA-1)</sup><sup>:115</sup> The [Soul Guard](Lore_Soul_Guard.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGGL-9)</sup><sup>:388</sup> also known as the **Blessed**, are the denizens of the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) that serve the Ideal Masters.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BSBLS-10)</sup>

## History
Sometime after the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) broke out in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), some [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) from [Stillrise Village](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stillrise_Village) that survived the plague aligned themselves with the Ideal Masters.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SR-11)</sup>

Documents dating from the second era indicate that at least one member of the [Waking Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waking_Flame), a cult dedicated to the Daedric Prince [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), were collecting souls in black soul gems for the Ideal Masters. The possible boon for the cult is unknown.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) armies of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) captured the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) and invaded the Soul Cairn. The Daedra planned to use as it as a waystation between their own realms and their newly-conquered domains. The Daedra killed the Masters' servants and plundered their treasure, although they also released the souls of several prominent mortal allies whom Dagon had betrayed into the realm. An [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md#The_Apprentice) from the Battlespire entered the realm, following the retreating army. Most of the Ideal Masters were unwilling to help the apprentice, but when they learned of the Daedric invaders who would supposedly continue to regularly pass through their realm, one Master instructed the apprentice on how to use the Emerald Gates to leave the realm and continue stalking Dagon's army. The apprentice used them to teleport to the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), and was eventually successful in halting the flow of Daedra through the Soul Cairn by forcing them to relinquish their hold on the newly-conquered realms.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Battlespire-7)</sup>

The Masters kept the [Vampire Lord](Lore_Vampire.md) [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) a prisoner within the realm for several eras, when she fled to the Soul Cairn with an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md#Last_Dragonborn) and the Vampire Lord [Serana](Lore_Serana.md) entered the Soul Cairn in search of Valerica. The interlopers defeated several of the Masters' most powerful servants and freed Valerica.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DG-6)</sup>
