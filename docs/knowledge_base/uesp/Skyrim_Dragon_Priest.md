[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg) A Dragon Priest **Dragon Priests** were once the loyal servants of the ancient [dragons](Skyrim_Dragon.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They ruled over countless armies of men in their gods' names. They are now found residing in coffins throughout [areas](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) of prominent dragon worship. If their sleep is disturbed, the Dragon Priest will burst out of its coffin with considerable force and prepare to destroy whomsoever intruded upon their resting place.

In combat, Dragon Priests are incredibly dangerous and can kill even high-level players quite quickly. They will constantly backpedal at a high speed, making it difficult to land any melee strikes until the Dragon Priest is driven into a corner. While moving, they will attack with various staves and destructive spells, making for an effective opponent both offensively and defensively.

Dragon Priests are at the high end of the leveled list of possible [draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md) -type spawns (see [effects of leveling](Skyrim_Leveling.md#Effects_of_Leveling) for a description of how creatures are generated according to the player-character's level). Because of this, other radiant named bosses, such as [Curalmil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Curalmil) and [Red Eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Eagle), may also appear as Dragon Priests if at a high enough level. These enemies will not carry an enchanted mask, however.

When one is close enough to a Dragon Priest sarcophagus, it will burst open forcefully. All random Dragon Priests' sarcophagi are horizontal, so that the lid bursts vertically. When a Dragon Priest is killed, it falls to the ground and almost immediately turns into a pile of ash. The clothing remains intact and is still searchable for loot.

## Named Dragon Priests

![](https://images.uesp.net/8/8a/Skyrim-Box-TL.gif)

![](https://images.uesp.net/f/f4/Skyrim-Box-TR.gif)

![](https://images.uesp.net/6/60/Skyrim-Box-BL.gif)

![](https://images.uesp.net/9/99/Skyrim-Box-BR.gif)
There are eight named Dragon Priest bosses in the province of Skyrim, each of which wear an [eponymous mask](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest_Mask.md) with a powerful effect. Each of the eight is challenging in their own way and has access to powerful spells, staves, and certain dragon shouts. Some, but not all, of these Dragon Priests are associated with particular quests. The [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) expansion adds five new named Dragon Priests, four of which carry unique enchanted masks (the exception being [Vahlok the Jailor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vahlok_the_Jailor)).

## Bromjunaar Sanctuary
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg) The Mask pedestals inside the Sanctuary In the center of ground-level [Labyrinthian](Skyrim_Labyrinthian.md) lies a small weathered dome. Within is a weathered altar with pedestals for each of the eight Dragon Priest masks. Next to the altar is a [wooden mask](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wooden_Mask) beside a skeleton. When it is equipped, and the player is inside the dome, the player will be transported back to a time when it was in its prime. When all eight of the unique Dragon Priest masks have been collected, the player can place them all on their respective pedestals. Doing this will reveal the ninth, most powerful, and final mask: [Konahrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Konahrik).

For more information, see the [Bromjunaar Sanctuary](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bromjunaar_Sanctuary) article.

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/SR-creature-Dragon_Priest_Dead.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dragon_Priest_Dead.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dragon_Priest_Dead.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dragon_Priest_Dead.jpg) A dead Dragon Priest - All Dragon Priests turn to ash upon death, the sole exception being Miraak, who turns into a skeleton instead.
- In terms of stats, [Malkoran's Shade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Malkoran%27s_Shade) is considered to be a Dragon Priest, although its level is only 26 and its health is only 300.
- Some named [Draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md), most notably [Red Eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Eagle), [Warlord Gathrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Warlord_Gathrik), and Yngol, may appear as Dragon Priests at higher levels. This is determined by the [radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md) system.
- The only location in the game where a Dragon Priest will reliably respawn is [Forelhost](Skyrim_Forelhost.md), after killing Rahgot. Each time the ruin respawns, a new generic Dragon Priest will spawn in Rahgot's place.

## Bugs
- Dragons and draugr are considered enemies, even though dragon priests are allies to both. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2, addresses this issue. Dragons and draugr are no longer hostile to one another.
- Although Red Eagle, Warlord Gathrik, and Yngol can all spawn as Dragon Priests at higher levels, the backstories behind these characters makes this extremely unlikely to have been the case. Plus, in the case of Warlord Gathrik, spawning him as a dragon priest will break the quest at [Ironbind Barrow](Skyrim_Ironbind_Barrow.md). - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.2, fixes this bug.
