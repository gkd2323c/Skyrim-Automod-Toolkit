# Rajhin

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/ON-npc-Rajhin%27s_Shadow_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Rajhin%27s_Shadow_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Rajhin%27s_Shadow_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Rajhin%27s_Shadow_02.jpg) One of Rajhin's Shadows Rajhin the Purring Liar, also known as the **Trickster God**, the **Footpad**, **Cat Who Walks**, and the **Cat-King of Thieves** is the Thief god of the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) people.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSSR-1)</sup> Tales of Rajhin are an integral part of Khajiiti culture and is popular among their storytellers,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONFWS-3)</sup> but he also finds worship among thieves and deceivers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONBrelor-4)</sup> Though he is the most famous burglar in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s history, he also targeted many other lands, even venturing to the realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) to add other's possessions to his collection.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-5)</sup> He was also very successful in wooing his lovers with his great treasures.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> It is said that every idol of Rajhin ever made will find its way into a pouch of a thief,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> but his idols are also popular as a ward against thieves.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Rajhin himself was described as being "the very embodiment of speed, agility, and slyness".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-5)</sup> He has Seven Shadows that each represent different aspects of his being and carry out his bidding.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSSR-1)</sup>

## History

### Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg/200px-OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg) Ring of Khajiiti Rajhin's date of birth is unknown, but he was around since before the Khajiiti people recognized a [Mane](Lore_Mane.md), and before the city of Rim'kha would become known as [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Rajhin Shadow-10)</sup> He grew up in the slums of [Black Kiergo](Lore_Black_Kiergo.md) in [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VOF-11)</sup>

Rajhin stole the [Ring of Khajiiti](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) from [Mafala](Lore_Mafala.md) prior to his ascension to godhood. The moons were new on the night of the crime, thus shined no light upon Nirn. The darkness gave Rajhin access to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), where he found Mafala, [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md), and a third Daedric Prince arguing over who claimed dominion over the night. Rajhin listened to their argument for a while before revealing his presence. He offered to settle their debate, as there is no one more familiar with the night than a thief. At first, Mephala regarded Rajhin with malice, and sought to eat him. However, Azurah stopped her. Each Prince told Rajhin why they deserved to be chosen, and the thief caught sight of the Ring of Khajiiti on Mephala's eighth arm. He seduced Mafala by appealing to her vanity, and stole the ring off her arm while they made love.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> He spotted the [Ebony Blade](Lore_Ebony_Blade.md) as he was leaving, and made off with both artifacts.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-5)</sup> Rajhin used the Ring of Khajiiti's powers to make himself as invisible, silent, and quick as a breath of wind. Using the Ring, he became the most successful burglar in the history of Elsweyr, eventually being elevated to the Thief God of the Khajiit.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-5)</sup>

His companion during his misadventures was his legendary mount, a Black Senche-Lion named Umbrage,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONBlack Senche Lion-13)</sup> more commonly known as Southpaw.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> He could both fly for brief periods, as well as talk.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONBlack Senche Lion-13)</sup> Additionally, he would keep watch while his master was breaking and entering.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONDark Moons Lynx-15)</sup> Thanks to their reverence towards Rajhin, thieves today prefer to ride [Senche-Cats](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) of Southpaw's breed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONBlack Senche Lion-13)</sup> The Dark Moons Lynx is said to be a worldly incarnation of Southpaw.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONDark Moons Lynx-15)</sup> Another breed of Senche-Cats associated with Rajhin is the Black Senche-Panther, a breed said to have been created when some of Rajhin's Shadows took a life on their own.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONBlack Senche-Panther Kitten-16)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/ON-misc-Grand_Larceny_02.png/200px-ON-misc-Grand_Larceny_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Grand_Larceny_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Grand_Larceny_02.png) Rajhin depicted stealing the Amulet of Kings from Alessia A story about one of Rajhin's most legendary heists claims he had stolen the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) from [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) for a time.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TOT1-17)</sup> Another of the notable feats he performed when he was alive was when he visited [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) seeking treasure, which led to him stealing the [Oghma Infinium](Lore_Oghma_Infinium.md) from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). Rajhin kept the book hidden in his possession, and occasionally the Daedric Prince managed to find it, but only because Rajhin chose to give it back to him on his own whim.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-5)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-HRSTBTK-18)</sup> It is also said that Rajhin pilfered enchanted purple horses and ponies from the realm of [Noweyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Noweyr).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-NP2-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-NS-20)</sup>

Rajhin's eventual fate is a mystery, but according to legend, the Ring rebelled against such constant use and disappeared, leaving Rajhin helpless before his enemies.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TL-21)</sup> After his death, his spirit was bound to the land until the ring was retrieved by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Hero.md) from a dank abyss in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) where it had been hidden by the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ARTIL-22)</sup> "Rajhin's Final Cups" are the alleged cups used in a drinking contest between him and the Daedric Prince [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md), which have since then been found in circulation in various shady markets and sold for a hefty sum.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

### Post-Mortem
Rajhin's Shadows still roam Nirn after his death, causing various forms of mischief. He is said to have stolen a tattoo from the neck of Empress [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra.md) as she slept.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VOF-11)</sup>

One story tells of a bard wanting the power to seduce any person he wished with his music. One of Rajhin's Shadows granted this wish, giving him an enchanted lyre, and the bard enjoyed inspiring lustful passion among his fans. Unfortunately, the lyre was a double-edged sword, as it bred jealously among his rivals, which eventually led to him being slain out of frustration. The lyre would pass many hands, and those who used it would also share the same fate. The lyre would eventually make its way into the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md), where it would be warded and kept safe, and any members of the college would be warned to play it at their own risk. The lyre would become known as the Shadow of Rajhin, after the trickster gods' shadows.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

Another tale of mischief involves the city of [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md). In seeking to punish an Altmer woman named Nairume for not returning his love, Rajhin's Shadow representing his desires trapped her in an icy prison, seeking to steal her love away from her husband. She would be freed of her prison if she were to touch Rajhin's Mantle, which would then steal her love from her husband, and replace it for love for the Trickster God. Refusing to betray her husband's trust, she refused to give in to the Shadow's desires, and remained trapped there since the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), until [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). [Queen Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) sought to prove herself as a worthy leader using the Orrery of Elden Root, and one of the pieces required to do so was Rajhin's Mantle. This search led to the liberation of Nairume from her containment, as well as the acquisition of the Mantle for Ayrenn's trial by the Orrery. Rajhin's Shadow claimed to have stolen the city, but it is unknown if this was a bluff.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONALW-25)</sup> Other evidence points to Mephala, who claims the city is under her web.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONM-26)</sup> Regardless, various theories have sprouted on how the city went missing.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCTH-27)</sup>

The story of [Rajhin and the Stone Maiden](Lore_Rajhin_and_the_Stone_Maiden.md) tells of the Trickster God preventing a young maiden from committing suicide. Concerned, Rajhin asked her what would drive her to do such a thing, and she explained that her greedy step-father demanded an insane bride-price, and caused the village he ran to seethe with corruption. Seeking to teach the step-father a lesson, Rajhin aided the maiden in a plan that would allow her to be with her beloved. And so the couple met with the step-father to arrange a deal, and offered something greater than the bride-price. It was a great land that can be seen from their village that has no ruler. One that "shines like a pearl in the darkness", can be claimed, but cannot be reached without their agent. The greedy step-father agreed to the terms, which then prompted Rajhin to manifest behind him. As per their agreement, Rajhin quickly seized him and took him to the moon of [Jode](Lore_Jode.md), far from where he could interfere with the couple's relationship. The villagers were initially shocked, but celebrated that their home was freed of the tyrant, and held a wedding ceremony for the couple. During the night, Rajhin manifested once more, for he traveled too fast which caused the step-father to be separated from his shadow, which threatened the newly-weds. Because they could not see it, from their perspective it appeared like Rajhin was reaching for air, and upon the Thief God's explanation, they were relieved and their laughter echoed across the riverbank.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESORSM-28)</sup>

During the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md), cat masks depicting Rajhin's visage are a staple.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONRCM-29)</sup> The Khajiit of [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md) celebrate the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) by finding true wealth in their actions. They honor Rajhin with the Trial of the Five-Clawed Guile. The challenge involves stealing coins from either locked boxes, or the pockets of a previous winner. This shows the participant has such little regard for barriers as Rajhin did.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONTumira-30)</sup>

One of the pieces of the [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md), a popular card game originating from the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) played continent-wide during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TSOT-31)</sup> bore the image of Rajhin. A deck themed after him was part of the game.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TOT1-17)</sup>
