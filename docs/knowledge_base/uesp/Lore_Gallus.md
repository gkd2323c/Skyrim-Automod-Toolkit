Gallus Desidenius was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) thief who became Guild Master of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TN2-1)</sup> He purportedly maintained strong ties with many influential families in Skyrim, which allowed the Guild to operate relatively safely.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FFG-2)</sup> He was also a member of the [Nightingale Trinity](Lore_Nightingales.md), along with [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), with whom he had a romantic relationship, and [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> The three of them swore an oath to [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) to protect the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md), which holds the [Ebonmere](Lore_Ebonmere.md) portal open at the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TN1-4)</sup> Gallus was the author of *[The Nightingales](Lore_The_Nightingales.md)*.

Over time, Gallus became suspicious of Mercer's behavior. Mercer, in turn, became aware of Gallus' scrutiny. Though he was unable to find hard proof, Gallus became convinced that Mercer was responsible for desecrating the Twilight Sepulcher and stealing the Skeleton Key.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GJ-5)</sup> Before he could dig any further, Mercer arranged a meeting, where he betrayed and murdered Gallus.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GJ-5)</sup> Gallus' spirit became the last of the Nightingale Sentinels and defended the Sepulcher alone for about 25 years.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup>

When Mercer Frey was finally defeated around [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Gallus was released from this duty by Nocturnal and allowed to become one with the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup>
