[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg/200px-SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg) Cracked Tusk Keep **Cracked Tusk Keep** is a medium-sized [fort](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md) west of [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath.md) containing [Orc](Skyrim_Orc.md) [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md) and [hunters](Skyrim_Orc_Hunter.md) led by [Ghunzul](Skyrim_Ghunzul.md). It contains two zones: *[Cracked Tusk Keep](#Cracked_Tusk_Keep)* and *[Cracked Tusk Vaults](#Cracked_Tusk_Vaults)*.

## Related Quests
- **[Amulet of the Moon](Skyrim_Amulet_of_the_Moon.md)**: Find the [Moon Amulet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Moon_Amulet) for [Kharjo](Skyrim_Kharjo.md). ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[Bandit Attack](Skyrim_Bandit_Attack.md)**<sup>[HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)</sup>: Rescue your beloved from kidnappers. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[Kill the Bandit Leader](Skyrim_Kill_the_Bandit_Leader_(Jarl).md)**: Slay a [bandit](Skyrim_Bandit.md) leader for [Siddgeir](Skyrim_Siddgeir.md), the [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) of [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath.md). ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[Pieces of the Past](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pieces_of_the_Past)**: Retrieve the scattered pieces of [Mehrunes' Razor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mehrunes%27_Razor) and reforge this deadly blade.

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](Skyrim_Radiant.md#Quest_Locations).

## Walkthrough

### Exterior
The grounds have the typical wooden palisade surrounding the keep. The guards are Orc bandits and they make good use of the many elevated positions around the keep. The western tower ruin contains a semi-buried master-locked [chest](Skyrim_Chest.md). The easiest way to access the chest is to climb up the rubble from outside of the wall. An apprentice-locked chest is located on the very top of the keep, between the two rear towers. Using the keep's series of ramps and lookout platforms, approach from the west and climb/jump up the rubble to the top. The keep's courtyard has several outbuildings designed for sustaining an army. From left to right around the courtyard is a smokehouse with a variety of [meats](Skyrim_Food.md) being cured and various animal [pelts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelt), a small rest area containing a copy of *[The Code of Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Code_of_Malacath)*, and then the main door to the keep. Across from the door is the fully equipped smithy, complete with a [forge](Skyrim_Forge.md), [workbench](Skyrim_Workbench.md), [grindstone](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) and a [tanning rack](Skyrim_Tanning_Rack.md). There is a random [sword](Skyrim_Sword.md)<sup>[*verification needed — best material?*]</sup> on top of the workbench. Inside the smithy's shed is a table with a selection of poor quality [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md) and [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) and a copy of the [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) [skill book](Skyrim_Skill_Books.md) *[Light Armor Forging](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Armor_Forging)*.

There are three entrances into the keep. The first, and most direct, is the front door. The second, ideal for stealth-based characters, is a level-locked door directly south of the smithy and down a flight of stairs. This is the door to the cellar and can grant an advantage against the guards in the main room. The third is a trapdoor on top of the southeast tower, which leads directly to the boss.

### Cracked Tusk Keep
Entering through the main door leads you right into the well-lit guards' area on two levels with at least two bandits; one on each level. A dresser just inside the entrance on the left has a [potion of minor healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing) and a [potion of minor stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina) on top. Opposite the entrance is a cupboard holding two common [books](Skyrim_Books.md), a bottle of [alto wine](Skyrim_Alto_Wine.md) and a bottle of [wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wine). There is a counter against the northeast wall with a haunch of raw [venison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Venison), a joint of [raw beef](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Raw_Beef), a [sliced goat cheese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sliced_Goat_Cheese) and two [carrots](Skyrim_Carrot.md) on top. Hanging above from racks is a [rabbit](Skyrim_Rabbit_(dead).md) and [pheasant](Skyrim_Pheasant.md). To the right is a barrel with a basket containing a [cabbage](Skyrim_Cabbage.md) and some [red apples](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Apple). To the left of the counter is a wooden door with stairs leading down to the cellar and *[Cracked Tusk Vaults](#Cracked_Tusk_Vaults)*. There are six unowned bedrolls on the floor.

There are stairs against the rear wall leading up to the upper level, where a long table and benches are laid out with a [baked potato](Skyrim_Baked_Potatoes.md), a portion of [grilled leeks](Skyrim_Grilled_Leeks.md), a [leg of goat roast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leg_of_Goat_Roast), a [tomato](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Tomato) and a loaf of [bread](Skyrim_Bread.md). There is a chair by the top of the stairs with an [iron shield](Skyrim_Iron_Shield.md) and random [warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Warhammer) leaning against it. There is an end table along with several food sacks around the walls and a set of shelves against the southwest wall holding a random [weapon](Skyrim_Weapons.md), a random [potion of healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing), [magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka) or [stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina), a medium [coin purse](Skyrim_Coin_Purse.md), five common books including a copy of *[The Legend of Red Eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Legend_of_Red_Eagle)* and several food sacks. In the eastern corner there has been a cave-in.

There is a wooden door to the southeast to the bandit leader's quarters. Through the door is a room with a fireplace on the right and a pair of [leather boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Boots) on the hearth that you cannot interact with. There are food sacks on either side and a ladder to the left that leads to a trapdoor exit out onto one of the towers. Opposite this is an opening to Ghunzul's bedroom, where you will find him. There is an unowned double bed coming out from the northeast wall with a dresser on the right with a copy of the [Cracked Tusk Vault key](Skyrim_Cracked_Tusk_Vault_Key.md) on top and a basket beside it containing two sheaves of [wheat](Skyrim_Wheat.md). To the left of the bed is a set of shelves holding a random [helmet](Skyrim_Helmet.md), a [steel mace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Mace), a small coin purse, a leveled potion of healing, and nine common books. At the foot of the bed is an unlocked boss chest and in the corner a round table and chair with a copy of *The Code of Malacath* on top. Ghunzul also carries a copy of the Cracked Tusk Vault key.

Through the door on the ground floor to the northwest is a passage that descends a flight of stairs. At the bottom is a cupboard holding only clutter, with a random pair of [boots](Skyrim_Armor.md#Boots) to the left. On your right is the room where you would arrive if entering through the leveled entrance south of the smithy. Beside the entrance is a long wooden table with an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Labs.md) built in. On the table is a random [potion](Skyrim_Potions.md) or [poison](Skyrim_Poisons.md) and [dagger](Skyrim_Dagger.md), while hanging above are two bunches of dried [elves ear](Skyrim_Elves_Ear.md) and three bunches of dried [frost mirriam](Skyrim_Frost_Mirriam.md). To the right is a cupboard holding several [ruined books](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ruined_Book), two empty random [soul gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md), a sprig of [snowberries](Skyrim_Snowberries.md), a [thistle branch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thistle_Branch), a random potion of healing and a food sack. Beside the cupboard is an end table with a basket of [potatoes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potato) and a random pair of [gauntlets](Skyrim_Gauntlets.md) on top. Opposite the table is a dresser containing [clothes](Skyrim_Clothes.md) with a [long bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Long_Bow), a quiver of [iron arrows](Skyrim_Iron_Arrow.md) and a potion of minor stamina on top. There are a couple of barrels and sacks around the room and in the southwestern corner is a passage blocked by spears with an expert-locked cage on the wall, containing a button that activates the spears. The cage can be unlocked with the key taken from Ghunzul or [lockpicked](Skyrim_Lockpicking.md) without it. Beyond the spears is a flight of stairs down to a wooden door leading to *Cracked Tusk Vaults*.

### Cracked Tusk Vaults
There are no enemies within the vaults, but the vaults have suffered quite a bit of damage from cave-ins and there are still several traps that are armed and can cause damage to the unwary. There is a passage behind the door leading down to the southeast. In front of you is a counter with a random potion of healing and two empty random soul gems on top. Beside this is a basket containing a [gourd](Skyrim_Gourd.md). There are a number of food sacks and barrels of [ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md) throughout the vaults. At the bottom of the stairs is a room with spears blocking access. To the right is a button that lowers the spears. The room contains a dresser, cupboard and set of shelves holding a small coin purse, a random [mace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mace), an [iron dagger](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md), a copy of *[Lost Legends](Lore_Lost_Legends.md)*, a potion of minor healing and a random poison. There is also a broken table in the corner with a potion of minor stamina, a random potion, an empty [petty soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Petty_Soul_Gem) and a filled random soul gem among the remains. Slightly farther along the passage, on the left is another room, again with the spears and button. Inside this room is a set of shelves holding clutter with a [fox pelt](Skyrim_Fox_Pelt.md) on top and a random [shield](Skyrim_Shield.md) leaning against it, a wooden bench with a copy of the [Light Armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) skill book *[Rislav The Righteous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rislav_The_Righteous)* at one end and a random potion of healing at the other. There is an [iron battleaxe](Skyrim_Iron_Battleaxe.md) on top of a couple of barrels and beside these a cupboard holding a set of [iron armor](Skyrim_Iron_Armor.md), a random helmet and gauntlets. Farther along the passage opens up in front of double sets of spears blocking the passage both horizontally and vertically. On the left is an unlocked chest and an [iron helmet](Skyrim_Iron_Helmet.md) with a set of shelves that have been knocked over by a cave-in, knocking a random potion of magicka onto some more barrels. There are pillars with levers either side of the passage that activate the spears and giving access ahead and beyond these are three [tripwires](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Tripwire) in a row along the central passage. These trigger [dart traps](Skyrim_Dart_Trap.md) that are easily avoided. To the left is a fourth tripwire that triggers a [spike wall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spike_Wall) trap. To the right is an adept-locked chest. At the end of the passage is a plinth with the [shards of Mehrunes' Razor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shards_of_Mehrunes%27_Razor) sitting on a [weight-sensitive plate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Weight_Sensitive_Plate) that also triggers a dart trap. Standing to the left when taking them and jumping back allows you to avoid damage.

## Notes
- According to a [Development Map](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-map-Development_Map_02.png), this location was originally called **Gularz Khazgur**. Its presence on the map might also imply that Gularz Khazgur was the first [Orc stronghold](Skyrim_Orc_Strongholds.md) to be implemented in the game. - While the overall location was called Gularz Khazgur, it seems that the keep also still existed, which would've been called **Gularz Khazgur Keep**. A [key](Skyrim_Keys.md#Key_to_Gularz_Khazgur_Keep) to this location still exists in the files, but is unobtainable and not linked to any doors.
- The script for [Pieces of the Past](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pieces_of_the_Past) still refers to this location as "Gularz", and there is also a marker present called `Gularz Khazgur Center Marker REF` that can still be seen in the Creation Kit.
- The stronghold of [Mor Khazgur](Skyrim_Mor_Khazgur.md) might have gotten part of its name from Gularz Khazgur.
- In the final game, Cracked Tusk Keep still has various Orcish themes. It is inhabited exclusively by Orc hunters and bandits, multiple copies of *The Code of Malacath* can be found, and much of the architecture outside the fort resembles that which you would expect to find in an Orc stronghold, albeit without a longhouse. It's unknown if this layout was repurposed from the original vision of the stronghold, or if the designers added Orcish elements to the keep in reference to its former appearance.
- Ghunzul's ancestor, as mentioned in *[The Keepers of the Razor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Keepers_of_the_Razor)*, was named "Othmash gro-Gularz". It's also stated he had served time in the Imperial Legion. Stronghold Orcs who leave to serve in the Legion often take up their stronghold name during this time (as stated by [Ghorbash the Iron Hand](Skyrim_Ghorbash_the_Iron_Hand.md), who went by "Ghorbash gro-Dushnikh" during his service). This might have also been the case for Othmash, such that his last name relates to when the fort was known as Gularz Khazgur.
- The Orcs here will always be hostile, regardless of whether you are [Blood-Kin](Skyrim_Blood-Kin.md). In addition, killing the Orcs here won't cause Orcs in other locations to become hostile, as they don't belong to the same faction.
- There is an [iron ore vein](Skyrim_Iron_Ore_Vein.md) 150 feet due north of the keep.

## Bugs
- One of the Orcs here is using a generic bandit name, which is inconsistent with the others at this location. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.8, fixes this bug.
- If the quest [Amulet of the Moon](Skyrim_Amulet_of_the_Moon.md) directs you here, check to see if you have already visited this location. This location does not respawn, so if you have been here before, the Moon Amulet cannot spawn here and you cannot complete the quest. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.0, addresses this issue. The location will be forced to reset and anything stored here will be lost.

- Without the Unofficial Patch, you will need to reload a previous save to get a different location from [Kharjo](Skyrim_Kharjo.md).
- The map marker location data for Cracked Tusk Keep points to a random XMarker rather than to the map marker itself. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.2.3, fixes this bug.
- Everything outside the dungeon may respawn despite it being classed as a "no reset zone". <sup>**?**</sup>
