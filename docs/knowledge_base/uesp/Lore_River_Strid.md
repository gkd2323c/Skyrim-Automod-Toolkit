[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/OBR-place-Strid_River.jpg/200px-OBR-place-Strid_River.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Strid_River.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Strid_River.jpg) The Strid River circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) The Strid River is a large river that forms most of the boundary between [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). It rises in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) to the southeast of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) and flows west-southwest, bordering the [Strid Vale](Lore_Strid_Vale.md) and the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) before emptying into the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) south of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md). To the south, the river borders the Valenwood regions of [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md) and [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md). The city of [Arenthia](Lore_Arenthia.md) is located on the southern bank of the river.

The Strid is an important trade route to the interior of the continent, and has therefore occasionally been preyed upon by pirates.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GCGP1-1)</sup> The jungle-covered islands in its treacherous estuary provide ample locations for criminals to shelter out of the reach of the authorities, including Horseshoe Island and the infamous [Tempest Island](Lore_Tempest_Island.md), which features a warren of subterranean caverns.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoading-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

The ruined settlement of [Strid River Valley](Lore_Strid_River_Valley.md), located near the river, was named after the river itself.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRV-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRV2-5)</sup> The Richtons are a minor [Colovian](Lore_Colovian.md) noble family that lives near the Strid River.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RGBio-6)</sup> The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and Colovians often met trade in the annual Stridmeet Caravans. For the longest time, this was how the elves gained resources from outside the province until the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) was founded.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1-7)</sup>
