#### Namira
[Namira](Lore_Namira.md) is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose sphere is the ancient [Darkness](Lore_Void.md). She is also known as the **Daedric Prince of Hunger**, the **Mistress of Decay** or **Lady of Decay**, the **Spirit Daedra**, and the **Goddess of the Dark**. Namira is the ruler of sundry dark and shadowy spirits, and is often associated with [slugs](Lore_Slug.md), [spiders](Lore_Spider.md), and other things that inspire mortals with instinctive revulsion. The symbol of a [bat](Lore_Bat.md) is featured on her emblem. The Khajiit believe that all creatures who feed on rotten flesh are her spies. She is the Daedric Prince of spirits and shadows, as well as the patron of vermin and squalor. Namira also appears to be associated with beggars and the beggaring gifts of disease, pity, and disregard. Namira and her shadowy endeavors are often recognized to bear some association with eternity. Somewhat in line with this, the Khajiit believe that she is a spirit of infinite realms. Namira sees herself as the rightful ruler of all spirits. In some culture's creation stories, she is responsible for corrupting [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), or making a deal with him during his endeavor to create [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).

Mortals who become ensnared by this spirit are tortured until they forget who they were and know only Namira. Ancient Khajiit knew her as an **[Ur-dra](Lore_Ur-dra.md)**, a term also used to describe [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). It implies that the owner of the title is the "eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes". [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) is similarly called the Ur-Daedra who is "elder than Ada". Namira's followers and the priesthood of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) have clashed in the past. Namira counts [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md), the [Twilight Cantors](Lore_Twilight_Cantors.md), [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) and [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md) among her enemies. Her summoning day is [Marukh's Day](Lore_Marukh%27s_Day.md), the 9<sup>th</sup> of Second Seed.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Namira.md).

#### Nerevar
[Lord Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), also called **Saint Nerevar Moon-and-Star**, the **Champion of Azura**, the **Hero of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md)**, and **Godkiller**, was the **[Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) King** of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) (modern-day [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)). As the Hortator, or Great Ashkhan, of the united Chimer people, he forged an alliance with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), and he was one of the founders of the [First Council](Lore_First_Council.md), uniting all the people of Resdayn for the first time in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416). Nerevar then took on the title of "First Councilor". The alliance eventually broke down, leading to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md). At the climactic [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), Nerevar led the Chimer to victory, though he died shortly after the battle under disputed circumstances.

Under the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), he was known as the **Herald of the [Triune](Lore_Tribunal.md) Way**. [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) [prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) of his "rebirth" as the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) and heroic return to Morrowind fueled the creation of the [Nerevarine Cult](Lore_Nerevarine_Cult.md). St. **Nerevar the Captain**, patron of Warriors and Statesmen, became one of the foremost saints of the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith, along with his wife, [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) the Warden, Lord [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) the Magus, Lord [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) the Poet, and Saint [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) the Pilgrim. Nerevar became the patron saint of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md).

#### Nine Divines
Also known as the **Nine** or the **Nines**. The nine great faiths and [gods](Lore_Divines.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They are the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md): [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), [Mara](Lore_Mara.md), [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), and the once mortal god [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) (some stories maintain that Arkay was also once a mortal).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SATD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATG-2)</sup> Before the apotheosis of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) as Talos in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), it was the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), and the Empire would officially return to the worship of only the Eight Divines pursuant to the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) at the end of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTM-3)</sup>

#### Nir
[Nir](Lore_Nir.md) is the source of Creation as mentioned in the [Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md) creation myth. Anthropomorphized as feminine, she is said to have formed through the interaction of the primal forces of Stasis and Change, who are represented by [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md) respectively. Given this, Nir is understood to be a personification of the Possibility and Patterns of [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) itself.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TMF-5)</sup> In this story, while both are said to have fallen in love with Nir, she chose Anu over Padomay and became pregnant. When Nir rejected Padomay's confession of love he became enraged and attacked her. Afterwards, she gave birth to Creation but soon died from her injuries.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAA-6)</sup>

This can be compared and contrasted with [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md) in the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) creation myth who shares an equivalent place within their respective mythologies.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> According to *[Varieties of Faith in Tamriel](Lore_Varieties_of_Faith_in_Tamriel.md)*, the goddess [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) is sometimes associated with Nir.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOFIT-8)</sup> [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md), [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md)' daughter, was said to embody the beauty of Nir.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup>

#### Nirni
[Nirni](Lore_Nirni.md) is the personification of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) in [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) mythology and is considered the mother of all mortal life in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). She is one of the children of [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md)'s third litter, along with [Jone](Lore_Jone.md), [Jode](Lore_Jode.md), [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) and [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md). From birth, Nirni and Azurah were rivals and fought for their mother's favor, which led to one of Nirni's epithets as the **Jealous Sister**. This jealousy is used in some tales to explain the harshness of the Khajiit homeland, as a response to their status as Azurah's favored people. Other titles she is known by are **The Green Mother**, **Known Mother**, and the **Spirit of Harmony**.

On Fadomai's deathbed, she gifted Nirni with knowledge that she would become the mother of numerous children. In turn, it was Nirni who asked Lorkhaj to create a place so she could birth them. This resulted in Lorkhaj's trickery of the other spirits and his subsequent punishment. Various stories have romantically linked Nirni to both [Y'ffer](Lore_Y%27ffer.md) and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), with some stories claiming that the latter fathered the children that would be used to create the first Khajiit. Her relationship with Y'ffre depends on which legend is being told; in [Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md)'s texts Nirni was said to have chosen Y'ffer as her mate after he created the first [flower](Lore_Flora.md) for her. On the other hand, the stories attributed to [Clan Mother Ahnissi](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Clan_Mother_Ahnissi) show their relationship as something more platonic. After Y'ffre recounted Azurah's creation of the Khajiit from spirits to Nirni, she allowed him to alter some of her other children in the same manner, resulting in the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).

#### Nocturnal
[Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), or "Daedric Princess", whose sphere is the night, darkness, and mystery. She is also known as the **Lady of the Night**, the **Mistress of Shadows**, the **Unfathomable Mistress of Shadows**, **the Shadow Thief**, **the Unfathomable**, the **Empress of Murk**, the **Shadow Queen**, the **Shadowy Hag** (while donning her [cowl](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md)), the **Lady of the Twilight**, the **Daughter of Twilight**, the **Lady of Shadows**, the **Mistress of Mystery**, the **Mother of Shadows**, **Lady Luck**, the **Night Mistress**, the **Prince of Night and Darkness**, and the **Saint of Suspicion**.

The most prestigious title Nocturnal goes by is the title of **[Ur-dra](Lore_Ur-dra.md)**, which implies that she is the "eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes", and is a title which the [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) claims nearly all the Royalty of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) recognize, and which has also been used to address Nocturnal by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). The title was also used by ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) to describe [Namiira](Lore_Namiira.md), the Eldest Spirit. While this belief conflicts with Nocturnal's claim over the title, it does complement her claim that she is part of the original void, in that she came from the [black blood](Lore_Ebony.md) of the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md) of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md), which is a piece of "primal Void". Nocturnal herself claims that "before Oblivion, there was Nocturnal", and her followers believe she predates the current [reality](Lore_Aurbis.md) entirely. [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) is similarly called an Ur-Daedra who is "elder than Ada".

Nocturnal is said to be capable of shaping the night. She is associated with, and often depicted alongside, jet-black [ravens](Lore_Raven.md) and [crows](Lore_Crow.md), which are said to possess the ability to speak. Nocturnal's summoning day is the 3rd of Hearth Fire, although she may be summoned at any time at a shrine dedicated to her. Nocturnal's enemies are [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and [Azura](Lore_Azura.md).

#### Notorgo
[Notorgo](Lore_Notorgo.md) (or **Torgo**) is the Messenger God of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). He is mentioned in *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)* when Queen Aliera praises Notorgo for bringing her husband [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md) and son [Edward](Lore_Edward.md) to her safely.

Notorgo lends his name to spells such as Notorgo's Curse and Feet of Notorgo. He is often invoked to request fleetness in the delivery of a message.
