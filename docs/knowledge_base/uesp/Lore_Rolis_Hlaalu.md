*Trade makes the world go 'round, and in trade, reputation is everything, so maintaining House Hlaalu's reputation for reliability is critical to our continued pre-eminence in mercantile affairs.*
—Rolis Hlaalu<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM51-1)</sup> Rolis Hlaalu (also occasionally referred to simply by his initials, **R. H.**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Writ Holiday1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Writ Holiday3-3)</sup> was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) mastercraft mediator, trader, and merchant, renowned for delivering rare and exceptionally high-quality goods to an elite clientele.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RH-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FC-5)</sup> He was also a prominent member of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) and a published author.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM51-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/ON-npc-Faustina_Curio.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Faustina_Curio.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Faustina_Curio.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Faustina_Curio.jpg) Faustina Curio At some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Rolis Hlaalu authored a comprehensive guide to the armor and attire of House Hlaalu, emphasizing the importance of maintaining a proper reputation.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM51-1)</sup> Master Rolis was known to be exceptionally selective about his clientele, maintaining a very specific client list and expecting prospective customers to "start making waves" before earning his attention. He also took an apprentice, Faustina Curio, who also acted as his assistant.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FC-5)</sup> Faustina occasionally arranged private trade dealings, including one with the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) [Hag](Lore_Hag.md) Alyxe, which she deliberately kept concealed from Master Rolis to avoid scandalizing him.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Writ Holiday4-6)</sup> Both him and Faustina offered their wares in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), and [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ME-7)</sup>

He was known for arranging the delivery of highly specific, high-quality crafted goods, including fine jewelry made to exacting specifications,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Writ1-8)</sup> garments fashioned from cloth and leather,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Writ2-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Writ6-10)</sup> and custom plate armor designed for adventurers.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Writ8-11)</sup> His commissions also included particular [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md) glyphs for "academic purposes",<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Writ3-12)</sup> specialized weapons of wood and metal,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Writ4-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Writ5-14)</sup> some specifically intended for family duels,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Writ4-13)</sup> as well as shields ordered to "satisfy an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) parable".<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Writ7-15)</sup> Other requests ranged from discreetly supplied [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) goods, provided without questions,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Writ10-16)</sup> to elaborate dishes prepared for themed banquets.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Writ9-17)</sup>

Rolis Hlaalu also engaged in charitable work, arranging the provision of simple furnishings for disadvantaged children in [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Writ Holiday2-18)</sup> and sending food and emergency supplies to those living in [Skyrim's](Lore_Skyrim.md) coldest regions. He commissioned contractors to craft and deliver these goods to designated turn-in sites, while insisting that his efforts remain strictly confidential, as he did not view charity as a means to enhance his reputation.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Writ Holiday1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Writ Holiday3-3)</sup>

He was known to accept Writ Vouchers in exchange for his goods, which included wares such as [Frikkhild Coldheart](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Frikkhild_Coldheart) of [Windhelm's](Lore_Windhelm.md) texts on [crafting](Lore_Smithing.md) with [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-GTMW-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-CMES-20)</sup> a variety of furnishing plans, rare potions, and other items of note.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RH-4)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-GTMW-19)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Writ Vouchers-21)</sup>
