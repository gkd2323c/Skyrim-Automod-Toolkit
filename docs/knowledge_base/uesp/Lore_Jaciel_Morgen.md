*The shackles that bind me are far stronger than any mere magics. I shall remain here, alone. Let the Nocturnals come and find me stricken here. They will punish me, but they will punish the Dagon far more. There will be war in the heavens. The sky will crack, and the earth will split in two. All mortal things must perish in this howling storm of fire and night.*
—Jaciel Morgen (listen Link ⏵) Jaciel Morgen is a [Nocturnal Shrike](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) and lieutenant of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-1)</sup><sup>:129</sup> responsible for ruling over the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), a pocket realm of the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md). Despite being a powerful [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), she is prone to bouts of debilitating melancholy, as is commonly the case with her kind, especially over disruptions to her duties or threats to her most loyal servant, Deyanira Katrece, who serves as subaltern of Nocturnal.

At one time, Morgen allowed the clanless [Seducers](Lore_Seducer.md) refuge within the Shade Perilous for their service as retainers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lt J-2)</sup> In preparation for his invasion of [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) allied himself with the [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md), and many Seducers in the Shade Perilous duly entered into his service, betraying Morgen. The realm was subsequently invaded by the armies of Dagon. In their fervor to please Dagon, [Shardai Clan](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md) and [Xivilai's Clan](Lore_Xivilai%27s_Clan.md) conquered the realm rather than simply requesting passage, and the Shade Perilous was abandoned to be fought over between [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md) and [Frost Daedra](Lore_Frost_Daedra.md) where they captured Deyanira and continued hunting for Jaciel as well as any Seducers who remained in service to Nocturnal.

Amidst this horror, Morgen's sorrow and shame at the loss of her realm drove her into deep despair. She cast her spirit into the [Dreamsleeve](Lore_Dreamsleeve.md) and hid herself behind the Night Portal, which was subsequently sealed when the invaders stopped the flow of Nocturnal mana through the realm. Meanwhile Deyanira blamed herself for her mistress' downfall, having pled the Seducers' case.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lt J-2)</sup> Though she had been captured and imprisoned, Deyanira was freed by the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) from the Battlespire. After some reflection, she orders them to strike her with an enchanted dagger, banishing her to the [Void](Lore_Void.md) in a ritual suicide.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-1)</sup><sup>:129</sup> After her banishment, the Apprentice brought Morgen the dagger, and the rage caused by her servant's death was enough to break the Nocturnal from her melancholy.

Despite her initial anger, Morgen agreed to help the Apprentice pass through the realm and continue following Dagon's retreat. As vengeance for the invasion of her realm, Morgen freed the Shade Perilous and subsequently aided the [hero](Lore_Hero.md) in a final confrontation with Dagon at his [Hunting Lodge](Lore_Dagon%27s_Hunting_Lodge.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSJM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BSFire-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BSFrost-5)</sup> where she created illusory images of herself to distract the Prince long enough for the Apprentice to strike him with the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](Lore_Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver.md) and banish him to the Void.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-1)</sup><sup>:179</sup>
