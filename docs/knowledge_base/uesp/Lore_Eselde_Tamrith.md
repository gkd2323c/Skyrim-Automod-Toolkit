Eselde Tamrith was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noblewoman who reigned as the Countess of [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Following the death of her father, Baron [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md), in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Eselde succeeded him as the head of House Tamrith and served as a member of the triumvirate Council of the North, which governed [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) after [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EOd-3)</sup> In 2E 582, Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md)'s representative on the Council of the North, initiated a civil war in Rivenspire so he could become King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HRP-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BOR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lf LL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup> During the civil war, Eselde and House Tamrith allied with [Houses Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) and [Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) to prevent House Montclair from conquering Rivenspire. Ultimately, Countess Tamrith played a crucial role in defeating House Montclair and killing Baron Montclair at the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Rivenspire-8)</sup>

## Biography
Eselde Tamrith was the eldest child of Baron [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md) and his wife, Janece.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> She had a younger sister named Janeve.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Janeve-10)</sup> Eselde took after Janece, while Janeve took after Esmark.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CVRd-11)</sup> As a young girl, Eselde was frightened of the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md) in northern [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DLot D-12)</sup>

In [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), Eselde left Rivenspire to further her education and religious studies in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> For the majority of her time in Stormhaven, she was a guest of High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md)'s court and reportedly gained his favor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup> Eselde excelled in her studies, taking a particular interest in history, politics, diplomatic studies, and theology.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> She studied with the [Order of Arkay](Lore_Order_of_Arkay.md), demonstrating a deep conviction in the teachings of the God of Life and Death and the Way of the Light.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSo PE-13)</sup> She also surpassed her peers as both a healer and a champion in riddle contests.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> She became extremely pious and a devout follower of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup>

During her time in Stormhaven, [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) produced and distributed a piece of thinly veiled propaganda titled "*[The Story of Princess Eselde](Lore_The_Story_of_Princess_Eselde.md)*". According to the narrative of *The Story of Princess Eselde*, Eselde was the daughter of the long-lost twin sister of King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) (known in this work as Lancer), which would also make her a granddaughter of King [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) (known in this work as Rulthrub). This propaganda was likely created to provide Eselde with a claim to the vacant throne of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSo PE-13)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), her father, Baron Tamrith, died of natural causes, so Eselde returned to Rivenspire to assume her responsibilities as the Countess of House Tamrith.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> As Countess of House Tamrith, she took her father's place as a member of the triumvirate Council of the North, which was composed of Houses Tamrith, [Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md), and [Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and governed Rivenspire after [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EOd-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-14)</sup> Countess Tamrith and Baron [Alard Dorell](Lore_Alard_Dorell.md), who was House Dorell's representative on the triumvirate, had constant disagreements as members of the Council of North.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> Tamrith considered Dorell rash, pompous, and overly militant, while he perceived her as too young, inexperienced, and hesitant.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BADd-15)</sup>

In 2E 582, Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), House Montclair's representative on the Council of the North, initiated a civil war to take over Rivenspire following the death of his wife and his transformation into a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) due to the Lightless Remnant of the Doomcrag.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HRP-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BOR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lf LL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup> House Montclair also planned to assassinate Countess Tamrith.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lo T-16)</sup> Baron Montclair's court magician Reezal-Jul and the House Montclair forces under his command captured Shornhelm's upper city,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LTRJ-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rf RJ-18)</sup> aided by treachery from two of the city's guard captains.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BADd-15)</sup> Bickering between Countess Tamrith and Baron Dorell hindered their efforts to retake the upper city from House Montclair's forces.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BADd-15)</sup> After capturing the upper city, Montclair demanded that Tamrith and Dorell surrender and crown him king, a demand that they rejected.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BADd-15)</sup>

Following House Montclair's capture of Shornhelm's upper city, Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), a vampire and former friend of Baron Montclair, attempted to assist Countess Tamrith and Baron Dorell, but they did not trust him.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CVRd-11)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BADd-15)</sup> After the upper city was seized, Countess Tamrith sent Janeve and some members of the Shornhelm Guard to investigate rumors in western Rivenspire, which displeased Baron Dorell.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CETd-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Janeve-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CVRd-11)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BADd-15)</sup> An agent of High King Emeric eventually arrived in Shornhelm to assist Tamrith and Dorell in retaking the upper city. Tamrith convinced the agent to enter the upper city and attempt to find evidence regarding why Baron Montclair had started the civil war. While the agent was rescuing three captured nobles in the Fevered Mews, a cave system in the hillside behind Shornhelm, Emeric arrived, and the forces of House Tamrith, House Dorell, and the Shornhelm Guard retook the upper city from House Montclair, although Reezal-Jul escaped.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SD-19)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-quest-The_Blood-Splattered_Shield_05.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Blood-Splattered_Shield_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Blood-Splattered_Shield_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Blood-Splattered_Shield_05.jpg) Tamrith fighting Reezal-Jul After Shornhelm's upper city was retaken, Countess Tamrith traveled to Camp Tamrith, a refugee camp for the residents of Crestshade that had been established by Janeve following the town's devastation by Reezal-Jul. House Montclair eventually attacked the camp, and Tamrith and the agent tracked Reezal-Jul to nearby Shadowfate Cavern, where they killed him. However, Reezal-Jul infected Janeve with vampirism before his death. Countess Tamrith wished to kill Janeve before she became a vampire, but she was also given the option of joining the vampires of [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md). It is unknown whether Janeve was ultimately killed or spared.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBSS-20)</sup>

After killing Reezal-Jul, Countess Tamrith traveled to [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) to assist Baron Dorell and his forces in retaking the city from House Montclair, led by Lady Lleraya Montclair.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Ni P-21)</sup> Tamrith was part of the small team, which included Emeric's agent and Baron Dorell, that infiltrated Northpoint, killed Lleraya, and liberated the city.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLo N-22)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Lightless_Remnant_03.jpg) Countess Tamrith trapped in a blood ward Following the liberation of Northpoint, the forces of Houses Tamrith, Dorell, and Ravenwatch traveled to the Shrouded Pass to confront Baron Montclair at the Doomcrag.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Pot P-23)</sup> After battling through the Shrouded Pass and establishing a base near the top of the Doomcrag, Count Ravenwatch attempted to dissuade Countess Tamrith and Baron Dorell from joining him in the final assault, but they refused to listen to him.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Pot P-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TLR-24)</sup> Tamrith, Dorell, Emeric's agent, Count Ravenwatch, and the Count's protégé, Gwendis, then ascended the Doomcrag and confronted Montclair. However, Montclair captured Tamrith, Dorell, and Gwendis, and imprisoned them within a blood ward, but Verandis and the agent managed to release them.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TLR-24)</sup> While Tamrith and Dorell descended the Doomcrag, the agent fought and killed Montclair, and Verandis made a deal with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to transport himself and the Lightless Remnant to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) so it would not destroy Rivenspire.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CVRd-11)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TLR-24)</sup>

After the defeat of House Montclair, a gathering took place at Shornhelm Castle to celebrate and select Shornhelm's new monarch. However, it is unknown whether Emeric, who based his decision on the recommendation of the agent, selected Countess Tamrith or Baron Dorell as the new ruler of Shornhelm.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TCo S-25)</sup>
