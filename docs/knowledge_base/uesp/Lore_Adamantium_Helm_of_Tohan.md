# Adamantium Helm of Tohan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1c/MW-quest-Helm_of_Tohan.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Helm_of_Tohan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Helm_of_Tohan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Helm_of_Tohan.jpg) The Helm of Tohan The Adamantium Helm of Tohan is a legendary [Adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). The helmet is medium weight, while still being very durable and offering superior protection. It has an immensely high potential for [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho T-1)</sup> Nothing is known of Tohan or the helm's origins.

The helm came to rest in the ruins of Onnissiralis, a shrine to [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) located on a small island in the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), far west of [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md). It was kept in a tightly locked chest, openable only by key. In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) man by the name of Norvayne attempted to cleanse the ruins, but was slain by the Daedra worshippers. Years later, in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), his sons, Daris and Dalin Norvayne, set out to avenge their father and cleanse the ruins. Following rumors of the Helm of Tohan, the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) arrived at Onnissiralis. The brothers disagreed on whether to accept the Nerevarine's aid or not. It is unknown how the events unfolded, but the ruins were cleansed of worshippers. The brothers handed the Nerevarine the key to the chest which contained the helm, and it was taken from the ruins.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho T-1)</sup>

## Notes
- The helm was featured in the *[Helm of Tohan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Helm_of_Tohan)* official plugin for [*Morrowind*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind).
- According to [Douglas Goodall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Douglas_Goodall), the helm is named after [Tohan Kim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Tohan_Kim), who is [credited](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Credits) under Character and Creature Art for *Morrowind*. Goodall stated that in-universe, a Tohan "could and should be a lore figure".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-2)</sup>
