# Soul

This article is about NPCs. For the creature of the same name, see [Soul (cow)](Skyrim_Soul_(cow).md). For souls trapped in soul gems, see [Souls](Skyrim_Souls.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/SR-npc-Soul_17.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Soul_17.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Soul_17.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Soul_17.jpg) Four souls praying The **souls** of mortals are captured in black [soul gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md) or obtained by the [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md) through deceit. They are doomed to spend the rest of eternity in the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md). They ignore all combat, as they are invulnerable anyway, and prefer to remain in one place rather than wander around. The game treats them as [undead](Skyrim_Undead.md) ghosts with [morality](Skyrim_Morality.md) of `No Crime` and [aggression](Skyrim_Aggression.md) of `Unaggressive`.

## Related Quests
- **[Soul Cairn Horse Quest](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn_Horse_Quest.md)**: Find [Arvak](Skyrim_Arvak.md)'s skull.

## Notes
- They are all members of [Soul Cairn Soul Faction](Skyrim_SoulCairnSoulFaction.md), except for [xx002B0D](#xx002b0d) and [xx0150B6](#xx0150b6), which are members of [Soul Cairn Special Faction](Skyrim_SoulCairnSpecialFaction.md).
- There are initially 39 souls (excluding named ones), 19 of which may later disappear from the game due to various quests or events.
- Their spectral appearance differs from that of most other ghosts.
- They cannot be detected by the [Detect Dead](Skyrim_Detect_Dead.md) spell or the [Aura Whisper](Skyrim_Aura_Whisper.md) shout.
- There are a few named souls, such as [Jiub](Skyrim_Jiub.md) and [Morven Stroud](Skyrim_Morven_Stroud.md).

### Quest-specific
The [Redguard soul](#xx002b0d) will force you into dialogue with a request: *"You must help me find my Arvak. He doesn't deserve to be in a place like this!"* After telling him to calm down and asking who Arvak is, he will explain, *"Arvak. My horse. We came to this horrible place together. We were attacked by monsters, so I told him to run,"* and will plead: *"Please, he's such a loyal creature, and he's been running for so long. You have to save him! A place like this will change you..."* If you ask him how you can help Arvak, he will leave your question unanswered and call, *"Arvak! Arvak, where are you? Arvak, please come back! Come back!"*

When you retrieve Arvak's skull, he will appear once more and thank you: *"Arvak...You saved him! His soul is free, I can feel it! He's such a loyal beast. Here, I'll teach you how to call him to you. He'll help you get around this wretched place, but I'm sure he'd be much happier someplace sunnier."* He will then bid you farewell: *"Goodbye, hero! Take good care of Arvak for me! Such a good horse..."*
