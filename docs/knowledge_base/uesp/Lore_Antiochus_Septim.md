Antiochus Septim (sometimes enumerated as **Antiochus I**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MOP-1)</sup> was the seventh [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for twenty-one years, from [3E 99](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_99) to [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120). His reign was plagued by civil wars and disgruntlement among the kings and queens of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The most notable event of his reign occurred in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), when the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) nearly conquered the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md), but a freak storm enabled the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md) and royal navies of the Summerset Isles to defeat the [Pyandonean](Lore_Pyandonea.md) Armada. He was preceded by his father, [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md), and was succeeded by his daughter, [Kintyra II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup>

Despite his personal "moral laxity" and disgruntlement among some of the provincial royalty, he was generally considered an able and popular leader by the end of his reign.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup>

## Biography
Antiochus Septim was born in [3E 51](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_51) to Prince [Pelagius Septim](Lore_Pelagius_II.md) (later Emperor Pelagius II) and his first wife.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ1-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ3-6)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_birth)</sup> He had three siblings: [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ3-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup> By the time he was sixteen, he had the reputation of a drunkard and womanizer, which made him infamous in the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup> Antiochus was one of the most flamboyant members of the usually austere [Septim family](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> He was renowned for his grandness of dress and his good sense of humor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup>

On 15 Evening Star, [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98), Pelagius II died, and Antiochus attended his funeral in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDC-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ3-6)</sup> As the eldest child of Pelagius II, Antiochus was the heir presumptive to the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ3-6)</sup> The [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), however, delayed Antiochus' coronation for at least six weeks, presumably due to their aversion to his father.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ3-6)</sup> Finally, in [3E 99](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_99), Antiochus was crowned Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTET-9)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Coronation)</sup>

Antiochus had many wives and many mistresses.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> With his wife, Gysilla, he fathered his daughter and heir, [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md), in [3E 104](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_104).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_Wife)</sup> He also had several other children.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup>

By [3E 109](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_109), many of the kings and queens of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) had become disgruntled with Antiochus' reign.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup> Antiochus had also impressed his subjects with little but the enormity of his lust for carnal pleasures.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup> During the first ten years of his reign, he became quite fat and contracted many venereal [diseases](Lore_Disease.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup>

Antiochus' reign was rife with civil wars, surpassing even the number of wars that occurred during the reign of his grandfather, [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_II.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> In [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) was nearly lost to the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) in the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDC-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3S-10)</sup> The united alliance of the royal navies of the Summerset Isles and the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md) only managed to defeat the [Pyandonean](Lore_Pyandonea.md) Armada, commanded by [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md), King of the Maormer, due to a freak storm.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup> Legend credits the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) with the magic behind the storm.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3S-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup> The destruction of the Pyandonean Armada was the Emperor's greatest victory.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup>

By [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119), his siblings King Magnus, King Cephorus of [Gilane](Lore_Gilane.md), and Potema, the Wolf Queen of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), had all ruled well, and relations between the Empire and the kingdoms of Tamriel were much improved. However, relations between the Empire and the kings of High Rock and Skyrim remained strained.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup> In 3E 119, during a visit from Potema and his nephew [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_III.md), Antiochus lapsed into a coma.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup> For months, he lingered in between life and death while the Elder Council prepared for the accession of Kintyra to the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup> In [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120), Antiochus died and was succeeded by Kintyra.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ5-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MOP-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_Death)</sup>

## Notes
- | [1](#note_birth) | Sources give different years for Antiochus' birth. *[Biography of the Wolf Queen](Lore_Biography_of_the_Wolf_Queen.md)* states that Antiochus was 16 years old in 3E 67 and 48 years old at the time of his coronation in 3E 99, putting his birth year in 3E 51. However, *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v3.md)* states that he was 43 years old in late 3E 98, putting his birth year in 3E 55. 3E 51 is most likely the correct date, as *The Wolf Queen* directly contradicts itself by stating that Antiochus was eleven years old in the autumntide of 3E 63, putting his birth year in 3E 51 or 3E 52. Additionally, *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v1.md)* states that Pelagius had been a widower for eleven years by 3E 63, putting the death of Antiochus' mother in 3E 51 or 3E 52. |
| --- | --- |
- | [2](#note_Coronation) | *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v4.md)*, *[The Third Era Timeline](Lore_The_Third_Era_Timeline.md)*, and *Biography of the Wolf Queen* state that Antiochus was crowned in 3E 99. *[The Daggerfall Chronicles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles)* states that he was crowned in 3E 98. And *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire_v_1)* states Antiochus assumed the throne twelve years prior to 3E 110, which also places his accession in 3E 98. |
| --- | --- |
- | [3](#note_Wife) | *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v5.md)* states that the name of Antiochus' wife was Gysilla, while *Biography of the Wolf Queen* states that her name was Magna. |
| --- | --- |
- | [4](#note_Death) | *[The Wolf Queen, v5](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v5.md)* states that Antiochus fell into a coma in [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119) and that his funeral was in [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120). *[The Madness of Pelagius](Lore_The_Madness_of_Pelagius.md)* states that Antiochus died when Pelagius was just over a year old (Pelagius was born in 3E 119), which places Antiochus' death in 3E 120. Both books are contradicted by the *Biography of the Wolf Queen*, which explicitly places his death in [3E 112](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_112). |
