*Well, I have had two hundred years' more practice!*
—Dram **Dram** was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) lieutenant who served as Tiber Septim's personal Imperial Emissary during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGArchive-1)</sup> He was provided in the service of [Admiral Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md), who had tasked him with eliminating any resistance in [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) from the [Restless League](Lore_Restless_League.md) and other [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), including [Cyrus the Redguard](Lore_Cyrus.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Redguard-2)</sup>

Dram's ancestors were distinguished [Nightblades](Lore_Nightblades.md) in the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md). Dram himself was also a member of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), though at an unknown date, he was exiled from Morrowind. According to one Morag Tong agent, his whole House fell after trafficking in the affairs of the Cyrodiils.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-RGForum Madness-3)</sup> Dram continued the Morag Tong's worship of [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), even after his exile,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGArchive-1)</sup> and according to some sources the Morag Tong was known to employ simulacrums of Dram to carry out certain tasks.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TXGL-4)</sup>

There were many rumors surrounding the mysterious assassin and the origin of his unwavering loyalty to [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), with some saying that Dram had been secretly executed by [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) in [2E 800](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_800), "[incarnating](Lore_Reincarnation.md)" for the third time afterwards, and invoking the wrath of the Morag Tong against House Indoril. Others said that Dram had been a companion of Tiber Septim since he had first taken the Cyrodilic Crown, and furthermore that he was the apprentice of [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGArchive-1)</sup>

Dram was in possession of the legendary [Bow of Shadows](Lore_Bow_of_Shadows.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-6)</sup> He used the Bow during the [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md), where he shot [Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md) with the poisoned arrow that severed his flesh, but his soul survived.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AJ-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Dram also made use of the bow during the rebellion of Stros M'Kai, most notably in battle against Cyrus the Redguard.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Redguard-2)</sup>

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Dram was stationed in Stros M'Kai under Lord Amiel Ricton, where he worked as his bodyguard and spy. When Cyrus had killed the Imperial guards in the Imperial Palace, Richton had ordered for Cyrus's execution, but Dram stayed his hand to interrogate Cyrus first. He later killed [Joto](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Joto), a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) who had been helping Cyrus. He proceeded to use the opportunity to attack Cyrus, but he used a recall spell to escape after being defeated. Dram then proceeded to find the Restless League's hideout. He fought Cyrus once more on the boarding platform of Richton's [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) airship, with Cyrus being victorious due to his use of the [Soul Sword](Lore_Soul_Sword.md) containing Prince A'tor's spirit. Dram retreated, but then snuck up behind Cyrus and held a knife to his throat. To his shock, the Soul Sword rose up and chopped him in half.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Redguard-2)</sup>

## Notes
- During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) copies of the artifact book written by Yagrum Bagarn mentioned Dram, placing his birth earlier than the understood date of [2E 612](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_612).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TA-9)</sup> However, given this timeline discrepancy it is possible this book circulating at this time is an anomaly, perhaps tied to the [Gandranen Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gandranen_Ruins) phenomenon.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONGRL-10)</sup>
- Dram is based off of [Boba Fett](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boba_Fett) from the *Star Wars* franchise.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
- One Morag Tong agent mentioned a fallen "House Dram" during conversations surrounding the events of the Stros M'kai rebellion. During the conversation, one [Morag Sultha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) threatens House Indoval with assassination due to their becoming too mannish and abandoning traditional Dunmeri culture, stating that *"The mer we knew as Indoval Ros is now in the business of producing men. Their House should fall as quickly as House Dram's, when lo those heirs trafficked in the affairs of the Cyrodiils."*<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-RGForum Madness-3)</sup> It is unclear whether House Dram refers to Dram's literal house, or the idea that Dram is multiple simulacra created by the Morag Tong.
