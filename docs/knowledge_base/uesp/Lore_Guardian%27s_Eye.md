# Guardian's Eye

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-item-Guardian%27s_Eye.jpg/200px-ON-item-Guardian%27s_Eye.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Guardian%27s_Eye.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Guardian%27s_Eye.jpg) The Guardian's Eye The Guardian's Eye is an ancient artifact of immense power, found within the lost [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city of [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md). It is of great mystery, and its true nature is unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJ-2)</sup> A number of halls within the city are mysteriously named in connection to the "Guardian" including the Guardian's Helm, the Guardian's Skull, and the Guardian's Orbit. The exact meaning of the names is unclear, though it is suggested that the transition between "helm" and "skull" denotes vulnerability.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Volenfell-3)</sup> The Eye is of great repute and worth a small fortune, and as such researchers such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s leading archeologists are willing to do anything to get it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/ON-quest-Blood_and_Sand.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Blood_and_Sand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Blood_and_Sand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Blood_and_Sand.jpg) Tharayya triggers the Guardian's Eye's defense mechanism before it disappears The origins of the Guardian's Eye are unknown, though it is theorized to be a Dwemer artifact. Old Dwemer logs indicated that the Eye was briefly being worked on (likely for maintenance or cleaning) within [Santaki](Lore_Santaki.md) at some point after [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420). It was then transported to the Dwemer mine of Aldunz for a brief respite, before being moved to Yldzuun, and afterwards made its final trip to the city of Volenfell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJ-2)</sup>

The Eye's Chamber was constructed alongside the Hall of [Volendrung](Lore_Volendrung.md), the site of where the [Rourken King](Lore_Rourken_(person).md)'s [hammer fell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), to serve as the Eye's final resting place.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJ-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Volenfell-3)</sup> A number of safeguards consisting of traps, [automatons](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md), and even [undead](Lore_Undead.md) were set up to safeguard the Eye. The Guardian Council, consisting of three [Dwarven Centurions](Lore_Dwarven_Centurion.md), were built to guard the Eye.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ba S-4)</sup> The Dwemer [disappeared](Lore_Disappearance_of_the_Dwarves.md) in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), and the Eye was sealed there ever since. Mentions of the Guardian's Eye were found in old [Dwemer inscriptions](Lore_Dwemeris.md) within various ruins of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), and for centuries Dwemer researchers attempted to find the Eye to no avail.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJ-2)</sup>

For many months in [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581), the researcher Tharayya journeyed through the ruins of the Alik'r and found leads in Yldzuun that placed the Eye's location as being within Volenfell. She and her ex-husband Quintus had been developing competing studies on the Dwemer until he poisoned her, stole her journals and notes, and left for Volenfell to find the Eye first.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJ-2)</sup> Tharayya brought a number of guards and was accompanied by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) through the ruins, killing Quintus, entering the Eye's Chamber, and defeated the Guardian Council. Tharayya attempted to carefully dislodge it, but it began to spark and released a wave of energy that pushed the expedition team back before vanishing in a blinding light. A swarm of undead made up of [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md) and [mummies](Lore_Mummy.md) arose from the ground, forcing the team to flee. Tharayya theorized that the Eye awoke the dead, and was unsure if it was destroyed as the Dwemer magic likely caused it to vanish to someplace else. Tharayya was unable to find any leads, and could not solve the mystery of the Eye or its relationship to the undead that overran Volenfell. The [authorities](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) were alerted, but curiously the undead seemed content to remain within the confines of the ruins, and the authorities opted to leave them there.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ba S-4)</sup>

## Notes
- In early drafts the artifact was called the Resplendent Pearl of Zeht, which legends said could bring an endless supply of clean pouring water. [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) sought control it so that his leadership would go unopposed, and sent Vizier Arzedram to retrieve it from Volenfell.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ba S-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-CC-5)</sup> It was ultimately cut and renamed to the Guardian's Eye, but mentions of it remained in [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md) mythology, which posits that the Pearl is the Way and the Guide to the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md) and back again. In creation myth, [Tall Papa](Lore_Tall_Papa.md) created the stars of the [Great Map](Lore_Mundus.md), one of which [Sep](Lore_Sep.md) put in his pocket when he tricked the [other spirits](Lore_Aedra.md), and it fell out when Tall Papa crushed Sep with his staff. Sand covered the Pearl, and it came to [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md), a god who works the land. When the Pearl is uncovered, the time of Sep's Hunger will be over and water from the Pearl will run over the skins, so the Hungry Stomach will [at last be full](Lore_Kalpa.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOHayazzin-6)</sup>
- There were plans to elaborate on the mysteries of the Guardian's Eye in Veteran Volenfell II, but as sequel stories to base game dungeons were scrapped, the nature of the Eye was never expanded on.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-CC-5)</sup>
