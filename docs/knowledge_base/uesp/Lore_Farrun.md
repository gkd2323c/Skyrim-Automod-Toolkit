—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Farrun (or **Fharun**) is a northern port city on the north shore of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AFDOA-2)</sup> and one of eight kingdoms in the province.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> It is known for its icy winters.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup> The kingdom of Farrun has existed since the First Era; one notable citizen of Farrun in the First Era was Lord Storig, who commanded the vanguard of the [Imperial army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) at the Battle of Bodrum in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSV3-5)</sup> By the Second Era it was an Orcish stronghold by the name of Fharun, named after the [Fharun Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fharun_Clan). The stronghold was infamous for its [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) -designed prison complex, which was notorious even among the Orcs.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Fharun was also home to [Agra Crun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Agra_Crun), a religious order devoted to [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath), who protected a sacred scroll that supposedly contained the words of Malacath himself. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the clan was led by Chief [Bazrag gro-Fharun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bazrag_gro-Fharun), a Malacath worshipper. Bazrag refused to bend the knee to the [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) -worshipping King [Kurog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog), despite being an old friend of the king. Because of this, Fharun did not join the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567) and didn't take part in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESO-7)</sup>

Following the end of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), Kurog began a concerted effort to rebuild his seat of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) and unite the whole of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) under his rule. Bazrag and several other clan chiefs resisted, and Kurog enlisted the aid of the [Vosh Rakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vosh_Rakh) to assassinate the chiefs. Fharun was seized by the Vosh Rakh, who threw their captives into the prison and released the prisoners to kill them. Bazrag was rescued from such a fate by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), and returned to Orsinium to foil Kurog's assassination attempt. Following the death of Kurog, the surviving chiefs appointed Bazrag as king, uniting Wrothgar and ending the worship of Trinimac. The Vosh Rakh fled from Fharun Stronghold, although it is unknown if the settlement was reclaimed by the remaining clan members.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESO-7)</sup>

Kurog's Orsinium eventually fell, and at some point Fharun became the Breton city-state of Farrun. In 3E 120, Farrun was ruled by King Lleromo, who the Wolf Queen of Solitude, [Potema Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Potema_Septim), judged to be a difficult ally to keep.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ5-8)</sup>

An unnamed king was known to have fought a war against [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) and its allies some time late in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Farrun was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Tristyrick, and it had a rivalry with [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup> Farrun was neighbored by several settlements, including [Cloud Spring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Spring) to the southeast, [Dunlain Falls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dunlain_Falls) to the south, and Jehanna to the east.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Map Arena-10)</sup>
