# House Tamrith

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/ON-npc-Countess_Eselde_Tamrith.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Countess_Eselde_Tamrith.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Countess_Eselde_Tamrith.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Countess_Eselde_Tamrith.jpg) Countess Eselde Tamrith House Tamrith is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble family from [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). They have holdings in the western half of the region and strong trading ties with [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). Their main business interests are agriculture, mercantiles, and commerce. The house is noted for its strong tradition of piety and religious commitment, with its favored deity being [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTa RH-1)</sup> This is reflected in the house's motto, "By Arkay's grace we live and die, but there is no end to the Tamrith line."<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

## History
House Tamrith, along with the other prominent noble houses of Rivenspire, established its fortune and reputation during the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), helping the region prosper.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTa RH-1)</sup>

Despite their devoutness, House Tamrith had a history of dealings with [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). At one point, a minor noble of the house engaged in a trade with Madam Whim of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md). In exchange for a small chalet in Rivenspire, Madam Whim granted the noble a title and hereditary rights.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LAMAA-3)</sup>

Later, in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the future king of Wayrest, [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), spent part of his youth in the company of the baron of the house, [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md). Despite this and their strong ties with Wayrest, House Tamrith took the side of [King Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) at the onset of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in 2E 566 and fielded its troops against Emeric and Wayrest. However, as the war turned against Ranser, the house withdrew its support of him and sued for peace, becoming the first Ranser ally to do so. They later declared that they were forced into supporting his side of the conflict.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTa RH-1)</sup>

After the war's end, Esmark emerged as a force for peace and cooperation. He is credited with proposing the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North, which governed Rivenspire following Ranser's death. Subsequently, the baron married Janece Elde, combining the fortunes of House Tamrith and House Elde and creating a more formidable political entity. The couple had two daughters, [Eselde](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md) and Janeve. When Esmark died of natural causes, leadership of the house fell to Eselde.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTa RH-1)</sup> Under her leadership, House Tamrith fought against [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) during its attempt to take over Rivenspire.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>
