[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/MW-place-Ashlands.jpg/200px-MW-place-Ashlands.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ashlands.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ashlands.jpg) The Ashlands of Vvardenfell [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/ON-place-Ashlands.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ashlands.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ashlands.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ashlands.jpg) The Ashlands circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Ashlands region was the largest contiguous geographical region on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) —a dry, inhospitable wasteland characterized by clusters of stone obelisks; minimal, highly-adapted vegetation; and bubbling ash mires. It encompassed land from the [Urshilaku Camp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urshilaku_Camp) on the northern shore of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), west to the [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) Council seat [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), then south to where the slopes of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) leveled off into the lava fields of [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> The southern border ran on an approximate line from [Foyada Mamaea](Lore_Foyada_Mamaea.md), near [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), to the southern border of the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md).

The mountainous region to the southeast of Molag Amur was sometimes considered part of the Ashlands because of the similarity of its ecology, but was generally flatter, with open lava flows. Also, the slopes of Red Mountain itself were a separate region, bound by the [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md). Frequent ash storms limited visibility and spread [the Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md), affecting almost every creature found in the region.

Only the western part of the Ashlands was under permanent control of House Redoran. The only large, permemnant settlements were [Maar Gan](Lore_Maar_Gan.md) and Ald'ruhn, though some considered Ald'ruhn to be just on the margins rather than part of the Ashlands itself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> The shrine in Maar Gan was an important Temple pilgrimage site, and the village's houses volunteered militia for the fight against the forces of [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md). The nomadic [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribe of the [Urshilaku](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md) hunted for game, foraged, and their herds found sparse grazing around ragged tent villages in the far northwest,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> but there were small Ashlander camps throughout the area outside the Ghostfence. The rest of the region was embattled with infractions of rogue Telvanni, bandits, and even vampires trying to gain a foothold. The interior of the Ashlands was thus largely devoid of permanent, occupied settlements, with the citadel of [Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md) being the major exception. This was due to the lack of water and vegetation in the area, as it rarely ever rained. Scattered throughout the land were various caves, eggmines, [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) and [Dwemer ruins](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md), and wizards' towers.

The Ashlands were presumably heavily impacted by the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) due to the eruption of Red Mountain.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRY-4)</sup>
