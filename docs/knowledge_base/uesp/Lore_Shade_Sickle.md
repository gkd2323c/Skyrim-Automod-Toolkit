# Shade Sickle

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg/200px-ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg) Shade Sickle The Shade Sickle is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) -made [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) with the ability to separate the living from their [shadows](Lore_Shadow.md). This leaves its victims fated to gradually become hapless beings akin to thralls.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ND-1)</sup> Though mortal in origin, it is now a daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), who was given the weapon at one point after its potential was realized. Nocturnal considers it a powerful instrument of her will.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SON-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESON-3)</sup> Despite its common appearance, the blade is preternaturally sharp, and those who gaze at it experience a deep sense of foreboding.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-QID-4)</sup>

## History
At some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Breton artificers crafted the Shade Sickle. Eventually, one of the Shade Sickle's owners realized its power, and attempted to hide it by offering it at the Shrine of Nocturnal in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) under the guise of a gift.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ND-1)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the artifact was stolen by two thieves, Darene Gavendier and Giovanni Corvus. They were hired by a powerful necromancer, Tumma-Maxath, who coveted the power of the Shade Sickle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SON-2)</sup> He needed the artifact to complete his ritual in his lair in Bloodrun Cave.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DG-5)</sup> [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) requested [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to retrieve the sickle to her shrine. They ventured to Bloodrun Cave in northeastern [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) and took the artifact from the altar in the necromancer's lair.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SON-2)</sup> The Vestige returned the artifact to the shrine of Nocturnal. Her follower, Millona Brolus, intended to hide the artifact in a more secure place than the shrine. According to Nocturnal's faithful, the artifact should never be used in necromantic rituals.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MB-6)</sup>
