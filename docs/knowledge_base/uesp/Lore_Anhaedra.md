## History
Little is known of Anhaedra's past. He was the subject of a reenactment between a conflict between Vivec and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) that would become his eternal plight. Before the construction of [Maar Gan](Lore_Maar_Gan.md), and the village's temple, Vivec had taunted Mehrunes Dagon so that he would throw a rock at him, rather than his people. The site would then become a pilgrim spot and the location of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) in Maar Gan. The rock that Mehrunes Dagon had hurled would be known as the "Magic Rock of Maar Gan", forever guarded by Anhaedra. Pilgrims would re-create the situation during their pilgrimage. Anhaedra, assuming the role of Dagon, would have to endure taunts from pilgrims and try to harm them while they are protected by a powerful blessing.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWPilgrimage-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MROMG-5)</sup> Whenever Anhaedra was slain his body reformed within days in the Maar Gan's temple.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Anhaedra-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tuls_Valen-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Tralas_Rendas-7)</sup>

As of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Anhaedra was known to have become unusually short-tempered.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AB-8)</sup> Sometime after the disappearance of the Tribunal and the dissolution of the Tribunal Temple, Anhaedra was freed from his bonds. He was encountered again around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), when the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md) banished him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Blades-2)</sup>
