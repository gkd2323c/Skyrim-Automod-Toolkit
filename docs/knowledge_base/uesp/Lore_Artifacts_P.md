## Paragons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/SR-item-Paragons.jpg/200px-SR-item-Paragons.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Paragons.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Paragons.jpg) The Paragons The [Paragons](Lore_Paragons.md), also known as the **Paragon Stones** are a collection of five fabled large, ornate oval [gemstones](Lore_Crystals.md) set in ancient, intricately wrought silver crafted by the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md).

**Found in:** [Skyrim](Skyrim_Forgotten_Vale.md#Paragon_Platform), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Treasures_R#The_Ruby_Paragon)
## Penitent
The [Penitent](Lore_Penitent.md), the **Blade of Courage** (also named **Vosh Rakh** in [Orcish](Lore_Old_Orcish.md)), is the legendary blade of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) used in ancient times. Statues depicting Trinimac often show him holding his sword, and worshipers of the god swear by it for aid. Priests of Trinimac consider themselves blessed by the holy sword Penitent, and that his blessing is his sword and shield being at one's side to protect them. The [Vosh Rakh](Lore_Vosh_Rakh.md), an Orcish extremist faction, named themselves after the blade's Orcish name as they considered themselves a manifestation of it to "cut a path through stifling traditions", with some members bearing the rank "penitent". The name of the sword is often used as a possessive adjective in regards to worship of Trinimac.

[Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) also wields a sword, which he is also seen wielding in depictions in statuary, as does [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) who some myths identify as Trinimac instead, but it is unknown if there is any connection.

## Poetic Edda
The [Poetic Edda](Lore_Poetic_Edda.md) is an ancient collection of poems, stories, and legends that depict the extensive history of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), which is why it earned the moniker, *"the living history of Skyrim."* For an untold amount of time, bards and [skalds](Lore_Skald.md) have contributed to the Poetic Edda, which is often kept by the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Tending_the_Flames.md)

## Prismatic Core
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg/200px-ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg) The Prismatic Core The [Prismatic Core](Lore_Prismatic_Core.md) is a powerful crystal that powered the **Prismatic Weapon**, created by the willing sacrifices of many priestesses of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). It was given to the forces of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city-state of [Delodiil](Lore_Delodiil.md) during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), so that they could defeat their [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) -worshipping enemy of [Abagarlas](Lore_Abagarlas.md) and destroy the [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md). The destruction of Abagarlas was successful, but the [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) was ruined in the attempt. The crystal powering it was salvaged, although drained of its power, and five Ayleid heroes set out to the Dwemer ruin of Mzeneldt to hide the crystal so that the forces of Bal could not find it. The priestess Valasha sacrificed herself to re-power the crystal, with the rest of the heroes giving their lives to safeguard. It was later recovered by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and re-forged into the Prismatic Weapon by [Merric at-Aswala](Lore_Merric_at-Aswala.md), assisted by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> The Prismatic Weapon was used by the guild's champions to destroy the Mortuum Vivicus once and for all, and was left in the Vestige's possession afterwards.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Will_of_the_Council)

## Predator's Grace
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/SR-item-Predator%27s_Grace.jpg/200px-SR-item-Predator%27s_Grace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Predator%27s_Grace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Predator%27s_Grace.jpg) Predator's Grace [Predator's Grace](Lore_Predator%27s_Grace.md) is a pair of unique [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) hide shoes, imbued with a powerful [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that causes its wearer to be perpetually muffled and to move in near-total silence, while also slightly hastening the natural regeneration of their stamina. This pair of shoes also restored stamina at an accelerated rate whenever the wearer was wounded.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Predator%27s_Grace.md)
