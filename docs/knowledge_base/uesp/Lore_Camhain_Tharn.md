*Walk the untrod paths, feel the land with your bare feet, and keep a keen ear out for what the spirits speak in the rustle of the leaves.*
—Camhain Tharn<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTG-1)</sup> Camhain Tharn was a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) member of [House Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) and a nephew of the first of the [Longhouse](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) [Emperors](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) of the [Blackdrake Clan](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTG-1)</sup> and possibly [Veraxia Tharn](Lore_Veraxia_Tharn.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-COTFC4-3)</sup> He was an author of the *[A Reach Travel Guide](Lore_A_Reach_Travel_Guide.md)* and was known for his pro-Longhouse sentiments.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTG-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/SR-place-The_Reach.jpg/200px-SR-place-The_Reach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Reach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Reach.jpg) The Reach, land described by Camhain Little is known of Camhain Tharn's history. As a Reachfolk relative of Emperor Durcorach and a member of the prominent Tharn family of [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) he had ties to both [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). At some time prior to [2E 558](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_558) he traveled through the land of his ancestors and explored numerous sites such as [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md), [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), [Briar Rock](Lore_Briar_Rock_Ruins.md), [Lost Valley](Lore_Lost_Valley_Redoubt.md) and [Valthume](Lore_Valthume.md). In his works he referred to the Reach as "our lands", suggesting his close ties to the region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTG-1)</sup>

In [2E 558](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_558) he wrote his guide to the Reachfolk lands, *A Reach Travel Guide*.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTG-1)</sup>

His fate following the fall of the Longhouse dynasty in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577) is unknown.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-COTFC4-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EFEV-4)</sup> Many Reachfolk retreated from Cyrodiil following the death of Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-5)</sup> but the relatives of the Black Drake met their end at hands of Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), who purged<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-6)</sup> or banished all his potential rivals among his relatives.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MTC-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONCaddach-8)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_race) | Camhain refers to the Reach as his own lands and expresses belief in Reachfolk spiritual traditions. Notably, he speaks of the people of Cyrodiil, those conquered by Durcorach, as though they are separate from himself, despite professing allegiance to the Empire. Furthermore, he calls Durcorach, a Reachman, his uncle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTG-1)</sup> While his surname, Tharn, is of Nibenese origin, House Tharn was known for intermarrying with the Longhouse Emperors' bloodline. Additionally, Durcorach is known to have had at least one consort prior to his marriage to Veraxia.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup> As such, Camhain may also have ties to the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) race as well. |
