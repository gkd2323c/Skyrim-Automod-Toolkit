# Yokudan Empire


By the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Yokuda had become a vast empire, and developed a tradition of being ruled by a single emperor, however this was eventually abolished in [1E 376](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_376). Although each successive emperor remained as the figurehead of the empire, his powers were very much reduced. As a result, the nation descended into three hundred years of continuous civil war between the provincial lords, warrior monks, and brigands, all fighting each other for land and power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHATH-1)</sup> In [1E 609](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_609), [Mansel Sesnit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mansel_Sesnit) became the Elden Yokeda, or military dictator and waged many of the battles of unification and succeeded in gaining control of almost the entire empire until he was assassinated in [1E 617](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_617). His successor, [Randic Torn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Randic_Torn), took over and continued the work of Sesnit, however by the time of his death in [1E 737](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_737) internal disturbances still had not been completely eliminated. After his death, another vicious civil war broke out.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHATH-1)</sup>

In the chaotic aftermath, the "Last Emperor" of Yokuda, [Hira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hira), rose to power and attempted to wrestle control of the empire from the people by exterminating the [sword-singers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers). This conflict became known as the [War of the Singers](Lore_War_of_the_Singers.md). In [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), it came to a climax at the foot of [Hattu Mountain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hattu_Mountain), where [Frandar Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frandar_Hunding)'s army of singers crushed the emperor's forces.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DTS-6)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHATH-1)</sup>

While the Yokudan Empire in its original form fell after Yokuda sank into the ocean around [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792), the Na-Totambu ruling class who migrated to Hammerfell attempted to reproduce the culture and rule of Yokuda as close as possible, with mixed results. This meant that while the empire itself was gone, the Yokudans (and later the Redguards) continued the tradition of monarchic rule in some capacity.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONload Totambu-7)</sup> One notable example was a Yokudan noble named [Tarish-Zi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tarish-Zi) who proclaimed himself emperor and led his warrior band to [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) in order to establish his own empire.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Wot RG-8)</sup> While he ruled in the ancient Yokudan way, eventually his empire faded into obscurity in the years that followed.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTitus-9)</sup> Another example was a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) noble named [Prince Hubalajad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hubalajad) who colonized [Hew's Bane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hew%27s_Bane) and established [Abah's Landing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abah%27s_Landing), where his descendants would rule for several generations before fading away.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-IDo PH-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Load-11)</sup> Another example of parts of Hammerfell being ruled under a monarchy was the rise of Queen [Afsar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Afsar) of [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md). According to legend, the ruling king died in the late First Era with no heir, which led to squabbling between the Na-Totambu over succession to the throne. Afsar was said to have seized the throne the ancient way by killing the opposition and solidifying her rule. Despite its bloody start, her reign was supposedly very peaceful.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONRAG-12)</sup>

During the Second Empire, Hammerfell was ruled by the Phyllocid Dynasty; however, the position of High King was weakened, and the Na-Totambu and Ra Gada (now known as [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) respectively) would go on to aid [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s administration of Hammerfell. The Crowns retained the rights of noble council and the Forebears were granted rights of ownership within their tribal districts.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-13)</sup> This, however, did not last, and the [Kingdom of Hammerfell](Lore_Kingdom_of_Hammerfell.md) that emerged during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) almost seven hundred years later could be considered the successor to the Yokudan Empire, given the Na-Totambu ruling class took power and reverted back to a hereditary monarchy.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1H-14)</sup>
