# Nightblades

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-render-Nightblade.jpg/200px-ON-render-Nightblade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nightblade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nightblade.jpg) A Nightblade Nightblades are opportunistic adventurers known for their ability for getting in and out of trouble. They wield magic and use it to enhance their mobility, concealment, and stealthy close combat. They move swiftly and with haste by darkness, and use spells to benefit their circumstances. Nightblades thrive on conflict and misfortune, spell and shadow are their friends. Believed to be the most intriguing and shadowy of people, they trust their luck, cunning, and intelligence to survive, combining the skills of thieves with the powers of mages. They have a sinister reputation, as many nightblades act as thieves, enforcers, murderers, or secret agents. The mastery of their arts allows them to thrive as infiltrators and spies. Their presence is seldom seen but certainly felt.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESONightblade-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oblivion Nightblade-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Nightblade-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlespire Nightblade-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Morrowind Nightblade-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Arena Nightblade-6)</sup>

Trickery, illusion, stealth, and agility make for a deadly combination, giving nightblades an edge in war.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Nightblade-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Nightblade-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlespire Nightblade-4)</sup> Nightblades also wield **Night Magic**, which not only grants them additional magicka, but increases the accuracy of their spells. They are capable of creating night runes, which ravage the souls of foes while siphoning life-force from them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Nightblade-7)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESONightblade-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SS-8)</sup> In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), nightblades were known for their use of [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESONightblade-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SDAH-9)</sup>, of which they are said to draw out the most through the art of Siphoning. With it, they call to the blood of their victims, cursing and draining them in manners considered diabolical.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Bahtra Hun-10)</sup> Assassination is more than just an action for Nightblades, instead it is both a martial and magical skill-set.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Bahtra Hun-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-11)</sup> Despite shadow stepping being commonplace in a nightblade's repertoire during this time,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-STS-12)</sup> [teleportation spells](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md#Short_Range_Spells) were seemingly no longer associated with them come the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RBS-13)</sup> The term nightblade was considered by magical educators a fancy title with little relevance.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-WAA-14)</sup>

## History
During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of the Second Era, nightblades were employed by the alliances.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ATROCSDOTEP-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KEN-16)</sup> The [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) also took nightblades into its service,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mo T-17)</sup> and during the Third Era nightblades were considered a standard part of any modern army.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TWQ-18)</sup> The [black bear](Lore_Black_Bear.md) is the preferred mount of [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) nightblades, as it blends into the dark, and makes no hoofbeats when it gallops.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CSS-19)</sup> Nightblade's Indigo is a hue named after the nightblades.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-NBDye-20)</sup> The [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md) is known to attract nightblades to towns it is celebrated in.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-WFest-21)</sup>

## Notable Nightblades
- **[Bahtra at-Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bahtra_at-Hunding)** — The descendant of [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md), student of assassination magic.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-11)</sup>
- **[Dhaunayne Aundae](Lore_Dhaunayne_Aundae.md)** — The vampiric leader of the [Aundae Clan](Lore_Aundae_Clan.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWAundae-22)</sup>
- **[The Glimmering Foxbat](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#The_Glimmering_Foxbat)** — The Glimmering Foxbat is the author of books regarding shadow magic found in a nightblade's skillset.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-STS-12)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SDAH-9)</sup>
- **[Sumeer Jabran](Lore_Sumeer_Jabran.md)** — Sumeer Jabran was the Nightblade of the trio known as the Dremora Travelers, who were considered the most fearless of their kind in [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s service,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Anchor-23)</sup> and served as generals to his invading forces.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Anchor G-24)</sup>
- **Unnamed [Witchman](Lore_Reachman.md) Assassin** — Nightblade assassin of the [King of the Colovian Estates](Lore_Cuhlecain.md). They also razed the Imperial Palace and slit [General Talos](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s throat.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE1C-25)</sup>
- **[Dram's](Lore_Dram.md) ancestors** — Dram's ancestors were distinguished nightblades in the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-RGArchive-26)</sup>
- **[Baynarah's nightblade](Lore_Poison_Song.md)** — Baynarah's nightblade is a character from a historically inaccurate epic set in the aftermath of the war with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PS-27)</sup>
- **[Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md)** — A [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) assassin and practitioner of assassination magic.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-11)</sup>
- **[Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md)** — An [Eye of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md) and practitioner of assassination magic.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-11)</sup>
