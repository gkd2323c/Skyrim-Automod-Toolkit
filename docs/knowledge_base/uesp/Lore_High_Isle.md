[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-map-Systres_Archipelago.jpg/200px-ON-map-Systres_Archipelago.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Systres_Archipelago.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Systres_Archipelago.jpg) High Isle is the largest island in the Systres [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f8/ON-concept-High_Isle_Biome.jpg/600px-ON-concept-High_Isle_Biome.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-High_Isle_Biome.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-High_Isle_Biome.jpg) High Isle High Isle is the largest island of [the Systres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Systres) archipelago, far to the southwest of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and northwest of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGMap-1)</sup> High Isle serves as the center of politics and commerce for the island chain, predominantly from the port city of [Gonfalon Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gonfalon_Bay) and the Dufort Shipyards.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad High Isle-2)</sup> High Isle is the southernmost island of the archipelago as well as the most populated. It is known for its idyllic and vibrant summer landscape and its friendly population. On its southeast coast is the largest city in the region, [Gonfalon Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gonfalon_Bay) and deep in its interior is a basin that houses the All Flags Harbor, where the many captains of the eponymous [naval fleet](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) gathered for their incursion of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md). High Isle, like the rest of the archipelago, is volcanic, and there are plenty of steam vents, hot springs, and other such features in the island's back country.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VGTHI-3)</sup> The southern half of High Isle is the Sapphire Coast, while the northern is Oakenvald.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

The island's name was coined by Duchess [Martinne Guimard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Martinne_Guimard), a noblewoman who bought the Systres in [1E 2327](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2327). A year later during the region's induction ceremony, she dubbed its largest island, "High Isle", in honor of the Breton's ancestral homeland of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH4-5)</sup>

Harbor Island is located just south of the isle, connected via bridge to Gonfalon Bay and acting as an extension of the city's land.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VGTHI-3)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) Map of western Tamriel showing the Systres [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/ON-load-High_Isle.png/200px-ON-load-High_Isle.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-High_Isle.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-High_Isle.png) The coast of High Isle, looking towards [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md) According to the druids, the archipelago has a special connection to [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESODA-7)</sup> The Systres were first settled by the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) around [1E 330](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_330), fleeing the power struggles between the rising [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) and [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order). These [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) druids supposedly followed "a song on the wind", which led them to the island of [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md), though other legends state they were guided by an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md). The druids claim they used the power of the [Earthbones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Earthbones) to transform the volcanic Y'ffelon into a verdant paradise. After this the druids set out across the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md), transforming the nearby islands as well. During this golden age the druids fractured into the Stonelore, Eldertide, and Firesong Circles.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

In [1E 2241](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2241) the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) King of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo), called for a fleet to sail against [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) and avenge the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md). Olo was soon joined by the other nations of Tamriel, and the fleet was dubbed the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1Wild-9)</sup> In [1E 2243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2243), the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) and its founding members landed on the Systres Archipelago to begin construction of the fleet, and to prepare for war against the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md). Colovian engineers and their Breton and [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) laborers began work on building shipyards across the isles, with the two significant shipyards being the ones in All Flags Harbor and Gonfalon Bay.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SH3-10)</sup> After the navy's victory over Thras, their master builder, Tobin Moorcroft wanted to build a monument to the navy's leader, Baron-Admiral Bendu Olo.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCOGB-11)</sup>

Although the project was initially rejected by Bendu Olo himself, it was revived years later after the monument on All Flags Islet finished construction in [1E 2274](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2274). Tobin Moorcroft spearheaded the project and chose a rocky island southeast of Gonfalon Bay as the place to build it. He created an expert team of stone-carvers and craft-mages,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCOGB-11)</sup> as well as a large force of Breton workers to turn the rock into a work of art. Many of these Breton workers brought their families from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) to live on High Isle and built villages around the shipyards, more specifically near Gonfalon Bay. The Colossus of Gonfalon Bay was completed in [1E 2290](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2290) and an elderly Bendu Olo returned to the islands to accept this honor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH4-5)</sup> The statue has since stood over the island as a key attraction to the city.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCOGB-11)</sup> Despite the high Breton influence the islands became a part of Colovia as one of Bendu Olo's holdings. When High Rock seceded from the Empire several decades later in [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305) the Colovians refused orders from the emperor to exile its Breton population, claiming it to be too costly.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/ON-place-Gonfalon_Bay_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Gonfalon_Bay_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Gonfalon_Bay_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Gonfalon_Bay_02.jpg) Gonfalon Bay, the largest settlement on High Isle [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/ON-load-All_Flags_Islet.png/200px-ON-load-All_Flags_Islet.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-All_Flags_Islet.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-All_Flags_Islet.png) All-Flags Islet When Colovia followed High Rock's example and declared independence in [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321), the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) broke out.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Cyrodiil-12)</sup> The cost of the ensuing war forced the Colovians to put non-essential territory, leading a group of Breton coin-barons led by Duchess Martinne Guimard to purchase the Systres for an undisclosed sum in [1E 2327](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2327). When Duchess Guimard visited the Systres for the first time the following year she dubbed its largest island "High Isle" after the Breton homeland of High Rock.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

House Guimard ruled the Systres for one hundred and fifty years before falling into disfavor because of a jilted marriage proposal, leading the islands to fall into the hands of [House Mantel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Mantel). House Mantel's reign was short-lived however, as in [1E 2484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2484) Mount Firesong erupted once more, shattering the shipyards, damaging the All Flags monument, and claiming the lives of nearly one-third of the archipelago's inhabitants, including nearly all of the nobility. Calls for aid to the mainland were ignored, and the Systreans soon found themselves facing starvation and illness, killing thousands. Systrean society was on the brink of collapse when the druids of the Stonelore Circle resurfaced, providing the people with food and fresh water. This prompted a short, but significant renaissance of druidic faith known as "the Green Years". While the druids did not reestablish their rule over the islands they did earn a place of respect and bolstered ranks.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

The Systrean pastoral renaissance ended on the 11th of [Sun's Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Dusk) in [1E 2704](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2704), when the emissaries of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) arrived onto Gonfalon Bay with an entourage of Breton coin-barons to re-establish the Systres as property of Cyrodiil. These coin barons brought about the immediate return of the island's feudalism, but this time under [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) and later [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie)'s banner. The Breton overseers cultivated a reputation of joyful compliance and servility as they proudly waved the Potentate's banner in Gonfalon Bay, but behind this façade, they planned their escape from his rule.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SH6-13)</sup> Several revolts broke out against Imperial rule, most prominently by the Eldertide Circle, but none were successful.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1c/ON-place-Castle_Navire_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Castle_Navire_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Castle_Navire_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Castle_Navire_02.jpg) Castle Navire, seat of power of House Dufort [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-trailer-Gonfalon_Bay.png/200px-ON-trailer-Gonfalon_Bay.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Gonfalon_Bay.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Gonfalon_Bay.png) Gonfalon Bay after the Empire's arrival By [2E 110](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_110), a patriarch of the Breton noble house, [Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md), was instated to be the Imperial Governor of the Systres,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Mornard-14)</sup> with another House, Dufort, arriving to the archipelago in [2E 125](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_125).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dufort-15)</sup> Both houses soon established mercantile empire on the Systres, and would eventually be granted noble titles, with House Mornard becoming Dukes of the Systres as vassals of the Gardner kings of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and lieges of the Dufort Barons.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Mornard-14)</sup>

The following years saw the partnership of the two houses devolve into a rivalry, attributed by some to the madness of Baron [Densil Dufort](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Densil_Dufort),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dufort-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Mad Baron-16)</sup> which was only exacerbated by the start of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430). House Mornard and House Dufort's jockeying for power in the court of Wayrest eventually came to a head in [2E 478](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_478), when Duke Ruffe Mornard seized a full third of House Dufort's lands and exiled its leadership to Amenos, claiming sedition against the crown with a raft of forged documents and forced testimony. The Gardners viewed the coup with suspicion but were unable to interfere due to troubles with an ascendant [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563) the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) reached the Systres, slaying commoner and noble alike. Duke [Avrippe Mornard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Avrippe_Mornard) ordered the archipelago sealed off in [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565), causing trade to stagnate, which caused Duke Avrippe to fall into disfavor in Wayrest's court. When King Ranser of Shornhelm attacked Wayrest in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566) Duke Avrippe allied with him, forsaking ties of vassalage, but King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Wayrest managed to bring together [an alliance](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to defeat Ranser. Once matters on the mainland were settled and Ranser had been defeated Emeric turned his eyes to the Systres, stripping Duke Avrippe of his titles and exiling him to Cyrodiil. The duchy was granted to House Dufort, while Avrippe's son Leonard was left with a county and rule over the island of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) and their operations in [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dufort-15)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), ships carrying leaders of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) were set to land in Gonfalon Bay at the behest of Lord [Bacaro Volorus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bacaro_Volorus) but never arrive. The Breton noble and investigator, Lady [Arabelle Davaux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arabelle_Davaux) was hired by Lord Bacaro to investigate and met with Gonfalon Bay's dockmaster, Arnauld. Arabelle enlisted the help of a [mainlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to find more clues around the city. When they went to the Gonfalon Head Lighthouse, they found it attacked by a group of lawless knights called the [Ascendant Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascendant_Order) and later retrieved the Kynareth headpiece of the lighthouse from Ascendant members.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONKnaves-17)</sup> The investigation eventually brought them to Castle Navire where they were confronted by the [Ascendant Magus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Damard_Dufort). After an attack on the castle, an earthquake ruptured all over High Isle and the seismic force was strong enough to shatter the Colossus of Gonfalon Bay.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONImport-18)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), [Pieron Desant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pieron_Desant) of the [Gleaners of Aurbis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gleaners_of_Aurbis) traveled there for a heist and returned to [Fargrave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave) with a valuable haul.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TFG-19)</sup>

## Notable Locales
[Gonfalon Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gonfalon_Bay) The largest settlement on the Systres Archipelago and its main port-of-entry. Castle Navire The seat of power House Dufort, located far west of Gonfalon Bay, southwest of All Flags Islet. Dufort Shipyards The shipyards for House Dufort located in the northeastern part of High Isle. All-Flags Islet An islet in the middle of the island, which served as the gathering point for the All-Flags Navy. Stonelore Grove A settlement of the Druids of the Stonelore Circle. Steadfast Manor The ancestral home of Lord Bacaro Volorus, which serves as a sanctuary ran by the Steadfast Society. Abhain Chapel A crypt where generations of High Isle's nobles lie buried in. Spire of the Crimson Coin A ruined Breton coin baron settlement.
