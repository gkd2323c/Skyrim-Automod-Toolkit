# Akatosh Chantry

[![ON-banner-Akatosh 02.gif](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/ON-banner-Akatosh_02.gif/200px-ON-banner-Akatosh_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Akatosh_02.gif) [![](https://images.uesp.net/a/a9/DF-sign-Akatosh_Chantry.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-sign-Akatosh_Chantry.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-sign-Akatosh_Chantry.png) Akatosh Chantry The Akatosh Chantry (also called the **Chantry of Akatosh**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM40Oot H-1)</sup> is the temple dedicated to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) the Great Dragon, God of Time and Chief Deity of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md). They are generally the place of worship for Akatosh. The priests of Akatosh always call their temples chantries, supposedly because they do a lot of singing there. The Chantry believes Akatosh is the most constant of [gods](Lore_Gods.md), for his sphere is time itself. Donations to the Chantry result in a blessing from Akatosh, and the priests and priestess have a variety of other skills and services available for members. One has to be judged worthy by the Chantry before being sheltered under the Great Wings of Akatosh, however. The Order of the Hour is their militant arm, and the [Benevolence of Mara](Lore_Benevolence_of_Mara.md) is considered one of their allies. The Akatosh Chantry is enemies with the [House of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFAC-2)</sup>

## Description
There are many known titles that can be attributed to the leading position of the Akatosh Chantry. The Patriarch/Matriarch,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFRanks-3)</sup> the High Priest/High Priestess,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAAD-4)</sup> and the Primate,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mt CPA-5)</sup> which is alternatively called a Bishop.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-APAYQ-6)</sup> There are other positions, such as the Grand Chanter and the Grand Sermonizer, that are of equal level to these leading positions. In the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), the chantry is prevalent in the [Kingdom of Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), the [Barony of Bergama](Lore_Bergama_(barony).md), and the [Isle of Balfiera](Lore_Isle_of_Balfiera.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFAC-2)</sup>

The Akatosh Chantry believe in an afterlife simply referred to as [Heaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heaven), and, like other groups, believe that the [consecrations](Lore_Consecrations_of_Arkay.md) of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) would guide them there. One of the three purposes of the Akatosh Chantry is to teach the truth of the Covenant, made between Akatosh and Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). Mass at the Akatosh Chantry is held at noon. Except on days considered holy to Akatosh, the mass is brief and leads into the Orison of Gratitude.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-APAYQ-6)</sup> The Akatosh Chantry is known to hold [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) (possibly just [dragonlings](Lore_Dragonling.md)) in their sacred groves. These dragons serve as the holy symbol of the Akatosh Chantry.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFAt D-7)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Primate [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md) of the chantry in [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) had established himself as one of the three most influential figures in the independent region of the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), utilizing the Order of the Hour to enforce his will.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mt CPA-5)</sup> He ascended to this position after ordering the assassination of the previous Primate [Jonas Coventine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jonas_Coventine).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TP3-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONGFTP-9)</sup> His chantry would wage war against the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md), which ended with his assassination in the Cathedral of Akatosh.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> In times prior, the Akatosh Chantry in Kvatch had an equal power with the Counts of Kvatch.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-11)</sup> In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), the healer of the Court of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), Charvek-si, was associated with the Akatosh Chantry. During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), before he was discovered, [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) was a priest at the Akatosh Chantry in Kvatch and was present when the city was besieged. Around [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the High Priest of the Chantry in [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) was Alexandre Simon.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAAD-4)</sup>

Members of Akatosh Chantry wear ceremonial stoles made of silk and satin with embroidered hourglasses during ceremonies of the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Contraband A1-12)</sup> Primates usually wield crosiers with a golden hourglass at their finial.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Contraband A2-13)</sup>

## Order of the Hour
The [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) (also called the **Hour Order**) is one of the oldest [knightly orders](Lore_Knights.md) in the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire.md), serving as the militant arm of the Akatosh Chantry. Their sole purpose is to enact the will of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) the Great Dragon, as well as preserve the stability and permanence the God of Time represents. The Order has scattered and re-organized on several occasions throughout history, but their mission has remained unchanged.
