[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg/200px-Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg) **Solstheim** Solstheim (meaning Sun's Home)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MNP-1)</sup> is a large island in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) to the north of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) were the first inhabitants of the region, and built many grand settlements that far outlasted their mainland holdings.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTSP-2)</sup> It officially became a region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16). The island has a long history of habitation by other races, but it has traditionally been primarily [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) in influence.

## History

### Merethic Era
Despite being in the general area of Morrowind, the climate of Solstheim is more like that of Skyrim. According to Skaal legend, it was originally a part of the mainland until a titanic battle between two Dragon Priests, the Guardian and the Traitor, ended in Solstheim being sundered from the rest of Skyrim in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGATT-3)</sup> The Traitor, [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), had turned on his [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) masters under the influence of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and fled to Solstheim. The Guardian, Vahlok the Jailor, fought with Miraak, and the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) burned his [temple](Lore_Temple_of_Miraak.md) to the ground. Miraak escaped their vengeance by retreating into [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). The Dragon Cult then ruled over Solstheim until the time of the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), when the Nords rose up against [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md)'s tyrannical rule.

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/SR-place-Skaal_Village.jpg/200px-SR-place-Skaal_Village.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Skaal_Village.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Skaal_Village.jpg) Skaal Village The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), an offshoot tribe of Nords, are known as the "heirs of the Guardian", and may have come to the island at this time by boat.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCHT-4)</sup> Like on the mainland, the Nords shared the island with the native [elves](Lore_Mer.md). The final stand between the Nords and the Snow Elves took place on Solstheim, at the [Battle of the Moesring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Moesring.md). The [Snow Prince](Lore_Snow_Prince.md) was killed during the battle and the elven forces were routed, leading to the ultimate collapse of Snow Elf society.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTSP-2)</sup> The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) also once had a presence on the island, until the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) led to the disappearance of the entire race from [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). The Nords subsequently attacked the Dwemer holdings on Solstheim.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Solstheim is home to an indigenous race of small, goblin-like creatures called [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md). Their origins are a mystery, and they have little culture of their own. The island is filled with outlaws such as [witches](Lore_Witches.md) and [reavers](Lore_Reaver.md). Solstheim was often used as a Hunting Grounds by [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), who would return to the island every era as part of the [Bloodmoon Prophecy](Lore_Bloodmoon_Prophecy.md). The Hunt was marked by several signs, the most significant of which being the return of [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) to Solstheim.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BM-6)</sup>

### Second Era
The Kamal fleet sailed around the northeast corner of Tamriel into the Sea of Ghosts during the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md), bypassing Solstheim, before finally landing at the mouth of the White River northeast of Windhelm in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSAI-7)</sup>

### Third Era
According to written history, [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) was founded early in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) by a group of Skaal who left the [Skaal Village](Lore_Skaal_Village.md) to live in the wilds as true Nords. Although the existence of Solstheim was common knowledge in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESO-8)</sup> and it had been generally considered a part of Skyrim at the time,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM46-9)</sup> Solstheim was apparently forgotten by the late Third Era, when it was rediscovered by the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-10)</sup> It was claimed as an "Imperial territory" with the building of [Fort Frostmoth](Lore_Fort_Frostmoth.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The [East Empire Trading Company](Lore_East_Empire_Company.md) identified the island as a rich source of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md), and founded the mining settlement of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). The Bloodmoon Prophecy was fulfilled that same year, but the Hunt was foiled by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BM-6)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the money had run out at Raven Rock, and the Nords of Skyrim launched a campaign to claim the whole of Solstheim and remove the Imperial fort.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBRumors-12)</sup> They were successful, and Solstheim was integrated into the province of Skyrim,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-13)</sup> but Fort Frostmoth remained occupied by [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) forces. During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), no Oblivion Gates were reported to have opened on Solstheim. In [4E 1](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_1), after the destruction of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), [Great House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) sent an expedition group led by [Brara Morvayn](Lore_Brara_Morvayn.md) to settle in Raven Rock. The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) were welcomed to Raven Rock, but the expedition may have been seen as an invasion by [Falx Carius](Lore_Falx_Carius.md) of Fort Frostmoth, who repeatedly wrote to the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) seeking assistance in removing them from the island. However, as only the third letter survived, was not dated, and was not discovered until the resurrected Carius' defeat by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), it is unknown if Carius viewed it as an invasion before his death in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5) or after his reanimation by [Ildari Sarothril](Lore_Ildari_Sarothril.md). Some time before [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), [Tel Mithryn](Lore_Tel_Mithryn.md) was founded by [Master Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [Great House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md).

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg/200px-SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Raven_Rock.jpg) Raven Rock circa 4E 201 During the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of 4E 5, the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) had a devastating effect on the southern half of Solstheim. Fort Frostmoth was destroyed, leaving few Imperial guards on the island and killing General Carius. Raven Rock replaced the remaining garrison with elite members of the [Redoran Guard](Lore_Redoran_Guard.md). After only a few years, the ash from Red Mountain had transformed southern Solstheim into ashlands. At the behest of Brara Morvayn, the East Empire Company provided the funds for a great wall of Morvayn's design known as the Bulwark to prevent Raven Rock from being buried beneath the ash.

In [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16), the High King of Skyrim ceded control of Solstheim to Morrowind. Ostensibly a sign of compassion for the suffering Dunmer people, the act served a pragmatic political purpose: Skyrim could not deny Dunmer refugees access to Solstheim without sparking an unwanted conflict with Morrowind, which had long maintained a theoretical claim to the island, nor could it stand by and allow the Dunmer to settle its territory without losing face. By relinquishing its own claim to Solstheim, Skyrim defused the situation and emerged looking like a savior. Dunmer refugees, among them the minor House Sathil, immediately began flocking to the island to escape their beleaguered homeland.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LOS-14)</sup> The East Empire Company was forced to turn over Raven Rock to House Redoran,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AHRR-15)</sup> and by [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48) the island was no longer considered part of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LOS-14)</sup>

Some time during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Neloth left [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md) to study the effects of Red Mountain's eruption at Tel Mithryn. In [4E 150](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_150), a small force of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) landed on Solstheim with the intent of wreaking havoc on the island, but were driven off by House Redoran.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AHRR-15)</sup> In [4E 181](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_181), Raven Rock's ebony mine was said to have dried up, and Raven Rock fell on hard times. In truth, the mine had more ebony to offer, but sections of the mine had been sealed off by the East Empire Company after the mine had broken into Bloodskal Barrow in [4E 10](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_10). The packs of werewolves that once roamed Solstheim were mostly wiped out during the Fourth Era by encroaching [werebears](Lore_Werebear.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Miraak attempted to return to Mundus and claim Solstheim for himself. He used the power of his [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to bend the will of the island's inhabitants, forcing them to corrupt the island's All-Maker Stones by building shrines to worship him. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) arrived on Solstheim after an assassination attempt by Miraak's cultists, and ventured into Apocrypha to defeat him. Raven Rock's mine was also reopened that year, when the lower sections were unsealed and the ruins cleared.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DB-17)</sup>

## Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/BM-creature-Wolf_Pack.jpg/200px-BM-creature-Wolf_Pack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-creature-Wolf_Pack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-creature-Wolf_Pack.jpg) A pack of wolves [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/SR-flora-Trama_Root.jpg/200px-SR-flora-Trama_Root.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-flora-Trama_Root.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-flora-Trama_Root.jpg) Trama Root, a plant native to Morrowind that has spread to Solstheim Before the Red Year, Solstheim had a climate very similar to that of mainland Skyrim. The southern half of the island was relatively temperate, and was covered by the coniferous [Hirstaang Forest](Lore_Hirstaang_Forest.md). The central tundra of the island was known as the [Isinfier Plains](Lore_Isinfier_Plains.md). Further north, the weather was (and remains) predominantly much colder. The barren [Moesring Mountains](Lore_Moesring_Mountains.md) lie to the northwestern side of the island, while the frigid [Felsaad Coast](Lore_Felsaad_Coast.md) lies to the northeast. The [Mortrag Glacier](Lore_Mortrag_Glacier.md) was located along the northwestern coast, but it collapsed in 3E 427.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BM-6)</sup> The island has three rivers: the [Harstrad](Lore_Harstrad_River.md), the [Iggnir](Lore_Iggnir_River.md) and the [Isild](Lore_Isild_River.md). The frozen [Lake Fjalding](Lore_Lake_Fjalding.md) is the largest body of water on the island. Solstheim is geologically rich; although it lacks more common metals such as [iron](Lore_Iron.md), the island is rich in gems and ebony. Local Nords also use [stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) for various crafting purposes. Frykte Peak, Hvitkald Peak, [Mortrag Peak](Lore_Mortrag_Peak.md) and Mount Moesring are the four tallest mountains on the island. Other landmarks include [Horker Island](Lore_Horker_Island.md), [Castle Karstaag](Lore_Castle_Karstaag.md) and [Hrothmund's Bane](Lore_Hrothmund%27s_Bane.md).

The island is home to many wild beasts and dangerous creatures. [Wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) and [bears](Lore_Bear.md) roam the island freely, as do [deer](Lore_Deer.md) and [elk](Lore_Elk.md). A type of golden-white [dire wolf](Lore_Dire_Wolf.md) called the Solstheim Lunar Wolf was bred by the Snow Elves, and for millennia have been known for their fearlessness when dealing with frigid terrain, [trolls](Lore_Troll.md), and avalanches.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SLW-18)</sup> [Horkers](Lore_Horker.md) are a common sight along the coast and waterways. An animal called [muskrey](Lore_Muskrey.md) is often hunted alongside deer.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LOS-14)</sup> [Rabbits](Lore_Rabbit.md), [torchbugs](Lore_Torchbug.md), [mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md) and the indigenous [Felsaad Tern](Lore_Felsaad_Tern.md) also call the island home. The [bristleback](Lore_Bristleback.md) is a unique species of [boar](Lore_Boar.md) native to the island.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Monsters such as the [Grahl](Lore_Grahl.md), [Frost Giants](Lore_Frost_Giant.md), [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md), and Albino Spiders can be a danger to explorers. Other dangers include rieklings, [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md), [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md), [bonewolves](Lore_Bonewolf.md) and undead [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md). Local flora include [holly bushes](Lore_Holly_Bush.md), [snowberry bushes](Lore_Snowberry_Bush.md), [Nirnroot](Lore_Nirnroot.md), [Belladonna](Lore_Belladonna.md), and the extremely rare [Wolfsbane](Lore_Wolfsbane.md) flower.

The Red Year marked the beginning of a dramatic change to the geography of southern Solstheim. Following the eruption of Red Mountain, the Hirstaang Forest was transformed into [ashland](Lore_Solstheim_Ashlands.md). The island continued to be exposed to ash storms and earthquakes long into the future. Flora and fauna native to Morrowind soon found its way to Solstheim, either through natural migration or deliberate planting by the Dunmer. [Netch](Lore_Netch.md), [silt striders](Lore_Silt_Strider.md), [ash hoppers](Lore_Ash_Hopper.md), [nix-hounds](Lore_Nix-Hound.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-20)</sup> [scathecraw](Lore_Scathecraw.md), [trama root](Lore_Trama_Root.md) and [emperor parasols](Lore_Emperor_Parasol.md) all became commonplace in the new ashlands. The spread of ash also affected local plants such as [creep clusters](Lore_Creep_Cluster.md) and [spiky grass](Lore_Spiky_Grass.md), and the destruction of the Hirstaang seemingly gave rise to the mysterious [burnt spriggans](Lore_Burnt_Spriggan.md).

Falling rock from the eruption landed along the southern coast of Solstheim, scarring the land with impact craters and rubble. This volcanic rock contained [heart stones](Lore_Heart_Stone.md), mysterious magical stones from deep within Red Mountain. Although their nature is largely unknown, the heart stones have the ability to grant life. Their presence in the ashlands led to the formation of the [ash spawn](Lore_Ash_Spawn.md), dangerous humanoid creatures animated by a stone, which rise from the ash and regularly attack settlements. The Telvanni of Tel Mithryn studied the ash spawn and learned to control and summon them. Experimentation with heart stones also resulted in the creation of [Ash Guardians](Lore_Ash_Guardian.md) and powerful undead who had had their hearts replaced with a stone.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DB-17)</sup>

## Notes
- The name of the island is given as *Soulstheim* in *[The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City)*, but [Kurt Kuhlmann](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Kurt_Kuhlmann) explained that this was a mistake.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-21)</sup> It was subsequently spelled correctly in *[Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls)*.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LOS-14)</sup>
- The [Solstheimstone Incarnate](Lore_Incarnate.md) is a theoretical [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) being of destruction, said to be an incarnation of a Solstheim blizzard. It is unknown if such a being exists, as its form has only been assumed through the use of polymorph magic.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-22)</sup>
