[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-prerelease-Volcanic_Vents.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Volcanic_Vents.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Volcanic_Vents.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Volcanic_Vents.jpg) Vulk'esh emerging from volcanic vents Vulk'esh are creatures of fire and lava that can be found all across the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md), such as in [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md), and [Galen](Lore_Galen.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> They are known to live in underground volcanic depths, swimming through seas of fire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NOV-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vinilsare-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Egg-4)</sup> Some have even described them as being born in magma,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CRD-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FFGalen-6)</sup> though they do possess actual flesh.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CRD-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Meat-7)</sup> They have been observed emerging from volcanic vents,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vinilsare-3)</sup> and [Ash Hoppers](Lore_Ash_Hopper.md) can often be found in their wake.

Vulk'esh and the conditions that resulted in their creation are considered somewhat mysterious. The idea of them being created by mages is considered unlikely, while other passing theories include the idea of them originating in some fiery realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Given how they thrive in volcanic depths, it is speculated they may simply be natural residents of the deep underground of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). It is suggested there could be thousands of the creatures beneath the crust, with the their range theoretically extending as far as [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NOV-2)</sup>

Despite their strange morphology, they have some traits akin to [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) like [lizards](Lore_Lizard.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NOV-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FFGalen-6)</sup> such as laying eggs to proliferate.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Egg-4)</sup> They are known to shed their outer layers of rock like a [snake](Lore_Snake.md). After being shed, the cooled molten crust of these creatures is known as "Vulk'esh [Scoria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scoria)", and it is believed to have magical properties.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vinilsare-3)</sup> It is speculated Vulk'esh eat [minerals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minerals), but there is no actual evidence for this.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NOV-2)</sup> Their excrement has a tendency to harden into dense, heavy, igneous [coprolites](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coprolite).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-IC-8)</sup>

## Uses
The inhabitants of Galen are known to eat savory Vulk'esh meat. The reddish-black flesh of the Vulk'esh is hot enough to cook itself when harvested, it is said to smell of [brimstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sulfur).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Meat-7)</sup> One cut of the animal that is used in cooking is the rib-eye.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CRD-5)</sup> Vulk'esh Scoria is occasionally harvested for its assumed magical properties.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vinilsare-3)</sup> Those who go on Vulk'esh hunting expeditions have been known to wear heavy magma-heat resistant boots.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

Some have attempted to tame or control Vulk'esh with varying levels of success. Members of the [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md) such as [Damard Dufort](Lore_Damard_Dufort.md) were known to have unleashed the creatures on their enemies in battle.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Inhabitants of High Isle have attempted to ride the creatures, though this had disastrous results.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
