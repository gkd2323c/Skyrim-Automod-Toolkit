Magnus Septim (sometimes enumerated as **Magnus I**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> was the eleventh emperor of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for five years, from [3E 140](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_140) to [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145). During the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), Magnus fought alongside his brother [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) against their nephew [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_III.md) and their sister [Potema](Lore_Potema.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> In [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137), Magnus took part in the final battle of the war, the month-long [Siege of Solitude](Lore_Siege_of_Solitude.md), alongside Cephorus I, which ended when Potema died in her [castle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup> Magnus was already sixty-one when he acceded to the throne, and the business of punishing the traitorous kings of the War of the Red Diamond drained much of his remaining strength. He was preceded by his older brother, [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
Magnus Septim was born in [3E 79](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_79), the youngest child of Prince [Pelagius Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II) (later Emperor Pelagius II) and his second wife, [Quintilla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quintilla) of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ1-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ3-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup> He had two brothers, [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) and [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), and a sister, [Potema](Lore_Potema.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> By the late first century of the Third Era, in his early adulthood, Magnus lived in [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia_(city).md), where he served as a counselor in the royal court of Queen [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ3-5)</sup> In [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98) and [3E 99](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_99), Magnus attended the funeral of Pelagius II and the coronation of his successor, Antiochus, in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ3-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ4-8)</sup> By [3E 109](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_109), Magnus had married [Hellena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hellena),<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Wife)</sup> the Cyrodilic Queen of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth_(kingdom).md), making him the city-state's king,<sup>[[Note 2]](#intnote_King)</sup> a role in which he admirably represented the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s interests in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ4-8)</sup> As of [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119), he was considered to have ruled Lilmoth well during his reign.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> In 3E 119, Magnus' son [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_III.md) was born.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTET-9)</sup> He also fathered a daughter named [Jolethe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jolethe), as well as more sons and another daughter.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup>

### The War of the Red Diamond
In [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120), Antiochus died, so Magnus attended his funeral and the coronation of his niece [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) in the Imperial City.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ6-10)</sup> Before the funeral of Antiochus, Potema gave a speech before the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) in which she asserted that Kintyra was not the daughter of Antiochus.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> Magnus' nephew, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_III.md), made a similar accusation, referring to the infamous decadence of the Imperial City during the reign of Kintyra's father.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-11)</sup> After hearing of this accusation, Kintyra banished Potema and Uriel from the Imperial Court, and they returned to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> On 3 First Seed, 3E 120, he attended the coronation of Kintyra II as Empress of Tamriel.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ6-10)</sup>

In [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121), the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) broke out and split the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in half.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ6-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TKot N-12)</sup> Uriel and Potema captured Kintyra II at [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ6-10)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-11)</sup> Uriel then invaded the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), capturing the under-defended Imperial City after a fortnight-long siege and then assuming the Ruby Throne as Uriel Septim III.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-11)</sup> After Uriel III was crowned emperor, Magnus allied with Cephorus to oppose him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup> The kings of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), southern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) mostly supported Magnus and Cephorus, while Uriel III had the combined support of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), northern Morrowind, and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup>

Magnus led his [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) troops through Morrowind and into Skyrim to fight in Potema's home province. The Argonians fought well in the summer months, but during the winter, they retreated south to regroup and attack again when the weather was warm, resulting in a two-year stalemate.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup> While Magnus and his troops fought in Skyrim, Cephorus concentrated his army on the war in High Rock.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup> In the summer of [3E 125](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_125), Cephorus conquered western High Rock, and by [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127), all of High Rock had defected to the side of Magnus and Cephorus.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup> In 3E 127, Magnus and his forces occupied the ruins of Kogmenthist Castle in Skyrim and attempted to defend it against Potema's forces during the Battle of Falconstar, while Cephorus and his troops fought Uriel III and the Imperial Army at the concurrent Battle of [Ichidag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ichidag) in Hammerfell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup> Cephorus' army defeated the Imperial Army and captured Uriel III. Uriel III was placed in a cart for transport to the Imperial City to stand trial, but while en route, a mob overtook his carriage and burned him alive within it; Cephorus was then proclaimed emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup> When Potema learned of Uriel III's defeat and capture, she flew into a rage and personally led her troops to assault Magnus' army. Despite the superior defensive position of Magnus and his troops, Potema and her forces managed to defeat them, forcing them to flee the battlefield.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWQ7-13)</sup>

Although Uriel III was dead, Potema continued to fight Magnus and Cephorus I for the next decade. Potema mounted a series of attacks against her brothers by using her army of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead), but her allies began abandoning her until only the [Kingdom of Solitude](Lore_Solitude_(kingdom).md) remained under her control. In [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137), Magnus took part in the final battle of the war, the month-long [Siege of Solitude](Lore_Siege_of_Solitude.md), alongside Cephorus I, which ended when Potema died in her [castle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup> After the siege, Cephorus I placed Pelagius on the throne of Solitude.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup>

### Emperor of Tamriel and Death
In [3E 140](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_140), Cephorus I died after falling from his [horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup> Cephorus had no living children, so Magnus succeeded him as Emperor of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup> Magnus was already sixty-one when he acceded to the throne, and the business of punishing the traitorous kings of the War of the Red Diamond drained much of his remaining strength.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> The [Atrius Building Commission](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atrius_Building_Commission) was founded during his reign.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ADi F1-14)</sup>

Due to Magnus' advanced age, Tamriel turned its attention toward his oldest living child, Pelagius. As King of Solitude, Pelagius began displaying eccentricities, which were already becoming infamous by the time Magnus took the throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup> In 3E 141, on the orders of Magnus, Pelagius was married to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), [Katariah Ra'athim](Lore_Katariah.md), Duchess of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup> Magnus arranged the marriage of Pelagius and Katariah to try and cement relations with [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart), the seat of power for the [Ra'athim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra%27athim) Clan. Magnus also arranged the marriage because he hoped that Katariah's shrewdness as a diplomat and beauty would hide the madness of Pelagius.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup>

On the 8th of [Second Seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Second_Seed), [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145), Magnus died quietly in his sleep.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTET-9)</sup> Legend accuses Pelagius of patricide, but this is generally dismissed as false because he was the King of Solitude and seldom visited the Imperial City.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup> Pelagius succeeded him as emperor and subsequently became known as "Pelagius the Mad".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ8-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-6)</sup>

## Notes
- | [1](#note_Wife) | Sources give conflicting accounts of the identity of Magnus' wife. The historical fiction *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen.md)* states that Magnus' wife was Hellena, the Queen of Lilmoth, but the biographical non-fiction *[The Madness of Pelagius](Lore_The_Madness_of_Pelagius.md)* states that Magnus' wife was [Utheilla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Utheilla_Direnni) of Clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). When asked about this contradiction, the writer of both books, [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson) stated: "Madness is wrong: Pelagius did not have a Direnni mom. Tsathenes got Pelagius's birthdate and his early history completely wrong."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-15)</sup> *The Wolf Queen* states that Hellena's race was [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md), while in *Skyrim*, Pelagius III is depicted as a Breton, which would be more in line with Utheilla Direnni being Pelagius' mother, as the Direnni of that time period were described as Bretons. The exact truth of this has not been canonically confirmed one way or the other. |

Additionally, Magnus' grandson Cassynder later reigned as the King of Wayrest. |
