Dunerippers are carnivorous desert-dwelling [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) with protective scales on their backs. A duneripper can reach the length of three horses, and its armored tail is covered in chitinous scales. Dunerippers are ambush predators, and have a digestion system that requires only a modicum of food to sustain their large bodies. The duneripper burrows beneath the sand, sometimes lying motionless for days, waiting to strike its victim.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EG-1)</sup> [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) nomads claim that dunerippers of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) are a fraction of the size they used to be when the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) first encountered them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AMOA-2)</sup>

The first sign of an imminent duneripper attack is its dorsal crest piercing the sand, causing the sand to churn like a whirlpool.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KC-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EG-1)</sup> They rip through the dunes with their fins, and should a bite not incapacitate its prey, the duneripper will try to clamp down on it, roll onto it, constrict it, or burrow back into the sand to smother its prey.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KC-3)</sup> The duneripper will also sweep its tail around to upend its prey, and it exhibits shorts bursts of impressive speed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KC-3)</sup> The [Giant Scorpion](Lore_Giant_Scorpion.md) is known to fall prey to dunerippers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EG-1)</sup>

Their armored plates and durable neck spines generally fetch good prices at markets.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NOA-4)</sup> Duneripper blood is believed to prolong life by the people of [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md), though that is untrue as duneripper blood can be detrimental to one's health if handled poorly.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NOA-4)</sup> The spine of a duneripper can been used to make drinking horns.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The spine has also been made into dowsing sticks.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EG-1)</sup>

They share many similarities with the [sea-drakes](Lore_Sea-Drake.md) found in the waters of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EG-1)</sup> and are comparable in appearance to the [Duneracers](Lore_Duneracer.md) of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).
