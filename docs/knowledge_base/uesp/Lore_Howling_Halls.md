[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/SI-place-Howling_Halls.jpg/200px-SI-place-Howling_Halls.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Howling_Halls.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Howling_Halls.jpg) The Howling Halls circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [Emperor Pelagius Septim III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) died on the 2nd of Sun's Dawn, [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153) in the [Temple of Kynareth](Lore_Temple_of_Kynareth.md) on the Isle of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Do HHR-1)</sup> This Temple was destroyed promptly after the Emperor's death. Not long after, residents of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) came to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), stole the stones of the ruined Temple, brought them to the Isles, and rebuilt the Temple in exact detail atop the [Heretic's Horn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heretic%27s_Horn) in [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md). This structure became known as the **Howling Halls**, as it was said that one could still hear Pelagius' screams.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Do HHR-1)</sup>

During a [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md), a mortal woman named Ciirta came to Sheogorath's throne room for aid, only to find it empty. Deciding that Sheogorath was a false god, Ciirta made it her goal to slay the Madgod. Along with a group of supporters, she attempted to conquer the Shivering Isles and slay Sheogorath by leading a revolution of sorts when Sheogorath returned at the end of the Greymarch. She was the only survivor of the assault, and was imprisoned within the Howling Halls.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Ciirta then founded the [Apostles of Light](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Apostles_of_Light) and plotted to dethrone Sheogorath. Ciirta operated out of the Howling Halls, fooling her Apostles with visions of light to convince them to support her, until she was betrayed in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) by a splinter group of her Apostles and slain by Sheogorath's [Champion](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The pelvis bone of Pelagius was also recovered from the ruins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Eo SI-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
