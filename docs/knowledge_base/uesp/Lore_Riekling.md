Rieklings are a race of blue-skinned ice [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), related to the other [Goblin-ken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goblin-ken).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Landal Gevont-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM71:CS-2)</sup> The most common Rieklings are small in stature and found on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), but larger Rieklings were once present on mainland [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM71:CS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOM.26M-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOL.26K-4)</sup> They are aggressive, and can be dangerous in groups. Despite being considered little more than beasts by the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), Rieklings are naturally cunning. Rieklings speak the goblin language, and can be conversed with by expert linguists.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Landal Gevont-1)</sup> Some speak [Cyrodilic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodilic), although they rarely say anything intelligible.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Apart from this, they seemingly have little culture of their own. They scavenge what few relics they can find from the other races, and form strange attachments to these treasures.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LS-6)</sup>

The origins of Rieklings are a mystery to the people of Tamriel. The local Nords believe them to be descendants of the [snow elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md), related to the other [elven races](Lore_Mer.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> but this claim is not supported by scholars.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) consider them to be nothing more than "ice goblins". The Rieklings of Solstheim consider it to be the entire world "within great water",<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Chief-10)</sup> meaning that the Solstheim Rieklings are unaware of any tribes on the mainland. The [Riekr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riekr) of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) are sometimes described as [crossbreeds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Multiraciality) between standard Goblins and Rieklings,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Live-11)</sup> or as just a larger variety of Riekling.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM71:CS-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Live-11)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/SR-creature-Riekling_Charger.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Riekling_Charger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Riekling_Charger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Riekling_Charger.jpg) A Riekling mounted on a Tusked Bristleback (Skyrim) The Rieklings have some notable history, such as in the mainland in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Ice-Biter Tribe](Lore_Riekling.md#Ice-Biter_Tribe) of rieklings attacked the town of [Cragwallow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cragwallow) in [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) and were wiped out in retaliation.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOM.26M-3)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), The [Coldsnap Tribe](Lore_Riekling.md#Coldsnap_Tribe) inhabited the Dwemer [Vault of Mhuvak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vault_of_Mhuvnak), making the retrieval of the legendary [Wrathstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrathstone) problematic.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOL.26K-4)</sup> They used some Dwarven gear as a result of their discovery of an abandoned Dwarven site (even using it to armor a [troll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll)), with some believing that their hoarding of scrap would result in them achieving more advanced civilization,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Landal Gevont-1)</sup> but they were slaughtered by members of the Wrathstone expedition.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOL.26K-4)</sup>

In Solstheim, the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) are attacked by them frequently.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup> [Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karstaag) the [frost giant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Giant) once employed Rieklings to guard his fortress, [Castle Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Karstaag). When he was taken by [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) to participate in [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), a Riekling named Dulk took control of the castle in his master's absence. A rebellious Riekling known as Krish attempted to enlist the help of several [grahl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahl) to overthrow Dulk, but the creatures turned on the Rieklings and killed many of them. The grahl were killed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), though it is unknown which of the two Rieklings they assisted.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BMRiek-13)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a tribe of Rieklings attacked the mead hall at [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) and drove out the Nords.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DB-14)</sup> Another tribe from an island on the northern coast made friendly contact with outsiders in an attempt to trade for [netch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Netch) [leather](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Netch_Leather).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Culture
*Hawala faaaakara. Baaaa rakhee kaloo. Pooja kan faroo kee jaa. Goora! Goora! Goora!*
—Riekling Godspeak Song Rieklings have a tribal society, often led by a chief. Members of the tribe are known as "tribe-kin". Rieklings can construct basic structures, such as tents, fences, bridges and lookout towers. They partake in ritualistic "Godspeak" ceremonies. One such ceremony involves the burning of [Scathecraw](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scathecraw), or "redgrass", a plant native to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) which spread to southern Solstheim after the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md). Rieklings are fond of "dizzydrink", alcoholic beverages that they scavenge.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Chief-10)</sup>

Rieklings are known to use spears,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DB-14)</sup> lances, blades, bolas,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-WT-16)</sup> shields and axes in combat.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BM-17)</sup> They often tame [Tusked Bristlebacks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tusked_Bristleback), a native type of [boar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boar), for use as warmounts. They also have the ability to create simple traps to guard their lairs. They commonly dwell in ice caves or remote locations, but they have been known to inhabit [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and Nordic ruins as well. Rieklings can be found wandering the wilds of Solstheim, in their lairs, or waiting in ambush for an unlucky traveler.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DB-14)</sup>

## Named Riekling Tribes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg) Armaments of the Coldsnap Tribe Below are a list of Riekling Tribes whose names are known. Other tribes exist, but have not been named and are not included in the list.

### Coldsnap Tribe
The **Coldsnap Tribe** were a riekling tribe who squatted in the [Vault of Mhuvnak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vault_of_Mhuvnak) of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Riekling Slayer-18)</sup> They were believed to be related to the Ice-Biter Tribe, possibly an offshoot, and included standard goblins, [ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), and even a troll among their ranks.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-riek-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LG-20)</sup>

### Ice-Biter Tribe
The **Ice-Biter Tribe** were a riekling tribe found in the [Cragwallow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cragwallow) caves of Eastmarch. They were known to abduct people.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOM.26M-3)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-riek-19)</sup>
