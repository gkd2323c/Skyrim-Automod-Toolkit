# Vigil of Stendarr

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/SR-npc-Vigilant_of_Stendarr_05.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Vigilant_of_Stendarr_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vigilant_of_Stendarr_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vigilant_of_Stendarr_05.jpg) Vigilants of Stendarr confronting an abomination The **Vigil of Stendarr** is an order of holy warriors who fight in the name of the divine [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md). Vigilants can be found journeying across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in their dedication to wiping out [Daedra](Skyrim_Daedra.md) and any other "abominations" (such as [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md), [werewolves](Skyrim_Werewolf.md), and [witches](Skyrim_Witch.md)). The Vigil's base of operations in Skyrim is the [Hall of the Vigilant](Skyrim_Hall_of_the_Vigilant.md), located south of [Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md). They also gather near [Stendarr's Beacon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stendarr%27s_Beacon) in [The Rift](Skyrim_The_Rift.md). The Vigilants in Skyrim are led by [Keeper Carcette](Skyrim_Keeper_Carcette.md).

The Vigil has close ties to the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard_(faction).md) in Skyrim; not only do the two factions have a similar focus, but the newly-reformed Dawnguard was started by two former members of the Vigil, [Isran](Skyrim_Isran.md) and [Celann](Skyrim_Celann.md).

Vigilants can often be found walking in groups along roads, or found individually in certain places. Any Vigilant that is not in combat will [cure](Skyrim_Cure_Disease.md) any [diseases](Skyrim_Disease.md) you have, if asked. If you have lycanthropy, they will not attack you unless you approach them in beast form.

You may have to fight them during [Daedric quests](Skyrim_Daedric_Quests.md); for instance, when following [Barbas](Skyrim_Barbas.md) at the beginning of the quest [A Daedra's Best Friend](Skyrim_A_Daedra%27s_Best_Friend.md), you might cross paths with a group of Vigilants of Stendarr who will attack Barbas and will have to be killed before you can proceed.

During the events of the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md), the Hall of the Vigilant is destroyed by vampires, and most of its membership is killed. [Vigilant Adalvald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant_Adalvald) and [Vigilant Tolan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vigilant_Tolan) survived and were taken to [Dimhollow Crypt](Skyrim_Dimhollow_Crypt.md) for interrogation, where they later died.

## Vigilant of Stendarr
Vigilants of Stendarr make use of both [one-handed](Skyrim_One-handed.md) melee attacks and defensive magic in battle. They are armed with a leveled [mace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mace) (any quality from [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) to [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md)), and all members have the [Oakflesh](Skyrim_Oakflesh.md), [Healing](Skyrim_Healing.md), and [Lesser Ward](Skyrim_Lesser_Ward.md) spells.

They wear the following outfit, which varies slightly depending on their level:

- [Amulet of Stendarr](Skyrim_Amulet_of_Stendarr.md)
- **Boots**: [steel cuffed boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Cuffed_Boots) or [steel plate boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate_Boots)
- **Gauntlets**: 50% chance to have either [steel Nordic gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Nordic_Gauntlets), [steel Imperial gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Imperial_Gauntlets), or [steel plate gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate_Gauntlets)
- **Hood**: a [novice hood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Novice_Hood)
- **Robes**: either [novice robes of alteration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Novice_Robes_of_Alteration) or [novice robes of restoration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Novice_Robes_of_Restoration).

In addition to the gear listed above, Vigilants carry a [belted tunic](Skyrim_Belted_Tunic.md), which they wear when sleeping. They also carry two leveled healing potions and a potion of cure disease, a torch, and may carry a book or recipe (*[Physicalities of Werewolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Physicalities_of_Werewolves)*, *[The Book of Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Book_of_Daedra)*, *[Varieties of Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Varieties_of_Daedra)*, *[Immortal Blood](Skyrim_Immortal_Blood.md)*, *[Cure Disease Potion Recipe](Skyrim_Recipes.md#000f5cb8)*, or *[Resist Poison Potion Recipe](Skyrim_Recipes.md#000f5cc5)*). Vigilants will drop a small amount of gold on death.

## Charmed Vigilant<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d9/SR-npc-Charmed_Vigilant.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Charmed_Vigilant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Charmed_Vigilant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Charmed_Vigilant.jpg) A Charmed Vigilant in Ruunvald Charmed Vigilants are found in [Ruunvald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ruunvald_Excavation) during the quest [Bolstering the Ranks](Skyrim_Bolstering_the_Ranks.md). A [warlock](Skyrim_Warlock.md) named [Minorne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Minorne) charmed a temple leader, [Moric Sidrey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Moric_Sidrey), while he was excavating the Ruunvald site. Under her guidance, he gathered more Vigilants there, and they were all eventually charmed by her as well. They worship her as a god, and will fight to protect her against anyone who comes along. They die after Minorne is killed.

## Dialogue

### Greetings
The first time you encounter Vigilants in Skyrim, they will greet you with this following line:

- *"You speak to a Vigilant of Stendarr. Cavort with any Daedra, and we will hunt you down."*

When you encounter Vigilants in Skyrim, they will greet you with one of the following lines:

- *"Stendarr's Mercy be upon you, for the Vigil has none to spare."*
- *"The suffering the Daedra caused will not go unpunished."*
- *"Walk always in the light, or we will drag you to it."*
- *"Wherever the Daedra hide, the Vigil of Stendarr will cast them into the light."*
- *"The Mercy of Stendarr does not extend to Daedra worshippers."*
- *"You're bloated with disease. The Vigilant of Stendarr can help you, as long as you promise to never assist the Daedra."*

If you meet a group of Vigilants [fighting an abomination](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Vigilants_fighting_Atronach) and help them fight it, they will greet you with one of the following lines:

- *"Well met, friend. We've been chasing that fiend for a long time. On behalf of the Vigil, you have my thanks."*
- *"It's done, then. We've been chasing that fiend for a long time."*

### Goodbyes
Vigilants will say one of the following three lines when you exit dialogue:

- *"Stendarr be with you."*
- *"The Vigil will be watching you."*
- *"None escape the Vigil. All come into the light."*

### Conversations
When you meet a Vigilant of Stendarr, you can ask a few questions about their purpose and their beliefs:

- **You're a Vigilant of Stendarr?**
- *"Yes. Our order was founded after the Oblivion Crisis. We dedicate our lives to facing the threat of Daedra wherever they appear. We're quartered in the [Hall of the Vigilant](Skyrim_Hall_of_the_Vigilant.md). [Keeper Carcette](Skyrim_Keeper_Carcette.md) heads Skyrim's branch of our Order there, providing healing and justice as needed."*
- **Who's Stendarr?**
- *"He is the God of Mercy. The patron of order and justice for all of Tamriel. We bring his compassion where none can be found, by cleansing all those who would offend his children."*
- **You hunt daedra?**
- *"And any other abominations that prey on mortals. Vampires. Werewolves. Witches. But the Daedra are the worst. Their callous disregard for our lives is abhorrent in the eyes of the God of Mercy."*
- **What's dangerous about daedra?**
- *"They are profane creatures with no hope of redemption. They lure innocents to their deaths and corrupt the souls of all they touch. And their heretical artifacts grant powers to mortals that wreak havoc across Tamriel. They must be destroyed or guarded to prevent temptation."*

If you have a disease, you will have the option to ask for healing:

- **Please cure me.**
- *"Stendarr's Light purify you of your ills."*

## Notes
- Be careful when using [Healing Hands](Skyrim_Healing_Hands.md); if a Vigilant of Stendarr is healed by this spell while out on the road, there is a chance they will become hostile. They will also turn hostile if under the effects of [Rally](Skyrim_Rally_(effect).md).
- If you accidentally strike a Vigilant of Stendarr while in combat, there's a chance that all Vigilants will turn hostile.
- They will attack [Barbas](Skyrim_Barbas.md) if they encounter him during "[A Daedra's Best Friend](Skyrim_A_Daedra%27s_Best_Friend.md)." Unfortunately, since Barbas is invulnerable, they will perish.
- Although the Vigilants always wear Amulets of Stendarr, they never carry [shields](Skyrim_Shield.md).
- If you ask the Vigilants stationed at the Hall of the Vigilant who they are, only the "Yes. Our order was founded after the Oblivion Crisis. We dedicate our lives to facing the threat of Daedra wherever they appear." line will be heard.
- Even after the Hall is destroyed with the *Dawnguard* add-on, they will still act as if it's still active, and Keeper Carcette is still alive.
- It is unknown if the Vigil are opposed to the worship of Daedric Princes who aren't considered evil, such as [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) and [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). They also won't take action against [Orc Strongholds](Skyrim_Orc_Strongholds.md) who worship [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).
- Although the Vigil claim to be against all things daedric, you can approach them with Daedric weapons or armor equipped, you can conjure Atronachs (such as a [frost atronach](Skyrim_Frost_Atronach.md)) in their presence, or have thralls around (like [Dead Thrall](Skyrim_Dead_Thrall.md) or [a flame one](Skyrim_Flame_Thrall.md)) and they will not be hostile. Same goes for being a [vampire](Skyrim_Vampirism.md) or [werewolf](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md): they will not recognize you as one, despite their hatred of vampires and werewolves, too.

## Bugs
- They can sometimes be found carrying [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md) weapons, despite their opposition to all things Daedric. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.6, addresses this issue. They will only carry non-Daedric weapons.
- Vigilant Tolan and Keeper Carcette do not wear their Stendarr amulets because they were placed in their inventories instead of being part of their outfits. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.2.9, fixes this bug.
- Vigilants of Stendarr do not comment on any of the diseases from traps, the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on, or [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md). - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.5, fixes this bug.

### Unused content
In the game data, there is dialogue for Vigilants stopping you on the road if you are carrying a Daedric [artifact](Skyrim_Artifacts.md). However, this is set to never happen in-game:

- *"Stop! We know you're holding a Daedric artifact. Hand it over."*
- **Why should I hand you anything?**
- *"The Vigil of Stendarr cleanses all Daedric filth from Tamriel. Give us any artifacts you have, or we'll purge you like all the others."*

You could choose to turn over your artifacts, ending the conversation peacefully:

- **Fine. Take all the artifacts I have.**
- *"Wise decision. The vaults in the Hall of the Vigilant will guard this against future temptation."*

You could also refuse them, at which point they would engage you in combat:

- **I'm not giving you anything.**
- *"Then prepare yourself to receive Stendarr's Mercy."*
