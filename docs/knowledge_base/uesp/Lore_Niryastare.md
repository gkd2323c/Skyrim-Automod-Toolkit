[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/OB-place-Niryastare.jpg/200px-OB-place-Niryastare.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Niryastare.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Niryastare.jpg) Niryastare circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Niryastare ([/n iː. r i ˈ ɒ. s t. ə. r eɪ/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ([![About this sound](https://images.uesp.net/e/e8/Speaker.svg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-audio-word-Niryastare.ogg) [listen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-audio-word-Niryastare.ogg))) is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located on the northern edge of the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) at the banks of the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md), northeast of [Sutch](Lore_Sutch.md) and south of the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border. Parts of the ruin extend into the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md), towards [Fort Hastrel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Hastrel).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBMap-1)</sup> They have long been known to be haunted by all manner of [undead](Lore_Undead.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Aranande Blades-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Beragon-3)</sup>

## History
In the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) was confronted at Niryastare by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), who threatened to align the other Princes against her if she did not restrain her power. Ithelia did not heed this warning, and she was subsequently imprisoned by Mora with the aid of [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). Mephala's contribution to this was the creation of the Skein of Secrets, a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that can be used to track Ithelia. Following Ithelia's imprisonment, Mora wiped all memory of her, and the Ayleids of Niryastare built a vault to house the Skein of Secrets. Using [Aetherial](Lore_Aetherius.md) [light](Lore_Elements.md#Light), they created an impenetrable magical seal which could only be unlocked by harnessing the power of a [Great Welkynd Stone](Lore_Great_Welkynd_Stone.md). This, in turn, was powered by four Lesser [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md), which were then scattered throughout the ruin and magically hidden.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESORot TP-4)</sup>

Niryastare is presumed to have been abandoned following the fall of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) at the end of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), as the Empire toppled the city-states one by one and the clans inhabiting them fled.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ASi V-5)</sup>

At some point between [2E 538](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_538) and [2E 544](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_544), Beragon Sharp-Dagger and his adventuring company explored Ayleid ruins across the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), attempting to penetrate the depths of Niryastare without success. The group could not enter the vault, and disbanded following their failure.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt C:Beragon-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Beragon-3)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Ithelia was freed from her prison in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). In order to defend her against her rival Princes, a cult known as the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md) set up wooden fortifications around Niryastare and began occupying the ruins. They entered the underground ruins and attempted to recover the Skein of Secrets, but between the ruin's traps, undead guardians, and magical defenses, the cultists could not get any deeper into the ruins than the Welkynd Chamber. The Skein was instead recovered by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and Beragon, who used [Abolisher](Lore_Abolisher.md) and the [Lamp of Clarity](Lore_Lamp_of_Clarity.md) to recover the hidden Welkynd Stones and enter the vault.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESORot TP-4)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ancient derelict ruins were still accessible, but its subterranean halls were crawling with undead such as [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md), [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md), [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md), and [liches](Lore_Lich.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OB-7)</sup> It was among the ruins explored by [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) adventurers that were sent out by [Gortwog](Lore_Gortwog.md) to seek treasure for the glory of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBAdventurer-8)</sup>

Due to its haunted nature and shared Ayleid origins, inhabitants of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) sent out [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) to investigate the ruins in [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180) in search of the lich [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril.md), viewing it as a possible lair for his operations.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Saashi Blades-9)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Aranande Blades-2)</sup> The crumbling ruins were filled with overgrown plantlife and sarcophagi, and while the Warrior cleared out the many wights, skeletons, and [skeevers](Lore_Skeever.md) within, Celemaril was nowhere to be found, having made his lair at Drachurst Keep instead.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Saashi Blades-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRo N-10)</sup>
